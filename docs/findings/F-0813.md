## Finding

The H1 of `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/unknown-reason-rule.md` is `# Unknown reason rule` (un-hyphenated). Every other surface that names the rule spells it `Unknown-reason rule` with a hyphen: the file's own slug and anchor (`unknown-reason-rule`), the in-body self-references inside the rule's paragraph (`per the **Unknown-reason rule** above`), and every inbound spec reference in `drain-state-contract.md`, `host-prerequisites.md`, `patch-skew-degradation.md`, `session-only-degraded-state.md`, `version-bump-intro.md`, and `version-bump-triggers.md`, plus the plan-side references in `plan_topics/conventions.md`, `plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md`, `plan_topics/V9h-unknown-reason-rule.md`, and `plan_topics/V9h-T-unknown-reason-rule.md`.

The hyphenated form is the load-bearing term ("Unknown-reason" is a compound modifier on "rule"); the un-hyphenated H1 is the lone outlier. A reader landing on the page sees a title that does not match the term every other page uses to point at it, and a `grep "Unknown-reason rule"` against the corpus silently misses the document's own H1.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** f5e89f4 (`docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set`, 2026-06-04)
**History:** The file did not exist before f5e89f4. That commit ran the one-shot split driver `docs/_tools/split_spec.py`, which generated each child page's H1 from its slug via `title_from_slug(slug) = slug.replace("-", " ")[:1].upper() + slug.replace("-", " ")[1:]` (line 115). For the slug `unknown-reason-rule` this produces `Unknown reason rule` — every hyphen in the slug is replaced by a space, including the compound-modifier hyphen that belongs in the term. The defect is purely mechanical: the splitter had no way to distinguish a structural hyphen (slug separator) from a load-bearing hyphen (compound modifier). The same routine is the source of the sibling H1-vs-canonical-term mismatches called out elsewhere in this review (`queryerror-variants.md`, the `audit-*.md` filename/H1 transpositions, etc.); the inbound-reference form `Unknown-reason rule` was already in use in the pre-split monolithic `pi-integration-contract.md` and survived the split unchanged.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Change the H1 of `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/unknown-reason-rule.md` from `# Unknown reason rule` to `# Unknown-reason rule`. No other edit is required: the anchor (`unknown-reason-rule`) is the file's slug and is unaffected by H1 text, every inbound link already uses the hyphenated form, and the in-body bold self-references (`**Unknown-reason rule**`) are already correct.

Edge case for the implementer: do not rename the file or its anchor — `unknown-reason-rule.md` and `#unknown-reason-rule` are inbound contract surface (the slug appears in `plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md`'s `cka-28` row and in every spec-side cross-reference) and a slug rename would cascade into a corpus-wide link sweep this finding does not justify.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader following an inbound `Unknown-reason rule` link lands on a page titled `Unknown reason rule` and has to confirm they are in the right place; corpus-wide greps on the canonical hyphenated form do not find the H1 line. No runtime behaviour, no normative obligation, and no anchor is affected.
