## Finding

The `## Session Model` bullet list in `docs/spec_topics/session-model-and-appendix.md` enumerates SM-1 … SM-8 as a single flat sequence. Four of those bullets — SM-3b (nested under SM-3) and the top-level SM-4, SM-5, SM-6 — close with an italicised `*Implementation gate:*` clause stating that the obligation is gated on the same host-prerequisites clause (a) open contradiction. The other bullets (SM-1, SM-2, SM-3a, SM-7 and its sub-letters, SM-8) carry no such clause and are presently buildable. There is no sub-heading, no visual separator, and no aggregator paragraph at the top of the list that partitions the obligations into "pickable now" and "gated pending clause (a) resolution".

The result is that the gating status of SM-3b/4/5/6 is communicated only through a trailing italic phrase inside each obligation's body, several lines of dense link-laden prose into the bullet. An implementer scanning the Session Model list for what to build next has no scannable signal: SM-3 looks active (because SM-3a is), but its SM-3b leg is blocked; SM-4/5/6 sit visually inline with SM-7/SM-8 even though only the latter two are pickable. The orientation page's headline contract — "the runtime's session-level contract decomposes into the eight sub-obligations below" — is also weakened, since four of the eight are gated on a still-unresolved contradiction owned elsewhere.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit

**Introducing commit:** `b8f004f` (2026-06-19) — *pi-loom spec-plan: fix F-0719 — SM-4/5/6/3b degraded-state implementation-gate contingency markers*.

**History:** The eight-bullet SM-N decomposition was introduced in `40ffdc5` (2026-05-27, "T19a — Replace session-model paragraph with eight SM-N sub-units") inside `docs/spec.md`, with no implementation-gate markers — at that point no SM-N was gated, so a flat list raised no scannability concern. `81bf52a` (2026-06-11) relocated the list verbatim into `docs/spec_topics/session-model-and-appendix.md` as part of the spec-topics split. `b8f004f` then resolved F-0719 by appending the `*Implementation gate:*` clauses to SM-3b/4/5/6 inline, retaining the flat-list shape; this is the commit at which the SM-N list first contained a mix of gated and active obligations without structural separation. `80fcd63` (2026-06-20, F-0718+F-0823) later refined SM-4's marker to cite the `provably-differs` verdict and the authoritative-determination recording shape, but did not alter the list's structure. The defect is therefore the structural choice made in `b8f004f` — adding gate markers in-place rather than extracting a sub-section — rather than an interaction across commits.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Restructure the `## Session Model` bullet list so the gated obligations are visually distinct from the active ones, in place, without changing any anchor IDs (GOV-23 forbids reuse or renumbering of published `sm-N-…` anchors).

Concretely:

1. Keep the umbrella prose paragraph at the top of `## Session Model` unchanged, but add a one-sentence aggregator at its end naming the partition: e.g. *"Of the eight, SM-1, SM-2, SM-3a, SM-7a–SM-7e, and SM-8 are presently active; SM-3b, SM-4, SM-5, and SM-6 are gated on [Pi Integration Contract — Host prerequisites for the degraded-state branch, clause (a)](./pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md#degraded-state-host-prerequisites) and are grouped under "Obligations gated on PIC host-prerequisites clause (a)" below."*
2. In the bullet list, leave the active obligations (SM-1, SM-2, SM-3 with SM-3a, SM-7 with SM-7a–SM-7e, SM-8) where they are.
3. After SM-8, introduce a third-level heading **"Obligations gated on PIC host-prerequisites clause (a)"** and move the SM-3b, SM-4, SM-5, SM-6 bullets verbatim under it — preserving each bullet's `<a id="sm-…">` anchor, its full body prose, and its trailing `*Implementation gate:*` clause (the markers stay; the heading complements them, it does not replace them).
4. At the SM-3 bullet (which retains SM-3a beneath it), add a single forward-link sentence at the end of SM-3's umbrella paragraph: *"SM-3b's session-swap semantics are gated and live in [Obligations gated on PIC host-prerequisites clause (a)](#obligations-gated-on-pic-host-prerequisites-clause-a) below."* — this keeps SM-3's two-sub-obligation decomposition discoverable from the active list while pulling SM-3b's body into the gated section.
5. Cross-reference the gated section once from `plan.md` §Blocked obligations' `V9l` entry (read-only addition, not a plan edit required by this finding) and from `host-prerequisites.md` clause (a)'s existing back-link to SM-4/5/6/3b — both already cite per-obligation anchors, so the relocation does not require call-site fix-ups.

**Edge cases the implementer must watch.** The four `sm-3b-…`, `sm-4-…`, `sm-5-…`, `sm-6-…` anchors are inbound-cited from at least `host-prerequisites.md` clause (a), `version-bump-step2.md`, `model-changes-and-non-goals.md`, and `plan.md`; under GOV-23 the anchors MUST move with their content rather than being renumbered. The new section heading is editorial-only and is not a `sm-…` anchor, so it does not enter GOV-23's stability promise. The umbrella paragraph's claim of "eight sub-obligations" remains true after restructuring — no sub-obligation is added, removed, or renumbered.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer reading the Session Model list for orientation can mistake SM-3b/4/5/6 for buildable obligations alongside SM-1/2/3a/7/8 unless they read each bullet's trailing italic clause carefully. The corresponding plan leaf (`V9l`) is already correctly marked blocked, so the misread is recoverable at plan-pickup time rather than producing wrong code; the cost is wasted implementer attention and weaker scannability of an orientation page, not divergent implementations.
