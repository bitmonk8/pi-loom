## Problem

Every SHOULD-level item (f)‚Äì(an) in `version-bump-step2.md` ends with the same ~56-word escalation clause, repeated verbatim 34 times with only the typed-contract slot varying (`session-lifetime`, `handler-promise-lifecycle`, `turn-liveness`, etc.) ‚Äî roughly 1,900 words of templated boilerplate inside a single ~11,200-word file. Per-item information density collapses to that single varying token: a reader who has parsed the clause once gains nothing from the next 33 repetitions. ## Solution approach

In `version-bump-step2.md`, restructure the SHOULD-level (f)‚Äì(an) block so the shared escalation rule is stated once in a lead note at the head of the block, then trim each item's closing escalation clause down to its unique typed-contract slot. In `future-considerations/surface-extensions.md`, add an entry under `id="tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact"` naming the collected post-loom-1.0 scope of converting these editorial audits to mechanical step-2(a) gates item by item. Add a forward-link from the lead note to that entry so a reader of either page sees the connection. The two edits are independent and each improves the spec on its own.

## Solution constraints

- Items whose closing sentence already carries item-specific content beyond the generic escalation tail (the corpus-currency fixture obligations on items (i)/(u)/(aa)/(ab)/(af)) MUST retain that item-specific content after the generic tail is trimmed.
- The lead note MUST NOT weaken the per-item recorded `pass` / `fail` / `N/A` / `not-performed` obligation, which is set in the preamble and item bodies, not in the escalation tail being collapsed.
