## Finding

Every SHOULD-level item (f)–(an) in `version-bump-step2.md` ends with the same closing sentence: "This obligation is SHOULD-level only at loom 1.0; it escalates to a build-time pin (on the same footing as the seven-capability literal-read assertion under step 2(a) above) once Pi exposes a typed `<X>` contract that step 2(a)'s surface-inventory probe can mechanically verify, at which point the editorial audit prescribed here is superseded by the mechanical gate." The clause is ~56 words and recurs verbatim 34 times across items (f) through (an); only the typed-contract slot `<X>` varies (`session-lifetime`, `handler-promise-lifecycle`, `ordering`, `turn-liveness`, `registry-population-ordering`, `command-registration-ordering`, etc.). That is roughly 1,900 words of templated boilerplate inside a single ~11,200-word file — the largest single source of inflation on the page, and a measurable share of the corpus-level word budget the §size-cap guidance targets.

Two problems follow. First, the per-item information density collapses to a single varying token: a reader who has parsed the clause once gains nothing from the next 33 repetitions, while every per-item context (the cited paragraph, the recording shape, what falsification looks like, why no SDK signal exists) gets pushed under that uniform tail. Second, the 34 escalation statements describe a single coherent post-loom-1.0 work item — convert this whole class of editorial audits to mechanical step-2(a) gates as Pi's typed surface grows — but that work item lives nowhere as a collected scope statement. `future-considerations/surface-extensions.md` already carries a `Tooling deferrals (no loom 1.0 impact)` section that is the natural home for it; today the post-loom-1.0 plan is reconstructable only by reading 34 inline clauses and noticing they say the same thing.

The two halves are independent: collapsing the per-item clause is an editorial refactor confined to `version-bump-step2.md`; surfacing the collected post-loom-1.0 scope is a new entry in `future-considerations/surface-extensions.md` (or sibling). Either lands without the other, and both improve readability and discoverability on their own.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Lift the escalation clause to a lead note above item (f)

**Approach.** Insert a single SHOULD-level header block (e.g. `#### SHOULD-level items (f)–(an)`) directly before item (f), and place the escalation rule in one paragraph immediately under it: "Each item below is SHOULD-level only at loom 1.0; each escalates to a build-time pin on the same footing as the seven-capability literal-read assertion under step 2(a) above once Pi exposes a typed contract of the kind named in the item's tail (e.g. `session-lifetime`, `handler-promise-lifecycle`, `turn-liveness`, ...) that step 2(a)'s surface-inventory probe can mechanically verify, at which point the editorial audit prescribed here is superseded by the mechanical gate." Then trim every (f)–(an) item's closing sentence to the unique typed-contract slot: e.g. item (f) ends "Escalation contract: `session-lifetime`." or similar terse marker; items that need to vary the escalation predicate keep their full closing sentence as an override.

**Spec edits.**
- One new heading + one lead-note paragraph above item (f) in `version-bump-step2.md`.
- 34 closing-sentence trims, one per item (f)–(an), reduced to the typed-contract-slot marker. Items whose typed-contract token is identical (e.g. several `complete()` items name `contract` generically) can simply omit the override and inherit the lead note.

**Pros.**
- Removes ~1,800 words from the page in one editorial pass; readability of every item improves because the per-item tail no longer drowns the per-item context.
- The forward-compatibility rule becomes a single citable paragraph rather than 34 distributed copies, and a future Pi minor that lands a typed contract resolves the per-item escalation against one anchor.
- Pairs cleanly with the related finding "tier boundary buried; no visual marker between (e) and (f)" (the new `#### SHOULD-level items (f)–(an)` heading is exactly the missing marker).
- Renders the related "directionally wrong `under step 2(a) above`" finding mechanical: the lead note carries the citation once, in the correct direction.

**Cons.**
- A reader of an isolated item (f)–(an) no longer sees the escalation behaviour inline; they must read the lead note. The cost is one short scroll-up per item.
- The per-item override mechanism (when an item's escalation predicate genuinely differs) adds a small consistency obligation: a future item-adder must check whether the lead-note default applies before copying a predecessor.

**Risks.**
- Minor: if the lead note is later edited without checking that every (f)–(an) item still wants its escalation contract, an item could silently inherit a wrong default. Mitigated by keeping each item's typed-contract token explicit in its trimmed closer.

### Option B — Collect the post-loom-1.0 mechanical-gate scope in `future-considerations/surface-extensions.md`

**Approach.** Add a bullet (or short sub-section) to the existing `Tooling deferrals (no loom 1.0 impact)` block in `surface-extensions.md` naming the full scope: "Convert `version-bump-step2.md` items (f)–(an) from editorial-review audits to mechanical step-2(a) build-time gates, item by item, as Pi exposes typed contracts for each named behavioural surface (`session-lifetime`, `handler-promise-lifecycle`, `turn-liveness`, `messages-ordering`, `registry-population-ordering`, `command-registration-ordering`, ...). Each escalation lands as a co-edit of one `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` entry plus deletion of the corresponding (f)–(an) item." Cross-link back from the `version-bump-step2.md` preamble (or the Option-A lead note) so a reader of either page sees the connection.

**Spec edits.**
- One new bullet / sub-section in `future-considerations/surface-extensions.md` under the `Tooling deferrals` block (anchor `tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact`).
- One cross-reference from `version-bump-step2.md` (cleanest at the bottom of the SHOULD-level block, or in the Option-A lead note if Option A lands first).

**Pros.**
- Surfaces the largest post-loom-1.0 maintenance scope in the document the spec already designates as the home for such deferrals; a planner reading `surface-extensions.md` no longer has to reconstruct it from 34 inline footnotes.
- Independently valuable even without Option A: it is purely additive and changes no existing normative text.

**Cons.**
- Without Option A the per-item boilerplate still bloats `version-bump-step2.md`; this option alone does not reduce page weight.
- Adds a synchronisation obligation: a future item that adds a new SHOULD-level audit should be added to both pages.

**Risks.**
- Negligible. The new entry is in `future-considerations`, which is non-normative by construction.

### Recommendation

Land **Option A first, then Option B**, as two separate edits.

Option A is the larger and bounded win — it removes the ~1,800-word inflation, supplies the missing tier-boundary marker, and creates the single citation site that Option B will cross-link back to. Sequencing it first means Option B lands against a stable anchor (the new lead-note paragraph) rather than against 34 individual item tails that no longer exist.

Option B is independently mergeable and should follow as a small additive edit to `surface-extensions.md`, with one cross-reference back into the now-collapsed SHOULD-level block.

Edge cases the implementer must watch on Option A: (i) items whose closing sentence already varies from the template (e.g. items (i)/(u)/(aa)/(ab)/(af), which carry corpus-currency fixture obligations and additionally name "and whenever a provider publishes an error-format or API-version change") must keep their item-specific second sentence even after the generic escalation tail is removed; (ii) the lead note must not weaken the per-item "recorded `pass` / `fail` / `N/A` / `not-performed`" obligation, which is set in the preamble paragraph and the item bodies, not in the escalation tail being collapsed; (iii) the `V18c` plan leaf's `(a)`–`(al)` range citation is independently stale and is not a blocker on this edit, but a touching commit should refresh it to `(a)`–`(an)`.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The spec is conformant and the per-item audit obligations are unambiguous — every item still states its own escalation behaviour, just verbosely. The cost is page weight and the implementer's ability to see the post-loom-1.0 mechanical-gate roadmap as a single planned scope rather than as 34 distributed footnotes. Left as-is, the file remains a discoverability tax on every contributor who reads it, and the corpus carries ~1,800 words of boilerplate that earn nothing.
