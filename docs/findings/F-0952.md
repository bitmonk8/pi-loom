## Finding

`V11j` and `V17c` split the binder-call cancellation surface: `V11j` owns the *in-flight* case (an abort landing during the binder's provider call), while `V17c` owns the *pre-call* `binder-call` checkpoint (the `binder-call` `CheckpointKind` firing immediately before the binder's LLM call is issued). That demarcation is stated only inside the single Tests bullet of `V11j` / `V11j-T` ("distinct from the pre-call `binder-call` checkpoint abort `V17c` owns…").

`V11j`'s Adds field describes the leaf as "In-flight binder-call cancellation forwarding…" and lists `V17c` in `Deps`, but never states what `V17c` owns or where `V11j`'s scope ends. The Adds field is the canonical "what this leaf owns" statement; a reader or implementer scanning Adds (the natural place to learn a leaf's boundary) to understand `V11j` sees the dependency edge to `V17c` with no explanation of the division of labour. The ownership split is documented, but in the wrong field.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** 66b3815 — pi-loom plan: resolve "V11f bundles binder retry budget with in-flight cancellation forwarding" (2026-06-16, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** Both `V11j-binder-call-cancellation.md` and `V11j-T-binder-call-cancellation.md` were created in 66b3815 (`git log --follow --diff-filter=A` reports that commit as the sole creation point). From inception the V11j/V17c scope demarcation lived in the Tests bullet and the Adds field never carried it. The later commit 5b95143 (2026-06-18) only retargeted the demarcation's owner reference from `V17a` to `V17c` and added `V17c` to `Deps` (`git show 5b95143` touches the Tests bullet and Deps line, not Adds); it preserved the placement rather than introducing it. The defect is therefore present at the leaf's inception.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V11j-binder-call-cancellation.md`, add the scope demarcation to the **Adds** field so the canonical ownership statement records the boundary. Insert a sentence stating that `V17c` owns the pre-call `binder-call` checkpoint (the `binder-call` `CheckpointKind` firing immediately before the binder's LLM call) and `V11j` covers the in-flight case (an abort landing during the provider call). The exact ownership fact is already worded in the Tests bullet and can be reused: "`V17c` owns the pre-call `binder-call` checkpoint (the `binder-call` `CheckpointKind` firing immediately before the binder's LLM call); `V11j` covers the in-flight binder-call abort."

`V11j-T`'s Adds is a stock pointer ("Failing tests for the paired `V11j` implementation leaf."); the demarcation's natural home is the impl leaf's Adds. Either mirror the same sentence into `V11j-T`'s Adds or leave it to inherit via its pointer — both satisfy the finding once `V11j` Adds carries the boundary.

The pre-call/in-flight wording in the Tests bullet may stay as the test specification; the fix is additive to Adds, not a relocation out of Tests.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer or reviewer reading `V11j`'s Adds to learn the leaf's scope sees only "in-flight binder-call cancellation forwarding" and a `Deps` edge to `V17c`, with no statement that `V17c` owns the pre-call `binder-call` checkpoint. Without reading the Tests bullet they cannot tell where `V11j` ends, and could under- or over-implement the pre-call checkpoint path that `V17c` already covers. The information exists, so no leaf is blocked; the cost is the avoidable scope ambiguity in the canonical ownership field.
