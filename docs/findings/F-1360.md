## Problem

The `cka-44` facets `V15a`, `V15e`, and `V7a` cite "(IMPL area)" prose in their `implementation-notes.md` ┬ºStatic-resolution `Tests` bullets with no `cka-44` token and no inline leaf-ID, so none carries the facet-naming citing test `H5f` requires. `V7d` is the row's `co-witness` and is correctly excluded from the facet partition. On the live-corpus footing that binds at the loom 1.0 release gate, `H5f` reddens once per affected facet, blocking the gate until the facet test corpus is retrofitted.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-later-edit
**Introducing commit:** `26a8794` (Thu Jun 18 2026, Thomas Andersen) ÔÇö "pi-loom plan: resolve 'split coverage-matrix rows can ship a facet untested'"
**History:** The facet leaves' `Tests` bullets have carried prose area labels ("(QRY code-keyed area)", "(IMPL area)", "cancellation.md ÔÇö *Race semanticsÔÇª*") since the initial plan build (`c6a664e`); the `cka-<n>` tokens were minted later (`7259777`, "code-keyed obligation areas lack a canonical citable token"). Neither created a defect, because no rule yet required a facet `Tests` bullet to cite the `cka-<n>` token plus its own leaf-ID inline. Commit `26a8794` introduced **both** the `coverage-matrix.md` *Multi-leaf-row per-facet citing tests* convention and the `H5f` gate arm that enforce exactly that inline citation; its diff touched only `H5f-per-facet-citing-test-gate.md`, `conventions.md`, `coverage-matrix.md`, `H5a`, `H5b`, and `H6a` ÔÇö it retrofitted none of the `cka-12` / `cka-33` / `cka-44` facet leaves' `Tests` bullets. The facet bullets became non-conforming at that commit.

## Solution approach

In `V15a`, `V15e`, and `V7a` (and their paired `-T` leaves), make the IMPL static-resolution `Tests` bullet cite both the `cka-44` token and that leaf's own leaf-ID inline alongside the existing prose area label.

## Solution constraints

- Leave the `V7d` co-witness bullet unchanged ÔÇö it is excluded from the `cka-44` facet partition.
- Out of scope: `coverage-matrix.md` (the `cka-44` row already maps these leaves correctly).
