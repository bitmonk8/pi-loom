## Problem

The `## Overview` section of `docs/spec_topics/overview-and-orientation.md`
contains two paragraphs — anchored `terminal-outcomes-aggregator` and
`file-extension-grammar` — that have grown into dense architecture-level
prose reciting normative material owned by canonical topic pages: the
closed terminal-outcome classification with its ceiling-#4 per-arm
exclusions, the `loomAbort.signal` forwarding contract, the partial-append
contract, and the `.loom` / `.warp` extension grammar. Unlike every sibling
aggregator on the same page, neither paragraph carries the
`*Orientation aggregator (per GOV-30)*` tag nor a GOV-30 registration, so
they are governed by no aggregator-vs-source lock-step and read as primary
normative text at the spec's most-visited entry point. The result is reader
load at the primary entry point and a dual-authority drift hazard against
the canonical owners.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the `terminal-outcomes-aggregator` and `file-extension-grammar`
paragraphs to conceptual orientation statements that name only the surfaces
— the success / fail / cancelled trichotomy, and the `.loom` / `.warp`
distinction — forward-linking each surface's canonical owner, and apply the
orientation framing the sibling aggregators carry (the GOV-30 tag and
registration). Relocate each normative-shaped clause to its owner page:
the terminal-outcome classification and ceiling-#4 exclusions to
`errors-and-results/error-model.md#terminal-outcomes` and
`hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md`, the `invoke(...)` `params` carve-out to
`invocation.md`, the partial-append clause to `#partial-append-contract`,
the `loomAbort.signal` forwarding to `cancellation.md` and the PIC
`#cancellation-source` anchor, and the `.warp` top-level form enumeration
and diagnostic-routing note to `imports.md`, `discovery/discovery-sources.md`,
and the invocation page.
