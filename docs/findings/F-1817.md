## Finding

The owning `argument-hint` autocomplete-contract paragraph in `docs/spec_topics/frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-a.md` (the `#argument-hint-binder-only` block) closes with the sentence: *"This extension-API gap is tracked in [Future Considerations](../future-considerations.md)."* The normative limitation it gestures at — that Pi's `RegisteredCommand` API has no `argumentHint` slot for extension-registered commands, so `argument-hint` is binder-grounding-only and never reaches the autocomplete dropdown — is already stated in full by the two preceding sentences of the same paragraph. The "is tracked in" phrasing is issue-tracker / project-management language that adds no normative constraint an implementer must satisfy.

The forward-pointer is also factually stale: `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations.md` contains no mention of `argument-hint`, `argumentHint`, or the `RegisteredCommand` slot gap, so the cross-reference does not resolve to any tracking entry. The sentence therefore asserts a tracking relationship that does not exist while contributing nothing to the contract the paragraph owns.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single-commit-introduced
**Introducing commits:** `1e103d5` ("spec: rescope argument-hint to binder grounding only (Decision #7)", 2026-05-05); relocated to its current site by `4da6c6c` ("pi-loom spec: resolve \"argument-hint field contract is defined once and duplicated\"", 2026-06-06)
**History:** `git log -S "gap is tracked in"` shows the phrase first entered the corpus in `1e103d5`, embedded in a slash-command-discovery paragraph as part of Decision #7's rescope of `argument-hint` to binder-grounding-only. When the duplicated `argument-hint` contract was consolidated into its single owning definition under `#argument-hint-binder-only` (`4da6c6c`, confirmed via `git show 4da6c6c -- docs/spec_topics/frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-a.md`), the project-management sentence was carried into the new home verbatim rather than dropped. The defect is thus the original phrasing from `1e103d5` surviving the consolidation; it was never a deliberate normative addition.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Delete the sentence *"This extension-API gap is tracked in [Future Considerations](../future-considerations.md)."* from the `#argument-hint-binder-only` paragraph. The preceding two sentences already state the binder-only limitation and the author workaround in full.

If a forward-pointer is genuinely wanted, replace it with a plain `See [Future Considerations](../future-considerations.md).` — but only after a corresponding entry actually exists there; `future-considerations.md` currently has no `argument-hint`/`argumentHint` entry, so the bare deletion is the safer default and the pointer should not be reintroduced against a non-existent target.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A redundant, factually-stale rationale sentence in a normative paragraph; no observer behaviour changes whether it stays or goes. The risk is purely editorial: a reader who follows the dead forward-pointer finds no tracking entry, eroding trust in the spec's cross-references.
