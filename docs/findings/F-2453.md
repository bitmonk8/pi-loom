## Problem

The `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed` trigger row in `code-registry-load.md` pairs two whole-extension abort consequences: a `pi.registerFlag` failure is fatal "(steps 2–5 do not execute)" — determinate, since `registerFlag` is step 1 — and a `pi.on(...)` subscription failure is "likewise fatal to the whole extension (the remaining steps do not execute)". But `pi.on(...)` is the call used at three distinct registration sites (the step-1 `resources_discover` subscription, the step-3 `session_start` handler, and the step-4 `session_shutdown` handler, per the row's own `details.event` enumeration), so "the remaining steps" is relative to an unstated current step. The adjacent determinate "steps 2–5" range invites a reader to transfer it onto the `pi.on(...)` clause, which is wrong for a step-3 or step-4 subscription failure where earlier steps have already run.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 79ea4e8 — pi-loom spec: resolve "throw from pi.on subscription has no granularity rule" (2026-06-06, spec-plan)
**History:** Before `79ea4e8` the `extension-bootstrap-failed` trigger row covered only `registerMessageRenderer` / `registerCommand` / `registerFlag`, with the single determinate fatal clause "(steps 2–5 do not execute)" for `registerFlag`. `79ea4e8` added the `pi.on(...)` subscription arm (and its `details.event` payload) to close the pi.on granularity gap, but phrased the new arm's consequence as the vague "the remaining steps do not execute" alongside the unchanged determinate `registerFlag` range — introducing the step-relative ambiguity in this row even though the owner page added in the same area carries the precise "every subsequent `pi.register*` / `pi.on` call" rule.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the `pi.on(...)` consequence in the `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed` trigger row to use the step-position-relative phrasing the owner page `pi-integration-contract/extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md` already carries — keyed to the failing subscription rather than a fixed numeric range (e.g. "no subsequent `pi.register*` / `pi.on` call executes after the failing subscription"). Leave the `registerFlag` "(steps 2–5 do not execute)" clause unchanged.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: `pi-integration-contract/extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md` — its `pi.on(...)` abort rule is already correct and is not edited.
