## Finding

`spec_topics/invocation.md` §*Cross-mode semantics* hosts the entire normative description of how the `pi.setActiveTools` snapshot/restore window is generalised from the per-query bracket (PIC-17 step 1–4) to the **whole body** of a prompt → prompt `invoke(...)`. The paragraph at line 66 fixes, in one place: snapshot on entry, `pi.setActiveTools(childCallableSet)` install, `finally`-block restore on `Err` / panic / cancellation, parent-suspends-at-call-site, "at most one prompt-mode body holds an open snapshot/restore window at a time," LIFO-safe-by-construction stack discipline, and the cross-extension `pi.setActiveTools` overwrite rule applied at every nesting level. These are PIC-shaped obligations — they constrain the loom runtime's interaction with the Pi extension API — and they extend a contract whose canonical owner is `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/tool-registration-lifetime.md` (PIC-17, the [`#snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions`](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/tool-registration-lifetime.md) anchor, PIC-8, PIC-19). The PIC page already mentions in passing, in the snapshot/restore-preconditions paragraph, that "two prompt-mode snapshot/restore windows against the same user session can only ever be strictly LIFO-nested," but only as a defensive aside — the per-body generalisation, the install/restore call sequence, and the cross-extension overwrite rule are all sourced from `invocation.md`, not pinned at the PIC home.

A PIC implementer who reads only the PIC pages therefore sees PIC-17 (per-query bracket), PIC-8 (restore-failure protocol), and PIC-19 (snapshot/swap-install-failure protocol) but no obligation telling them to extend the bracket across a child loom body. They have to follow the cross-reference into a core-language page (invocation.md) — a file whose subject matter is the cross-mode invocation matrix and `invoke`-chain depth, not the Pi tool-registration surface — to recover the contract they are required to implement. The placement also fragments the PIC-2 / PIC-17 / "single window" obligation set across three pages (invocation.md, tool-registration-lifetime.md, and subagent.md, where PIC-2 currently lives — see the related finding).

The defect is one of placement, not of normative content: every rule named is real and required. The page-of-record for "loom runtime interactions with `pi.setActiveTools`" should be the PIC tool-lifetime page, with invocation.md retaining at most a matrix cell + one-line cross-reference so a reader of the cross-mode matrix still knows the window exists.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `27ec53d` (2026-05-04, "Resolve Cluster-A: per-mode tool registration"); `e7ba4ba` ("pi.setActiveTools snapshot/restore relies on an unstated Pi serialisation guarantee"); `2a6cc61` (2026-05-07, "Concurrent prompt-mode invocations: isolation claim is unbacked for the prompt-mode arm")
**History:** `git log -L '66,66:spec_topics/invocation.md'` shows the prompt → prompt paragraph was first authored on invocation.md by `27ec53d`, which generalised the per-query snapshot/restore protocol (newly defined on the PIC page in the same commit) "to the child's whole body" with explicit `finally`-block restore prose and the nested-stacking reverse-order rule. `e7ba4ba` subsequently added the cross-extension `pi.setActiveTools` overwrite rule to the same paragraph rather than to PIC-17. `2a6cc61` then added the "at most one prompt-mode body holds an open snapshot/restore window," LIFO-safe-by-construction, and parallel-`invoke`-suspends clauses, plus the `tools:`-load-rejection composition argument — again all on invocation.md, with PIC-2 added separately to subagent.md (mis-placed per the related finding). Each commit added one well-motivated PIC obligation, but none relocated the accumulating obligation set to the PIC home; the defect is the *interaction* of three placements that each looked local at the time. The PIC page's preconditions paragraph eventually grew a defensive "strictly LIFO-nested" aside, but the source of truth for the body-scope contract still sits on the core-language page.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Relocate the body-scope generalisation paragraph from `spec_topics/invocation.md` §*Cross-mode semantics* to `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/tool-registration-lifetime.md`, immediately after PIC-17 / PIC-19, as a new explicitly-anchored rule (e.g. `PIC-17b. Body-scope extension to prompt → prompt invocations`) that:

1. States the snapshot-on-entry / install / `finally`-restore obligation as a generalisation of PIC-17 step 1–4 from the per-query bracket to the entire child body.
2. Names the parent-suspends-at-call-site invariant and the "at most one prompt-mode body holds an open snapshot/restore window" single-window invariant, citing PIC-2 (after PIC-2 itself is relocated per the related finding) as the supporting cross-body rule rather than restating it.
3. Names the LIFO-safe-by-construction stack discipline as a derived property of the suspend rule plus the load-time `tools:` prompt-mode rejection in `frontmatter-fields-a.md`.
4. States the cross-extension `pi.setActiveTools` overwrite rule once at this site, applying to "every per-query bracket and every body-scope bracket alike," subsuming the per-nesting-level restatement currently in invocation.md.

In `spec_topics/invocation.md` §*Cross-mode semantics* keep only:
- The matrix-cell observation that the prompt → prompt cell has a body-scope active-set window.
- A single cross-reference sentence pointing to the new PIC-17b anchor.
- The user-visibility line ("interleaving is invisible to the user other than via tool-call cards in the transcript") if a reader of the matrix benefits from it; this is observable behaviour and is on-topic for invocation.md.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:
- The relocation must preserve the existing inbound link from `V15d-prompt-suspend-snapshot.md` and from the PIC page's own snapshot/restore-preconditions paragraph; both currently point into invocation.md for the LIFO rule. After relocation, the PIC-page aside should be tightened to "see PIC-17b above" rather than repeating the rule.
- The cross-extension overwrite rule currently appears in two places (in the PIC-17 bullet list at "If another extension calls `pi.setActiveTools` between a loom's snapshot and its `finally` restore…" and in the invocation.md paragraph). After consolidation the rule appears once, with the bullet-list version replaced by a back-reference to PIC-17b.
- The related PIC-2 placement finding ("PIC-2 Prompt-mode sequential execution defined in the wrong file per the TOC") should be resolved first or in the same change, since PIC-17b's citation of PIC-2 reads more cleanly when PIC-2 already lives on `tool-registration-lifetime.md`.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A PIC implementer following the page-titled boundary will miss the body-scope generalisation: PIC-17 alone describes a per-query bracket, and nothing in the PIC pages tells the implementer to extend that bracket across the entire prompt → prompt child body or to apply the cross-extension overwrite rule at every nesting level. The obligations are technically reachable via the cross-reference in PIC-17, but the cross-reference is one-way and the PIC reader is steered away from invocation.md by topic. Implementations are not expected to diverge on the rules themselves (they are unambiguous once read), but the placement increases the chance that the body-scope rules are missed during review of the PIC surface.
