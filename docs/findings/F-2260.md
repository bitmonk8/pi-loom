## Finding

`version-bump-step2.md`'s step-2 editorial-review checklist runs items (a) through (aq). The **Outcome recording conventions** block (`#bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions`) sits at the bottom of the step-2 section — after item (aq) and after the "Any future presupposition…" closing paragraph. That block carries three item-specific recording contracts: (1) item (e)'s `N/A` definition (`N/A — superseded by <named Pi mechanism>`), (2) item (e)'s two-sub-outcome ((e.i)/(e.ii)) recording convention, and (3) item (a)'s authoritative-determination recording shape (the `governed-by-rebind` / `provably-differs — <cited evidence>` verdicts, evidentiary standard, and acceptance criterion). Items (a) and (e) are the first and fifth items in the checklist.

A contributor recording outcomes in reading order encounters item (a) — whose recording shape is exceptional (one-shot dispositive verdict, not pass/fail/N/A) — and item (e) — whose rationale slot must be split into two sub-outcomes with a special `N/A` definition — long before reaching the block that defines how those two items are recorded. The general pass/fail/N/A shape is stated in the step-2 preamble at the top, and the conventions block is forward-referenced via anchor links from the preamble and from items (a) and (e), so the contracts are navigable. The defect is one of co-location and reading order, not missing content: the recording shapes for the two earliest exceptional items are positioned downstream of every item they govern, so a contributor who records top-to-bottom must jump to the end and back, and risks revising item-(a)/(e) entries written before the conventions were consulted.

A prefatory "Recording conventions" sub-section in the step-2 preamble — or the conventions block relocated to immediately before the item list — would put the item-specific shapes in hand at the point the contributor first needs them.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add a prefatory **Recording conventions** sub-section in the step-2 preamble, immediately before the item (a)–(aq) list, and relocate the item-specific contracts there: item (e)'s `N/A` definition and two-sub-outcome ((e.i)/(e.ii)) convention, and item (a)'s authoritative-determination recording shape (verdicts, evidentiary standard, acceptance criterion). Keep the general pass/fail/N/A shape statement that already opens the preamble; the relocated block extends it rather than duplicating it. Preserve the `#bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions` anchor and the `(e.i)`/`(e.ii)` sub-anchors at the new location so the existing forward-reference links from the preamble, item (a), and item (e) continue to resolve (verify no inbound links from sibling files break — `session-model-and-appendix.md` and `host-prerequisites.md` cite `#bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions`). Replace the item-(a)/(e) forward-references ("…defined under Outcome recording conventions below") with backward-references now that the block precedes them.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A contributor recording bump outcomes encounters the exceptional item-(a) and item-(e) recording shapes only via forward-reference links to a block positioned after all 43 items; recording top-to-bottom may require revising earlier entries. All content is present and linked, so no audit outcome diverges — the cost is reading-order friction in the bump procedure.
