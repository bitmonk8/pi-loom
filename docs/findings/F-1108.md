## Problem

`H7a` describes its committed permitted-code list as "kept current by a per-Deps-slice provenance obligation" — a phrase used three times (once in `Adds.`, twice in the `Tests.` bullets) and presented as if it names an established, defined obligation. It is defined nowhere: not in `H7a`, not in `conventions.md`'s *REQ-ID discipline* section, and not in `leaf-template.md`. The phrase admits two materially different readings — a standing per-leaf maintenance duty on every `Deps` slice versus a one-time authoring audit at first commit — that disagree on who owns the list's accuracy and what event triggers an update. The plan asserts the list is "kept current" by the obligation but never says by whom or when, so the maintenance mechanism the "best-effort union" wording relies on is not actionable as written.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (of the committed permitted-code-list mechanism)
**Introducing commits:** `052b019` (2026-06-11) "pi-loom plan: resolve \"Real-host smoke pass criterion (e) names a permitted code set with no committed source\""; amplified at `f340c3b` (2026-06-17) "pi-loom plan: resolve \"permitted-code-list cannot-disagree guarantee unenforced\""
**History:** `git log -S "per-Deps-slice provenance obligation" -- docs/plan_topics/H7a-integration-acceptance.md` shows the phrase first entered the leaf in `052b019`, the commit that introduced the committed permitted-code list. At that introduction the text already referred to "the same per-Deps-slice provenance obligation" without ever defining it, and no companion definition was added to `conventions.md` or `leaf-template.md`. `f340c3b` later expanded the surrounding maintenance-posture prose ("manually-maintained, best-effort", "kept current by a per-Deps-slice provenance obligation") in both `Adds.` and the `Tests.` bullets but still added no definition. `grep` over `conventions.md` and `leaf-template.md` at HEAD confirms the term remains undefined corpus-wide; the gap has existed continuously since the mechanism was introduced.

## Solution approach

Define the obligation's owner and trigger explicitly, adopting the standing distributed-maintenance reading. State the rule once in `conventions.md`'s *REQ-ID discipline* section, mirroring the existing *Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance* obligation that already lives there: name the trigger (a `Deps`-member leaf's set of emittable `loom/...` codes changes) and the owner (the leaf making that change, which updates `H7a`'s committed permitted-code list in the same change). Replace the three bare uses of "per-Deps-slice provenance obligation" in `H7a` with a reference to that named rule, and add the establishment half — the `H7a` author enumerates the initial union at first commit from each `Deps` leaf's already-established expected output. Keep the existing statement that only `golden ⊆ permitted` is mechanically gated.
