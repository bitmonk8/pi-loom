## Finding

The `<a id="untyped-query-ok-extraction">` sentence in `conversation-drive.md` states that the extraction runs

> only once the cancellation short-circuit (`loomAbort.signal.aborted`, which synthesises `Err(QueryError { kind: "cancelled" })`) and the `stopReason: "error"` transport short-circuit pinned above have both been passed — the extraction runs downstream of those branches and never reorders or bypasses them.

"X has been passed" is ambiguous when X is a *short-circuit*. The intended reading is "both short-circuits have been navigated past without firing"; an equally available reading is "both [conditions/checks] have been satisfied", i.e. *both fired* — the exact opposite, where the extraction would be running on a session that has already cancelled and errored. The trailing clause ("runs downstream of those branches and never reorders or bypasses them") pins the intent on careful re-reading, but at the level of the load-bearing sentence the wording reads against itself. Because this anchor is the canonical seam consumed by `V9c` (trailing-turn `Ok(string)` extraction) and `V9n` (cancellation-precedence-over-transport-error-and-`Ok(string)` mapping), the sentence's precedence claim should be unambiguous on first reading.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace "have both been passed" with "have both been checked and found not to apply" in the `<a id="untyped-query-ok-extraction"></a>` sentence. The result:

> ... extracted from the user session after `waitForIdle()` resolves and only once the cancellation short-circuit (`loomAbort.signal.aborted`, which synthesises `Err(QueryError { kind: "cancelled" })`) and the `stopReason: "error"` transport short-circuit pinned above have both been checked and found not to apply — the extraction runs downstream of those branches and never reorders or bypasses them.

"Checked and found not to apply" forecloses the both-fired misreading because "found not to apply" makes the negative-condition entry-precondition explicit. The trailing clause stays as-is — it now reinforces rather than contradicts the local reading. Confine the edit to this one sentence; no other site uses the "have both been passed" formulation (verified via `grep -rn "have both been passed\|both been passed" docs/spec_topics docs/spec.md`).

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader who stops at "have both been passed" momentarily inverts the precedence and must back-read the tail clause to reconstruct it. No implementer would ship the inverted ordering — the surrounding sentence and the matching language in `V9c` / `V9n` make the intent unambiguous in aggregate — so this is wording polish on a normative anchor rather than a guidance gap.
