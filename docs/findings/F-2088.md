## Finding

`binder-bypass-and-envelope.md` → *System-prompt structure (normative)*, item 6 (Session-context block) opens with "the prompt MUST contain a Session-context block **framed exactly as follows**", then defines the **opening line** only by its endpoints: it "begins with the literal token `Recent session context` and ends with `:`". The body and the terminating-blank-line framing that follow are pinned to the byte (`\n\n`), but the opening line's *interior* — the text between `Recent session context` and the trailing `:` — is left unconstrained by that begins-with/ends-with definition. The illustrative rendering earlier in the section is `Recent session context (most recent 20 turns / 8000 tokens):`, which adds parenthetical content in exactly that interior region.

The word "exactly" therefore has two defensible scopes. Read narrowly, it pins the three-part *frame* (opening line + body + terminating blank line) and the begins-with/ends-with opening-line constraints, leaving the interior implementation-chosen (so the example's parenthetical is one conforming choice among many). Read broadly, "exactly … as follows" pins the opening line to a fixed byte string, which would make the begins-with/ends-with phrasing redundant and would arguably forbid the very parenthetical the example shows. Nothing in item 6 disambiguates the two readings.

The preamble at the top of the section ("Wording may vary; the listed tokens, line-prefixes, and conditional-presence rules are the contract") and the block-level note that "the exact wording is not part of the contract" both steer toward the narrow reading, but item 6's own self-described "framed exactly" language pulls the other way without resolving the tension at the obligation site where a test author reads it.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** `2fb5783` (2026-06-06, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Binder clarity nits + BNDR-7 system-prompt block framing\"")
**History:** Before `2fb5783`, item 6 read "the prompt MUST contain a delimited block whose opening line begins with the literal token `Recent session context` and whose body is the included transcript …" — no "framed exactly" phrasing, no "ends with `:`", and the opening-line interior was unambiguously loose because only a begins-with constraint existed. Commit `2fb5783` rewrote item 6 to harden the *structural* frame (adding the explicit terminating blank line and the byte-exact `\n\n` terminator) and prefaced the rewrite with "framed exactly as follows". The strong word "exactly" was introduced to pin that newly-added structural framing, but it now reads as if it also pins the opening line's interior, which the same edit left defined only by begins-with/ends-with. The scope ambiguity entered with that single rewrite; no earlier or later commit interacts with it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In item 6, separate the "exact" structural frame from the deliberately-loose opening-line interior. State that the *frame* — opening line, then body, then the terminating blank line ending in `\n\n` — is fixed, and that the opening line MUST begin with the literal token `Recent session context` and end with `:`, while the text between those two endpoints is non-normative (an implementation may include the illustrated `(most recent N turns / M tokens)` parenthetical or any other interior text, or none). Equivalently, replace "framed exactly as follows" with "structured as follows" so "exactly" no longer attaches to the opening line, and add one sentence pinning that only the begins-with token, the trailing `:`, and the body/terminating-blank-line bytes are normative. Either form keeps the byte-exact `\n\n` terminator intact and brings item 6 into line with the section preamble ("Wording may vary; the listed tokens, line-prefixes, and conditional-presence rules are the contract").

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A test author for `V11d-T` could legitimately encode item 6 as a byte-exact opening-line assertion, which would then red-fail a conforming renderer that chose a different (or no) parenthetical interior — even though the section's preamble says wording may vary. The defect does not block implementation (the example plus the preamble convey the likely intent) but leaves the conformance boundary for the opening line under-specified.
