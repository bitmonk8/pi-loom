## Problem

In `host-prerequisites.md`, the bold sub-heading anchored at `id="pi-slash-handler-promise-lifecycle-presupposition"` reads **"Pi-side slash-handler promise lifecycle (consumption posture)."** The "Pi-side" qualifier reads in any heading scan as a claim about Pi's own guarantees, but the body's first sentence states the opposite: the register records them as a loom-side consumption posture rather than Pi-side guarantees, and the six enumerated items (i)–(vi) are explicitly loom presuppositions about Pi's behaviour, not Pi-authored guarantees. The heading inverts exactly the distinction the register exists to draw, inviting mis-citation of the presuppositions as a Pi contract.

**Verdict:** present-since-inception

Later commits carried it forward unchanged: 605a3a7 relocated the paragraph into `host-prerequisites.md`, and the F-1618 region expanded the register from three presuppositions to six without touching the heading.

## Solution approach

Rename the bold heading at `id="pi-slash-handler-promise-lifecycle-presupposition"` so the ownership qualifier names the loom rather than Pi, matching the body's "loom-side consumption posture rather than Pi-side guarantees" framing — e.g. **"Pi slash-handler promise lifecycle (loom consumption posture)."** Only the displayed bold text changes.
