## Problem

The glossary's `caller` entry (`glossary.md`, `id="caller"`) documents two distinct referents under one anchor: the **slash caller** (the user whose slash-command turn dispatched a loom) and the **invoke parent** (the loom or `.warp` `fn` whose `invoke(...)` / `.loom`-callable call spawned the current loom). Neither sense has its own anchor, so every cross-reference resolves to the single combined `#caller` target. Bare "caller" is then used pervasively in normative prose (e.g. `invocation.md`, `error-model.md`, `binder-model-and-context.md`, `subagent.md`, `overview.md`) with the sense left to context, and the entry's disambiguation guidance is SHOULD-only. The two senses cannot co-occur for one invocation, so this is a naming-consistency defect — anchorability and reader/author load — not a behavioural contradiction.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `53f5831` (bare-"caller" prose, present since inception); `d54b426` (central `caller` glossary entry authored as a single combined entry)
**History:** Bare "caller" entered the corpus at `53f5831` (2026-05-03, "spec: rewrite query primitive, system prompts, and schema syntax") and was used in invocation prose for both senses well before any central glossary existed. No `caller` glossary entry existed until `d54b426` (2026-05-07, resolving "Glossary cluster: six independent defects in spec_topics/glossary.md"), which authored the term directly in its present dual-sense form — both referents under one entry, with the disambiguated terms named only inline and gated by a SHOULD. A later commit (`0033898`) added the `<a id="caller">` anchor but did not split the entry. The finding therefore sits at the interaction of pre-existing pervasive bare usage and the `d54b426` decision to document, rather than separate, the two referents.

## Solution approach

Add independently-targetable anchors for the `slash caller` and `invoke parent` senses while keeping the `id="caller"` entry as the disambiguation hub that states the no-co-occurrence rule. Replace bare `caller` with the disambiguated term in normative prose at cross-cutting sites where the local page context does not already fix the sense, reusing the `slash caller` / `invoke parent` terms already present in `slash-invocation.md` and the `error-model.md` per-cause table. Where a page uses `caller` uniformly in one sense (e.g. `invocation.md`'s "Caller mode" matrix, which means the invoke parent throughout), a page-local scoping note can substitute for site-by-site replacement.

## Solution constraints

- For nested invokes, the slash caller attaches to the root of the invoke chain, not the immediate invoke parent — preserve this when disambiguating "caller" at such sites.
