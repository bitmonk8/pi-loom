## Finding

The `terminal-outcomes-aggregator` paragraph in `docs/spec_topics/overview-and-orientation.md` defines the Failure outcome as

> it fails (by returning `Err`, by panicking, or via a [query-terminating] enforcement point of one of the runtime-class [hard ceilings] below — query-terminating points route via panic or `Err`, …)

This sentence simultaneously presents three coordinate sub-causes (`Err` return, panic, query-terminating ceiling enforcement) and then collapses the third into "routes via" the first two. A reader cannot tell whether the spec recognises three Failure-cause categories that share two routing mechanisms, or only two Failure categories (`Err` and panic) with ceilings being a *source* of those two and not a category in its own right. The same wording appears in the canonical `Terminal outcomes` definition at `docs/spec_topics/errors-and-results/error-model.md` (line 55), so the ambiguity is mirrored on the owner page.

The downstream surface map at `error-model.md` is organised "per-cause", and the `query-terminating` glossary entry resolves each ceiling-#4 enforcement point to one of the two mechanisms (`Err` or panic) before it reaches the trichotomy. So the underlying semantics are well-defined — three causes do collapse to two routing mechanisms at the trichotomy boundary — but the aggregator sentence does not say that. It reads as if the third cause is both a coordinate item and a derivative one. The "Failure-cause enumeration" exclusion list that immediately follows (cases (a) and (b) "excluded from the Failure-cause enumeration") presupposes that *there is* a Failure-cause enumeration with ceiling-breach in it, which only deepens the conflict for a reader trying to decide whether ceilings are causes or routes.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Restructure the Failure parenthetical in the `terminal-outcomes-aggregator` paragraph so that *causes* and *routing mechanisms* are named as distinct axes rather than as a flat coordinate list. Concretely:

- Open the parenthetical by naming the three distinct **causes** of a Failure outcome explicitly — (1) the loom body returning `Err`, (2) the loom body (or a callee surfaced through `?`) panicking per the closed runtime-panic list, and (3) breach of a runtime-class hard ceiling at a query-terminating enforcement point.
- Then add one explicit sentence that says all three causes resolve to one of **two** terminal-outcome routing mechanisms at the trichotomy boundary — panic-class or `Err`-class — and forward-link the `query-terminating` glossary entry as the per-arm resolution authority and the ceiling-#4 per-boundary table as the per-arm classification authority for ceiling #4 specifically.
- Close with a one-clause statement that the three causes therefore produce the same terminal-outcome categories — and the same per-surface mappings owned by `error-model.md` — regardless of which cause produced them.
- Rename the immediately-following exclusion clause from "excluded from the Failure-cause enumeration" to "excluded from the ceiling-breach Failure cause" (or equivalent), so the exclusion list is scoped to cause (3) and does not appear to amend a flat "Failure-cause enumeration" that the rewrite has just retired.
- Apply the matching rewrite to the canonical `Terminal outcomes` Failure bullet at `docs/spec_topics/errors-and-results/error-model.md`, since the two paragraphs carry word-for-word equivalent wording on this point — co-edit per GOV-30 lock-step.

Implementer-relevant edge cases:

- Preserve the existing forward links to `query-terminating`, `hard-runtime-ceilings`, the ceiling-#4 per-boundary table, and the `error-model.md#terminal-outcomes` page; none of those targets move under this fix.
- Do not change the per-cause caller-surface map owned by `error-model.md` — only the cause/mechanism wording in the aggregator's defining sentence and its mirror.
- Ceiling #3 (load-time binder per-class retry budget) remains *not* a Failure cause; that exclusion is already correct on both pages and must survive the rewrite.
- The bimodal-handled / unhandled `Err`-class refinement in the `query-terminating` glossary entry (panic-class breaches unconditionally reach *fail*; `Err`-class breaches reach *fail* only when unhandled) is the right place for that nuance — the aggregator should forward to it, not restate it.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer or external reader trying to enumerate the Failure-outcome categories — for example to write a per-cause test matrix, a per-cause user-surface table, or a `match` exhaustiveness audit — will get a different answer depending on whether they read the third sub-cause as a coordinate category or as a routing source. The semantics are recoverable from the owner page and the `query-terminating` glossary entry, so divergence is unlikely to produce incorrect runtime behaviour, but it will produce divergent prose, divergent diagrams, and unnecessary clarification questions back to the spec.
