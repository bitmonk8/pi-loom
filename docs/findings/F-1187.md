## Problem

The `terminal-outcomes-aggregator` paragraph in `docs/spec_topics/overview-and-orientation.md` defines the Failure outcome as failing "by returning `Err`, by panicking, or via a query-terminating enforcement point of one of the runtime-class hard ceilings below — query-terminating points route via panic or `Err`". The sentence presents three coordinate sub-causes and then collapses the third into the first two routing mechanisms, so a reader cannot tell whether ceiling breach is a third Failure-cause category in its own right or merely a source that routes into the `Err`/panic categories. The immediately-following exclusion clause ("excluded from the Failure-cause enumeration") presupposes a Failure-cause enumeration that includes ceiling breach, deepening the conflict. The same wording is mirrored in the canonical `Terminal outcomes` Failure bullet at `docs/spec_topics/errors-and-results/error-model.md`.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** a5ad529 — pi-loom spec: resolve "Orientation conflates cancellation with a separate failure mode" (2026-05-06, Thomas Andersen); 39045df — pi-loom spec: resolve "Ceiling #3 binder LLM-call cap misclassification" (2026-05-07, Thomas Andersen); 8168ea8 — pi-loom spec: resolve "T30 ceiling #4 model-driven row trichotomy carve-out" (2026-05-10, Thomas Andersen); 81bf52a — docs: collapse spec/ section pages into spec_topics/ (2026-06-11, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** `a5ad529` first introduced the three-outcome trichotomy with Failure parenthesised as "returning `Err`, panicking, or being cancelled" — at that point ceilings were not enumerated and the ambiguity did not yet exist. `39045df` then added "by exhausting one of the runtime-class hard ceilings below" as a third Failure sub-cause coordinate with `Err` and panic. `8168ea8` (the T30 ceiling-#4 carve-out) reframed that third sub-cause as "via a query-terminating enforcement point" and appended the inline gloss "query-terminating points route via panic or `Err`" — the exact wording that puts a coordinate sub-cause and its routing-into-the-other-two on the same sentence and creates the cause-vs-mechanism conflation. `81bf52a` later relocated the paragraph from `docs/spec.md` to `docs/spec_topics/overview-and-orientation.md` without altering its content, carrying the defect to its present location. The defect is therefore the cumulative shape that `39045df` and `8168ea8` together produced, not the work of any single commit.

## Solution approach

Restructure the Failure parenthetical in the `terminal-outcomes-aggregator` paragraph so the *causes* of a Failure outcome and the *routing mechanisms* they resolve to are named as distinct axes rather than a flat coordinate list, forward-linking the `query-terminating` glossary entry and the ceiling-#4 per-boundary table as the per-arm resolution authorities. Rename the immediately-following exclusion clause so it scopes to the ceiling-breach cause rather than amending a flat "Failure-cause enumeration". Apply the matching rewrite to the canonical `Terminal outcomes` Failure bullet at `error-model.md#terminal-outcomes`, which carries word-for-word equivalent wording, co-editing both in lock-step per GOV-30.

- Out of scope: the per-cause caller-surface map owned by `error-model.md#terminal-outcomes` — edit only the cause/mechanism wording in the aggregator's defining sentence and its mirror, not the surface map.
