## Problem

In sub-step 1 of the `session_shutdown` handler (`session-shutdown-semantics.md`), the `display: true` parenthetical of the slash-handler short-circuit note states that the setting "is fixed by this umbrella opener". The phrase "umbrella opener" is coined nowhere in the corpus and has no grammatical antecedent, so a reader cannot tell which textual unit it names. The `display: true` value itself is recoverable from the adjacent "applies uniformly to both arms below" clause; the defect is the dangling self-reference, which leaves an auditor unable to locate where the setting is established.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 52b9101 — pi-loom spec: resolve "T24 — Fork-reason watcher closure leaves the extension in an unspecified, silently degraded state" (2026-05-11, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The two-arm drain-state slash-note prose for sub-step 1, including the phrase "the `display` setting is fixed by this umbrella opener", was authored verbatim in 52b9101 into `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md`; the term was undefined from the moment it was coined. The prose later moved through the spec size-cap split (f5e89f4, 2026-06-04) and was finally relocated unchanged into `session-shutdown-semantics.md` in c3de47a (2026-06-27, F-1602); neither relocation introduced or altered the defect.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the `display: true` parenthetical in sub-step 1's slash-handler short-circuit note to replace "this umbrella opener" with a concrete locational referent naming the textual unit that establishes the setting. Use a plain locational phrase rather than a new coined term.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the closed two-arm drain-state behaviour (`substep-1-shutting-down-arm` / `substep-1-degraded-needs-reload-arm`) and the `content` templates — only the referent for `display: true` changes.
