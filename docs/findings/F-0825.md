## Finding

The first paragraph of item (e) closes with the sentence "any additional `.js` file matching the glob in the candidate minor that contains a `session.prompt(...)` call reachable from its entry point is audit-positive evidence of a new driver mode the cooperative-await argument under precondition (b) has not been load-tested against." The phrase "audit-positive evidence" is not defined anywhere in the page, appears nowhere else in `version-bump-step2.md`, and the sentence does not say what the auditor MUST do when it holds.

Its operational consequence — a `fail` outcome — is the third bullet of the fail-trigger list that opens the next paragraph ("Any newly-discovered `dist/modes/*/*.js` file with a reachable `session.prompt(...)` call site … appears in the candidate minor"). An auditor who reaches the end of the first paragraph carrying the term has no anchor pointing forward to that bullet; they must read on, identify the bullet as the matching trigger, and themselves connect the two phrasings. Because the term is bespoke and the link is left implicit across the paragraph break, two auditors can reasonably disagree on whether the newly-discovered driver file by itself is enough to record `fail` or whether some additional cooperative-await regression must accompany it.

The split improved scannability of the fail conditions but left the originating sentence terminal and unlinked, severing the textual adjacency that previously carried the consequence.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In item (e)'s first paragraph, replace the trailing clause "is audit-positive evidence of a new driver mode the cooperative-await argument under precondition (b) has not been load-tested against" with prose that names the consequence at the sentence and routes the auditor to the fail-trigger bullet — e.g. "is a newly-discovered driver mode the cooperative-await argument under precondition (b) has not been load-tested against, and independently triggers a `fail` outcome under the third bullet of the **fail** trigger list that opens the next paragraph." Retain the third fail-trigger bullet unchanged (the explicit link from the originating sentence is the fix; the bullet is the canonical statement of the trigger and stays the load-bearing one). If `tool-registration-lifetime.md`'s parallel "audit-positive evidence of a driver mode beyond …" phrasing is touched in the same edit, mirror the rename to "newly-discovered driver mode" there for term consistency; otherwise leave it, since that file is descriptive and does not own the trigger semantics.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A determined auditor reading both paragraphs will reach the right verdict; the third fail-trigger bullet is unambiguous on its own. The risk is that the first-paragraph sentence reads as advisory ("evidence of …") rather than as the trigger for a specific recorded outcome, encouraging an auditor who stops at paragraph one — or who treats the cooperative-await diff as the only `fail` axis — to record `pass` on a Pi minor that ships a third driver mode without a regression in the cited two.
