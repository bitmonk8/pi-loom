## Problem

Item (e)'s first paragraph closes with a sentence stating that any additional `.js` file matching the `dist/modes/*/*.js` glob with a reachable `session.prompt(...)` call is "audit-positive evidence of a new driver mode the cooperative-await argument under precondition (b) has not been load-tested against." The term "audit-positive evidence" is bespoke, is defined nowhere on the page, and the sentence does not state what the auditor MUST do when the condition holds. The operational consequence — a `fail` outcome — lives in the third bullet of the fail-trigger list that opens the next paragraph, but nothing links the originating sentence to that bullet. Two auditors can therefore reasonably disagree on whether the newly-discovered driver file alone records `fail` or whether some additional cooperative-await regression must accompany it.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** 57d8235 — pi-loom spec: resolve "T23 per-session slash-handler serialisation citation" (2026-05-11, Thomas Andersen); 5d73cc3 — pi-loom spec: resolve "Item (e) fail predicate operator-precedence ambiguity and buried sub-outcomes" (2026-06-07, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** Commit 57d8235 introduced item (e) and coined "audit-positive evidence of a new driver mode," inline in a single long-paragraph form where the fail-trigger prose ran on contiguously after that clause via semicolons. Commit 5d73cc3 split that mega-paragraph into a sentence ending at "load-tested against." followed by a separate "**fail** outcome is recorded when **any** …" paragraph and a bulleted trigger list (the third bullet covering the newly-discovered-driver case). The split improved scannability of the fail conditions but left the originating sentence terminal and unlinked, severing the textual adjacency that previously carried the consequence.

## Solution approach

Rewrite item (e)'s first-paragraph trailing clause so the sentence names the `fail` consequence rather than leaving the bespoke term "audit-positive evidence" standing alone, and forward-link the auditor to the third bullet of the fail-trigger list that opens the next paragraph.

## Solution constraints

- The third bullet of the fail-trigger list remains the canonical statement of the newly-discovered-driver trigger; do not relocate the trigger semantics into the first paragraph.
- Out of scope: `tool-registration-lifetime.md`'s parallel "audit-positive evidence" phrasing — mirror any rename there only if that file is already edited in the same change; do not author a standalone edit to it.
