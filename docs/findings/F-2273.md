## Problem

The only statement of the callable-set term-usage mandate (`MUST use the term `callable set`` and avoid the eroding synonyms) lives in the `callable set` entry of `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md`. That page's preamble declares every entry descriptive and cedes normative authority to the canonical `See:` target. A MUST lodged on a self-declared authority-ceding page is structurally weak: a reader who follows the entry to the canonical `tools` prose at `docs/spec_topics/frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-a.md#tools` finds the no-inheritance invariant the mandate protects, but no rule barring the synonyms that erode it.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** fec0870 — pi-loom spec: resolve "callable-set prose synonyms erode no-inheritance invariant" (2026-05-06, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The `callable set` glossary entry predates this commit, but commit `fec0870` resolved a prior review finding ("callable-set prose synonyms erode no-inheritance invariant") by appending the `MUST use the term \`callable set\`` mandate directly into the descriptive glossary entry. That commit touched the glossary, `tool-calls.md`, `frontmatter.md`, and others, but never added the term-usage MUST to the canonical `tools` prose — so the mandate has lived only on the self-declared descriptive page since it was introduced. (Paths later shifted: `81bf52a` collapsed `spec/` into `spec_topics/` and the frontmatter page was subsequently split into `frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-a.md`, but the misplacement is unchanged.)

## Solution approach

Move the term-usage mandate onto the canonical `tools` prose at `docs/spec_topics/frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-a.md#tools`, alongside the no-inheritance invariant it protects, so the canonical owner carries the rule. Demote the `callable set` entry in `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` to its declared descriptive role: keep the definition and `See:` pointer and drop the MUST.

## Solution constraints

- The mandate lands once, on the `#tools` canonical owner; do not duplicate it onto `tool-calls.md`, which already links to that surface.
