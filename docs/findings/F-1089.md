## Problem

`V18c` cites the contributor editorial-review checklist as items `(a)`–`(al)` (and the SHOULD-level tail as `(f)`–`(al)`) in four places: the two range citations in the **Adds** field and the two in the **Ships when** field. The cited authority, `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md`, now enumerates the checklist through `(an)` — its preamble audits `(f)` through `(an)` and it defines `(am)` and `(an)`. Because `V18c`'s **Ships when** acceptance check only requires recorded outcomes for `(a)`–`(al)`, items `(am)` and `(an)` fall outside the gate, even though the spec marks omitting any `(f)`–`(an)` row as non-conformant. The leaf's acceptance condition is therefore narrower than the obligation it claims to gate.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** 077afb5 — pi-loom spec-plan: fix F-0806 — tools-allowlist suppression pinned to new bump checklist item (am) (2026-06-20, Thomas Andersen); 2123dc9 — pi-loom spec-plan: fix F-0560 — surface V14b parallel-tool-mode presupposition via new bump checklist item (an) (2026-06-21, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** Commit 1ca13ec (2026-06-19) added spec item `(al)` and re-synced `V18c`'s range citations from `(ak)` to `(al)` in the same edit, so the leaf was consistent at that point. Commit 077afb5 then appended spec item `(am)` and commit 2123dc9 appended `(an)`, each extending `version-bump-step2.md` without propagating the new tail bound into `V18c` — leaving the leaf frozen at `(al)`. The defect is the accumulated cross-document drift from those two later spec-only extensions.

## Solution approach

Re-sync the four range citations in `V18c` (two in **Adds**, two in **Ships when**) so the SHOULD-level tail's upper bound covers the spec's current tail through `(an)` rather than freezing at `(al)`. Keep the MUST boundary `(a)`–`(e)` literal. Consider expressing the tail's upper bound symbolically — as the final item `version-bump-step2.md` enumerates — so a future spec item is covered automatically without re-chasing the bound. Move all four citations together; leaving any one at `(al)` reproduces the gate gap on that field.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: `version-bump-step2.md` and the rest of the spec are read-only for this fix; the spec already carries its own same-edit propagation rule.
