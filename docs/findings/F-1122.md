## Finding

The [*Category-(1)/(3) inventory join key (per-category specialisation)*](../../docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-resolution.md#audit-category-1-3-inventory-join-key) paragraph defines the lookup for `pi.<member>` and `ctx.<member>` accesses as an **exact match between `<member>` and the rightmost dot-separated segment of an entry's `path` field** — and that rule is the only one written. It never restricts the resolver's candidate set to entries of the matching category (kind). A category-(1) `pi.modelRegistry(...)` access therefore matches an `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` entry whose `path` is `"ExtensionContext.modelRegistry"` (a category-(3) entry), and a category-(3) `ctx.registerCommand(...)` access matches an entry with `path: "pi.registerCommand"` (a category-(1) entry). The rule is carrier-agnostic by omission.

The page does try to keep the world consistent through a side-channel: the [duplicate-`path`-segment uniqueness rule](../../docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-resolution.md#audit-category-2-inventory-join-key) under *Category-(2) inventory join key* extends symmetrically to "category-(1) and category-(3) audit-participating kinds … on the rightmost dot-separated segment" and pins the `literal-read/duplicate-rightmost-segment` discriminator. Read together with the under-specified resolver, that uniqueness rule is doing implicit work: it has to be an **inter-category** uniqueness invariant (`pi.modelRegistry` and `ExtensionContext.modelRegistry` may not coexist) rather than the natural **intra-category** one, otherwise resolution is non-deterministic. Whether this inter-category reading is intended — or whether the resolver is supposed to be kind-scoped and the uniqueness rule is intra-category — is exactly what the resolution paragraph leaves unsaid.

The consequence is that two compliant implementations diverge: one filters candidates by `kind`/category before applying rightmost-segment matching (and accepts side-by-side `pi.modelRegistry` and `ctx.modelRegistry` entries), the other does not (and rejects them as a duplicate-rightmost-segment collision). Both readings can be supported by the present text.

That commit introduced the per-category specialisation block to `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` in a single edit; the rule was written carrier-agnostic from inception (matching only on `<member>` against rightmost segment, with no category/`kind` filter). Subsequent edits — `81bf52a` (collapse of `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` into the per-page split under `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/`) and `23d28a1` (basename realignment) — relocated the prose without altering its shape. The companion duplicate-rightmost-segment uniqueness clause was introduced in the same `e5cd514` commit, so the under-scoping of the resolver and the implicit reliance on a global uniqueness rule entered the corpus together.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Scope resolution to entries of the matching category (`kind`-filtered)

**Approach.** Require the resolver to filter `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` to entries whose `kind` discriminator marks them as a category-(1) `pi.<member>` entry kind (for a `pi.<member>` access) or as a category-(3) `ctx.<member>` entry kind (for a `ctx.<member>` access) **before** applying the rightmost-segment match. The duplicate-rightmost-segment uniqueness rule is correspondingly read as **intra-category** (no two category-(1) entries may share a rightmost segment; no two category-(3) entries may share one), and sibling `pi.modelRegistry` and `ctx.modelRegistry` entries are explicitly legal.

**Spec edits.**
- Add a sentence to *Category-(1)/(3) inventory join key (per-category specialisation)* stating that resolution is restricted to inventory entries whose `kind` is the category-(1) (for `pi.<member>` accesses) or category-(3) (for `ctx.<member>` accesses) entry kind coined per *Target surface categories* above; entries of any other `kind` MUST NOT be candidates.
- Rewrite the symmetric duplicate-rightmost-segment sentence under *Category-(2) inventory join key* to scope the uniqueness rule per category (so `literal-read/duplicate-rightmost-segment` fires only when two entries of the *same* audit-participating category share a rightmost segment), and explicitly note that a category-(1) entry and a category-(3) entry with the same rightmost segment do not collide.

**Pros.** Aligns the resolver with the way the carrier is identified syntactically (every access already arrives tagged with its category); permits the natural inventory shape where `pi.<member>` and `ctx.<member>` surfaces coincidentally share a member name; the `kind` discriminator already exists per the minimum-entry-shape obligation in `audit-target-categories.md`, so no new field is introduced.

**Cons.** Slightly more resolver bookkeeping — the implementation must keep the entry's category alongside its rightmost-segment index. The `kind` enumeration on `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` becomes a load-bearing identifier of category membership and any future entry kind that participates in inventory-closure resolution must declare which category it lives in.

**Risks.** Under-specifying which `kind` discriminator strings count as "category-(1)" vs. "category-(3)" would re-introduce the same kind of ambiguity at the implementation seam. The spec edit must either enumerate the discriminator strings (or anchor-cite the implementation-owned enumeration that does) so the resolver's filter is unambiguous.

### Option B — Declare the resolver carrier-agnostic; pin uniqueness as a global invariant

**Approach.** State that rightmost-segment matching is intentionally carrier-agnostic across categories (1) and (3), and that the duplicate-rightmost-segment uniqueness rule is therefore a **global** invariant across the union of category-(1) and category-(3) audit-participating entries — i.e., no two such entries may share a rightmost segment, regardless of category. The implicit reading the spec already half-pins becomes the explicit reading.

**Spec edits.**
- Add a sentence to *Category-(1)/(3) inventory join key* explicitly noting that resolution is carrier-agnostic across categories (1) and (3) and that the cross-category uniqueness rule below is what makes this safe.
- Rewrite the duplicate-rightmost-segment sentence to state explicitly that the uniqueness rule spans the union of category-(1) and category-(3) audit-participating entries (so a `pi.modelRegistry` entry and an `ExtensionContext.modelRegistry` entry collide and fire `literal-read/duplicate-rightmost-segment`).

**Pros.** Smaller edit; preserves the resolver's existing one-line shape. Matches what the duplicate-rightmost-segment clause already implies.

**Cons.** Forces the inventory to never carry both a `pi.<member>` entry and a `ctx.<member>` entry whose final segments coincide, even though they describe distinct surfaces. This constrains future SDK growth (e.g. if Pi introduces a new `ExtensionContext.registerCommand` distinct from `pi.registerCommand`, the inventory cannot represent both). It also reads against the per-category structure the spec just introduced.

**Risks.** A future Pi minor that adds a colliding `ctx.<member>` for an existing `pi.<member>` surface (or vice versa) would have no in-spec way to be represented and would require either renaming an inventory entry's `path` (deviating from the natural `pi.<member>` / `<TypeName>.<member>` convention pinned for the two kinds) or a spec edit to switch to Option A.

### Recommendation

Adopt **Option A** (kind-scoped resolution). The carrier category is already syntactically available at every call site (the `pi` parameter carries `ExtensionAPI`, the `ctx` parameter carries `ExtensionContext` / `ExtensionCommandContext`), and the `kind` discriminator is already mandatory on every audit-participating entry per the minimum-entry-shape obligation in `audit-target-categories.md`. Routing resolution through the existing category metadata is the principled fix; it both matches how the audit identifies the carrier and leaves the inventory free to carry incidentally-name-sharing surfaces from the two categories. Implementer edge cases to watch: the resolver must compute the candidate set by `kind` *before* applying the rightmost-segment match (so a `pi.X` access against an inventory containing only `ExtensionContext.X` correctly fires the family-(1) off-inventory discriminator rather than silently resolving), and the `literal-read/duplicate-rightmost-segment` assertion must partition entries by category before checking for collisions.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable implementers produce different audit verdicts for the same source tree: under the kind-scoped reading, `pi.modelRegistry` correctly fires as off-inventory when only an `ExtensionContext.modelRegistry` entry exists; under the carrier-agnostic reading, it resolves silently. Either implementation also disagrees on whether the inventory may carry sibling `pi.<member>` and `ctx.<member>` entries with the same trailing segment without firing `literal-read/duplicate-rightmost-segment`. Surface-set closure — the audit's central contract — is implementation-defined where it should be pinned.
