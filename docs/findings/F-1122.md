## Problem

The `id="audit-category-1-3-inventory-join-key"` resolver matches a `pi.<member>` or `ctx.<member>` access to any `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` entry whose rightmost `path` segment equals `<member>`, without restricting the candidate set to entries of the matching category — so a category-(1) `pi.modelRegistry` access can resolve against a category-(3) `ExtensionContext.modelRegistry` entry, and vice versa. The companion duplicate-rightmost-segment uniqueness clause under `id="audit-category-2-inventory-join-key"` is left ambiguous between an inter-category reading (in which `pi.modelRegistry` and `ctx.modelRegistry` collide) and an intra-category one. Two compliant implementations therefore diverge on both resolution verdicts and on whether sibling `pi.<member>`/`ctx.<member>` entries may coexist, leaving surface-set closure implementation-defined.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commit:** `e5cd514` (2026-05-09) — `pi-loom spec: resolve "T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit"`
**History:** `git log -G"per-category specialisation" -- docs/spec_topics/` shows commit `e5cd514` as the first appearance of the `audit-category-1-3-inventory-join-key` anchor and the rightmost-segment rule it pins. That commit introduced the per-category specialisation block to `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` in a single edit; the rule was written carrier-agnostic from inception (matching only on `<member>` against rightmost segment, with no category/`kind` filter). Subsequent edits — `81bf52a` (collapse of `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` into the per-page split under `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/`) and `23d28a1` (basename realignment) — relocated the prose without altering its shape. The companion duplicate-rightmost-segment uniqueness clause was introduced in the same `e5cd514` commit, so the under-scoping of the resolver and the implicit reliance on a global uniqueness rule entered the corpus together.

## Solution approach

Narrow the `id="audit-category-1-3-inventory-join-key"` resolver so it filters `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` candidates to the entry kind matching the access category — category-(1) for `pi.<member>`, category-(3) for `ctx.<member>` — before applying the rightmost-segment match, grounding the `kind` discriminators against *Target surface categories*. Rewrite the symmetric duplicate-rightmost-segment uniqueness clause under `id="audit-category-2-inventory-join-key"` so `literal-read/duplicate-rightmost-segment` partitions entries by category, making sibling `pi.<member>` and `ctx.<member>` entries that share a rightmost segment legal.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the category-(2) leftmost-segment resolution rule and its `literal-read/duplicate-leftmost-segment` uniqueness semantics under `id="audit-category-2-inventory-join-key"` — only the symmetric rightmost-segment clause is edited.
