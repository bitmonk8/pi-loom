## Finding

The category-(3) carrier description in `audit-target-categories.md` (the *Target surface categories* bullet, `#audit-target-surface-categories`) embeds a hard-coded snapshot of `ExtensionContext` members: *"Illustrative members at the loom 1.0 pin include `waitForIdle`, `cwd`, `ui`, `model`, `modelRegistry`, `isIdle`, `getContextUsage`, `hasPendingMessages`, `compact`, `shutdown`, `getSystemPrompt`, `sessionManager`, and `signal`; this listing is illustrative only and the audit's category-(3) closed set MUST NOT be seeded from it."*

The sentence immediately preceding it already states the binding rule: the `@earendil-works/pi-coding-agent` `.d.ts` declarations at the loom 1.0 SDK pin are the single source of truth for category (3)'s closed in-scope set, and the implementation's `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` category-(3) entries MUST be derived from those declarations "rather than from any prose enumeration on this page." The illustrative listing therefore adds no normative force â€” it is explicitly disclaimed by its own trailing clause and contradicted-by-design by the derive-from-SDK rule. Its only effect is to plant a member-name snapshot that drifts the moment a Pi minor adds, removes, or renames a context member, after which the spec carries a list the spec itself forbids anyone to act on.

A defensive disclaimer ("MUST NOT be seeded from it") attached to content that exists only to be disclaimed is a maintenance liability: it is a snapshot the page must keep accurate purely to avoid misleading readers, with no compensating normative benefit.

### Recommendation

Delete the entire illustrative-listing sentence â€” from "Illustrative members at the loom 1.0 pin include `waitForIdle`, â€¦" through "â€¦ and the audit's category-(3) closed set MUST NOT be seeded from it." That sentence carries the entire normative contract on its own; no replacement illustrative text is needed.
