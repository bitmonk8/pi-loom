## Problem

`H4b` defines a single shared response-programming surface spanning seven injection categories (a)–(g) and gates all seven behind one `Ships when`. Categories (a)–(e) (scripted assistant turns/fragments, `tool_use` results, binder response/failure, `tool_loop.max_rounds` round-exhaustion, abort/cancellation) are designable directly from contracts that exist when `H4b` lands and are the categories most downstream leaves consume. Categories (f) and (g) are different in kind: they model the `V4f` ERR-13 completed-invoke-child and `V9i` subagent-mode contracts and carry modeled-behaviour authoring complexity consumed only by the ERR-13 / subagent vectors. Because the single `Ships when` covers (a)–(g), the core surface ~9 downstream leaves depend on cannot be marked complete until the harder (f)/(g) modeling is also done.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commit:** `fd6169b` (2026-06-12, "pi-loom plan: resolve 'H4a bundles three independent units into one leaf'")
**History:** `git log --diff-filter=A --follow` shows `H4b-response-programming-surface.md` was created at `fd6169b` when the response-programming surface was carved out of `H4a`. `git log -S'completed-invoke-child outcome'` and `-S'subagent-loom outcome'` against the file both return only `fd6169b`, and `git show fd6169b:…` confirms both the (f) completed-invoke-child and (g) subagent-loom categories were present in the leaf's first revision alongside (a)–(e). The bundle has therefore existed for the entire lifetime of the `H4b` leaf; later edits (`eaa5000`, `098ec80`) touched neighbouring vectors but neither introduced nor removed the (f)/(g) bundling.

## Solution approach

Split the response-programming surface so the core categories ship independently of the modeled-behaviour categories: narrow `H4b`'s Adds, Tests, and `Ships when` to categories (a)–(e). Add a new follow-on horizontal harness leaf (`Deps: H4b`) carrying the (f) completed-invoke-child and (g) subagent-loom clauses, their functional-effect assertions, and their `Ships when` coverage, and link it into `docs/plan.md`'s §Horizontal phases leaf list after `H4b`. Retarget the `H4b` dependency edge to the new leaf on the consumers whose vectors cite (f)/(g) — `V4f`, `V4c`, `V9i`, and `V9o` with their `-T` pairs — and leave the (a)–(e)-only consumers pointing at `H4b`.

## Solution constraints

- The new follow-on leaf must depend only on `H4b` and must not gain a `Deps` edge on `V4f`/`V9i`; those slices consume the harness surface, so a reverse edge would create a dependency cycle.
- The new harness leaf is not coverage-producing — add no `coverage-matrix.md` row and no `H5b` `Deps` update for it.
- Allocate the new leaf's ID per the `## Plan rules` `H<n><letter>` scheme (next free letter in the H4 phase); do not reuse a retired ID.
