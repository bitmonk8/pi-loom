## Problem

Rule 1 under **Conversation drive — subagent mode** in `subagent.md` leaves the built-in-tool lowering step open-ended: it exemplifies only `read` → `createReadToolDefinition(cwd)` and `bash` → `createBashToolDefinition(cwd)` and pins the factory family at the wildcard `dist/core/tools/*.d.ts`, without enumerating the closed set of built-in tool names, the name→factory mapping for the five unexemplified names (`edit`, `write`, `grep`, `find`, `ls`), or the per-factory call signature. A reasonable implementer infers a uniform `cwd`-only call across the whole set; that inference is wrong-by-construction if any factory requires additional arguments, and the divergence surfaces only at spawn through `loom/runtime/internal-error` rather than at load. The unpinning is editorial — the package already exports a `ToolName` union and a unified `createToolDefinition(toolName, cwd, options?)` dispatcher that closes the mapping in one named API.

## Solution approach

In rule 1 under **Conversation drive — subagent mode**, rewrite the per-factory "e.g." exemplar fragment to specify the lowering step in terms of the unified `createToolDefinition` dispatcher keyed on the `ToolName` string-literal union, naming the closed admissible-name set (`"read" | "bash" | "edit" | "write" | "grep" | "find" | "ls"` at the loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin) and the declaration site `dist/core/tools/index.d.ts` re-exported from `dist/index.d.ts`. Pin `createToolDefinition` and `ToolName` to the version-bump re-validation procedure (`#pi-version-bump-procedure`) on the same footing as the existing factory-family pin.
