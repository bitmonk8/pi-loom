## Finding

The PIC corpus carries four independent naming defects that each cost a reader a small navigation step or a moment of disambiguation:

1. **`audit-target-categories.md` H1â†”filename mismatch.** The file's H1 is `# Audit recognised shapes` and its body owns the *Recognised import/access shapes* paragraph; the file named `audit-recognised-shapes.md` symmetrically carries `# Audit target categories` and the *Target surface categories* paragraph. The two files' filenames are swapped relative to their headings/content/anchors. A maintainer who navigates by filename lands on the wrong page; the `[Target surface categories](./audit-recognised-shapes.md#audit-target-surface-categories)` self-link inside `audit-target-categories.md`'s prose visibly points off-page (correctly resolved, but at the cost of every reader noticing the inversion).

2. **`version-bump-triggers.md` H1â†”filename mismatch.** The file's H1 is `# Version bump steps 5â€“7`, while its filename promises "triggers". 3. **`bump-step-2-positive` / `bump-step-2-negative` anchors use opaque "positive" / "negative" tokens.** The anchor slugs on `version-bump-step2.md`'s sub-step 2 use "positive" / "negative" (a description of what the assertions check â€” positive-direction literal-read vs. negative-direction inventory-closure audit) where the prose throughout the corpus consistently refers to them as "step 2(a)" / "step 2(b)". The two siblings use the full word.

None of the four defects affects runtime behaviour or implementer correctness; each is a small, persistent navigation or readability tax across consumers of the PIC. The first sub-issue is also called out by the dedicated cross-shard finding ("audit-recognised-shapes.md / audit-target-categories.md â€” filenameâ†”heading/content inversion (CONFIRMED cross-shard)") and is best resolved there; the other three are independent of that fix.
