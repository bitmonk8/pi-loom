## Finding

The bullet that pins `pi.setActiveTools` / `pi.getActiveTools` runs the loom 1.0.0 normative snapshot/restore protocol (the synchronous-and-atomic precondition, the cooperative-`await` evidence trail, the SDK surface-inventory coverage, the closed loom 1.0.0 driver set) directly into a multi-clause description of a *defensive per-extension-instance runtime mutex* that loom 1.0.0 does **not** ship on a pass outcome of the [`version-bump-step2.md` item (e)](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md#bump-checklist-slash-dispatch-serialisation) audit recipe. The contingent material is substantial — it covers the `<a id="recovery-mutex-runtime-contract"></a>` runtime contract (strictly serialising FIFO `await`, no steady-state timeout), the `session_shutdown` teardown interaction (holder cancellation under sub-step 2's `loomAbort.abort()`, queued-waiter fall-through under sub-step 3's `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS`), the keying granularity (factory-captured `pi: ExtensionAPI`), and the forward-compatibility invariants that govern it — and is written in the same MUST/declarative voice as the surrounding loom 1.0.0 obligations.

The contingency is named only in a parenthetical mid-paragraph (*"the mutex exists only when the recovery path is engaged on a fail outcome of the [item (e)](…) audit recipe — loom 1.0 ships without it on a pass outcome…"*). There is no callout, no sub-heading, no typographic break, and no scope marker delimiting where the loom 1.0.0 MUSTs end and the contingent recovery-path contract begins. A reasonable implementer scanning the bullet for what they must build at loom 1.0.0 has to infer scope from a clause buried two sentences into the contract's keying-granularity discussion; an implementer who skims past that parenthetical will read the entire FIFO/teardown/keying contract as a loom 1.0.0 obligation and build a mutex the spec does not require to ship.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Partition the bullet in place with a contingent sub-block

**Approach.** Split the affected `pi.setActiveTools snapshots are name lists…` bullet into two adjacent blocks: the existing loom 1.0.0 normative material (signatures, surface-inventory coverage, the two behavioural preconditions, the cooperative-`await` evidence trail, the closed loom 1.0.0 driver set, the precondition (b)/Pi-version-bump re-validation hook) stays under the present bullet; the recovery-mutex contract becomes a clearly delimited follow-on block whose opening sentence labels it as a contingent recovery path (e.g. an indented sub-bullet headed *"Contingent — recovery path on a fail outcome of [item (e)](…), not shipped at loom 1.0.0"* or a `**Contingent — recovery path on a fail outcome of item (e); not shipped at loom 1.0.0:**` lead-in). The `<a id="recovery-mutex-runtime-contract"></a>` anchor stays on the contingent block so existing back-references from `version-bump-step2.md`, `V15d-prompt-suspend-snapshot.md`, and any other linking site continue to resolve.

**Spec edits.** `tool-registration-lifetime.md` only.

**Pros.** Anchor stays on its present page; existing back-references continue to resolve unchanged. The loom 1.0.0 MUSTs and the contingent contract become visually separable for any reader and any lens. The contingency phrase is hoisted out of mid-paragraph parenthesis and into a block-leading scope marker.

**Cons.** The page already runs near the spec-set size-cap soft target (a leading factor in the 2026-06-04 split that produced these topic pages); the partition adds prose for the block lead-in, marginally enlarging the page.

**Risks.** None substantive — the partition is a presentational refactor of an existing block under an existing anchor.

### Option B — Relocate the recovery-mutex contract to its partner anchor on the version-bump page

**Approach.** Move the recovery-mutex runtime contract (the FIFO-`await` sentence, the teardown-interaction sentence, and the keying-granularity invariant sentence) into `version-bump-step2.md`, under or immediately following item (e)'s existing recovery-serialisation prose (the *"A fail outcome requires adding a defensive per-extension-instance runtime serialisation…"* paragraph). The `<a id="recovery-mutex-runtime-contract"></a>` anchor relocates with the block. Update the inbound link in `version-bump-step2.md`'s own item (e) (the *"…is pinned at [snapshot/restore Pi behavioural preconditions — recovery-mutex runtime contract](./tool-registration-lifetime.md#recovery-mutex-runtime-contract) above and is not restated here"* sentence) to point at the new in-page location. Update `V15d-prompt-suspend-snapshot.md`'s spec citation and any other inbound link discovered by `grep -rn 'recovery-mutex-runtime-contract\|recovery-mutex' docs/`. Leave a one-sentence stub on `tool-registration-lifetime.md` noting that the recovery path on a fail outcome of item (e) — wrapping the snapshot → swap → body → restore sequence — is specified at the new anchor, so the page still names the existence of the contingent path without restating its runtime contract.

**Spec edits.** `tool-registration-lifetime.md` (excise the contract; leave the stub sentence), `version-bump-step2.md` (receive the contract; rewrite the inbound link in item (e)), `V15d-prompt-suspend-snapshot.md` (re-point its anchor citation), plus any other inbound link the `grep` turns up.

**Pros.** Owner-locality: item (e) is the audit recipe that *triggers* the recovery path, so its runtime contract sits next to its trigger and away from the loom 1.0.0 normative material that does not depend on it. Removes the contingent prose from the bullet entirely, so the scope-marker problem disappears at this site.

**Cons.** Anchor relocation breaks one cross-page link (item (e)'s back-reference) and requires re-pointing each existing inbound link; an inbound link missed by the `grep` resolves to a 404 anchor. Page-locality argument cuts the other way too: the runtime contract describes a mutex over the snapshot/restore sequence whose definition lives on the affected page, so an implementer reading the version-bump page now has to context-switch to the affected page to know what the mutex is wrapping.

**Risks.** Inbound-link breakage if the cross-reference sweep is incomplete; mitigated by the `grep` step and a CI link-check pass.

### Recommendation

Option A. The recovery-mutex contract's runtime semantics (what it wraps, the snapshot/restore sequence it serialises, the `Checkpoint` seam its queued waiters land at, the page-pinned one-`pi`-per-extension-instance lifetime its keying depends on) all reference machinery defined on the affected page; co-locating the contract with that machinery costs only a one-line scope marker, where Option B costs an anchor relocation plus a cross-page link sweep plus a context split between the mutex and the sequence it wraps.

Edge cases for the implementer:

- The scope marker must explicitly cover the *whole* contingent block (FIFO-`await` runtime contract + teardown-interaction sentence + keying-granularity invariant). The current parenthetical scoping inside the keying-granularity sentence is what creates the ambiguity; pulling that scope statement to a block-leading marker is the load-bearing edit.
- Preserve the `<a id="recovery-mutex-runtime-contract"></a>` anchor on the contingent block. Inbound back-references from `version-bump-step2.md` item (e) and from `V15d-prompt-suspend-snapshot.md` rely on it; moving or renaming the anchor turns this presentational fix into an inbound-link sweep.
- The precondition-(b) re-validation sentence at the end of the bullet (*"If either Pi behavioural precondition above weakens in a future Pi minor…"*) is loom 1.0.0 normative — it stays in the non-contingent block above the marker, not under it.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer building the loom 1.0.0 runtime against this bullet may construct a per-extension-instance mutex with FIFO-await semantics, the teardown interaction, and the keying invariants — none of which loom 1.0.0 actually requires to ship until and unless a future version-bump item (e) audit returns a fail outcome. The wasted effort is bounded, but it is wasted, and the surrounding text gives no signal that the work is contingent. A second observable consequence is review-time: lenses that key on "normative MUSTs in continuous prose" cannot distinguish this block's contingent obligations from the genuine loom 1.0.0 obligations, generating recurring spec-review noise on every pass over this bullet.
