## Problem

The `pi.setActiveTools` snapshots-are-name-lists bullet on `tool-registration-lifetime.md` runs the contingent recovery-mutex contract (the `id="recovery-mutex-runtime-contract"` FIFO-`await` runtime contract, its `session_shutdown` teardown interaction, and its per-extension-instance keying invariant) inline within the loom 1.0.0 normative snapshot/restore MUSTs, in the same MUST/declarative voice. That mutex is a defensive per-extension-instance runtime feature loom 1.0.0 does **not** ship on a pass outcome of the `version-bump-step2.md` item (e) audit recipe; its contingency is named only in a parenthetical buried in the keying-granularity sentence, with no callout, sub-heading, or scope marker delimiting where the loom 1.0.0 obligations end. An implementer scanning the bullet for what to build at loom 1.0.0 can read the entire contingent contract as a shipping obligation and build a mutex the spec does not require.

## Solution approach

Split the contingent recovery-mutex material (the `id="recovery-mutex-runtime-contract"` FIFO-`await` runtime contract, its teardown-interaction sentence, and its keying-granularity invariant) out of the loom 1.0.0 normative bullet into a delimited adjacent block whose lead-in labels it as a contingent recovery path not shipped at loom 1.0.0. Pull the scope statement currently embedded in the keying-granularity parenthetical up to that block-leading marker so it governs the whole contingent contract.

## Solution constraints

- Keep the `id="recovery-mutex-runtime-contract"` anchor on the contingent block — inbound back-references from `version-bump-step2.md` item (e) and `V15d-prompt-suspend-snapshot.md` resolve through it.
- The precondition-(b) re-validation sentence (*"If either Pi behavioural precondition above weakens in a future Pi minor…"*) is loom 1.0.0 normative and stays in the non-contingent block, not under the contingent marker.
