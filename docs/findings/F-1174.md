## Finding

The canonical-arm slug `tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact` appears at two distinct sites in the spec corpus:

- `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations.md:18` — `<a id="tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact"></a><a id="tooling-deferrals-no-v1-impact"></a>`, sitting between the `---` rule and the `## Contents` heading on the hub page.
- `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations/surface-extensions.md:3` — `<a id="tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact"></a>` immediately preceding the `## Tooling deferrals (no loom 1.0 impact)` heading on the normative-owner sub-page.

GOV-29 (`docs/spec_topics/governance/release-version-naming.md:55`) makes cross-page canonical-arm uniqueness a MUST: *"Every canonical-arm slug … MUST resolve uniquely across `docs/spec.md` + `docs/spec_topics/*.md`: no two distinct sites may carry byte-identical canonical-arm slugs."* The two occurrences above are a textbook violation of that rule. The single inbound `#tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact` cross-reference in the corpus (from `pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md:42`, pointing at the sub-page) is ambiguous on its face: which of the two sites a GFM renderer resolves it to is implementation-defined, and reviewers reading the link target by file path cannot disambiguate it from the slug alone.

The hub-page occurrence is also orphaned: after the spec collapse that moved the `## Tooling deferrals…` section body off the hub, the anchor stub remained behind on the hub but no longer precedes any matching heading — it now sits above `## Contents`. The collision and the orphan are the same underlying defect: a stale stub that should have been swept when the section moved to its sub-page.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `fe8aa5c` (2026-06-05, *"pi-loom spec: resolve 'GOV-12 bundles ≥3 independently-testable sub-obligations'"*) and `60fba43` (2026-06-07, *"pi-loom spec: resolve 'Operator-always-present invariant asserted without a Pi-side guarantee'"*).
**History:** The canonical-arm anchor was first authored on `future-considerations.md` in `8638c27` (2026-06-04, *"future-considerations.md category headings still spelled V1"*), where it correctly preceded the `## Tooling deferrals (no loom 1.0 impact)` heading on the (then still expanded) hub page. `fe8aa5c` (2026-06-05) collapsed the hub's `## Tooling deferrals`, `## Surface extensions`, `## Model-level changes`, and `## loom 1.0 non-goals` sections out of `future-considerations.md` into per-category sub-pages, but left the `<a id="tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact"></a><a id="tooling-deferrals-no-v1-impact"></a>` stub behind on the hub above the newly-stranded `## Contents` heading; at that point there was no collision because the sub-page didn't yet carry the anchor. The collision was introduced two days later by `60fba43` (2026-06-07), which added `<a id="tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact"></a>` on `surface-extensions.md` to give the new `host-prerequisites.md` cross-reference a target, without sweeping the now-stale hub stub. GOV-29 itself (`8e3ccb4`, 2026-06-05) postdates the hub stub by one day, so the rule was on the books when the collision-creating sub-page anchor was added, but neither commit's author noted the hub stub.
