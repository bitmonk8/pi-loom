## Problem

The canonical-arm slug `tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact` is carried at two distinct sites: an orphan stub on the hub page `future-considerations.md` (which sits above `## Contents` and no longer precedes any heading, after the `## Tooling deferrals` body moved to a sub-page) and the live anchor on its normative owner `surface-extensions.md`. GOV-29 makes cross-page canonical-arm slug uniqueness a MUST, so the duplicate is a violation. The single inbound `#tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact` cross-reference (from `host-prerequisites.md`) resolves nondeterministically between the two sites under GFM. The hub stub also carries the `tooling-deferrals-no-v1-impact` alias arm, which exists at no other site.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `fe8aa5c` (2026-06-05, *"pi-loom spec: resolve 'GOV-12 bundles ≥3 independently-testable sub-obligations'"*) and `60fba43` (2026-06-07, *"pi-loom spec: resolve 'Operator-always-present invariant asserted without a Pi-side guarantee'"*).
**History:** The canonical-arm anchor was first authored on `future-considerations.md` in `8638c27` (2026-06-04, *"future-considerations.md category headings still spelled V1"*), where it correctly preceded the `## Tooling deferrals (no loom 1.0 impact)` heading on the (then still expanded) hub page. `fe8aa5c` (2026-06-05) collapsed the hub's `## Tooling deferrals`, `## Surface extensions`, `## Model-level changes`, and `## loom 1.0 non-goals` sections out of `future-considerations.md` into per-category sub-pages, but left the `<a id="tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact"></a><a id="tooling-deferrals-no-v1-impact"></a>` stub behind on the hub above the newly-stranded `## Contents` heading; at that point there was no collision because the sub-page didn't yet carry the anchor. The collision was introduced two days later by `60fba43` (2026-06-07), which added `<a id="tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact"></a>` on `surface-extensions.md` to give the new `host-prerequisites.md` cross-reference a target, without sweeping the now-stale hub stub. GOV-29 itself (`8e3ccb4`, 2026-06-05) postdates the hub stub by one day, so the rule was on the books when the collision-creating sub-page anchor was added, but neither commit's author noted the hub stub. The defect is the *interaction* between the collapse that orphaned the stub and the subsequent sub-page anchor add, not a single faulty commit.

## Solution approach

Delete the orphan canonical-arm stub on `future-considerations.md` (both `<a id>` anchors) so the slug resolves uniquely to its normative owner on `surface-extensions.md`; if removing it leaves the preceding `---` rule separating nothing, delete that rule too. Because the hub stub is the sole site of the `tooling-deferrals-no-v1-impact` alias arm, record its retirement on `anchor-scheme-and-retired.md` per GOV-28(b).

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: `surface-extensions.md` (its anchor is the retained normative owner), GOV-29, and `pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md`.
