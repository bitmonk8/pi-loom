## Finding

The canonical-arm slug `tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact` appears at two distinct sites in the spec corpus:

- `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations.md:18` — `<a id="tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact"></a><a id="tooling-deferrals-no-v1-impact"></a>`, sitting between the `---` rule and the `## Contents` heading on the hub page.
- `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations/surface-extensions.md:3` — `<a id="tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact"></a>` immediately preceding the `## Tooling deferrals (no loom 1.0 impact)` heading on the normative-owner sub-page.

GOV-29 (`docs/spec_topics/governance/release-version-naming.md:55`) makes cross-page canonical-arm uniqueness a MUST: *"Every canonical-arm slug … MUST resolve uniquely across `docs/spec.md` + `docs/spec_topics/*.md`: no two distinct sites may carry byte-identical canonical-arm slugs."* The two occurrences above are a textbook violation of that rule. The single inbound `#tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact` cross-reference in the corpus (from `pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md:42`, pointing at the sub-page) is ambiguous on its face: which of the two sites a GFM renderer resolves it to is implementation-defined, and reviewers reading the link target by file path cannot disambiguate it from the slug alone.

The hub-page occurrence is also orphaned: after the spec collapse that moved the `## Tooling deferrals…` section body off the hub, the anchor stub remained behind on the hub but no longer precedes any matching heading — it now sits above `## Contents`. The collision and the orphan are the same underlying defect: a stale stub that should have been swept when the section moved to its sub-page.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `fe8aa5c` (2026-06-05, *"pi-loom spec: resolve 'GOV-12 bundles ≥3 independently-testable sub-obligations'"*) and `60fba43` (2026-06-07, *"pi-loom spec: resolve 'Operator-always-present invariant asserted without a Pi-side guarantee'"*).
**History:** The canonical-arm anchor was first authored on `future-considerations.md` in `8638c27` (2026-06-04, *"future-considerations.md category headings still spelled V1"*), where it correctly preceded the `## Tooling deferrals (no loom 1.0 impact)` heading on the (then still expanded) hub page. `fe8aa5c` (2026-06-05) collapsed the hub's `## Tooling deferrals`, `## Surface extensions`, `## Model-level changes`, and `## loom 1.0 non-goals` sections out of `future-considerations.md` into per-category sub-pages, but left the `<a id="tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact"></a><a id="tooling-deferrals-no-v1-impact"></a>` stub behind on the hub above the newly-stranded `## Contents` heading; at that point there was no collision because the sub-page didn't yet carry the anchor. The collision was introduced two days later by `60fba43` (2026-06-07), which added `<a id="tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact"></a>` on `surface-extensions.md` to give the new `host-prerequisites.md` cross-reference a target, without sweeping the now-stale hub stub. GOV-29 itself (`8e3ccb4`, 2026-06-05) postdates the hub stub by one day, so the rule was on the books when the collision-creating sub-page anchor was added, but neither commit's author noted the hub stub. The defect is the *interaction* between the collapse that orphaned the stub and the subsequent sub-page anchor add, not a single faulty commit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Delete the orphan hub stub

**Approach.** Remove the `<a id="tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact"></a><a id="tooling-deferrals-no-v1-impact"></a>` line at `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations.md:18`. The hub no longer carries any `## Tooling deferrals` content for the canonical arm to bind to; the sub-page is the natural and sole anchor target.

**Spec edits.**
- Delete line 18 of `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations.md` (the orphan `<a id>` stub).
- Optionally also delete the now-pointless `---` rule on line 16 if it leaves no surrounding section to separate.
- No edits to `surface-extensions.md`, GOV-29, or `host-prerequisites.md`.

**Pros.**
- Smallest diff; closes both the GOV-29 collision and the GOV-25 incidental-auto-id / orphan-stub defect in one stroke.
- The single inbound cross-reference (from `host-prerequisites.md`) already targets the sub-page and continues to resolve correctly without edit.
- Matches GOV-26's *Spec-corpus discharge predicate* posture for the `v1-*` alias: with zero inbound references to the hub-site alias, it is retirement-eligible.

**Cons.**
- Discards the hub-side `tooling-deferrals-no-v1-impact` alias arm. If any out-of-corpus document (external README, planning notes) links to the hub-site `#tooling-deferrals-no-v1-impact`, that link breaks silently; per GOV-29's *Cross-corpus scope* clause, however, such external links carry no spec-side governance.

**Risks.**
- Low. The alias-arm retirement on the hub side is bounded by the in-corpus discharge predicate; the audit-record row under GOV-28(b) is appended to `anchor-scheme-and-retired.md` as part of the same commit.

### Option B — Disambiguate the hub canonical arm with an aggregator tail

**Approach.** Per GOV-29's remedy paragraph: the normative-owner site (`surface-extensions.md`) retains the bare canonical arm `tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact`; the aggregator site (`future-considerations.md`) gets a tailed canonical arm — e.g. `tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact-aggregator` — pinned to the hub. The `v1-*` alias arms remain byte-identical across the two sites per GOV-26.

**Spec edits.**
- Rewrite line 18 of `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations.md` to `<a id="tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact-aggregator"></a><a id="tooling-deferrals-no-v1-impact"></a>`.
- Leave `surface-extensions.md:3` unchanged.
- No edits to GOV-29 or `host-prerequisites.md` (its existing link already resolves to the sub-page).

**Pros.**
- Preserves the hub as a citable aggregator anchor, consistent with the precedent GOV-29's worked example sets for `loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator` on `spec.md`.
- Maintains hub-side discoverability if a future cross-reference wants to point at "the hub's mention of tooling deferrals" rather than the normative owner.

**Cons.**
- Does not address the orphan-stub defect: the hub anchor still does not bind to any `## Tooling deferrals` heading, since the section's content has moved.
- Larger conceptual footprint than Option A for a hub that no longer carries category content.

**Risks.**
- The GOV-29 worked example (`loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator`) targets a hub paragraph that *still discusses* the aggregated content; adapting the same pattern to an orphan stub stretches the convention.

### Recommendation

Option A. The hub stub is orphaned from its former heading and serves no current navigation purpose; deleting it closes the GOV-29 collision with the smallest blast radius and incidentally resolves the orphan-stub defect. Append a `## Retired anchor aliases` row on `anchor-scheme-and-retired.md` per GOV-28(b) recording the hub-site `tooling-deferrals-no-v1-impact` retirement. Verify the inbound-reference count by running the GOV-28(a) detection recipe (`grep -rnE '#([a-z0-9]+-)*v1(-[a-z0-9]+|$)' docs/spec.md docs/spec_topics/`) against both retired arms before the commit lands.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Inbound `#tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact` cross-references resolve nondeterministically across the two sites under GFM, and GOV-28's `v1-*` alias retirement-discharge predicate (which greps for inbound `#fragment` references) cannot reliably attribute an inbound link to its intended target site. The corpus carries one such cross-reference today (from `host-prerequisites.md`); any future cross-reference compounds the ambiguity. The collision is mechanically detectable and would be flagged by any future GOV-29 audit script, blocking spec-side green builds the moment such a check lands.
