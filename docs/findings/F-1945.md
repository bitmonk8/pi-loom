## Problem

`V17a` (Cancellation core) — and its paired `V17a-T` — ships the entire remaining cancellation contract as one leaf even after `V17b` (throw-trap) and `V17c` (checkpoint granularity) were peeled off. Its **Ships when** and **Tests** enumerate roughly eight independently-observable assertions that split cleanly along two seams: a forwarding/propagation seam (the `loomAbort` controller, three-source forwarding, `CNCL-4` reason identity, downward-only propagation, `CNCL-1`/`CNCL-2`/`CNCL-3`) and a late-settlement-discard seam (`CNCL-5`, `CNCL-6`, and the `Checkpoint`-seam three-channel swallowing-handler suppression). Bundled together, the paired tests-first increment cannot land green until the full cancellation runtime exists, enlarging the increment well past the per-leaf atomicity bar the plan applies elsewhere.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** `c6a664e` (`pi-loom plan: build/update plan for spec.md + review`, 2026-06-10)
**History:** The plan corpus is git-tracked. `V17a-cancellation-core.md` was created at `c6a664e` already carrying the over-bundled scope: its inception **Adds** bundled the `loomAbort` controller, three-source forwarding, abort-reason propagation, downward-only propagation, the fixed checkpoint set, the late-settlement discard rules, and swallowing-handler suppression in one leaf. Subsequent commits *reduced* the bundle without eliminating it — `V17b` (throw-trap) and `V17c` (checkpoint set, peeled off at `5b95143`, 2026-06-18, which also retargeted consumer Deps) were extracted, and `CNCL-4/5/6` plus the seam-level swallowing assertion were elaborated — but the residual forwarding-plus-late-settlement bundle flagged here has been present continuously since inception. No single later commit introduced it.

## Solution approach

Split `V17a` and its paired `V17a-T` into two paired leaves along the forwarding/propagation-versus-late-settlement-discard seam, allocating the new `V17`-phase leaf IDs per the plan ID scheme. Assign the `loomAbort` controller, three-source forwarding, `CNCL-4` reason identity, downward-only propagation, and `CNCL-1`/`CNCL-2`/`CNCL-3` to the forwarding/propagation leaf; assign `CNCL-5`, `CNCL-6`, and the `Checkpoint`-seam three-channel swallowing-handler suppression to the late-settlement-discard leaf, moving the corresponding **Tests** and **Ships when** clauses with each. Reconcile `coverage-matrix.md` (the `CNCL` rows and the `cka-33` row's `V17a` facet), `plan.md` (the V17 slice listing and the `H5b` `V17a`–`V17c` **Deps** range), and every consumer **Deps** that cites `V17a`, retargeting each to whichever new leaf owns the seam it actually consumes.

## Solution constraints

- New leaf IDs must follow the `V<N><letter>` plan ID scheme (next free letter in the V17 phase, never a reused or retired ID); do not hardcode a final ID before it is allocated.
- Preserve the existing delegation note tying the seam-level swallowing-handler assertion to its four per-site owners (`V13f`, `V14f`, `V15h`, `V9o`) so the `H5f` per-facet gate still resolves.
