## Finding

`V17a` ‚Äî Cancellation core, even after `V17b` (forwarding-listener throw-trap) and `V17c` (checkpoint granularity) were peeled off into their own leaves, still ships the whole remaining cancellation contract as one leaf. Its **Ships when** enumerates eight independently-observable assertions: (1) forwarding a cancel into `loomAbort`; (2) downward-only propagation; (3) `CNCL-1`/`CNCL-2`/`CNCL-3` (no late rebind / no second `Err` / no second `RuntimeEvent`); (4) `CNCL-4` reason-identity propagation through all three forwarding paths, including the byte-exact `"loom cancelled by agent_end"` synthesised message; (5) `CNCL-5` (a completed `Ok` is not retroactively rewritten); (6) `CNCL-6` (a tail abort synthesises no top-level `cancelled`); and (7) three-channel swallowing-handler suppression at the `Checkpoint`-seam substrate.

These split cleanly along a forwarding/propagation seam versus a late-settlement-discard seam. The `loomAbort` controller, the three forwarding sources, `CNCL-4` reason identity, downward-only propagation, and `CNCL-1`/`CNCL-2`/`CNCL-3` together describe the *forwarding and propagation contract*. `CNCL-5`, `CNCL-6`, and the seam-level three-channel swallowing-handler suppression together describe *what happens to abandoned or late-settling work after a checkpoint has surfaced cancellation* ‚Äî a surface verifiable at the `Checkpoint` seam without exercising the forwarding contract at all.

Bundled as one leaf, the paired `V17a-T` / `V17a` increment cannot be landed green until the full cancellation runtime exists, enlarging the single tests-first increment well beyond the per-leaf atomicity bar the plan applies elsewhere (`V14a`/`V14b`, the `V15a` split proposal, and the prior `V17b`/`V17c` peel-offs from this very leaf).

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** `c6a664e` (`pi-loom plan: build/update plan for spec.md + review`, 2026-06-10)
**History:** The plan corpus is git-tracked. `V17a-cancellation-core.md` was created at `c6a664e` already carrying the over-bundled scope: its inception **Adds** bundled the `loomAbort` controller, three-source forwarding, abort-reason propagation, downward-only propagation, the fixed checkpoint set, the late-settlement discard rules, and swallowing-handler suppression in one leaf. Subsequent commits *reduced* the bundle without eliminating it ‚Äî `V17b` (throw-trap) and `V17c` (checkpoint set, peeled off at `5b95143`, 2026-06-18, which also retargeted consumer Deps) were extracted, and `CNCL-4/5/6` plus the seam-level swallowing assertion were elaborated ‚Äî but the residual forwarding-plus-late-settlement bundle flagged here has been present continuously since inception. No single later commit introduced it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Split `V17a` and its paired `V17a-T` into two paired leaves along the forwarding/propagation versus late-settlement-discard seam; the implementer allocates the two new IDs (the existing `V17a`/`V17b`/`V17c` peel-off naming pattern is the natural home ‚Äî do not hardcode a final ID in the plan until allocated).

Forwarding/propagation-core leaf (`<new>` / `<new>-T`) owns:
- the `loomAbort` controller and forwarding of Pi's per-handler `ctx.signal`, the tool-exposed `signal`, and parent-`invoke` signals into `loomAbort` (never `ctx.signal` directly);
- `CNCL-4` reason-identity propagation through all three forwarding paths, including the byte-exact `"loom cancelled by agent_end"` synthesised message;
- downward-only propagation;
- `CNCL-1`, `CNCL-2`, `CNCL-3`;
- the existing `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS` conceptual-consumer **Adds** text.

Late-settlement-discard leaf (`<new>` / `<new>-T`) owns:
- `CNCL-5` (a completed `Ok` is not retroactively rewritten to `Err({kind:"cancelled"})`);
- `CNCL-6` (a tail abort produces no synthesised top-level `cancelled`);
- the three-channel swallowing-handler suppression assertion at the `Checkpoint`-seam substrate (no `unhandledRejection`, no second `RuntimeEvent`, no diagnostic), including the existing note that per-site routing of the four abandonable-Promise sites is delegated to `V14f`/`V13f`/`V15h`/`V9o`.

Move the corresponding **Tests** bullets and **Ships when** clauses into each leaf. Reconcile references:
- `coverage-matrix.md`: the `CNCL-1, CNCL-2, CNCL-3` row and the `CNCL-4` row's `V17a` entry point at the forwarding/propagation-core leaf; the `CNCL-5, CNCL-6` row points at the late-settlement-discard leaf; the `cka-33` multi-leaf row's `V17a` facet retargets to the late-settlement-discard leaf (it owns the seam-level swallowing assertion).
- Consumer **Deps**: leaves consuming the `loomAbort`/`Checkpoint`-forwarding seam (`V4c`, `V4f`, `V9c`, `V9g`, `V9i`, `V9n`, `V11j`, `V17b`, `V17c`, `V18d`, `H7a` and their `-T` pairs) retarget to the forwarding/propagation-core leaf; the four per-site swallowing-handler leaves (`V13f`, `V14f`, `V15h`, `V9o`, and their `-T` pairs) consume the seam-level Œ≤ substrate and retarget to the late-settlement-discard leaf.
- `plan.md`: update the V17 slice listing and expand `H5b`'s `V17a`‚Äì`V17c` **Deps** range to name both new IDs.

Edge cases the implementer must settle while applying the split: declare each new leaf's own **Deps** for the seam it actually consumes (the late-settlement-discard leaf needs at least `V8a` for the `Checkpoint` seam, and depends on the forwarding-core leaf only if it consumes `loomAbort` directly); preserve the existing delegation note tying the seam-level swallowing assertion to its four per-site owners so the `H5f` per-facet gate still resolves.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

If `V17a` ships unsplit, the entire cancellation contract ‚Äî forwarding, propagation, `CNCL-1/2/3/5/6`, and seam-level swallowing-handler suppression ‚Äî must be brought up and proven green in a single tests-first increment, defeating the per-leaf atomicity the plan applies everywhere else and enlarging the review surface of the one leaf and its `-T` partner. No two implementers would diverge on behaviour, so the defect is one of increment size and reviewability rather than correctness.
