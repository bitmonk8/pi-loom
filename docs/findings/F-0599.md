## Finding

The bare noun **arm** is load-bearing in two unrelated governance sub-systems on the same page family (`docs/spec_topics/governance/`), with no glossary entry, no disambiguator, and no cross-reference distinguishing the senses:

1. **GOV-18 binding-scope arms.** `corpus-direction-and-scope.md` introduces *arm (a)* (the implementation target — runtime, binder, type system, Pi integration) and *arm (b)* (the spec corpus itself) as the exhaustive set of parties the spec's normative obligations bind. Anchors `gov-18-arm-a` / `gov-18-arm-b` are cited dozens of times across the corpus (e.g. `[GOV-18 arm (b)](./corpus-direction-and-scope.md#gov-18-arm-b)` in `req-id-prefix-table-active-a.md`, `release-version-naming.md`, `stable-inline-labels.md`).
2. **GOV-25/26/27/28/29 anchor-slug arms.** `release-version-naming.md` uses *canonical arm* (the `loom-1-0-*` / `loom-1-0-0-*` slug of a dual-anchored heading) and *alias arm* / *`v1-*` arm* (the `v1-*` back-compat slug paired with it) as a closed pair of anchor-slug variants. The same surface noun also appears in derived phrases on the same page — *retired alias arm*, *canonical-arm slug*, *cross-page canonical-arm uniqueness*, *canonical-arm citation*.

The two senses share the governance topic directory and one of them (GOV-25) references the other (`[GOV-18 arm (a)](./corpus-direction-and-scope.md#gov-18-arm-a)`) inside its own body, so a reader hitting "the canonical arm of the dual-anchored heading is bound to arm (b) by GOV-18" in `release-version-naming.md` § *Sibling-rule reconciliation* has to mentally re-bind *arm* mid-sentence. A `grep -n '\barm\b' docs/spec_topics/governance/` returns the two senses interleaved with no syntactic key to separate them; a future maintainer touching either rule cannot mechanically scope their edit.

The collision is purely terminological — the senses do not contradict each other, and neither is wrongly stated — but it is the second instance of this exact pattern in the same governance subtree (the sibling finding on `dual-form anchor` / `dual-anchored` flags the analogous `dual-*` collision), suggesting a small house-keeping drift rather than a one-off.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `64cdc60` (2026-05-26) — *pi-loom spec: resolve "governance.md plan-corpus dependency + corpus-direction articulation" (T27 + T28 cluster)* — introduced GOV-18 and coined *Arm (a)* / *Arm (b)* as the binding-parties pair (this commit added the entire `## Corpus direction and binding scope` section, with the `gov-18-arm-a` / `gov-18-arm-b` anchors and the *arm (a)* / *arm (b)* / *arm a* / *arm b* surface forms; previously no rule on the page used the noun *arm*).
- `4a7afbf` (2026-05-28) — *pi-loom spec: introduce release-version naming + legacy token aliases (GOV-19/20/21)* — coined *canonical arm* / *alias arm* as the anchor-slug pair under the (then-bundled) GOV-21 *Canonical arm* / *Alias permanence* sub-clauses, including the `gov-21-canonical-arm` anchor. The body of this commit cites `[GOV-18 arm (b)](#gov-18-arm-b)` from the new *Sibling-rule reconciliation* paragraph, so the second author knew the noun was already taken in the first sense and re-used it anyway.
- `8e3ccb4` (2026-06-05) — *pi-loom spec: resolve "GOV-21 bundles five independently testable sub-clauses"* — split GOV-21 into GOV-25/26/27/28/29 (the present-day anchor numbering), promoting *canonical arm* / *alias arm* from sub-clause labels to peer-REQ-ID concepts and so widening their surface area.

**History:** Confirmed via `git log --pretty='%h %ad %s' --date=short -S 'arm' -- docs/spec_topics/governance.md` (the pre-shard ancestor file) and via `git show 4a7afbf:docs/spec_topics/governance.md` to verify that the May-28 commit reuses the May-26 *arm (a)* / *arm (b)* anchors from within the body of the rule that introduces the second sense. The collision is therefore a two-commit-interaction layered onto by the later split commit — neither commit alone produced it, and there is no single commit that can be reverted to undo it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Rename the anchor-slug pair (currently *canonical arm* / *alias arm* / *`v1-*` arm*) to *canonical slug* / *alias slug*; leave GOV-18's *arm (a)* / *arm (b)* binding-parties pair untouched. Concretely, in the same commit:

1. On `release-version-naming.md`, sweep every occurrence of *canonical arm* → *canonical slug*, *alias arm* → *alias slug*, *`v1-*` arm* → *`v1-*` slug*, and the compound forms *canonical-arm citation* / *canonical-arm slug* / *canonical-arm uniqueness* → *canonical-slug citation* / *canonical slug* / *canonical-slug uniqueness* across the GOV-25, GOV-26, GOV-27, GOV-28, GOV-29 bodies, the *Version-segment position* clause, the *Incidental auto-id prohibition* clause, and the *Sibling-rule reconciliation* paragraph.
2. Rename the affected REQ-ID title fragments to match (`GOV-25 (canonical-slug citation)`, `GOV-29 (cross-page canonical-slug uniqueness)`); the bold-inline `**GOV-25.**` … `**GOV-29.**` anchor markers themselves do not change (the REQ-ID anchors are numeric, not titular, so GOV-7 *Rename* applies as a *Pure rewording* under GOV-8 — the rule's normative sentences are byte-for-byte preserved apart from the renamed noun).
3. The HTML anchors `gov-21-canonical-arm` and `gov-21-alias-permanence` are already retired (the May-28 sub-clause anchors were absorbed into the GOV-25..29 split); no `<a id>` rename is needed beyond removing any lingering body-text references to those retired anchors. Verify with `grep -rn 'canonical-arm\|alias-arm\|canonical arm\|alias arm' docs/spec.md docs/spec_topics/` — the post-edit result set MUST be empty.
4. On `anchor-scheme-and-retired.md`, sweep the *Retired anchor aliases* preamble and column-definition list (*retired alias arm*, *surviving canonical arm*) on the same terms.
5. On `glossary.md`, add a one-line disambiguation entry: *arm — overloaded; see GOV-18 for binding-scope arms (arm (a) / arm (b)) and GOV-25/26 for anchor-slug variants (now `canonical slug` / `alias slug`)*. Keep the entry even after the rename to anchor a future grep; the legacy *arm* surface will persist in commit history.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The `v1-*` HTML-anchor slug literal stays `v1-*` — only the prose noun changes; renaming the slug itself would violate GOV-26 *alias permanence* and is forbidden.
- The two adjective compounds *dual-anchored* (GOV-27) and *dual-form* (GOV-1) are governed by the sibling finding *`dual-form anchor` (GOV-1) vs `dual-anchored` (GOV-27)*; this rename leaves both untouched and does not preempt that finding's resolution.
- GOV-18's *arm (a)* / *arm (b)* surface forms — including the worked-examples line "*Retired GOV-2.* … neither the implementation target (arm a) nor the spec corpus itself (arm b)" — MUST NOT be swept; only the GOV-25..29 *anchor-slug* sense changes.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader of `release-version-naming.md` § *Sibling-rule reconciliation* must context-switch between the two senses of *arm* inside a single paragraph; a maintainer running `grep '\barm\b'` over the governance subtree to scope a future edit gets a mixed result set with no syntactic discriminator. No implementer would produce a wrong runtime behaviour from this — both senses are spec-corpus arm-(b) terminology that GOV-18 itself classifies as out of the implementation target's binding scope — but the collision raises the documentation-comprehension cost of every GOV-25..29 edit and silently catches every future term-search refactor.
