## Finding

`factory-probable` is a coined spec term meaning *a capability whose required SDK members have a stable `typeof`-checkable identity at extension-factory entry* — the criterion the Step 0(c) capability probe uses to verify members before any `pi.register*` call. It anchors a normative partition: the five factory-probable inventory items (capabilities 1, 2, 3, 4, 6 — nine function members) versus the two verified-otherwise items (item 5 cancellation propagation, item 7 binder LLM model).

The term is load-bearing across the corpus: it appears in ten files — `capability-probe.md`, `capability-inventory-items.md`, `overview-and-orientation.md`, `inventory-audit-intro.md`, `audit-target-categories.md`, `audit-resolution.md`, `audit-wire-and-canary.md`, `tool-registration-lifetime.md`, `version-bump-step2.md`, and `diagnostics/code-registry-load.md` — and several of those references use it bare (e.g. the cross-reference "the **factory-probable subset** of the seven SDK capabilities"). Yet the glossary, which registers comparable coined terms such as *surface-set closure*, *callable set*, and *schema slug*, carries no entry for it. Only `capability-probe.md` Step 0(c) defines the term in full; a reader arriving at any of the bare cross-references has no glossary target to consult, and the word "probable" reads as a likelihood qualifier rather than a checkability-timing criterion, compounding the ambiguity.

The discoverability gap has existed unchanged from the term's coinage.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add a glossary entry keyed on **`factory-probable`** (with **`factory-probable subset`** as the registered phrase the cross-references use): "The subset of SDK capability-inventory items (capabilities 1, 2, 3, 4, 6 — nine function members) whose required members have a stable `typeof`-checkable identity at extension-factory entry, enabling the Step 0(c) capability probe to verify them before any `pi.register*` call. Contrast the two verified-otherwise items — 5 (cancellation propagation) and 7 (binder LLM model) — which are not factory-probable." Add `See:` links to [Pi Integration Contract — Step 0 (Capability probe)](./pi-integration-contract/capability-probe.md#entry-capability-probe) and [Pi Integration Contract — SDK capability inventory](./pi-integration-contract/inventory-audit-intro.md#sdk-capability-inventory).

Keep the term spelling as-is and define it in place: `factory-probable` backs the exported constant `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES` (V9a) and is pinned in plan acceptance criteria (V9a PIC-5, V18a PIC-15), so registering a glossary entry — rather than renaming the term — is what closes the gap without desynchronising the spec from the plan leaves and the code symbol.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader who meets `factory-probable subset` via a cross-reference has no glossary entry to resolve the term and must navigate to `capability-probe.md` Step 0(c) for the definition. Implementers can still build a correct probe from the in-context definition, so this is a discoverability/guidance gap rather than a correctness divergence.
