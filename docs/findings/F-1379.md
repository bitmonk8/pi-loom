## Finding

Three of the five `V9b` *Tests* bullets in `V9b-registration-reload-wiring.md` open their spec-link parenthetical with `(PIC area; un-anchored GOV-22 residue; facet `cka-23.<x>`)` — the `getcommands`, `note-empty`, and `note-rename` bullets. The `un-anchored GOV-22 residue` clause is governance provenance: it records *why* the obligation carries no numbered `PREFIX-N` REQ-ID. That classification is already owned authoritatively by the `cka-23` row of `coverage-matrix.md`, whose cell ends with the same `(un-anchored; GOV-22 residue)` tag. The test bullet does not gate on the clause and an implementer cannot act on it — the only actionable cross-reference is the `cka-23.<x>` facet sub-label, which ties the bullet back to the coverage-matrix row that carries the classification.

The duplication is also internally inconsistent: the sibling `cka-23.swap` bullet (bullet 1) carries only `(PIC area; facet `cka-23.swap`)` with no GOV-22 clause, demonstrating that the facet sub-label functions without the provenance prose. The redundant clause was introduced as a side effect of two earlier plan fixes (see *Issue introduction*) rather than authored deliberately.

No closing-gate machinery reads the clause: the `cka-23.*` facet sub-labels are a within-leaf device explicitly out of scope of the `H5f` per-facet citing-test gate (per `coverage-matrix.md`'s *Multi-leaf-row per-facet citing tests* note), and the GOV-22 provenance text is free prose carrying no token. The duplicated classification is therefore pure cruft that can drift out of sync with the `cka-23` row on any future edit.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `b722ce7` (2026-06-17, "pi-loom plan: resolve \"registration-steps.md has normative MUSTs but no coverage-matrix row\""); `c1fa860` (2026-06-18, "pi-loom plan: resolve \"registration-steps.md V9b row bundles three obligation areas with no sub-IDs\"")
**History:** `git log --follow -S 'un-anchored GOV-22 residue'` over `docs/plan_topics/V9b-registration-reload-wiring.md` shows the clause first entered the leaf in `b722ce7`, the same commit that added the `cka-23` row to `coverage-matrix.md` — so the classification was duplicated into the single structural-change watcher-note bullet at the moment the authoritative coverage-matrix owner was created. `c1fa860` then split that bullet into the `note-empty` / `note-rename` pair and added the `getcommands` bullet with `cka-23.*` facet sub-labels, propagating the same clause onto all three. Neither commit's stated intent was the duplication; the residue is an unintended carry-through of the provenance prose across the split.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V9b-registration-reload-wiring.md`, strike the `un-anchored GOV-22 residue; ` clause from the three *Tests* bullet parentheticals that carry it (`getcommands`, `note-empty`, `note-rename`), leaving each parenthetical as `(PIC area; facet `cka-23.<x>`)` — parallel with the existing `cka-23.swap` bullet. Retain the spec link and the `cka-23.*` facet sub-label in every bullet; the sub-label is the load-bearing cross-reference to the `coverage-matrix.md` `cka-23` row, which keeps sole ownership of the GOV-22 classification.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The duplicated `un-anchored GOV-22 residue` clause adds no test constraint and no gate reads it; the test behaviour is identical with or without it. The only concrete downside is drift risk — the classification now lives in two places (the `cka-23` coverage-matrix cell and three `V9b` bullets) and can diverge on edit, with nothing flagging the divergence.
