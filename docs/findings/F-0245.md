## Problem

`V4c` (and its paired `V4c-T`) closes `ERR-8`…`ERR-13` in a single leaf. `ERR-8`…`ERR-12` are the closed success/failure/cancelled trichotomy, partial-append contract, and turn-grain finality — five compact test bullets. `ERR-13` is a materially heavier unit: it drives the no-rollback guarantee across five distinct `error-model.md` §No rollback authoring sites plus a second completed-callee finality vector, each scaffolded through the `H4a` session double and the `V17a` side-effect seam, making it comparable in implementation effort to the entire trichotomy it is stacked on. The project's right-sizing convention (`conventions.md` §3 / `plan.md` *Vertical slices*) holds that each leaf is the smallest feature that can ship and be tested independently; the no-rollback proof can ship and be tested independently of the trichotomy, so the two belong in separate leaves.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** emergent — multi-commit interaction
**Introducing commits:** `de19068` (2026-06-11), `db918a2` (2026-06-11)
**History:** `V4c` carried `ERR-8`…`ERR-13` in one leaf since plan inception (`c6a664e`, 2026-06-10), but at inception `ERR-13` was a single light bullet ("`?`/panic/cancel never unwind side effects; completed tool calls, queries, and invoke children are final"), comparable in weight to the other `ERR-*` bullets. The size imbalance was introduced by two later review-resolution commits that each grew `ERR-13`'s scope: `de19068` ("resolve 'ERR-13 no-rollback vectors do not span enumerated authoring sites'") expanded `ERR-13` to drive the five enumerated authoring sites with `H4a`/`V17a` scaffolding, and `db918a2` ("resolve 'ERR-13 no-rollback / completed-callee finality over-claimed in V4c'") added the second completed-callee-finality `ERR-13` bullet. Neither commit was wrong in isolation — each tightened coverage to close a prior finding — but their cumulative effect tipped `ERR-13` into a unit of comparable complexity to the rest of the leaf, which is the atomicity defect this finding names.

## Solution approach

Split the two `ERR-13` test bullets out of `V4c` and `V4c-T` into a new implementation leaf plus its paired `-T` tests leaf, keeping `V4c`/`V4c-T` as the `ERR-8`…`ERR-12` trichotomy leaf under its existing ID. Split the `ERR-8 … ERR-13 | V4c` row in `coverage-matrix.md` so the new leaf closes `ERR-13`, add the new leaf to `H5b`'s `Deps.`, and retarget `H4b`'s `ERR-13` completed-invoke-child references to the new leaf while leaving its `ERR-12` reference on `V4c`. Link the new leaf into `plan.md`'s `V4 — Errors and results` slice listing.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the spec corpus is read-only for this fix.
- Allocate the new leaf ID under the plan's `V<N><letter>` leaf-ID scheme with its paired `-T` tests leaf per the TDD pairing rule; do not reuse a retired ID.
- Keep `coverage-matrix.md` and `H5b`'s `Deps.` consistent with the split in the same edit (the new leaf closes coverage-matrix-mapped `ERR-13`, which the transitive-completeness gate requires).
