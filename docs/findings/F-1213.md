## Finding

Two normative blocks under PIC sit on pages whose subject is not the obligation they pin.

(1) **Factory-ordering pin.** The `<a id="factory-ordering-pin">` paragraph at the foot of [`session-shutdown-semantics.md`](../../docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/session-shutdown-semantics.md) is an ordering MUST over the Extension-entry-point sequence: "the `session_shutdown` handler MUST be subscribed (step 4 above) only after the `LoomRegistry` and watcher handles … are constructed (the [watcher / hot-reload registration step](../../docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/registration-steps.md#watcher-hot-reload-registration) — step 5 …)". Steps 4 and 5 are both defined in [`registration-steps.md`](../../docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/registration-steps.md), and the pin's own trailing paragraph already concedes the placement by adding "The watcher / hot-reload lifecycle this ordering pin protects … is specified at the [watcher / hot-reload registration step] …, not under this heading." The pin landed here as a residue of an earlier split (the watcher prose used to live in this file as an oversized step 5; see commit `6d1a949` `pi-loom spec: resolve "session-shutdown sub-step 5 hot-reload split"`, 2026-06-05, which relocated the watcher prose to `registration-steps.md` but left the ordering MUST behind).

(2) **Recovery-mutex runtime contract.** The `<a id="recovery-mutex-runtime-contract">` clause in [`tool-registration-lifetime.md`](../../docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/tool-registration-lifetime.md) (the "snapshot/restore Pi behavioural preconditions" bullet) pins the FIFO acquisition semantics and `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS` teardown interaction of a defensive per-extension-instance mutex. That mutex itself only exists on the **fail outcome** of [`version-bump-step2.md` item (e)](../../docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md#bump-checklist-slash-dispatch-serialisation) — which is the page that introduces the recovery path. The clause does carry conditional framing ("On that recovery path …", "the mutex exists only when the recovery path is engaged …"), but it is embedded mid-sentence inside a long load-bearing bullet that pins behaviour every prompt-mode reader must internalise unconditionally; a reader following the snapshot/restore protocol at the loom 1.0 pass outcome must skip past contract prose that does not apply at the pin. The contract belongs with the recovery path that owns it (item (e) of `version-bump-step2.md`), or — if kept on this page — under a sub-heading that makes the activation condition syntactically visible rather than mid-paragraph.

Both blocks are real obligations, well-defined; the issue is purely placement / conditional framing. Neither is a false positive — the prose itself flags the misalignment for (1) and the inline conditional for (2).

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

This finding bundles two independent placement obligations on two distinct spec files. Resolve sequentially, smaller-scope first, so the second edit lands on a stable baseline.

### Option A — Relocate the factory-ordering pin to `registration-steps.md` step 5

**Approach.** Move the `<a id="factory-ordering-pin">` paragraph from `session-shutdown-semantics.md` into the body of `registration-steps.md` step 5 (`<a id="watcher-hot-reload-registration">`), as a sub-paragraph immediately after the existing "The ordering constraint requiring step 4's `session_shutdown` subscription to follow this registry/watcher construction is the [**Factory-ordering pin**]…" sentence (which then becomes the in-place statement rather than a forward link).

**Spec edits.**
- `registration-steps.md` step 5: replace the existing forward-link sentence with the relocated paragraph (preserving the `<a id="factory-ordering-pin">` anchor so existing cross-refs continue to resolve).
- `session-shutdown-semantics.md`: delete the `<a id="factory-ordering-pin">` paragraph and the trailing "lifecycle this ordering pin protects … not under this heading" sentence (both become tautological once the pin lives on the right page).
- `coverage-matrix.md` row `cka-30`: drop "and the *Factory-ordering pin*" from the obligation list. The pin's new closing leaf is `V9b` (under `cka-23`), not `V9g`; either extend `cka-23`'s prose to name the Factory-ordering MUST, or open a new `cka-<n>` row for it.
- Audit any other `#factory-ordering-pin` reference. (`grep -rn '#factory-ordering-pin' docs/` finds it in `version-bump-step2.md` and elsewhere; the anchor stays valid post-move, so no link rewrite is needed.)

**Pros.** Eliminates the self-contradictory concession sentence; ordering rule sits adjacent to the steps it orders; `cka-30` and `cka-23` become honest about which page hosts which MUST.

**Cons.** Re-shapes `cka-30` / `cka-23` rows (one extra coverage-matrix touch).

**Risks.** Low. The pin is one paragraph and its inbound links are anchor-keyed, so they survive the move.

### Option B — Move the recovery-mutex contract to `version-bump-step2.md` item (e)

**Approach.** Cut the `<a id="recovery-mutex-runtime-contract">` clause out of the `snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions` bullet on `tool-registration-lifetime.md` and paste it into `version-bump-step2.md` item (e) as a dedicated sub-paragraph headed *Recovery-mutex runtime contract* (or similar). Replace the cut text on `tool-registration-lifetime.md` with a single forward sentence: "The runtime contract for this mutex — FIFO acquisition, no steady-state timeout, teardown observed under `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS` — is pinned at [recovery-mutex runtime contract](./version-bump-step2.md#…)." Preserve the `<a id="recovery-mutex-runtime-contract">` anchor at the new home; redirect with a stub if necessary.

**Spec edits.**
- `tool-registration-lifetime.md` snapshot/restore bullet: excise the relocated clause; replace with the forward sentence above.
- `version-bump-step2.md` item (e): append the *Recovery-mutex runtime contract* sub-paragraph (with the migrated anchor). Item (e)'s prose already mentions the contract by link, so the landing point is natural.
- Audit `#recovery-mutex-runtime-contract` callers (`version-bump-step2.md` item (e) currently links to it; if the anchor migrates, that link becomes intra-page).

**Pros.** Recovery-path runtime contract sits with the audit recipe that decides whether the recovery path is engaged; pass-outcome readers on the loom 1.0 happy path no longer wade past inapplicable contract prose.

**Cons.** Lengthens `version-bump-step2.md`, an already-large file (size-cap of 100 KB / 30 KB recommended applies; verify post-edit). Splits the conceptual unit "snapshot/restore preconditions" across two files.

**Risks.** Low–moderate. One anchor migration; one inbound forward sentence on the original page keeps the precondition bullet readable on its own.

### Option B' — Keep the recovery-mutex contract on `tool-registration-lifetime.md` under a clearly-labelled conditional sub-section

**Approach.** Lift the clause out of the snapshot/restore bullet and promote it to its own sub-section on `tool-registration-lifetime.md` with a heading that names the activation condition explicitly, e.g. *"Recovery-mutex runtime contract (engaged only on item-(e) fail outcome)"*. Keep the inline conditional phrasing already present.

**Spec edits.**
- `tool-registration-lifetime.md`: extract the clause to a dedicated sub-section under the snapshot/restore section, keeping the `<a id="recovery-mutex-runtime-contract">` anchor.
- Truncate the snapshot/restore bullet to its precondition prose; add a closing forward sentence "When the recovery path is engaged on a fail outcome, its runtime contract is pinned at [Recovery-mutex runtime contract](#recovery-mutex-runtime-contract) below."

**Pros.** No cross-file motion; lighter than Option B. Resolves the immediate readability defect (mid-paragraph normative prose with hidden activation condition).

**Cons.** Conceptually the contract still lives away from item (e); a future reader following the version-bump procedure still needs the round-trip.

**Risks.** Low.

### Recommendation

Land **Option A first**, then **Option B'** (with **Option B** as the fallback if `version-bump-step2.md` has headroom under the size cap). Order rationale: Option A is a single-paragraph move whose only coverage-matrix touch is `cka-30` / `cka-23`; resolving it first cleans the `session-shutdown-semantics.md` page before any other PIC re-shape touches it. Option B' is the lighter of the two recovery-mutex options and preserves the conceptual unity of `tool-registration-lifetime.md`'s snapshot/restore section while making the activation condition syntactically visible. Choose Option B over B' only if a contributor is independently editing `version-bump-step2.md` item (e) (then the marginal cost of co-locating is low and the conceptual coupling argument wins). Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- On Option A, after the move the trailing concession sentence on `session-shutdown-semantics.md` becomes redundant and MUST be deleted in the same edit; otherwise the page is left with a forward link to its own removed paragraph.
- On Option A, `cka-30`'s closing leaf is `V9g` but the relocated pin's natural closer is `V9b`. Either widen `cka-23` to name it or open a new `cka-<n>` row; do not leave the pin un-anchored under any row.
- On Option B / B', the anchor `#recovery-mutex-runtime-contract` is referenced from `version-bump-step2.md` (and possibly other places); `grep -rn '#recovery-mutex-runtime-contract' docs/` before moving to confirm the full inbound set.
- Neither option touches the FIFO / `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS` semantics themselves; this is placement / framing only. The behavioural prose ships verbatim.
