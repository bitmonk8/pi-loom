## Problem

The seam preamble at `id="loom-1-0-seam-binder-refinement-loop"` in `binder-bypass-and-envelope.md` coins "carriers" for the three BNDR-1/BNDR-2/BNDR-3 seam obligations without defining the term. "Carrier" already carries an unrelated glossary meaning (`query-terminating-arm carrier`), so a reader hits an undefined, overloaded coinage.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 7a6cf96 — pi-loom spec: resolve "V1 seam-preservation MUSTs hidden inside the deferred-features narrative page" (2026-05-07, Thomas Andersen); f5e89f4 — docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set (2026-06-04, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The "carrier" coinage entered at 7a6cf96 as "V1 carriers" when the deferred seam-preservation MUSTs were lifted into their own normative obligations; the V1→loom 1.0 prose rename (a6d82db, 2026-05-29) rephrased it to "loom 1.0 carriers" without defining it, and f5e89f4 carried it verbatim into the new `binder/` subtree. The `# Defaulting system note echo` title was authored fresh at f5e89f4, the commit that split the monolithic binder spec into the `binder/` cluster under the size-cap enforcement and created `defaulting-system-note-echo.md`. The two defects have distinct single-commit origins and do not interact.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the `id="loom-1-0-seam-binder-refinement-loop"` seam preamble to drop the undefined "carriers" coinage, referring to the three seam obligations by their BNDR-1, BNDR-2, BNDR-3 REQ-IDs directly so the referent is explicit and no new term is introduced.
