## Problem

`V4e`'s `ERR-16` arm is the slash-load `params` arm of ceiling #4 and is the live load-time site that wraps that ceiling's depth breach. The loom-owned depth walk that detects the depth-6 breach and computes the per-boundary routing decision is owned by `V5e`, which is decision-only for the slash-load row and explicitly delegates the actual wrapping to `V4e`'s slash-load cross-route — so `V4e` consumes `V5e`'s depth-detection output. But `V4e` Deps and `V4e-T` Deps name neither `V5e` nor `V5d`, and none of their declared deps transitively reach `V5e`. A literal build of the dependency graph can therefore schedule `V4e` before `V5e` exists, leaving `ERR-16` with no depth-detection source; the leaf also does not state whether the breach comes from `V5e`'s live walk or a synthetic signal injected at `V4e`'s load-time site, so two implementers would resolve the gap differently.

The consumption relationship was subsequently deepened without adding the reverse edge: `e2b7e81` added the `V16a` "consults" Adds language and the `V16a` dep, and `3fa39a9` ("V5e per-boundary routing test asserts destination error surfaces its Deps cannot reach") made `V5e`'s slash-load row decision-only and delegated the wrapping to "the slash-load cross-route at `V4e`." The defect is the original authoring of `V4e`, not a later regression.

## Solution approach

Add `V5e` to both `V4e` Deps and `V4e-T` Deps so the depth-walk producer is scheduled before `V4e` in build order. Name the consumed `V5e` depth-walk output in `V4e` Adds — matching how `V4e` already names the `V16a` seam — so `ERR-16` drives `V5e`'s live depth-walk emission rather than a synthetic load-time signal.

## Solution constraints

- Keep the `V4e` and `V4e-T` Deps lists identical apart from `V4e-T`'s self-reference (paired-leaf convention).
- This fix is plan-only; the spec is read-only.
