## Finding

In `provider-error-mapping.md` § **Stop-reason classification**, the catch-all bullet reads:

> Any other non-terminal stop reason — including a content-filter terminator, or any stop reason the runtime does not otherwise recognise — maps to `TransportError` with `retryable: false`.

The qualifier "non-terminal" admits at least three readings, none of which the surrounding prose pins:

1. **Not a turn-boundary stop reason** (i.e. not one of the `end_turn` / `stop` / `tool_use` values the tool loop treats as a normal boundary, per the section's intro paragraph). Under this reading the catch-all *includes* `"length"`, which is already handled by the bullet immediately above — the two bullets then overlap on `"length"`, and a reader must invoke implicit specific-over-general dispatch to keep the mapping deterministic.
2. **Not a truncation/output-boundary terminator** (i.e. not `"length"`). This is the disjoint-from-the-preceding-bullet reading and is almost certainly what the author meant, but the spec never says "terminal = `length`".
3. **Standard formal usage** (a terminator that does not end the response), in which case "non-terminal stop reason" is internally contradictory — a stop reason *is* a terminator by construction.

Because the section's intent is that the two bullets are disjoint and jointly cover every HTTP-200 stop reason outside the turn-boundary set, the cleanest fix is to drop the loaded word "non-terminal" entirely and name the excluded sets directly: `"length"` (covered by the preceding bullet) and the turn-boundary reasons (covered by the tool loop, not by this section). The catch-all then reads as a simple set complement and the reader does not have to reverse-engineer which sense of "terminal" was meant.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `provider-error-mapping.md` § **Stop-reason classification**, replace the second bullet's lead clause

> Any other non-terminal stop reason — including a content-filter terminator, or any stop reason the runtime does not otherwise recognise — maps to `TransportError` with `retryable: false` …

with wording that names the two excluded sets explicitly rather than leaning on "non-terminal", e.g.

> Any stop reason other than `"length"` (covered by the bullet above) and the `end_turn` / `stop` / `tool_use` turn-boundary reasons (handled by the tool loop, per the section intro) — including a content-filter terminator, or any stop reason the runtime does not otherwise recognise — maps to `TransportError` with `retryable: false` …

Keep the parenthetical justification ("the terminator is a definite outcome of the request, not a transient condition — the same rationale the **`TransportError.retryable` population** rule above applies to an HTTP-200 body-envelope error") verbatim; only the lead clause is reworded. No anchors, normative mapping, or cross-references in other files need to change — `query-tool-loop.md`'s forward-reference and `query-failure-and-repair.md`'s co-located clean-`length` note both already point at the subsection by anchor.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader who adopts reading (1) above can convince themselves the catch-all overlaps the `"length"` bullet and may add an explicit "if not already matched above" guard the spec never asks for; a reader who adopts reading (3) may conclude the bullet is dead code and elide the content-filter / unrecognised-reason mapping. In practice the explicit `"length"` bullet above wins by specificity, so a working classifier can still be produced, but conformance test authors and reviewers waste cycles reconciling the wording before they can write the test.
