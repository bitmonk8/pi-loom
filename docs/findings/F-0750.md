## Problem

In `provider-error-mapping.md` § **Stop-reason classification** (anchor `stop-reason-classification`), the second/catch-all bullet's lead clause qualifies its scope with "non-terminal stop reason". That qualifier admits multiple incompatible readings: it can mean "not a turn-boundary reason" (which then overlaps the preceding `"length"` bullet on `"length"`), "not `"length"`" (the disjoint reading the section's intent requires), or the formal sense in which a stop reason cannot be "non-terminal" at all. The section intends the two bullets to be disjoint and to jointly cover every HTTP-200 stop reason outside the turn-boundary set, but the wording never pins which sense is meant, so conformance-test authors and reviewers must reverse-engineer it.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commit:** `ef4cfb8` — *pi-loom spec: resolve "Stop-reason → QueryError variant mapping is undefined"* (2026-06-07)
**History:** The **Stop-reason classification** subsection (anchor `stop-reason-classification`) did not exist before `ef4cfb8`; that commit added both bullets in their current form as the resolution of T083, including the phrase "Any other non-terminal stop reason". No later commit touches the bullet (`git log -S 'non-terminal' -- docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/provider-error-mapping.md` returns only `ef4cfb8`). The wording entered with the subsection and has not been revised since.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the catch-all bullet's lead clause in § **Stop-reason classification** (anchor `stop-reason-classification`) to drop "non-terminal" and instead name the excluded sets directly — `"length"` (covered by the preceding bullet) and the `end_turn` / `stop` / `tool_use` turn-boundary reasons (handled by the tool loop, per the section intro) — so the catch-all reads as a plain set complement.
