## Finding

The `strict-capability-absence-pin` sub-paragraph in
`docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-recognised-shapes.md` says
the existing `strict-capability-probe` literal-read assertion "MUST
additionally fail if any reachable `Model<Api>` declaration at the cited
site exposes a `strictCapable` member under **the probed name**". The
phrase "the probed name" is used as if it were a previously-defined term
or a cross-reference, but neither the page itself nor the cited
[step 2(a)](./version-bump-step2.md#bump-step-2-positive) target defines
it, names it, or anchors it. The literal `strictCapable` appears
elsewhere in the same sentence and the surrounding paragraph, so a
careful reader can guess that "the probed name" is `strictCapable` — but
the spec does not say so, and crucially does not say *where the
canonical source of that string is held*.

This matters because `version-bump-triggers.md` step 7 already states
the probe "reads `Model<Api>.strictCapable` by name", and the version-bump
plan leaves (`V18c-T-version-bump-checklist.md`, `V18a-capability-inventory.md`)
already pin that the gate consumes a `probedName` payload field on the
`strict-capability-probe` row of `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY`, owned by
`V18a`. The spec sentence is the only piece of the chain that fails to
name the binding, leaving the assertion's input unanchored in the
normative text.

The defect entered with the sub-paragraph itself: the same commit pins the absence claim, names the citation site for it, and references `strict-capability-probe`'s literal-read assertion — but introduces the bare phrase "the probed name" without an inline definition or a cross-reference anchor.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Edit the `strict-capability-absence-pin` sub-paragraph to anchor "the
probed name" to the `probedName` payload on the `strict-capability-probe`
row of `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` (the row's `path`-exemption already
mentions `probedName` further up in the same file as part of the
"positive-direction-only literal-read entries … retain their
implementation-pinned payload shapes — `literal`, `probedName`,
`packages`, `apiNamespace`" sentence in *Target surface categories*).
Rephrase the operative clause along the lines of:

> "MUST additionally fail if any reachable `Model<Api>` declaration at
> the cited site exposes a member whose name equals the `probedName`
> payload on the `strict-capability-probe` row of `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY`
> (the literal `strictCapable` at the loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin; held in the
> inventory so a Pi/pi-ai rename routes through
> [version-bump-triggers.md step 7](./version-bump-triggers.md) rather
> than through a spec edit)."

Pinning the binding to the inventory row keeps the spec consistent with
the plan's `V18a` payload obligation and with `version-bump-triggers.md`
step 7's "reads `Model<Api>.strictCapable` by name" sentence, while
naming the literal inline so a reader of `audit-recognised-shapes.md`
alone does not have to chase the cross-reference to recover the
behaviour.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The two strict-capability gate arms (rename-detection at
  `version-bump-triggers.md` step 7; absence-under-the-probed-name
  here) MUST consume the same `probedName` payload, not two
  independently-pinned copies of `"strictCapable"`. The phrasing should
  not introduce a second source of the literal.
- The `path`-exemption sentence in *Target surface categories* names
  `probedName` as an implementation-owned payload field. The
  cross-reference here is to that same field — not to a new spec-pinned
  constant — so the "implementation owns the literal kind names and
  per-kind payload field shapes" delegation is preserved.
- The clause currently uses the phrase "exposes a `strictCapable`
  member under the probed name", which is self-contradictory if read
  literally (a `strictCapable` member necessarily has the name
  `strictCapable`). The rewrite should drop the inner `strictCapable`
  occurrence so the gate reads as "exposes a member whose name equals
  the probed name".

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reasonable implementer can recover the intended meaning by reading
`version-bump-triggers.md` step 7 and inferring from the surrounding
sentence that the probed name is `strictCapable`. The risk is that two
implementers diverge on *where the literal lives*: one hard-codes
`"strictCapable"` at the assertion site, another threads it through the
`probedName` payload on the inventory row (which is what the plan
expects). The audit's rename-detection arm (`version-bump-triggers.md`
step 7) and the absence-under-the-probed-name arm pinned here are meant
to read the same constant, so a divergence silently splits the
single-source-of-truth obligation.
