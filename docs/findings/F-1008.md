## Finding

In `query/query-failure-and-repair.md`, the "Non-validation failures during a respond-repair follow-up" section enumerates the proximate `QueryError` variants that can propagate out of a follow-up turn and terminate respond-repair: `transport`, `cancelled`, `model_tool`, `tool_loop_exhausted`, `context_overflow`, `invoke_infra`, `invoke_callee`. Respond-repair only applies to typed queries (it is the schema-validation repair path for the forced respond turn), and a respond-repair follow-up restarts the whole two-phase loop with a fresh `tool_loop` budget — i.e. each follow-up is itself a typed-query execution.

Elsewhere in the same corpus, the typed-query path normatively cannot surface `tool_loop_exhausted`. `query-failure-and-repair.md` §"Forced respond turn non-compliance" states that "Non-compliance does **not** surface as `tool_loop_exhausted`", and `query-tool-loop.md` §"Tool-call loop bound" states that "on a typed query the `max_rounds`-final branch instead dispatches the forced respond turn, so `tool_loop_exhausted` is unreachable on that path." `V6e-respond-repair-tool-loop.md` (`FRNT-1`) carries the same rule. The variant is therefore listed as reachable in the propagation enumeration but normatively unreachable on the very path that enumeration covers; no respond-repair conformance test can construct a `tool_loop_exhausted` propagation case.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `82be125` (2026-05-04, "spec: adopt P1 typed-query two-phase loop (Option A)") — introduced the `tool_loop_exhausted` variant and added it to the coercion-follow-up proximate-propagation list as a then-reachable case (the forced respond turn at that point "counts against the same iteration cap as free-phase turns (one slot, not zero)").
- `22619b0` (2026-05-25, "pi-loom spec: resolve T11d / T11a — forced-respond exempt-routed terminator") — made the forced respond turn the exempt-routed terminator dispatched by CIO-4's `max_rounds`-final branch and added the normative "`tool_loop_exhausted` is unreachable on [the typed] path" sentences in `query-tool-loop.md` §"Tool-call loop bound" and the new §"Forced respond turn non-compliance", but did not prune the variant from the respond-repair propagation enumeration.

**History:** `git log -G "tool_loop_exhausted"` on the predecessor `spec_topics/query.md` and the post-split `docs/spec_topics/query/query-failure-and-repair.md` shows the variant entered the list in `82be125` and the unreachability rule entered in `22619b0`; later renames (`8455d5e` invoke-variant wire-kind rename; `ff9986d` similarly) re-touched the list without re-evaluating membership. The defect is the interaction between the two architectural commits, not either one alone.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Remove `tool_loop_exhausted` from the proximate-propagation variant list in `query-failure-and-repair.md` §"Non-validation failures during a respond-repair follow-up". The remaining list (`transport`, `cancelled`, `model_tool`, `context_overflow`, `invoke_infra`, `invoke_callee`) is exactly the set reachable on a typed-query follow-up under the exempt-routed-terminator rule, and `query-tool-loop.md` already pins that rule. Do not add a "reserved" / "forward" parenthetical — it would re-establish the same misleading suggestion that a future revision might make the variant reachable on respond-repair, when the exempt-routed-terminator design is structural rather than provisional. The accompanying "Edge cases" sentence ("a follow-up's *own* tool-call loop may fail with `model_tool` mid-loop …") already names the only in-loop-failure variant that does propagate; no edit is needed there.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A normative enumeration carries a dead variant that two implementers reading only this section could believe must be tested, contradicting the same file's "Forced respond turn non-compliance" rule and `query-tool-loop.md`'s tool-loop-bound rule. Behaviour does not diverge — both rules pin unreachability — but a conformance test author wastes effort attempting to construct the case, and the inconsistency invites the spec to drift back to a reachable interpretation under a future edit.
