## Finding

The single bullet anchored at `#strict-capability-absence-pin` in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-target-categories.md` packs three distinct obligations into a file whose remit is the closed enumeration of category-(1)/(2)/(3) surfaces the inventory-closure audit covers:

1. A binder-runtime fact — that under the loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin, `Model<Api>.strictCapable` is absent on every Pi-supplied instance, so the duck-typed probe in `binder/binder-model-and-context.md` degrades unconditionally to the universal-`W` branch surfaced as `loom/load/binder-model-strict-capability-unknown`.
2. An anchor-role claim — that this bullet is the "single citation site" every other "absent / universal under the pin" reference to `strictCapable` (the `bind_model` row in `frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-a.md`, the strict-capability requirement in `binder/binder-model-and-context.md`, the `binder-model-strict-capability-unknown` diagnostic row in `diagnostics/code-registry-load.md`) must cite.
3. An extension of the SDK surface-inventory literal-read assertion — a MUST that the `strict-capability-probe` entry's literal-read additionally fail if a reachable `Model<Api>` declaration exposes a `strictCapable` member under the probed name. This is the *absence-under-the-probed-name* arm that pairs with the rename-detection arm owned by `version-bump-triggers.md` step 7, and the plan leaf `V18c` lists both arms as the gate's two negative fixtures.

Obligations (1) and (3) are subject-matter foreign to *Target surface categories*. The page's remit is purely surface taxonomy — defining the closed category-(1)/(2)/(3) sets and the `path`-resolution rules a static-AST walker uses. The strict-capability runtime degradation belongs in the binder section that owns the probe; the literal-read assertion arm belongs with the other inventory / literal-read content (in `inventory-audit-intro.md` or with the `strict-capability-probe` payload pinned at `capability-probe.md` step (d)/(e)). What does belong on this page is the narrow note that the `strict-capability-probe` entry kind is `path`-exempt — that is a category-taxonomy claim. Because the bullet's three obligations were welded together at authorship, every cross-reference into `strictCapable` (binder, frontmatter, diagnostics, plan `V18c`) now anchors into an audit-categories file, masking where the binder behaviour and the audit-side rule actually live.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Split `#strict-capability-absence-pin` into three pieces, placed where each obligation's subject lives:

1. **Binder-runtime fact and anchor-role declaration → `binder/binder-model-and-context.md`.** Append a clause to the existing *Strict-capability requirement* paragraph that already documents the three-valued probe (`true` / `false` / `undefined`): under the loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin, `Model<Api>.strictCapable` is absent on every Pi-supplied instance, so production behaviour is the universal-`W` branch `loom/load/binder-model-strict-capability-unknown`. Move the `<a id="strict-capability-absence-pin"></a>` anchor to this clause and make it the single citation site for the absence claim. The page already owns the probe contract; co-locating the pin restores the natural reading path (probe rule → behaviour under the pin) and lets the diagnostics row, frontmatter row, and `V11a` plan leaf cite the binder page directly instead of round-tripping through an audit-categories file.

2. **Literal-read assertion extension → `inventory-audit-intro.md` (preferred) or `capability-probe.md` step (d)/(e) area.** Move the MUST that "the existing `strict-capability-probe` literal-read assertion (per step 2(a)) MUST additionally fail if any reachable `Model<Api>` declaration at the cited site exposes a `strictCapable` member under the probed name" into the inventory hub — it pairs with the rename-detection arm owned by `version-bump-triggers.md` step 7 and is consumed by `V18c`'s two negative fixtures. The inventory hub is the natural home because it already enumerates `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY`'s relationship to the literal-read assertion and to the version-bump procedure. (Co-locating on `capability-probe.md` is acceptable if the editor prefers to keep all `strict-capability-probe` payload pins — `probedName`, `path`-exemption, and the absence arm — on one page.) Sweep `docs/plan_topics/V18c-version-bump-checklist.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V18c-T-version-bump-checklist.md` to re-point their `strict-capability-absence-pin` citations at the new home.

3. **`path`-exemption retention → stays in `audit-target-categories.md`.** Keep on this page only the narrow note that the `strict-capability-probe` inventory entry kind is `path`-exempt (one of the four kinds exempt from the audit-participating minimum-shape obligation). That claim is on-topic for *Target surface categories* because it is about which inventory kinds participate in surface resolution. Either fold the note into the existing exemption sentence on this page (which already names the four `path`-exempt kinds — `node-floor`, `strict-capability-probe`, `peer-dep-range`, `api-coverage`), or retain a one-line anchor stub that cross-references the binder and inventory-hub homes for the other two obligations.

Sweep all citations of `audit-target-categories.md#strict-capability-absence-pin` (presently in `binder/binder-model-and-context.md`, `frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-a.md`, `diagnostics/code-registry-load.md`, `docs/plan_topics/V18c-version-bump-checklist.md`, `docs/plan_topics/V18c-T-version-bump-checklist.md`) to point at the new homes — most at the binder page for the absence pin, the V18c plan leaves at the inventory hub for the literal-read arm. Preserve the `strict-capability-absence-pin` anchor identifier verbatim on whichever page owns the absence claim (the binder page under recommendation 1), so external citations remain stable; the audit-target-categories.md retention sentence does not need to keep the anchor.

Edge case: the bullet's "single citation site" framing for the absence claim is a deliberate dedupe discipline. The split MUST preserve that — the binder page becomes the new single site, and the diagnostics and frontmatter rows continue to cite the absence claim rather than restate it. If the editor instead drops the dedupe rule and lets each citing site restate "absent under the pin" inline, that is a separate decision that bypasses the split altogether and should be made explicitly rather than as a side-effect of moving the anchor.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Behaviour is fully specified and cross-references resolve, so an implementer following the chain ends up at the same MUSTs regardless of placement. The cost is discoverability and audit-surface bloat: a reader of `audit-target-categories.md` who wants the closed set of category surfaces wades through a bullet about binder runtime degradation; a reader of `binder/binder-model-and-context.md` does not find the universal-`W`-under-the-pin pin co-located with the probe it governs; the V18c gate cites the *absence-under-the-probed-name* arm via a file whose body is unrelated to literal-read assertions. The defect compounds with the recent `audit-recognised-shapes.md` ↔ `audit-target-categories.md` body-realignment (F-0958), which moved the bullet across files without addressing its internal-subject mismatch with either home.
