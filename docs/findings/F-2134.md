## Finding

The second paragraph of `diagnostic-shape.md` ("Persistent diagnostics (default)") carries a factory-lifecycle sequencing rule mid-sentence: *"The renderer MUST be registered synchronously inside the extension factory **before** the first discovery scan kicks off, so the first batch of scan diagnostics renders through the loom-specific renderer rather than as raw fallback text."* This is an extension-bootstrap registration-ordering constraint on the factory lifecycle. The diagnostic-shape page's role is to define the `loom-system-note` wire format, the delivery sink, and the fallback chain — not to prescribe when the renderer is registered relative to the discovery scan.

The canonical home for renderer-registration timing already exists: `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md` → **Renderer registration** → *Lifecycle* → **Registration timing** states *"The loom extension factory MUST call `pi.registerMessageRenderer("loom-system-note", renderer)` synchronously inside the factory body, before the factory returns."* So the obligation lives in two files with two different boundary phrasings — "before the first discovery scan kicks off" (diagnostic-shape) versus "before the factory returns" (PIC). The two are not obviously equivalent: whether the first discovery scan fires inside the factory body or only after it returns (via the `resources_discover` subscription) is not pinned here, so a reader of one page cannot tell whether it states a stronger, weaker, or identical boundary than the other.

The fix is editorial relocation plus consolidation: the timing MUST (and its rationale about the first scan batch rendering through the loom renderer) belongs in the PIC **Registration timing** bullet, with `diagnostic-shape.md` reduced to a cross-reference. Consolidating also forces the boundary to be pinned once, removing the latent two-phrasing divergence.

**Introducing commits:** `9eea3d9` (2026-05-06, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"pi.registerMessageRenderer signature, return type, and re-registration semantics not pinned\""); seeded by `ac8c3af` (2026-05-04, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"LoadExtensionsResult.errors is the wrong delivery channel for loom diagnostics\"")
**History:** `git log -G "first discovery scan kicks off"` shows the timing sentence ("The renderer MUST be registered synchronously inside the extension factory before the first discovery scan kicks off") first entered the diagnostics page at `ac8c3af` (then `spec_topics/diagnostics.md`, later split to `diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md`). At `ac8c3af^` the Pi Integration Contract had no renderer-registration-timing home (`git show ac8c3af^:spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` contains no "Registration timing" bullet). The canonical PIC **Registration timing** bullet for `pi.registerMessageRenderer` ("synchronously inside the factory body, before the factory returns") was introduced two days later at `9eea3d9` (`git log -G "synchronously inside the factory body"`), without removing or consolidating the earlier diagnostics-page statement. The misplacement-plus-divergence therefore became a real defect at `9eea3d9`, when the authoritative home appeared while the duplicate remained.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Make `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md` → **Renderer registration** → *Lifecycle* → **Registration timing** the sole owner of the renderer-registration-ordering rule. Move the discovery-scan-ordering obligation and its rationale ("so the first batch of scan diagnostics renders through the loom-specific renderer rather than as raw fallback text") into that bullet, and replace the sentence in `diagnostic-shape.md` with a cross-reference, e.g. "Registration timing is specified in [Pi Integration Contract — Renderer registration](../pi-integration-contract/extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md#renderer-registration)."

When consolidating, pin the boundary once so the two former phrasings cannot diverge: state explicitly whether the binding constraint is "before the factory returns" or the stronger "before the first discovery scan," and if the latter, fix which concrete call/event begins the first scan (factory-body discovery walk vs. the post-return `resources_discover` firing — see `registration-steps.md`). The consolidated text must preserve the stronger of the two boundaries so the first scan batch is guaranteed to render through the loom renderer.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A bootstrap sequencing rule living in the wire-format page is easy to miss when implementing the factory and is stated with a different boundary phrasing than its PIC home, so two implementers reading different pages can adopt different registration boundaries. A working system still results, but if the renderer is registered after the first scan diagnostics are emitted, that first batch renders as raw fallback text instead of through the loom renderer.
