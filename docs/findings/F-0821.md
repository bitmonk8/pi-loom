## Finding

Item (e) of the editorial-review checklist uses the word "baseline" with two distinct referents inside a single bullet. The first referent is the **canonical `from` side of every `dist/*.js` diff on the page**, established under the labelled paragraph `<a id="bump-baseline-acquisition"></a>` ("**Baseline acquisition (the canonical 'from' side for every `dist/*.js` diff on this page)**") and reinforced by the next paragraph ("the canonical baseline — i.e. the 'from' side of the diff — is that file in the extracted `package/dist/` tree the [Baseline acquisition](#bump-baseline-acquisition) paragraph above materialises …"). The second referent appears a few sentences later inside the fail-predicate's first bullet, in the parenthetical "(the comparator baseline — a per-session FIFO whose dequeue body is itself `await`ed end-to-end satisfies the non-overlap property and is a pass …)" — here "baseline" names **a reference example of a passing queue shape** against which other queue shapes in the surrounding enumeration are being judged. The two meanings are not subtly related: one is an extracted SDK tarball tree, the other is an exemplar code pattern.

The collision matters because item (e) is dense procedural prose with an anchor link (`#bump-baseline-acquisition`) elevating "Baseline" to a stable inline label. A reader who has just absorbed that label and then encounters "the comparator baseline" inside the queue-shape examples has to actively suppress the just-pinned referent to make sense of the sentence, and a follow-up reader doing an anchor search for "baseline" will land on the wrong sentence with no signal that the term has been overloaded. The fix is local — rename the worked-example use so the page reserves "baseline" for the `from` side of the diff.

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `57d8235` (2026-05-11, "pi-loom spec: resolve 'T23 per-session slash-handler serialisation citation'") established the "comparator baseline" wording; `ccc6e38` (2026-06-07, "pi-loom spec: resolve 'Item (e)/(r) dist/*.js baseline source unspecified'") added the labelled **Baseline acquisition** paragraph and the `bump-baseline-acquisition` anchor inside the same bullet, creating the overload.

The defect is the interaction of the two edits — neither commit on its own is the source of the overload — so the durable fix targets the term, not either commit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In item (e)'s fail-predicate first bullet, replace the parenthetical phrase "the comparator baseline —" with "a reference example of a passing queue shape —". This keeps the worked-example pattern (a per-session FIFO whose dequeue body is itself `await`ed end-to-end) intact while reserving the word "baseline" for the `bump-baseline-acquisition` referent across the page.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The rename is purely lexical inside item (e); do not touch the `<a id="bump-baseline-acquisition"></a>` anchor, the labelled paragraph's title, or the "canonical baseline — i.e. the 'from' side of the diff" wording in the diff-instruction paragraph — those uses are the protected referent.
- Re-check `version-bump-step2.md` for any other lower-case "baseline" usage that names a passing-example pattern rather than the `dist/*.js` `from` tree; `grep -n -i baseline docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md` currently shows the diff-side referent in the Baseline-acquisition paragraph, the canonical-baseline reference under item (e)'s diff instruction, and the `[Baseline acquisition](#bump-baseline-acquisition)` cross-references from items (r) and (aj) — all of those are the protected referent and should be left alone. The "comparator baseline" parenthetical is the sole offender; if a future reader-comprehension sweep surfaces a second worked-example use, apply the same rename pattern.
- Do not propagate the rename into `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` history files or any other archive; the live spec is the only edit site.
