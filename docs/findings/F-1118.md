## Finding

The Dispatch-site setup wrap closes with:

> A throw from `loomAbort.abort()` invoked by the `catch` arm during cleanup of a half-constructed entry is itself swallowed by the per-entry `try`/`catch` of the **`session_shutdown`** sub-step 2 rule above and MUST NOT mask the original setup throw.

The `session_shutdown` sub-step-2 per-entry `try`/`catch` (in `patch-skew-degradation.md`) is a guard wrapped around the *teardown handler's* iteration over the registry; it executes only when Pi fires `session_shutdown` and the handler iterates entries. It is not in the call stack of the dispatch-site setup-wrap's `catch` arm, which runs synchronously at invocation entry — typically with no `session_shutdown` even in progress, and in any case on a different async context than the teardown handler. A throw from `loomAbort.abort()` raised inside the dispatch-site `catch` cannot reach that guard; it propagates out of the `catch` arm and masks the original setup throw the arm was trying to route through the runtime-defect surface.

The behaviour the paragraph clearly intends is local: when the setup-wrap's `catch` calls `loomAbort.abort()` on the partially-constructed `AbortController` as part of its own cleanup, an abort-throw must not displace the captured original exception that the same `catch` is preparing to route to `loom/runtime/internal-error` / `InvokeInfraError { cause: "internal_error", ... }`. That guarantee requires a `try`/`catch` co-located in the dispatch-site `catch` arm itself — there is no other `try`/`catch` in scope at that point.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Rewrite the closing sentence of the **Dispatch-site setup wrap** bullet as a local MUST on the `catch` arm: the arm MUST wrap any cleanup `loomAbort.abort()` call in its own `try`/`catch`, MUST silently drop a throw from that nested call, and MUST then rethrow / route the originally captured setup throw to the runtime-defect surface unchanged. Drop the cross-reference to `session_shutdown` sub-step 2 — that guard is unrelated and naming it here is what produced the architectural mis-routing in the first place.

Implementer edge cases:

- The nested `catch` discards its throw; no diagnostic, no log line — symmetric to sub-step 2's "abort is best-effort" rule. The original setup throw is the single surfaced cause.
- The nested cleanup must guard only the `abort()` call; do not extend it to cover the listener-detach / barrier-settle steps the per-invocation `finally` already owns, since on a throw before `Set.add` no entry exists for `finally` to act on and on a throw after `Set.add` the per-invocation `finally` runs normally.
- The rule applies on all three insertion sites (slash-command handler, `tool.execute(...)` adapter, `invoke` spawn site) because the wrap is shared across all three; no per-site variation.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

An implementer who reads the rule literally adds no local guard and assumes some `session_shutdown` handler catches the abort throw; one who reads it for intent adds a local `try`/`catch`. The two implementations diverge on the visible failure: in the first, a half-constructed entry whose `AbortController.abort()` throws (e.g. an abort listener that synchronously throws) surfaces that abort-throw to the runtime instead of the original setup defect, hiding the actual cause from `loom/runtime/internal-error.error.message` / `.error.stack` and from the slash-command system note.
