## Problem

The **Dispatch-site setup wrap** bullet closes by asserting that a throw from `loomAbort.abort()`, raised by the setup-wrap's `catch` arm while cleaning up a half-constructed entry, "is itself swallowed by the per-entry `try`/`catch` of the `session_shutdown` sub-step 2 rule above". That sub-step-2 guard wraps the teardown handler's iteration over the registry; it runs only when Pi fires `session_shutdown` and on a different async context than the dispatch-site `catch` arm, which executes synchronously at invocation entry. An abort-throw raised inside the dispatch-site `catch` cannot reach that guard — it propagates out of the `catch` arm and masks the original setup throw the arm was preparing to route to the runtime-defect surface, hiding the actual cause from `loom/runtime/internal-error`.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the closing sentence of the **Dispatch-site setup wrap** bullet as a local MUST on the `catch` arm itself: the arm wraps its cleanup `loomAbort.abort()` call in its own `try`/`catch`, drops a throw from that nested call, and then routes the originally captured setup throw to the runtime-defect surface (`loom/runtime/internal-error` / `InvokeInfraError { cause: "internal_error" }`) unchanged. Delete the cross-reference to the `session_shutdown` sub-step 2 guard — that guard is unrelated to the dispatch-site `catch`.
