## Finding

The session-only degraded-state branch (`event.reason ∈ {"new","resume","fork"}`) presupposes that Pi keeps the same extension instance loaded across the swap and continues to route slash invocations on the swapped-in session to it, so the closed-over `LoomRegistry` the slash handler reads — drained flag and `drainStateTag` included — survives across the boundary and produces the operator-visible `"loom /<name>: extension degraded; /reload to recover"` note. Pi's published lifecycle documents the opposite at the loom 1.0 SDK pin: `@earendil-works/pi-coding-agent` `docs/extensions.md` ("Session replacement lifecycle and footguns") states that on `/new`, `/resume`, and `/fork` Pi fires `session_shutdown` for the old instance, **tears the old runtime down and reloads-and-rebinds extensions for the new session**, then fires `session_start`; captured old `pi` / old command `ctx` are stale and throw if used. The project's CLAUDE.md no-globals/statics/singletons rule and PIC's DI/closure routing rule out a process-global registry that could survive the rebind.

`host-prerequisites.md` clause (a) acknowledges this is an *open contradiction at the pin, not an unpinned presupposition*, and states that "the conflict MUST be resolved before SM-4 / SM-5 / SM-6 (and SM-3b) … are implemented." The resolution itself, however, is tracked only as one bullet in the multi-page version-bump editorial-review checklist (`version-bump-step2.md` item (a), `#bump-checklist-instance-survival`) — a procedure that fires on each Pi minor bump, not as a standing open question. Meanwhile the SM-4, SM-5, SM-6, and SM-3b sub-units in `session-model-and-appendix.md` are written as ordinary normative obligations with no contingency marker, no link to the open question, no Future-Considerations / open-questions entry, and no visual cue that they may be unreachable as written. An implementer reading the session-model file in isolation — exactly the orientation surface those entries advertise themselves as — has no in-prose signal that the obligations they are about to implement are blocked. The plan already treats this asymmetry as load-bearing: `V9l` and `V9l-T` are listed in `plan.md` §Blocked obligations and explicitly cite a pre-designed fallback ("if … resolves as *branch unreachable as written*, `V9l` instead collapses to full teardown + `/reload` with no degraded tag write"). The spec carries none of that visibility on the obligation pages themselves.

The branch is therefore not implementable as written, and the spec's own quoted "MUST be resolved before SM-4/5/6 are implemented" gate is not surfaced where SM-4/5/6 are defined.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `52b9101` (2026-05-11, "T24 — Fork-reason watcher closure leaves the extension in an unspecified, silently degraded state") — authored the session-only degraded-state branch, the watcher-closure-then-survival framing, and the host-prerequisites paragraph against an unverified instance-survival premise. The contradiction with Pi's `docs/extensions.md` was already documented at this commit but not surfaced by it.
- `40ffdc5` (2026-05-27, "T19a — Replace session-model paragraph with eight SM-N sub-units") — authored the SM-4 / SM-5 / SM-6 / SM-3b sub-units in `session-model-and-appendix.md` as ordinary normative obligations, with no contingency marker against the (already-latent) contradiction.
- `bfef26e` (2026-06-05, "Per-event.reason session lifecycle asserted as fact with no probe or cited contract") — expanded the degraded-state prerequisites from three to four behaviours (added clause (d), the teardown-vs-session-swap partition assignment) but left clause (a) as an ordinary unpinned presupposition.
- `7447d1e` (2026-06-10, "Session-only degraded-state presupposition (a) contradicts Pi's documented lifecycle") — recognised the contradiction in clause (a) and re-classified it as an *open contradiction at the pin* (not a future-minor presupposition), added the `bump-checklist-instance-survival` sub-anchor, and added the "MUST be resolved before SM-4/5/6 are implemented" sentence. This commit acknowledged the gate but parked it solely on the version-bump checklist; SM-4/5/6 and `session-only-degraded-state.md` were not touched, and no open-questions / Future-Considerations entry was added.

**History:** The defect is the residue of an incomplete mitigation across three editorial waves. The original branch was authored against an unverified premise (52b9101); the obligation sites were authored in the orientation surface (40ffdc5); only later (7447d1e) was the contradiction recognised, and the recognition was placed only at the premise site and on the bump checklist — not propagated to the obligation sites or to any standing open-questions surface. The defect is the interaction of those three waves, not the local mistake of any single commit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Resolve the determination now and rewrite the branch

**Approach.** Perform the authoritative determination clause (a) requires: read the package-loaded `extensions/index.ts` lifecycle path in `@earendil-works/pi-coding-agent`'s `dist/` (auto-discovered from `package.json#pi.extensions`, distributed via `pi install`) and decide whether it is governed by the same teardown-and-rebind lifecycle `docs/extensions.md` documents, or whether it provably differs for the session-only reasons. Then apply the pre-designed fallback the plan already cites — if branch is unreachable, collapse `session-only-degraded-state.md`, SM-4, SM-5, SM-6, and SM-3b to "full teardown + `/reload` with no degraded tag write" and remove the `markRuntimeDegraded` / `"degraded-needs-reload"` machinery; if branch provably differs, replace the open-contradiction framing in clause (a) with a positively-pinned behavioural-source citation and keep the rest.

**Spec edits.** Rewrite `session-only-degraded-state.md` end-to-end on the chosen resolution; rewrite SM-4/5/6 (and SM-3b's PIC link) accordingly in `session-model-and-appendix.md`; rewrite clause (a) of `host-prerequisites.md#degraded-state-host-prerequisites`; rewrite `version-bump-step2.md` checklist item (a) from "open question" to either a deleted entry (branch unreachable, nothing to re-audit) or an ordinary per-bump re-audit on the same footing as clauses (b)–(d).

**Pros.** Closes the contradiction at its source; removes the residual gap entirely; unblocks `V9l` / `V9l-T`; makes the bump checklist consistent with its sibling items.

**Cons.** Requires the determination work the spec has so far deferred — reading `dist/` code or making a contributor commitment about the package-loaded path; the determination's evidentiary standard is itself the subject of a sibling finding ("degraded-state determination has no defined evidentiary standard"), so Option A inherits that uncertainty.

**Risks.** If the determination misreads `dist/`, the rewrite locks in the wrong behaviour and the next Pi minor surfaces the error as a real bug rather than a spec gap.

### Option B — Raise the gate at the obligation sites without resolving

**Approach.** Leave the determination open (it stays parked on the bump checklist) but propagate the gate's visibility to every site where the blocked obligations are advertised: add a contingency marker (e.g. a one-line "**Implementation gate** — blocked on [host-prerequisites clause (a)](…); see also [`plan.md` §Blocked obligations](…)") at SM-4 in `session-model-and-appendix.md`, with SM-5, SM-6, and SM-3b carrying inherited cross-links since their normative contracts are owned by `session-only-degraded-state.md`; add a banner at the top of `session-only-degraded-state.md` flagging the entire page as gated; add a standing open-questions entry under `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md` (or wherever the corpus' open-questions surface lives) so the question has a permanent home that is not buried in a bump-procedure checklist.

**Spec edits.** Add a contingency block at SM-4 and pointers from SM-5 / SM-6 / SM-3b in `session-model-and-appendix.md`; add a top-of-page implementation-gate banner in `session-only-degraded-state.md`; add an open-questions / non-goals-adjacent entry in `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md` cross-linking clause (a). No prose deletion or rewrite of the degraded-state contract itself.

**Pros.** Cheap; immediately closes the implementer-blindness defect; preserves all current prose so a later Option-A resolution does not need to re-author anything; mirrors the plan-side visibility (`V9l` in §Blocked obligations) the spec currently lacks.

**Cons.** The underlying contradiction remains open; readers who reach the contingency block still have to follow links to learn what is blocked and why; the bump-checklist item (a) stays unusual (an "open question" rather than a per-bump re-audit).

**Risks.** A visible-gate banner that stays in place for many releases becomes background noise and stops being read; this option needs a follow-up commitment to Option A on a defined cadence, not an indefinite parking.

### Recommendation

Take **Option B immediately, then Option A as the durable fix.** Option B is essentially free, dissolves the implementer-blindness defect, and brings the spec's visibility into lock-step with the plan's existing `§Blocked obligations` treatment of `V9l`. Option A is the right destination but requires the determination work and the evidentiary-standard work (see the related "degraded-state determination has no defined evidentiary standard" finding) to land safely; sequencing it after Option B means the determination can take the time it needs without leaving the SM-4/5/6 reader exposed in the meantime.

Edge cases the implementer must watch when authoring Option B:

- The contingency block at SM-4 must inherit visibly into SM-5 and SM-6 (which are written as forward-references into the same `session-only-degraded-state.md` contract) — not just sit on SM-4 in isolation.
- The open-questions entry should cite both clause (a) and `bump-checklist-instance-survival` so a reader arriving from either site reaches the other.
- The banner on `session-only-degraded-state.md` must not duplicate clause (a)'s prose — it is a pointer, not a restatement, to avoid the divergence risk the corpus' decision-log discipline already flags elsewhere.
- When Option A eventually lands and resolves the question, every Option-B marker (contingency blocks, banner, open-questions entry) must be retracted in the same commit; leave a TODO at each so the retraction is one grep away.

## Consequence

**Severity:** blocking

An implementer who picks SM-4 / SM-5 / SM-6 / SM-3b off the session-model page — the page that names itself the spec's session-level contract surface — will author normative behaviour against a premise Pi's own lifecycle documentation contradicts at the pinned minor. The plan side already pre-empts that by marking `V9l` / `V9l-T` blocked, but nothing on the spec side makes the implementer reach the plan before authoring; the spec page they read is silent on the gate, and the gate that does exist is two pages and one cross-page navigation away on a version-bump checklist. Until the question is closed (or the gate is raised), every downstream reader bears the cost of independently re-deriving the contradiction.
