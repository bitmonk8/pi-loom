## Problem

The SM-4, SM-5, SM-6, and SM-3b sub-units in `session-model-and-appendix.md` are written as ordinary normative obligations, yet their session-only degraded-state premise — that Pi keeps the same extension instance loaded across a `{"new","resume","fork"}` session swap — is an open contradiction against Pi's documented teardown-and-rebind lifecycle, recognised in `host-prerequisites.md` clause (a) under `#degraded-state-host-prerequisites`. That clause's own gate ("MUST be resolved before SM-4 / SM-5 / SM-6 … are implemented") is surfaced only at the premise site and as one bullet on the per-minor version-bump editorial-review checklist (`version-bump-step2.md` item `#bump-checklist-instance-survival`), not at the obligation sites where SM-4/5/6/3b are defined, and there is no standing open-questions entry. An implementer reading the session-model orientation page in isolation has no in-prose signal that the obligations are blocked.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `52b9101` (2026-05-11, "T24 — Fork-reason watcher closure leaves the extension in an unspecified, silently degraded state") — authored the session-only degraded-state branch, the watcher-closure-then-survival framing, and the host-prerequisites paragraph against an unverified instance-survival premise. The contradiction with Pi's `docs/extensions.md` was already documented at this commit but not surfaced by it.
- `40ffdc5` (2026-05-27, "T19a — Replace session-model paragraph with eight SM-N sub-units") — authored the SM-4 / SM-5 / SM-6 / SM-3b sub-units in `session-model-and-appendix.md` as ordinary normative obligations, with no contingency marker against the (already-latent) contradiction.
- `bfef26e` (2026-06-05, "Per-event.reason session lifecycle asserted as fact with no probe or cited contract") — expanded the degraded-state prerequisites from three to four behaviours (added clause (d), the teardown-vs-session-swap partition assignment) but left clause (a) as an ordinary unpinned presupposition.
- `7447d1e` (2026-06-10, "Session-only degraded-state presupposition (a) contradicts Pi's documented lifecycle") — recognised the contradiction in clause (a) and re-classified it as an *open contradiction at the pin* (not a future-minor presupposition), added the `bump-checklist-instance-survival` sub-anchor, and added the "MUST be resolved before SM-4/5/6 are implemented" sentence. This commit acknowledged the gate but parked it solely on the version-bump checklist; SM-4/5/6 and `session-only-degraded-state.md` were not touched, and no open-questions / Future-Considerations entry was added.

**History:** The defect is the residue of an incomplete mitigation across three editorial waves. The original branch was authored against an unverified premise (52b9101); the obligation sites were authored in the orientation surface (40ffdc5); only later (7447d1e) was the contradiction recognised, and the recognition was placed only at the premise site and on the bump checklist — not propagated to the obligation sites or to any standing open-questions surface. The defect is the interaction of those three waves, not the local mistake of any single commit.

## Solution approach

This is visibility propagation, not a contract rewrite. Add an implementation-gate contingency marker at SM-4 (`#sm-4-session-only-degraded-state`) forward-linking to host-prerequisites clause (a) (`#degraded-state-host-prerequisites`), and add cross-links from SM-5 (`#sm-5-tag-transition-predicate`), SM-6 (`#sm-6-diagnostic-emission-predicate`), and SM-3b (`#sm-3b-session-swap-semantics`) back to that gate. Add a top-of-page implementation-gate banner to `session-only-degraded-state.md` flagging the page as gated. Add a standing open-questions entry in `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md` that forward-links both clause (a) and the `#bump-checklist-instance-survival` checklist item.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: rewriting or deleting the degraded-state contract prose, and resolving the underlying instance-survival determination — this change is visibility-only.
- The page banner and open-questions entry MUST be pointers, not restatements: they MUST NOT duplicate clause (a)'s prose (the corpus' decision-log / single-source discipline).
