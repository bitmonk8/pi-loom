## Finding

The `session_shutdown` teardown contract leans on a specific runtime behaviour of `chokidar`: that calling `discoveryWatcher.close()` (and `settingsWatcher.close()`) a second time on an already-closed watcher is tolerable. Three normative arms re-enter sub-step 4 ("Close watchers") against already-closed handles and accept whatever the second `.close()` does — the *idempotent*-clause **all-three-throw corner case** and the *Read-failure fallback* re-entry in `drain-state-contract.md`, and the drain-throw / read-failure fixtures (branches (j) and (m)) in `session-only-degraded-state.md`. Each pins the outcome on what the spec itself names "chokidar's acknowledged-unpinned double-close behaviour": the per-call `try`/`catch` of *Per-step isolation* routes any double-close throw into one additional `loom/host/session-shutdown-teardown-step-failed` row per failing call site, and no further compensation is specified.

The spec is explicit that this behaviour is unpinned. `implementation-notes.md` declares `chokidar` in the loom package's `dependencies` but states "this clause names the dependency, not its version," and `host-prerequisites.md` contrasts "the version-unpinned `semver` / `chokidar` choices" against loom's TypeScript build toolchain, which *does* carry a load-bearing version floor (`>=5.4.0`) precisely because a build-time gate relies on a specific `tsc` behaviour — and which `host-prerequisites.md` further protects with a re-confirmation obligation on every bump.

The asymmetry is the defect. `tsc`'s load-bearing behaviour is both pinned and gated against silent regression; `chokidar`'s double-close behaviour is load-bearing in the teardown acceptance yet carries no version floor, no behaviour-locking test, and no re-confirmation entry. A future `chokidar` upgrade that changed double-close semantics — for example, a `.close()` that on the second call rejects asynchronously (chokidar's `.close()` returns a Promise) rather than throwing synchronously, escaping the synchronous per-step `try`/`catch` — would silently invalidate the re-entry / all-three-throw / read-failure-fallback acceptance with no signal. Because `chokidar` is loom's own dependency rather than a Pi-supplied presupposition, loom controls when that upgrade lands, which makes a loom-side guard both feasible and the natural home.

The reliance has been unguarded since `52b9101`.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Pin a `chokidar` version floor and add a bump re-confirmation entry

- **Approach:** Give `chokidar` a load-bearing version floor in `implementation-notes.md` (replacing "this clause names the dependency, not its version" for `chokidar` specifically), mirroring the `tsc` floor in `host-prerequisites.md`, and add a re-confirmation obligation that a `chokidar` bump past the floor be re-verified against the double-close acceptance.
- **Spec edits:** Amend the `chokidar` clause in `implementation-notes.md` to carry a pinned floor and a cross-reference from the three teardown arms; add a `chokidar`-double-close item to the unpinned-presupposition re-confirmation surface (the natural analog is `V18c`'s editorial-review checklist, though it is currently Pi-presupposition-scoped).
- **Pros:** Directly follows the spec's own established pattern for load-bearing dependency behaviour; cheap, localized.
- **Cons:** A pin documents the relied-on version but does not *prove* the behaviour; a contributor can bump the floor without re-checking. The re-confirmation checklist is Pi-scoped, so adding a loom-own-dependency item stretches its remit.
- **Risks:** The guard is procedural, so it degrades to "someone remembered to re-audit."

### Option B — Add a behaviour-locking fixture over a real double-`close()`

- **Approach:** Add a test that double-closes a real `chokidar` watcher through the teardown path and asserts the spec's accepted outcome (the second `.close()` is absorbed and surfaces at most one `loom/host/session-shutdown-teardown-step-failed` row per failing call site, with no escape — including no unhandled async rejection). Any `chokidar` bump that changes double-close semantics then fails loom's own CI.
- **Spec edits:** Pin the behaviour-locking obligation as a normative fixture in the teardown-contract test surface (referenced from the *all-three-throw corner case* and *Read-failure fallback* arms); state explicitly that the close calls must absorb both synchronous throws and asynchronous rejection.
- **Pros:** `chokidar` is loom's own dependency under loom's own CI, so a fixture is the natural and self-enforcing guard; matches the teardown contract's existing fixture-heavy posture; catches an actual semantic change rather than a version number.
- **Cons:** Adds a fixture that must run against the real library, not a `FakeFileWatcher`; slightly couples a test to `chokidar` internals.
- **Risks:** If written only against synchronous throw it would miss the async-rejection regression, so the assertion must cover the Promise-rejection path.

### Option C — Make the teardown behaviour-agnostic

- **Approach:** Remove the dependency on the specific double-close semantic by hardening sub-step 4's close calls so any double-close outcome (no-op, synchronous throw, or asynchronous rejection) is absorbed without escaping the handler, and rephrase the three arms to stop naming "chokidar's double-close behaviour" as the thing relied on.
- **Spec edits:** Rework sub-step 4 in `session-shutdown-semantics.md` to wrap each `.close()` such that a returned Promise's rejection is also caught; rewrite the *all-three-throw* and *Read-failure fallback* prose in `drain-state-contract.md` and branches (j)/(m) in `session-only-degraded-state.md` to assert the outcome behaviourally rather than by reference to the library.
- **Pros:** Eliminates the reliance entirely; most robust against any future `chokidar` change.
- **Cons:** Touches the most carefully-pinned acceptance prose across three pages; the largest edit surface of the three options.
- **Risks:** Reworking the precisely-counted emission acceptance risks introducing a new inconsistency in the row-count fixtures.

### Recommendation

Adopt **Option B**. Because `chokidar` is loom's own dependency exercised by loom's own test suite, a behaviour-locking fixture is the guard that actually fails when double-close semantics change — a version floor (A) only records the relied-on version, and the behaviour-agnostic rewrite (C) disturbs the most delicate acceptance prose. The fixture MUST assert the asynchronous-rejection path, not only a synchronous throw, since `chokidar`'s `.close()` returns a Promise and an escaped rejection is the most plausible regression. Pair it with a one-line cross-reference from the `chokidar` dependency clause in `implementation-notes.md` so the relied-on behaviour is documented at the declaration site.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The spec is internally consistent today: the per-step `try`/`catch` absorbs a synchronous double-close throw, and the fixtures phrase row counts conditionally ("per double-close that throws"). The exposure is durability, not present-tense divergence — a `chokidar` upgrade that altered double-close semantics (notably an async rejection that escapes the synchronous catch) would silently break the teardown re-entry acceptance with no version floor, behaviour-locking test, or re-confirmation gate to catch it, unlike the parallel `tsc` floor the spec already pins and guards.
