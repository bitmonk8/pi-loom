## Finding

`overview-and-orientation.md` concentrates the spec's cross-cutting orientation aggregators under one `## Orientation` heading: `### Scope` (forward-compatibility seams, hard ceilings) and `### Non-functional requirements` (trust boundary, source-language stability, runtime observability). These are the informative, forward-linking summaries of the spec's cross-cutting scope-boundary and NFR dispositions.

The eighth such aggregator — the consolidated loom 1.0 non-goals list (no per-loom sandbox/capability model; no formal source-language migration mechanism; no non-Node host; no concurrent user sessions in the same host process; no reliance on a stdio-capture facet; no parallel-`invoke` surface; no parallel fan-out to prompt-mode `.loom` callees; no admission cap) — sits instead at the top of `language-and-architecture.md` as a `### V1 non-goals` subsection, above that file's first `## ` heading and adjacent to `### Reading order`. The non-goals are the negative scope-boundary counterpart to the `### Scope` aggregator's seams-and-ceilings dispositions: seams enumerate deferred extensions that *do* reserve a forward-compatibility seam, while the non-goals enumerate the cross-cutting dispositions loom 1.0 ships *without* a seam. Splitting these two halves of the same scope-boundary statement across two documents forces a reader assembling loom 1.0's scope picture to read both files.

The split also leaves a stale pointer: the `### Non-functional requirements` paragraph in `overview-and-orientation.md` states the non-goals list is "forward-linked from [loom 1.0 non-goals](./language-and-architecture.md#loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator) **below**" — but the target is in a different file, not "below". Restoring the aggregator's adjacency to `### Scope` (its position before the spec-split, when it lived in `spec.md`'s Orientation) makes that "below" wording correct again.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** f5e89f4 — docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set (2026-06-04, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The consolidated non-goals aggregator was introduced into `spec.md`'s `## Orientation` as a subsection sibling of `### Scope` by commit 0e12140 (2026-05-08, "resolve 'Non-goals are not consolidated into a single section'") — correctly co-located with the other orientation aggregators. Commit f5e89f4 (2026-06-04) split the over-size `spec.md` into the topic-page layout and partitioned the Orientation content across two new files: `overview-and-orientation.md` received `### Scope` and `### Non-functional requirements`, while `language-and-architecture.md` received the `### V1 non-goals` aggregator and `### Reading order`. That mechanical split severed the non-goals aggregator from its sibling aggregators, introducing the placement inconsistency.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Relocate the `### V1 non-goals` aggregator subsection from `language-and-architecture.md` into `overview-and-orientation.md`'s `## Orientation`, positioned as a sibling subsection adjacent to `### Scope` and `### Non-functional requirements` (restoring the pre-split adjacency). Carry both anchors — `#loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator` and `#v1-non-goals` — to the new site so existing cross-links keep resolving.

Then:

- Repoint the two file-qualified inbound links from `language-and-architecture.md#…` to `overview-and-orientation.md#…`: the `### Non-functional requirements` "forward-linked … below" link in `overview-and-orientation.md` (now a same-file `#loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator` reference — drop or keep "below" as appropriate now that it is genuinely below) and the `../language-and-architecture.md#v1-non-goals` link in `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md`.
- Leave `### Reading order` and the `<!-- … -->` NFR-disposition comment in `language-and-architecture.md` in place; only the non-goals subsection moves.

Edge cases the fixer must watch: (a) both `<a id="loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator">` and `<a id="v1-non-goals">` must travel together — GOV-29 names `loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator` as the orientation copy's canonical arm and `v1-non-goals` as its permanent alias, and GOV-26 (alias permanence) forbids dropping the alias; (b) GOV-30 treats the aggregator as a "`spec.md` aggregator paragraph" where "`spec.md`" denotes the split corpus collectively, so the relocation stays inside GOV-30's framing without a GOV-30 prose edit; (c) the relocated subsection should sit under `## Orientation` (not above it), since `overview-and-orientation.md`'s `## Overview` precedes `## Orientation`.
