## Finding

`pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md` §*Tool execution from loom code* says only that `toolCallId is a synthesised UUID prefixed loom-direct:`, and the `loom 1.0 seam — per-call timeout` block in `tool-calls.md` repeats the same prefix-only framing. Three independently-observable properties of the id are left unspecified:

- **Format of the suffix.** "UUID" is not pinned to RFC 4122, and not pinned to the canonical lowercase 8-4-4-4-12 hex form that the corpus does pin elsewhere — the `invocationId` spec at `host-interfaces-services.md` §PIC-20, the `RuntimeEvent.invocation_id` field at `runtime-event-channel.md`, and the `<invocation-id>` placeholder rule at `diagnostics/placeholder-rendering-b.md` §7 all carry that form explicitly. A reasonable implementer reading just this paragraph may pick uppercase hex, the no-hyphen 32-char form, a v7 time-ordered UUID, or a non-UUID token; nothing here rules any of them out.
- **Minting seam.** The corpus already has the `IdSource` DI seam (`PIC-20`) precisely because the no-globals rule forbids drawing ids straight from `crypto.randomUUID()`, and the `ActiveInvocationRegistry` insertion site states normatively that `invocationId` is sourced only from `IdSource.newInvocationId()`. The tool-execution paragraph never says where the `loom-direct:<suffix>` suffix comes from — neither `IdSource` nor `crypto.randomUUID()` is named — leaving the runtime free to call an ambient `crypto.randomUUID()` directly and quietly violate the seam contract that `V8d` and `H3a`'s ambient-access scan enforce.
- **Uniqueness scope.** Nothing states whether two successive code-side tool calls within one invocation get distinct ids, whether ids are unique within an invocation, within a runtime instance, or across the whole process. The downstream consequence (collisions when any Pi surface keys on `toolCallId` — e.g. `tool_execution_start/_update/_end` correlation in `pi.on(...)`, RPC `tool_execution_*` events, or transcript persistence) is mentioned implicitly by the Finding's framing but never spelled out as a normative obligation.

A plan-side gap that *cited* this paragraph as the authoritative source (`docs/plan-review.md` archive: "V14c `toolCallId` format underspecified relative to spec", resolved 2026-05-05 in commit `38af74d`) was closed by tightening the plan's leaf, not by tightening the spec. The spec still under-commits relative to the plan it was used to justify.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md` §*Tool execution from loom code*, replace the `toolCallId is a synthesised UUID prefixed loom-direct:` bullet with a three-clause obligation that pins format, minting seam, and uniqueness scope, modelled on the `invocationId` precedent at `active-invocation-registry.md`:

- `toolCallId` is of the form `loom-direct:<uuid>`, where `<uuid>` is a freshly minted UUID in the canonical lowercase 8-4-4-4-12 hex form pinned by the [§7 `<invocation-id>` placeholder convention](../diagnostics/placeholder-rendering-b.md#7-identifier--descriptor--and-closed-enum-placeholders).
- `<uuid>` is sourced only from `IdSource.newInvocationId()` (see [`PIC-20`](./host-interfaces-services.md#pic-20)); the runtime MUST NOT call `crypto.randomUUID()` (or any other ambient UUID source) at this site, on the same footing as the `invocationId` allocation at [`ActiveInvocationRegistry` insertion](./active-invocation-registry.md#active-invocation-registry).
- Each code-side tool call within a runtime instance receives a distinct `toolCallId`; uniqueness is per-runtime (inherited from `IdSource`).

In `docs/spec_topics/tool-calls.md`, update the `loom 1.0 seam — per-call timeout` blockquote's parenthetical from "the synthesised `loom-direct:` `toolCallId`" to "the synthesised `loom-direct:<uuid>` `toolCallId` (format, minting seam, and uniqueness scope pinned at [Tool execution from loom code](./pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md#tool-execution-from-loom-code))". In `docs/spec_topics/implementation-notes.md`, change "with a synthesized `toolCallId`" to "with a `toolCallId` minted per [Tool execution from loom code](./pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md#tool-execution-from-loom-code)" so the three sites converge on a single owner.

Re-using `IdSource.newInvocationId()` rather than introducing a new `newToolCallId()` method is the cheaper edit and consistent with `PIC-20`'s framing (the method name describes the canonical hex form, not the consumer); a future revision MAY rename the method additively if the asymmetry becomes load-bearing. Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The `IdSource` already exists; this finding adds a *call site*, not a new seam. The `H3a` ambient-access scan should continue to allow only `IdSource.newInvocationId()` as the direct `crypto.randomUUID` carrier.
- The `cka-46` coverage-matrix row currently lists only the envelope-lowering MUSTs; once the format rule lands, `V14a` / `V14a-T` should grow a test bullet matching `/^loom-direct:[0-9a-f]{8}-[0-9a-f]{4}-[0-9a-f]{4}-[0-9a-f]{4}-[0-9a-f]{12}$/` and asserting distinctness across two successive calls in the same invocation, using a `FakeIdSource` for determinism.
- The related "`loom-direct:` vs `code_tool` vocabulary divergence" finding (below) may rename the prefix to `loom-code:`; if both findings land together, the format/minting/uniqueness rule above applies verbatim with the renamed prefix.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Two implementers reading just the cited paragraph will produce wire-incompatible `toolCallId` values (canonical hex vs uppercase vs non-UUID token), and one of them may legitimately mint the suffix straight from ambient `crypto.randomUUID()`, silently violating the `IdSource` DI seam that the `H3a` ambient-access scan otherwise enforces. Pi surfaces that key on `toolCallId` (`tool_execution_start/_update/_end` correlation in `pi.on(...)`, RPC `tool_execution_*` envelope wiring) collide on duplicates when the uniqueness scope is left to chance; the corpus's "no operator-visible collisions on sibling invocations" stance is built on per-invocation id uniqueness elsewhere and should be built on it here too.
