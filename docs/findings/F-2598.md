## Problem

The "Caching and reload" paragraph (anchor `#caching-and-reload`) mandates a file-watcher on each of the two settings paths that invalidates the cache on change, debounced via the injected `Clock` seam, but it never names the seam that *watches* the two files. The only filesystem seams it cites are `FileSystem` (PIC-13, whose surface exposes no watch member) and `Clock` (PIC-12); a `FileWatcher` seam (PIC-14, `watch(roots, handler): Unsubscribe`) exists but is introduced scoped to the discovery roots, and nothing binds the settings watcher to it. An implementer therefore cannot tell whether to route the two settings paths through PIC-14, construct a second `FileWatcher` instance, or call Node's `fs.watch` directly ‚Äî and the last breaks the no-direct-Node injected-seam test discipline the sibling seams enforce, leaving the debounce/coalesce behaviour the spec asserts is `Clock`-deterministic untestable through any fake.

**History:** The "Caching and reload" paragraph ‚Äî "A file-watcher on each of the two paths invalidates the cache on change" ‚Äî was authored in 043762e (then in `spec_topics/discovery.md`) with no watch seam, because no watcher seam existed in the corpus yet. The `FileWatcher` seam (PIC-14, `FakeFileWatcher`) was introduced two days later in 8a6d15f, but scoped to the discovery-root watcher; the pre-existing settings-watcher prose was never updated to bind to it. The gap is the interaction: the settings watcher predates the seam, and the seam's later arrival covered only the discovery roots, leaving the settings watcher unanchored to any seam through the f5e89f4 topic split and every edit since.

## Solution approach

In the `#caching-and-reload` paragraph, state that the settings-file watcher attaches through the PIC-14 `FileWatcher` seam ‚Äî the same injected seam the discovery-root watcher uses ‚Äî passing the two resolved settings paths as its roots, and that the `settingsWatcher.close()` call in session-shutdown teardown step 4 is the `Unsubscribe` the `watch(...)` call returns. Clarify that the settings path consumes only the seam's raw `add`/`change`/`unlink` stream feeding cache invalidation and the debounced re-merge, not the structural-change machinery PIC-14's discovery-root consumer layers on top.
