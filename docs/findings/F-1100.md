## Problem

`V15b` closes two unrelated mechanisms in one leaf: its `Adds.` declares both the `invoke`-chain depth bound (hard ceiling #1, cap 32) and the parse-time invocation-cycle detector over the static-resolution graph, and its `Tests.` carries one bullet per mechanism (the `INV-4` runtime `loom/runtime/invoke-depth-exceeded` bullet and a separate `loom/load/invocation-cycle` parse-time bullet). The two mechanisms execute in different phases (runtime frame counting vs. parse-time static-graph traversal), are independently triggerable, surface under different code namespaces, and need disjoint test infrastructure; only the depth half consults `V16a`'s arbitration seam. Per `conventions.md` §3 a vertical leaf is "the smallest feature that can ship and be tested independently," and each mechanism meets that bar alone, so the bundling exceeds the stated atomicity granularity. With only two coarse `Tests.` bullets and one `Ships when.`, an implementer can land one mechanism broken while the other's bullet stays green, hiding partial correctness behind a single leaf boundary.

## Solution approach

Split `V15b` along the phase boundary into two single-concern leaf pairs. Retain `V15b`/`V15b-T` as the runtime invoke-depth ceiling leaf — keep the `INV-4` `Tests.` bullet, the `V16a` consult sentence in `Adds.`, and the `V16a`/`V15c` `Deps` — and narrow its `Adds.`/`Ships when.` and title to the depth bound only, striking the `loom/load/invocation-cycle` bullet and its `Adds.` clause. Allocate a new leaf pair (impl + `-T`) owning the parse-time invocation-cycle detector, moving the `loom/load/invocation-cycle` bullet and its `Adds.` clause there, citing `invocation.md`, carrying no `V16a` consult, and depending on `V15a`/`V15c`. Then add the new cycle leaf to `docs/plan.md` §V15, route the `loom/load/invocation-cycle` obligation in `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md` to the new leaf (leaving `INV-4` on `V15b`), and confirm `docs/plan_topics/V16a-ceiling-order-masked.md` leaves no ceiling-#1 reference pointing the cycle detector at the arbitration seam.
