## Finding

Step 2 of the version-bump procedure carries a sub-section titled **"Outcome recording conventions"** (anchor `bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions`) whose entire content is item-(e)-specific: it defines the item (e) `N/A` verdict (`N/A — superseded by <named Pi mechanism>`) and the item (e) two-sub-outcome recording convention that mandates separate (e.i)/(e.ii) verdicts. That sub-section sits at the very end of the items list — after item (aj) and the "any future presupposition" trailing paragraph — roughly thirty checklist items past the item (e) it governs.

Item (e)'s own prose forward-references the sub-section twice (the recovery-serialisation paragraph: *"Item (e)'s `N/A` definition and its `N/A — superseded by <named Pi mechanism>` recording shape are stated under [Outcome recording conventions](#bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions) below"*; the per-item rationale paragraph: *"the recording conventions governing both sub-outcomes — the `N/A`-propagation rule and the partial-outcome rule — are stated under [Outcome recording conventions](#bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions) below"*). A contributor reading item (e) top-to-bottom is sent ~30 items downstream to fetch the rules, and a contributor reading the items list top-to-bottom encounters the conventions only after scrolling past the entire (f)–(aj) audit set, with no in-place signal that the block they just reached is item-(e)-specific.

The block's content itself is fine — it was extracted out of item (e) in a prior fix that resolved an earlier "N/A and recording conventions buried" finding by lifting the conventions out of item (e)'s already-dense fail-trigger prose. That extraction solved the inside-item-(e) burial but parked the block at the structurally most-distant location available within step 2; the placement defect is the residue. The sub-section's sole reader is item (e), so the natural home is the step-2 preamble (or immediately after item (e)), not after item (aj).

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Promote the `bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions` block to a standalone labelled sub-section of step 2, **before the items (a)–(ak) list**, sitting in the step-2 preamble alongside the existing per-item recording shape sentence (the *"pass / fail / N/A with a one-line rationale, recorded in the bump commit message"* clause the conventions block currently forward-references). Keep the existing anchor ID (`bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions`) so the two forward-references inside item (e) and any other in-corpus link continue to resolve, and so the prior fix's discoverability rationale (*"collected here so they are discoverable apart from item (e)'s fail-trigger prose"*) is preserved — the block stays a separable sub-section, just earlier in step 2.

Do not inline the block back into item (e): that would undo the `c5f97f6` extraction and re-create the in-item-(e) burial it resolved. Do not leave the block where it is and add a duplicate up-top "see below" pointer either; the pointer-and-block split would add a third hop on top of the existing one.

Implementer-relevant edge cases:
- The block's preamble sentence currently reads *"defined in the step-2 preamble above"* in reference to the per-item recording shape sentence. Once relocated, both clauses sit in the same preamble; rephrase that intra-block back-reference so it reads as a continuation (*"the two item-(e)-specific conventions below extend that shape"*) rather than a back-link to text now adjacent.
- The "any future checklist item that carries independently falsifiable sub-checks; item (e) is the worked example" clause is generic and worth keeping verbatim at the relocated site — it justifies why the convention lives at step-2 scope rather than inside item (e).
- Re-check the two forward-references inside item (e) after the move. The link targets stay valid by anchor, but the word *"below"* in both surrounding clauses becomes false; replace it with *"above"* (or drop the directional adverb) so the prose matches the new ordering.
