## Finding

The four leaves that touch the numbered hard-ceiling set (ceilings #1–#4) each
refer to a member of that set with a different prose shorthand:

- `V15b` Adds writes `hard ceiling #1` (space form, with the `hard` qualifier) and, two sentences later, `ceiling-#1` (hyphenated).
- `V16a`'s body uses the space form throughout (`ceiling #1` … `ceiling #4`); its heading is `Hard-ceiling interaction order`.
- `V14e`'s heading (and the matching `plan.md` index line and `V14e-T`) uses `Ceiling-#4` (hyphenated); its body mixes `ceiling-#4` and `ceiling #4`.
- `V15j`'s heading (and the `plan.md` index line and `V15j-T`) uses `ceiling-#4` (hyphenated); its body mixes `ceiling-#4`, `ceiling #4`, and `ceiling #3`.

So one numbered set is named three parallel ways — `hard ceiling #N` (space, qualified), `ceiling #N` (space), and `ceiling-#N` (hyphenated) — with no first-occurrence equivalence note. The spec's own prose (`spec_topics/hard-ceilings.md` and its sub-topics) predominantly uses the space form `ceiling #N`, and `V16a`'s body already matches it, so a single canonical form is available.

Distinct and out of scope for this consolidation: (1) the category noun "hard ceiling" / "Hard-ceiling" (e.g. `V16a`'s heading, the spec's "Hard ceiling") is the name of the concept, not the numbered-member shorthand, and is consistent with the spec; (2) the `"ceiling#1"` … `"ceiling#4"` no-space tokens in `V16a` are the pinned string-identifier *values* of the arbitration seam's `surfaced`/`masked` output, cross-referenced to `ceilings-3-and-4.md#masked-field` — they are normative wire values, not prose, and must not be reshaped.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Adopt the space form `ceiling #N` as the single numbered-shorthand across the shard-06 ceiling leaves — it already matches `V16a`'s body and the spec's predominant prose in `hard-ceilings.md`. Concretely:

- In `V15b-invoke-depth-cycle.md` Adds: replace `hard ceiling #1` with `ceiling #1`, and replace `ceiling-#1 first-enforcement point` with `ceiling #1 first-enforcement point`.
- In `V14e-tool-calls-depth-ceiling.md` (heading, Adds, Tests) and its `V14e-T` heading and the `docs/plan.md` index title for `V14e`: replace the hyphenated `Ceiling-#4` / `ceiling-#4` with `ceiling #4`.
- In `V15j-invoke-ceiling-depth.md` (heading, Adds, Tests) and its `V15j-T` heading and the `docs/plan.md` index title for `V15j`: replace the hyphenated `ceiling-#4` with `ceiling #4` (and any `ceiling-#3` with `ceiling #3`).

Leave unchanged: the category noun "hard ceiling" / "Hard-ceiling" wherever it names the concept (including `V16a`'s `Hard-ceiling interaction order` heading and the `hard-ceilings.md` references), and the pinned string-identifier literals `"ceiling#1"` … `"ceiling#4"` in `V16a` (the arbitration seam's `surfaced`/`masked` output values, cross-referenced to `ceilings-3-and-4.md#masked-field`).

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The numbered set is unambiguous in every form, so no implementer is misled and no behaviour diverges; the cost is purely readability and the loss of a single greppable token for "ceiling #N" across the shard. Pure wording.
