## Finding

Several V9 and V14a leaf bullets annotate a spec citation with a parenthetical that pairs a functional qualifier (`un-anchored`) with a governance-ticket provenance tag (`GOV-22 residue`). The `un-anchored` qualifier is actionable: it tells the test author the cited obligation carries no numbered `PREFIX-N` REQ-ID, so the test cites the spec section by name rather than a REQ-ID. The `GOV-22 residue` half is governance-machinery provenance — it is defined in `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline and `coverage-matrix.md` (where `GOV-22` is the un-anchored-obligation class), but it is never defined at the point of use in any leaf, and it adds nothing the test author can act on while writing the test.

The tag is also internally inconsistent in both phrasing and placement. Three distinct orderings are in use: `(un-anchored; GOV-22 residue)` (V9l/V9l-T, V14a/V14a-T), `(... un-anchored GOV-22 residue; facet cka-23.*)` without the semicolon (V9b/V9b-T), and the reversed `(GOV-22 un-anchored residue)` (V9f impl). The annotation appears on the V9f implementation leaf's `ToolDefinition.label` derivation bullet but is absent from the identical bullet in its V9f-T partner — an impl/-T asymmetry with no functional basis.

These annotations are human-facing prose inside Tests/Spec bullets; no closing gate consumes them (the `H5e` un-anchored-MUST gate scans `spec_topics/**` pages, and the `H5d` transitive-completeness gate reads `coverage-matrix.md` closing-leaf columns — neither reads leaf-bullet annotations). The defect is therefore reader friction and inconsistency, not a gate or coverage hazard.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Strip the provenance tag, keep the functional qualifier

- **Approach:** In every cited bullet, remove `GOV-22 residue` (and its word-order/semicolon variants), retaining only the functional `un-anchored` qualifier, normalized to a single parenthetical form `(un-anchored)`. Add `(un-anchored)` to the V9f-T `ToolDefinition.label` bullet so it matches its impl partner.
- **Plan edits:** V9b/V9b-T — drop `GOV-22 residue` from each `cka-23.*` facet bullet, keeping `un-anchored` and the `facet cka-23.*` token; V9f impl `(GOV-22 un-anchored residue)` → `(un-anchored)`; V9f-T — add `(un-anchored)`; V9l/V9l-T — each of the four `(un-anchored; GOV-22 residue)` bullets → `(un-anchored)`; V14a/V14a-T — `(un-anchored; GOV-22 residue)` → `(un-anchored)`.
- **Spec edits:** None.
- **Pros:** Removes governance jargon that carries no meaning at the leaf level; `un-anchored` alone tells the test author to cite the spec section rather than a REQ-ID; the GOV-22 provenance is already authoritatively held by `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline and `coverage-matrix.md`; smallest diff.
- **Cons:** Drops the per-bullet reminder that the obligation is tracked under the GOV-22 un-anchored-obligation class.
- **Risks:** A reader who used the leaf-level `GOV-22` cue to find the governance machinery must instead follow `conventions.md`.

### Option B — Define the tag once and normalize phrasing

- **Approach:** Keep `GOV-22 residue` but give it one definition (a short line in `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline) and normalize every occurrence to one phrasing; add the tag to V9f-T for symmetry.
- **Plan edits:** Add a defining sentence to `conventions.md`; rewrite each cited bullet to the canonical form; add the tag to V9f-T.
- **Spec edits:** None.
- **Pros:** Retains the provenance link from each leaf bullet to the GOV-22 governance class.
- **Cons:** Keeps jargon at the point of use; adds a glossary entry; larger surface than Option A.
- **Risks:** The new definition must stay in sync with the existing `conventions.md` GOV-22 wording.

### Recommendation

Take Option A. For a test author the actionable content is `un-anchored` (cite the spec section, not a REQ-ID); the GOV-22 provenance is redundant at the leaf level because `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline and `coverage-matrix.md` already own the un-anchored-obligation class and its closing gate. Keep each bullet's `(un-anchored)` and leave the `facet cka-23.*` tokens on the V9b bullets intact (those are functional facet IDs, separate from the GOV-22 tag), and resolve the V9f impl/-T asymmetry in the same pass.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

An implementer reading these leaf bullets meets a governance-ticket tag that is undefined at the point of use, phrased three different ways, and present on V9f's impl leaf but missing from its -T partner — reading friction with no payoff. Nothing breaks: the annotation is not read by any closing gate, so no leaf becomes unpickable and no coverage gate misfires whether the tag stays or goes.
