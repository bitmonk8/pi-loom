## Problem

The *Exemption mechanism* paragraph in `audit-resolution.md` says an `// allow-pi-surface:` marker placed on a family-(4) source line "MUST NOT suppress the family (4) discriminator and is itself an audit failure under that discriminator." The antecedent of "that discriminator" is ambiguous: the only discriminator named in the preceding clause is family (4), but the conceptual referent — a marker-shaped failure — belongs to family (5). The downstream machinery does not resolve it: the *Malformed-marker discriminator* clauses (a)–(g) in `audit-failures.md` name no "marker on a non-exemptible family-(4) line" case, and the *Malformed-marker dual-emission co-commit obligation* in `version-bump-step2b.md` co-emits only family (1)/(2)/(3) records alongside a family-(5) red, not family (4). With the *Failure-surface contract* declaring the five families exhaustive, conforming implementations cannot determine which `<family>` token to mint and will silently diverge on the wire.

**Verdict:** single-commit introduction
**Introducing commit:** `e5cd514` (2026-05-09, *pi-loom spec: resolve "T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit"*)

The defect is present at first introduction — the sentence with the ambiguous *"that discriminator"*, the malformed-marker clause list with no (h) covering this case, and the dual-emission rule scoped to family (1)/(2)/(3) only all entered together in that single commit. Subsequent edits to the same paragraphs (the `@mariozechner → @earendil-works` rename in `5e73072`, the corpus-direction sweep in `9e53613`, and the size-cap collapse / file split in `f5e89f4` and `81bf52a`) preserve the wording verbatim and do not address the gap.

## Solution approach

Route the family-(4)-line marker case to family (5) as a malformed-marker failure and make that routing unambiguous across the coordinating landmarks. Clarify the *Exemption mechanism* sentence in `audit-resolution.md` so the "audit failure" it names resolves to the malformed-marker discriminator (family (5)) rather than the family-(4) discriminator. Add a malformed-marker clause in the *Malformed-marker discriminator* of `audit-failures.md` covering a marker on a non-exemptible family-(4) source line, carrying its own `<symptom>` token, and reconcile the *Failure-surface contract* per-`<symptom>` fixture-floor count and the *Per-family record-shape table* family (5) row accordingly. Extend the *Malformed-marker dual-emission co-commit obligation* in `version-bump-step2b.md` to co-emit the family-(4) shape record alongside the family-(5) marker record.

## Solution constraints

- The family-(4) absolute prohibition stands: the fix MUST NOT make a family-(4) line exemptible — the marker routes to family (5) and the family-(4) shape discriminator still fires independently.
