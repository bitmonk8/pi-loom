## Problem

Version-bump step 5 in `version-bump-triggers.md` carries a standing build-time feature requirement — the audit pass over the `src/`, `test/`, `docs/` grep targets and second-stage anchor filter that fails `npm test` red on a result-delta against the documented enumeration — as a single sentence with no anchor, no label, and no REQ-ID, buried mid-paragraph in the dense procedural prose about reconciling the `SessionShutdownEvent['reason']` snapshot. This gate runs on every `npm test` invocation and is a permanent build-time obligation the loom must ship, on the same footing as the sibling gates (SDK surface-inventory assertion, `engines.node` floor, provider seed-field coverage, strict-capability probe). Unlike those siblings — each anchored as a named obligation or owned by a labelled section, e.g. the `Inventory-closure audit` at `#sdk-cap-inventory-closure-audit` — the triplet-sweep audit has no referenceable handle, so a reader scanning the corpus for the loom's standing build-time gates will not find it presented as one.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `52b9101` (2026-06-05, *pi-loom spec: resolve "T24 — Fork-reason watcher closure leaves the extension in an unspecified, silently degraded state"*); `c0259ca` (2026-06-07, *pi-loom spec: resolve "Version-bump procedure: four obligations have no verifiable acceptance criterion"*)
**History:** The inbound-reference sweep and its build-time audit pass entered the step-5 prose at `52b9101`, where the audit was an **optional** clause — *"The H1 leaf … MAY additionally implement a build-time audit pass over the same three grep targets … PIC does not require the audit"* — embedded in the procedural paragraph from the outset. `c0259ca` then promoted the clause from `MAY` to `MUST` (*"The implementation MUST implement a build-time audit pass …"*), converting an optional aside into a mandatory feature requirement while leaving it in the same unanchored mid-paragraph position. The placement was always somewhat off; the `MAY`→`MUST` promotion is what turned it into a feature-requirement-buried-in-a-procedure defect. Both commits resolved unrelated findings, so the obligation rode along without ever being given its own anchor or named-obligation framing. Later structural commits (`f5e89f4` page-split under the size cap, `81bf52a` collapse into `spec_topics/`) only relocated the file; they did not introduce the defect.

## Solution approach

Add an anchor and a bold label to the build-time audit-pass sentence in step 5 of `version-bump-triggers.md`, framing it as a named standing-gate obligation mirroring the `Inventory-closure audit` treatment at `#sdk-cap-inventory-closure-audit` (PIC pins the gate's behaviour; concrete tooling and discriminator strings stay implementation-owned). Keep the obligation physically in step 5, where its full contract already lives. Wire the new anchor into the existing "build-time audit gate (pinned later in this step)" forward-reference earlier in the step.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: altering the audit's existing behavioural contract (grep targets, second-stage anchor filter, documented-enumeration exclusion list); this is an anchoring/discoverability change only.
