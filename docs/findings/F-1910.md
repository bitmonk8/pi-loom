## Finding

The `H6a` leaf's `Deps.` field is `H5b, H5c, H7a`, immediately followed by a long multi-sentence parenthetical that explains *why* the dependency graph is shaped the way it is: that the `H5c` and `H7a` edges are "pinned directly here rather than threaded through `H5b` because each closes no new spec REQ-ID and so is not a member of `H5b`'s coverage-producing set", that `H5b` owns the complete coverage-producing set and `H6a` "inherits that completeness transitively", and that the live surfaces `H6a` hard-fails on match those `H5b` warn-only-reconciles.

None of this prose adds an actionable obligation. The dependency edges and their build-order constraint are fully carried by the `Deps. H5b, H5c, H7a` line itself (the `Deps.` pickability predicate in `conventions.md` is what binds the implementer). The parenthetical is design-rationale / decision-log narrative — it documents the reasoning behind a dep-graph decision, not a requirement the implementer must satisfy. The transitive-completeness *rule* the note once carried was already relocated to `conventions.md` *REQ-ID discipline*; what remains is the explanatory residue.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/H6a-live-corpus-activation.md`, delete the parenthetical note that follows the `**Deps.** ` `H5b, H5c, H7a` line — the whole `_(This leaf depends on the warn-only canary … so the two footings cannot drift.)_` block. Keep the `**Deps.** ` `H5b, H5c, H7a` line itself unchanged: it carries the binding edges, and the `Deps.` pickability predicate in `conventions.md` enforces the after-`H5b` / after-`H5c` / after-`H7a` build order without the prose. The relocated transitive-completeness obligation already lives in `conventions.md` *REQ-ID discipline*, so no requirement is lost by removing the note.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

No implementer is misled into a wrong action: the `Deps.` line carries the binding edges and the `conventions.md` pickability predicate enforces ordering. The cost is reading burden and a divergence risk — the parenthetical restates facts owned by `H5b` and `conventions.md`, so if those change the rationale here can silently drift out of sync.
