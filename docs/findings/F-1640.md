## Finding

The glossary preamble (`docs/spec_topics/glossary.md`) declares the page strictly descriptive and non-authoritative: *"Each entry is descriptive — a one-paragraph reminder of what a term means and where it is used. The canonical, normative definition lives on the feature page named in the entry's `See:` reference; if a glossary entry and its canonical page disagree, the canonical page wins."* This establishes a single, unambiguous authority rule — for any term, the feature page governs and the glossary merely mirrors it.

Three entries contradict that rule by carrying standalone normative directives that exist nowhere else in the corpus:

- **`callable set`** — *"Authors and implementers MUST use the term `callable set` … the bare phrases `tools: set`, `tool set`, `loom's tools`, and `available tools` are spec-prose synonyms to be avoided."*
- **`schema slug`** — *"Authors and implementers MUST use `schema slug` (or the bare form `slug` …). The synonyms `schema hash`, `schema-hash`, `sha12`, `lowered-schema hash`, and `lowered-schema content hash` are spec-prose drift to be avoided; a future grep gate uses this entry as its source of truth."*
- **`Pi tool` vs. `.loom callable`** — *"Authors and implementers MUST use the canonical term `.loom callable` … the synonyms `registered loom`, `registered subagent loom`, `registered-loom call`, and `loom callee` are spec-prose drift to be avoided."*

None of these MUSTs is presented as a ratified copy of a feature-page obligation, and no feature page is named as their authority. The canonical pages run the dependency the other way: `tool-calls.md` cites *"[Glossary — `callable set`]"* as the term's reference, and the `schema slug` entry explicitly declares itself *"the source of truth"* for a future grep gate. The glossary is therefore the sole bearer of these obligations while simultaneously declaring itself non-authoritative — a two-authority contradiction. A reader who applies the preamble's "canonical page wins" rule treats the MUSTs as non-binding reminders and looks to the feature page for the real constraint, finding none; a reader who takes the MUSTs at face value treats the glossary as binding. The two readings diverge on whether, e.g., `tool set` is a forbidden synonym.

The contradiction is reinforced by the corpus's own governance machinery: `glossary.md` is registered in the REQ-ID prefix table (`governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`) as `(no IDs — narrative)`, which structurally exempts it from the un-anchored-MUST closing gate (plan leaf `H5e`). These three normative MUSTs thus escape both REQ-ID anchoring (GOV-3) and the gate's coverage-matrix reconciliation — they are normative obligations sitting on a page classified as non-normative narrative, and consequently are unenforced.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Promote the glossary to a declared terminology-governance authority

**Approach:** Amend the preamble to state explicitly that terminology MUST/MUST-NOT directives in the glossary are authoritative for terminology governance (synonym avoidance, canonical-term selection) and are the corpus's single source of truth for those obligations, carved out from the otherwise-descriptive "canonical page wins" rule. Re-classify `glossary.md` in the REQ-ID prefix table so the carried MUSTs are subject to the un-anchored-MUST gate, and anchor each MUST (REQ-ID or `coverage-matrix.md` row with a closing leaf).

**Spec edits:** preamble paragraph in `glossary.md`; the `glossary.md` row in `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`; coverage-matrix / REQ-ID anchoring for the three MUSTs.

**Pros:** Keeps the terminology directives co-located with the term definitions where authors already look; matches the de-facto reality that canonical pages cite the glossary as the term reference and that the `schema slug` entry is named as a grep-gate source of truth.

**Cons:** Introduces a dual-authority document (descriptive for definitions, normative for terminology governance), which is a more complex contract to maintain; requires removing the blanket narrative exemption for the whole page.

**Risks:** A future "canonical page wins" reading still mis-applies to the carved-out MUSTs unless the carve-out wording is tight.

### Option B — Move each MUST to its canonical feature page; reduce glossary entries to descriptive stubs

**Approach:** Relocate each terminology MUST and its forbidden-synonym list to the canonical feature page (`callable set` → `frontmatter.md`/`tool-calls.md`; `.loom callable` → `frontmatter.md`/`tool-calls.md`; `schema slug` → `schema-subset.md`, Canonical schema hash subsection), anchored with a REQ-ID or coverage-matrix row. Reduce the glossary entries to descriptive one-paragraph reminders that point to the relocated obligation via `See:`. The preamble's existing "canonical page wins" rule then applies uniformly with no carve-out.

**Spec edits:** delete the MUST sentences and synonym lists from the three glossary entries; add anchored normative paragraphs to the named feature pages; the relocated MUSTs become subject to `H5e` and need coverage-matrix dispositioning.

**Pros:** Preserves the glossary's single, simple declared role; obligations land on the non-narrative pages where the gate already enforces un-anchored MUSTs; implementers and grep-gate authors reading the canonical page encounter the rule directly.

**Cons:** Wider edit surface across three feature pages; the grep-gate "source of truth" pointer must be repointed from the glossary entry to the new home.

**Risks:** Splitting a MUST across two candidate homes (e.g. `frontmatter.md` vs `tool-calls.md`) could re-introduce drift unless one page is designated owner.

### Recommendation

Option B. The preamble's single-authority rule is the simpler, already-load-bearing contract, and the corpus's gate machinery (`H5e`) already enforces un-anchored MUSTs on the non-narrative feature pages — relocating the obligations there makes them enforced rather than exempt, without inventing a dual-authority document. Designate one owning page per term (frontmatter.md for the two `tools:`-derived terms, schema-subset.md for the slug) and repoint the `schema slug` grep-gate "source of truth" reference to its new home. Watch that the three separate per-entry placement findings in this review (see Related Findings) are executed as the per-site work of this single authority decision, not as independent edits.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable spec authors diverge on whether the glossary MUSTs bind: one applies the preamble's "canonical page wins / descriptive" rule and treats them as non-binding reminders (looking to a feature-page authority that does not exist), the other treats them as authoritative. Because `glossary.md` is classified as `(no IDs — narrative)`, the obligations also escape the un-anchored-MUST gate, so the terminology constraints they assert are structurally unenforced. The defect degrades the spec's terminology-governance discipline rather than runtime behaviour, so implementation can proceed.
