## Finding

The *Stale-citation limitation (acknowledged soft surface)* sub-bullet under *Stale sub-kinds* in `audit-failures.md` carries exactly two normative non-requirements: (1) the *Citation-token grammar* check is purely syntactic and the audit performs no citation-resolution sub-check, and (2) the resulting soft surface is **not** owed an additional audit discriminator (it is instead mitigated by the contributor re-read obligation in step 2(b) branch (4)). Both are stable, implementer-facing constraints.

Appended to the same sentence is a future-work aside: *"widening the audit to validate citation resolution would require attaching a registry of valid REQ-IDs and PIC anchors to the audit input, which is a PIC edit deferred past loom 1.0."* This is design rationale plus a deferral note describing what the spec is choosing *not* to build. Neither normative constraint depends on knowing that the expansion is deferred — an implementer satisfies the requirement identically whether or not the registry-based widening is ever planned. The deferral clause is decision-log commentary embedded in a normative body, where it will go stale once the deferral is reconsidered.

This is the same defect pattern as the sibling *Substitution limitation (acknowledged soft surface)* sub-bullet immediately above it, which is the subject of a separate finding; here the rationale additionally carries the explicit "deferred past loom 1.0" clause.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Delete the deferred-work clause — `; widening the audit to validate citation resolution would require attaching a registry of valid REQ-IDs and PIC anchors to the audit input, which is a PIC edit deferred past loom 1.0` — from the end of the *Stale-citation limitation* sub-bullet, and trim the surrounding rationale so the sub-bullet keeps only the two normative non-requirements: (1) the *Citation-token grammar* check is purely syntactic and the audit performs no citation-resolution sub-check, and (2) the soft surface is mitigated by the step 2(b) branch (4) re-read obligation and is **not** owed an additional audit discriminator. Retain the link to step 2(b) branch (4) and the *citation has gone stale* rewrite case, which carry normative weight. Do not relocate the deferral statement into the normative body of any other audit page.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The audit's behaviour is fully determined by the two normative non-requirements; the deferral clause changes no observer's reading and constrains no implementation. Its only effect is staleness risk — it documents an internal scope decision that becomes inaccurate the moment the deferral is revisited.
