## Problem

The *Wire serialisation* clause in `audit-wire-and-canary.md` packs each record as four tab-delimited tokens on one stdout line and obliges every field value — including `path` — to contain no ASCII tab (U+0009), justifying this for `path` by claiming "file paths are tab-free by their pinned shapes." That justification fails: nothing in the audited-source-tree definition (`audit-resolution.md` *Audit scope*, glob normalisation, source-file encoding) or the failure surface constrains the *content* of an audited source path, and POSIX and `tsc` both permit U+0009 in pathname components. A tab-bearing `path` breaks the "splitting each line on ASCII tab obtains four fields" guarantee a CI parser is explicitly permitted to rely on, and the segment-(1) right-most-`:` recovery of `path` / `line` then runs against the wrong substring. The audit emits no infrastructure-failure record for this case, so the malformed wire output is silent-by-omission rather than fail-closed.

## Solution approach

Add a normative precondition to `audit-resolution.md`'s `id="audit-scope"` clause establishing that an audited source file's path MUST NOT contain the ASCII tab character (U+0009), and that a tab-bearing path surfaces under the *Infrastructure-failure handling* (`id="audit-infrastructure-failure-handling"`) category (i) implementation-agnostic failures on the same footing as a UTF-8 decode failure, with the offending pathname carried verbatim. Rewrite the unsupported "file paths are tab-free by their pinned shapes" clause in `audit-wire-and-canary.md`'s *Wire serialisation* paragraph as a back-reference to that *Audit scope* precondition. Clarify that the infrastructure-failure record reporting a tab-bearing path is the one wire-permitted exception, identified by its `audit/infra/...` leading discriminator.
