## Problem

The catch-all bullet of the *Stop-reason classification* rule under
`#stop-reason-classification` opens "Any other **non-terminal** stop reason",
but "non-terminal" is the wrong predicate and contradicts the bullet that
carries it: a stop reason is by construction the signal that a 200 response has
terminated, and the same sentence justifies the mapping because "the terminator
is a definite outcome of the request." A reader taking "non-terminal" as the
operative predicate is left with an empty set, and the named example — a
content-filter terminator — is itself terminal, so the qualifier contradicts its
own illustration. The intended set is every stop reason not already classified
by the rows above (the turn boundaries `end_turn` / `stop` / `tool_use` and the
output-boundary `length`).

The misnomer was therefore baked in at the section's inception, not introduced by a later interaction.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the catch-all bullet of *Stop-reason classification* to drop the
"non-terminal" adjective and define the residual set by exclusion — any stop
reason other than the turn-boundary reasons (`end_turn` / `stop` / `tool_use`)
and the output-boundary `length` handled above. Preserve the existing example
list ("content-filter terminator … or any stop reason the runtime does not
otherwise recognise") and the parenthetical rationale; the mapping is unchanged.
