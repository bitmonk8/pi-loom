## Problem

`PIC-22. Parallel subagent spawn initiation` gates its parallel-spawn MUST and its conformance witness on N ≥ 3, but the adjacent `<a id="no-invocation-cap">` paragraph independently MUSTs that the runtime interpose no admission cap between sibling invocations. At N = 2, a runtime that serialises a parallel pair — admit the first `createAgentSession`, wait for its `sendUserMessage` to return, then admit the second — violates the no-admission-cap MUST yet never trips PIC-22's witness, so two implementers can produce divergent N = 2 behaviour with identical conformance results. N = 1 is trivially vacuous, but N = 2 is the smallest meaningfully observable case and the spec leaves it on the wrong side of the trigger.

**History:** `git log -S 'for any fixed N' -- docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` identifies `7afe9bb` as the sole introducing commit; the rule was added to `pi-integration-contract.md` and later moved to `subagent.md` under the topic-split. The "small fixed N (≥ 3)" wording originated in `docs/spec-review.md`'s T14 *Solution approach* (also part of `7afe9bb`) and was carried verbatim into the normative paragraph and the downstream `V9i` / `V9i-T` plan leaves. No later commit revised the bound; the gap has been present since the witness was first defined.

## Solution approach

Narrow PIC-22's trigger bound from N ≥ 3 to N ≥ 2 in both the MUST sentence and the conformance-test sentence of the `id="pic-22"` paragraph in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/subagent.md`. Mirror the lowered bound in the `V9i` and `V9i-T` subagent-isolation plan leaves, where the conformance fixture is pinned at N = 3.
