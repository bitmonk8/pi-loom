## Problem

The interleaving clause in the `per-invocation-retry-budget` paragraph of `determinism-cancellation-failure.md` — "A transport failure observed on the retry of a malformed envelope consumes the transport budget (not a second malformed attempt)" — is ambiguous over whether observing the cross-class failure merely marks that class's budget as consumed (chain terminates at 2 calls, transport row surfaced) or fires the cross-class retry as a further binder call (chain reaches 3 calls, surfaced row depends on the third call's outcome). The follow-on 3-call ceiling sentence and HC3-d are consistent with both readings. The divergence is observable both in binder call-count telemetry and in the user-visible system-note row.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (of the per-invocation-budget paragraph)
**Introducing commits:** `e4d2c7d` ("pi-loom spec: resolve 'Binder retry budget: maximum total LLM calls per slash invocation is not bounded'", 2026-05-05)
**History:** The clause `A transport failure observed on the retry of a malformed envelope consumes the transport budget (not a second malformed attempt)` was authored verbatim in `e4d2c7d` when the per-invocation-budget paragraph was first added to `spec_topics/binder.md` (resolving an earlier finding about the unbounded LLM-call count). `git log -G "transport budget"` confirms `e4d2c7d` is the first add of the defect token outside review files; subsequent touches preserve the wording: `d0bcdb2` ("binder per-invocation retry inter-attempt timing", 2026-06-XX) appended the immediate-re-issue sentence to the same paragraph without altering the interleaving clause, and `f5e89f4` ("enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set", 2026-06-04) only relocated the paragraph into `spec_topics/binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md` as part of the file split. The ambiguity is therefore not a regression — it has been latent in the paragraph since the per-invocation budget was first defined, and was simply not surfaced by the earlier review that motivated `e4d2c7d`.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the interleaving clause in the `per-invocation-retry-budget` paragraph so it states explicitly whether a cross-class failure fires the other class's retry as a further binder call. Resolve the ambiguity in favour of firing the cross-class retry, grounding the resolution against `#hc3-d`, whose `1 + 1 + 1 = 3` decomposition presupposes both retry slots can fire on an interleaved chain. Reference the most-recent-failure rule (`#hc3-e`) for which system-note row the chain surfaces.
