## Finding

The per-invocation retry-budget paragraph in `determinism-cancellation-failure.md` describes interleaved failure chains with the sentence:

> A transport failure observed on the retry of a malformed envelope consumes the transport budget (not a second malformed attempt); a malformed envelope observed on the retry of a transport failure symmetrically consumes the malformed budget.

"Consumes the transport budget" is observably ambiguous over two implementations:

- **Reading A (observation consumes the slot).** The chain is `call 1 = malformed initial; call 2 = malformed retry, which fails transport`. The transport failure observed on call 2 marks the transport budget as used by virtue of *observation*; no further retry fires, and the chain terminates at **2 calls** with the transport row surfaced.
- **Reading B (the retry fires).** The transport failure on call 2 triggers the transport-class retry as **call 3** (`call 1 malformed → call 2 malformed-retry yields transport → call 3 transport-retry`). The chain reaches the HC3-d worst case of 3 calls; the surfaced row is whichever class is observed on call 3 (or success / AJV on a successful envelope).

The follow-up sentence "the runtime MUST issue at most **3** binder LLM calls per slash invocation (1 initial attempt + at most 1 transport-class retry + at most 1 malformed-envelope-class retry)" is consistent with both readings — Reading A simply never reaches the ceiling on this chain, Reading B does. Neither HC3-a/-b/-d in `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md` nor the V11f test list distinguishes the two. The user-visible system note also diverges: under Reading A the surfaced row is always the transport row from call 2; under Reading B it is the row matching whatever call 3 returned (possibly success, possibly the other class).

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Adopt **Reading B** (the retry fires) and rewrite the clause to make the firing explicit. Reading B is the only one consistent with HC3-d's worst-case sum of `1 initial + 1 transport-class retry + 1 malformed-envelope-class retry = 3`: under Reading A the interleaved chain can never reach 3 calls, which makes HC3-d's third term dead text for any chain that mixes classes. It is also the only reading under which "regardless of how the two interleave" delivers what it advertises — both retry slots remain usable across any interleaving order.

Concrete edit in `docs/spec_topics/binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md`, replacing the two interleaving sentences in the `<a id="per-invocation-retry-budget">` paragraph:

> A transport failure observed on the retry of a malformed envelope consumes the transport budget by *firing the transport-class retry as the next binder call* (the third and final call of the chain), not by suppressing it; symmetrically, a malformed envelope observed on the retry of a transport failure consumes the malformed-envelope budget by firing the malformed-envelope retry as the third call. In both interleavings the runtime issues exactly the HC3-d maximum of 3 calls (1 initial + the same-class retry that failed into the other class + the other class's retry); the surfaced system note is the row matching the *most recent* failure observed on the third call, per the most-recent-failure rule below.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- **Success on call 3.** If the third call returns a `kind: "ok"` envelope whose `args` pass AJV, the loom runs normally; no system note is surfaced. The earlier interleaved failures are not retained.
- **AJV-on-`args` on call 3.** Routes through the no-retry HC3-c arm; the AJV row is surfaced (it is "the most recent failure" under the existing rule).
- **Cancellation between calls 2 and 3.** The abort-during-retry rule already in the paragraph ("An abort observed during any retry permitted by the budget above suppresses that retry and surfaces the cancelled-binder note immediately") covers this — the transport-class retry that would have fired as call 3 is suppressed and the cancelled-binder row is surfaced. No change needed there.
- **HC3-d wording.** No edit required to `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md` if Reading B is adopted — HC3-d's "1 + 1 + 1 = 3" decomposition already presupposes both retry slots can fire. If reviewers want belt-and-braces, add a one-line parenthetical to HC3-d: "(both retry slots may fire on any interleaving of the two classes; the chain terminates at most at the third call)."
- **V11f / V11f-T tests.** The HC3-d test must be tightened from "at most three LLM calls" to an exact-count assertion on the interleaved fixture: chain `[malformed, transport-on-retry, transport-retry]` issues exactly 3 calls and surfaces the row of the third-call outcome.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable implementers will diverge: one (Reading A) issues 2 binder calls and always surfaces the transport row on an interleaved chain; the other (Reading B) issues 3 and may surface success, transport, or malformed depending on call 3. The divergence is observable both in provider call-count telemetry (a 50 % cost delta for the failing slash-invocation hot path) and in the operator-visible system note, so a single conformance test against the same fixture will pass for one implementation and fail for the other.
