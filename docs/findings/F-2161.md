## Finding

The `CIO-1` … `CIO-6` REQ-IDs are coined and registered without ever stating what the prefix `CIO` stands for. The defining mention is in the *Identifier classes used on this page* paragraph of `hard-ceilings.md` ("The `CIO-1` … `CIO-6` markers are full REQ-IDs under the `CIO` prefix — the second of two prefixes co-owned by the `hard-ceilings/` subtree (the other being `CEIL`)"), and the rules themselves live in `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md` under the `## Interaction between ceilings` section (anchor `#ceiling-interaction-order`). The expansion "Ceiling Interaction Order" is strongly inferable from the section title and the anchor slug, but it is never written out anywhere in the corpus.

`CIO` is the one hard-ceilings-cluster prefix whose letters do not telegraph their meaning. `CEIL` reads as "ceiling", `HC3` as "hard ceiling 3", `NOCEIL` as "no ceiling"; `CIO` decomposes to nothing self-evident. A reader encountering a bare `CIO-3` back-reference on another page — e.g. `errors-and-results/error-model.md` (ERR-16), `query/query-tool-loop.md`, `glossary.md`, or `implementation-notes.md` — sees an opaque three-letter token with no in-place gloss and no expansion at the anchor site.

The REQ-ID prefix table (`governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-a.md` / `-b.md`) is intentionally a two-column `Page | Prefix` table and never expands any prefix; this is a deliberate convention (GOV-3 treats prefixes as opaque uppercase tokens), so the table is not the place to fix this and adding a `Description` column there would break the table's uniform shape. The narrow, convention-preserving fix is a one-time parenthetical gloss at the prefix's defining mention.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** 96b80ec — pi-loom spec: resolve "Hard-ceiling interaction paragraph: missing anchor, undefined jargon, and bundled ordering rules" (2026-05-07, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** `git log -S 'CIO-1' --reverse -- docs/` and `git log -G 'second of two prefixes co-owned'` place the first appearance of the `CIO-N` markers in `96b80ec`, the commit that reformatted the fixed cross-ceiling evaluation order into a numbered REQ-ID list. That commit's own resolution text coined the prefix as an arbitrary token ("suggested prefix `CIO-`, but any prefix that satisfies the … prefix-table check works") and never glossed the acronym. Later commits touched the construct without adding an expansion: `c4ad105` (2026-05-08) added the *Identifier classes used on this page* paragraph that the finding cites; `1d773d6` (2026-06-04) added the `hard-ceilings/ | CIO` prefix-table row; `0e2eb05` (2026-06-05) added the `#cio-n` anchors. The defect was present the moment the prefix was coined and was never introduced by a later regression.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Expand the acronym once, at its defining mention in the *Identifier classes used on this page* paragraph of `hard-ceilings.md`: change "full REQ-IDs under the `CIO` prefix" to "full REQ-IDs under the `CIO` (Ceiling Interaction Order) prefix". This is a single parenthetical gloss at the one site that introduces the prefix; it preserves the deliberate two-column, no-description shape of the REQ-ID prefix table and the GOV-3 opaque-token convention. Use the expansion "Ceiling Interaction Order", consistent with the section title `## Interaction between ceilings` and the `#ceiling-interaction-order` anchor slug already in use on both `ceilings-3-and-4.md` and `overview-and-orientation.md`. Do not add a `Description` column to the prefix table and do not gloss the other prefixes (`CEIL`, `HC3`, `NOCEIL`), whose letters are already self-evident.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader who meets a bare `CIO-N` citation away from the defining page cannot decode the prefix without navigating to the anchor and inferring the expansion from the section title. No runtime behaviour or implementer decision diverges; the cost is reader/auditor legibility only.
