## Finding

`version-bump-step2.md` is the file for step 2 of the Pi-version-bump procedure, and it contains two independent letter-keyed enumerations:

1. An *editorial-review checklist* of host presuppositions with items `(a)`–`(ak)`, where item `(a)` is the extension-instance-survival open question.
2. A numbered sub-section "2. Re-run the SDK surface-inventory tests" with its own internal sub-items **(a)** "Positive direction — literal-read of the imported namespace" and **(b)** "Negative direction — inventory-closure audit over `src/`".

The phrase **"step 2(a)"** is used 32 times on the page as the escalation anchor in every SHOULD-level item — e.g. *"escalates to a build-time pin (on the same footing as the seven-capability literal-read assertion under step 2(a) above)"* — and is also the citation form used from `version-bump-step2b.md`, `version-bump-triggers.md`, `audit-wire-and-canary.md`, the `V18c` plan leaves, and `V18d-version-bump-acceptance.md`. Because the file *is* step 2, "step 2(a)" parses just as naturally as "step 2's checklist item (a)" — pointing at the extension-instance-survival audit — as it does as "step 2, sub-section 2, sub-item (a)" — pointing at the literal-read assertion. Surrounding prose (`"the seven-capability literal-read assertion under …"`) usually disambiguates, but the bare token "step 2(a)" appears in plan-leaf prose, in commit-message templates, and in cross-page citations where the disambiguating context does not travel with it.

The collision compounds the existing ambiguity that item `(a)` of the editorial-review checklist itself is non-trivial (the unresolved Pi-lifecycle open question on which `V9l` is gated), so a contributor reading `V9l`'s blocked-leaf note and following `step2.md#bump-checklist-instance-survival` may reasonably read a downstream "step 2(a)" reference as pointing back to that anchor rather than at the surface-inventory assertion.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Re-label the internal sub-items as 2(i)/2(ii)

**Approach.** Rename the numbered sub-step's internal labels from `(a)` / `(b)` to `(i)` / `(ii)` (or `2.i` / `2.ii`); update the recurring escalation phrase across the ~32 SHOULD-level items to "step 2(i)" / "step 2(ii)"; update the four cross-citing pages (`version-bump-step2b.md`, `version-bump-triggers.md`, `audit-recognised-shapes.md`, `audit-wire-and-canary.md`) and the three `V18c`/`V18d` plan leaves; update the anchors `#bump-step-2-positive` and `#bump-step-2-negative` only if the slug rule warrants it (the existing slugs are concept-named, not letter-named, so they can stay).

**Spec edits.** ~32 occurrences in `version-bump-step2.md`; ~6 occurrences in `version-bump-step2b.md`; 2 in `version-bump-triggers.md`; 1 each in `audit-recognised-shapes.md` and `audit-wire-and-canary.md`; 5 in `V18c-version-bump-checklist.md` / `V18c-T-version-bump-checklist.md` / `V18d-version-bump-acceptance.md`.

**Pros.** Mechanical, grep-driven sweep; preserves the established Roman/letter distinction the spec already uses elsewhere (e.g. item (e)'s sub-outcomes `(e.i)` / `(e.ii)`, and steps 1/2/3/4/5 use Arabic numerals while their sub-items use letters); leaves the recurring escalation boilerplate's *shape* unchanged, minimising review surface; the resulting `step 2(i)` cannot collide with any editorial-review checklist letter.

**Cons.** Touches a large surface (~46 sites including plan leaves); cross-pages need to land in the same commit; the relabel does not, on its own, address the parallel finding that the escalation boilerplate repeats ~31× and is itself a triage problem.

**Risks.** A miss in any of the cross-citing pages leaves a dangling "step 2(a)" reference that now means something different; CI cannot catch this on its own without a one-off grep gate. The internal `(e.i)` / `(e.ii)` naming for item (e)'s sub-outcomes happens to use `(i)` / `(ii)` already, so a careless reader of a renamed `step 2(ii)` might briefly conflate it with `(e.ii)`.

### Option B — Replace the recurring phrase with a self-contained name

**Approach.** Keep the internal `(a)` / `(b)` sub-item labels (they read naturally inside their numbered sub-step). At the ~32 escalation sites, replace the bare token "step 2(a)" with a self-contained descriptive name — e.g. "the SDK surface-inventory literal-read assertion" — and replace "step 2(b)" with "the inventory-closure audit". Keep an explicit link (`[the SDK surface-inventory literal-read assertion](#bump-step-2-positive)`) for navigability. Update cross-citing pages and plan leaves to the same descriptive names.

**Spec edits.** Same site count as Option A but each edit is a phrase swap rather than a one-character relabel; the existing anchors `#bump-step-2-positive` / `#bump-step-2-negative` stay.

**Pros.** Removes the ambiguity at its root by retiring the structurally ambiguous notation rather than re-spelling it; the replacement phrase is self-describing in any context (commit-message guidance, plan-leaf prose, cross-page citation) without needing the reader to load the step-2 file; the resulting prose is longer but clearer; co-aligns with the spec's general preference for descriptive names over positional citations.

**Cons.** Higher edit cost per site (phrase + link vs. one letter); slightly inflates the boilerplate; "literal-read assertion" / "inventory-closure audit" still need to be the canonical names everywhere or a third synonym creeps in.

**Risks.** A future contributor who finds the descriptive phrase verbose may re-shorten it to "step 2(a)" and reintroduce the collision; only an editorial convention (or an automated check) prevents the regression.

### Recommendation

Resolve in **Option B** order — replace the recurring phrase with the self-contained descriptive name across the ~32 escalation sites in `version-bump-step2.md`, then the cross-citing pages and `V18c`/`V18d` plan leaves, then update `version-bump-step2b.md` to follow the same convention. The collision is rooted in a notation that overloads two enumerations on the same page; Option B retires the notation rather than re-spelling it, which is what the *multi-commit-interaction* introduction history calls for (the right fix addresses the *interaction* of the two letter-keyed lists, not either list in isolation). Implementer-watch items: pick canonical replacement phrases up front (literally "the SDK surface-inventory literal-read assertion" and "the inventory-closure audit"), keep the `#bump-step-2-positive` / `#bump-step-2-negative` anchors so existing inbound links survive, and run a final `rg -P 'step[- ]?2\([ab]\)'` sweep across `docs/spec_topics/` and `docs/plan_topics/` to confirm no stragglers remain.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

A contributor reading an isolated "step 2(a)" reference (in a downstream page, in a plan leaf, or in commit-message guidance) can plausibly resolve it to either the editorial-review extension-instance-survival item or the surface-inventory literal-read assertion. The two have completely different audit shapes — a free-form editorial determination recorded in the bump commit message vs. an `npm test`-gated assertion — and routing a fix to the wrong one leaves the actual gate unfixed while looking conformant on the diff.
