## Problem

`version-bump-step2.md` is the file for step 2 of the Pi-version-bump procedure and carries two independent letter-keyed enumerations: an editorial-review checklist with items `(a)`–`(ak)` (item `(a)` being the extension-instance-survival open question) and a numbered sub-step "2." whose internal sub-items are `(a)` the SDK surface-inventory literal-read assertion (anchor `#bump-step-2-positive`) and `(b)` the inventory-closure audit (anchor `#bump-step-2-negative`). The bare token "step 2(a)" appears 32 times as the escalation anchor and parses just as naturally as the checklist's item `(a)` as it does as the sub-step's sub-item `(a)`. In plan-leaf prose, commit-message templates, and cross-page citations the disambiguating surrounding context does not travel, so a reader can route a fix to the wrong referent — and the two have different audit shapes (a free-form editorial determination vs. an `npm test`-gated assertion).

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `e5cd514` (2026-05-09) "pi-loom spec: resolve 'T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit'" — introduced the numbered sub-step "2." with internal labels `(a)` Positive direction and `(b)` Negative direction, *and* coined the recurring phrase "step 2(a)" / "step 2(b)" as the escalation anchor.
- `52b9101` (2026-05-11) "pi-loom spec: resolve 'T24 — Fork-reason watcher closure leaves the extension in an unspecified, silently degraded state'" — introduced the *Editorial-review checklist for unpinned host presuppositions* with letter-keyed items `(a)`–`(d)` (later widened to `(ak)`) on the same page, layering a second letter-keyed enumeration on top of the one `e5cd514` had already established.

**History:** `git log --follow` on `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md` (and on its pre-split source `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` before the `81bf52a` "collapse spec/ section pages into spec_topics/" reorganisation) shows the numbered `2.` sub-step and its internal `(a)`/`(b)` labels first appearing in `e5cd514` together with the recurring "step 2(a)" / "step 2(b)" escalation phrase. The colliding editorial-review checklist entered two days later in `52b9101` as a four-item list (`(a)`–`(d)`); subsequent commits widened it to `(e)` and then through `(ak)` without revisiting the duplicate-letter problem, and each widening commit re-used the existing "step 2(a)" escalation boilerplate verbatim, replicating the ambiguity ~31 additional times. Neither introducing commit was wrong on its own — the collision is the product of the two letter-keyed enumerations co-existing on the same page, with the boilerplate-driven 32-fold repetition amplifying it.

## Solution approach

Rename the ambiguous bare token: replace "step 2(a)" with the self-contained descriptive name "the SDK surface-inventory literal-read assertion" and "step 2(b)" with "the inventory-closure audit" across the escalation sites in `version-bump-step2.md`, retaining an explicit link to the `#bump-step-2-positive` / `#bump-step-2-negative` anchors for navigability. Keep the internal `(a)` / `(b)` sub-item labels inside the numbered sub-step. Apply the same descriptive-name convention to the cross-citing pages (`version-bump-step2b.md`, `version-bump-triggers.md`, `audit-recognised-shapes.md`, `audit-wire-and-canary.md`) and the `V18c` / `V18d` plan leaves.

## Solution constraints
