## Finding

The single normative paragraph that enumerates the complete loom 1.0 routing of every `execute()` outcome at the code-side tool-call boundary — clean `{isError:false}` → `Ok(<text>)`; clean `{isError:true}` → `Err(CodeToolError{cause:"execution"})`; `execute()` throw → `Err(CodeToolError{cause:"execution"})` with coerced message; `loomAbort.signal`-aborted call → `Err(CodeToolError{cause:"cancelled"})`; non-conforming envelope → `loom/runtime/internal-error{tool-return-shape}` (deliberately off `CodeToolError`); non-settling Promise → blocks until cancellation; late settlement → silently discarded per CNCL-1/CNCL-2/CNCL-3 with a swallowing handler installed before the first microtask — lives in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md` as the `*Outcome routing summary.*` paragraph immediately after the envelope-lowering rules.

The contract surface this paragraph defines is the contract loom *authors* observe at `<name>(args)` call sites; the topical home for that surface is `docs/spec_topics/tool-calls.md`. What `tool-calls.md` carries today is fragmentary: its `**Failures.**` paragraph enumerates the four `CodeToolError.cause` members, and its `*Outcome enumeration (normative).*` block lists the three outcomes routed *off* `CodeToolError` (non-settling Promise, non-conforming return shape, post-cancel resolution, plus the `.loom`-adapter pre-evaluation throw). Both then defer to the host-interfaces page for "the lowering rule" rather than restating it. A reader who lands on `tool-calls.md` to find out what `<name>(args)` returns for each disposition of `execute()` cannot get the answer without chasing a cross-link into a PIC page whose title (Pi Integration Contract — Host interfaces) signals host-shape pinning, not author-facing surface contract. PIC-only readers and tool-calls-only readers each see half of the same total enumeration.

The split also fragments anchoring: `coverage-matrix.md` token `cka-46` (claimed by `V14a`) points at `host-interfaces-core.md#tool-execution-from-loom-code` for the accepted-path lowering MUSTs, while `cka-13` (`tool-calls.md`, claimed by `V14a`/`V14b`/`V14c`/`V14e`) carries the off-`CodeToolError` dispositions — splitting one author-facing contract surface across two coverage tokens in two files, with the per-channel routing summary anchored only on the PIC side.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-commit
**Introducing commits:** `11ef3b9` (2026-05-07) — *pi-loom spec: resolve "Tool failure modes beyond throw / isError: true unspecified"*
**History:** `git log -G "Outcome routing summary" --diff-filter=A` shows commit `11ef3b9` as the sole content-bearing introduction of the `*Outcome routing summary.*` paragraph; that commit added the paragraph to `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` immediately after the envelope-lowering rules in §*Tool execution from loom code*. The same commit, in the same diff, added the `*Outcome enumeration (normative).*` block to `spec_topics/tool-calls.md` — listing the three off-`CodeToolError` outcomes and explicitly deferring to "the lowering rule in [Pi Integration Contract — Tool execution from loom code]". The placement split was therefore introduced in a single editorial act, not by drift. Subsequent commits only refined the prose: `2ebe288` (post-cancel `unhandledRejection` discard) and `bf85092` (`<underlying-error string truncated>` re-wording) edited the existing paragraph in place, and `f5e89f4` migrated `pi-integration-contract.md` to the `pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md` subdirectory layout without moving the paragraph between topical homes. No earlier revision placed any equivalent of the per-disposition routing summary in `tool-calls.md`; the file the finding flags as the natural home has never carried it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Move the `*Outcome routing summary.*` paragraph verbatim to `tool-calls.md`

**Approach.** Cut the `*Outcome routing summary.*` paragraph (line 93 of `host-interfaces-core.md`), paste it under `tool-calls.md` §Failures immediately after the `*Outcome enumeration (normative).*` block (or fold the two blocks together: the enumeration becomes the per-outcome bullet list and the routing summary becomes the by-`cause` truth table). Replace the original paragraph with a one-line forward reference: "*Outcome routing summary lives at [Tool Calls — Outcome routing](../tool-calls.md#outcome-routing).* The envelope-lowering rules above pin the per-field mechanics that the by-cause truth table consumes."

**Spec edits.**
- `docs/spec_topics/tool-calls.md` — add `<a id="outcome-routing"></a>` anchor, paste the paragraph (rewriting relative paths: `../cancellation.md` → `./cancellation.md`, `../overview-and-orientation.md` → `./overview-and-orientation.md`, `../errors-and-results/queryerror-variants.md` → `./errors-and-results/queryerror-variants.md`, `../tool-calls.md` self-reference dropped).
- `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md` — replace the paragraph with the one-line forward reference; keep the envelope-shape and lowering-mechanics prose (it is genuinely PIC-topical — it pins what Pi's `{content, isError}` envelope looks like and how the runtime walks it).
- `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md` — re-point `cka-46`'s anchor citation from `host-interfaces-core.md#tool-execution-from-loom-code` to `tool-calls.md#outcome-routing` for the by-cause summary; keep `cka-46` on the host-interfaces page for the envelope-walking mechanics, or split into two tokens if the auditor wants per-anchor granularity.
- `docs/plan_topics/V14a-tool-calls.md`, `V14a-T-tool-calls.md` — update the `**Spec.**` heading list to cite `tool-calls.md#outcome-routing` for the routing matrix and keep the `host-interfaces-core.md#tool-execution-from-loom-code` citation for the envelope-lowering MUSTs.

**Pros.**
- The author-observable contract (what does `<name>(args)` return for each outcome?) lives where authors look (`tool-calls.md`).
- The two halves of the existing surface — `*Outcome enumeration*` (which outcomes exist) and the routing summary (where each one surfaces) — sit together; readers no longer chase a cross-link to complete the picture.
- `V14a`'s contract surface becomes citable from a single anchor in a single file.

**Cons.**
- Touches 5 files (one spec page edited, one spec page reduced, one coverage row, two leaf-spec citations).
- The host-interfaces page's prose flow loses its closing paragraph; the forward reference is a slightly awkward terminal sentence.

**Risks.**
- Anchor inversions or stale forward references after the move. Mitigated by `docs/_tools/check_links.py` (the link/anchor validator pinned in commit `f5e89f4`).

### Option B — Promote the routing summary in place and add a same-strength duplicate in `tool-calls.md`

**Approach.** Leave the paragraph at `host-interfaces-core.md` for the PIC-side "complete view of how the runtime walks `execute()`" framing it currently has, but add a topically-shaped, equivalent-strength routing table or bulleted summary in `tool-calls.md` §Failures, marked normative and with a "see also" pointer in each direction. The two MUST stay in lockstep (a dual-source contract).

**Spec edits.**
- `docs/spec_topics/tool-calls.md` — insert a new "*Outcome routing summary (normative).*" paragraph (or a per-`cause` table) under §Failures.
- `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md` — keep the existing paragraph, add a "see [Tool Calls — Outcome routing]" parenthetical.
- `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md` — add a second coverage token (or a multi-anchor `cka-46`) so the two sites are both audited.

**Pros.**
- No reader of either page is left with half the picture.
- No re-pointing of existing leaf citations.

**Cons.**
- Dual-source normative content is a known drift hazard; the spec's audit / fingerprint tooling has no native "these two paragraphs MUST match" check, so future edits to one side can silently desynchronise.
- Adds bytes to two spec pages instead of moving them; weakly works against the 30KB recommended-cap discipline (`f5e89f4`).
- The very split the finding flags is preserved structurally — the fix is "make the duplicate explicit" rather than "single-source the contract".

**Risks.**
- The two paragraphs drift on a subsequent edit (e.g. a future revision additively widens `CodeToolError.cause` and only one side is updated). The spec-review lens that produced this finding would catch the drift on the next pass, not at write time.

### Recommendation

Take Option A. The defect entered the spec in a single commit (`11ef3b9`) as part of a deliberate "pin total `execute()` coverage" edit; the placement split is an artefact of that commit choosing the PIC page as the anchoring site because the envelope-lowering rules already lived there, not a load-bearing design choice. Moving the by-`cause` summary into `tool-calls.md` and leaving the envelope-walking mechanics on the PIC page restores the spec's intended division of labour: PIC pins what the host's surface looks like; `tool-calls.md` pins what the author observes. The forward reference from `host-interfaces-core.md` keeps the cross-link a PIC reader needs without re-stating the contract.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The `details.shape_check` discriminator pin (`tool-return-shape`) the routing summary cites lives in `diagnostics/code-registry-runtime.md`; the moved paragraph's link to it must use the correct relative path from `tool-calls.md` (`./diagnostics/code-registry-runtime.md`).
- The paragraph references `CNCL-1`, `CNCL-2`, `CNCL-3` and the swallowing-handler MUSTs from `cancellation.md`; relative paths flip from `../cancellation.md#cncl-1` (PIC page) to `./cancellation.md#cncl-1` (tool-calls.md).
- The `[Tool Calls]` self-link inside the current paragraph (the "open-struct seam in Tool Calls" parenthetical pointing to `../tool-calls.md`) becomes an in-page anchor link in the moved version; verify it resolves to the `loom 1.0 seam — per-call timeout` callout that already lives in the same file.
- `coverage-matrix.md` `cka-46`'s text explicitly lists *both* the accepted-path envelope-lowering MUSTs and excludes "the non-conforming-shape / non-settling-Promise dispositions". If `cka-46` is re-pointed at the new `tool-calls.md` anchor, that exclusion note becomes circular (the new anchor *includes* the excluded outcomes). Either split `cka-46` into two tokens (envelope-walking on PIC, by-cause routing on tool-calls.md) or rewrite its exclusion clause.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Two readers approaching the same author-observable contract from their natural entry points see only their half: a `tool-calls.md` reader sees a closed enum and a list of off-`CodeToolError` outcomes but not the per-disposition lowering matrix; a PIC reader sees the matrix but encounters `CodeToolError` as an alias to a pre-existing concept. Implementers can still produce a working system by chasing the cross-link, and no behavioural contract is undefined — but the routing-by-`cause` truth table that `V14a` needs as a single fixture is split across two files, two coverage tokens, and two anchors.
