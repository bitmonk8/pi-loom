## Finding

The opening paragraph of `active-invocation-registry.md` ends its discussion of the `invocationId` registry-side / wire-side casing asymmetry with:

> The sibling `loom` field happens to spell identically on both sides because its token is already in snake-shape, not because the registry-side and wire-side names are uniformly identity-mapped.

This sentence is pure rationale about a naming coincidence, not a requirement. The two normative facts it leans on are already pinned earlier in the same paragraph: the `loom` field carries the invocation's canonical-key name verbatim, and "the wire shape on `details.event.loom` admits this slash-name verbatim on every arm." Given those, the registry-side and wire-side `loom` spellings are necessarily identical. The trailing sentence only explains *why* they coincide (the token is already snake-shaped) and warns against an inference — uniform identity-mapping across all fields — that the spec never asserts. An implementer gains no constraint from it; the immediately-preceding sentences fully specify the `invocationId` camelCase↔snake_case mapping and the `loom` verbatim rule.
