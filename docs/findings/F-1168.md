## Finding

The drain-state surface on `pi-integration-contract/drain-state-contract.md` and `pi-integration-contract/session-only-degraded-state.md` carries four independent naming defects. They share a single page-cluster but are otherwise unrelated; bundling them in one fix is what produced this review entry, and untangling them is the first thing this finding asks the next fixer to do.

1. **`drainStateTag` (internal field) vs `tag` (snapshot key) is introduced too late.** The *Fields* paragraph introduces the field as `drainStateTag` and the *Methods* writer bullets continue using that name; the snapshot returned by `readDrainState()` then exposes the same field under the shorter key `tag`, and dispatch sites read `snapshot.tag === "shutting-down"`. The disambiguation sentence ("The internal field is named `drainStateTag`; the public snapshot key … is the shorter `tag`. The rename is intentional …") is buried mid-*Methods*, after the writer methods have been listed and before `readDrainState` itself is specified. A reader who scans *Fields* and then jumps to the `readDrainState` bullet meets both names without the bridge. Note: the unification sweep that would collapse one name to the other is explicitly out of scope for loom 1.0 (the *Methods* paragraph enumerates the sweep cost — `initDrainStateTag`, `readDrainState`, the `"loomRegistry.initDrainStateTag"` `details.call` label, *Host prerequisites* clause (a), and the *unset tag fallback* prose) so the editorial fix is reader-orientation, not renaming.

2. **`markRuntimeDegraded` breaks the field-name-based naming convention its siblings use.** Three of the four methods on the `LoomRegistry` drain-state surface — `drain`, `initDrainStateTag`, `readDrainState` — name the field they touch (in full or as its `DrainState` prefix). The fourth, `markRuntimeDegraded`, names the *effect* and silently hides which field it writes (`drainStateTag = "degraded-needs-reload"`). A reader pattern-matching from the other three would not predict that this method writes the same field as `initDrainStateTag`. The asymmetry is internal-shape only — the method's behavioural contract is pinned — but it is a real inconsistency on the four-method surface that the *Methods* paragraph itself promotes to a closed editorial convention.

3. **`## Emergent fast-path (sub-steps 2–3 only)` mislabels deliberately-pinned behaviour.** The body of the section reads "The fast-path framing applies to **sub-steps 2 and 3 only** … sub-step 2's per-entry stamp + abort and sub-step 3's bounded await handle that on the same footing as the always-tear-down branch. A session-only reason does **not** skip sub-step 4 or any other sub-step. This fast-path is available only for the five enumerated reasons …" — i.e., a normatively-scoped, design-by-construction behaviour. *Emergent* suggests the opposite (incidental, falling out of other rules, not deliberately specified), and a reader who treats the heading as a sign-post may infer the section is non-normative commentary. The body is normative.

4. **"Session-only reasons" / "session-only" has no glossary entry though it is load-bearing across SM-4 / SM-5 / SM-6 / SM-3b, host-prerequisites clauses (a)–(d), and the entire `session-only-degraded-state.md` page.** The term is italicised at first use (a coined-term marker the spec uses elsewhere), and it picks out the partition `{"new", "resume", "fork"}` of the `SessionShutdownEvent['reason']` closed set whose semantics — "Pi swaps the user session without tearing the extension runtime down" — is the entire premise of the degraded-state branch. The glossary already contains terms of similar weight (`always-log set`, `callable set`, `respond-repair`, `surface-set closure`); the omission of *session-only reasons* leaves a reader without a single canonical anchor for the partition.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

The finding bundles four independent obligations on the same page cluster. They must be addressed as four separate edits, in the order below, so each lands on a stable baseline and the lens surface presented to the next review pass stays small.

### Option A — Hoist the `drainStateTag` / `tag` disambiguation to the head of *Fields*

**Approach.** Add a one-sentence disambiguation at the head of the *Fields* paragraph in `drain-state-contract.md`, immediately after the field-tuple is introduced and before `drainStateTag` is named, pairing both names before either is used in a writer/reader contract. Do **not** rename either `drainStateTag` or `tag` — the *Methods* paragraph explicitly defers the unification sweep and enumerates the cost (`initDrainStateTag`, `readDrainState`, the `"loomRegistry.initDrainStateTag"` `details.call` label, *Host prerequisites* clause (a), the *unset tag fallback* prose). Leave the existing mid-*Methods* disambiguation sentence intact as the source-of-truth rationale.

**Spec edits.**
- `drain-state-contract.md` *Fields* opener: prepend a sentence of the form "*Fields.* The contract carries two fields, exposed to readers as the snapshot tuple `{ drained, tag }` that `readDrainState()` returns (`tag` is the snapshot key for the field the rest of this section names `drainStateTag`; the rename is intentional and out of scope for loom 1.0 to unify — see *Methods* below for the full rationale). (1) A boolean `drained` flag …"
- Leave the existing mid-*Methods* disambiguation paragraph unchanged.

**Pros.** Minimal surface area; no rename; reader meets both names paired on first contact; existing *Methods* cross-references remain valid; resolves the same issue as the in-store F-0688.

**Cons.** Adds a forward reference (*Fields* → *Methods*) that already exists in the opposite direction; does not address sub-issues B/C/D.

**Risks.** None observable; the loose alternation among `snapshot.tag`, "drain-state tag", and `drainStateTag` in the *idempotent* clause does not need a sweep — once the *Fields* opener pairs the names, surrounding prose reads unambiguously.

### Option B — Rename `markRuntimeDegraded` → `setDrainStateTagDegraded` (or align the four siblings on a single convention)

**Approach.** Bring the fourth method on the `LoomRegistry` drain-state surface into the same field-name-based convention its three siblings use. Two acceptable spellings: `setDrainStateTagDegraded(): void` (matches `initDrainStateTag` in keying on the field name and disambiguates *set* from *init*'s once-only contract); or, more invasively, rename all four methods on the surface to a uniform pattern (e.g. `drainSet` / `drainStateTagInit` / `drainStateTagSetDegraded` / `drainStateRead`). The recommendation here is the narrower of the two — rename only `markRuntimeDegraded`.

**Spec edits.**
- `drain-state-contract.md` *Methods* paragraph: rename the third method bullet's identifier and update the in-bullet `details.call` label string (`"loomRegistry.markRuntimeDegraded"` → `"loomRegistry.setDrainStateTagDegraded"`).
- `drain-state-contract.md` *idempotent* clause prose: sweep the four references to `markRuntimeDegraded` (including in the *all-three-throw corner case* and *Read-failure fallback* paragraphs).
- `session-only-degraded-state.md`: sweep the *Drain-state tag write* section (one prose reference + one parenthetical), *Co-emission rules*, *Idempotent re-entry and the all-three-throw corner case*, *Accepted loom 1.0 residual gaps* (Compound-failure case), and acceptance branches (i), (j), (k), (m).
- `patch-skew-degradation.md` *Per-step isolation* paragraph: sweep the `loomRegistry.markRuntimeDegraded` `details.call` label.
- `host-prerequisites.md` clause (a): no edit (does not name the method).
- Plan-leaf citation strings: `V9l-session-only-degraded-branch.md` *Adds* and *Tests* sweep `markRuntimeDegraded` (two occurrences); `V9l-T-session-only-degraded-branch.md` likewise (one occurrence).
- `diagnostics/code-registry-host.md`: no edit (the `loom/host/session-shutdown-runtime-degraded` row narrates the post-sub-step-4 transition without naming the method).

**Pros.** Restores the editorial convention the surface already half-keeps; reader can predict the field a method touches from its name; preserves the surface's closedness and avoids a third naming style.

**Cons.** Wide-surface sweep (≥10 sites across 5 spec pages plus 2 plan-leaves); the *Methods* paragraph's "an editorial rename of any method MUST sweep all four sites in the same edit" rule applies and would catch any miss.

**Risks.** Plan-leaf citation drift if the sweep misses V9l/V9l-T (caught by any in-store traceability pass). `details.call` label is operator-visible in diagnostic JSON; a contributor consuming the label across releases would observe the rename. Acceptable on the same loom-1.0-acceptance footing as other internal-naming sweeps the spec already permits, but worth a one-line note in `model-changes-and-non-goals.md` or the diagnostic-shape conventions if such a note is the norm for label renames.

### Option C — Retitle `## Emergent fast-path (sub-steps 2–3 only)` to remove the misleading *Emergent* connotation

**Approach.** Rename the H2 to a heading that signals the section is normatively scoped, not incidental. Concrete spellings, in decreasing order of fidelity to the existing wording: `## Sub-steps 2–3 fast-path on session-only reasons`; `## Bounded-fast-path scope (sub-steps 2–3 only)`; `## Session-only fast-path is sub-steps 2–3 only`. Within the section body, replace the single in-prose phrase "This fast-path is available only for the five enumerated reasons" with the new heading's wording so the section opener and heading agree.

**Spec edits.**
- `session-only-degraded-state.md`: rename the H2 (one line) and align the section's closing-sentence phrasing (one line).
- No anchor change is required (the H2 has no `<a id>` and no inbound link references it as `#emergent-fast-path` per a corpus-wide grep — confirm before landing).

**Pros.** One-file, one-section edit; restores the heading's signposting function; aligns the section's surface with its normative body.

**Cons.** None material.

**Risks.** None observable. Worth confirming via `grep -rn 'emergent-fast-path\|Emergent fast-path' docs/` that no inbound link or cross-reference relies on the old heading text before landing.

### Option D — Add a *session-only reasons* glossary entry

**Approach.** Add an entry to `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` for the term *session-only reasons* (the spec's existing italicised coined-term spelling) with a one-paragraph descriptive definition and a `See:` pointer to the canonical owner page. Concrete form:

> **session-only reasons** — The partition `{"new", "resume", "fork"}` of the `SessionShutdownEvent['reason']` closed set on which Pi swaps the user session without tearing the extension runtime down (as opposed to the partition `{"quit", "reload"}` on which Pi tears the runtime down after the `session_shutdown` handler returns). The partition is the premise of the degraded-state branch (SM-3b/4/5/6): on a *session-only reason*, the watchers are still closed by sub-step 4 and the runtime is left in a deterministically degraded state until operator-issued `/reload`. See: [Pi Integration Contract — Host prerequisites for the degraded-state branch, clause (d)](./pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md#degraded-state-host-prerequisites), [Pi Integration Contract — Session-only degraded state](./pi-integration-contract/session-only-degraded-state.md).

**Spec edits.**
- `glossary.md`: insert the entry in alphabetical position (between `respond-repair` and `surface-set closure`, or wherever the alphabetisation lands it).
- No edits to consuming pages; the term is already used consistently and is now anchored.

**Pros.** Single-file edit; adds a canonical anchor that the other three options can cross-reference if useful; reduces the cost of every subsequent finding that touches the same partition.

**Cons.** None material.

**Risks.** None observable.

### Recommendation

Address the four obligations as four separate edits, in this order:

1. **Option D first** (glossary entry). Smallest scope, single file, no risk of clashing with the other three, and gives the subsequent edits a canonical anchor to point at if they want one.
2. **Option C second** (heading rename). Single file, single section, no cross-file sweep, lands cleanly on top of D.
3. **Option A third** (hoist the disambiguation). Single file, single paragraph, no rename — the `drain-state-contract.md` *Fields* opener gets one new sentence. Lands cleanly on top of C.
4. **Option B last** (method rename). Widest-surface sweep, touches plan-leaves, requires the *Methods* paragraph's "rename sweeps all four sites" rule to be satisfied. Landing it after A means the *Fields* opener already pairs `drainStateTag` and `tag` before the sweep starts, so the renamed method bullet does not need to re-establish the disambiguation in flight.

Each step is its own commit and its own loop. Do **not** bundle: a single commit covering all four would land a wide diff and re-expose all four lens surfaces in the same review pass, which is what produced the bundled finding in the first place. The bimodal-decomposition rule applies: each obligation is independently testable, independently loopable, and independently revertable.

Implementer edge cases the next fixer must watch:

- Under option A, leave the mid-*Methods* disambiguation sentence intact verbatim — it is the source-of-truth rationale and enumerates the sweep cost; deleting it would orphan the new opener's forward reference.
- Under option B, the rename sweep MUST cover the `details.call` string literal (`"loomRegistry.markRuntimeDegraded"`) as well as the prose identifier; the `loomRegistry.*` `details.call` namespace is the diagnostic-channel anchor for the per-call swallow rule and a missed sweep would silently emit a stale label.
- Under option B, do **not** also rename `markRuntimeDegraded`'s effect-keyed semantics (the method is normatively unconditional/state-independent); only the identifier changes.
- Under option C, confirm via corpus-wide grep that no cross-reference links to `#emergent-fast-path` before landing the H2 rename; if any do, sweep them in the same edit.
- Under option D, place the entry in alphabetical order with the existing entries (the page's preamble says "alphabetised list"); the canonical-owner pointer should name `host-prerequisites.md` clause (d) first (the partition's normative source) and `session-only-degraded-state.md` second (the page that consumes the partition).
- None of the four edits unblock V9l — `V9l` remains blocked on the host-prerequisites clause-(a) resolution under all options.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

None of the four sub-defects cause an implementer to produce the wrong behaviour: the field-tuple state space, the three `readDrainState` arms, the unconditional `markRuntimeDegraded` write contract, the *Emergent fast-path* section's normative body, and the partition `{"new", "resume", "fork"}` are all pinned unambiguously by surrounding prose. The cost is reader friction — a fresh reader spends extra cycles reconciling `drainStateTag`/`tag`, pattern-matching `markRuntimeDegraded` against its three field-named siblings, deciding whether *Emergent fast-path* is normative, and chasing the *session-only reasons* partition across pages without a glossary anchor — and a contributor-side cost when the implementer's name choices have to be reconciled against the four spec-pinned identifiers. The defects are durable, not transient: they have stood since the surface entered the corpus (see *Issue introduction* below) and have survived every subsequent edit.
