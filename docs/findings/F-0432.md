## Problem

`plan.md` §How to use this plan, step 2 opens the transitive-completeness obligation with the modal verb "can introduce" ("If the new leaf **can introduce** an executable REQ-ID … add it to `H5b`'s **Deps**"). The authoritative clause it defers to — `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline (*Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance*) and the §Leaf format *Deps* summary — instead keys on a present-state predicate, "whose `Tests.` bullets carry", and excludes a co-witness leaf that merely re-cites an already-closed REQ-ID. The two readings diverge: a restructured co-witness leaf does not qualify under the authoritative predicate but reads as a qualifying trigger under step 2's "can introduce", so an author following step 2 would over-include it in `H5b`'s `Deps`.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `ab8e297` (2026-06-11, "pi-loom plan: resolve \"Transitive-completeness obligation invisible at leaf authoring\"") — authored the transitive-completeness pointer in three places at once (plan.md step 2, conventions.md §Leaf format *Deps* summary, and the §REQ-ID discipline authoritative clause), all using the verb "can introduce".
- `1cd23a6` (2026-06-18, "pi-loom plan: resolve \"Transitive-completeness trigger verb 'can introduce' is ambiguous\"") — corrected **conventions.md only** (both the §Leaf format *Deps* summary and the authoritative §REQ-ID discipline clause) from "can introduce" to "whose `Tests.` bullets carry", and did **not** touch `docs/plan.md`.
**History:** `git log -G 'can introduce an executable REQ-ID' -- docs/plan.md` returns only `ab8e297`; the verb in plan.md step 2 has never been revised since it was authored. `git show 1cd23a6 --stat` confirms its diff covers `findings/F-0305.md`, `plan-review.md`, and `conventions.md` — plan.md is absent. The divergence is therefore a partial-fix interaction: both files agreed on "can introduce" after `ab8e297`; `1cd23a6` moved conventions.md to the present-state "carry" while leaving plan.md's paraphrase behind, creating the mismatch this finding reports.

## Solution approach

In `docs/plan.md` §How to use this plan, step 2, rewrite the trigger clause's opening verb from the modal "can introduce" to the present-state predicate "whose `Tests.` bullets carry" (the wording `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline and its §Leaf format *Deps* summary already use), so step 2 mirrors the authoritative clause. Leave step 2's existing pointer to `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline intact.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: `conventions.md` is read-only for this fix — it remains the authoritative owner of the exact triggers; step 2 mirrors that clause and does not redefine it.
