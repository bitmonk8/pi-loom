## Finding

The "Pi version bump procedure" is split across four sub-pages — `version-bump-intro.md`, `version-bump-step2.md`, `version-bump-step2b.md`, and `version-bump-triggers.md` — with the contributor-facing entry point being the intro page (it carries the procedure's `<a id="pi-version-bump-procedure">` anchor and the cross-references from elsewhere in the spec). The summary that describes *what the procedure produces* — outputs (a) green build-time test run, (b) the single bump commit's contents, (c) green end-to-end runtime evidence, plus the "MUST NOT be merged" gate — is the only place in the procedure where a reader can see those three outputs together. It currently sits as the trailing paragraph of `version-bump-triggers.md`, on the page that covers steps 5–7.

A contributor entering the procedure through `version-bump-intro.md` (the path every cross-link in the corpus and the per-step navigation drive them down) cannot see the procedure's outputs without reading all the way through the intro, the step-2 page, the step-2b page, and the steps-5–7 page. The outputs block is also load-bearing as a citation target: plan leaves and the coverage matrix cite "output (c)" by name (e.g. `V18d-version-bump-acceptance.md` and `coverage-matrix.md` row `cka-19` both name it), and the only definition of "output (a) / (b) / (c)" lives in this trailing paragraph — so the citation target is currently in the least-discoverable location.

The natural home for a procedure-output summary is the procedure intro, alongside the "this subsection enumerates the contributor checklist…" framing paragraph that already orients the reader.

While the procedure lived on a single page, the summary's "trailing" placement was the natural reader-sees-procedure-then-outputs flow and carried no navigation defect.

The split moved the summary paragraph along with steps 5–7 onto `version-bump-triggers.md` rather than promoting it onto the intro page; from that commit forward, "the end of the procedure" no longer coincides with "the page the reader entered through". The defect is the file-split commit's placement choice, not the original authoring of the summary.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move the trailing "The procedure produces no separate artefact file — its outputs are (a) … (b) … (c) …" paragraph from the end of `version-bump-triggers.md` to `version-bump-intro.md`, placing it immediately after the existing introductory paragraph that opens the **Pi version bump procedure** section (i.e. between that paragraph and the **Non-goals** sub-section) so a reader sees what the procedure produces before reading any step. Preserve the paragraph's prose verbatim. Give the summary a stable anchor — `<a id="procedure-outputs"></a>` is the natural slug since downstream plan citations already use the phrase "output (c)" — so the moved block has a citable name.

Co-update the three inbound citations that name `version-bump-triggers.md` as the home of "output (c)" to point at the new anchor on `version-bump-intro.md`:

- `docs/plan_topics/V18d-version-bump-acceptance.md` (the `Adds.` paragraph and the first Tests bullet).
- `docs/plan_topics/V18d-T-version-bump-acceptance.md` (the first Tests bullet).
- `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md` row `cka-19` (the spec-citation column).

The plan-side citations of the merge-blocking MUST itself ("a bump whose runtime-evidence run is red MUST NOT be merged at the candidate pin") that appear immediately after the output-list in the same paragraph travel with it — the sentence's home moves to the intro page along with the rest of the summary. `V18c` / `V18c-T` already cite `version-bump-intro.md` in their `Spec.` lists and need no edit; the move strengthens those leaves' existing entry-point citation.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- `version-bump-triggers.md` after the move ends on step 7's last sentence; no trailing punctuation or transition prose needs to be invented to replace the removed paragraph, because steps 5–7 are a numbered list whose final entry is itself terminal.
- The phrase "spec sentences a bump touches" inside output (b) carries an inline cross-link to `version-bump-intro.md#pi-version-bump-procedure` (`[step 1](...)`); after the move that link points back into the same page. Either retarget it to `#pi-version-bump-procedure` (an anchor self-reference is fine since it lands the reader on the page header) or simplify to "step 1 above"; do not delete the cross-reference entirely.
- Verify no other page in the corpus links into `version-bump-triggers.md` with a fragment that targets the moved paragraph (a quick `grep -rn 'version-bump-triggers.md#' docs/` suffices); at the time of this finding the inbound citations all name the page without a fragment.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A contributor following the cross-link into `version-bump-intro.md` cannot see the procedure's three outputs (build-time test run, bump-commit contents, runtime-evidence acceptance) without reading the entire four-page procedure. The outputs are also the named anchor for downstream plan-leaf citations of "output (c)", so the most-cited block of the procedure is the least-discoverable one. No runtime behaviour diverges; this is a navigation/orientation cost, not a correctness gap.
