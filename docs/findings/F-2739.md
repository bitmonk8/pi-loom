## Problem

The `V15a` invocation-core leaf (and its paired `V15a-T`) bundles eight independently red-testable obligations under one impl+tests pair, spanning four unrelated verification concerns: load-time path containment (INV-1), static parse-time resolution (the per-pass parse cache closing `cka-44`), runtime value/mode behaviour (INV-2, INV-3, FN-5, the fresh-vs-attach cross-mode matrix), and cancellation/concurrency (the `cka-47` checkpoint and the ERR-13 completed-callee-finality witness). Each concern carries its own setup, fixtures, and seam dependencies, so a single agent cannot hold all eight in scope in one pass. Because the `Ships when` gate fires only when all eight assert together, a partial implementation cannot ship a verified subset and a dropped or weakly-asserted facet ships silently â€” most easily the two cancellation facets, which were appended last and sit furthest from the core invocation path.

## Solution approach

Split `V15a` along its four verification-concern seams, retaining `V15a` as the load-time/static-resolution core (INV-1 containment plus the static-resolution per-pass parse cache and transitive walk) and extracting the runtime arg/options+final-value facets (INV-2, INV-3, FN-5 against `V3d`'s function-result seam), the fresh-vs-attach cross-mode matrix, and the cancellation facets (`cka-47` `invoke`-site checkpoint and the ERR-13 completed-callee-finality witness) into new leaves, mirroring the established `V15f`/`V15g`/`V15h` carve-out pattern. Carry each extracted facet's seam Deps onto its owning leaf and reduce `V15a`'s Deps to the load/static seams. Propagate the split through `coverage-matrix.md` (re-point the INV-1/INV-2/INV-3, `cka-44`, `cka-47`, and ERR-13 closing-leaf cells), `H5b`'s Deps range, `V17c`'s Ships-when `invoke`-checkpoint arm, and `plan.md`'s Â§V15 listing and Â§V17 interleave note.
