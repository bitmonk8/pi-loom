## Finding

The `operator` glossary entry (`docs/spec_topics/glossary.md`, `#operator`) distinguishes the operator from "the *caller* (which may be another loom via `invoke(...)` or a Pi prompt-mode user turn)." That parenthetical simultaneously names both senses the bare word *caller* is overloaded across: the **invoke-parent** sense (`invoke(...)`) and the **slash-caller** sense (the Pi prompt-mode user turn).

The `caller` entry (`#caller`) in the same glossary defines *caller* as having "two distinct senses" and instructs: "Authors and implementers SHOULD use the disambiguated terms `slash caller` and `invoke parent` whenever the bare word `caller` is ambiguous in context." The operator entry's parenthetical is exactly such an ambiguous context — it spans both senses in one phrase — yet uses the bare overloaded term instead of the disambiguated sub-terms. The glossary therefore fails to follow its own normative SHOULD at one of the clearest sites the guidance was written for.

This is a glossary self-consistency defect: the operator definition is still understandable, but it models the very pattern the `caller` entry tells authors to avoid, which undercuts the disambiguation guidance and leaves a reader unsure whether the bare-*caller* usage is intentional or stale.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `52219c4` (operator parenthetical, latent) → `d54b426` (caller-entry SHOULD disambiguation, defect-completing)
**History:** The operator entry's parenthetical "the *caller* (which may be another loom via `invoke(...)` or a Pi prompt-mode user turn)" was introduced by `52219c4` ("Address all 41 spec-review findings", 2026-05-06) and was benign at that time — the glossary carried no rule against the bare term. The `caller` entry's two-sense split plus the "SHOULD use the disambiguated terms `slash caller` and `invoke parent`" guidance was added later by `d54b426` ("pi-loom spec: resolve \"Glossary cluster: six independent defects in spec_topics/glossary.md\"", 2026-05-07), which is a descendant of `52219c4`. The inconsistency entered the corpus at `d54b426`: that commit created the disambiguation obligation without updating the pre-existing operator parenthetical to comply, leaving it stale.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In the `operator` entry, replace the overloaded parenthetical "the *caller* (which may be another loom via `invoke(...)` or a Pi prompt-mode user turn)" with a form that maps each clause to its disambiguated sub-term — e.g. "the *invoke parent* (another loom via `invoke(...)`) or the *slash caller* (a Pi prompt-mode user turn)". This brings the operator entry into compliance with the `caller` entry's SHOULD guidance at the site that most clearly triggers it.

Edge case for the fixer: keep the chosen wording in lockstep with whatever canonical form the `caller` entry settles on. If the related caller-entry finding resolves by demoting the SHOULD to a non-normative parenthetical (rather than registering the sub-terms), prefer a non-overloaded phrase such as "the upstream invoker, whether a slash dispatch or an `invoke(...)` call" so this entry does not lean on sub-terms the glossary no longer promotes.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The operator definition still conveys the correct meaning, so no implementer diverges in behaviour. The cost is a glossary that contradicts its own SHOULD-level disambiguation guidance at a representative site, weakening that guidance and inviting future authors to treat the bare overloaded *caller* as acceptable.
