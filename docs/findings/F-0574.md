## Finding

After the forwarding-listener throw-trap (`V17b`) and the checkpoint-granularity surface (`V17c`) were peeled out, `V17a` still carries two distinct, independently-shippable mechanisms in a single impl+`-T` pair:

1. **Forwarding contract** — `loomAbort` construction, forwarding the three entry-point signals (slash-command `ctx.signal`, tool-exposed `signal`, parent-`invoke` signal) into `loomAbort` (never `ctx.signal` directly), downward-only propagation, and abort-reason propagation (`CNCL-4`, including the byte-exact `"loom cancelled by agent_end"` synthesised message).
2. **Race semantics** — the late-settlement discard rules (`CNCL-1`/`CNCL-2`/`CNCL-3`), the no-retroactive-rewrite and no-tail-synthesis rules (`CNCL-5`/`CNCL-6`), and the three-side-channel swallowing-handler suppression at the `Checkpoint`-seam substrate.

The `Tests.` field holds nine-plus distinct assertions (`CNCL-1`…`CNCL-6`, three-path forwarding-into-`loomAbort`, downward-only propagation, and swallowing-handler substrate suppression), each an independent fixture. Per `conventions.md` §3, each leaf inside a slice should be the smallest feature that can ship and be tested independently. The forwarding contract is observable purely through the three entry-point harness paths; the race-semantics surface is observable purely through the `V8a` `Checkpoint`-seam substrate. The two share the `loomAbort` primitive but are otherwise separately exercisable, so a single leaf collapses two cohesion units into one red-green batch and forces the reviewer to track every cancellation invariant simultaneously.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (narrowed by later peels, not eliminated)
**Introducing commit:** `c6a664e` (2026-06-10, "pi-loom plan: build/update plan for spec.md + review") — `V17a` was created as a single leaf carrying the entire cancellation contract.
**History:** Two subsequent atomicity peels narrowed the leaf without separating the two surfaces this finding targets: `0bc84dd` (2026-06-11) peeled the forwarding-listener throw-trap into `V17b`, and `5b95143` (2026-06-18, "resolve 'V17a bundles forwarding contract and checkpoint-site wiring'") peeled the checkpoint-granularity surface into `V17c`. The forwarding-contract and race-semantics surfaces have been co-located in `V17a` continuously since `c6a664e`; the residual bundle is original plan structure, not a regression introduced by a later edit. The repeated peeling trajectory is the corpus's established response pattern to `V17a` over-bundling.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

This finding asks for a leaf split, which entails both authoring the split and retargeting every dependent's `Deps` and the coverage-matrix rows — a broad-blast-radius change across ~10 leaves plus the matrix. The two obligations must be ordered, and there is a genuine accept-as-residue alternative.

### Option A — Peel the race-semantics surface into a new leaf, retaining `V17a` as the forwarding-contract core

- **Approach:** Keep `V17a` (and `V17a-T`) as the forwarding-contract core — `loomAbort` construction, three-path forwarding, downward-only propagation, `CNCL-4`. Move the race-semantics surface (`CNCL-1`/`CNCL-2`/`CNCL-3` late-settlement discard, `CNCL-5`/`CNCL-6` no-rewrite/no-tail-synthesis, and the three-side-channel swallowing-handler substrate suppression) into a new V17-series leaf `<new>` with its `<new>-T` partner.
- **Plan edits:** strike the race-semantics bullets from `V17a` `Adds`/`Tests`/`Ships when` and add a scope-exclusion pointer to `<new>`; author `<new>`/`<new>-T` (`Spec.` = `cancellation.md` race sections; `Deps.` ≥ `<new>-T`, `V8a`, `V4d`, `H4a`, `H4b`); in `coverage-matrix.md` move the `CNCL-1,2,3` and `CNCL-5,6` rows' closing leaf and the `cka-33` substrate token from `V17a` to `<new>` (leave `CNCL-4 | V17a, V9g`); retarget the race-dependent `Deps` (`V9o`, `V13f`, `V14f`, `V15h`, `V4f`, and confirm `V4c`/`H7a`) from `V17a` to `<new>`; extend `H5b`'s `Deps` enumeration to include `<new>` and link it into `docs/plan.md`'s V17 list.
- **Spec edits:** None.
- **Pros:** Restores conventions §3 cohesion; smaller fixtures; consistent with the `V17b`/`V17c` peeling precedent; minimal-churn (core-dependents untouched).
- **Cons:** Broad blast radius across ~10 leaves plus the coverage-matrix; each dependent retarget needs per-leaf judgment.
- **Risks:** A dependent retargeted to the wrong sub-leaf produces a `Deps` edge satisfiable before the surface it needs exists; the new leaf's `Deps` must be re-derived, not copied.

### Option B — Accept the residual bundle as recorded atomicity residue

- **Approach:** Leave `V17a` unified and record a brief rationale that the forwarding contract and race semantics are kept together as one cohesive cancellation-core leaf, the two most-separable sub-mechanisms (`V17b`, `V17c`) having already been peeled.
- **Plan edits:** A one-line residue note on `V17a` (or in `conventions.md`'s residue inventory) stating the deliberate co-location.
- **Spec edits:** None.
- **Pros:** Zero blast radius; no risk of mis-pointed `Deps`.
- **Cons:** Leaves a nine-plus-fixture leaf against the conventions §3 bar.
- **Risks:** A future atomicity pass re-raises the same finding (third round on `V17a`).

### Recommendation

Take **Option A**. The peeling trajectory (`V17b`, then `V17c`) is the corpus's established response to `V17a` over-bundling, and the two residual surfaces are separately exercisable (entry-point harness vs `Checkpoint` seam). Order the work so the scope-bounding obligation lands first: **(1)** author `<new>` + `<new>-T` and move the race-semantics `Adds`/`Tests`/`Ships when` clauses and the three coverage-matrix rows onto it, leaving `V17a` green as the core; **(2)** only then retarget the dependent `Deps` edges onto `<new>`. Watch these edges: the swallowing per-site sites (`V9o`, `V13f`, `V14f`, `V15h`) and `V4f` move to `<new>`; `V17b`, `V17c`, `V9c`, `V9g`, `V9i`, `V9n`, `V11j` stay on `V17a`; `V4c` and `H7a` need explicit confirmation of which surface they consume. Coordinate which sub-leaf inherits `CNCL-3`'s "no second `RuntimeEvent`" assertion with whichever sub-leaf must gain the `V9d` `Deps` edge. Honour the no-invented-IDs policy: use `<new>`/`<new>-T` until the implementer allocates real IDs.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A single impl+`-T` pair carrying nine-plus independent cancellation fixtures across two distinct mechanisms enlarges the TDD red-green batch and forces a reviewer to hold every forwarding and race invariant in view at once. The leaf is still implementable as one unit and is pickable, so no gate breaks; the cost is review granularity and conformance to the `conventions.md` §3 smallest-shippable-feature rule, not correctness or pickability.
