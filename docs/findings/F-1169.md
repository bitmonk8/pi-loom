## Finding

`match` is a first-class expression form in loom, but its normative definition — pattern grammar, disambiguation rule, exhaustiveness contract, arm-syntax / LUB-typing rule, and runtime no-match panic — lives entirely in `errors-and-results/error-model.md`. `expressions.md` (the page whose lede declares itself the home for "the same grammar [that] applies wherever an expression is expected: the RHS of `let`, `if` / `match` scrutinees, function arguments, …") names `match` four times — in the lede, the *Not supported* list ("`match` inside `${...}`"), and the grammar-disambiguation rules (scrutinee parens, arm body single-expression) — but never defines it and never links forward to where it is defined. `grammar.md` carries the `MatchArm`/`ArmBody` productions and a back-reference to `error-model.md` for the surface; `control-flow.md` does not mention `match` at all.

The practical effect is one-way discoverability. A reader who treats `expressions.md` as the language reference encounters `match` four times as an undefined construct with no in-page cross-reference to its semantics. A reader of `error-model.md` finds the full surface but co-mingled with `Result`, `?`, `ERR-18`, runtime panics, and terminal outcomes — none of which are conceptually required to understand `match` itself (a `match` on a non-`Result` scrutinee is a perfectly ordinary loom expression). The implementation-side leaf `V4a` does cite both pages and so produces a correct implementation, but the spec-as-document fails to surface a canonical home that a language-side reader (or a `match`-only feature-page xref from a sibling page) can point at.

The finding's separate framing that "an agent must invent parse-time vs runtime no-match behaviour and the diagnostic" overstates the gap — the runtime `loom/runtime/match-error` panic is normatively pinned in `error-model.md` (Exhaustiveness paragraph), the parse-time `loom/parse/match-arm-type-mismatch` / `loom/parse/match-guard-not-supported` / `loom/parse/rest-pattern-not-supported` / `loom/parse/statement-in-arm-body` codes are in the parse registry, and `grammar.md` owns the arm-body production. The real defect is placement, not under-specification.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 53f5831 — spec: rewrite query primitive, system prompts, and schema syntax (2026-05-03, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** `match` entered the corpus in commit 53f5831 as part of the query/`Result`/`?` rewrite of the monolithic `spec.md`; the rewrite created the *Expression Sublanguage* and *Errors and Results* sections in the same commit and placed the full `match` definition (pattern grammar, exhaustiveness, arm syntax) under *Errors and Results* with only naming references from the expression section. The subsequent reorganisations preserved that placement: `fecb504` split the monolith into `spec_topics/errors-and-results.md` and `spec_topics/expressions.md` as separate files, and `f5e89f4` (the 30 KB / 100 KB cap split) moved the `match` paragraphs into `errors-and-results/error-model.md` without revisiting which page should own them. The placement decision has therefore never been re-examined since the construct was first authored.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Move the normative `match` semantics into `expressions.md`

**Approach.** Relocate the `match` expression / Pattern grammar / Disambiguation / Exhaustiveness / Arm syntax paragraphs from `error-model.md` into a new `## `match` expression` section in `expressions.md`, immediately adjacent to *Grammar disambiguation* (which already carries the scrutinee-parens and arm-body rules). Leave a one-paragraph anchor in `error-model.md` that names `match` as the destructuring form for `Result` and links forward to the expressions-page section.

**Spec edits.** Move five paragraphs from `error-model.md` to `expressions.md` (heading, pattern table, disambiguation note, exhaustiveness paragraph, arm-syntax paragraph). Add a back-link from the new expressions-page section to *Errors and Results — Runtime panics* for the `loom/runtime/match-error` row. Update `grammar.md`'s "see [Errors and Results — Pattern grammar]" cross-reference and the four `code-registry-parse.md` rows whose *Doc anchor* column points at `error-model.md`. Update `V4a` / `V4a-T` **Spec.** pointers.

**Pros.** Matches the finding's recommendation. Makes `expressions.md` self-contained for the forms its lede claims. The disambiguation rules (struct-expression-in-scrutinee, arm-body single-expression) move from a *Grammar disambiguation* island into the section they actually describe.

**Cons.** Breaks the `match` ↔ `Result` ↔ `?` co-located story in *Errors and Results* — the `?` equivalence example (`let _ = match @… { Ok(s) => s, Err(e) => return Err(e) }`) now sits one page away from the `match` definition it depends on. A second-pass reviewer is likely to flag *Errors and Results* as having a dangling forward reference.

**Risks.** Cross-reference churn (≥ 4 diagnostic-registry rows, `grammar.md`, two plan leaves) without resolving the discoverability issue for `control-flow.md`, which still does not mention `match`.

### Option B — Place `match` on `control-flow.md` alongside `if` / `for` / `while`

**Approach.** Treat `match` as a control-flow construct (it is one — exhaustive destructuring dispatch), and move the same five paragraphs into `control-flow.md` instead of `expressions.md`. Update `expressions.md`'s lede to link to the new home; keep the same anchor stub in `error-model.md`.

**Spec edits.** Same paragraph move as Option A, but to `control-flow.md`. Add a `match`-as-expression note to `control-flow.md`'s lede (the page currently opens "Loom has three loop and branch forms" — that count becomes four). Same cross-reference updates as Option A.

**Pros.** `control-flow.md` is the natural home for branching forms. Preserves the symmetry with `if` / `for` / `while`. Avoids loading `expressions.md` with a multi-paragraph section in a page otherwise structured as terse-rule lists.

**Cons.** Currently `control-flow.md` is statement-form-only ("Loom has three loop and branch forms" — all three are statements); adding the only expression-form branching construct to it forces a structural rework of the page. The arm-body rule (`statement-in-arm-body`) and scrutinee-parens rule sitting in `expressions.md`'s *Grammar disambiguation* are then split from the construct they describe.

**Risks.** Same registry-row and plan-leaf churn as Option A, plus a structural rewrite of `control-flow.md`'s lede.

### Option C — Keep the canonical text in `error-model.md`; add a definition stub + forward link in `expressions.md`

**Approach.** Leave the normative paragraphs where they are (preserving the `match` ↔ `Result` ↔ `?` story). Add a brief `## `match` expression` section to `expressions.md` containing the one-paragraph form description (exhaustive destructuring, arm bodies are expressions, scrutinee may be any expression) plus an explicit forward link: "The full pattern grammar, disambiguation rule, exhaustiveness contract, arm-syntax LUB rule, and `loom/runtime/match-error` runtime panic are defined in [Errors and Results — `match` expression](./errors-and-results/error-model.md)." Tighten the lede of `error-model.md`'s `match` section to acknowledge it owns the language-wide construct (not only the error-handling instance), so a sibling-page xref to "the canonical match definition" lands here legitimately.

**Spec edits.** New ~5-line section in `expressions.md` immediately before *Grammar disambiguation*. One sentence retitled / clarified in `error-model.md`. No paragraph moves, no diagnostic-registry row touches, no plan-leaf pointer changes.

**Pros.** Smallest edit. Preserves the coherent error-handling story in `error-model.md`. Gives `expressions.md` a real entry point for `match`, so a reader of the language reference finds it without a search. Leaves the `V4a` plan leaf untouched (it already cites both pages).

**Cons.** The canonical home is still on a page whose name advertises error handling, so a reader who only ever sees `expressions.md` learns *that* `match` exists and where to read about it, not the rules themselves. Does not resolve `control-flow.md`'s silence on `match`.

**Risks.** Minimal. The stub paragraph could drift from the canonical paragraphs in `error-model.md` if both are edited independently; this is mitigated by keeping the stub deliberately rule-free (it asserts *that* the construct exists, not *what* the rules are).

### Recommendation

Option C. The discoverability gap the finding identifies is real and worth fixing; the construct's semantic story (especially the `?`-to-`match` equivalence and the `loom/runtime/match-error` panic's bypass-of-`?`-and-`match` rule) is genuinely intertwined with `Result` and the error-handling story, and splitting them produces a worse spec than the one it replaces. A definition stub plus a tightened canonical-home statement closes the discoverability gap at minimum churn, leaves the plan leaves untouched, and avoids the lede-rewrite of `control-flow.md` that Option B forces. Edge cases for the implementer: the stub in `expressions.md` MUST NOT restate any of the rules (pattern grammar, exhaustiveness, arm-syntax LUB) — it asserts existence and links forward only — and the stub MUST be placed before *Grammar disambiguation* so that the scrutinee-parens and arm-body rules in that section read in their right context.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A spec reader who opens `expressions.md` expecting it to define every expression form named in its own lede is left to follow a forward chain (`expressions.md` lede → `match` undefined → search → `error-model.md`) to find pattern grammar, exhaustiveness, and arm-body typing. Two implementers will still converge on the same runtime behaviour because the plan leaf `V4a` and the diagnostics registry pin everything that matters, and `grammar.md` already cross-references `error-model.md`; nothing observable diverges. The cost is reader friction and a missing canonical anchor for sibling pages that today have to point at an error-handling page to talk about a non-error expression form.
