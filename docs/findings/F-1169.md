## Problem

`match` is a first-class expression form, but its normative definition — pattern grammar, disambiguation rule, exhaustiveness contract, arm-syntax LUB-typing rule, and the `loom/runtime/match-error` runtime panic — lives entirely in `errors-and-results/error-model.md`. `expressions.md`, whose lede declares itself the home for the grammar that applies to `if` / `match` scrutinees, names `match` four times (lede, *Not supported* list, and the two `## Grammar disambiguation` rules) but never defines it and never links forward to where it is defined. The defect is one-way discoverability: a language-side reader treating `expressions.md` as the reference meets `match` as an undefined construct, and sibling pages have no canonical `match` home to point at outside an error-handling page.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 53f5831 — spec: rewrite query primitive, system prompts, and schema syntax (2026-05-03, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** `match` entered the corpus in commit 53f5831 as part of the query/`Result`/`?` rewrite of the monolithic `spec.md`; the rewrite created the *Expression Sublanguage* and *Errors and Results* sections in the same commit and placed the full `match` definition (pattern grammar, exhaustiveness, arm syntax) under *Errors and Results* with only naming references from the expression section. The subsequent reorganisations preserved that placement: `fecb504` split the monolith into `spec_topics/errors-and-results.md` and `spec_topics/expressions.md` as separate files, and `f5e89f4` (the 30 KB / 100 KB cap split) moved the `match` paragraphs into `errors-and-results/error-model.md` without revisiting which page should own them. The placement decision has therefore never been re-examined since the construct was first authored.

## Solution approach

Add a `match` entry-point section to `expressions.md` ahead of `## Grammar disambiguation`, describing the form (an exhaustive destructuring expression whose arm bodies are expressions and whose scrutinee may be any expression) and forward-linking to the canonical definition in `errors-and-results/error-model.md`. Clarify the lede of that page's `match` section so it reads as owning the language-wide construct rather than only the error-handling instance, so sibling pages can cross-reference it legitimately.

## Solution constraints

- The new `expressions.md` entry asserts only that `match` exists and links forward; it MUST NOT restate pattern grammar, exhaustiveness, or arm-syntax LUB rules (those remain owned by `error-model.md`).
- Out of scope: moving the normative `match` paragraphs, touching diagnostic-registry rows, or changing plan-leaf pointers.
