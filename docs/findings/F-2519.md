## Problem

The `label` field derivation for a `.loom`-as-tool `ToolDefinition`, given in the `**Per-loom registration.**` field-derivation list, is "the loom file's basename with hyphens preserved and the leading character capitalised" (`summarise.loom` ŌåÆ `"Summarise"`, `code-review.loom` ŌåÆ `"Code-review"`). The transform is undefined when the basename's leading character has no uppercase form ŌĆö a digit, an underscore, or a non-ASCII codepoint ŌĆö so two implementers diverge on the derived label for basenames such as `2fa-check.loom` or `_internal.loom`.

A later commit, `6d8b472` (2026-06-05, "resolve \"label derivation rule and example capitalisation disagreement\""), touched the `label` line but only corrected the worked example (`Code-Review` ŌåÆ `Code-review`); it did not address the edge case. All three obligations are present-since-inception of the field-derivation block.

## Solution approach

Clarify the `label` derivation in the `**Per-loom registration.**` field-derivation list to define the transform's behaviour when the basename's leading character has no uppercase form (digit, underscore, or non-ASCII codepoint).
