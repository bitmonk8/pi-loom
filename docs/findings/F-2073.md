## Problem

The *Inventory-first resolution* paragraph (`#audit-inventory-first-resolution`) closes its precedence rule with "…and neither the inventory nor the typebox sibling allow-lists **MUST** be widened to mask a marker." Under RFC 2119, "neither X nor Y MUST P" negates the obligation rather than the action, so the clause reads as an absence of obligation (permissive) when the intended meaning is a prohibition. This clause is the sole statement of the no-widening guarantee, and it is load-bearing for `(s2)` stale-marker detection: if the inventory or a typebox sibling allow-list may be widened to absorb a marked line's references, a stale `// allow-pi-surface:` marker keeps authorising and the surface-set-closure guarantee the audit enforces degrades with no test failing.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** introduced-by-commit (single commit, with the rule)
**Introducing commits:** `e5cd514`
**History:** The *Inventory-first resolution* paragraph was added whole in `e5cd514` (2026-05-09, "pi-loom spec: resolve 'T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit'"), and the inverted modal clause "neither the inventory nor the typebox sibling allow-lists MUST be widened to mask a marker" was present in that first authoring. `git log -S` over the defect token shows the clause unchanged in substance since; the later count change in `f5e89f4` ("docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set") reflects only the relocation of the surrounding text during the spec-set size split, not an edit to the clause's polarity. The defect was therefore present from the rule's introduction, not injected by a subsequent edit.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the inverted clause in `#audit-inventory-first-resolution` to an explicit RFC 2119 prohibition — the split MUST NOT form "the inventory MUST NOT be widened to mask a marker, and the typebox sibling allow-lists MUST NOT be widened to mask a marker" — consistent with the adjacent "MUST NOT be consulted" clauses in the same sentence. Preserve the "to mask a marker" qualifier so the prohibition stays scoped to widening that suppresses an otherwise-stale marker, not a general ban on adding inventory entries.
