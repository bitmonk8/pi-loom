## Finding

The opening sentence of the `(P-a)/(P-b)/(P-c)` fixture-obligation enumeration in the *Patch-skew degradation contract* interleaves a normative requirement with author-facing naming-convention rationale:

> "Three behaviours pin this contract and MUST be covered by build-time fixtures; the (P-a)/(P-b)/(P-c) labelling here uses a `"P-"` prefix to be deliberately distinct from both the (i)/(ii)/(iii) sub-anchor labelling of the *Sub-anchors and pinned substrings* paragraph immediately above **and** the (a)–(d) anchor-stable substring labelling of that same paragraph (the substring labels are anchor-stable surface and cannot themselves be relabelled), so the three adjacent enumerations cannot collide on cross-reference:"

Only the lead clause ("Three behaviours pin this contract and MUST be covered by build-time fixtures") and the colon introducing the enumeration carry normative weight. Everything between them explains *why the author picked a `"P-"` prefix* — a labelling-convention justification aimed at editors, not a constraint on implementers. The label set `(P-a)/(P-b)/(P-c)` is self-evident from the enumeration that follows; the reader does not need the collision-avoidance rationale to apply the contract correctly. Embedding it inside a MUST-bearing sentence forces every implementer to parse design-process commentary before reaching the three fixture obligations.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Delete the labelling-prefix rationale clause. Open the sentence directly at the normative content and proceed to the enumeration:

> "Three behaviours pin this contract and MUST be covered by build-time fixtures: (P-a) … ; (P-b) … ; (P-c) … ."

Keep the `(P-a)/(P-b)/(P-c)` labels themselves — they are referenced by sibling fixture-obligation prose and by the *Fixture-obligation categories* paragraph below. Only the parenthetical justification for the prefix choice (the "deliberately distinct from … cannot collide on cross-reference" span) is removed.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The rationale is correct and harmless; removing it does not alter any obligation. The only cost of leaving it is reading friction — an implementer must wade through editor-facing convention commentary embedded mid-sentence in a MUST clause. No observable behaviour or implementer divergence results either way.
