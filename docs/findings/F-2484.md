## Problem

The "Transient toasts (auxiliary)" paragraph in `diagnostic-shape.md` (anchor `transient-toasts`) prohibits all three toast-surface arms from writing the `LoomRegistry` drain-state tag, and justifies the prohibition with an inline paraphrase of PIC's `session_shutdown` handler-entry short-circuit predicate and its skip-teardown semantics. That predicate and its semantics are owned and fully specified by the PIC drain-state contract (`drain-state-contract.md`). Embedding the paraphrase here creates a second normative-adjacent surface for the same invariant with no lockstep coupling, so a future change to PIC's predicate silently leaves the `diagnostic-shape.md` restatement stale.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** c2e278f — pi-loom spec-plan: fix F-1398 — pin per-arm post-failure disposition for transient toasts (2026-06-25, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The transient-toasts paragraph carried only the `ctx.ui.notify`-swallow rule until c2e278f, which added the per-arm post-failure disposition block. That same commit introduced both the drain-state-tag prohibition and the inline short-circuit-predicate rationale verbatim (confirmed by `git log -S` on "None of the three arms writes" and "short-circuit predicate assumes the tag is written only there", both first appearing in c2e278f). The duplicated invariant entered with the prohibition it justifies; the PIC drain-state contract predicate it paraphrases already existed at that point.

## Solution approach

Delete the inline short-circuit-predicate-and-skip-teardown rationale from the transient-toasts paragraph, leaving the existing `drain-state-contract.md#loom-registry-drain-state-contract` cross-reference as the single pointer to why the prohibition exists.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: removing the drain-state-tag prohibition sentence or the three per-arm "writes no drain-state tag" statements (arms (1)–(3)) — they stay normative on this page.
- `drain-state-contract.md` remains the sole owner of the short-circuit predicate expression and its skip-teardown consequence; `diagnostic-shape.md` MUST NOT restate either.
