## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/schema-subset.md` § Lowering Algorithm step 3 leaves the lowered form of a pure-primitive union under-pinned: *"Union of primitives only: `{ "type": ["a", "b", "null"] }` form preferred for readability; falls back to `anyOf` if any arm is non-primitive."* The Nullability bullet at the top of the file is the same shape — *`string | null` lowers to `{"type": ["string", "null"]}` **or** an `anyOf` with `{"type": "null"}`* — so two conformant implementations can legally pick different lowered fragments for the same source.

That permissiveness collides head-on with the *Canonical schema hash* section in the same file, whose premise is that the slug is a content-address over the **bytes** of the lowered fragment (SHA-256 → first 16 hex). The four slug-keyed sites the spec pins downstream — `__inline_<slug>` `$defs` dedup, `__loom_respond_<slug>`, `__loom_callee_<slug>__…`, `__loom_bind_<slug>` — and the byte-equality collision check at every slug match (`loom/load/schema-slug-collision`, `loom/runtime/validator-cache-collision`, `loom/runtime/registration-cache-collision`) all assume two implementations starting from the same source land on identical bytes. They do not, for any union of primitives, and they do not for nullability. A conformance suite cannot snapshot the lowered fragment, the slug, or the synthesised name without first choosing one of the two emitted shapes — and the spec gives no normative basis for that choice.

The fix is mechanical: replace "preferred" / "or" with a single MUST that fixes the form by the arms' kinds — multi-type-array when every arm is a primitive (including the `string | null` case), `anyOf` otherwise — and add reference vectors fixing the rendering for the two-arm primitive case, the `T | null` case, and a mixed union. The arm-order rule (source order, `"null"` last when admitted) already exists in the *Array element order* clause at the bottom of step 3 and composes unchanged.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace the *Union of primitives only* bullet in step 3 with a normative MUST that fixes the lowered form by the arms' kinds, and tighten the top-of-file *Nullability* bullet to match:

- **Step 3 primitive-union bullet (rewritten):** *"Union whose every arm is a primitive (including `null`): MUST lower to `{ "type": [<arm>, …] }`, with the arms ordered per the *Array element order* clause below (source order, `"null"` last when the union admits it). Any union with at least one non-primitive arm MUST lower to `{ "anyOf": [...] }`."* The `"readability"` clause and the "falls back" framing are dropped — the choice is mechanical, not aesthetic.
- **Top-of-file Nullability bullet (rewritten):** *"A `string | null` Loom type lowers to `{"type": ["string", "null"]}`; the `anyOf`-with-`{"type": "null"}` alternative is **not** emitted (it is the residue of a pre-MUST formulation). The non-standard `nullable: true` modifier is **not** emitted."* — i.e. the `or` becomes one fixed shape.
- **Reference vectors (added beneath the bullet, as a short list the canonical-hash recipe can be tested against):**
  - `string | integer` → `{"type": ["string", "integer"]}`
  - `string | null` → `{"type": ["string", "null"]}`
  - `integer | null` → `{"type": ["integer", "null"]}`
  - `string | Author` (mixed) → `{"anyOf": [{"type": "string"}, {"$ref": "#/$defs/Author"}]}`
  - `Author | null` (named + null, mixed because `Author` is not a primitive arm) → `{"anyOf": [{"$ref": "#/$defs/Author"}, {"type": "null"}]}`

Implementer-relevant edge cases: (1) `null` is treated as a primitive arm in this rule — so `T | null` for a primitive `T` takes the `{ "type": [...] }` form, while `Author | null` does not (`Author` is a `$ref`, not a primitive). (2) `enum` is *not* a primitive arm for this purpose — a union like `enum E | null` already lowers via the *Enum* bullet plus an outer `anyOf` and is unaffected. (3) The arm-order rule in step 3's *Array element order* paragraph already pins `"null"` last when the union admits it; no edit is needed there. (4) The canonical-hash recipe's existing key-sort rule does not apply inside `"type": [...]` (it is an array, not an object) — the *Array element order* clause is the sole order authority for that position, which is why the source-order MUST in that clause is load-bearing for the slug.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable implementers will produce different lowered fragments — and therefore different schema slugs — for any pure-primitive union or any nullable primitive. Every slug-keyed surface (the four synthesised-name forms, the three byte-verifying collision caches, AJV cache reuse, replayable provider payloads, fixture snapshots) silently diverges across implementations. Conformance tests on those surfaces are unwritable until one form is fixed; cross-implementation cache or transcript replay is unsound in the meantime.
