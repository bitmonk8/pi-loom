## Finding

BNDR-1, BNDR-2 and BNDR-3 in `binder-bypass-and-envelope.md` are three normative MUSTs whose entire reason for existing is to preserve forward-compatibility with the deferred binder refinement loop — they pin the three-arm discriminator, retain `ambiguous.candidates` in the schema, and keep the two failure-arm template prefixes distinct, none of which has an observable consequence in loom 1.0. The seam character is signalled only by the preceding blockquote ("**loom 1.0 seam — binder refinement loop.**"); each obligation then renders as a top-level `**BNDR-N.**` paragraph in exactly the same tier and styling as the functional MUSTs that surround it (e.g. BNDR-7/8/9 on `binder-model-and-context.md` or the eight `System-prompt structure (normative)` items in this same file).

The strain shows up in BNDR-2, which now reads as a paired build-it / hide-it MUST in a single paragraph: "The `ambiguous.candidates` field MUST remain in the envelope schema … The loom 1.0 runtime MUST NOT surface it on the user-facing system note …". A reader scanning the BNDR-N list outside the context of the seam blockquote can plausibly read this as two conflicting functional requirements — the implementation owes both a populated schema field and a guarantee that the same field is never rendered — and is left to reconstruct the seam motivation by walking back up to the blockquote. The other two pins are smaller stand-alone obligations, but they inherit the same flattening: their "do not collapse, even though loom 1.0 doesn't need the distinction" character is invisible to ID-driven scanners (the coverage matrix, REQ-ID greps, the V11c leaf bullets) that pull each BNDR-N out one at a time.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 8c5f867 — pi-loom spec: resolve "Binder refinement-loop seam bundles three independent MUSTs" (2026-05-08, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** Before 8c5f867 the three obligations lived as numbered list items *inside* the `> **V1 seam — binder refinement loop.**` blockquote, where their seam character was structurally inherited from the enclosing quote. 8c5f867 resolved a prior finding that the bundle hid three independent MUSTs behind one numbered list by lifting each item out of the blockquote into its own top-level `**BNDR-<i/j/k>.**` paragraph (later renamed to BNDR-1/2/3 with anchors in 1161fa0, 2026-05-31, and carried unchanged into `binder-bypass-and-envelope.md` when `binder.md` was split for the 30/100 KB size cap in f5e89f4, 2026-06-04). The split achieved citability at the cost of the visual seam-vs-functional tier separation; the current finding is the second-order defect introduced by the first finding's fix.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Group BNDR-1/2/3 under a delimited "Seam obligations" sub-section

**Approach.** Promote the existing `> **loom 1.0 seam — binder refinement loop.**` blockquote into a real `#### Seam obligations (binder refinement loop)` sub-section nested under `## Binder envelope`, and move the three BNDR-N paragraphs into it. Keep the existing per-paragraph `<a id="bndr-N"></a>` anchors verbatim so all inbound links and the V11c coverage row continue to resolve. The blockquote's framing sentence ("The structural distinction between the two failure arms exists for the deferred binder refinement loop …") becomes the sub-section's opening paragraph.

**Spec edits.**
- In `docs/spec_topics/binder/binder-bypass-and-envelope.md`, replace the blockquote-plus-three-BNDR-paragraphs block (lines beginning `> **loom 1.0 seam — binder refinement loop.**` through the BNDR-3 paragraph) with a `#### Seam obligations (binder refinement loop)` sub-heading carrying the existing `loom-1-0-seam-binder-refinement-loop` / `v1-seam-binder-refinement-loop` anchor ids, the same framing paragraph (de-blockquoted), then the three `**BNDR-N.**` paragraphs unchanged.
- No edits elsewhere; the anchors used by `defaulting-system-note-echo.md`, `determinism-cancellation-failure.md`, the V11c leaves, and the coverage matrix all remain stable.

**Pros.** One structural edit covers all three obligations; BNDR-2's build-it / hide-it pair no longer looks like a stray dual-MUST because the whole sub-section is visibly tagged as seam preservation; the title is grep-discoverable.

**Cons.** Adds a nesting level under `## Binder envelope`; one of the few `####`-depth headings in the file.

**Risks.** Tooling that diffs heading skeletons across the spec may flag the new heading until coverage tables / TOCs are re-scanned (no normative consequence, just churn).

### Option B — Per-paragraph "Seam obligation" prefix plus an in-line clarifier on BNDR-2

**Approach.** Keep BNDR-1/2/3 at their current paragraph tier but prefix each with an italicised tag — e.g. `*Seam obligation (binder refinement loop).*` — that names the seam and forward-links to the framing blockquote. On BNDR-2, split the build-it / hide-it sentences with an explicit "the two clauses are not in conflict: the schema field exists for the post-loom-1.0 loop; the loom 1.0 runtime simply does not surface it" connective.

**Spec edits.**
- In `docs/spec_topics/binder/binder-bypass-and-envelope.md`, edit each of the three BNDR-N paragraphs to begin with the italicised seam tag (anchor unchanged).
- Add one connective sentence between BNDR-2's first and second `MUST` sentences explaining that the pair is the schema/runtime split, not a contradiction.
- Leave the blockquote and headings in place.

**Pros.** No heading structure change; the connective explicitly defuses the BNDR-2 dual-MUST reading at its exact location.

**Cons.** The tagging is per-paragraph rather than structural — a future BNDR-N addition has to remember to wear the tag; coverage-row scanners cannot infer "this is a seam obligation" from structure alone.

**Risks.** Italicised inline tags are a new convention in this file; reviewers may want it documented in `governance.md` before propagating, which expands scope.

### Recommendation

Option A. Promote the seam-and-obligations block to a `#### Seam obligations (binder refinement loop)` sub-section under `## Binder envelope`, preserving every BNDR-N anchor verbatim. It resolves the BNDR-2 dual-MUST readability concern, the BNDR-1/2/3-look-like-functional-MUSTs concern, and the framing-walks-back-to-the-blockquote concern in one structural move, with no per-obligation prose change and no churn to the V11c leaves or the coverage matrix. The implementer must watch one edge case: the framing paragraph currently sits in a blockquote and is referenced (only) by the inline `loom 1.0 seam — binder refinement loop` phrase; carry both legacy anchor ids (`loom-1-0-seam-binder-refinement-loop`, `v1-seam-binder-refinement-loop`) onto the new heading so existing inbound `#…` links continue to resolve.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A spec reader (or an LLM fixer) scanning BNDR-2 in isolation can read the build-it / hide-it pair as a self-contradiction and try to "fix" it — most plausibly by dropping `ambiguous.candidates` from the schema (because the runtime never surfaces it anyway), which is exactly the breaking change BNDR-2 was authored to prevent. The other two pins are robust on their own but lose the framing that explains why a coverage gate exists at all.
