## Finding

The **Binder inference call** in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/binder-inference.md` enumerates `model`, `context.*`, `options.temperature`, `options.signal`, the seed key, and `options.onResponse`, and closes by *delegating per-attempt backoff and any server-requested `Retry-After` wait to this call's `StreamOptions.maxRetries` / `StreamOptions.maxRetryDelayMs`*. Three independent gaps follow from that wording, each fixable by an isolated edit and each producing a distinct conformance hazard:

1. **Retry-field disposition is unstated.** The option enumeration never lists `options.maxRetries` or `options.maxRetryDelayMs`, yet the closing sentence presupposes loom configures them. Implementers cannot tell whether loom is supposed to set those fields (and to what values), or to inherit pi-ai's defaults — which directly changes the HC3-d "≤3 binder LLM calls per slash invocation" budget interpretation (does an SDK-internal wire retry count, and is one `complete()` call per HC3 slot or one outer `complete()` per slash invocation?). This facet is the same call-shape ambiguity already triaged into the findings store as `F-0622`.

2. **`onResponse` cardinality under SDK-internal retries is undefined.** `options.onResponse` is described as the runtime's "provider-response capture callback" feeding the `ProviderResponse { status, headers }` to the `provider-error-mapping.md#classifier-input-surface` classifier. If pi-ai's `complete()` performs any internal retry (which (1) above leaves open), the callback may fire more than once per `complete()` resolution, and the spec gives no rule for which `ProviderResponse` is joined with the resolved `AssistantMessage`. The classifier's `TransportError.retryable` (and the `5xx` / `429` / other-`4xx` split) depend on `status`, so two conformant implementations can disagree on `retryable` and on context-overflow precedence for the same provider trace.

3. **`complete()`'s `options.signal` honouring is asserted without a local presupposition flag.** The `options.signal` bullet states loom forwards `loomAbort.signal` and the cancellation paragraph relies on `complete()` aborting the in-flight provider call. That behavioural property is in fact pinned — at `conversation-drive.md#complete-retry-and-cancellation-presupposition` (property 2), with a re-validation route through version-bump checklist item `(ab)` — but the binder page itself carries no marker or cross-reference, so a reader of `binder-inference.md` alone sees an unbacked behavioural assertion rather than a named, version-bump-tracked host presupposition.

The three gaps are independent: (1) is an option-bullet edit; (2) is a one-sentence rule in either the `onResponse` bullet or `provider-error-mapping.md#classifier-input-surface`; (3) is a cross-reference inserted in the `options.signal` bullet. Bundling them into a single prose pass would inflate the diff and obscure which of the three classifier / HC3 / cancellation surfaces actually moved.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

The finding decomposes into three independent obligations. Resolve them in the order below so each fix lands on a stable baseline; in particular facet 1's chosen `maxRetries` disposition determines whether facet 2 needs a true last-wins rule or a trivial single-invocation rule, and facet 3's wording can cite the post-facet-1 binder option enumeration verbatim.

### Option A — Pin the `maxRetries` / `maxRetryDelayMs` disposition and the HC3-d unit

**Approach.** Add `options.maxRetries` and `options.maxRetryDelayMs` bullets to the *Binder inference call* option enumeration, stating loom's chosen values explicitly. Recommended values: `options.maxRetries = 0` and `options.maxRetryDelayMs` left unset (the field has no effect when retries are zero), so the binder issues exactly one wire attempt per outer `complete()` call and the HC3 retry budget — and only that budget — drives re-issue. Rewrite the section's closing delegation sentence as a declarative consequence ("each outer `complete()` invocation is a single wire attempt; the HC3 budget governs all re-issues, and loom inserts no inter-attempt delay") instead of an open delegation. In `determinism-cancellation-failure.md#per-invocation-retry-budget`, restate HC3-d as "at most three outer `complete()` invocations per slash invocation" so the unit of counting is unambiguous against the pinned `maxRetries = 0`.

**Spec edits.**
- `binder-inference.md`: two new bullets in the `complete()` enumeration; rewritten closing paragraph.
- `determinism-cancellation-failure.md#per-invocation-retry-budget`: HC3-d unit-of-count sentence revised; the "client-side retry of a *single* underlying attempt" clause that currently delegates to `StreamOptions.maxRetries` / `maxRetryDelayMs` collapsed to a one-line "loom pins `maxRetries = 0`; the pi-ai inter-attempt backoff layer is unused by the binder" note with a back-reference to the binder-page bullet.
- Version-bump item `(aa)` (`bump-checklist-complete-retry-timing`): note that, with `maxRetries = 0`, the property is observable but unused by the binder; the editorial review remains useful for any other loom call routed through `complete()`.

**Pros.** Eliminates ambiguity in a single direction; makes HC3-d directly testable; removes loom's dependence on the pi-ai backoff layer for the binder, which collapses the co-resolve target ("binder retry timing delegates backoff/`Retry-After` to pi-ai without verification") and reduces facet 2's surface (a single attempt → `onResponse` fires at most once → no cardinality rule needed for the binder).

**Cons.** Forecloses an alternative where loom defers all per-attempt backoff to pi-ai (`maxRetries` unset) and lets the SDK handle `Retry-After` for transient `429`/`5xx`. Behavioural sites outside the binder that call `complete()` (e.g. the typed-query forced respond turn) inherit the pin transitively only if they also opt into `maxRetries = 0`.

**Risks.** A future loom call type that wants pi-ai's backoff will need an explicit per-call override; documenting that as a follow-up is cheap, but the cross-reference must be added at the new call's introduction.

### Option B — Pin the `onResponse` cardinality / last-response-wins join rule

**Approach.** Extend the `options.onResponse` bullet (and the mirror clause in `provider-error-mapping.md#classifier-input-surface`) with an explicit cardinality rule: "If `onResponse` fires more than once before `complete()` resolves, the *last* invocation's `ProviderResponse` is the one joined with the resolved `AssistantMessage` and read by the classifier. If `onResponse` does not fire, the resolution is classified as no-HTTP-response (network-level) per the existing rule." Pair that with a one-sentence note that, under Option A's `maxRetries = 0` choice, multi-fire is not reachable from the binder call but the rule remains in effect for any other `complete()`-routed loom call.

**Spec edits.**
- `binder-inference.md`: one-sentence extension to the `options.onResponse` bullet.
- `provider-error-mapping.md#classifier-input-surface`: the existing "keyed per `complete()` invocation" sentence is extended with the last-wins clause; the no-HTTP-response branch is left as it stands.

**Pros.** Eliminates the only remaining classifier non-determinism on the same provider trace; aligns with the co-resolve target *"provider-error-mapping.md — rejected/thrown `complete()` (non-cancellation) has no `QueryError` mapping; multi-`onResponse` join rule undefined"* and resolves the multi-`onResponse` half of that finding at the same anchor.

**Cons.** "Last-wins" is one of two defensible rules (first-wins is the other); some pi-ai providers may emit a preliminary `200` and then a body-level error, where first-wins would surface the body-level transport failure more usefully. The choice is local and reversible.

**Risks.** None material; the alternative (first-wins) is one-word edit if a fixture surfaces a counter-case during `V9j-T`.

### Option C — Cite the `options.signal` honouring presupposition locally

**Approach.** Extend the `options.signal` bullet in *Binder inference call* with a parenthetical cross-reference: *"(per [Conversation drive — `complete()` retry-timing and cancellation behavioural presupposition](./conversation-drive.md#complete-retry-and-cancellation-presupposition), property 2; re-validated under version-bump item [(ab)](./version-bump-step2.md#bump-checklist-complete-signal-honouring))"*. No new normative text is added — the presupposition already exists and is already in the version-bump checklist; the citation makes its dependency local to the binder page.

**Spec edits.**
- `binder-inference.md`: one parenthetical cross-reference appended to the `options.signal` bullet.

**Pros.** Smallest possible diff; preserves the single source of truth in `conversation-drive.md`; lifts the local assertion from "asserted without backing" to "named, re-validated host presupposition" with zero new obligation.

**Cons.** None.

**Risks.** None.

### Recommendation

Resolve in the order **C → B → A**, smallest first so each subsequent diff lands on a stable baseline:

1. **C first** (one-line citation): scope-bounding, no behavioural decision.
2. **B next** (cardinality rule): pure clarification, decoupled from the call-count decision in A.
3. **A last** (retry-field disposition + HC3-d unit): the largest change and the one that touches `determinism-cancellation-failure.md`; landing it after B means B's last-wins rule is already in force when A's `maxRetries = 0` choice collapses its reachable surface in the binder path.

The orchestrator should pick these up as three separately-loopable per-finding fixes rather than a single bundled pass. The edges to watch when reviewing each diff:

- A: confirm `V11f`'s HC3-d test is restatable as "outer `complete()` count" without spec-language drift, and that the `(aa)` checklist item's "still relevant for non-binder `complete()` callers" qualifier is added.
- B: confirm the rule is mirrored verbatim between `binder-inference.md` and `provider-error-mapping.md#classifier-input-surface` so the two pages cannot drift.
- C: confirm the cross-reference text matches the actual anchor (no typos in `#complete-retry-and-cancellation-presupposition`).

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Without facet 1, two reasonable implementers will diverge on per-invocation provider-call count, observed latency, and `Retry-After` handling, and `V11f`'s HC3-d test cannot be written deterministically. Without facet 2, the classifier's `TransportError.retryable` is non-deterministic against the same provider trace whenever pi-ai retries internally — directly affecting whether the binder's transport-class retry slot is consumed. Facet 3 is the smallest of the three (the property is actually pinned upstream) but its absence on `binder-inference.md` leaves a load-bearing behavioural assertion locally unbacked, masking which Pi-minor bumps would surface a cancellation regression.
