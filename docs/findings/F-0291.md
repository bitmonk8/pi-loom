## Finding

`V15a` (`docs/plan_topics/V15a-invocation-core.md`) and its paired `V15a-T` carry a single **Tests.** list of roughly fourteen bullets drawn from six different spec topics: `invocation.md`, `discovery/discovery-sources.md`, `cancellation.md`, `return.md`, `implementation-notes.md`, and `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md`. The bullets cover concerns that are largely independent of one another: discovery-root `realpath` containment (`INV-1`/`INV-2`/`INV-3`), the fresh-vs-attach cross-mode matrix, the static-resolution transitive parse-cache walk, the `invoke` final-value propagation contract, six `loom/parse/*` + `loom/load/*` diagnostic codes, the swallowing-handler cancellation-suppression assertion delegated from `V17a`, two ceiling-#4 depth-surface rows delegated from `V5e`, and the `SLSH-5` per-frame invocation-record provenance seam consumed by `V12b`.

The leaf was reasonably scoped at inception (four bullets) and grew to its current size as a sequence of unrelated gap-closing edits each parked its obligation here without re-checking the cumulative size (see Issue introduction). The result is a leaf whose single `Ships when.` gate fires only when all fourteen obligations are green at once, and whose review surface is too wide for one pass to validate. Several of the parked facets already have distinct downstream consumers (`V12b` consumes the provenance record; the six diagnostic codes are the closing leaf for an `invocation.md` code-keyed coverage-matrix row; the ceiling-#4 rows are a delegated witness for `V5e`), which makes them natural candidates for their own paired leaves.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction (accretion)
**Introducing commits:** `7a8565a` (added the `invoke` final-value-propagation bullet), `249cec5` (added the static-resolution transitive parse-cache walk bullet), `cddd2b4` (added the six `loom/parse/*` + `loom/load/*` diagnostic-code bullets), `75a9bcd` (added the swallowing-handler cancellation-suppression bullet), `65363c9` (added the `SLSH-5` provenance bullet), `3d1ffd7` (added the ceiling-#4 depth-surface bullet)
**History:** At inception (`c6a664e`) `V15a` carried four test bullets — `INV-1`/`INV-2`/`INV-3` and the cross-mode matrix. The defect emerged from the cumulative interaction of the six later gap-closing "resolve …" commits above, each of which closed an unrelated coverage gap by parking its obligation onto `V15a` without re-checking the leaf's growing total. A "Systemic leaf over-bundling" pass (`cb8737a`) ran in the same window and peeled the prompt→prompt parent-suspend facet off into `V15d`, but did not arrest the continuing accretion that brought the bullet count to ~14. No single commit is responsible; the over-bundling is the product of their interaction.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Decompose by concern cluster into separate paired leaves

**Approach.** Peel the independent parked obligations off `V15a` into their own paired `<new>`/`<new>-T` leaves, leaving `V15a` as the invoke-execution core. Candidate cluster boundaries that already have distinct consumers or coverage-matrix homes:
- the six `loom/parse/*` + `loom/load/*` diagnostic codes (their own coverage-matrix INV row);
- the `SLSH-5` per-frame provenance producer seam (consumed by `V12b`);
- the ceiling-#4 depth-surface rows + the swallowing-handler suppression assertion (delegated witnesses for `V5e` / `V17a`).
`V15a` retains `INV-1`/`INV-2`/`INV-3` containment, the cross-mode matrix, the static-resolution cache + walk, and final-value propagation.

**Plan edits.** Move the relevant bullets out of `V15a` / `V15a-T` into the new leaf files; add the new leaves to the V15 listing in `docs/plan.md`; repoint the `invocation.md` six-code coverage-matrix row to the new parse-codes leaf; repoint `V12b` / `V12b-T` `Deps.` to the new provenance leaf; set each new leaf's `Deps.` and `Ships when.` to cover only its own obligation. Use `<new>` placeholders for the new leaf IDs.

**Spec edits.** None.

**Pros.** Each leaf becomes single-concern and independently reviewable; downstream deps name the leaf that actually owns the facet they consume; matches the existing pattern (the `V15d` peel).

**Cons.** Larger diff; more new leaves to thread through `Deps.` and the coverage matrix; more cross-leaf links to keep consistent.

**Risks.** A mis-repointed `Deps.` or coverage-matrix row could leave a facet uncovered or a dependent pointing at the wrong owner.

### Option B — Minimal high-value peel

**Approach.** Peel only the two most independent, highest-blast-radius clusters — the six diagnostic codes and the `SLSH-5` provenance seam — into their own paired leaves, leaving the ceiling-#4 and swallowing-handler witnesses in `V15a` because they share the live invoke-execution path the core already stands up.

**Plan edits.** Same mechanics as Option A but for two new leaves only; repoint the `invocation.md` six-code coverage-matrix row and `V12b` / `V12b-T` `Deps.`.

**Spec edits.** None.

**Pros.** Smaller diff and fewer new `Deps.` edges; removes the two clusters whose downstream owners make the cleanest boundaries.

**Cons.** `V15a` stays moderately large (containment + cross-mode + cache + return + ceiling + cancellation), so the review-surface problem is reduced but not eliminated.

**Risks.** The retained ceiling/cancellation bullets still couple two delegated-witness concerns into the core leaf's `Ships when.`.

### Recommendation

Take Option A. The parked obligations are genuinely independent and several already have distinct downstream owners (provenance → `V12b`; the six codes → their own coverage-matrix row; ceiling-#4 → `V5e`'s delegated witness), so concern-cluster boundaries fall out naturally and each new leaf gets a self-contained `Ships when.`. Peel the parse-codes and provenance leaves first — they are the clearest cut and the provenance peel is what forces the `V12b` `Deps.` repoint — then peel the ceiling/cancellation witnesses onto a stable remainder. When repointing, make every downstream dep (`V12b` for provenance; `V15c` / `V14a` / `V6c` / `V15b` / `V15d` / `V15e` for the invoke-core they consume) name the leaf that actually owns the facet it depends on, and confirm the `invocation.md` six-code coverage-matrix row points at the new parse-codes leaf so the closing-gate stays satisfied.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Shipping `V15a` as-is leaves a leaf whose `Ships when.` gate bundles fourteen independent obligations spanning six spec topics, so no single review pass can validate it and a regression in any one facet reddens the whole leaf without localising the failure. An implementer can still produce a working leaf, but the coarse scope makes review, bisection, and incremental TDD progress more costly than for a single-concern leaf.
