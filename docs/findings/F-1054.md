## Finding

Step 2 of the Pi version-bump procedure (split across `version-bump-step2.md` and
`version-bump-step2b.md`) accumulates three independent labelling collisions
inside one short stretch of normative prose. Each collision is small on its
own; together they make the most-cited sub-step of the bump procedure ("step
2(a)") read ambiguously, and the spec corpus references both layers — the
editorial-review checklist items and the SDK-test directions — using the same
`(a)` / `(b)` short form roughly sixty times across the PIC pages and the
plan leaves.

The three collisions are independent — they live in disjoint paragraphs,
have disjoint fix surfaces, and the prose carries no shared mechanism that
would resolve them together:

1. **Letter labels `(a)`/`(b)` overload two distinct enumerations.** The
   step-2 preamble in `version-bump-step2.md` opens an editorial-review
   checklist whose items are labelled `(a)` … `(an)`. Sub-step 2 of the
   same step then opens a second enumeration — the build-time SDK
   surface-inventory test directions, labelled `(a)` (positive) and
   `(b)` (negative). A bare cross-reference to "step 2(a)" therefore
   has two readings, and neither the preamble nor sub-step 2 declares a
   disambiguation convention (e.g. "item (a)" for the checklist vs.
   "step 2(a)" for the test direction). The corpus relies on context to
   pick the right reading, which works in most cases but fails on the
   bump-procedure pages themselves where both enumerations are nearby.

2. **Parent/child anchor stems disagree on the `2b` segment.** The
   parent test-direction anchors live in `version-bump-step2.md` as
   `bump-step-2-positive` and `bump-step-2-negative` (no `2b` segment,
   even though the negative-direction prose lives in
   `version-bump-step2b.md`). The five resolution branches of the
   negative direction, defined in `version-bump-step2b.md`, are anchored
   as `bump-step-2b-delete`, `bump-step-2b-promote`,
   `bump-step-2b-exempt`, `bump-step-2b-stale-rewrite`, and
   `bump-step-2b-rewrite-shape`. So the branches carry a `2b` segment
   their own parent anchor doesn't, and the inventory-closure-audit
   prose elsewhere on the PIC pages cross-references the parent under
   `step2.md#bump-step-2-negative` while citing the children under
   `step2b.md#bump-step-2b-…` — the same anchor space split inconsistently
   across two files.

3. **Three names for the (e.i)/(e.ii) construct.** Item (e)'s body
   under `version-bump-step2.md` calls the two atomic verdicts
   "sub-outcomes" in one paragraph and "sub-check" in another; the
   appendix's *Outcome recording conventions* (anchored at
   `bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions`) carries the section
   heading "two-sub-outcome recording convention" and refers back to
   "two sub-checks against distinct file groups" in the same paragraph.
   Items (a)–(c), separately, call the corresponding host-prerequisite
   bullets "sub-item (a)" / "sub-item (b)" / "sub-item (c)". Three
   names for two structurally identical concepts (independently
   falsifiable atomic verdicts beneath a parent checklist item) leaves
   the appendix's normative MUST ("MUST be recorded as the two atomic
   sub-outcomes [(e.i)] and [(e.ii)]") with no anchored term a future
   "sub-check" or "sub-item" item could re-cite without ambiguity.

**History:** Collisions 1 and 2 entered together in `e5cd514`, which split step 2 into the editorial-review checklist (items `(a)`–`(an)`) and the SDK-test directions (sub-step 2, `(a)` positive / `(b)` negative) and minted the `bump-step-2-positive` / `bump-step-2-negative` parent anchors alongside the five `bump-step-2b-…` child branches; the `2b` segment asymmetry was present in this single commit. Collision 3 grew over two later edits: `c5f97f6` introduced the *Outcome recording conventions* appendix and pinned the term "sub-outcome" for (e.i)/(e.ii); `5d73cc3` then split item (e)'s body into a separately-anchored sub-item list and used "sub-check" in the same paragraph, with the appendix's "sub-outcome" wording left in place — neither commit's predecessor carried both terms together, so the three-way drift ("sub-outcome" / "sub-check" / "sub-item" for the same construct) is the joint product of the two commits. The 2026-06-11 file split (`81bf52a`) physically separated the parent (`bump-step-2-positive` / `bump-step-2-negative`) from the children (`bump-step-2b-…`) into `version-bump-step2.md` and `version-bump-step2b.md` without renaming the anchors, sharpening collision 2's parent/child anchor split across two files without itself introducing it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

Each collision is its own one-paragraph (or one-anchor-rename) edit
with no shared mechanism, so this finding is decomposed into one option
per collision. The implementer should land them sequentially in the
recommended order so each diff lands on a stable baseline.

### Option A — Disambiguate `(a)` / `(b)` between the two enumerations

**Approach.** Keep the letter labels intact on both enumerations (item
(a)–(an) on the editorial-review checklist; (a)/(b) on the SDK-test
directions) but pin a one-sentence disambiguation convention in the
step-2 preamble of `version-bump-step2.md`: spec prose cites the
editorial-review enumeration as "item (a)" / "item (e)" / "item (ah)"
and the SDK-test directions as "step 2(a)" / "step 2(b)" / "sub-step
2(a)". Then sweep the two pages, the inbound citers, and the plan
leaves so every bare "(a)" / "(b)" reference carries the right
qualifier verbatim.

**Spec edits.** Add the one-sentence convention to the step-2 preamble;
sweep `version-bump-step2.md`, `version-bump-step2b.md`,
`version-bump-intro.md`, `version-bump-triggers.md`,
`inventory-audit-intro.md`, `audit-recognised-shapes.md`,
`audit-wire-and-canary.md`, `host-interfaces-core.md`,
`overview-and-orientation.md`, `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`,
`V18c-version-bump-checklist.md`, and `V18c-T-version-bump-checklist.md`
to apply the qualifier on every existing citation.

**Pros.** Zero anchor churn; no inbound link breakage; the editorial-
review item labels (which the bump-commit message format embeds
verbatim) stay untouched, so the commit-message format documented at
`V18c`'s *Ships when* doesn't move.

**Cons.** The disambiguator lives in one prose sentence the reader has
to find; future authors can still drop "item" or "step" by accident.

**Risks.** A sweep that misses a citation leaves a residual ambiguous
"(a)" on the page where the convention is pinned, which reads worse
than the status quo. The sweep is mechanical (literal grep on
`step 2(a)` / `step 2(b)` / `\(a\)` / `\(b\)` inside the spec corpus
plus the two `V18c` leaves) and can be checked by re-running the grep
post-edit.

### Option B — Re-anchor `bump-step-2-positive` / `bump-step-2-negative` to `bump-step-2a` / `bump-step-2b`

**Approach.** Rename the parent test-direction anchors in
`version-bump-step2.md` to `bump-step-2a` and `bump-step-2b` so the
parent/child anchor stems share the `2a` / `2b` segment naming the
children already use. Sweep every inbound citation across the PIC
pages, the orientation page, governance, and the `V18c` leaves to the
new anchors. Drop the editorial label "positive direction" / "negative
direction" from the anchor and keep it only in the prose, since the
short letter is what the inbound citers already use mechanically.

**Spec edits.** Rename two `<a id>` attributes in
`version-bump-step2.md`; update ~15 inbound `step2.md#bump-step-2-positive`
and ~3 `step2.md#bump-step-2-negative` cross-references across the
spec corpus and plan to `step2.md#bump-step-2a` /
`step2b.md#bump-step-2b` (the negative-direction anchor moves with the
prose to `step2b.md`, since that's where the negative-direction prose
lives). No prose changes are required beyond the link-target updates.

**Pros.** Parent/child anchor stems become uniform (`2a` parent + zero
children; `2b` parent + five `bump-step-2b-…` children); the anchor
stem matches the file basename it lives in (`step2.md#bump-step-2a` /
`step2b.md#bump-step-2b`); cross-references read the same way the
prose cites them ("step 2(a)" → `#bump-step-2a`).

**Cons.** Two-anchor rename with ~18 inbound citers across nine files;
any external citers (none in-repo, but downstream forks or planning
docs may carry them) would need re-pinning. The audit's
*Failure-surface contract* documentation in `audit-resolution.md` and
`inventory-audit-intro.md` cites the negative-direction anchor by name
under text like "[step 2(b)](./version-bump-step2.md#bump-step-2-negative)";
the rename moves the link's file half across the page split as well as
the fragment.

**Risks.** A missed inbound citation breaks the deep-link silently
(GitHub renders the page but the fragment is dead). Mitigation: ripgrep
for `bump-step-2-positive` and `bump-step-2-negative` across the
corpus before merge and re-grep post-merge.

### Option C — Standardise on a single term for the (e.i)/(e.ii) construct

**Approach.** Pick "sub-item" as the canonical term (matching the
already-pinned `(a)` / `(b)` / `(c)` / `(d)` "sub-item" usage in items
(a)–(c) of the editorial-review checklist) and rewrite the two
remaining usages in item (e)'s body and the *Outcome recording
conventions* appendix to use "sub-item" verbatim. The appendix
section's bold heading "**Item (e) two-sub-outcome recording
convention.**" stays as a section heading (i.e. the term "two-sub-outcome"
is preserved as a heading; the running prose under it switches to
"sub-item") so the heading slug doesn't change.

**Spec edits.** Two paragraphs in `version-bump-step2.md`: the
introductory sentence to (e.i)/(e.ii) at the end of item (e)'s body
("two atomic sub-outcomes below … each carrying its own scope") and
the appendix paragraph anchored at
`bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions` ("two atomic sub-outcomes
[(e.i)] and [(e.ii)] defined as sub-items under item (e)"). Update
`V18c-version-bump-checklist.md`'s *Ships when* phrasing "item (e)
recording its two sub-outcomes" / "either sub-outcome `(e.i)`/`(e.ii)`"
to use "sub-item" verbatim.

**Pros.** Single canonical term across both the host-prerequisites
"sub-item" usage in items (a)–(c) and the (e.i)/(e.ii) construct;
removes the appendix's two-of-three terms-for-one-concept reading.

**Cons.** "sub-outcome" is structurally accurate (a sub-outcome is a
pass/fail/N/A verdict; a sub-item is a structural sub-bullet under a
parent item) and "sub-item" merges the two. The merge is harmless at
loom 1.0 because every present sub-item happens to carry exactly one
verdict, but a future checklist item that adds two separate verdicts
under one structural sub-item would re-open the distinction.

**Risks.** None mechanical; the appendix's normative MUST keeps the
same propositional content under the renamed term.

### Recommendation

Land all three options sequentially as separate commits, in this order
so each fix lands on a stable baseline: **C → A → B**.

1. **C first** because it is the narrowest (two prose paragraphs plus
   two `V18c` `Ships when` substitutions). It removes the "sub-outcome"
   / "sub-check" drift before the corpus-wide letter-label sweep in A
   touches the same paragraphs.

2. **A second** because the letter-label disambiguation sweep covers
   most of the bump-procedure pages and the `V18c` leaves; doing it
   after C means C's terminology change is folded into the same
   paragraphs A re-reads, so the sweep doesn't risk re-introducing
   "sub-outcome" while qualifying "(a)".

3. **B last** because the anchor rename's inbound-citation sweep is
   the highest-blast-radius arm (~18 inbound `step2.md#bump-step-2-positive`
   / `step2.md#bump-step-2-negative` citations across nine files) and
   benefits from a clean baseline where the cited prose already uses
   the new terminology.

The implementer should run `rg 'bump-step-2-(positive|negative)' docs/`
after B's sweep and `rg 'sub-outcome|sub-check' docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/`
after C's sweep to confirm no residual usages remain. Edge cases to
watch:

- The audit pages' family→branch routing tables in
  `audit-resolution.md` (collapsed under `pi-integration-contract.md`'s
  consolidated section before the file split, now back at the individual
  topic files) carry `step2.md#bump-step-2-negative` and
  `step2b.md#bump-step-2b-…` links in adjacent table rows; both must
  be updated together by B.
- GOV-31 in `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` cites
  `bump-step-2-positive` by anchor in its in-code-constant carve-out
  prose; that citation is the test for B's sweep completeness.
- `V18c`'s `Ships when` prose embeds the bump-commit recording format
  verbatim ("item (e) recording its two sub-outcomes"). C must update
  that phrasing in lock-step with the spec text it mirrors.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A practitioner reading the bump procedure can still resolve each
cross-reference by re-reading the cited paragraph, so neither the
editorial-review checklist nor the SDK-test directions misfire in
practice. The cost is recurring reader friction every time
"step 2(a)" or "sub-outcome" is cited — ~60+ occurrences across the
PIC pages and the plan — and a long-running risk that a future spec
edit lands a fix or audit obligation under the wrong reading because
the surface prose admits both.
