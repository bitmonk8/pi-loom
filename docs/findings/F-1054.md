## Problem

Step 2 of the Pi version-bump procedure carries three independent labelling collisions across `version-bump-step2.md` and `version-bump-step2b.md`. (1) The letter labels `(a)`/`(b)` overload two distinct enumerations — the editorial-review checklist items `(a)`–`(an)` and the SDK-test directions anchored at `bump-step-2-positive`/`bump-step-2-negative` — with no disambiguation convention, so a bare "step 2(a)" cross-reference reads ambiguously. (2) The parent test-direction anchors `bump-step-2-positive`/`bump-step-2-negative` lack the `2b` segment their five `bump-step-2b-…` child branches carry, splitting the same anchor space inconsistently across the two files. (3) The (e.i)/(e.ii) construct is named three ways — "sub-outcome", "sub-check", and "sub-item" — across item (e)'s body and the appendix anchored at `bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions`, leaving its normative MUST with no stable anchored term.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** e5cd514 — pi-loom spec: resolve "T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit" (2026-05-09, Thomas Andersen); c5f97f6 — pi-loom spec: resolve "Item (e) recovery over-prescribes mutex; N/A and recording conventions buried" (2026-06-07, Thomas Andersen); 5d73cc3 — pi-loom spec: resolve "Item (e) fail predicate operator-precedence ambiguity and buried sub-outcomes" (2026-06-07, Thomas Andersen); 81bf52a — docs: collapse spec/ section pages into spec_topics/ (2026-06-11, Thomas Andersen).
**History:** Collisions 1 and 2 entered together in `e5cd514`, which split step 2 into the editorial-review checklist (items `(a)`–`(an)`) and the SDK-test directions (sub-step 2, `(a)` positive / `(b)` negative) and minted the `bump-step-2-positive` / `bump-step-2-negative` parent anchors alongside the five `bump-step-2b-…` child branches; the `2b` segment asymmetry was present in this single commit. Collision 3 grew over two later edits: `c5f97f6` introduced the *Outcome recording conventions* appendix and pinned the term "sub-outcome" for (e.i)/(e.ii); `5d73cc3` then split item (e)'s body into a separately-anchored sub-item list and used "sub-check" in the same paragraph, with the appendix's "sub-outcome" wording left in place — neither commit's predecessor carried both terms together, so the three-way drift ("sub-outcome" / "sub-check" / "sub-item" for the same construct) is the joint product of the two commits. The 2026-06-11 file split (`81bf52a`) physically separated the parent (`bump-step-2-positive` / `bump-step-2-negative`) from the children (`bump-step-2b-…`) into `version-bump-step2.md` and `version-bump-step2b.md` without renaming the anchors, sharpening collision 2's parent/child anchor split across two files without itself introducing it.

## Solution approach

Standardise the (e.i)/(e.ii) construct on a single term across item (e)'s body and the appendix anchored at `bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions` in `version-bump-step2.md`, eliminating the "sub-outcome"/"sub-check"/"sub-item" three-way drift (the editorial-review checklist already uses "sub-item" for items (a)–(c)). Add a disambiguation convention to the step-2 preamble distinguishing editorial-review-item citations from SDK-test-direction citations, keeping both `(a)`/`(b)` enumerations' labels intact. Rename the parent test-direction anchors `bump-step-2-positive`/`bump-step-2-negative` to `bump-step-2a`/`bump-step-2b` so the parent stem shares the `2b` segment its `bump-step-2b-…` children carry, carry the negative-direction anchor to `version-bump-step2b.md` where its prose lives, and update the inbound cross-references across the spec corpus and plan.

## Solution constraints

- The editorial-review checklist item labels (a)–(an) MUST NOT be renumbered or relabelled — the bump-commit message format documented at `V18c-version-bump-checklist.md`'s *Ships when* depends on them.
