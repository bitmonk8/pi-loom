## Finding

The opening paragraph of `capability-probe.md` declares that the factory "runs a synchronous probe of three host preconditions in the **fixed order (a) → (b) → (c)** below and stops at the first failure (short-circuit)." The section that follows, however, defines **five** sub-steps `(a)`–`(e)` — Node floor, `AbortSignal`/`AbortController` shape, factory-probable SDK capabilities, peer-dep version (lock-step, four packages), and `typebox` host-shape — and the brevity paragraph near the bottom of the section gives the actual short-circuit order as `(a) → (b) → (c)+(d) → (e)`. `PIC-5` further declares "Probes MUST NOT be added beyond the five enumerated checks," and the per-`kind` discriminator enumeration in *On failure: refusal and diagnostic* now carries seven values (including `"peer-dep-malformed-version"` and `"typebox-shape"`) that cannot arise under a three-precondition reading.

An implementer who wires from the opening sentence omits `(d)` and `(e)` entirely — i.e. the peer-dep lock-step iteration and the `typebox` `Type.Unsafe` host-shape check — and therefore ships a probe that never emits the `peer-dep-out-of-range`, `peer-dep-malformed-version`, or `typebox-shape` diagnostic kinds, and that does not surface a `details.package` field on the `peer-dep-*` routes. The contradiction is internal to a single section that the same paragraph designates the "**canonical source of truth** for every probe rule, the floor literal, the member-with-kind enumeration, and the factory-probable capability list," so a downstream test author or extension-runtime author resolving the contradiction by preferring the canonical opener (a reasonable reading discipline) silently drops two whole checks and two whole diagnostic-kind values.

The fix is mechanical: the opener must name five checks and cite the full short-circuit order `(a) → (b) → (c)+(d) → (e)` already pinned in the brevity paragraph, so the canonical preamble agrees with `PIC-5`, the brevity paragraph, the seven-value `kind` enumeration, and the Self-failure block's `details.step` set.

## Issue introduction

**History:** The Step 0 section was rewritten on 2026-05-06 by commit `126ff8a` ("pi-loom spec: Step 0 capability probe rewrite + V1.0 deferrals + Choice B"), which authored the current opener with the literal "three host preconditions in the **fixed order (a) → (b) → (c)**" and a brevity paragraph that — at that revision — described the probe as "four sub-steps `(a)`–`(d)`" with short-circuit `(a) → (b) → (c)+(d)`. Even at that revision the opener already mis-stated the count (three vs. four), but the discrepancy was local to that one phrase and the rest of the section was internally consistent. Two days later, commit `f072903` added sub-step `(e) typebox host-shape`, extended the brevity paragraph to "five sub-steps `(a)`–`(e)`" with short-circuit `(a) → (b) → (c)+(d) → (e)`, grew `PIC-5` from "four enumerated checks" to "five enumerated checks," extended the `kind` enumeration to include `"typebox-shape"`, and extended the Self-failure `details.step` set accordingly — but did **not** touch the opener's "three host preconditions / (a) → (b) → (c)" wording. That single missed edit is the present defect; it widened a pre-existing one-step undercount (three vs. four) into a two-step undercount (three vs. five) and turned the opener into a contradiction of its own section's `PIC-5`. The defect is therefore a single-commit miss within an otherwise complete rewrite, not a multi-commit interaction; the file was first added at commit `f5e89f4` (the 100KB/30KB size-cap migration) and `81bf52a` later collapsed `spec/` into `spec_topics/`, but neither touched the opener wording.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Reword the Step 0 opening paragraph so that:

1. The precondition count is "**five** host preconditions" (matching `PIC-5` and the brevity paragraph), and
2. The short-circuit order cited inline is the full `(a) → (b) → (c)+(d) → (e)` (matching the brevity paragraph's normative order and the Self-failure block's `details.step` set).

Suggested replacement for the opening sentence: "Before any `pi.registerFlag`, `pi.registerCommand`, `pi.registerTool`, `pi.registerMessageRenderer`, or `pi.on` call — collectively, the **factory-time host-binding calls** — the factory runs a synchronous probe of **five** host preconditions in the **fixed short-circuit order `(a) → (b) → (c)+(d) → (e)`** below — Node floor, `AbortSignal`/`AbortController` shape, factory-probable SDK capabilities, peer-dep lock-step version, and `typebox` host-shape — and stops at the first failure."

The five-check enumeration is named inline (not just by letter) so a reader who only ingests the opener cannot omit a check; the parenthetical naming `(c)+(d)` preserves the existing claim that the peer-dep iteration shares step `(c)`'s pinned-constants execution group and short-circuit slot. Keep the "canonical source of truth" claim and the forward links unchanged. No other paragraph in `capability-probe.md` needs to move — every downstream block (`PIC-5`, the brevity paragraph, the seven-value `kind` enumeration, the Self-failure `details.step` set, the *Post-probe SDK-shape drift* block) is already aligned with five checks.

Implementer edge case to watch: the rewritten opener must continue to forward-link to `spec.md — Host runtime preconditions 1–3` and to *Governance — GOV-30* unchanged, even though the probe now covers five sub-steps rather than three Host runtime preconditions — those two concepts have always had different cardinalities (the `spec.md` preconditions count host-level obligations; the probe sub-steps count probe checks), and that asymmetry is intentional under `GOV-30`'s lock-step-with-sources convention.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two implementers reading the same `capability-probe.md` section reach divergent surfaces: one that ships all five checks and emits seven `details.kind` values, and one that ships three checks and emits four `details.kind` values, omitting the peer-dep lock-step and the `typebox` host-shape entirely. The probe contract is the gate for every later `loom/load/host-incompatible` route and for the build-time inventory partition (`V18a`/`V18c` reconcile `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES` against `V9a`); a probe that quietly drops checks defeats both the runtime refusal and the build-time gates.
