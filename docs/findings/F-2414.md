## Finding

The inventory-audit corpus mixes two phases of obligation on the same pages without a legible boundary. `inventory-audit-intro.md` opens with the loom-1.0 **PIC-15 SDK capability inventory** (a 1.0 MUST), then flows — with no visible divider — into the **Inventory-closure audit** paragraph (an explicit post-1.0 hardening the implementation only *MAY* publish), and then into the **Strict-capability absence-under-the-probed-name arm**, which is again a loom-1.0 MUST ("MUST additionally fail …") relocated onto this page. `audit-target-categories.md` carries a top-of-page blockquote stamping the whole page as "post-1.0 hardening", yet the same page states two loom-1.0 step-2(a) obligations as normative MUSTs buried inside multi-hundred-word paragraphs: the **minimum-entry-shape enforcement** ("The SDK surface-inventory literal-read assertion … MUST additionally enforce the minimum-entry-shape obligation …") and the **branch-table-arm precondition** ("The … branch table MUST gain an arm for each new kind … before the first `npm test` invocation that runs the audit on `main`").

The page does carry one carve-out sentence ("the positive-direction step-2(a) literal-read-assertion obligations referenced on this page … are loom-1.0 gates and are not affected"), so the phase ownership is *recoverable* by a careful reader. But the loom-1.0 normative text remains physically interleaved with post-1.0 audit-detection mechanics under a post-1.0 banner, with no per-obligation phase label, no visual separator, and no consolidated index of what is deferred. There is no Non-goals section on `inventory-audit-intro.md` itself (only `version-bump-intro.md` carries one, scoped to the bump procedure), so the post-1.0 deferral of the automated audit is discoverable only by reading the inline prose.

The net effect is that the loom-1.0/post-1.0 phase boundary across the inventory-audit pages is not legible at a glance, and the loom-1.0 gates that must ship for 1.0 are the hardest part of the corpus to extract from the post-1.0 mechanics they are tangled with.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Make the loom-1.0/post-1.0 phase boundary legible in place across the inventory-audit pages, keeping each loom-1.0 obligation adjacent to the audit context that motivates it.

On `inventory-audit-intro.md`:

- Insert a visible phase divider between the loom-1.0 PIC-15 SDK capability inventory and the post-1.0 Inventory-closure audit paragraph, so the transition from 1.0 MUST to post-1.0 MAY is unmistakable at a glance.
- Lift the strict-capability absence-under-the-probed-name arm out of its surrounding paragraph into its own marked sub-block tagged `loom-1.0 gate`.
- Add a short Non-goals index mirroring the form of `version-bump-intro.md` §Non-goals: a scannable list of cross-links (not a restatement), naming the deferred post-1.0 items (the automated inventory-closure audit) and forward-linking the project-wide `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md` §loom 1.0 non-goals.

On `audit-target-categories.md`:

- Extract the minimum-entry-shape enforcement MUST and the branch-table-arm precondition MUST out of their multi-hundred-word paragraphs into their own `loom-1.0 gate` sub-blocks, keeping them on-page next to the post-1.0 mechanics they reference.

Ensure the phase divider and the per-block `loom-1.0 gate` tags are unambiguous enough to override the page-level "post-1.0 hardening" banner in a quick read. Keep the Non-goals index a scannable cross-link list rather than a restatement, so it does not become a second drift surface against `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md`.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer skimming the post-1.0 banner on `audit-target-categories.md` (or the inline "post-1.0 mechanization" framing on `inventory-audit-intro.md`) can mis-scope the buried loom-1.0 MUSTs — either deferring the minimum-entry-shape enforcement and branch-table arms as post-1.0, or over-building the post-1.0 audit detector as a 1.0 merge gate. The explicit carve-out sentences make the correct scoping recoverable, so a careful reader can still produce a working 1.0 system; the cost is discoverability and the elevated risk of mis-scoping under a quick read.
