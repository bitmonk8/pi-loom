## Finding

`host-prerequisites.md` opens by declaring "Four host-side prerequisites are load-bearing for the contract below" — the Pi SDK pin, the binder model, binder credentials, and the Pi-supplied `AbortSignal`. These are things the **Pi host** must provide. The page then explicitly carves loom's own implementation dependencies out of that contract: the "Loom-package implementation dependencies" paragraph states that `semver` and `chokidar` "are implementation-side choices rather than part of the Pi-supplied contract this page owns" and defers their detail to `implementation-notes.md`.

Immediately after that carve-out, the page adds a **`Build-toolchain floor (TypeScript brand-string surfacing)`** section (anchor `#tsc-toolchain-floor`) pinning `tsc >= 5.4.0`. This is a constraint on the **loom implementer's own build setup**, not a Pi-supplied host prerequisite — the same category as the `semver`/`chokidar` choices the preceding paragraph just deferred. Placing it under the "Host prerequisites" heading, one paragraph after a paragraph that explicitly says loom-side build dependencies belong elsewhere, blurs the Pi-side/loom-side boundary the page otherwise maintains, and no scoping statement separates the two concerns.

The floor itself is genuinely load-bearing: the build-time `SessionShutdownEvent['reason']` brand-string assertion (gated by the Pi version-bump procedure) relies on `tsc` surfacing `loom/typecheck/session-shutdown-reason-snapshot` verbatim, a property confirmed only at `>= 5.4.0`. So the defect is purely organisational — the floor is correctly stated and consumed; it is filed under the wrong conceptual heading.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Relocate to `implementation-notes.md`

- **Approach:** Move the `Build-toolchain floor` section out of `host-prerequisites.md` and into `implementation-notes.md`, adjacent to the existing "Loom-package implementation dependencies (loom 1.0)" section, carrying the `#tsc-toolchain-floor` anchor with it. Repoint the three remaining citers (`version-bump-intro.md`, `version-bump-triggers.md`, and `H1a`) to the new file path. Optionally leave a one-line pointer paragraph on `host-prerequisites.md` mirroring how that page already pointer-references the `semver`/`chokidar` deferral.
- **Spec edits:** Cut the `<a id="tsc-toolchain-floor">` paragraph from `host-prerequisites.md`; paste under `implementation-notes.md` §"Loom-package implementation dependencies (loom 1.0)"; repoint `version-bump-intro.md`, `version-bump-triggers.md`, and `H1a`'s anchor citations to `implementation-notes.md#tsc-toolchain-floor`.
- **Pros:** Places the loom-side toolchain floor with loom's other implementation-side build dependencies, fully resolving the Pi-side/loom-side category mismatch; matches the disposition the adjacent `semver`/`chokidar` paragraph already prescribes.
- **Cons:** Cascades citation repointing across two PIC version-bump pages and one plan leaf. Moves a normative floor (`>= 5.4.0` MUST, plus the re-confirm-on-bump MUST) away from the version-bump procedure that consumes it, increasing the distance between the rule and its callers.
- **Risks:** A missed citer would dangle the anchor. The `implementation-notes.md` destination section is itself flagged for possible relocation out of the spec body (see Related Findings), so Option A may move the floor into a section that subsequently moves again.

### Option B — Reframe the scope boundary in place

- **Approach:** Keep the section on `host-prerequisites.md` (preserving the anchor and all citations) but make the Pi-side/loom-side boundary explicit — e.g. introduce a short "Loom-side build prerequisites" sub-heading covering both the existing implementation-dependencies pointer paragraph and the toolchain floor, or add a one-clause scope note distinguishing the loom-side build requirements from the four Pi-supplied prerequisites.
- **Spec edits:** Add a sub-heading or scope sentence on `host-prerequisites.md` grouping the loom-side build/toolchain material and marking it distinct from the four host-side prerequisites; no anchor move, no citation repoint.
- **Pros:** Zero citation cascade; keeps the normative floor adjacent to the version-bump build gates (`version-bump-intro.md`, `version-bump-triggers.md`) that key on it. Resolves the heading-level scope blur the finding identifies.
- **Cons:** Leaves loom-side build material on a page titled "Host prerequisites," so the page is no longer purely Pi-supplied contract; relies on a sub-heading rather than physical separation to carry the distinction.
- **Risks:** A future reader skimming top-level headings may still mis-read the floor as a host-supplied prerequisite if the sub-heading is subtle.

### Recommendation

Option B. The floor is normative and is anchor-cited by the version-bump procedure that exists to keep it honest; keeping it adjacent to those build gates is a feature, and reframing the scope boundary resolves the heading mismatch at far lower cascade cost than relocation. Watch that the new sub-heading or scope sentence clearly partitions the loom-side build prerequisites from the four Pi-supplied ones, so the page's "host-side" framing is no longer contradicted.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The TypeScript floor is correctly stated and is consumed unchanged by `H1a`'s build-time assertion and the version-bump brand-string gate, so no implementer is misled into wrong behaviour. The cost is purely organisational: a reader auditing loom's packaging against the page's "host-side prerequisites" framing encounters a loom-side toolchain requirement filed alongside Pi-supplied contracts, one paragraph after the page disclaimed ownership of exactly that category.
