## Finding

The spec consistently attributes the `AbortSignal` / `AbortController` shape it depends on to the WHATWG standard ("the Node-bundled WHATWG implementation", "WHATWG `AbortSignal` / `AbortController` member surface", "whose derived signal's `reason` mirrors the source's `reason` per WHATWG"), but nowhere in the corpus is the WHATWG document actually identified — no URL, no section number, no edition tag. A corpus-wide search for `whatwg.org` or `dom.spec` returns zero hits. The standard is named in prose only.

The attribution appears at five sites: `overview-and-orientation.md:35` (Non-functional / Host runtime prerequisite 2), `pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md:14` (Host prerequisite 4 — Pi-supplied `AbortSignal`), `pi-integration-contract/capability-probe.md:13` (Step 0 (b), declared by the spec itself as the *canonical source of truth* for the member surface), `pi-integration-contract/capability-inventory-items.md:11` (SDK capability 5 — Cancellation propagation), and `cancellation.md:13` (CNCL-4's `AbortSignal.any([source])` reason-mirroring claim, which is the one site that asserts a specific behavioural property "per WHATWG"). The other corpus references to `AbortSignal` (e.g. `errors-and-results/error-model.md:56`, `errors-and-results/queryerror-variants.md:134, 166`, `session-model-and-appendix.md:27`) only describe loom-side observable behaviour and don't carry a WHATWG attribution, so they don't need a locator themselves — they inherit from the canonical site.

The defect is therefore a single missing pointer at the spec's own self-declared canonical site (`capability-probe.md` Step 0 (b)) plus the one normative claim that depends on a specific WHATWG behavioural property (`cancellation.md` CNCL-4's reason-mirroring assertion). Without a locator a future reviewer cannot mechanically verify either the member-surface enumeration or the reason-mirroring claim against the actual standard, and a WHATWG-side rename or behavioural drift in `AbortSignal.any` is silently absorbed.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 022bff9 — pi-loom spec: resolve "Orientation prerequisites + Host runtime + pi-integration-contract Host prerequisites" (2026-05-06, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** `AbortSignal` / `AbortController` were referenced behaviourally in `spec.md` from `fae85d3` (2026-05-04) and earlier — but with no standard attribution and so no locator obligation. Commit `022bff9` introduced the WHATWG attribution at two sites (`spec.md` Host runtime, Host prerequisite 4) without a URL or section pointer, and that omission was carried forward by the subsequent `f4b6738` (the `cancellation.md` "per WHATWG" reason-mirroring paragraph) and `f5e89f4` (the size-cap split into `overview-and-orientation.md`, `capability-probe.md`, and `capability-inventory-items.md`, which only relocated existing prose). The defect — "named by standard but no locator" — is therefore localised to the standard-attribution edit `022bff9`; every later WHATWG mention is a downstream restatement.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add one normative external-reference line at `pi-integration-contract/capability-probe.md` Step 0 (b) — which the spec already declares is "the canonical source of truth for the WHATWG `AbortSignal` / `AbortController` member surface" — pointing at the WHATWG DOM Standard's AbortController section, citing both the human-readable URL and the section number, e.g.:

> *External reference (non-normative locator).* The WHATWG `AbortSignal` / `AbortController` types referenced throughout this sub-step are defined by the [WHATWG DOM Standard §3.2 *Interface AbortController* and §3.3 *Interface AbortSignal*](https://dom.spec.whatwg.org/#interface-abortcontroller). The member surface enumerated below is the loom-consumed subset of that standard's published interface.

At `cancellation.md` CNCL-4, replace the bare "per WHATWG" parenthetical on the `AbortSignal.any([source])` claim with a sentence-anchor reference to the specific WHATWG step that defines `AbortSignal.any`'s reason mirroring (currently `dom.spec.whatwg.org#dom-abortsignal-any`), and cross-link the rest of CNCL-4's standard-dependent claims back to Step 0 (b)'s new locator block by anchor.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The fix is additive prose at a self-declared canonical site; do not delete the existing "WHATWG" attributions at the four other sites — they remain in force, each already cross-links to Step 0 (b), and the new locator block is the single place those forward-links land.
- The WHATWG DOM Standard is a Living Standard with no edition / version tag; the URL is the stable identifier and section numbers are stable across edits. Do not invent a version pin the standard itself does not publish.
- WHATWG URLs are external Markdown-link targets; under [GOV-17](./governance/corpus-direction-and-scope.md#gov-17)'s External-non-Markdown-resources rule they are vacuously dependencies of the spec corpus and the cross-reference is permitted.
- Do not add the locator at the four downstream sites — they already correctly defer to Step 0 (b) as the canonical source, and duplicating the URL there re-introduces the multi-source-of-truth shape the spec already eliminated.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reviewer or future maintainer who needs to check the WHATWG surface that the build-time SDK capability probe and CNCL-4's reason-mirroring claim depend on must guess the right WHATWG document and section. A WHATWG-side rename of an enumerated member or a change in `AbortSignal.any`'s reason-propagation semantics is not detectable from the spec text alone. Implementers can still build a working system; the gap is in audit traceability, not in correctness.
