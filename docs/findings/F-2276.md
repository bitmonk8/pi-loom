## Problem

The `query-terminating` entry in `glossary.md` (`#query-terminating`) violates the glossary preamble's contract that each entry be a one-paragraph descriptive reminder whose canonical, normative definition lives on the page named in its `See:` reference. Instead the entry is a multi-sentence normative specification of ceiling routing: which ceilings panic versus return `Err`, the exact condition under which an `Err`-class breach reaches the trichotomy *fail* arm, and the discard observability of the carrier. That normative content is already owned canonically by `ceilings-3-and-4.md` (the Ceiling-#1 panic-uniqueness invariant and the ceiling-#4 per-boundary table) and by `error-model.md` (the unhandled-`Err`→*fail* discard semantics), so the entry restates rules in a container the spec explicitly designates non-authoritative.

A later edit, 51ae8f1 (2026-06-06, "Glossary query-terminating parks always-log membership owned elsewhere"), trimmed the always-log membership out but left the ceiling-routing prose in place, so the placement defect has persisted unchanged since the entry's birth.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the `#query-terminating` entry to the one-paragraph descriptive stub the glossary preamble requires — naming what the term denotes and contrasting it with *in-loop* — and delete the normative ceiling-routing prose. Replace the stripped rules with `See:` forward-links to `ceilings-3-and-4.md#ceiling-set-invariants` (panic-uniqueness invariant) and `#ceiling-4-table` (ceiling-#4 per-boundary routing), and to the `error-model.md` *Failure* bullet (unhandled-`Err`→*fail* discard semantics). Point the discard-observability aside to its canonical owner rather than restating it.
