## Problem

In `overview-and-orientation.md`'s `## Orientation` section, the **Hard ceilings** bullet is filed under `### Scope` alongside **Forward-compatibility seams**, but it does not match its sibling in kind. Hard ceilings are runtime operational resource limits (an `invoke`-chain depth bound, a per-query `tool_loop.max_rounds` cap, a binder per-class retry budget, and a JSON-document depth limit) — non-functional operational constraints of the same character as the bullets the `### Non-functional requirements` subsection already collects (trust boundary, source-language stability, runtime observability), not scope-boundary dispositions. Filing the bullet under `### Scope` also forces the Scope intro into the umbrella phrase "scope-boundary dispositions" to cover a bullet that is not a scope boundary. This is an organization/taxonomy defect in the orientation layer only; the normative ceiling contracts in `hard-ceilings.md` and its forward-linked owner pages are unaffected.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** b78063e — pi-loom spec: resolve "Cross-cutting NFR bullets lack a discoverable home in spec.md" (2026-06-03, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** Before b78063e the `### Scope` subsection (then in `spec.md`) listed five cross-cutting dispositions together — Trust boundary, Source-language stability, Runtime observability, Forward-compatibility seams, and Hard ceilings — so no Scope-vs-NFR mismatch existed. That commit created the `### Non-functional requirements` subsection and relocated the three operational-attribute bullets (Trust boundary, Source-language stability, Runtime observability) into it, but left Hard ceilings — an operational resource-limit disposition of the same character — behind under `### Scope`, relabelling the Scope intro from "five cross-cutting dispositions" to "two cross-cutting scope-boundary dispositions." That asymmetric relocation is what placed an NFR-class bullet under `### Scope`. The later 81bf52a collapse of `spec/` into `spec_topics/` carried the arrangement into `overview-and-orientation.md` unchanged.

## Solution approach

Move the `id="hard-runtime-ceilings"` **Hard ceilings** bullet — together with its trailing `id="ceiling-interaction-order"` cross-ceiling paragraph and the closing GOV-15 carve-out sentence, which form one disposition — from `### Scope` into `### Non-functional requirements` as a fourth NFR bullet. Rewrite the `### Scope` intro to name only Forward-compatibility seams and drop the "scope-boundary dispositions" framing. Update the `### Non-functional requirements` intro to enumerate hard ceilings as the fourth operational disposition.

## Solution constraints

- Keep the `id="hard-runtime-ceilings"` and `id="ceiling-interaction-order"` anchors unchanged on the moved block — inbound cross-links resolve to these page-local anchors and would break if renamed.
- Do not add hard ceilings to the loom 1.0 non-goals index or change the NFR intro's "two no-seam dispositions" count — hard ceilings are not a non-goal.
