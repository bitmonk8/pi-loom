## Problem

PIC-55 in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/registration-steps.md` (anchor `#pic-55`, under step 5) splits the post-`error` / post-throw watcher posture into two arms keyed on whether the watcher "keeps delivering events on the watched roots after the error" (transient `ctx.ui.notify` toast, no further action) versus the error/throw leaving "one or more watched roots no longer delivering events" (persistent `loom/runtime/watcher-terminated` `loom-system-note`, watcher torn down, operator runs `/reload`). The classifying predicate is not observable at the failure site: the `FileWatcher` seam (PIC-14) carries only `add | change | unlink` plus `Unsubscribe`, and chokidar's `error` event payload is an `Error` with no terminal / "I have stopped delivering" flag, so "no longer delivering events" is an absence-of-future-events condition that cannot be decided when the `error` or throw is observed. Two conforming implementers therefore diverge â€” one routes every `error` to the transient toast and never emits `watcher-terminated` (a dead watcher silently strands hot-reload), the other routes every `error` to the persistent note and prompts `/reload` for benign errors. The `loom/runtime/watcher-terminated` row in `code-registry-runtime.md` and the `transient-toasts` case (1) in `diagnostic-shape.md` repeat the same unobservable predicate.

The commit subject names F-1299 + F-1308 as the drivers; neither prior finding raised the observability of the classifying predicate, so the defect is an authoring gap in the coining edit itself rather than a regression of an earlier resolved finding.

## Solution approach

Rewrite PIC-55 (`#pic-55`) so the watcher post-`error` / post-throw arm selection keys on an observable input â€” either the act of observing the `error` / synchronous throw itself, or a defined terminal condition the adapter can evaluate at the failure site â€” rather than on the unobservable future-delivery predicate. Reconcile the `loom/runtime/watcher-terminated` trigger clause in `code-registry-runtime.md` and the `transient-toasts` case (1) in `diagnostic-shape.md` so neither continues to reference the "no longer delivering events" predicate.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the chokidar library-naming reword of the `watcher-terminated` trigger clause and PIC-55, owned by F-1796.
- Preserve PIC-55's existing drain-state-tag MUST-NOT-write rule; the `session_shutdown` handler-entry short-circuit depends on the tag being written only by the teardown path.
