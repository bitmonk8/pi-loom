## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings/ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` ends its *Audit methodology* section with a `***Release gate.***` paragraph that is pure spec-maintenance / release-process governance, not runtime-implementation content. It defines how a four-axis "closure defect" surfaces "during the GOV-15 reviewer-inspection step at release time as a GOV-30 lock-step violation and blocks the release until the per-operation acceptance criterion below is satisfied," then enumerates the per-operation acceptance criteria for an introduction / retirement / relaxation and states that "The GOV-15 reviewer-inspection step … MUST confirm" the post-edit ceiling-set invariants.

Every actor in this paragraph is a future spec editor or release reviewer; every MUST is discharged by editing the spec and running the GOV-15 inspection, not by anything a loom-runtime implementer builds. It sits among genuinely normative ceiling content (the `NOCEIL-1`…`NOCEIL-4` claims and the four-axis closure list), so an implementer reading the page forward to understand the runtime ceiling contract encounters release-gating procedure that does not constrain the implementation. The governance home for this material already exists: GOV-15's ceiling-set carve-out (`docs/spec_topics/governance/source-language-stability.md`) and the GOV-30 lock-step rule the paragraph itself cites.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commit:** `2ff03c2` (2026-05-10, *pi-loom spec: resolve "Source-language stability bullet leaves equivalence undefined at the aggregator and silently contradicts the open-set ceiling clause"*)
**History:** The entire `*Audit methodology*` / four-axis closure list / `*Routing obligation for future ceilings*` / `*loom 1.0.0 rejected-candidate record*` / `*Release gate*` block was added wholesale to `docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings.md` in `2ff03c2` (`git log -G "Routing obligation for future ceilings" --all --reverse` first match). The three-case introduction taxonomy, the four-trajectory retirement rewrite forms (i)–(iv), the SHA-or-tag pointer form, and the release-gate acceptance criteria were all present at first introduction — they were never staged in separately. Subsequent commits (`81bf52a` collapsed `spec/` into `spec_topics/` and relocated the block to `hard-ceilings/ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md`; `f5e89f4` enforced the size cap; `7995d3c` and `fe8aa5c` did the GOV-15 / GOV-12→GOV-30 relettering across the corpus) edited cross-references and labels but left the inline expansion in place. The over-broad placement is therefore an inception-time scoping choice in `2ff03c2`, not drift accumulated through later edits, and the parallel content in `*Operational definitions*` on `source-language-stability.md` was added across `2ff03c2` and follow-on commits without ever being deduplicated against the ceiling-page copy.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move the `***Release gate.***` paragraph out of `ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` into the GOV-15 ceiling-set carve-out in `docs/spec_topics/governance/source-language-stability.md` (`#ceiling-set-carve-out`), where the GOV-15 reviewer-inspection step and the GOV-30 lock-step obligation already live. The paragraph's acceptance criteria for introduction / retirement / relaxation reference the *Routing obligation* and four-axis closure list that remain on the ceiling page; preserve those as resolving cross-links (by anchor) from the relocated paragraph back to `ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md`. Leave the `<a id="release-gate"></a>` anchor reachable — either carry it to the new location or leave a one-line forward-link stub at the old site — so existing inbound references to `#release-gate` do not break.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Release-process governance addressed to spec editors is interleaved with normative runtime-ceiling content, adding reading burden for implementers and splitting the ceiling-set change procedure across the hard-ceilings page and the GOV-15/GOV-30 governance cluster. No runtime observer behaves differently; this is an organisational placement defect.
