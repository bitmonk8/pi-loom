## Finding

Capability inventory item 3 (`sdk-cap-subagent-isolated-session`) in `capability-inventory-items.md` closes its transcript-privacy discussion with a sentence that defends *why* on-bump re-validation is split across two checklist items:

> "A Pi-side change that preserved the signature but persisted the transcript would pass item (o) yet be caught by item (aj)'s behavioural audit, so this presupposition's detection is routed to editorial review at bump time on the same footing as the others audited under item (o)."

The two preceding sentences already state the normative content: item (o) confirms the `static inMemory(cwd?: string): SessionManager` signature, item (aj) audits the no-persistence behaviour by reading the in-memory-session branch's JS body, and step 2(a)'s seven-capability assertion does not enumerate `SessionManager.inMemory`. The quoted sentence adds no obligation, no signature, and no checklist semantics an implementer needs — it is design-rationale prose that justifies a decision already fully expressed by the obligations it references. It also carries one instance of the imprecise "on the same footing as" connector.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** 252acda — pi-loom spec: resolve "SessionManager.inMemory transcript-privacy guarantee is unverified" (2026-06-05, Thomas Andersen); 9ced9f1 — pi-loom spec: resolve "subagent transcript-discard not routed to bump checklist" (2026-06-08, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The defensive "accepted risk … on the same footing as the others audited under item (o)" prose first entered item 3 in 252acda, which resolved a prior finding about the unverified transcript-privacy guarantee by recording the gap as an explicit unpinned presupposition. 9ced9f1 then resolved a follow-up finding by splitting re-validation into items (o) and (aj) and rewrote that defence into its current form — the exact sentence the present finding quotes. The cruft therefore accreted across two successive remediation commits, each layering rationale onto item 3 rather than confining its edit to the normative obligation.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Delete the sentence beginning "A Pi-side change that preserved the signature but persisted the transcript…" from item 3. The two preceding sentences (item (o) confirms the signature; item (aj) audits the no-persistence behaviour) plus the step-2(a) non-enumeration note already carry every obligation an implementer or auditor acts on. Leave the surrounding normative prose and the satellite-pin pointer untouched.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Pure wording. No implementer or runtime observer is affected; the normative obligations (items (o) and (aj), and the step-2(a) exclusion) remain intact whether or not the defence sentence is present. The only cost of leaving it is reading burden and one more occurrence of the vague "on the same footing as" connector.
