## Finding

The `<a id="loom-typecheck-namespace"></a> **Out of scope: `loom/typecheck/*`.**` paragraph in [`docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md`](../../docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md) opens by declaring the `loom/typecheck/*` namespace out of scope for the runtime diagnostics machinery the page owns — the brands carry no registry row, are never emitted through `loom-system-note` or `console.error`, and are not subject to the Code registry rules below — then immediately attaches three normative obligations about that out-of-scope surface:

1. **Naming convention** — pins the `loom/typecheck/<kebab-case-name>` brand grammar and forbids `<...>` placeholders, justified by `tsc`'s verbatim string-literal-type comparison.
2. **Definition site** — requires each brand to be defined inline at the spec-prose site that introduces its build-time assertion and forbids a central registry analogous to the runtime Code registry.
3. **Audit-gate carve-out** — obligates build-time audit gates that scan source or spec for `loom/...` literals to treat `loom/typecheck/` as a prefix-disjoint namespace.

All three are facts about a build-time `tsc` surface whose only existing instance, `loom/typecheck/session-shutdown-reason-snapshot`, is born in [Pi Integration Contract — Pi version bump procedure step 5](../../docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-triggers.md), whose verbatim-surfacing dependency is anchored at [Host prerequisites — Build-toolchain floor](../../docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md#tsc-toolchain-floor), and whose audit-gate carve-outs are already restated on the consuming leaves ([`H5a`](../../docs/plan_topics/H5a-closing-gate-automation.md), [`V18b`](../../docs/plan_topics/V18b-inventory-audit.md)). The runtime-diagnostics page is neither the source-of-truth for the brand grammar nor the home of its enforcement; collocating these obligations there forces a reader of the version-bump procedure (or the inventory-closure audit) to discover them on an unrelated runtime page, and conversely invites a contributor adding a runtime `loom/host/*` row to read the brand rules as adjacent to the Code registry surface they share a section with.

The misalignment is narrow but real: `loom/typecheck/*` shares the `loom/...` lexical prefix with the runtime namespaces but is otherwise an unrelated surface, and the spec already has natural homes for each of the three rules in the PIC build-time-machinery prose.

## Issue introduction

Commit `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04, "docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set") split the monolith into the `diagnostics/` subdirectory and carried the paragraph unchanged into `diagnostic-shape.md`, preserving but not introducing the defect. `git log -L` and `git log -S 'loom/typecheck'` over the anchor confirm no intervening edit re-shaped the three sub-clauses; the only post-introduction touches are downstream wording polish on the surrounding paragraphs (`8837a93`, `b7d383a`).

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Single relocation to PIC version-bump step 5, with a one-sentence pointer left on `diagnostic-shape.md`

**Approach.** Move all three sub-clauses verbatim into [`version-bump-triggers.md`](../../docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-triggers.md) step 5, immediately after the existing *Bundled-concerns orientation* paragraph that introduces the `loom/typecheck/session-shutdown-reason-snapshot` brand. Replace the body of the `loom-typecheck-namespace` paragraph on `diagnostic-shape.md` with a single short sentence: that `loom/typecheck/*` is a parallel build-time surface owned by [PIC step 5](../../docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-triggers.md), is not a runtime diagnostic namespace, carries no registry row, and is carved out of the Code registry below by reference. Preserve the `<a id="loom-typecheck-namespace"></a>` anchor in place so inbound references (currently none by URL, but future-proofed) still resolve.

**Spec edits.**
- `diagnostic-shape.md`: replace the three sub-clauses' prose with the one-sentence pointer; keep the anchor.
- `version-bump-triggers.md` step 5: insert the three sub-clauses as a dedicated paragraph (suggested anchor `loom-typecheck-namespace-governance`) and update the existing brand-introducing sentence to cross-link the new home for the grammar / definition-site / audit-gate rules.
- No other edits.

**Pros.** Collocates the brand grammar with the only assertion that produces a brand string in loom 1.0; the *Audit-gate carve-out* sits one section away from the inventory-closure audit prose on the same `pi-integration-contract/` directory; the *Definition site* "no central registry" rule sits adjacent to step 5's own non-central authoring discipline; a future contributor adding a second brand at a different PIC step finds the naming and definition-site rules where they are looking; the diagnostic-shape page is left scope-clean.

**Cons.** The audit-gate carve-out is normatively read by build-time audit gates that primarily live downstream of `inventory-audit-intro.md`, not `version-bump-triggers.md`; readers of the audit pages have to follow one extra hop to reach it.

**Risks.** Low. The relocation is a pure prose move with one-sentence pointer; no normative drift if the three sub-clauses move verbatim.

### Option B — Split by concern: brand grammar + definition site to PIC step 5, audit-gate carve-out to PIC inventory-audit

**Approach.** Same as Option A for the *Naming convention* and *Definition site* sub-clauses, but move the *Audit-gate carve-out* sub-clause separately into [`inventory-audit-intro.md`](../../docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/inventory-audit-intro.md) (the existing *Audit scope* / *Target surface categories* section), where audit-gate normative discipline already lives. The pointer paragraph on `diagnostic-shape.md` covers all three by reference.

**Spec edits.**
- `diagnostic-shape.md`: same pointer-only replacement as Option A.
- `version-bump-triggers.md` step 5: insert *Naming convention* and *Definition site* only.
- `inventory-audit-intro.md`: insert the *Audit-gate carve-out* paragraph as a `loom/...`-prefix scoping clause (suggested anchor `loom-typecheck-audit-carveout`), restating the prefix-disjoint obligation the carve-out pins.
- No other edits.

**Pros.** Each sub-clause lands on the page whose readers it actually constrains; the audit-gate carve-out reaches inventory-audit readers without a hop.

**Cons.** Splits a coherent three-rule namespace-governance paragraph across two pages, weakening the "one paragraph, one surface" property; doubles the relocation surface area and the diff blast radius; future additions to the namespace governance (a second brand, a second audit gate) require deciding which of the two pages to extend.

**Risks.** Splitting raises the future-edit coordination cost; a future contributor extending the namespace governance may update one page and forget the other.

### Recommendation

Option A. The brand-grammar / definition-site / audit-gate rules are three facets of one namespace contract; keeping them in one paragraph at the page where the only brand is born (PIC step 5) preserves coherence, and the one-sentence pointer on `diagnostic-shape.md` keeps the runtime-diagnostics reader oriented without restating any normative content. Implementer should watch two edge cases: (i) the relocated paragraph MUST keep the existing `<a id="loom-typecheck-namespace"></a>` anchor live on the destination page (or add a redirect anchor) so any future cross-page reference using that fragment continues to resolve; (ii) the cross-references this defect cited as "owned by PIC step 5 brand-string surfacing" already in the relocated sub-clauses (e.g. the `version-bump-intro.md` step 5 link, the `host-prerequisites.md` `#tsc-toolchain-floor` link) need re-pointing if they currently dereference back through `diagnostic-shape.md`, but a verbatim move keeps the link targets unchanged.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A contributor reading the version-bump procedure or the inventory-closure audit cannot reach the brand grammar, definition-site discipline, or audit-gate carve-out from those pages; conversely a contributor adding a runtime `loom/host/*` row encounters build-time brand rules adjacent to the Code registry. No implementer would diverge — the rules are unambiguous in their current location — but the spec's scope discipline is degraded and the build-time-machinery prose is left without its governing rules.
