## Problem

The `loom/typecheck/*` namespace-governance rules — the brand naming convention, the inline definition-site discipline, and the audit-gate carve-out — are authored in the `loom-typecheck-namespace` paragraph of `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md`, a runtime-diagnostics page. `loom/typecheck/*` is a build-time `tsc` brand surface owned by the PIC version-bump procedure step 5, where its only instance (`loom/typecheck/session-shutdown-reason-snapshot`) is born; it carries no registry row and shares only the `loom/...` lexical prefix with the runtime namespaces. Collocating the three obligations on the runtime page forces a reader of the version-bump procedure to discover the brand grammar and enforcement on an unrelated page, and invites a contributor adding a runtime `loom/host/*` row to read the build-time rules as adjacent to the Code registry.

Commit `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04, "docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set") split the monolith into the `diagnostics/` subdirectory and carried the paragraph unchanged into `diagnostic-shape.md`, preserving but not introducing the defect. `git log -L` and `git log -S 'loom/typecheck'` over the anchor confirm no intervening edit re-shaped the three sub-clauses; the only post-introduction touches are downstream wording polish on the surrounding paragraphs (`8837a93`, `b7d383a`).

## Solution approach

Move the three `loom/typecheck/*` governance sub-clauses (*Naming convention*, *Definition site*, *Audit-gate carve-out*) out of `diagnostic-shape.md`'s `loom-typecheck-namespace` paragraph into the PIC version-bump procedure step 5 in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-triggers.md`, near the prose introducing the `loom/typecheck/session-shutdown-reason-snapshot` brand. Add an anchor on the relocated governance and a forward-link from step 5's brand-introducing sentence to it. Rewrite the `loom-typecheck-namespace` paragraph on `diagnostic-shape.md` to a stub stating that `loom/typecheck/*` is a build-time surface owned by PIC step 5, carries no registry row, and is carved out of the Code registry below by reference.
