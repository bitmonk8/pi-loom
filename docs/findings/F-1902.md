## Finding

`H4a` introduces the test double under the full name **"in-process Pi session double"** and then refers back to it with two different short forms within the same leaf: **"in-process double"** (Adds paragraph and the fidelity-contract self-check `Convention:` bullet) and **"session double"** (the `Convention:` (end-to-end harness) bullet — "dispatched end-to-end against the session double"). The hyphenated compound **"session-double fidelity contract"** is a separate, deliberately-fixed term and is used consistently; it is not part of this inconsistency.

The bare form "session double" is the more ambiguous of the two because "session" is an overloaded `@earendil-works/pi-coding-agent` concept in this corpus (`AgentSession`, `createAgentSession`, the `session_start` / `session_shutdown` events, "Pi session"). A reader scanning the harness leaves can read "session double" as a double *of a session object* rather than the in-process stand-in for the whole Pi host. The unambiguous short form "in-process double" does not carry that collision.

The drift is not confined to `H4a`: sibling leaves reach for further variants — `V14b` writes "in-process session double" (dropping "Pi"), and `V4f` / `V4f-T` / `V18d` write the bare "session double" / "H4a session double". The corpus would read more clearly if the bare short form were standardised on "in-process double" while the fixed compound "session-double fidelity contract" is preserved verbatim.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Standardise the bare short form on **"in-process double"** and leave the full form "in-process Pi session double" (acceptable as the expanded first reference) and the fixed compound "session-double fidelity contract" untouched.

- In `docs/plan_topics/H4a-factory-shell-and-harness.md`, the `Convention:` (end-to-end harness) `Tests` bullet currently reads "asserts a registered command can be dispatched end-to-end against the **session double**." Change "session double" to "in-process double" so the leaf uses one short form throughout. (The Adds paragraph and the fidelity-contract self-check bullet already use "in-process double" and the compound "session-double fidelity contract" — leave those.)
- In `docs/plan_topics/V4f-no-rollback.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V4f-T-no-rollback.md`, the ERR-13 bullet's "modelled through the `H4a` session double" → "modelled through the `H4a` in-process double".
- In `docs/plan_topics/V18d-version-bump-acceptance.md`, the runtime-evidence-gate bullet's "the `H4a` session double's fidelity" and "a green double-backed run" → use "in-process double" for the bare reference.
- In `docs/plan_topics/V14b-tool-calls-parallel-batch.md`, the Adds and Ships-when references "in-process session double" → "in-process double".

Wherever the literal compound "session-double fidelity contract" appears (`H4a`, `H4b`, `V12a`, `V12a-T`, `V18d`, `real-host-smoke-gate.md`), preserve it verbatim — it is the fixed name of the contract, not a stray short form. The full form "in-process Pi session double" used at `H7a` / `V12a` / `V12a-T` is the canonical expanded reference and may stay.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader of the harness leaves can misread "session double" as a double of a Pi *session* object rather than the in-process stand-in for the whole host, because "session" is otherwise an SDK concept (`AgentSession`, `session_start`). No implementer diverges materially; the meaning is recoverable from context. This is a wording / consistency defect.
