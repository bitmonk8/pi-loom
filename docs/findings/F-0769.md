## Problem

GOV-9 (`id="gov-9"`) in `docs/spec_topics/governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` opens with a MUST that is mechanically undecidable in both halves. Its trigger predicate — "depends on a normative rule from another topic page" — has no structural witness: no surface signal (cross-link, token mention, shared identifier) is necessary or sufficient to decide whether one page's prose presupposes another's obligation. Its compliance arm — "state the rule locally" — is by construction indistinguishable from original content, with no oracle for "states the same rule" (a limit the spec itself concedes for prose equivalence in GOV-8 and GOV-30). Two reviewers enforcing GOV-9 on a PR will diverge on which paragraphs trigger the MUST and on whether a paraphrase satisfies it.

## Solution approach

Rewrite GOV-9's opening MUST so its trigger and compliance arm turn on a structurally decidable predicate over surface tokens rather than the semantic cross-page-dependency judgement, and drop the "state the rule locally" arm. Keep the narrative-page section-link allowance (already structural) and leave the universal-resolvability sentence and the GOV-22-residue clause unchanged. A paraphrased local restatement that names no foreign token falls outside the narrowed trigger by construction; note that explicitly if useful.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: GOV-22 (`id="gov-22"`) and its progressive-coinage obligation, owned by the GOV-22 decision-dependency finding — do not redefine it here.
- The narrowed MUST must continue to carry the `#prefix-n` cross-link contract that GOV-22's back-link cites, so that back-link still resolves.
