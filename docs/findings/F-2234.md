## Problem

In the *Partial-append fate during teardown* bullet of `session-shutdown-semantics.md`, the phrase "remain in whatever conversation Pi assigns them to under the swap's `event.reason`" reads two ways: that the reason value determines the destination conversation, or that the reason is merely contextual framing for a placement Pi controls independently of it. The surrounding text confirms the latter — the runtime makes no rollback, migration, or compensation attempt regardless of reason — but the function-input phrasing invites the former, sending an implementer hunting for a per-reason placement rule that does not exist anywhere in the corpus. No normative MUST depends on the phrase, so the cost is interpretive rather than behavioural.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** introduced-in-single-commit
**Introducing commit:** `7f37dd7` — *pi-loom spec: resolve "Session-swap behaviour for in-flight loom invocations is under-specified"* (2026-05-07)
**History:** The corpus is git-tracked. `git log -S "whatever conversation Pi assigns them to under the swap"` and `git log -S "Partial-append fate during teardown"` show the bullet did not exist before `7f37dd7`; that commit authored the *Partial-append fate during teardown* bullet into `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` (`git show 7f37dd7` line +155), carrying the "under the swap's `event.reason`" phrasing verbatim from the resolving review proposal it consumed. The defect has been present since that authoring. A later size-cap split (`f5e89f4`) relocated the bullet into `session-shutdown-semantics.md` without altering the phrase; `bfef26e` ("Per-event.reason session lifecycle asserted as fact …") touched neighbouring reason-handling prose but did not address this phrase. The ambiguity is intrinsic to the original wording, not the product of a multi-commit interaction.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the trailing phrase in the *Partial-append fate during teardown* bullet so that the conversation assignment is stated as Pi's own behaviour, identical for every `event.reason` value and neither controlled nor branched on by the loom runtime. Preserve the bullet's role as the `event.reason`-scoped restatement of the [Partial-append contract](../errors-and-results/error-model.md#partial-append-contract) and keep the existing follow-on sentence about the outgoing session on `reason: "new" | "resume" | "fork"`, which is already consistent with the reason-independent reading.

## Solution constraints

- Do not introduce a per-reason placement table or any per-`event.reason` branch — the correct contract is reason-independent no-op behaviour, so the edit removes a phantom dependence rather than specifying one.
