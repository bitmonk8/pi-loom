## Problem

The `loom/runtime/match-error` Tests bullet in both `V4a-match-result.md` and `V4a-T-match-result.md` ends with "`V4b` also closes `loom/runtime/match-error`; co-asserted here", leaving `V4a-T`'s assertion scope undetermined. The clause admits both an independent-full-assertion reading (`V4a-T` duplicates the panic's `?`/`match` bypass and message-template properties) and a note-only cross-reference reading (`V4a-T` asserts only the raise/bind exhaustion behaviour and defers the rest). The spec splits these concerns — exhaustion semantics versus the bypass rule and normative message templates — and the plan mirrors that split, with `V4b` owning the closed panic set and its templates. Two reasonable implementers therefore diverge on what `V4a-T` must test.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 22f762c — pi-loom plan: resolve "V4a third Tests bullet conflates three match behaviours" (2026-06-10, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The `match`-error Tests bullet originally read as one terse line ("`match` exhaustiveness over the six patterns; a non-matching value raises `MatchError`; arm result type is the LUB of arms") with no cross-leaf note. Commit 22f762c split that bullet to resolve an earlier "conflates three match behaviours" finding and, in doing so, appended the "`V4b` also closes `loom/runtime/match-error`; co-asserted here" sentence to both `V4a-match-result.md` and `V4a-T-match-result.md`. The ambiguous scope of "co-asserted here" entered the corpus with that single commit.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the trailing "co-asserted here" clause of the `loom/runtime/match-error` Tests bullet in both `V4a-match-result.md` and `V4a-T-match-result.md` to name `V4a-T`'s assertion scope explicitly: it asserts only the raise-versus-bind exhaustion behaviour (a value matching none of the six pattern forms raises the panic; a value matching one binds and evaluates the selected arm), and the panic's `?`/`match` bypass and its registered `loom/runtime/match-error` message template are deferred to and closed by `V4b-T`. Keep the two files' bullets in agreement, since the impl leaf mirrors the test leaf's bullet.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the `?`/`match` bypass and message-template properties for `loom/runtime/match-error`, which are owned and correctly asserted by `V4b`/`V4b-T`; do not touch those leaves.
- The spec is read-only for this fix; do not edit `error-model.md` or the diagnostics registry.
