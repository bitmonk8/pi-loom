## Finding

The *Failure-surface contract* paragraph (`<a id="audit-failure-surface-contract">`) in `audit-failures.md` is the normative anchor for the entire inventory-closure audit: it establishes the **exhaustive five-family taxonomy** — (1) unauthorised `pi.<member>`, (2) unauthorised peer-package / typebox surface, (3) unauthorised `ctx.<member>`, (4) out-of-scope import/access shape, (5) stale or malformed exemption marker — together with the `audit/<class>/<family>/<symptom>` discriminator shape, the three-class partition, the per-`<symptom>` fixture floor, and the family→step-2(b) routing table. Every downstream discriminator obligation, fixture floor, and routing rule in the cluster cites this taxonomy by family number or by anchor.

Yet this paragraph is positioned **last** among the substantive sections of `audit-failures.md`. The four sections that precede it — *Stale sub-kinds*, *Substitution limitation*, *Stale-citation limitation*, and *Malformed-marker discriminator — family-(5) routing* — all reference the families before they are defined. *Stale sub-kinds* opens with "The audit MUST surface every stale marker under **family (5)** … (per the *Failure-surface contract* **below**)"; the malformed-marker routing section asserts "family (5)" and "family (1) / (2) / (3) discriminator" routing; both lean on the family numbering and the discriminator shape that only appear further down the file. A reader working the file (or the broader six-file cluster) top-to-bottom encounters the family identifiers as undefined forward references and must jump ahead to the closing paragraph to resolve them. The arrangement is inverted: the definition that all dependent rules cite is the terminal section in reading order rather than the preamble.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Reorder within `audit-failures.md`

- **Approach:** Move the *Failure-surface contract* paragraph (and its co-located *Three-class partition*, family→step-2(b) routing table, and *Per-family record-shape table*) to the top of `audit-failures.md`, ahead of *Stale sub-kinds*, so the five-family taxonomy and discriminator shape are established before any section references them. The `<a id="audit-failure-surface-contract">` anchor moves with the paragraph but stays in the same file.
- **Spec edits:** Relocate the closing paragraph block to the head of the file; adjust the now-stale "*Failure-surface contract* **below**" directionals in *Stale sub-kinds* / *Malformed-marker discriminator* to "**above**".
- **Pros:** Minimal, self-contained diff; the cross-page anchor target is unchanged so no other file needs editing; fixes the concrete within-file forward references.
- **Cons:** Does not change the cluster-level reading order — a reader traversing the six audit files in sequence may still meet family numbers in earlier files (`audit-resolution.md`, `audit-recognised-shapes.md`) before reaching `audit-failures.md`.
- **Risks:** Low. Only directional words ("above"/"below") within the same file need re-checking after the move.

### Option B — Promote the taxonomy to the cluster preamble

- **Approach:** Lift the five-family taxonomy and the `audit/<class>/<family>/<symptom>` discriminator shape into a preamble in `inventory-audit-intro.md` (the cluster's entry point), leaving the routing table, fixture floor, and per-family record-shape detail in `audit-failures.md` with a back-link.
- **Spec edits:** Author the taxonomy preamble in `inventory-audit-intro.md`; reduce the `audit-failures.md` opening to a reference; repoint `#audit-failure-surface-contract` cross-references in `audit-resolution.md`, `audit-wire-and-canary.md`, and any other citing file to the new anchor (or re-create a resolvable anchor at the relocation site).
- **Pros:** Establishes the taxonomy before *any* file in the cluster references it, fixing the broad reading-order defect across all six files.
- **Cons:** Larger, higher-churn diff; splits the taxonomy from its tightly-coupled routing table and record-shape table; requires repointing multiple cross-page anchors.
- **Risks:** Moderate — every `#audit-failure-surface-contract` inbound link must be re-verified; a missed link silently dangles.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A**. The concrete defect is the within-file forward reference in `audit-failures.md`, which a same-file reorder fixes completely with no anchor churn; the cross-page anchors that other cluster files use already resolve irrespective of position, so Option B's larger relocation buys cluster-reading-order tidiness at the cost of splitting the taxonomy from its co-dependent routing and record-shape tables. After the move, sweep the *Stale sub-kinds* and *Malformed-marker discriminator* sections for any remaining "below" directional that should now read "above".

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

No implementer is misled into a divergent implementation — the taxonomy is complete and unambiguous once read, and the cross-page anchors resolve regardless of position. The cost is readability and orientation: every reader of the cluster meets `family (N)` tokens and discriminator-shape references before the section that defines them, forcing a forward jump to the file's last paragraph to establish the vocabulary the dependent rules already assume.
