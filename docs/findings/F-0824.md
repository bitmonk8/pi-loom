## Finding

Checklist item `(ah)` (`pi.on` process-global delivery and no-per-session-origin-marker) opens with the SHOULD-level framing shared by every (f)–(ak) entry — "The contributor SHOULD confirm against the candidate `@earendil-works/pi-coding-agent` minor that …" — and then, after a paragraph arguing that each half is independently load-bearing, switches register to a bare imperative: "Falsification of either half independently invalidates a distinct PIC-18 argument, so **the contributor MUST audit both**." No other entry in the (f)–(ak) block uses an embedded MUST; every other item stays uniformly SHOULD-level (the only MUSTs in this section of the file are the recording-and-completeness MUSTs in the preamble, which scope the audit shape rather than escalating an individual item's audit obligation).

The bare MUST admits two incompatible readings. Under the **conditional** reading the MUST is governed by the enclosing SHOULD: if the auditor chooses to perform the (ah) audit at all, both halves (process-global delivery and no-per-session-origin-marker) must be covered — auditing just one half is non-conformant for a *performed* (ah) audit, but skipping (ah) entirely remains conformant (the auditor records `not-performed` per the preamble). Under the **absolute** reading the MUST overrides the SHOULD frame and elevates (ah) into the (a)–(e) MUST-audited tier, which would also reroute the merge-gate posture from "recorded fail/not-performed is non-blocking" (the V18c acceptance treatment of SHOULD-level items) to "fail blocks the bump" (the V18c acceptance treatment of MUST-level items). These two readings yield different conformance verdicts on the same auditor behaviour.

The intended reading is the conditional one — `(ah)` belongs to the SHOULD-tier block by structure and is treated as SHOULD-level by both the preamble's range-recording rule and the V18c acceptance criterion — but the surface wording does not say so.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Rewrite the embedded MUST sentence so the conditional reading is explicit and the SHOULD-tier classification is preserved. Replace

> "Falsification of either half independently invalidates a distinct PIC-18 argument, so the contributor MUST audit both."

with

> "Falsification of either half independently invalidates a distinct PIC-18 argument, so when the auditor performs the (ah) audit it MUST cover both halves; auditing only one half is non-conformant for a performed (ah) audit (skipping the audit entirely remains a conformant `not-performed` outcome per the preamble's SHOULD-level scope)."

The qualifier "when the auditor performs the (ah) audit" binds the MUST to the SHOULD-gated antecedent, the explicit "non-conformant for a *performed* (ah) audit" clause names what the MUST forbids, and the parenthetical references the preamble's `not-performed` slot so the SHOULD-tier classification is unambiguous. Edge cases the editor must watch: do not introduce a second MUST elsewhere in the (ah) body, do not paraphrase the preamble's `pass`/`fail`/`N/A`/`not-performed` vocabulary, and leave the escalation clause ("This obligation is SHOULD-level only at loom 1.0 …") unchanged so the tier classification still survives a future re-read.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable auditors would diverge: one reads the embedded MUST as bounded by the enclosing SHOULD and records `not-performed` as a conformant outcome for `(ah)`; the other reads the MUST as elevating `(ah)` to the (a)–(e) tier and treats `not-performed` as a blocking conformance failure. The V18c acceptance gate keys its block/non-block decision on that tier classification, so the divergence reaches the merge gate, not just the audit log.
