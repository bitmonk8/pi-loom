## Problem

The closing clause of the `id="audit-recognised-shapes"` enumeration in `audit-target-categories.md` reads "…are out of scope of inventory-resolution and MUST NOT be used…". On the same MUST-bearing clause that defines the family-(4) discriminator — the spec's only non-exemptible audit family — the "out of scope" idiom contradicts "MUST NOT be used": it reads as "the audit need not address these shapes" while also prohibiting them. The clause conflates two distinct properties: these shapes have no resolution target in `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` (true, and the actual reason for the prohibition) versus these shapes are out of scope of the audit (false — they are precisely in scope under family (4)). A first-reading implementer could conclude family-(4) shapes carry no audit-side detection obligation, contradicting `V18b`'s non-exemptible discriminator.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the closing clause of the `id="audit-recognised-shapes"` enumeration to surface the unresolvability-against-`SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` property as the reason for the prohibition, dropping the "out of scope" idiom. Preserve the clause's existing enforcement obligation (the audit MUST detect these shapes and route them under the family-(4) discriminator). The rewrite is the natural site to also assert family-(4) non-exemptibility (an `// allow-pi-surface:` marker MUST NOT silence a family-(4) record), currently asserted only in `V18b` and `audit-failures.md`.

## Solution constraints

- The rewritten clause MUST retain the entire enumeration as its grammatical scope — the MUST-detect obligation covers every shape above the clause, not only the three named immediately before the em-dash.
- Out of scope: renaming the `out-of-scope import/access` family discriminator (any coordinated rename spans `audit-failures.md`, `V18b-inventory-audit.md`, `V18b-T-inventory-audit.md`, and `coverage-matrix.md`).
