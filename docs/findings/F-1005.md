## Problem

The active REQ-ID prefix table at `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` registers the `CEIL` prefix to the `hard-ceilings/` subtree, but no `CEIL-N` REQ-ID is anchored anywhere in the corpus. Meanwhile several set-wide cross-ceiling obligations carry normative MUSTs with no REQ-ID anchor — the two ceiling-set invariants at `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md#ceiling-set-invariants`, the `masked: []` prohibition at `#masked-field`, and the *Routing obligation for future ceilings* and *Release gate* MUSTs in `hard-ceilings/ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` — leaving them standing GOV-22 defects. Consumers needing to cite these obligations fall back on the informative aggregator anchor `overview-and-orientation.md#hard-runtime-ceilings`, in tension with GOV-9's `#prefix-n` cross-link contract. The corpus thus holds a live prefix with zero live IDs adjacent to un-anchored MUSTs that prefix could host, leaving the GOV-5 / GOV-7 prefix-binding contract under-discharged.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `1c7f550` (2026-05-07, "pi-loom spec: resolve 'Hard ceilings block does load-bearing definitional work inside informative orientation'"); `c4ad105` (2026-05-08, "pi-loom spec: resolve 'HC3-a–HC3-e and NOCEIL-N labels escape GOV-3/GOV-8'")
**History:** Commit `1c7f550` created `spec_topics/hard-ceilings.md` and added a single `| hard-ceilings.md | CEIL |` row to the REQ-ID prefix table on the same commit, but coined no `CEIL-N` REQ-IDs at that introduction. The companion commit `c4ad105` one day later added a second row `| hard-ceilings.md | CIO |` plus the GOV-16 inline-label machinery for `HC3-a` … `HC3-e` and `NOCEIL-1` … `NOCEIL-4`, absorbing the identifier work the original `CEIL` registration could have hosted; `CEIL` was left in place with zero allocations. Subsequent edits (split into `hard-ceilings/` subtree per `f5e89f4`, and prefix-table rewrites per `1d773d6` retargeting the row to `hard-ceilings/`) preserved the empty-`CEIL` state without revisiting the original allocation intent. No later commit coins, retires, or otherwise discharges `CEIL`; the plan-side coverage-matrix rationale that documents the empty state was added by the plan-review pass `aa1b666` and again carries no forcing function. The defect is the *interaction* between the introduction commit's reservation and the follow-up commit's substitute identifier scheme — neither commit in isolation creates the empty-slot tension that the present state exhibits.

## Solution approach

Allocate `CEIL-N` REQ-IDs under the existing `CEIL` row to the un-anchored set-wide cross-ceiling MUSTs — the two invariants at `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md#ceiling-set-invariants`, the `masked: []` prohibition at `#masked-field`, and the *Routing obligation for future ceilings* and *Release gate* MUSTs in `hard-ceilings/ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` — coining each in GOV-1 dual-form at the body line that carries the MUST modal. Repoint the non-aggregator cross-links that currently target `overview-and-orientation.md#hard-runtime-ceilings` (in `errors-and-results/error-model.md` ERR-5 and its *Hard-ceiling exhaustion* row, `glossary.md`, `schema-subset.md`, and `governance/source-language-stability.md`) to the stable `#prefix-n` anchors they should cite. Update `plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md` to drop the "currently anchors no live REQ-ID" snapshot.
