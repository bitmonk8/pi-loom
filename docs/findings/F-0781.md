## Finding

`runtime-value-model.md` § *JavaScript engine assumptions* closes with a free-standing paragraph asserting a cross-cutting scope decision: *"loom 1.0 targets Node exclusively. Bun, Deno, browser embeds, and other JavaScript hosts are out of loom 1.0 scope; … Adding a non-Node host is a probe re-design and is out of loom 1.0 scope (see [Future Considerations](./future-considerations.md))."* That sentence reads as a free-standing normative scope statement made by the runtime-value-model page.

The canonical owner of this disposition already exists: `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md` § *loom 1.0 non-goals* item **"No non-Node JavaScript host support."** — whose *Recorded at:* trailer names `overview-and-orientation.md` (Host runtime) and `runtime-value-model.md` (JavaScript engine assumptions) as the two orientation/cross-reference sites. `overview-and-orientation.md` § *Prerequisites — Host runtime* already carries the orientation-grade restatement (*"loom 1.0 targets Node exclusively (Bun, Deno, browser-embed are out of loom 1.0 scope)."*), forward-linking the engine-assumptions section for engine-prerequisite purposes. The runtime-value-model paragraph therefore duplicates a scope decision the corpus already vests in Future Considerations, and does so in a section whose stated job is *engine-level non-checked invariants*, not scope declarations. A reader of the value-model page in isolation cannot tell that this paragraph is a back-reference rather than a primary normative claim, and a maintainer updating the non-goals item (e.g. on a future Bun/Deno re-scope) would have to remember to also rewrite a third site that does not advertise itself as part of the lock-step group.

The fix is to demote runtime-value-model's paragraph to a pure engine-prerequisite back-reference (one sentence: the engine-assumptions section presupposes the Node host floor; the canonical scope decision lives at Future Considerations § loom 1.0 non-goals), and leave the orientation-grade restatement in `overview-and-orientation.md` § Host runtime where it already sits.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `runtime-value-model.md` § *JavaScript engine assumptions*, replace the standalone Node-exclusivity paragraph (line 47) with a one-sentence engine-prerequisite cross-reference, e.g.:

> The Step 0 (a) probe is Node-specific (`process.versions.node`); the scope decision that loom 1.0 binds to Node exclusively is owned by [Future Considerations — loom 1.0 non-goals — No non-Node JavaScript host support](./future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md#loom-1-0-non-goals).

Leave the orientation-grade restatement in `overview-and-orientation.md` § Prerequisites — Host runtime unchanged; it is already a forward-link site. Do not touch the canonical non-goals item — it already names both orientation sites in its *Recorded at:* trailer, and the back-reference framing keeps it a valid `Recorded at:` target.

Edge cases for the implementer:

- The replacement sentence must preserve the engine-prerequisite framing — the surrounding paragraph (engine value model contingencies, undefined behaviour on violation) presupposes the Node host floor, so a reader needs the cross-reference without the cross-reference *being* the scope decision.
- Do not delete the paragraph outright: the *Recorded at:* trailer on the non-goals item explicitly names `runtime-value-model.md` as one of two recording sites; an empty section there would break the lock-step convention.
- The "and is out of loom 1.0 scope" clause currently appearing inside the parenthetical *"Adding a non-Node host is a probe re-design and is out of loom 1.0 scope"* must not survive in the demoted form; it is the normative-claim fragment whose presence is the actual defect.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Two co-existing normative-sounding statements of the same scope decision (one on the canonical Future-Considerations owner, one in the value-model page) silently drift the day either is reworded. The current text is not contradictory, so no implementer is misled today; the cost is maintainer-facing — the lock-step convention (GOV-30) is harder to honour when one of the lock-step sites is buried in an engine-assumptions section rather than declared as an aggregator.
