## Finding

The very first paragraph of `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/conversation-drive.md` (anchor `#no-extra-mediation`) is a cross-version security / access-boundary NFR scoped to *all loom 1.x releases* — it states that the runtime interposes no privilege layer, MUST NOT introduce additional access channels (sandbox, capability filter, mediated proxy) in any loom 1.x release, declares widening or narrowing a major-version concern, and pins the host-side denial-surface rule (silent success on denial forbidden). None of that is about driving the user's conversation: it is the trust/security disposition of the runtime, with major-version-stability consequences.

The page is titled "Conversation drive" and its TOC slot signals prompt-mode / typed-query driving mechanics; an NFR reader (operator, integrator, security reviewer) scanning `overview-and-orientation.md § Non-functional requirements` will not navigate into a conversation-drive page to find a cross-version `MUST NOT`. The orientation page does cover the same ground in its **Trust boundary** bullet, but that bullet treats the no-extra-mediation rule as *forward-linked* normative content owned elsewhere — and the place it forward-links to is precisely this misplaced opening paragraph. The NFR's authoritative home is the orientation NFR section itself (or a dedicated security-constraints section in the PIC index), not the first paragraph of a page about conversation driving.

The anchor `#no-extra-mediation` is load-bearing: `overview-and-orientation.md`, `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md`, `tool-calls.md`, and both `V14d` plan leaves cite it by anchor. Any relocation must carry the anchor with it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Relocate the **No additional access channels** paragraph and its `<a id="no-extra-mediation"></a>` anchor out of `pi-integration-contract/conversation-drive.md` and into `overview-and-orientation.md § Non-functional requirements`, attached to the existing **Trust boundary** bullet — either as a normative sub-paragraph of that bullet or as a sibling bullet titled the same as the relocated section. The orientation NFR section is already the section the relocated paragraph's only inbound *concept* citations (the `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md` *Recorded at* line, and the Trust boundary bullet's own forward link) intend; collocating eliminates the round trip.

Implementer-relevant edge cases:

- The anchor `#no-extra-mediation` is referenced from five files (`overview-and-orientation.md`, `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md`, `tool-calls.md`, `plan_topics/V14d-tool-calls-host-denial.md`, `plan_topics/V14d-T-tool-calls-host-denial.md`). Move the anchor with the content — do not rename it — and repoint every cross-reference to `overview-and-orientation.md#no-extra-mediation`. `docs/_tools/check_links.py` covers anchor validity post-move.
- The orientation **Trust boundary** bullet already paraphrases part of the relocated text ("The runtime itself interposes no additional access channels (sandbox, capability filter, mediated proxy) per …"). Consolidate so the normative MUST NOT lives in exactly one place; demote the paraphrase to a short pointer or fold it inline. Do not leave both a normative copy and a paraphrase.
- The denial-surface rule (thrown / `isError: true` → `Err(QueryError { kind: "code_tool", cause: "execution" })`; silent success forbidden) is the V14d test's spec pin. The relocated paragraph must keep that clause verbatim, or `V14d` / `V14d-T` lose their normative anchor.
- Leave a short stub in `conversation-drive.md` only if the page genuinely needs to forward-reference the trust posture for the tool-execution surface it describes; otherwise drop without replacement (the existing orientation bullet is reachable from every PIC entry point).
- Per GOV-30 (aggregator-vs-source lock-step), once the canonical paragraph moves into the orientation aggregator, mark or treat the Trust-boundary bullet as the source of truth so subsequent edits do not drift between the two locations.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The NFR is normatively reachable today (the orientation Trust-boundary bullet forward-links into it), so no implementer is denied the rule — but the cross-version MUST NOT is filed under the wrong topic. A reader auditing loom 1.x's security posture, or an editor enforcing GOV-30's aggregator-vs-source lock-step convention, lands in a page titled "Conversation drive" to find the canonical statement; an editor pruning the PIC for shard placement could plausibly demote, reword, or lose the MUST NOT on the assumption it is conversation-drive scaffolding rather than the corpus's authoritative no-extra-mediation rule.
