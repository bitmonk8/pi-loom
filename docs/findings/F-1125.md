## Finding

The closing paragraph of `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-wire-and-canary.md` ("Item 5 (cancellation propagation) is verified jointly by Step 0 item (b)'s `AbortSignal` / `AbortController` shape probe … Item 7 (binder LLM model) is verified at per-loom load time … name-links each item back to the obligation here so that downstream tests cite a single anchored source.") is orientation for the **SDK capability inventory**: it routes items 5 and 7 to their non-Step-0 verification mechanisms and asserts a "single anchored source" claim. The rest of the file is the audit's wire-serialisation contract, infrastructure-failure handling, and non-empty-scan canary — none of which has any subject overlap with item-5/7 capability verification. The paragraph landed here only because the file-split commit sliced the monolithic `pi-integration-contract.md` and the trailing sentences of the original `## SDK capability inventory` lead paragraph fell into the wrong child page.

The dangling pronoun makes the placement defect provably wrong, not merely awkward. The paragraph's tail claims `spec.md` "name-links each item back to the obligation **here** so that downstream tests cite a single anchored source", but the obligations for items 5 and 7 do not live on this page: item 5's normative probe rules live in `capability-probe.md` Step 0(b) (the `AbortSignal` / `AbortController` member-with-kind table) and Step 0(c) (the `AgentSession.prototype.abort` factory-probable member), with the inventory-side framing in `capability-inventory-items.md` item 5; item 7's normative rule lives in `binder/binder-model-and-context.md` (Strict-capability requirement), with the inventory-side framing in `capability-inventory-items.md` item 7. A test author following the `audit-wire-and-canary.md` claim lands on a page about wire serialisation rather than on the rules they need.

The same routing claim is already half-stated at the head of `## SDK capability inventory` in `inventory-audit-intro.md` (the PIC-15 paragraph: *"Items 1, 2, 3, 4, and 6 are factory-probable and verified by Step 0 (Capability probe) above …"*) and is restated locally at the relevant points in `capability-probe.md` Step 0(c) and `capability-inventory-items.md` item 5. The orphaned paragraph is therefore both misplaced and the second-best home for content the inventory page is already the obvious owner of.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move the orphaned paragraph (the two sentences beginning *"Item 5 (cancellation propagation) is verified jointly by Step 0 item (b)…"* and ending *"…name-links each item back to the obligation here so that downstream tests cite a single anchored source."*) out of `audit-wire-and-canary.md` and merge its content into the **lead PIC-15 paragraph of `## SDK capability inventory` in `inventory-audit-intro.md`**. That paragraph already carries the items-1/2/3/4/6 half of the routing ("Items 1, 2, 3, 4, and 6 are factory-probable and verified by Step 0 (Capability probe) above …"); append the items-5 and items-7 sentences to it so the seven-item routing sits in one place.

Rewrite the tail to remove the false "here" anchor. Replace *"name-links each item back to the obligation here so that downstream tests cite a single anchored source"* with *"name-links each item back to its anchored obligation on this page (the seven inventory items below) or to the forward-linked owner page named above (`capability-probe.md` for items 5 and 7's Step-0 surface; `binder/binder-model-and-context.md` for item 7's per-loom-load-time surface), so that downstream tests cite a single anchored source per item."*

Implementer edge cases:
- The relocated text already contains a working forward-link to `binder/binder-model-and-context.md#strict-capability-requirement`; preserve it byte-exact when moving (the binder forward-link is the load-bearing pointer for item 7).
- The disjointness clause ("the two diagnostic surfaces (`loom/load/host-incompatible` and `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved`) are disjoint") is also asserted at the end of `capability-probe.md` Step 0(c) as "MUST stay disjoint". Keep the inventory-page restatement as informative orientation; do not promote it to a second MUST.
- After the move, `audit-wire-and-canary.md` should end at the end of its preceding paragraph (the `npm test` wiring contract). No replacement text or pointer-back is owed — the inventory page is the canonical owner and the cross-link from `spec.md` already lands readers there.
- The `[`spec.md` — Orientation — Prerequisites]` link uses the relative path `../overview-and-orientation.md#orientation` from the wire/canary page; the same link from `inventory-audit-intro.md` resolves to the same target (both files share the directory), so no path rewrite is needed beyond moving the link.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The orphaned paragraph misleads a test author or reviewer following the cross-reference: the page they land on does not own the obligations the text claims live "here", so they must hunt for the items-5/7 normative rules across `capability-probe.md` and `binder/binder-model-and-context.md` without an in-text pointer. Nothing fails to build, but the spec's own anchored-source-of-truth invariant is observably false at this site.
