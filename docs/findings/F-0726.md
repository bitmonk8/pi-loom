## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/language-and-architecture.md` opens — after the H1 and a single HTML comment recording that the NFR dispositions were moved to `overview-and-orientation.md` — with `### V1 non-goals`, an orientation aggregator that enumerates the eight cross-cutting loom 1.0 scope decisions that ship without a forward-compatibility seam. Everything else on the page (Reading order, Language index, Extension Architecture index) sits *below* that block, so the file's first substantive section is an aggregator about scope rather than language or architecture content.

The non-goals are scope-boundary material by their own framing ("cross-cutting loom 1.0 scope decisions where loom 1.0 ships without a forward-compatibility seam"). The sibling Scope aggregators — Forward-compatibility seams and Hard ceilings — already live under `overview-and-orientation.md` § Scope, and the NFR section of that same file even forward-links *into* `language-and-architecture.md#loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator` to reach this block. A reader following the orientation flow therefore has to leave the orientation page to read a list that belongs next to the other scope dispositions, and the architecture page carries content that has no relationship to language or extension architecture.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `0e12140` (2026-05-08, *pi-loom spec: resolve "Non-goals are not consolidated into a single section"*) → `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04, *docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set*) → `510f74f` (2026-06-05, *pi-loom spec: resolve "NFR aggregator delivers one of three announced bullets" (T13/T14)*) → `81bf52a` (2026-06-11, *docs: collapse spec/ section pages into spec_topics/*)
**History:** `git log -S "### V1 non-goals" -- docs/spec.md docs/spec/language-and-architecture.md docs/spec_topics/language-and-architecture.md` returns the four commits above in order. The block was first authored by `0e12140` directly inside the monolithic `docs/spec.md`, immediately *after* the Scope subsection's three bullets (Trust boundary, Source-language stability, Runtime observability) and the Hard-ceilings bullet — i.e. it landed in the right orientation neighbourhood. `f5e89f4` then split `spec.md` for the 100KB/30KB size cap and carried the block, together with the Source-language-stability and Runtime-observability bullets, into a new `docs/spec/language-and-architecture.md` file; the size-cap split was mechanical and did not consider the topical fit of the carved-out aggregator. `510f74f` subsequently relocated the two NFR bullets to `overview-and-orientation.md`'s NFR section to repair an unrelated finding ("NFR aggregator delivers one of three announced bullets"), leaving only the non-goals aggregator and an HTML comment behind on the architecture page. `81bf52a` then collapsed `docs/spec/` into `docs/spec_topics/` without revisiting placement. The defect is the residue of `f5e89f4`'s mechanical split plus `510f74f`'s repair: neither commit on its own placed scope content in the wrong file, but their interaction stranded a scope aggregator as the opening content of the architecture page.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move the `### V1 non-goals` block (the aggregator paragraph + the trailing seam-vs-non-goal paragraph + both `<a id>` anchors) out of `language-and-architecture.md` and into `overview-and-orientation.md` § Scope as a third Scope bullet/sub-section sibling to **Forward-compatibility seams** and **Hard ceilings**.

Implementer-relevant edge cases:

- Preserve both legacy anchors (`loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator` and `v1-non-goals`) verbatim at the new location so existing inbound references keep resolving — at minimum from `overview-and-orientation.md` § NFR (which already forward-links `./language-and-architecture.md#loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator`) and from `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md` (which cites `../language-and-architecture.md#v1-non-goals`). Both citations become same-page (overview-and-orientation) or sibling (future-considerations) and must be repointed to `./overview-and-orientation.md#…` / `../overview-and-orientation.md#…` respectively in the same commit.
- Run `docs/_tools/check_links.py` after the move to confirm no other in-corpus references to these anchors were missed (the canonical Scope-bullet repointing is mechanical, but the corpus also carries narrative cross-links that grep alone may not surface).
- Once the block has moved, the residual HTML comment at the top of `language-and-architecture.md` (`<!-- The cross-cutting NFR dispositions … are recorded in the Non-functional requirements section of overview-and-orientation.md#non-functional-requirements. -->`) is the only opening content; either leave it as a placement note or delete it — both are within the page-editorial conventions and neither affects the fix.
- The move is a same-section GOV-30 aggregator relocation: the source-of-truth list at `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md#loom-1-0-non-goals` is untouched and the lock-step convention is preserved.
