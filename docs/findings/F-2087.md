## Problem

In `binder-bypass-and-envelope.md`'s `## Binder envelope` subsection, BNDR-1, BNDR-2, and BNDR-3 are written as standalone `MUST`/`MUST NOT` obligations that sit as peers to every other loom 1.0 requirement, yet their sole purpose is to preserve a forward-compatibility seam for the deferred binder refinement loop (anchored at `#v1-seam-binder-refinement-loop`) that loom 1.0 does not surface. The only marker of their seam-only character is the preceding non-normative seam blockquote; the three REQ-IDs themselves carry no annotation distinguishing "must build now for loom 1.0 behaviour" from "must not foreclose for a later phase." An implementer scanning the binder contract cannot tell from the REQ-IDs that the three-arm enum, the mandatory `candidates` field, and the distinct template prefixes add schema complexity purely to keep a future phase migratable. The risk runs both ways: over-building seam machinery as live behaviour, or treating the MUSTs as droppable hints and collapsing the arms — silently breaking the deferred-loop migration the seam protects.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 8c5f867f33023bc27eb47dbafedaf583cd7bfd67
**History:** The spec corpus is git-tracked. The three seam-preservation obligations originally lived *inside* the `loom 1.0 seam — binder refinement loop` blockquote as a numbered list, framed "Three V1 carriers preserve the post-V1 migration and MUST be retained — none of them MAY be 'simplified' in V1 on the grounds that V1 does not surface the distinction." Commit `8c5f867` ("Binder refinement-loop seam bundles three independent MUSTs", 2026-05-08) lifted them out of the blockquote into three standalone peer obligations (`BNDR-<i>/<j>/<k>`, later relabelled `BNDR-1/2/3` in `1161fa0`), removing the visual grouping that had signalled their seam-only nature — which is the defect this finding identifies. `git log -S`/`git show` on the file confirm the obligations carried the seam framing before this commit and lost it after; subsequent commits (relabelling, the `binder.md` → `binder/binder-bypass-and-envelope.md` topic split in `f5e89f4`) preserved the un-grouped peer form without restoring the distinction.

## Solution approach

Clarify BNDR-1/BNDR-2/BNDR-3 at their REQ-ID site in `binder-bypass-and-envelope.md`'s `## Binder envelope` subsection so their seam-preservation (forward-compatibility) character is legible without reading the seam blockquote, keeping their `MUST` weight intact. Add a forward-link from that site to `future-considerations/surface-extensions.md#binder-refinement-loop`, making the round-trip with the existing back-reference there explicit.

## Solution constraints

- The change is presentational: it MUST NOT alter the normative content of BNDR-1/2/3, including BNDR-2's split obligation (keep `ambiguous.candidates` in the schema; `MUST NOT` surface it on the user-facing system note).
