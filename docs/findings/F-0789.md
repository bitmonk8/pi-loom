## Finding

SM-8 in `session-model-and-appendix.md` names the concept once as "the binder retry budget", then immediately re-names it as "per-invocation retry budget" in the link text and the anchor target it forward-links to:

> "this non-sharing rule is owned, per budget, by its topic page: the binder retry budget by [Slash-Command Argument Binding — per-invocation retry budget](./binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md#per-invocation-retry-budget)…"

The owning paragraph in `determinism-cancellation-failure.md` exhibits the same split internally: it carries the anchor `<a id="per-invocation-retry-budget">` and opens with "Each retry-eligible failure class has a single retry budget **per slash invocation**…", but later in the same paragraph re-introduces the concept as "This binder retry budget is scoped to a single slash invocation…". Downstream callsites have inherited the drift — `binder-inference.md` cites "the [per-invocation retry budget]" with link text matching the anchor, while `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md`, `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`, and `version-bump-step2.md` cite the concept inconsistently as "binder retry budget", "binder's per-invocation retry budget", and "binder's [per-invocation retry budget]" respectively. The plan adds a third spelling on the leaf side — `V11f-binder-retry-taxonomy.md` titles the work item "Binder per-class retry budget and failure taxonomy".

There is no glossary entry on `glossary.md` to canonicalise either spelling, and no equivalence note that pins the two terms to the same referent.

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** `7d7bfe2` (2026-06-01, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"SM-7c/SM-8 authoritative-in-place relocation (T08/T10/T11)\"")
**History:** `git log -G'id="per-invocation-retry-budget"'` and `git log -G'binder retry budget' -- docs/spec.md docs/spec_topics/` show that before `7d7bfe2` SM-8 cited only "binder retry budget" with a plain forward-link to `binder.md` and the owning paragraph carried no inline anchor. Commit `7d7bfe2` did three things in a single edit: added the anchor `<a id="per-invocation-retry-budget">` to the owning paragraph; added the sentence "This binder retry budget is scoped to a single slash invocation; it is not shared, pooled, or replenished across sibling invocations…" to the same paragraph (importing the "binder retry budget" wording from SM-8); and rewrote SM-8 to forward-link with link text "per-invocation retry budget" against the new anchor while leaving the lead-in "the binder retry budget" intact. The split-naming is therefore baked in at the moment the anchor was minted. The subsequent file-split commit `f5e89f4` moved the paragraph from `binder.md` to `binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md` and the inter-attempt-timing edit `d0bcdb2` extended the same paragraph, but neither touched the naming.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Adopt **"binder retry budget"** as the canonical term across the corpus and add a glossary entry that pins the referent.

Concrete edits:

1. Add a `glossary.md` entry:
   > **binder retry budget** — The per-slash-invocation retry budget governing the binder's transport-class and malformed-envelope-class retries: at most one of each, regardless of interleaving, capping the runtime at three binder LLM calls per slash invocation. Scoped to a single slash invocation and not shared, pooled, or replenished across siblings. See: [Slash-Command Argument Binding — Binder retry budget](./binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md#binder-retry-budget).
2. In `binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md`, rename the anchor `<a id="per-invocation-retry-budget">` to `<a id="binder-retry-budget">` and add the previous anchor as a stability alias on the same paragraph (`<a id="per-invocation-retry-budget"></a><a id="binder-retry-budget"></a>…`) so existing inbound links keep resolving. Replace the paragraph's lead "Each retry-eligible failure class has a single retry budget **per slash invocation**…" with "The **binder retry budget** is a per-slash-invocation budget…" so the canonical noun phrase appears at the anchor site.
3. In `session-model-and-appendix.md` SM-8, change the link text from "per-invocation retry budget" to "binder retry budget" and repoint the URL to `#binder-retry-budget`.
4. Repoint each downstream `#per-invocation-retry-budget` citation in `binder-inference.md` (two sites), `conversation-drive.md`, `version-bump-step2.md`, `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md`, and `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` to `#binder-retry-budget`, and harmonise the surrounding link text to "binder retry budget" (preserving any qualifiers like "the binder's …" where they read naturally).
5. Rename the plan leaves `V11f` and `V11f-T` from "Binder per-class retry budget and failure taxonomy" to "Binder retry budget and failure-class taxonomy" (the "per-class" framing belongs in the body — "one budget per failure class" — not in the leaf title, where it competes with the spec's canonical term). Update every internal cross-reference accordingly.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The two-anchor alias on the owning paragraph is the only mechanism preserving the existing inbound `#per-invocation-retry-budget` URLs; do not drop the alias without doing a corpus-wide repoint first.
- `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` enumerates the session-model forward-link inventory under GOV-30's lock-step convention; the rename of SM-8's link text must be mirrored in that inventory in the same commit.
- The phrase "per-invocation" is still load-bearing elsewhere (`SM-8 — Per-invocation budget non-sharing`, "per-invocation budgets") because it describes the *scope* of three budgets, not the *name* of the binder one. Keep "per-invocation" in those structural framings; only the binder-budget's proper noun changes.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Two terms with no equivalence note force a reader who lands on SM-8's link text to silently identify "per-invocation retry budget" with the surrounding paragraph's "binder retry budget", and force a reader of `determinism-cancellation-failure.md` to do the same within a single paragraph. Implementation is not threatened — the link still resolves and the budget is described in normative detail at the owner — but every downstream citation has to make a per-callsite naming choice (the corpus currently shows at least four variants), and any future grep-based audit of the concept misses sites under whichever spelling it doesn't search for.
