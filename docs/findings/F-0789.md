## Problem

The binder retry-budget concept carries two competing names. SM-8 in `session-model-and-appendix.md` calls it "the binder retry budget" and then immediately re-names it "per-invocation retry budget" in the same sentence's forward-link text and anchor target. The owning paragraph in `binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md` (anchor `id="per-invocation-retry-budget"`) exhibits the same split internally, and downstream callsites in `binder-inference.md`, `conversation-drive.md`, `version-bump-step2.md`, `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md`, and `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` have inherited at least four variant spellings. No `glossary.md` entry pins the two terms to a single referent.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** `7d7bfe2` (2026-06-01, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"SM-7c/SM-8 authoritative-in-place relocation (T08/T10/T11)\"")
**History:** `git log -G'id="per-invocation-retry-budget"'` and `git log -G'binder retry budget' -- docs/spec.md docs/spec_topics/` show that before `7d7bfe2` SM-8 cited only "binder retry budget" with a plain forward-link to `binder.md` and the owning paragraph carried no inline anchor. Commit `7d7bfe2` did three things in a single edit: added the anchor `<a id="per-invocation-retry-budget">` to the owning paragraph; added the sentence "This binder retry budget is scoped to a single slash invocation; it is not shared, pooled, or replenished across sibling invocations…" to the same paragraph (importing the "binder retry budget" wording from SM-8); and rewrote SM-8 to forward-link with link text "per-invocation retry budget" against the new anchor while leaving the lead-in "the binder retry budget" intact. The split-naming is therefore baked in at the moment the anchor was minted. The subsequent file-split commit `f5e89f4` moved the paragraph from `binder.md` to `binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md` and the inter-attempt-timing edit `d0bcdb2` extended the same paragraph, but neither touched the naming.

## Solution approach

Settle a single canonical term for the concept — the recommendation adopts `binder retry budget` — and pin it with a new `glossary.md` entry. Rename the `id="per-invocation-retry-budget"` anchor in `binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md` to match the canonical term, retaining the old anchor as a stability alias on the same paragraph so existing inbound links keep resolving, and bring the canonical noun phrase to the anchor site. Repoint and harmonise SM-8's link text and URL and the downstream citations to the canonical term and renamed anchor.

## Solution constraints

- Do not drop the `per-invocation-retry-budget` anchor without a corpus-wide repoint first — the alias on the owning paragraph is the only mechanism keeping existing inbound `#per-invocation-retry-budget` URLs resolving.
- Mirror the SM-8 link-text rename in GOV-30's session-model forward-link inventory in `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` in the same commit (GOV-30 lock-step convention).
- Out of scope: the "per-invocation" wording in structural framings (SM-8's heading `id="sm-8-per-invocation-budget-non-sharing"`, "per-invocation budgets") — it names the *scope* shared by three budgets, not the binder budget's proper noun, and must not be renamed.
