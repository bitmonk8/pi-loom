## Problem

The *Exemption mechanism* paragraph (`#audit-exemption-mechanism`) frames the `// allow-pi-surface:` citation rule with temporal hedges — "before REQ-ID anchors land" and "the marker MUST cite either a REQ-ID (once those anchors land) or, in the interim, a PIC anchor" — but no in-spec event ever constitutes REQ-IDs "landing": there is no milestone, commit-time predicate, or observer a contributor or audit can read to decide whether the condition is met. The *Citation-token grammar* sub-paragraph already admits both `REQ-<digits>` and `PIC#<kebab-case-anchor>` forms unconditionally and purely lexically, so the temporal framing is meaningless. A contributor writing a new marker cannot tell whether to cite the interim PIC form or the post-landing REQ-ID form, even though either form satisfies the audit identically.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `e5cd514` (2026-05-09) seeded the temporal scaffold; `9e53613` (2026-05-26) broke its anchor.
**History:**

- `e5cd514` *"pi-loom spec: resolve 'T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit'"* introduced the *Exemption mechanism* paragraph wholesale into the then-monolithic `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md`, with the temporal hedge phrased explicitly as `"pre-H6 there are no REQ-IDs to cite for PIC-owned surface"` and `"the marker MUST cite either a REQ-ID (once H6 lands those anchors)"`. As written, the hedge was anchored to a concrete plan-side milestone (the `H6` horizontal-phase leaf, then `H6a-live-corpus-activation.md`).
- `9e53613` *"pi-loom spec: resolve T03/T24a/T25/T26 corpus-direction sweep — drop H1 / plan_topics / plan-leaf-ID references from spec corpus"* rewrote the paragraph to comply with [GOV-18](../../docs/spec_topics/governance/corpus-direction-and-scope.md) (the spec corpus MUST NOT bind third parties, including plan-leaf identifiers). The literal `"pre-H6"` became `"before REQ-ID anchors land"` and `"once H6 lands those anchors"` became `"once those anchors land"`. The sweep mechanically removed the plan-leaf identifier but did not substitute an in-spec event that the now-anchorless temporal hedge could refer to, because no such in-spec event exists.
- `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04) extracted the paragraph from the monolithic `pi-integration-contract.md` into its current home `pi-integration-contract/audit-resolution.md` verbatim, carrying the orphaned hedge forward unchanged.

The defect is a residue of a deliberate-and-correct GOV-18 cleanup that did not have a same-edit replacement to land at the cut site. Neither commit on its own is wrong (the original was GOV-18-non-compliant; the sweep was GOV-18-compliant cleanup); the defect is the *interaction* — the cleanup removed the only anchor the hedge had, but the hedge stayed.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the *Exemption mechanism* paragraph at `#audit-exemption-mechanism` to state the citation rule statically, removing the temporal framing: `REQ-<digits>` and `PIC#<kebab-case-anchor>` citations are peers, either suffices, on equal footing at every commit. Add a forward-reference to the *Citation-token grammar* sub-paragraph as the authoritative shape rule for both forms so no hidden precedence can be inferred from the prose.

## Solution constraints

- The static phrasing MUST NOT re-introduce a coupling to plan-side milestones or coinage cadence (GOV-18); GOV-22 may be cited only as informative orientation, not as a temporal trigger.
