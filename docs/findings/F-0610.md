## Finding

The *Exemption mechanism* paragraph at `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-resolution.md` (`#audit-exemption-mechanism`) states two coupled claims about the `// allow-pi-surface:` citation token: first, that "PIC owns the inventory surface this audit guards and **before REQ-ID anchors land** there are no REQ-IDs to cite for PIC-owned surface"; and second, that "the marker MUST cite either a REQ-ID (**once those anchors land**) or, in the interim, a PIC anchor". Neither phrase identifies the event that constitutes REQ-IDs "landing" — there is no in-spec milestone, no commit-time predicate, and no observer a contributor or audit can read to decide whether the condition is currently met.

Three independent spec rules already determine the static state today and make the temporal framing meaningless. (1) The page's *Citation-token grammar* sub-paragraph (`#audit-resolution.md` body) admits **both** shapes unconditionally and purely lexically: `(i)` `REQ-<digits>` or `(ii)` `PIC#<kebab-case-anchor>`. The audit performs no registry lookup, so a marker citing either form parses as well-formed regardless of any "landing" event. (2) The `PIC` prefix is already registered in [`req-id-prefix-table-active-a.md`](../../docs/spec_topics/governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-a.md) and [GOV-22](../../docs/spec_topics/governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md) (*progressive coinage of un-anchored obligations*) already pins the per-commit mechanism by which PIC obligations acquire REQ-ID anchors — no separate "anchors land" event exists for the exemption mechanism to depend on. (3) [GOV-18](../../docs/spec_topics/governance/corpus-direction-and-scope.md) prohibits the spec corpus from binding plan-side milestones, which is why an earlier draft's literal *"once H6 lands those anchors"* was rewritten — but the rewrite preserved the temporal scaffold without substituting an equivalent in-spec anchor, leaving a dangling promise.

The defect is contributor-direction prose drift, not an audit-mechanism gap: a contributor writing a new marker today reads the *Exemption mechanism* prose and cannot tell whether to cite a `PIC#…` anchor (the "interim" form) or a `REQ-<digits>` (the post-landing form), even though either form satisfies the audit identically. The spec promises an obligation that no event ever triggers.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `e5cd514` (2026-05-09) seeded the temporal scaffold; `9e53613` (2026-05-26) broke its anchor.
**History:**

- `e5cd514` *"pi-loom spec: resolve 'T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit'"* introduced the *Exemption mechanism* paragraph wholesale into the then-monolithic `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md`, with the temporal hedge phrased explicitly as `"pre-H6 there are no REQ-IDs to cite for PIC-owned surface"` and `"the marker MUST cite either a REQ-ID (once H6 lands those anchors)"`. As written, the hedge was anchored to a concrete plan-side milestone (the `H6` horizontal-phase leaf, then `H6a-live-corpus-activation.md`).
- `9e53613` *"pi-loom spec: resolve T03/T24a/T25/T26 corpus-direction sweep — drop H1 / plan_topics / plan-leaf-ID references from spec corpus"* rewrote the paragraph to comply with [GOV-18](../../docs/spec_topics/governance/corpus-direction-and-scope.md) (the spec corpus MUST NOT bind third parties, including plan-leaf identifiers). The literal `"pre-H6"` became `"before REQ-ID anchors land"` and `"once H6 lands those anchors"` became `"once those anchors land"`. The sweep mechanically removed the plan-leaf identifier but did not substitute an in-spec event that the now-anchorless temporal hedge could refer to, because no such in-spec event exists.
- `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04) extracted the paragraph from the monolithic `pi-integration-contract.md` into its current home `pi-integration-contract/audit-resolution.md` verbatim, carrying the orphaned hedge forward unchanged.

The defect is a residue of a deliberate-and-correct GOV-18 cleanup that did not have a same-edit replacement to land at the cut site. Neither commit on its own is wrong (the original was GOV-18-non-compliant; the sweep was GOV-18-compliant cleanup); the defect is the *interaction* — the cleanup removed the only anchor the hedge had, but the hedge stayed.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Remove the temporal framing entirely and state the citation rule statically: `// allow-pi-surface:` markers MUST cite either a `REQ-<digits>` REQ-ID **or** a `PIC#<kebab-case-anchor>` PIC anchor, with both forms permitted on equal footing at every commit on `main`. The two forms are interchangeable from the audit's perspective; per the *Citation-token grammar* sub-paragraph the audit already treats them purely lexically and performs no registry resolution, so the citation choice is contributor judgement.

Concretely, in the *Exemption mechanism* paragraph at `#audit-exemption-mechanism`:

- Replace `"before REQ-ID anchors land there are no REQ-IDs to cite for PIC-owned surface"` with a static justification of the dual-form admission: e.g. `"PIC owns the inventory surface this audit guards, and the citation slot admits a PIC anchor as a peer of a REQ-ID (rather than as a deprecated transitional form) because PIC obligations are coined as REQ-IDs progressively under GOV-22 — a PIC anchor remains a valid first-class citation form whether or not the cited obligation has yet been coined as a REQ-ID"`.
- Replace `"The marker MUST cite either a REQ-ID (once those anchors land) or, in the interim, a PIC anchor explaining why this specific surface is intentionally outside the inventory"` with `"The marker MUST cite either a REQ-ID or a PIC anchor (the two forms are peers; either suffices), and the cited anchor MUST identify the spec section that authorises this specific surface as intentionally outside the inventory"`.

Add a single forward-reference clause noting that the *Citation-token grammar* sub-paragraph is the authoritative shape rule for both forms, so a future reader cannot infer a hidden precedence from the prose. Do not add a "REQ-IDs are preferred when allocated" SHOULD: it re-introduces the same contributor-confusion surface the static rule is meant to close, and the audit cannot enforce it.

Implementer-relevant edge cases: (1) The companion *Stale-citation limitation* sub-bullet under [`audit-failures.md`](../../docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-failures.md) cites the *Citation-token grammar* and refers to both `REQ-<digits>` and `PIC#…` shapes symmetrically — it requires no edit, but the editor MUST re-read it on the same diff to confirm no contradiction has been introduced. (2) The version-bump-step2b *exempt* branch (`#bump-step-2b-exempt`) restates the citation grammar inline as `<REQ-ID-or-PIC-anchor>` and inherits the static rule by reference — no edit required there either, but again the editor MUST re-read it. (3) The static phrasing must not anchor itself to GOV-22 in a way that re-introduces a coupling to plan-side coinage cadence; GOV-22's role here is informative orientation only.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The audit mechanically accepts both citation forms today, so a contributor who picks either form passes audit regardless of how the prose is read. The defect surfaces only as contributor-direction confusion (which form is *required* for a new marker? which form should a reviewer push back on?) and as a small reviewer-disagreement surface on bump-commit diffs. No implementer is blocked and no audit can diverge between two reasonable readings.
