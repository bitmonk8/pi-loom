## Finding

Item (a) in the **Editorial-review checklist for unpinned host presuppositions** has fundamentally different semantics from every other entry in the same list, yet is rendered as a peer of those entries. Items (b)‚Äì(ao) are *recurring per-bump audits* whose conformant outcome on every bump is normally `pass` / `fail` / `N/A` / `not-performed`; item (a) is a *one-shot, dispositive open question* that "MUST be resolved before SM-4/5/6 are implemented" and that ‚Äî per the **Item (a) authoritative-determination recording shape** convention at the bottom of the page ‚Äî does not even take the per-item recording shape the preamble describes (it takes one of two verdicts: `governed-by-rebind` or `provably-differs ‚Äî <cited evidence>`). The blocking nature is therefore knowable only by reading two widely-separated paragraphs of the page back-to-back; an implementer scanning the checklist sees row (a) sandwiched between (b) and (c) and treats it as "one of the audit items".

Three concrete drift symptoms follow. First, the ordering MUST ("MUST be resolved before SM-4/5/6 are implemented") has no mechanical enforcement: nothing in `V18c`'s build-time gates or `V9l`'s acceptance criteria fails red if the SM-4/5/6 implementation lands without an item-(a) verdict in the bump commit message ‚Äî the gate is "editorial review" on a row in a 41-item list and the per-item recording for the rest of that list explicitly tolerates `not-performed`, so a missing item-(a) verdict is indistinguishable on the surface from a deliberately skipped SHOULD-level row. Second, "SM-4/5/6" at its first occurrence in the item-(a) body is plain text ‚Äî the cross-link to `session-model-and-appendix.md#sm-4-session-only-degraded-state` exists, but only in the **Outcome recording conventions** paragraph ~100 lines further down. Third, the page-level title ("Version bump step2") and the preamble describe a per-bump audit procedure; nothing in either page-level surface signals that a present-tense blocker also lives here.

The peer finding "host-prerequisites.md ‚Äî clause (a) (root of the contradiction)" frames the same defect from the canonical location of the open contradiction; this finding is its `version-bump-step2.md` reflection. The placement defect on `version-bump-step2.md` is partially independent ‚Äî even if `host-prerequisites.md` clause (a) is left exactly as-is, the row on `version-bump-step2.md` is still mis-shaped for its job and the ordering MUST is still mechanically un-enforced.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A ‚Äî Extract item (a) to a dedicated blocking-issues section on `version-bump-step2.md`; leave the per-bump checklist as a homogeneous list of recurring audits

**Approach.** Remove item (a) from the `(a)`‚Äì`(ao)` enumeration. Add a sibling top-level section to `version-bump-step2.md` (e.g. **Pre-implementation blockers** or **Implementation-gating open questions**) that owns clause-(a)'s text, the `governed-by-rebind` / `provably-differs` verdicts, the evidentiary standard, and the SM-4/5/6 cross-link at first occurrence. Re-letter the remaining items `(a)`‚Äì`(an)` (now starting with what was (b)) and reconcile `host-prerequisites.md` clause (a)'s "tracked on the version-bump editorial-review checklist at item (a)" reference to point at the new section instead.

**Spec edits.**
- `version-bump-step2.md` ‚Äî delete the item-(a) row; add a new top-level **Pre-implementation blockers** section above the checklist preamble; fold the **Outcome recording conventions** ¬ß**Item (a) authoritative-determination recording shape** paragraph into the new section (it currently lives at the bottom of the page); re-letter (b)‚Äì(ao) to (a)‚Äì(an) in the preamble's range references and in the **Ships when** mirror in `V18c-version-bump-checklist.md`.
- `host-prerequisites.md` ‚Äî repoint the cross-link in clause (a) from `#bump-checklist-instance-survival` to the new anchor.
- `model-changes-and-non-goals.md` ‚Äî repoint the cross-link in the **Open contradictions** entry to the new anchor.
- `V18c-version-bump-checklist.md` ‚Äî update **Ships when** to refer to the per-item-`(a)`‚Äì`(an)` recording shape (and to enumerate the new blocker section as its own check) so a missing item-(a) verdict no longer hides inside a 41-row list.

**Pros.**
- Cleanly separates the two semantically distinct surfaces (recurring audit vs one-shot blocker).
- The page-level table of contents now signals the blocker exists.
- `V18c`'s **Ships when** can name the blocker section explicitly rather than rely on a "(a) is the exception" carve-out.

**Cons.**
- Re-lettering (a)‚Äì(ao) is a cross-file rename that ripples into every cross-link to a checklist item (the file has many such anchors).
- Two pages ‚Äî `version-bump-step2.md` and `model-changes-and-non-goals.md` ‚Äî now both carry the blocker prose. A future drift in the prose between them is exactly what the **`docs/spec_topics/future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md` ‚Äî "Open contradictions" blocking item misplaced in a deferrals/non-goals page** sibling finding wants to avoid.

**Risks.**
- A botched re-letter breaks a non-trivial set of intra-page anchors that other pages link into (e.g. `#bump-checklist-slash-dispatch-serialisation`, `#bump-checklist-instance-survival`, ‚Ä¶).

### Option B ‚Äî Keep item (a) in place but visually and structurally hard-segregate it

**Approach.** Leave the row at position (a) in the enumeration (no re-lettering), but: (i) re-cast its preamble carve-out so the preamble lists item (a) as a *separate, blocking category* rather than as the lead element of an `(a)`‚Äì`(e)` MUST-audited group; (ii) prefix the row body with a bold **BLOCKING ‚Äî one-shot pre-implementation gate** marker; (iii) hoist the **Item (a) authoritative-determination recording shape** sub-paragraph from the bottom of the page to inline-immediately-after the item-(a) row body; (iv) add the missing `SM-4/5/6` cross-link at first occurrence inside the row body.

**Spec edits.**
- `version-bump-step2.md` ‚Äî rewrite the preamble's "all five of which ‚Ä¶" clause to separate item (a) out as its own category; inline-hoist the **Item (a) authoritative-determination recording shape** paragraph next to the item-(a) row; add the `SM-4/5/6` cross-link inside the row body at first occurrence.
- `V18c-version-bump-checklist.md` ‚Äî update **Ships when** so the item-(a) verdict's presence is asserted *as its own line item*, not folded into the existing "every item `(a)`‚Äì`(ao)`" recording check.

**Pros.**
- No re-lettering; preserves every existing intra-page and cross-page anchor.
- Smaller diff surface; less risk of a co-edit miss in the lock-step files.
- Co-locates item (a)'s prose with its recording shape, removing the read-back-and-forth ergonomics defect.

**Cons.**
- The blocker still lives "inside the per-bump checklist file" at the page-level ‚Äî the TOC signal added by Option A is not present.
- The preamble carve-out grows even more involved (it already carries an "item (a) is the exception" parenthetical; this option deepens that).

**Risks.**
- The bold-marker discipline ("**BLOCKING ‚Ä¶**") is contributor-discipline only; a future author copying item (b)'s shape may revert it without noticing.

### Recommendation

Take Option B and pair it with a small mechanical enforcement adjustment (described below). Option A's TOC win is real but bought with a re-letter that ripples through every cross-link to a checklist anchor ‚Äî and the sibling **`model-changes-and-non-goals.md` ‚Äî "Open contradictions" blocking item misplaced** finding indicates that an authoritative blocker-home should land *there* (or in a new `open-questions.md`), not get re-created on `version-bump-step2.md`. The right division of labour is: keep the bump-procedure obligation on `version-bump-step2.md` (where the bump contributor actually consults it) but hard-segregate it from the recurring audit shape; let `model-changes-and-non-goals.md`'s sibling finding resolve where the canonical blocker prose ultimately lives.

**Enforcement** ‚Äî the original suggested fix offered "demote the ordering MUST to SHOULD" or "add a build-time stub gate". Demoting the MUST is wrong: the gate really is blocking and the spec is correct to say so. The build-time stub gate is the right addition: at `V18c` build time, assert that a marker file or pinned constant (e.g. `INSTANCE_SURVIVAL_VERDICT: "governed-by-rebind" | "provably-differs"` in the same pinned-constants block that holds `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS`) carries a non-placeholder value before `V9l`'s degraded-branch obligations may compile against the symbol. That moves the ordering MUST from "editorial-review of a row in a 41-row list" to a typecheck-or-test red, on the same footing as the `loom/typecheck/session-shutdown-reason-snapshot` brand-string gate `V18c` already owns. The stub-gate sub-fix is independently landable from the placement edits and can be sequenced second.

**Edge cases the implementer must watch.**
- The preamble's "all five of which route their detection to editorial review under this procedure" sentence currently counts item (a) inside the "five MUST items" ‚Äî if item (a) is hoisted out of that group, the count language and the `(a)`‚Äì`(e)` range references in the preamble both need re-wording (the count becomes four MUST items (b)‚Äì(e); the carve-out parenthetical disappears as redundant).
- `host-prerequisites.md` clause (a) currently ends "tracked on the version-bump editorial-review checklist at [item (a)](./version-bump-step2.md#bump-checklist-instance-survival)" ‚Äî Option B keeps the anchor, so no cross-file edit; Option A breaks it.
- `V18c`'s **Ships when** today says "no MUST-level item `(a)`‚Äì`(e)` carries a `fail` outcome" ‚Äî item (a)'s verdict is `governed-by-rebind` / `provably-differs`, not `pass` / `fail`, so this clause already treats item (a) inconsistently. Whichever option is taken, this **Ships when** wording needs updating in the same edit; do not leave the inconsistency in place.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

An implementer scanning `version-bump-step2.md` reads item (a) as a normal SHOULD-record-or-`not-performed` audit row, fails to discover the present-tense pre-implementation gate it actually carries, and ships `V9l` (and `SM-4`/`SM-5`/`SM-6`/`SM-3b`) against the un-resolved open contradiction with no build-time test reddening ‚Äî exactly the failure mode `plan.md`'s ¬ßBlocked obligations entry is meant to prevent. The placement also amplifies the cross-cutting cluster's other defects (the session-model page lists SM-4/5/6 inline with active obligations, the model-changes-and-non-goals page hides a blocker in a non-goals file): every page that touches the blocker presents it as a routine entry.
