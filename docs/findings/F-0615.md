## Finding

The closing paragraph of `audit-wire-and-canary.md` ("Item 5 (cancellation propagation) is verified jointly by Step 0 item (b)'s `AbortSignal` / `AbortController` shape probe … Item 7 (binder LLM model) is verified at per-loom load time … name-links each item back to the obligation here so that downstream tests cite a single anchored source.") is orientation for the **SDK capability inventory** — it routes items 5 and 7 to their non-Step-0 verification mechanisms and asserts a "single anchored source" claim. The rest of the file is the audit's wire-serialisation contract, infrastructure-failure handling, and non-empty-scan canary, all of which target the inventory-closure audit's emission shape. The closing paragraph shares no subject with the surrounding contract; it landed here only because the f5e89f4 size-cap split sliced the monolithic `pi-integration-contract.md` at line boundaries and the trailing sentences of the original `## SDK capability inventory` section fell into the wrong child page.

The mismatch has two operative consequences. First, the "name-links each item back to the obligation **here**" claim is false: the obligations for items 5 and 7 do not live on this page. Item 5's normative probe rules live in `capability-probe.md` Step 0(b) (the `AbortSignal`/`AbortController` member-with-kind table) and Step 0(c) (the `AgentSession.prototype.abort` factory-probable member), and the inventory-side framing lives in `capability-inventory-items.md` item 5. Item 7's normative rule lives in `binder/binder-model-and-context.md` (Strict-capability requirement) with `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved` semantics, and the inventory-side framing in `capability-inventory-items.md` item 7. A test author following the `audit-wire-and-canary.md` claim lands on a page about wire serialisation rather than on the rules they need. Second, the same routing claim is already restated at the head of `## SDK capability inventory` in `inventory-audit-intro.md` (where it is on-topic) and at the relevant points in `capability-probe.md` Step 0(c) and `capability-inventory-items.md` item 5 — so the paragraph is both misplaced and redundant.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move the closing paragraph (the two sentences beginning "Item 5 (cancellation propagation) is verified jointly by Step 0 item (b)…" and ending "…name-links each item back to the obligation here so that downstream tests cite a single anchored source.") out of `audit-wire-and-canary.md` and merge its content into the **lead paragraph of `## SDK capability inventory`** in `inventory-audit-intro.md` (the `PIC-15` paragraph). The on-page `PIC-15` paragraph already states the items-1/2/3/4/6 half of the routing ("Items 1, 2, 3, 4, and 6 are factory-probable and verified by Step 0 (Capability probe)…"); append the items-5 and items-7 routing sentences to the same paragraph so the full routing claim sits in one place. Update the "name-links each item back to the obligation here" tail to read "name-links each item back to its anchored obligation on this page or its forward-linked owner page" (the items-5/7 obligations live on `capability-probe.md` and `binder/binder-model-and-context.md`, not on the inventory page itself), so the orientation claim matches the file layout post-split.

Concretely:

- Delete the trailing paragraph from `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-wire-and-canary.md`. The file then ends at the `## Non-empty-scan canary` bullet's closing sentence followed by the existing "The audit runs as part of the SDK surface-inventory tests step 2 below names…" paragraph that closes the audit-wiring contract.
- Extend the `PIC-15` paragraph in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/inventory-audit-intro.md` with the items-5 / items-7 routing sentences, refreshed link targets (`./capability-probe.md#entry-capability-probe` and `../binder/binder-model-and-context.md#strict-capability-requirement`) and corrected "here / forward-linked owner page" tail.
- No edit is needed at the duplicate sites in `capability-probe.md` Step 0(c) or `capability-inventory-items.md` items 5/7 — they already carry the same routing in their probe-side and inventory-item-side voices respectively, and serve as the in-page neighbours of the rules they front for. The `overview-and-orientation.md` "Host runtime" precondition 3 bullet already forward-links to the inventory page and continues to work unchanged after the move.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The closing paragraph is the file's only inbound landing site for the `spec.md` "single anchored source" orientation claim. After the move, the `inventory-audit-intro.md` `## SDK capability inventory` section must carry the same "downstream tests cite a single anchored source" assurance the trailing paragraph currently makes, otherwise `overview-and-orientation.md`'s forward-link to the inventory page becomes weaker than the prior cross-reference promised.
- The link in the moved paragraph currently reads `[``spec.md`` — Orientation — Prerequisites](../overview-and-orientation.md#orientation)`. On the destination page it should remain a relative link at the same depth (`../overview-and-orientation.md#orientation`); since both files live in `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/`, no path adjustment is needed.
- The trailing `[Slash-Command Argument Binding — Strict-capability requirement](../binder/binder-model-and-context.md#strict-capability-requirement)` link likewise needs no path change.
- Preserve the `loom/load/host-incompatible` and `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved` disjointness assertion — it is the load-bearing claim the binder strict-capability degradation rule on `binder-model-and-context.md` depends on, and it MUST survive the move.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A test author or implementer looking up how item 5 or item 7 is verified can be misdirected to the audit wire/canary page, but the same routing is already authoritatively stated on the three on-topic pages (`inventory-audit-intro.md`, `capability-probe.md` Step 0(c), `capability-inventory-items.md`), so the misdirection is at worst a wasted click. No observer behaviour changes either way.
