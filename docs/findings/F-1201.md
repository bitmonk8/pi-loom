## Finding

Four small but corrosive clarity defects sit on the query topic. Each is local; collectively they touch every page of the topic and bleed into the test surfaces (`V13a`, `V13c`, `V13d`, `V13h`).

1. **`loom/parse/empty-template` predicate ordering reads as a pipeline that contradicts the runtime/parse-time split.** `query-forms.md` (§ *Degenerate rendered templates*) says the static body is evaluated "after newline-trim and dedent but **before** the escape rewrites". Newline-trim and dedent are *runtime* normalisations applied to the fully-rendered string immediately before the user turn is issued; they are not parse-time passes. A literal reading therefore demands the parser execute two runtime passes against a non-existent rendered string. The intended meaning is conceptual: the predicate is evaluated against each literal segment **as it would survive newline-trim/dedent and before the escape rewrites would fire** — i.e. a static-analysis approximation, not a real pipeline step. The same paragraph then leans on the pre-escape vs post-escape distinction (`\n` reads as the two-character sequence pre-escape, a single whitespace newline post-escape) to make the author-suppression hatch work, so the ordering is load-bearing and cannot be left as ambient prose.

2. **`respond_repair.attempts` "whether successfully or not" blurs what is being counted.** `query-failure-and-repair.md` (§ *Non-validation failures during a respond-repair follow-up*) defines an attempt as "follow-ups that produced an assistant response which was then re-validated (whether successfully or not)". The natural reading of "successfully" is the validation outcome; the actual intent is that the re-validation step *ran*, regardless of pass/fail. The same paragraph immediately above the predicate already says "each follow-up that produces an assistant response the runtime then re-validates counts as one … regardless of how many tool-call rounds it contains" — so the parenthetical adds nothing and introduces an ambiguity (does a successful re-validation count? if so, what would it mean for repair to continue after a passing attempt?). `V13h` and `V13d` both cite this section for their per-attempt-accounting tests, so the fuzz propagates into test wording.

3. **The schema-inference *Stopped (opaque)* operator set is left open with "etc.".** `query-forms.md` (§ *Schema inference algorithm*) enumerates the opaque positions as "binary and unary operators (`+`, `==`, `!`, etc.); member access (`a.b`); …". The other half of the same bullet-pair, *Crossed (transparent)*, is enumerated exhaustively (parenthesisation, `let x: T = …` RHS, typed-parameter argument, declared-return-type tail, `return` operand, ternary branches, array-literal elements). A normative classification of expression positions cannot end its hostile half with "etc." — two implementers reading the spec will disagree on whether, say, `?.` chains, the `as` cast, range operators, or method-call receivers count as opaque, and `V13b` (which tests the crossed/stopped behaviour) has no way to write its vectors against an open set.

4. **`query-tool-loop.md` names a "leading non-compliance sentence" that nothing in the spec defines.** The Initial forced respond turn template paragraph says the body "is byte-identical to the `schema_repeat` respond-repair template minus that template's leading non-compliance sentence". The `schema_repeat` template (in `query-failure-and-repair.md`) is two lines: `Your previous response did not match the required schema. Return your final answer using the \`__loom_respond_<slug>\` tool, conforming to this schema:` followed by `<schema-json>`. Nothing in either page identifies the first sentence — *"Your previous response did not match the required schema."* — as the "non-compliance sentence", and the term appears nowhere else. The reader has to reverse-engineer which sentence is meant by diffing the two templates byte-for-byte, which defeats the whole point of pinning one template by reference to the other.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**

- `fae85d3` *Tighten spec for implementation-plan readiness* (2026-05-04) — introduced the *Stopped (opaque)* enumeration ending in "etc." in the original monolithic `spec.md`.
- `82be125` *spec: adopt P1 typed-query two-phase loop (Option A)* (2026-05-04) — introduced the `loom/parse/empty-template` predicate with the "after newline-trim and dedent" wording, already treating runtime normalisations as parse-time inputs.
- `06491ee` *pi-loom spec: resolve "Coercion follow-up failure modes unspecified"* (2026-05-04) — introduced the "whether successfully or not" parenthetical on `attempts` accounting (then phrased against `retry.attempts` / "coercion", later renamed to `respond_repair.attempts`).
- `bbe2602` *pi-loom spec: resolve "Initial forced respond turn — instruction wording status undeclared"* (2026-06-05) — introduced the *Initial forced respond turn template* section in `query-tool-loop.md` and the "leading non-compliance sentence" reference to `schema_repeat`.
- `2637aec` *pi-loom spec-plan: fix F-0755 — empty-template predicate pinned to pre-escape source segments* (2026-06-20) — extended the empty-template predicate wording with the "but **before** the escape rewrites" clause, deepening the literal-pipeline reading without flagging that the prior runtime-pass wording was already conceptual.

**History:** Each defect entered on a different commit doing unrelated work; the four sit on three different files in the same topic. None of them is the inverse of a previous fix — each is a fresh phrasing choice in the area the commit was editing. The interaction lives in their co-location: implementers and reviewers encounter all four within the same topic walk and they erode confidence in the topic as a whole, even though no single commit is the natural revert target. The durable fix is editorial across the three files rather than a revert.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

A bimodal-plus finding: four independent obligations, each on a different sentence (and three of them on different files). Each is a small, separately-loopable edit; they should land in the order below so the smaller scope-bounding edits stabilise their callers before the wider ones.

### Option A — Empty-template predicate: frame as conceptual static analysis

**Approach.** Rewrite the parse-time bullet in `query-forms.md` § *Degenerate rendered templates* to make the static-analysis intent explicit, so the "after newline-trim and dedent but before escapes" clause reads as a conceptual ordering of the source-segment transformations the predicate reasons about, not a sequence of pipeline passes the parser executes.

**Spec edits.** In `query-forms.md`, the parse-time bullet becomes (sketch): "the parser approximates the rendered static body — for each literal segment between interpolations, taking what newline-trim and dedent would leave behind and the escape rewrites would *not yet* have applied — and warns when that approximation is empty or whitespace-only …". Keep the existing suppression-hatch sentence; it remains correct under the conceptual framing because the predicate is explicitly evaluated against the pre-escape source bytes.

**Pros.** Resolves the literal-pipeline contradiction without changing the observable predicate. The `V13a` test (degenerate template emits the warning and short-circuits) keeps the same expected outcome.
**Cons.** Slightly longer prose.
**Risks.** None — the predicate's behaviour is unchanged.

### Option B — `attempts` accounting: drop the ambiguous parenthetical

**Approach.** In `query-failure-and-repair.md` § *Non-validation failures during a respond-repair follow-up*, replace "whether successfully or not" with wording that names the *step* that triggers the debit, not the validation outcome.

**Spec edits.** The second sub-bullet becomes (sketch): "`attempts` counts only follow-ups that produced an assistant response the runtime then ran through re-validation, regardless of whether re-validation passed or failed." The paragraph above already says "each follow-up that produces an assistant response the runtime then re-validates counts as one …"; consider deduplicating, but at minimum align the two sentences on the same predicate.

**Pros.** Eliminates the ambiguity at the only site that introduces it.
**Cons.** None.
**Risks.** None — the predicate is observable in `V13d`'s attempt-accounting test.

### Option C — *Stopped (opaque)* operator set: close the enumeration

**Approach.** In `query-forms.md` § *Schema inference algorithm*, replace the open "etc." with either (i) an exhaustive list of opaque expression positions (drawn from `grammar.md` and `expressions.md`) or (ii) an explicit residual rule of the form "every expression position not enumerated under *Crossed (transparent)* above is opaque."

**Spec edits.** Recommended sub-option: the residual form. The *Crossed* half is already exhaustive, so making *Stopped* the complement is the smaller edit and stays robust if the grammar grows. Drop the parenthetical operator examples or keep them as "e.g." only. (Sub-option (i) — full enumeration — works too but creates a maintenance obligation each time the grammar gains an operator.)

**Pros.** Closes the classification; `V13b`'s crossed/stopped vector set becomes well-defined.
**Cons.** Loses some illustrative concreteness if the operator examples are dropped.
**Risks.** None.

### Option D — Name the `schema_repeat` non-compliance sentence

**Approach.** In `query-failure-and-repair.md` § *Follow-up turn templates (normative)*, label `schema_repeat`'s first sentence as the *non-compliance sentence* at its point of definition. In `query-tool-loop.md`'s *Initial forced respond turn template* introduction, either keep the existing reference (now defined) or quote the sentence inline.

**Spec edits.** Add a parenthetical to the `schema_repeat` block introduction: "(its first sentence — *Your previous response did not match the required schema.* — is the **non-compliance sentence**; the *Initial forced respond turn template* in `query-tool-loop.md` is `schema_repeat` minus this sentence)." Then `query-tool-loop.md`'s existing wording resolves correctly.

**Pros.** Single-site definition; `V13c` and `V13h` can both cite it.
**Cons.** None.
**Risks.** None.

### Recommendation

Land all four. Sequence: **D → B → A → C**.

- **D first** because it is a single named-token edit confined to a definition that two other leaves cite; landing it before any test-wording churn lets `V13c` and `V13h` quote the term as it appears in the spec.
- **B second** because it is also confined to a single sentence and unblocks `V13d`'s test wording; it does not interact with A or C.
- **A third** because it rewrites a single bullet but the rewrite is prose-shaped (not a token swap), so it benefits from D and B having already stabilised the surrounding section structure.
- **C last** because closing the *Stopped* enumeration is the option with the largest design choice (residual rule vs full enumeration) and benefits from the other three having already removed noise from the topic walk that the closing-residual phrasing will need to read against. Pick the residual sub-option unless `grammar.md` and `expressions.md` reveal that the opaque set is small and stable enough to enumerate exhaustively.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- **Option A** — do not weaken the *post-escape* check that the runtime short-circuit performs; the two predicates (parse-time pre-escape, runtime post-render) are intentionally asymmetric, and the asymmetry is what makes the `\n` suppression hatch work.
- **Option B** — if the deduplication is taken, keep the *rationale* sentence ("`attempts` is the bound on respond-repair, not on incidental infrastructure failure …") on the surviving bullet; it is the only place that explains why a transport blip cannot silently shorten the repair budget.
- **Option C** — if the residual form is chosen, verify against `grammar.md` that the *Crossed* enumeration is in fact exhaustive of context-preserving positions; otherwise a context-preserving position omitted from *Crossed* would be silently miscategorised as opaque under the residual.
- **Option D** — `schema_repeat`'s first sentence is the exact token sequence pinned by the verbatim-rendering rule; if the wording of the sentence is ever changed (a spec-versioned breaking change per the same section), the *non-compliance sentence* term must move in lockstep.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Two implementers can today produce divergent behaviour or divergent tests: one may take the empty-template ordering literally and implement two runtime passes at parse time; another may treat the *Stopped* set as exhaustive at `+`, `==`, `!` and admit other operators silently; a third may interpret "whether successfully or not" as meaning a passing re-validation still consumes an attempt. None of these is a correctness wedge in isolation, but each makes the corresponding test ambiguous. The fourth defect (the dangling "leading non-compliance sentence") is purely editorial — there is exactly one way to back into the intended meaning — but it costs every future reader a diff.
