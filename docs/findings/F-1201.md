## Problem

Four clarity defects sit across the query topic's three pages. In `query-forms.md` § *Degenerate rendered templates*, the `loom/parse/empty-template` predicate is described as applying newline-trim and dedent "before the escape rewrites" — wording that reads as a runtime pipeline executed at parse time rather than the intended conceptual static-analysis approximation of the rendered body. In the same file's § *Schema inference algorithm*, the *Stopped (opaque)* operator set ends in "etc." while its *Crossed (transparent)* counterpart is enumerated exhaustively, leaving positions such as `?.` chains, the `as` cast, and range operators unclassified. In `query-failure-and-repair.md` § *Non-validation failures during a respond-repair follow-up*, the `respond_repair.attempts` gloss "whether successfully or not" blurs whether the counter debits when re-validation *runs* or only on a particular pass/fail outcome. And `query-tool-loop.md` § *Initial forced respond turn template* references a "leading non-compliance sentence" of the `schema_repeat` template that nothing in the spec defines.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**

- `fae85d3` *Tighten spec for implementation-plan readiness* (2026-05-04) — introduced the *Stopped (opaque)* enumeration ending in "etc." in the original monolithic `spec.md`.
- `82be125` *spec: adopt P1 typed-query two-phase loop (Option A)* (2026-05-04) — introduced the `loom/parse/empty-template` predicate with the "after newline-trim and dedent" wording, already treating runtime normalisations as parse-time inputs.
- `06491ee` *pi-loom spec: resolve "Coercion follow-up failure modes unspecified"* (2026-05-04) — introduced the "whether successfully or not" parenthetical on `attempts` accounting (then phrased against `retry.attempts` / "coercion", later renamed to `respond_repair.attempts`).
- `bbe2602` *pi-loom spec: resolve "Initial forced respond turn — instruction wording status undeclared"* (2026-06-05) — introduced the *Initial forced respond turn template* section in `query-tool-loop.md` and the "leading non-compliance sentence" reference to `schema_repeat`.
- `2637aec` *pi-loom spec-plan: fix F-0755 — empty-template predicate pinned to pre-escape source segments* (2026-06-20) — extended the empty-template predicate wording with the "but **before** the escape rewrites" clause, deepening the literal-pipeline reading without flagging that the prior runtime-pass wording was already conceptual.

**History:** Each defect entered on a different commit doing unrelated work; the four sit on three different files in the same topic. None of them is the inverse of a previous fix — each is a fresh phrasing choice in the area the commit was editing. The interaction lives in their co-location: implementers and reviewers encounter all four within the same topic walk and they erode confidence in the topic as a whole, even though no single commit is the natural revert target. The durable fix is editorial across the three files rather than a revert.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the `query-forms.md` § *Degenerate rendered templates* parse-time bullet so the newline-trim / dedent / pre-escape ordering reads as a conceptual static-analysis approximation of the rendered static body, not a sequence of pipeline passes the parser executes. Clarify the `query-failure-and-repair.md` § *Non-validation failures during a respond-repair follow-up* `attempts` sentence to name the step that triggers the debit — re-validation having run — rather than its pass/fail outcome. Close the § *Schema inference algorithm* *Stopped (opaque)* enumeration with a residual rule that defers to the *Crossed (transparent)* set, or enumerate the opaque positions exhaustively. Define the "non-compliance sentence" term at the `schema_repeat` block in § *Follow-up turn templates (normative)* so the `query-tool-loop.md` reference resolves against a defined term.

## Solution constraints

- The parse-time pre-escape predicate and the runtime post-render short-circuit in `query-forms.md` § *Degenerate rendered templates* are intentionally asymmetric; the rewrite MUST NOT collapse or weaken the runtime post-render check.
- The residual-rule form of the *Stopped (opaque)* set MUST NOT be adopted unless the *Crossed (transparent)* enumeration is verified exhaustive of context-preserving positions against `grammar.md`; otherwise enumerate the opaque set exhaustively.
