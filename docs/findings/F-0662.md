## Problem

In `conversation-drive.md`'s prompt-mode bullet, the *Resolution*, *Error detection*, and *Hang handling* clauses all build on `ctx.waitForIdle()` "never rejecting" — the *Resolution* clause's `MUST NOT` against a wrapping `try`/`catch`, the trailing-turn `stopReason: "error"` probe, and cancellation handling all depend on it. That property is a behavioural commitment of an external Pi method (`ExtensionCommandContext.waitForIdle()`) that the `Promise<void>` typed surface does not encode. Every sibling host presupposition on the same page is captured under a named `*-presupposition` consumption-posture paragraph and routed to a lettered item in the *Editorial-review checklist for unpinned host presuppositions* in `version-bump-step2.md`; the never-rejects property carries neither half. A future `@earendil-works/pi-coding-agent` minor that made `waitForIdle()` reject would silently falsify the `MUST NOT` and the error probe, with no SDK surface-inventory signal and no editorial-review prompt at the bump.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `d54d798` (2026-05-06, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"waitForIdle resolution, error, and hang semantics not contracted\""); subsequent commits established the consumption-posture / editorial-review pattern around it (`e89e42e` 2026-05-17 "T21 Pi-side slash-handler promise lifecycle taken as given"; `aebec4e` 2026-06-04; `3683345` 2026-06-06; `803106d` 2026-06-07; `85fc906` 2026-06-10) without back-filling this one.
**History:** `git log -S "never rejects"` on the spec corpus shows the *Resolution / Error detection / Hang handling* triplet, which is what introduces the "never rejects" claim and the dependent `MUST NOT` and post-`waitForIdle()` probe, first lands in `d54d798`. At that point the editorial-review-checklist machinery for unpinned host presuppositions did not yet exist in its current form: the *Pi-side slash-handler promise lifecycle (consumption posture)* paragraph and its routed bump-checklist items were introduced in `e89e42e` eleven days later, and the catalogue of named `*-presupposition` anchors with bump-checklist routing was then expanded incrementally by the later commits enumerated above — each one adding (or relocating) the sibling presuppositions that now surround the unrouted `waitForIdle()` claim. The current defect is therefore the interaction between (a) the inline-MUST-NOT framing chosen in `d54d798` before the framework existed and (b) the framework's later adoption everywhere on the same page except this one site; no single commit introduced an inconsistency on its own.

## Solution approach

Add an anchored consumption-posture paragraph to `conversation-drive.md` capturing the `ctx.waitForIdle()` no-rejection property, modelled on the existing `#driven-turn-commit-ordering-presupposition` and `#user-visible-streaming-ordering-presupposition` siblings on the same page. Rewrite the `*Resolution.*` clause's bare "never rejects" claim — and the mirror `waitForIdle()` annotation in `host-interfaces-core.md`'s `ExtensionCommandContext` block — as a forward-link to the new anchor. Add a corresponding editorial-review checklist item to `version-bump-step2.md`, modelled on items (ac)/(ak), routing detection of a future Pi minor that regresses the property.

## Solution constraints

- Widen the step-2 preamble item-range references and the per-item-recording clause in `V18c-version-bump-checklist.md`'s **Ships when** section in the same edit, so the new checklist item is covered by the recording gate.
- Preserve the inline `MUST NOT` against wrapping the `await` in a `try`/`catch` at full normative strength; do not downgrade it to a SHOULD.
