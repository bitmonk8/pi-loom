## Finding

`version-bump-step2.md`'s items (a)–(ak) are framed by the section preamble as an **editorial-review checklist** — a contributor read/diff of presupposition prose against the candidate Pi minor — and items (a)–(e) plus most of (f)–(ak) hold to that scope (re-read a paragraph, diff a `dist/*.js` body, confirm a member shape, surface a divergence on the bump commit). Six items break that scope by inlining active test-fixture maintenance, naming the loom test harness, and shifting the responsibility for keeping a checked-in capture corpus current onto the bump contributor:

- (i) — *re-run the provider-error fixtures*, plus a sentence assigning "keeping that corpus current — re-capturing each provider's error-body text when it publishes an error-format or API-version change" to the contributor performing the bump.
- (u) — *re-run the per-provider forced-tool fixtures* against four named providers, plus the same "keeping that corpus current — re-capturing each provider's forced-tool and off-session-turn behaviour" contributor assignment.
- (aa) — *re-run the per-provider retry-timing fixtures* against four named providers.
- (ab) — *re-run the per-provider cancellation fixtures* against four named providers.
- (af) — `complete()` `onResponse` provider-response delivery, audited per-provider against four named providers (fixture-shaped audit even where the prose does not say "fixture").
- (aj) — *read the `SessionManager.inMemory` in-memory-session branch's JS body in `dist/core/session-manager.js`*, layered with a behavioural transcript-non-retention claim that the body-read alone cannot witness.

The drivers and trigger surface for these obligations are not editorial. Provider error-body wording, forced-tool acceptance, retry/backoff timing, `AbortSignal` honouring, and `onResponse` delivery change on a provider's API-version or error-format publication schedule — not on a Pi minor bump — so the natural trigger is *whichever event comes first* and the natural owner is whoever owns the captured-corpus root, not specifically the contributor performing a Pi-SDK bump. Folding the fixture re-run, the corpus re-capture, and the harness pin into the editorial checklist gives the audit a testing-maintenance scope its preamble never declared, and gives the bump contributor an obligation whose preconditions (live provider access, an up-to-date capture corpus, fixture-run infrastructure) are silently inherited from elsewhere.

The scope confusion is concrete: item (i)'s prose now spans a re-read of a presupposition paragraph, a re-run of `npm test` fixtures, a description of what the captured corpus holds (post-formatter `errorMessage` bodies vs raw HTTP error bodies), and a contributor obligation to re-capture provider error bodies on a provider-driven trigger — four scopes in one bullet, each with a different actor and a different recurrence. Item (aj) further conflates a `dist/*.js` body diff (an editorial-review operation under the same [Baseline acquisition](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md#bump-baseline-acquisition) recipe items (e)/(r) use) with a transcript-non-retention *behavioural* claim that a body read cannot positively witness — it can only spot a `writeFile`-shaped regression — which is a fixture-style observation in editorial-review clothing.

**History:** The checklist was extended one earlier-resolved finding at a time. Each individual extension was reasonable in isolation (a specific presupposition needed a per-bump audit anchor), but no single commit chose between two homes — *editorial checklist* vs *fixture-maintenance ledger* — so the fixture-maintenance scope accreted into the editorial section by repeated default rather than by a deliberate placement decision. The transcript-non-retention claim in `9ced9f1` further blurred the seam by giving an editorial-review-shaped body read a behavioural target that a body read cannot fully witness. The defect is the absence of a separating sub-heading the six landings could have routed to, not any single landing.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add a new **Fixture maintenance** sub-section to `version-bump-step2.md` (sibling to the editorial-review checklist, not nested inside it), and route the fixture-rerun / captured-corpus / per-provider-audit obligations of items (i), (u), (aa), (ab), (af), and (aj) there. Each affected checklist item retains a one-sentence editorial stub of the form "confirm against the candidate minor that the presupposition cited in `<paragraph>` still holds; the fixture rerun and captured-corpus maintenance for this item are owned by **Fixture maintenance** below" with a same-page anchor link.

Concretely the new sub-section MUST:

1. Enumerate the per-item fixture artefacts (the overflow-signature corpus for (i), the forced-tool corpus for (u), the retry-timing corpus for (aa), the cancellation corpus for (ab), the `onResponse` corpus for (af), and the `SessionManager.inMemory` no-persistence corpus or runtime probe for (aj)).
2. State the **trigger union** for each: re-run on Pi minor bump **or** on a provider API-version / error-format change, whichever comes first — the bump contributor owns the rerun at bump time, and the corpus root's owner (named in the new section) owns it on a provider-driven trigger.
3. Name the harness in one place (`npm test`) so individual checklist items don't pin it and the harness can be relocated without touching every (i)/(u)/(aa)/(ab) bullet.
4. State the recording shape for the bump-time rerun outcome — reuse the existing `pass` / `fail` / `not-performed` vocabulary from the preamble, recorded under the cross-referencing checklist item's rationale slot, so the merge-gate disposition arm of the preamble continues to apply unmodified.
5. Address (aj)'s special case: keep the `dist/*.js` body read as an editorial stub under item (aj) (parallel to (e)/(r)), and route the transcript-non-retention *behavioural* check to **Fixture maintenance** as a separate sub-bullet — either a fixture that asserts no on-disk artefact appears for an in-memory subagent run, or an explicit *no behavioural probe at loom 1.0; detection is body-read-only with the known limitation that a non-`writeFile` retention pathway would be missed* statement that names the gap honestly.

Edge cases an implementer must watch:

- The preamble currently bounds the checklist as `(a)`–`(ak)` and pins the per-item recording shape; the extraction must not break that surface, so each (i)/(u)/(aa)/(ab)/(af)/(aj) item remains in the checklist with an outcome slot and only its *body* moves to the new section.
- `provider-error-mapping.md`'s *Re-validation gate (loom 1.0.0)* paragraph already points at item (i) as the owner; that pointer must be re-aimed at the new **Fixture maintenance** sub-section in the same edit, or its claim that the contributor's rerun obligation lives at item (i) goes stale.
- `V18c` / `V18c-T`'s **Adds** prose enumerates the editorial-review checklist by item-letter range `(a)`–`(ak)`; if the extraction renames or re-numbers items, those plan leaves must be co-edited. If extraction only relocates the body and keeps the (a)–(ak) letters, the leaves stay text-stable.
- The escalation clause boilerplate ("escalates to a build-time pin … once Pi exposes a typed contract …") on (i)/(u)/(aa)/(ab)/(af) is about typed-contract escalation, not fixture-maintenance scope, and should remain on each item's editorial stub rather than moving to the new section.
- The trigger-union wording must avoid weakening the bump contributor's obligation: a bump contributor who cannot reach a live provider does not become exempt from recording an outcome — the recording falls to `not-performed` with the live-access reason cited, on the same footing the preamble already establishes.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A bump contributor reading the checklist sees six items asking them to keep a per-provider capture corpus current on triggers they did not cause and may not be able to satisfy. In practice this either silently shifts to `not-performed` rationales on (i)/(u)/(aa)/(ab)/(af) (still conformant per the preamble, but the trigger-mismatch reason is opaque to anyone auditing the record) or the obligations float without an owner between Pi-bump events and provider API-format changes. No correctness arm flips; the bump-time editorial gate still records something on every item. The cost is owner/trigger drift on the fixture corpus and a checklist scope that no longer matches its preamble.
