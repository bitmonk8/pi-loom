## Problem

`version-bump-step2.md`'s section preamble frames items (a)–(ak) as an editorial-review checklist (a contributor read/diff of presupposition prose against the candidate Pi minor), but six items — (i), (u), (aa), (ab), (af), and (aj) — break that scope by inlining active test-fixture maintenance: re-running per-provider `npm test` fixtures, naming the loom harness, and assigning ongoing captured-corpus re-capture to the bump contributor. The natural trigger for those obligations is a provider API-version / error-format change (not a Pi minor bump), and the natural owner is whoever owns the captured-corpus root, so folding them into the editorial checklist gives the audit a testing-maintenance scope its preamble never declared and gives the contributor an obligation whose preconditions (live provider access, an up-to-date corpus, fixture infrastructure) are silently inherited. Item (i) alone now spans four scopes in one bullet (presupposition re-read, fixture re-run, corpus description, contributor re-capture obligation), each with a different actor and recurrence. Item (aj) additionally conflates an editorial `dist/*.js` body read with a transcript-non-retention behavioural claim a body read cannot positively witness.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `2ecf650` (2026-06-05) — "Re-validating provider-owned behaviour has no enumerated data source or owner": added the *corpus / `npm test` harness / contributor-keeps-current* clauses to item (i) (then also folded into (u)).
- `e63ba6c` (2026-06-05) — "Per-provider complete() forced-tool re-validation gate": authored item (u) as a per-provider fixture re-run (initial scope without the captured-corpus clause).
- `3683345` (2026-06-06) — "Binder relies on three unpinned complete() behaviours": added items (aa) and (ab) as per-provider retry-timing and cancellation fixture re-runs.
- `44782e9` (2026-06-06) — "TransportError catch-all / pi-ai provider-error surface": added item (af), the `onResponse` per-provider audit.
- `9ced9f1` (2026-06-08) — "subagent transcript-discard not routed to bump checklist": added item (aj), folding a `dist/*.js` body read together with the transcript-non-retention behavioural claim.

**History:** The checklist was extended one earlier-resolved finding at a time. Each individual extension was reasonable in isolation (a specific presupposition needed a per-bump audit anchor), but no single commit chose between two homes — *editorial checklist* vs *fixture-maintenance ledger* — so the fixture-maintenance scope accreted into the editorial section by repeated default rather than by a deliberate placement decision. The transcript-non-retention claim in `9ced9f1` further blurred the seam by giving an editorial-review-shaped body read a behavioural target that a body read cannot fully witness. The defect is the absence of a separating sub-heading the six landings could have routed to, not any single landing.

## Solution approach

Split the fixture-rerun and captured-corpus-maintenance obligations now bundled into editorial-review checklist items (i), (u), (aa), (ab), (af), and (aj) out into a dedicated fixture-maintenance home separate from the editorial-audit scope the section preamble declares, leaving each affected item an editorial stub that cross-references the new home. Re-aim `provider-error-mapping.md`'s *Re-validation gate (loom 1.0.0)* and *Authoritative source* paragraphs — which currently name item (i) as the rerun-obligation owner — at the separated home in the same edit so their pointer does not go stale. For item (aj), keep the `dist/*.js` body read as the editorial operation (parallel to items (e)/(r) under [Baseline acquisition](#bump-baseline-acquisition)) and separate the transcript-non-retention behavioural claim a body read cannot witness.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: renumbering or removing the (a)–(ak) item letters — the `V18c` / `V18c-T` plan leaves enumerate that range and co-break if the letters change. Relocate item bodies only; keep each affected letter and its outcome slot in the checklist.
- Out of scope: the items (f)–(ak) escalation-clause boilerplate cleanup and the items (r)/(aj) `dist/*.js` drift-handling fallback — both are separate same-cluster findings that resolve independently of this scope extraction.
