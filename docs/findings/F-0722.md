## Finding

The typed-query reference-implementation bullet in `docs/spec_topics/implementation-notes.md` (the fourth Runtime sub-bullet, under "Runs the two-phase loop") closes with a normative-shaped sentence whose subject is a mechanism that loom 1.0 does **not** use:

> The runtime does **not** install a `before_provider_request` payload-rewrite hook in loom 1.0 — a global hook that rewrites every provider call in every session is the mechanism Option B of the resolved typed-query design rejected; the per-call `options.toolChoice` route on `complete()` is scoped to exactly the forced respond turn and avoids the cross-session-fingerprinting machinery a global hook would require.

Two scope/prescription problems run together. First, the sentence forecloses an implementation strategy (a `before_provider_request` hook) by *negative prescription* rather than stating the observable property that matters — that the forced tool choice is scoped to a single off-session `complete()` call and therefore cannot leak into any other session's provider payloads. The same observable is already pinned positively two sentences earlier ("`for that turn only*`"; "the forced respond turn runs off-session through `complete()` and attaches no turn to the driven session"), so the negative form adds no behavioural content. Second, "Option B of the resolved typed-query design" is a label the corpus does not resolve anywhere — there is no design-options register, no commit-pinned decision log, and no anchor — so the reader cannot tell what Option A and Option B were, who rejected which, or when. The label is a private artefact of the resolution process leaking into the normative text.

The same defect propagates through one cross-reference: `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/conversation-drive.md` (the typed-query bullet) closes with "The full mechanism — including the `before_provider_request` payload-rewrite hook loom 1.0 rejects — is described in [Implementation Notes — Runtime]" — i.e. it teaches that the IMPL page contains a hook-rejection rationale, so any rewrite of the IMPL sentence must drop that pointer's "including … rejects" framing too.

The defect therefore entered the spec with the architectural decision itself rather than as drift on top of it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace the final sentence of the "Runs the two-phase loop" sub-bullet in `docs/spec_topics/implementation-notes.md` with a positive observable guarantee, and either delete the "Option B" reference or demote it to a parenthetical, clearly non-normative design-history note. Concrete edit:

- Strike: *"The runtime does **not** install a `before_provider_request` payload-rewrite hook in loom 1.0 — a global hook that rewrites every provider call in every session is the mechanism Option B of the resolved typed-query design rejected; the per-call `options.toolChoice` route on `complete()` is scoped to exactly the forced respond turn and avoids the cross-session-fingerprinting machinery a global hook would require."*
- Replace with prose to this effect (paraphrase, not a verbatim template): *"Because the forced tool choice is carried on a single off-session `complete()` invocation, the runtime does not need — and does not install — any cross-session payload-rewriting machinery to scope the forcing to the respond turn. Other sessions' provider payloads are not inspected or rewritten as part of typed-query dispatch."*
- If a design-history pointer is judged useful, append a clearly non-normative parenthetical such as *"(Design-history note: an earlier option considered a global `before_provider_request` payload-rewrite hook; that option was rejected for the cross-session-fingerprinting machinery it would require.)"* — without the "Option B" label, since the corpus does not enumerate options elsewhere.

Co-edit `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/conversation-drive.md`'s cross-reference: change *"including the `before_provider_request` payload-rewrite hook loom 1.0 rejects"* to *"including why the forced tool choice does not require cross-session payload-rewriting machinery"* (or simply drop the "including …" clause and keep the bare pointer to Implementation Notes — Runtime).

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The surviving observable guarantees the negative-prescription sentence currently carries — (a) forcing is *scoped to the respond turn only*, (b) other sessions' payloads are untouched — must remain present after the rewrite; do not drop them along with the mechanism reference. (a) is already pinned by the earlier "*for that turn only*" clause in the same sub-bullet; (b) is the one new fact the rewritten sentence needs to carry positively.
- Do not import any of the introducing commits' SHAs or the "Option A/Option B" labels into the spec text; that history belongs in commit messages and the spec-review log, not the normative corpus.
- The rewrite touches the `## Runtime` bullet only; the upstream "behavioural contract" sentence (which delegates to `pi-integration-contract.md` and `query.md`) and the downstream sub-bullets (`max_rounds` exemption, user-facing rendering) must remain unchanged.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader encountering "Option B of the resolved typed-query design" cannot resolve the reference inside the corpus, so the rationale reads as either a private joke or an instruction to consult an external document that does not exist; either way it weakens trust in nearby normative prose. A future implementer who reads the negative prescription as a hard ban also loses a valid degree of freedom — Pi could grow a same-session-scoped tool-choice hook tomorrow and loom would have to amend the spec to permit it, even though the observable guarantee (one off-session forced turn, no cross-session payload rewriting) would still hold. No behaviour changes under either reading today.
