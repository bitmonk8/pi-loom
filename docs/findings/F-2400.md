## Problem

The *Citation-token grammar* paragraph and the *Generated-output-in-src limitation* paragraph on `audit-resolution.md` point "below"/"there" to the *Stale-citation limitation* and *Substitution limitation* sub-bullets, but those sub-bullets now live on the sibling shard `audit-failures.md` under the *Stale-marker discriminator* — only the discriminator's heading remains on `audit-resolution.md`. A reader following the intra-page directional words cannot locate the referenced material on the named page. The two target sub-bullets on `audit-failures.md` currently carry no `<a id>` anchors, so a precise cross-file link has no target to point at.

**History:** e5cd514 authored the inventory-closure-audit prose inside the single monolithic `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md`, where *Citation-token grammar*, the *Stale-marker discriminator* sub-bullets, the seven-capability enumeration, *Wire serialisation*, and *Failure-surface contract* were all contiguous, so the "below"/"above"/"there" directional words and the "clause of the *Failure-surface contract*" nesting all resolved correctly within one page. f5e89f4 enforced a size cap by splitting that 967-line page into sibling shard files (`audit-resolution.md`, `audit-failures.md`, `audit-wire-and-canary.md`, `inventory-audit-intro.md`, `capability-inventory-items.md`, …) but carried the intra-page directional language over verbatim, turning each reference into a broken cross-file pointer. A later cross-ref sweep (23d28a1, F-0958) realigned link basenames on `inventory-audit-intro.md` but did not rewrite these directional words.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the "below"/"there" directional qualifiers in the *Citation-token grammar* and *Generated-output-in-src limitation* paragraphs on `audit-resolution.md` as cross-file forward-links to the *Stale-citation limitation* and *Substitution limitation* sub-bullets on `audit-failures.md`. Add `<a id>` anchors to those two sub-bullets on `audit-failures.md` so the links target the precise sub-bullets rather than the page.
