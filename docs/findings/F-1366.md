## Finding

`V11a`'s **Adds** describes the binder-model resolution as a "two-step binder-model resolution via loom's own exact-match resolver over `ctx.modelRegistry.getAvailable()` (no `model:` fallback)". The bare parenthetical "(no `model:` fallback)" asserts a negative constraint without naming what the fallback would otherwise be, leaving a reader of `V11a` in isolation to choose between two distinct readings: (A) when `bind_model:` (and the `looms.binderModel` setting) are absent, the resolver does **not** fall back to the loom's own frontmatter `model:` field; or (B) the resolver carries no internal default and simply returns unresolved.

The spec settles this unambiguously. `binder/binder-model-and-context.md` (the binder-model rule cited by the leaf) states there is "**no further fallback** — no 'tier-2' default, and the loom's own `model:` is not consulted (using it silently negates the cost premise that motivates a separate binder model)", and that the empty-chain case fails to load with `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved`. Reading (A) is therefore the intended meaning — `V6a` owns `model:` resolution as a separate seam, and the binder-model chain deliberately does not reach across to it.

The leaf prose should carry that meaning explicitly rather than leaning on the reader to infer it from the spec. The fix is wording-only; the Tests bullets already pin the actual two-step chain and the `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved` outcome, so no behavioural divergence is at stake.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V11a-binder-model-resolution.md`'s **Adds** field, replace the bare parenthetical "(no `model:` fallback)" with text that names the constraint and its outcome — that when `bind_model:` (and the `looms.binderModel` setting) are absent the resolver does not fall back to the loom's frontmatter `model:` value, and the empty chain fails the load with `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved`. Keep the wording consistent with the spec's "no further fallback" rule at `binder/binder-model-and-context.md` (Binder model section / `#binder-model-parse-rule`). The Tests and Ships-when fields already encode the behaviour and need no change.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader of `V11a` in isolation must consult the spec to learn what "(no `model:` fallback)" forbids. Both readings still forbid consulting `model:`, so no implementer divergence is likely; the cost is reduced readability of the Adds prose.
