## Problem

`audit-wire-and-canary.md`'s step-2(b) wiring paragraph permits the surface-set-closure audit's fail-closed gate to run either in-process to the harness (a Vitest case asserting on the audit's exit code) or as a spawned child process, and pins that the audit's record emission MUST reach a sink whose contents survive the harness wrapping. That sink-survival rule is written as an obligation on the audit, but the in-process variant pushes it onto the harness: test runners routinely capture, reformat, or buffer in-process `stdout`, so a green `npm test` can ship an `audit/<class>/<family>/<symptom>` stream the CI parser cannot consume while violating none of the audit's own obligations. Two preconditions the in-process variant needs to satisfy the wire contract end-to-end are unstated: raw audit stdout must pass through unmodified, and no harness-emitted line may share the `audit/<class>/<family>/<symptom>` leading-token shape the CI parser routes by.

**Introducing commits:** `e5cd514` — *pi-loom spec: resolve "T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit"* (2026-05-09).

The original `e5cd514` paragraph already stated the in-process / child-process choice, the empty-stdout prohibition, and the sink-survival rule — and already omitted the harness-side stdout-cleanliness precondition the finding names. A later edit (`9e53613`, the corpus-direction sweep that dropped `H1`/`plan_topics` references) and the `f5e89f4` split rewrote the surrounding prose but did not touch the precondition gap. The defect is therefore a single-commit inception in `e5cd514`, untouched by subsequent edits.

## Solution approach

Clarify the step-2(b) wiring paragraph to bind the in-process variant with a MUST: when the audit runs in-process, the harness MUST pass the audit's raw stdout bytes through to `npm test`'s stdout without per-test capture, reformatting, or reporter-side suppression of passing-test output, and no harness-emitted line may share the `audit/<class>/<family>/<symptom>` leading-token shape. State that an implementation unable to meet both preconditions under its chosen harness MUST use the child-process variant.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the *Wire serialisation* record-format clause — the in-process precondition is wiring-shape-specific, not a record-format change.
