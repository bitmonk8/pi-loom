## Problem

QRY-11 states the conversation history "including the malformed response and any tool calls that preceded it, stays intact." For a typed query the "malformed response" is the structured payload the model produces on the forced respond turn. But the reference runtime runs that forced respond turn off-session through pi-ai's `complete()` (per `implementation-notes.md`), attaching no turn to the driven session — so the malformed structured payload never enters the conversation respond-repair subsequently drives. The two statements cannot both hold for the off-session path, and QRY-12's follow-up templates re-inline `<ajv-summary>` and `<schema-json>` precisely because the prior attempt is absent from history; an implementer cannot tell whether a repair follow-up sees the prior payload, producing divergent prompts and outcomes on every repair attempt.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `53f5831` ("spec: rewrite query primitive, system prompts, and schema syntax", 2026-05-03) established the "history … including the malformed response … stays intact" wording; `82be125` ("spec: adopt P1 typed-query two-phase loop (Option A)", 2026-05-04) introduced the forced-respond-turn two-phase model; `7f75947` ("Typed-query forced-respond tool choice delivery channel", 2026-06-05) refined that turn to run off-session via `complete()` and "attaches no turn to the driven session".
**History:** The "stays intact" claim (`git log -G 'including the malformed response'`, traced via `git blame`/`git show`) was written for the original conversation-drive coercion model, in which the malformed response was an ordinary in-conversation turn. The two-phase loop adopted the next day relocated the malformed structured payload to a forced respond turn, and the later delivery-channel commit moved that turn off-session entirely. Neither subsequent commit reconciled QRY-11's "malformed response stays intact" wording against the off-session technique; the contradiction is the residue of the off-session model being layered onto unchanged inherited prose. The defect is the interaction, not any single commit.

## Solution approach

Narrow QRY-11's "stays intact" claim so it covers the in-session free-phase turns and tool calls, and state that the off-session forced-respond turn's malformed structured payload is not part of the conversation a respond-repair follow-up drives. Clarify that the QRY-12 follow-up's inlined `<ajv-summary>` and `<schema-json>` are what convey the prior failure to the model. Ensure the narrowed wording also covers the QRY-9 forced-respond non-compliance path, whose non-compliant output is likewise produced off-session and absent from the driven history.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: `implementation-notes.md`'s off-session `complete()` forced-respond technique — reconcile on the QRY-11 prose side.
