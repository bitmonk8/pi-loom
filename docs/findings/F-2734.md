## Problem

`V13c` (Query tool loop and typed two-phase) carries six distinct verification points in one leaf behind a single `Ships when` gate: the `CIO-4` free-phase tool-loop mechanics, the `QRY-14` `max_rounds:0` forced-respond boundary, the `QRY-16` untyped exhaustion path, the typed depth-6 cross-ceiling co-fire vector gated on `V5e` and `V16a` being operative, the `cka-47` `@`-query-dispatch cancellation checkpoint facet distributed off `V17c`, and the `ERR-13` completed-callee-finality live-carrier witness delegated from `V4f`. The plan's leaf-grouping principle is that each leaf is the smallest feature that can ship and be tested independently, yet `V13c` aggregates a core query-tool-loop feature, a multi-system cross-ceiling integration scenario, and two independently-owned resilience facets into one TDD pass. The depth-6 integration scenario is categorically harder to isolate than the core mechanics, so the leaf is slow to drive red-for-the-right-reason and a partial implementation can land with one facet never independently observed.

## Solution approach

Split `V13c` so each resulting leaf-pair covers one concern cluster. Keep the query-tool-loop core (`CIO-4` free phase, `QRY-14` `max_rounds:0`, `QRY-16` untyped exhaustion) on `V13c`/`V13c-T`; move the typed depth-6 cross-ceiling co-fire vector to a new leaf pair with `Deps` on `V13c`, `V5e`, `V16a`; and move the `cka-47` cancellation-checkpoint facet and the `ERR-13` live-carrier witness to a second new leaf pair with `Deps` on `V13c`, `V8a`, `V4f`. Link the new leaves under `docs/plan.md` §V13, re-point the `coverage-matrix.md` `cka-47`/`ERR-13` closing-leaf cells and the depth-6 `QRY` worked-example cells to their new owning leaves, and re-target the `V18d.Deps` and `H7a.Deps` edges off `V13c` to the new owning leaves.
