## Finding

The multi-error-assembly Tests bullet in `V7a` (and the identical bullet in `V7a-T`) describes the assembled `Diagnostic[]` as "ordered by `(file, line, col)` across an entry `.loom` and ≥2 transitively-imported `.warp` modules", then appends a parenthetical citing implementation-notes.md: "…which aggregates each visited file's diagnostics into the entry loom's drain **in this order**."

The phrase "in this order" has two readings. It could refer to the `(file, line, col)` ordering named earlier in the sentence — meaning the drain ends sorted by `(file, line, col)`, which requires a dedicated post-aggregation sort step. Or it could attach to the verb it follows ("aggregates … in this order"), reading as the load-pass *visitation* order — i.e. the walk emits diagnostics in their final order and no sort step is needed.

The spec resolves the ambiguity, and against the second reading. Implementation-notes.md (§Static-resolution load pass) states the pass "walks transitively from the entry loom across literal `invoke` paths and `.loom` entries in `tools:`" and "aggregates each visited file's diagnostics into the entry loom's drain (**sorted by `(file, line, col)`** per Diagnostics — Multi-error reporting)." The walk order is `invoke`/`tools:` traversal order, which is not `(file, line, col)` order; the final ordering is achieved by a global sort applied after aggregation. The leaf's "in this order" wording invites an implementer (or the `-T` test author) to conflate visit order with the final sorted order.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In the multi-error-assembly Tests bullet of both `V7a-diagnostics-primitive.md` and `V7a-T-diagnostics-primitive.md`, replace the trailing parenthetical clause "which aggregates each visited file's diagnostics into the entry loom's drain in this order" with wording that names the post-aggregation global sort, so the parenthetical matches implementation-notes.md. Concretely, state that the load pass aggregates each visited file's diagnostics into the entry loom's drain and then globally sorts the drain by `(file, line, col)` (the traversal order across `invoke`/`tools:` paths is independent of the final order). The spec is the authority here and is read-only for this fix; the change is confined to the two plan leaf files and must not alter the bullet's leading "ordered by `(file, line, col)`" end-state assertion or the Ships-when gate.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The bullet's leading clause still pins the end-state ("ordered by `(file, line, col)`"), and Ships-when gates on that ordering, so a careful implementer produces a correct leaf. But the "in this order" tail can lead an implementer to conclude the walk already yields the final order and omit the post-aggregation sort — producing a `Diagnostic[]` in traversal order on any corpus where visit order differs from `(file, line, col)` order, and risking a `-T` assertion written to the wrong mechanism.
