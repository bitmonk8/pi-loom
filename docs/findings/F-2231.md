## Problem

The `session_shutdown` handler's fixed five-sub-step teardown sequence rests on a no-throw-escapes-the-handler invariant so that sub-step 4 (close watchers) and sub-step 5 (detach forwarding listeners) always run and the handle/listener leaks the handler exists to prevent cannot occur. The **Per-step isolation** paragraph wraps sub-steps 1, 4, and 5 each in their own `try`/`catch`, but states that sub-steps 2 and 3 retain only their existing per-entry isolation rules (`Promise.allSettled` over `disposeBarrier`s) and do not emit `teardown-step-failed`. That carve-out ignores sub-step 3's three bare `Clock` calls — the handler-entry deadline capture `Clock.now()`, the cap-arming `Clock.setTimeout(SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS, ...)`, and the success-path `Clock.clearTimeout(...)` — none of which `Promise.allSettled` protects. A throw from the injected `Clock` seam therefore propagates out of the handler and skips sub-steps 4 and 5, the exact leak the no-throw-escapes invariant is meant to prevent.

The next day, `6ce416d` authored the **Per-step isolation** rule, and its diff (verified via `git show 6ce416d`) scoped the new `try`/`catch` obligation to sub-steps 1/4/5 while asserting "Sub-steps 2 and 3 retain their existing per-entry isolation rules (per-entry `try`/`catch` around `loomAbort.abort()`; `Promise.allSettled` over `disposeBarrier`s)." That characterisation treated `Promise.allSettled` as sub-step 3's *only* failure surface and so left the pre-existing bare `Clock` calls unprotected. The gap is the interaction of the two commits, not a defect in either alone; the no-throw-escapes invariant was later named and strengthened for the sub-step-2 stamp-throw path (`c3de47a10efeb493ac9a95b5f3c2aa5fb0e2e43b`, 2026-06-27, the relocation into `session-shutdown-semantics.md`), which sharpened the contradiction without closing the sub-step-3 hole. The durable fix addresses the interaction (bring sub-step 3's `Clock` calls under isolation), not a revert of either commit.

## Solution approach

Extend the **Per-step isolation** rule so sub-step 3's bounded-await `Clock` calls run under throw-isolation on the same footing as sub-steps 1/4/5: a throw from the deadline capture (`Clock.now()`), the cap-arming `Clock.setTimeout(...)`, or the success-path `Clock.clearTimeout(...)` is caught, MUST NOT prevent sub-steps 4 and 5 from running, and surfaces as one `loom/host/session-shutdown-teardown-step-failed` with `details.step: 3`. Add the sub-step-3 call-site labels to the closed `(code, details.step, details.call)` set in the same edit. Update the `loom/host/session-shutdown-teardown-step-failed` row in `code-registry-host.md` (and the `<step>` enumeration in `placeholder-rendering-b.md`) so `details.step` admits `3` and the "sub-steps 2 and 3 do not emit this code" wording excludes sub-step 2 only. Pin the degraded behaviour when the cap cannot be armed (deadline capture or `Clock.setTimeout` throws) so the handler still reaches sub-steps 4 and 5 rather than entering the await unbounded.
