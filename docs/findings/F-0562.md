## Finding

The `V13f` leaf (`docs/plan_topics/V13f-query-cancellation-routing.md`) and its `V13f-T` partner both title the deliverable `@`-query provider swallowing-handler **per-site routing**, but the V13f Adds field describes the role as "the per-site **witness** that the `@`-query dispatch Promise attaches its swallowing handler at the construction site." The two nouns are not synonyms: "routing" names the data-flow concept (the abandonable Promise routes through the `Checkpoint`-seam swallowing-handler substrate), while "witness" names an evidentiary / coverage role. The spec anchor the leaf cites (`cancellation.md — Race semantics — swallowing-handler attachment on every abandonable Promise`) uses a third noun, **attachment**.

The mismatch is not isolated to V13f. All four members of the "four-site routing set" that `V17a` delegates to — `V9o`, `V13f`, `V14f`, `V15h` — carry the identical pattern: each title (and `-T` title) reads "swallowing-handler per-site routing" while each Adds body introduces "the per-site witness." The set-level framing in `V17a` and in every leaf body ("four-site routing **set**") consistently uses "routing"; "witness" appears only inside the Adds prose of the four leaves.

Because the two terms denote different things and "witness" reads as a defined role on first use, a reader cannot tell whether the leaf delivers a routing wiring or a separate evidentiary artefact. The leaf's behaviour is unambiguous from the Tests/Ships-when bullets, so no implementer diverges, but the term drift defeats any naming check keyed on a single canonical noun and is replicated four times.

There is no commit that diverged a previously-consistent term; the drift is original to the template the four leaves share.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — harmonise the set on "per-site witness"

**Approach:** Make the leaf titles match the Adds bodies by changing the four titles (and four `-T` titles) from "swallowing-handler per-site routing" to "swallowing-handler per-site witness."

**Plan edits:** Retitle `V9o`, `V13f`, `V14f`, `V15h` and their `-T` partners; bodies already say "witness" and need no change. The "four-site routing **set**" phrasing in each Adds and in `V17a` would then read against title noun "witness" — adjust the set name to "four-site witness set" for coherence, or leave it as the set's own label.

**Spec edits:** None.

**Pros:** Keeps the evidentiary meaning the bodies already carry; bodies need no rewrite.
**Cons:** "Witness" diverges further from the spec's own noun ("attachment") and from the "four-site routing set" label used in `V17a` and every body; eight titles change.
**Risks:** Renaming the set requires touching `V17a` and four Adds paragraphs in addition to the titles.

### Option B — harmonise the set on "per-site routing"

**Approach:** Keep the titles as-is and replace the standalone noun "witness" in each Adds body with "routing", rephrasing so the evidentiary nuance is expressed descriptively rather than via a competing noun (e.g. "the per-site routing that witnesses that the `@`-query dispatch Promise attaches its swallowing handler …").

**Plan edits:** In the Adds of `V9o`, `V13f`, `V14f`, `V15h`, change the appositive "the per-site witness that …" to a "routing"-anchored phrasing; titles, `-T` titles, the "four-site routing set" label, and `V17a` are untouched.

**Spec edits:** None.

**Pros:** Aligns with the title noun already present in all eight headings and with the "four-site routing set" framing in `V17a` and every body; titles and `V17a` need no change; lowest churn.
**Cons:** Drops "witness" as a noun; the evidentiary sense must be carried by a verb/relative clause.

### Recommendation

Take Option B: in the Adds field of each of `V9o`, `V13f`, `V14f`, `V15h`, strike the standalone noun "witness" and express the role in terms of "routing" (the noun already used in all eight titles, in the per-leaf "four-site routing set" sentence, and in `V17a`). Concretely, in `V13f` Adds replace "— the per-site witness that the `@`-query dispatch Promise attaches its swallowing handler at the construction site and suppresses a late settlement along all three side channels." with a "routing"-anchored phrasing that keeps the same observable obligation (the Promise attaches its swallowing handler at the construction site and suppresses late settlement on all three side channels), and apply the parallel edit to the three sibling Adds. Resolve all four sibling leaves together so the set stays internally consistent; do not edit `cancellation.md` (the spec term "attachment" stays its own; the plan leaves reference it via the existing anchor and need no spec change).

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Pure wording drift: the leaf title and body name one role with two non-synonymous nouns, and a third (the spec's "attachment") sits in the cited anchor. No implementer produces a divergent leaf, but the inconsistency is replicated across four sibling leaves and any naming check keyed on a single canonical term will miss the variant.
