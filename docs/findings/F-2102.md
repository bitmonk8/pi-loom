## Finding

The positive-direction build-time gate defined at `version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a) (anchor `#bump-step-2-positive`) — which imports `@earendil-works/pi-coding-agent` at the candidate version and asserts each of the seven named SDK capabilities is present on the imported namespace — is referred to under inconsistent names across the pages that cite it. Two of those references collide most visibly:

- `capability-inventory-items.md`, item 3: *"Step 2(a)'s **seven-capability surface-inventory assertion** does not enumerate `SessionManager.inMemory` at all."*
- `registration-steps.md`, step 4: *"Neither the member nor that post-invalidation throw behaviour is factory-probed or enumerated by step 2(a)'s **seven-capability literal-read assertion**…"*

Both phrases name the same assertion and both explicitly anchor it to "step 2(a)", but one emphasises the SDK surface being inventoried ("surface-inventory") and the other emphasises the mechanism ("literal-read"). The divergence is not confined to these two sites: the same assertion is additionally called the *"build-time SDK surface-inventory assertion"* (`capability-inventory-items.md` re-validation paragraph; `host-prerequisites.md` PIC-34), the *"positive-direction literal-read assertion"* (`inventory-audit-intro.md`), and the bare *"literal-read assertion"* at the definition site itself (`version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a)), while the enclosing numbered step is headed *"Re-run the SDK surface-inventory tests."* The corpus uses both "surface-inventory" and "literal-read" for the same step-2(a) artefact with no declared equivalence.

A single canonical name should be adopted for this assertion and used at every cross-reference, so a reader following a link from one page to another sees one name rather than having to infer that "seven-capability surface-inventory assertion" and "seven-capability literal-read assertion" denote the same gate.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** 158ed09 (2026-06-05), 252acda (2026-06-05)
**History:** The two divergent "seven-capability … assertion" phrasings were introduced by two independent same-day commits, neither of which alone constitutes the defect. `158ed09` ("resolve 'Missing owning-declaration pins for bare Pi SDK symbols'") added the *"seven-capability literal-read assertion"* phrasing in `registration-steps.md` step 4; `252acda` ("resolve 'SessionManager.inMemory transcript-privacy guarantee is unverified'") added the *"seven-capability surface-inventory assertion"* phrasing in `capability-inventory-items.md` item 3 (`git log -S` returns a single introducing commit for each phrase, confirming each was coined — not edited — at that commit). The inconsistency is the product of the two edits not coordinating on a single name; it sits on top of a pre-existing split at the definition site, where the assertion was already described both as a "literal-read" (step-2(a) prose) and as part of the "SDK surface-inventory tests" (step-2 heading).

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Adopt one canonical name for the step-2(a) positive-direction assertion and use it verbatim at every cross-reference. Standardise on **"seven-capability SDK surface-inventory assertion"** (and its `build-time SDK surface-inventory assertion` long form where "build-time" is wanted): it matches the numbered step heading ("Re-run the SDK surface-inventory tests"), the `host-prerequisites.md` PIC-34 wording, and the `capability-inventory-items.md` re-validation paragraph, which together are the plurality of existing usages.

Concretely:

- In `registration-steps.md` step 4, change *"step 2(a)'s seven-capability literal-read assertion"* to *"step 2(a)'s seven-capability SDK surface-inventory assertion."*
- Leave `capability-inventory-items.md` item 3 as-is (already uses the canonical form) and confirm its re-validation paragraph's "build-time SDK surface-inventory assertion" matches.
- Where "literal-read" is genuinely describing the *mechanism* (the step-2(a) definition prose: "Positive direction — literal-read of the imported namespace"), it may remain as a mechanism description; the constraint is only that the *cross-reference label* for the assertion be uniform. Optionally align the bare/positive-direction "literal-read assertion" references in `inventory-audit-intro.md` to the canonical label for full consistency.

Edge case for the implementer: the corpus also carries a distinct **`peerDependencies` literal-read assertion** and **`engines.node` literal-read assertion** (host-prerequisites.md PIC-34, version-bump-step2b.md). Those are separate gates from the seven-capability SDK-surface assertion — do not collapse their names into the canonical name chosen here.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader crossing from `capability-inventory-items.md` to `registration-steps.md` (or to the other citing pages) encounters two or more names for one build-time assertion with no stated equivalence. Both citations explicitly anchor to "step 2(a)", so the antecedent is recoverable and no implementer is expected to build two gates; the cost is interpretive friction, not divergent implementation.
