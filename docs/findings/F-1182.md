## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/invocation.md` declares `<a id="argument-binding"></a>` on line 24, immediately followed by `<a id="typed-return"></a>` on line 26 and the paragraph **Typed return.** on line 28. The paragraph actually labelled **Argument binding.** does not begin until line 38 — three normative paragraphs further down (Typed return, *Empty-tail callee compatibility*, *Final-value propagation across callees* all intervene). Every external link to `invocation.md#argument-binding` therefore scrolls the reader to the typed-return discussion rather than to the argument-binding rule.

The anchor was introduced by commit `333ba2f` (the *Static resolution* redesign) at what was then the correct slot — at that revision the **Argument binding** paragraph was the next paragraph after the anchor. Subsequent edits (notably `52219c4`, "Address all 41 spec-review findings", which inserted the `<a id="typed-return"></a>` anchor and the new *Typed return* / *Empty-tail* / *Final-value propagation* paragraphs at the slot directly below the existing anchor) did not move `<a id="argument-binding">` down past the new content, so the anchor was silently orphaned above paragraphs it does not name.

The mis-placement is observable at five live in-corpus links — two diagnostic-registry rows (`loom/parse/invoke-arity-too-few`, `loom/parse/invoke-arity-too-many`), one `params:` defaults paragraph, the Pi-tool argument-shape paragraph, and the V15f plan leaf pair — every one of which lands a reader following "Argument binding" / "Argument arity" link text on the typed-return discussion instead.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `333ba2f` (2026-05-04, "spec: define static resolution for invoke/tools callees (Option C)"); `52219c4` (2026-05-06, "Address all 41 spec-review findings")
**History:** `git log -S '<a id="argument-binding">' -- docs/spec_topics/invocation.md` (and its pre-move path `spec_topics/invocation.md`) returns a single commit: `333ba2f`, which added the anchor as part of the *Static resolution* redesign. At that revision the anchor was directly above the **Argument binding.** paragraph and the cross-link `[Argument binding](#argument-binding)` from the new §"Static resolution" resolved correctly (`git show 333ba2f:spec_topics/invocation.md` lines 18–22 confirm the anchor and the bolded paragraph were adjacent). `git log -S '<a id="typed-return">'` returns `52219c4`, which inserted the `<a id="typed-return"></a>` anchor immediately below the existing `<a id="argument-binding"></a>` and — in the same commit — promoted **Typed return.** into a standalone paragraph and added the *Empty-tail callee compatibility* and *Final-value propagation across callees* paragraphs into the slot between the two anchors and the **Argument binding.** label. Neither commit alone produced the defect: `333ba2f` placed the anchor correctly given its contemporaneous content, and `52219c4` was an unrelated spec-review consolidation that inserted three new paragraphs at a slot that happened to live below the *argument-binding* anchor. The defect is the joint product — `52219c4` did not move `<a id="argument-binding">` down past the content it added, so the anchor was orphaned above paragraphs it does not name. No commit is individually responsible, and reverting either is not the right fix.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move `<a id="argument-binding"></a>` down to immediately precede the **Argument binding.** paragraph (currently line 38), leaving `<a id="typed-return"></a>` in its current slot above **Typed return.** (line 28). After the move the two anchor blocks sit each above the paragraph whose label they name; cross-links and the §"Static resolution" back-reference `[Argument binding](#argument-binding)` (line 20) resolve to the paragraph their text promises.

No cross-file edits are required — every external link already spells `#argument-binding` and the rename of the anchor's target paragraph is exactly what those links want. The §"Final-value propagation across callees" `<a id="final-value-propagation"></a>` anchor (currently above line 36) is correctly placed and is not affected. The renaming-the-label-to-`argument-type-checking` alternative documented in `docs/findings/F-0725.md` is a separate, broader change — it addresses a *naming collision* with the slash-command binder, not the anchor's *position* — and is out of scope for this finding; F-0725 owns it.

Edge cases for the implementer:
- Preserve the blank lines around the moved anchor block so the surrounding markdown paragraph boundaries do not shift.
- Do not re-order the four anchors that already live in this section (`static-resolution`, `inv-1`, `typed-return`, `final-value-propagation`) — only the position of `argument-binding` changes.
- After the move, re-grep the corpus for `#argument-binding` to confirm every link still resolves; the count should be unchanged (the link surface is just retargeted to the correct paragraph by the move).

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Every cross-file link that points at `invocation.md#argument-binding` lands the reader on the *Typed return* paragraph (and the two paragraphs beneath it) rather than on the argument-binding rule, so a diagnostic-registry reader chasing the spec home for `invoke-arity-too-few` reads about typed-return AJV behaviour and has to scroll three paragraphs to reach the actual binding rule. No implementer is misled into wrong behaviour — the body prose disambiguates — but the corpus's "click the anchor to read the canonical rule" navigation is broken at four documented cross-link sites plus the V15f plan leaf.
