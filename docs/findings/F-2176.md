## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/implementation-notes.md` is a page whose title and framing ("This page collects the parser, runtime, and orientation notes") signal informative, implementer-facing material, yet it carries live normative obligations interleaved with explicitly non-normative content, and the two classes are separated only at the bullet level — never grouped or fenced at section level.

The normative items include the Parser bullet's `**Contract.**` ("The parser MUST emit diagnostics matching [Diagnostics]…, including multi-error reporting…before failing") and the Clock bullet's prose obligation ("The runtime MUST NOT call `Date.now`, `performance.now`, `Date.prototype.getTime`, or the global `setTimeout` / `clearTimeout` outside the `WallClock` adapter (a conforming implementation MUST carry a build-time check rejecting direct lexical references…)"). The non-normative items are bullets explicitly tagged `**Implementation hint (non-normative).**` (the Chevrotain parser-toolkit note and the AJV-flags note).

Both MUST obligations are restatements: the Clock MUST NOT and its build-time-check conformance criterion are owned verbatim by `PIC-12` in `pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-services.md` (and the Implementation Notes bullet itself cites "per the conformance criterion in PIC-12"); the parser MUST is owned by `diagnostics.md`. So the page hosts duplicated normative text whose canonical homes are elsewhere, with no section heading marking which bullets bind an implementer and which merely advise. A reader skimming a page titled "Implementation Notes" can reasonably treat the whole page as advisory and miss the MUSTs, and the verbatim restatement of PIC-12's clock ban carries a drift risk: the two copies can fall out of sync as PIC-12 evolves.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** 27583dd (2026-05-04, "resolve 'Implementation toolkit prescribed in place of behavioural contract'"); reinforced by bc50e69 (2026-05-05, "H2 Clock seam") and 4556623 (2026-06-25, "F-1178 — PIC-12 timing-primitive ban gets observable build-time conformance criterion")
**History:** The page was carved out of `spec.md` at fecb504 (2026-05-04, "Split spec.md and plan.md into per-topic / per-phase files"). The normative/non-normative co-location was not present as a single defect-introducing edit; it accreted. Commit 27583dd introduced the first `**Contract.**` (normative MUST) bullet sitting beside an explicitly-labelled `**Implementation hint (non-normative).**` bullet on the Parser entry — establishing the page's bullet-level labelling device in place of section-level grouping. bc50e69 added the Clock seam bullet, and 4556623 added the "MUST carry a build-time check" restatement of PIC-12 into the Clock bullet's prose. The defect is therefore the cumulative interaction of these edits: each individually labelled its own bullet, but no edit ever introduced a section-level normative/non-normative boundary, and the page's title kept signalling "notes." `git log -S` confirms each fragment entered at the commits above; the page has never had a `### Conformance requirements` / `### Non-normative hints` split.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Section-level normativity grouping

- **Approach:** Keep the obligations on the page but fence them. Introduce explicit subheadings (e.g. `### Conformance requirements` and `### Non-normative hints`, or per-section `**Normative**` / `**Non-normative**` group labels) so the Parser/Clock MUST bullets sit under a clearly normative heading and the Chevrotain/AJV hints sit under a clearly non-normative one.
- **Spec edits:** Add the two subheadings to `implementation-notes.md`; move the `**Contract.**` and Clock-MUST bullets under the normative heading and the `**Implementation hint (non-normative).**` bullets under the non-normative heading. No wording change to the obligations.
- **Pros:** Smallest behavioural-text churn; preserves the page as a single reading surface for an implementer; resolves the skim-past-MUST risk.
- **Cons:** Leaves the PIC-12 clock-ban restatement duplicated on two pages, so the drift risk persists; perpetuates Implementation Notes as a normative owner of restated text, which sits against the corpus's owner/aggregator discipline (informative pages forward-link the page that owns the contract).
- **Risks:** Future PIC-12 edits must remember to re-sync the restated copy here.

### Option B — Demote restated MUSTs to forward-only citations

- **Approach:** Recognise that both MUSTs are restatements whose canonical owners are PIC-12 (clock primitive ban + build-time check) and `diagnostics.md` (parser multi-error emission). Replace the restated obligation text with forward-only citations, leaving Implementation Notes genuinely non-normative. The descriptive (non-normative) summary of the Clock DI seam and its production wiring can remain; only the verbatim MUST restatement is demoted to "owned by PIC-12 / Diagnostics."
- **Spec edits:** In the Parser bullet, replace "The parser MUST emit diagnostics matching [Diagnostics]…" with a non-normative pointer ("The parser's diagnostic-emission obligations — multi-error reporting across the file and its transitively imported `.warp` modules — are owned by [Diagnostics]"). In the Clock bullet, drop the verbatim "MUST NOT call `Date.now`…" / "MUST carry a build-time check" sentences in favour of "the timing-primitive ban and its build-time-check conformance criterion are owned by [PIC-12]." Retain the seam-description prose as non-normative.
- **Pros:** Eliminates the cross-page duplication and the drift risk; aligns with the corpus convention that obligations live on one owning page and informative pages forward-link it (the same discipline applied by the "DI-seam vs reference-implementation placement boundary" resolution); makes Implementation Notes uniformly non-normative, matching its title.
- **Cons:** A reader who wants the full clock contract must follow a link rather than read it inline; slightly more wording change than Option A.
- **Risks:** Must verify no other page relies on `implementation-notes.md` as the citable home for these MUSTs (the obligations are anchored at PIC-12 and Diagnostics, so the citation surface is preserved).

### Recommendation

Adopt **Option B**. Both MUSTs are already restatements that cite their canonical owners, so demoting them to forward-only citations removes the duplication/drift risk and makes the page's normativity match its title, consistent with the corpus's owner/aggregator discipline. Watch one edge case: keep the non-normative Clock-seam description (members, production `WallClock` wiring, `FakeClock` test behaviour) — only the MUST sentences move; do not strip the explanatory prose that gives the hint bullets their context.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader treating the page as wholly advisory (consistent with its title) can overlook the parser and clock MUSTs, and the verbatim copy of PIC-12's timing-primitive ban can drift from its canonical owner as PIC-12 is edited. Behaviour does not diverge for an implementer who reads carefully — the bullets are individually labelled and cite their owners — so this is an organisation/normativity-clarity gap, not a correctness defect.
