## Finding

Across the spec corpus, "event" is a settled term of art for Pi-subscription events delivered through `pi.on(...)` / session-local `subscribe` channels — `session_start`, `session_shutdown`, `resources_discover`, `agent_end`. The glossary reinforces this (the `always-log set` entry routes via `details: { event: RuntimeEvent }`; `occurrence` vs. `origin` is defined as a "runtime event"), and the registration-steps and extension-bootstrap pages use `event` exclusively for these subscriptions.

`binder-inference.md`'s second paragraph reuses the same word for two ordinary lifecycle occurrences: *"The binder has two distinct **events** that occur at different phases: binder-*model* resolution runs at loom-load time …, whereas each `complete(...)` call is issued per slash dispatch."* Neither of these is a Pi subscription — they are execution phases of the binder. The collision is sharpened by the very next sentence in the same paragraph, which correctly uses the term of art ("not an `agent_end` event — the `pi.on("agent_end", …)` … channels … play no part in the binder call"). A reader who carries the term-of-art meaning into "two distinct events" is invited to look for a subscription model that does not exist for the binder call.

The fix is a one-word terminology correction: name the two binder occurrences "phases" (the paragraph already says they "occur at different phases"), reserving "event" for Pi subscriptions.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 740130f — pi-loom spec: resolve "Binder complete() call execution phase contradicts cancellation/argument wiring" (2026-06-06, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The "two distinct events that occur at different phases" sentence did not exist before 740130f; the prior text read "it runs at loom-load time before any conversation exists". That commit rewrote the opening to distinguish the load-time binder-model resolution from the per-dispatch `complete(...)` call and, in doing so, introduced the word "events" for those two occurrences — the term that collides with the established Pi-subscription term of art. The defect was introduced solely by this single edit; `git log -S "two distinct events"` and `git log -G "binder has two distinct"` localise it to that one commit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `binder-inference.md`'s second paragraph, replace "The binder has two distinct **events** that occur at different phases" with "The binder has two distinct **phases**" (dropping the now-redundant "that occur at different phases"). Leave the rest of the paragraph unchanged — its later use of "`agent_end` event" is correct term-of-art usage and must stay. Do not introduce a new coined term for the binder phases; "phase" is descriptive and already used in this exact sentence. Reserve "event" for Pi subscriptions throughout.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Pure wording: reusing "event" for binder phases risks a momentary misread, but the same paragraph explicitly disambiguates by stating the binder consumes no `pi.on`/`subscribe` channels, so no implementer behaviour diverges. No runtime contract or acceptance criterion is affected.
