## Finding

The final clause of the **Concurrency** paragraph in `docs/spec_topics/tool-calls.md` reads:

> The schema cache (see [Implementation Notes — Runtime](./implementation-notes.md)) is read-mostly and safe to share across concurrent invocations; compiled validators are immutable post-compile and cache writes only occur on initial compile or file-watcher invalidation.

These two sentences state properties of an implementation-internal data structure (the schema-validator cache and its compiled validators) in terms — *"read-mostly"*, *"safe to share"*, *"immutable post-compile"* — that no conformance test can observe. The cache is not on any normative surface: callers cannot read it, the lowering pipeline does not expose it, and the canonical-form / cache-collision behaviour that *is* observable is owned separately by `host-interfaces-services.md#pic-11` and the canonical-schema-hash recipe. The only externally visible consequence the claim alludes to — that concurrent `.loom`-callable adapter invocations into the same callee don't corrupt one another — is already pinned by the preceding sentences of the same paragraph (each invocation gets its own `AgentSession`, captured callable set, derived `AbortSignal`, and private transcript) and is exercised by the parallel-tool-batch correctness gate in `V14b`.

Furthermore, the same prose appears almost verbatim in `docs/spec_topics/implementation-notes.md` under the *Per-invocation single-threaded execution* bullet ("The schema-validator cache and the file-watcher are shared across invocations but are read-mostly: compiled validators are immutable post-compile and cache writes only occur on initial compile or watcher-driven invalidation, so concurrent reads from sibling invocations are safe without locking"). That bullet is the proper home for non-normative reference-implementation properties; carrying the duplicate in `tool-calls.md` adds prose without adding contract.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Delete the two sentences from the **Concurrency** paragraph in `tool-calls.md` outright. The cross-link to `implementation-notes.md` (already present in the deleted sentence as `see [Implementation Notes — Runtime](./implementation-notes.md)`) should be preserved by re-anchoring it on whichever neighbouring sentence still benefits from pointing at the runtime notes — e.g. append "see [Implementation Notes — Runtime](./implementation-notes.md)" as a bare parenthetical to the sentence ending "two independent invocations on the event loop." The kept-in-`implementation-notes.md` wording already says the same thing in the right register (non-normative implementation colour).

Do **not** rewrite the clause as a non-normative note in `tool-calls.md`. That would leave the duplicate in place; the point of the demotion is that `implementation-notes.md` already owns it.

Edge cases for the editor:

- Verify no plan leaf or test fixture greps for the literal strings *"safe to share"* or *"immutable post-compile"*. A scan of `docs/plan_topics/` and `docs/plan.md` finds zero hits today, so the deletion has no downstream citation breakage.
- Leave `implementation-notes.md`'s wording alone — it is the surviving copy.
- The anchor `<a id="concurrency"></a>` is referenced from `V14b-tool-calls-parallel-batch.md` and `coverage-matrix.md` (`cka-13`); keep the anchor intact.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reviewer scanning `tool-calls.md` for normative obligations encounters an untestable quality claim about runtime-internal state, has to decide whether it is intended as a MUST, and finds the same content in `implementation-notes.md`. No implementer is misled; the spec is merely longer and less crisp than it needs to be.
