## Problem

GOV-1 *Canonical form* pins the anchor shape `**PREFIX-N.**` and the *Dual-form layout* witness regex `<a id="prefix-n"></a> \*\*PREFIX-N\.\*\*`, declared normative for any third-party REQ-ID extractor. The corpus, however, overwhelmingly uses the titled PIC form `**PIC-N. Title.**` and the parenthetical governance form `**GOV-N (desc).**`, neither of which matches because the regex requires the closing `**` immediately after the period that follows `PREFIX-N`. Around 55 anchor sites are invisible to the witness regex — 37 of 55 PIC sites, plus every GOV anchor except `**GOV-3.**`, including GOV-1's own `**GOV-1 (anchor placement).**`. A reviewer auditing through the projected `ids.md` index therefore cannot confirm a cited ID is defined, and a spec-conformant extractor silently drops those obligations.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** ce1af28 — pi-loom spec: resolve "GOV-1 leaves anchor placement ambiguous in two ways H6 cannot mechanically resolve" (2026-05-06, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** Commit ce1af28 introduced the GOV-1 *Canonical form* rule (`**PREFIX-N.**`, "bold-with-period decoration is normative") together with the dual-form witness regex `<a id="prefix-n"></a> \*\*PREFIX-N\.\*\*`, and in the same diff wrote GOV-1's own anchor in the contradicting parenthetical form `**GOV-1 (anchor placement).**`. The rule therefore never matched the corpus it governs. Subsequent commits propagated the divergence — the titled `**PIC-N. Title.**` form appears across the PIC subtree from at least 7d7bfe2 (2026-06-01) and was reinforced by later relocations such as 2d11ac9 (2026-06-25) — but the governing contradiction traces to the canonical-form rule's introduction in ce1af28.

## Solution approach

Rewrite GOV-1 *Canonical form* and the *Dual-form layout* witness regex in `req-id-prefix-table-active-a.md` so the bold-with-period span admits an optional descriptive remainder between `PREFIX-N` and its terminating period, covering both the titled PIC form `**PIC-N. Title.**` and the parenthetical governance form `**GOV-N (desc).**`. Preserve the bold-with-period decoration as the discriminator between an anchor definition and a back-reference.

## Solution constraints

- The widened pattern MUST still require a period immediately before the closing `**`, so non-bold references and period-less bold back-references (e.g. `**GOV-7**`) remain non-matching.
- The widened pattern MUST continue to match GOV-16's inline-label anchors, which inherit this witness regex by reference for the inline-label class.
