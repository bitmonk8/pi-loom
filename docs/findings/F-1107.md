## Problem

The "Edge cases" clause in the Tests bullet of both `V17b` and `V17b-T` describes the first-source-wins one-shot guard with the phrase "(a throw on a re-entrant second trigger must not re-stamp the reason)". The verb "re-stamp" is undefined anywhere in the plan corpus and admits two distinct test vectors: that the abort reason set by the first source must not be overwritten, or that the throw-trap must not emit `loom/runtime/internal-error` a second time for the re-entrant trigger. A test-author who picks the second reading writes a test the leaf did not intend.

Commit `cb8737a` ("resolve \"Systemic leaf over-bundling…\"", 2026-06-12) peeled the throw-trap out of V17a into the new `V17b`/`V17b-T` leaves, copying the clause verbatim; it relocated the defect rather than introducing or altering it. The term has been undefined since the contract sentence was first authored in `0bc84dd`.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the undefined "re-stamp the reason" parenthetical in the "Edge cases" clause of both `V17b` and `V17b-T` to name the observable directly: a re-entrant second trigger must not overwrite the abort reason already set by the first source. This binds the clause to the reason-identity reading, consistent with V17a's CNCL-4 ("the first source's reason wins under the one-shot guard").

## Solution constraints

- The clause is verbatim-identical across the paired `V17b` / `V17b-T` set today; apply the rewrite to both so they remain in agreement.
