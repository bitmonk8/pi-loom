## Problem

The editorial-review checklist in `version-bump-step2.md` assigns the bump contributor ongoing currency of a "maintained corpus of captured provider responses" across items (i), (u), (aa), (ab), and (af), but no spec page establishes that corpus, names its in-repo location, names its owning section, or pins its initial provisioning. The maintenance obligation references a presupposed artefact whose existence and owner the spec never asserts. A conformant `pass` recorded against those items therefore carries no signal about whether the corpus the audit names is the same artefact the contributor actually maintained — there is nothing to compare the maintenance step against.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `aebec4e` (2026-06-04) — "Pi version-bump gate cannot detect three unstated host/provider presuppositions"; `2ecf650` (2026-06-05) — "Re-validating provider-owned behaviour has no enumerated data source or owner"; `e63ba6c` (2026-06-05) — "Per-provider complete() forced-tool re-validation gate"; `3683345` (2026-06-06) — "Binder relies on three unpinned complete() behaviours"; `44782e9` (2026-06-06) — "TransportError catch-all / pi-ai provider-error surface".
**History:** `aebec4e` first introduced item (i) as a bare "re-run the provider-error fixtures" obligation with no corpus framing. `2ecf650` then patched item (i) to add the *maintained corpus of captured provider error bodies* sentence and the *contributor performing the bump is responsible for keeping it current* clause, and folded the same shape into item (u). Items (aa)/(ab) (`3683345`) and (af) (`44782e9`) landed within hours, each prescribing per-provider fixture re-runs against the same four-provider set, two of them (per F-0834) without the corpus-not-live clause. None of these commits added a spec-side anchor establishing the corpus or naming its owning leaf — the maintenance language accreted item-by-item while the establishment side stayed unstated. The defect is the multi-commit interaction: each individual landing extended a *maintain* obligation without ever depositing a matching *establish* anchor, so the corpus referent grew by accretion into a presupposed-but-unowned artefact.

## Solution approach

Add an establishing anchor for the captured-response corpus that names its in-repo location, its owning surface, and its initial provisioning. Rewrite the "maintained corpus of captured … responses in the repo's `npm test` suite" references in `version-bump-step2.md` items (i), (u), (aa), (ab), and (af) to cite that anchor, retaining the contributor's re-capture-on-API-version-change clause as the *maintain* obligation. Reconcile `provider-error-mapping.md`'s *Authoritative source* paragraph to cite the same anchor so the two restatements track one referent.
