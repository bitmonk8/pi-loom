## Finding

The V3 slice listing in `docs/plan.md` runs `V3a … V3d` and then jumps straight to `V3f`, and the V13 slice listing runs `V13a … V13d` and then jumps to `V13f`. Neither `V3e` nor `V13e` appears anywhere in `docs/plan.md`, in `docs/plan_topics/`, or in any other plan file, and neither slice section carries a note explaining the gap.

The mechanical gates that consume leaf IDs are unaffected — `H5b`'s Deps use explicit ID ranges rather than assuming dense lettering, so the gap does not break dependency expansion or coverage reconciliation. The defect is purely a reader-comprehension one: encountering `V3d → V3f` (or `V13d → V13f`), a reader cannot tell whether the missing letter was a leaf that was retired, a letter that was deliberately reserved and never used, or a future leaf that has yet to be authored. That ambiguity is sharpened by the plan's `no-invented-ids` posture: a reader unsure whether `V3e` is "reserved" or "free" has no signal to resolve it.

The two gaps have different provenance (one letter was retired after a real leaf was split away; the other was skipped at the moment a new leaf was added), so a single uniform note is not strictly accurate for both — the note in each section should reflect that section's actual situation.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** eaa5000 — pi-loom plan: resolve "V13c bundles four independent spec obligations into one leaf" (2026-06-16, Thomas Andersen); 2a4f797 — pi-loom plan: resolve "V3e bundles three independent stdlib type namespaces" (2026-06-18, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The two holes were each opened by a separate later commit, neither of which left a note. The `V13e` gap dates to eaa5000 (2026-06-16), which split the over-bundled `V13c` and inserted `V13f` directly after `V13d`, skipping the `V13e` letter — `V13e` never existed as a leaf in `docs/plan.md`. The `V3e` gap dates to 2a4f797 (2026-06-18), where the pre-existing `V3e` ("Expression stdlib members") leaf was split into `V3f`/`V3g`/`V3h` and the `V3e` letter was retired rather than reassigned. Both gaps are present in the current `docs/plan.md`.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add a brief retirement/reservation note to each affected slice section in `docs/plan.md`, placed in the V3 and V13 slice listings so it reads adjacent to the gap.

- In the V3 slice section (between the `V3d` and `V3f` list entries, or as a trailing note on the section), record that `V3e` was the former "Expression stdlib members" leaf, now split into `V3f`–`V3h`, and that the `V3e` letter is retired and not reassigned. Example content: `V3e (former "Expression stdlib members") was split into V3f–V3h; the letter is retired, not reassigned.`
- In the V13 slice section (between the `V13d` and `V13f` list entries, or as a trailing note on the section), record that the `V13e` letter was never used and is reserved, not reassigned. Example content: `V13e was never used; the letter is reserved, not reassigned.`

The two notes are independently worded because the underlying provenance differs (V3e retired after a split; V13e skipped on insertion); do not collapse them into one identical sentence that misstates either case. The fix is confined to `docs/plan.md`; the spec and all leaf files are read-only for it.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader of the plan cannot disambiguate the `V3e`/`V13e` gaps between "retired", "reserved-unused", and "future-unauthored", which slows comprehension of the ID scheme and undermines the `no-invented-ids` signal (an implementer cannot tell whether the letter is safe to claim). No coverage gate, dependency edge, or leaf pickup is blocked, so nothing ships incorrectly as a result.
