## Finding

`H3a` Adds states that it declares each seam interface's member signatures "sourced from the seam-contract pages the `V8*` leaves (`V8a`â€“`V8e`) also cite", then adds a parenthetical: "the `Checkpoint` contract is anchored at `host-interfaces-services.md#checkpoint-seam`, even though `V8a` lists `Checkpoint` under `host-interfaces-core.md`". The contrastive "even though" clause sets up a discrepancy between the two leaves.

That discrepancy does not exist. `V8a`'s current `Spec.` field cites `host-interfaces-services.md` and `implementation-notes.md` â€” it does **not** cite `host-interfaces-core.md`. The `Checkpoint` seam (PIC-10, anchor `#checkpoint-seam`) lives entirely in `host-interfaces-services.md`; `host-interfaces-core.md` contains no PIC-10 / Checkpoint-seam content. So both `H3a` and `V8a` point at the same page (`host-interfaces-services.md`) for the `Checkpoint` contract, and the parenthetical's claim about `V8a` is simply false.

The clause is a stale artefact: when it was written, `V8a` did cite `host-interfaces-core.md`; that citation was subsequently corrected to `host-interfaces-services.md`, but the explanatory parenthetical in `H3a` was not updated to match. As it stands it directs a reader toward a page (`core.md`) that neither defines the Checkpoint contract nor is cited by `V8a`, and invents a contradiction the reader may try to reconcile.
