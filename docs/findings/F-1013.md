## Problem

`runtime-event-channel.md` makes three normative statements about a terminal `validation` outcome (respond-repair exhaustion) that cannot all hold simultaneously. The **Runtime event channel** exclusion paragraph and the **Discard-site disposition** paragraph list `validation` among the four kinds deliberately excluded from the always-log set, implying a terminal `validation` `Err` emits nothing. The **Deduplication and lifetime rules** second bullet and the `RuntimeEvent.attempts` field comment state that respond-repair exhaustion emits exactly one terminal `validation` event carrying `attempts`, and PIC-1 clauses (c)(i)/(d) treat that same `validation` event as the only V1 surface that can carry a non-empty `masked` (`["ceiling#2"]`). The exclusion contradicts both other sites, so a respond-repair-exhaustion conformance fixture cannot decide the exactly-once or PIC-1-(d) reachable-predicate outcome against a defined ground truth.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (single-commit)

**Introducing commit:** `b3bc4ce` — *spec: pin failure-observability surface (Cluster A, Option C)* (2026-05-04)

**History:** `git log -S` for both halves of the contradiction lands on `b3bc4ce` as the originating edit. That commit created the always-log channel from scratch in the (then-monolithic) `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` and, in a single hunk, introduced both the exclusion paragraph (with `validation` listed as deliberately excluded and `attempts` framed as captured on "the eventual terminal event") and the dedup-rule bullet asserting a terminal `validation` event with `attempts` set on respond-repair exhaustion, plus the `RuntimeEvent.attempts` field comment. The two halves contradicted each other on arrival. Commit `78a2f94` (2026-06-04, *Misplaced sections within the pi-integration-contract pages*) carried both paragraphs verbatim into the new `runtime-event-channel.md` sub-page and added the *Discard-site disposition* paragraph, which re-asserts the exclusion side ("the four excluded kinds emit nothing on this channel even at a discard site") and so extended the contradiction surface without introducing it. The PIC-1 third site landed in `d75f2a8` and was further qualified in `85daf5a` (2026-06-20, *PIC-1 (c)(i) qualified as ValidationError.cause*), at which point PIC-1's mask-domain table began explicitly presupposing that `validation` events flow through the channel — but neither commit revisited the exclusion paragraph. Verdict is therefore single-commit (`b3bc4ce`) rather than multi-commit-interaction: the two later commits propagated and intensified an inconsistency that was already complete at inception.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the **Runtime event channel** exclusion paragraph in `runtime-event-channel.md` to scope the `validation` exclusion to *per-attempt* respond-repair signals only, and admit the *terminal* `validation` outcome — respond-repair exhaustion, including the `respond_repair.methodology: none` and `attempts: 0` arms — into the Group A always-log set. Reconcile the **Discard-site disposition** paragraph so the per-attempt-vs-terminal distinction reads consistently across both sites. The **Deduplication and lifetime rules** `validation`-arm bullet, the `RuntimeEvent.attempts` field, and PIC-1 clauses (c)(i)/(d) already presuppose terminal `validation` emission and need no change.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: do not rewrite or remove the **Deduplication and lifetime rules** `validation`-arm bullet, the `RuntimeEvent.attempts` field, or PIC-1 clauses (c)(i)/(d); the fix scopes the exclusion text to match those sites, not the reverse.
