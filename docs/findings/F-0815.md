## Finding

The *Lookup-failure-to-discriminator routing* paragraph of [`unknown-reason-rule.md`](../../docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/unknown-reason-rule.md) opens by declaring the iteration primitive used to scan `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` (`Array.prototype.find`, a derived-index `Map.get`, an explicit `for`/`switch`, "or any equivalent") to be **leaf-owned and not pinned here**, then immediately pins one specific code shape onto arm (1) of the routing: *"the handler MUST guard the lookup with an explicit `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY === undefined` check **before** invoking the iteration primitive."* The observable contract this arm exists to enforce — *the `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY === undefined` failure mode routes to the `"missing-entry"` discriminator, not to the `"throw:TypeError: …"` arm* — is already stated separately at the head of the arm and is what any conformance fixture would actually assert.

Mandating the explicit `=== undefined` comparison and its positional ordering relative to a leaf-owned iteration primitive contradicts the leaf-owned declaration in the same paragraph. Equivalent shapes that satisfy the observable contract (a `try`/`catch` that maps `TypeError`s thrown by an optional-chain on `undefined` to `"missing-entry"`, a `typeof SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY === "undefined"` check, a `Map.get` against a derived index that returns `undefined` for both the missing-entry and missing-inventory cases collapsed into one arm, etc.) are non-conformant under the current wording even though their observable behaviour is identical. The prescription leaks implementation shape into a contract whose load-bearing pin is purely observable.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In arm (1) of the *Lookup-failure-to-discriminator routing* paragraph, keep the observable requirement and demote the guard-expression-and-ordering text to a non-normative implementation note. Concretely:

- Retain: "`SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` itself resolves to `undefined` (a circular-init or live-binding gap…) — discriminator MUST be `"missing-entry"`, **not** any `"throw:TypeError: …"` value."
- Replace the MUST sentence (*"to satisfy this routing the handler MUST guard the lookup with an explicit `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY === undefined` check before invoking the iteration primitive"*) with a non-normative implementation note such as: *"(Non-normative: one shape that satisfies this routing is an explicit `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY === undefined` check before the iteration primitive; a naive `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY.find(…)?.literals` pattern instead routes the undefined-inventory case to the throw arm and is non-conformant.)"*
- Leave arms (2)–(4) unchanged — they already pin observable discriminators without prescribing the iteration primitive's surface.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The four-arm exhaustiveness claim (*"An implementation that routes any of (1)–(4) to a different discriminator is non-conformant regardless of which iteration primitive it chose"*) is the load-bearing observable invariant and must stay verbatim — it is what makes the demotion safe.
- The cross-reference from `diagnostics.md`'s `loom/host/session-shutdown-pinned-constant-unreadable` row to the composite-predicate text in this paragraph is unaffected (the predicate is about lookup keying, not about the undefined-inventory guard).
- Sub-anchor (iii) (`#unknown-reason-rule-handler-trycatch`) currently enumerates "the routing through the unknown-reason path on a throwing lookup / missing entry / non-array `literals`" without naming the guard expression; no anchor-stable substring needs to change.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Implementers face two conflicting signals on the same paragraph — *iteration primitive is leaf-owned* and *guard expression and ordering are spec-pinned* — and either honour the prescription (foreclosing the equivalent shapes the leaf-owned declaration invited) or treat it as residual prose and risk a reviewer flagging the deviation. The observable contract is fully expressible without the prescription, so no behaviour is at risk; the cost is contributor friction and an over-tight implementation envelope.
