## Finding

The parenthetical tag that marks a normative-MUST obligation as a GOV-22 un-anchored-obligation residue appears in three non-equivalent forms across the plan corpus:

- `(... un-anchored GOV-22 residue)` — `V9b-registration-reload-wiring.md` and `V9b-T-registration-reload-wiring.md` (no semicolon).
- `(GOV-22 un-anchored residue)` — `V9f-tool-registration-lifetime.md` (reversed word order).
- `(un-anchored; GOV-22 residue)` — the dominant form: every `coverage-matrix.md` residue row (≈29 rows), `V9l-session-only-degraded-branch.md`, and `V9l-T-session-only-degraded-branch.md`.

The concept is identical in all three: a normative MUST/MUST-NOT on a non-narrative `spec_topics/**` page carrying no numbered `PREFIX-N` REQ-ID and no `loom/...` registry code — the un-anchored-obligation class defined in `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline and `coverage-matrix.md`. The term is rigorously defined there, so the defect is not an undefined concept (an implementer who reads `conventions.md`, as step 1 of the plan directs, has the definition). The defect is that one tag has three spellings, none of which links to or matches the canonical definitional form. A reader text-searching for the tag by the form they first encountered will miss the other variants, and the divergent word order in V9f invites the mistaken inference that the three forms denote different things.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `cfd76f7` (2026-06-12) "pi-loom plan: resolve \"Per-loom registration ToolDefinition field-derivation MUSTs unnamed by any leaf\"" — introduced `(GOV-22 un-anchored residue)` in `V9f`.
- `55ed974` (2026-06-12) "pi-loom plan: resolve \"degraded-state obligations green over open spec contradiction\"" — introduced `(un-anchored; GOV-22 residue)` in `V9l`/`V9l-T` (the form that also dominates `coverage-matrix.md`).
- `b722ce7` (2026-06-17) "pi-loom plan: resolve \"registration-steps.md has normative MUSTs but no coverage-matrix row\"" — introduced `(... un-anchored GOV-22 residue)` in `V9b`/`V9b-T`.
**History:** No single commit introduced the inconsistency; it accreted across three independent per-finding plan-fix commits, each coining its own parenthetical form for the residue tag without converging on the form already in use elsewhere. The divergence is the interaction of those independent edits, not a regression in any one of them.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Standardise the residue tag on the dominant form `(un-anchored; GOV-22 residue)` already used in `coverage-matrix.md`, `conventions.md`-adjacent rows, and `V9l`/`V9l-T`:

- In `docs/plan_topics/V9f-tool-registration-lifetime.md`, rewrite the bullet's `(GOV-22 un-anchored residue)` to `(un-anchored; GOV-22 residue)`.
- In `docs/plan_topics/V9b-registration-reload-wiring.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V9b-T-registration-reload-wiring.md`, rewrite the bullet's `(PIC area; un-anchored GOV-22 residue)` so the residue token matches the canonical `un-anchored; GOV-22 residue` form.

The canonical definition already lives in `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline and `coverage-matrix.md`; once the tag spelling matches what those pages use, the leaf bullets align with the defined term and no per-leaf gloss is required. If a pointer is wanted, the bullet may reference the `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline definition, but that is optional — the spelling alignment is the substantive fix.

Edge case for the implementer: do not alter the meaning of the V9b bullet's existing `PIC area;` qualifier — only the residue-token spelling is in scope. The `coverage-matrix.md`/`conventions.md`/`V9l` occurrences are already canonical and are the reference, not edit targets.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

One concept carries three parenthetical spellings; the obligations themselves are fully specified, so no implementer diverges on behaviour. The cost is read-time friction: a search by one variant misses the others, and V9f's reversed word order can be misread as a distinct class.
