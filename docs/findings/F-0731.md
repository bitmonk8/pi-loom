## Finding

The second paragraph of `overview-and-orientation.md` § Overview opens as the three-outcome (success / fail / cancelled) summary readers expect from an Overview, but its sole sentence then expands into a dense routing-exception listing: the per-arm classification *for ceiling #4*, a parenthetical that hard-ceiling cases (a) binder argument-binding failure (with the slash-load / `invoke(...)` arm split spelled out, including the surfaced `Err(InvokeInfraError { cause: "validation", ... })` envelope) and (b) ceiling #4's in-loop model-driven tool-call args row are excluded from the Failure-cause enumeration, the `loomAbort.signal` plumbing and per-entry-point forwarding rule, the per-cause caller-surface map, and the partial-append contract — each with its own forward-link. The single sentence runs to roughly 1,500 characters and exceeds the surface area an Overview paragraph can carry without functioning as a second normative source.

Two sibling aggregator paragraphs on the same page (Orientation › Scope's Hard-ceilings bullet, and the Non-functional requirements bullets) are explicitly tagged `*Orientation aggregator (per [Governance — GOV-30](./governance.md)).*`, telling the reader the bullet is informative orientation that forward-links a normative owner. The Overview terminal-outcomes paragraph carries no such tag, so its forward-links read as parenthetical clarifications inside a normative statement rather than as the entry point to the real owner — and the carve-out detail (slash-load vs. `invoke(...)` `params` arm, the in-loop tool-call-args row, the partial-append contract) reads as the place the rule is stated rather than where it is summarised. The carve-outs and per-arm routing are already authoritatively stated at their owners (`errors-and-results/error-model.md` § Terminal outcomes for (a) and (b); `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md#ceiling-4-table` for the per-boundary routing; `errors-and-results/error-model.md#partial-append-contract` for partial-append), so the Overview restatement creates a second authoritative-seeming surface that drifts independently — historically it has: each successive resolution against this paragraph (the slash-load split, the in-loop row carve-out, the `invoke(...)` envelope qualification) was layered onto the Overview text in addition to the owner.

The Orientation › Scope › Hard-ceilings bullet (`<a id="hard-runtime-ceilings"></a>`) is the natural home for the per-ceiling routing-exception detail: it is already an explicit GOV-30 aggregator, it already enumerates the four ceilings with their routing classes, and ceilings #3 and #4 already carry forward-links to the per-boundary destination/surface table. Hoisting the ceiling-#3/#4 cross-routing exceptions and the partial-append-contract pointer next to the hard-ceilings bullet (or directly onto the ceiling-#4 entry) leaves the Overview paragraph as a clean three-outcome summary with one forward-link to the closed enumeration owner.

The accumulated weight — three carve-out layers plus the per-arm classification forward-link plus the partial-append-contract pointer — is what now exceeds what an Overview paragraph can carry. ## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Restore the Overview terminal-outcomes paragraph to a Overview-shaped summary, and move the routing/exclusion detail to the Orientation › Scope › Hard-ceilings bullet that already owns the per-ceiling routing-class enumeration.

Concretely:

1. In § Overview, replace the current single-sentence paragraph with a short trichotomy statement: name the three outcomes (success / fail / cancelled), forward-link [Errors and Results — Terminal outcomes](./errors-and-results/error-model.md#terminal-outcomes) as the closed-enumeration owner, forward-link [Cancellation](./cancellation.md), and tag the paragraph `*Orientation aggregator (per [Governance — GOV-30](./governance.md)).*` so future qualifications land on the owner page under the lock-step convention rather than being layered back into Overview.

2. Move the routing-detail content — the per-arm classification *for ceiling #4*, the (a) binder argument-binding / slash-load `params` cross-routing carve-out with its `invoke(...)` `params` arm split, and the (b) in-loop model-driven tool-call args row exclusion — next to the ceiling #4 entry in the Orientation › Scope › Hard-ceilings bullet (`<a id="hard-runtime-ceilings"></a>`). That bullet is already a GOV-30 aggregator and already enumerates ceiling #4 with a forward-link to the per-boundary destination/surface table; the carve-out wording is a natural continuation of the same one-liner.

3. Move the `loomAbort.signal` plumbing sentence (cancellation source + per-entry-point forwarding) into a separate Orientation paragraph next to the Cancellation forward-link, or fold it into the Cancellation forward-link itself — it is a separate concern from the trichotomy summary and does not need to ride the same sentence.

4. Move the partial-append-contract pointer and the per-cause caller-surface map pointer into the same Orientation › Scope vicinity (or omit them from Overview entirely, since the closed-enumeration owner forward-link already covers them transitively). Both are already cleanly owned by `error-model.md`; the Overview need not reach into them.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The `<a id="terminal-outcomes-aggregator"></a>` HTML anchor must be preserved at the rewritten paragraph (or, if the routing detail moves under the hard-ceilings bullet, the anchor must move with whichever block downstream cross-references resolve into) — `grep -rn 'terminal-outcomes-aggregator' docs/` before editing to confirm none of the inbound link targets break.
- The `errors-and-results/error-model.md` § Terminal outcomes Failure-cause carve-out enumeration must remain the single normative source for (a) and (b); the relocated Orientation text should forward-link there rather than restate the envelope details.
- Keep the in-loop model-driven row exclusion's reconciliation note (no `Err` flows to loom code at that event, no `loom-system-note`, `Err` only flows if ceiling #2 subsequently exhausts) at its owner in `error-model.md`; the Orientation restatement only needs the one-liner that the row is excluded from the trichotomy.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader entering through Overview is asked to absorb the closed trichotomy alongside three layers of carve-out (ceiling #4 per-arm classification, ceiling #3 cross-routing of slash-load `params`, in-loop model-driven row exclusion) before the file has introduced what those ceilings even are; the conceptual orientation Overview promises is diluted. The carve-out detail is also restated in addition to its owner, so successive normative refinements at the owner must be mirrored here or the two surfaces drift — the paragraph's own edit history shows that lock-step has not held automatically.
