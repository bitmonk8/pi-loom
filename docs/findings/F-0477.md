## Problem

`H4a`'s session-double fidelity-contract self-check (Tests, third bullet) asserts two emission-dependent behaviours: (i) streamed assistant tokens observable in the transcript before `ctx.waitForIdle()` resolves, and (ii) one streamed assistant response appended as a single prompt-mode turn. Both presuppose the in-process double can *emit* assistant tokens and turns, but `H4a` grants no such capability — its Adds states the response-programming surface "is owned by `H4b`", and `H4b` declares `Deps: H4a`. The one mechanism the plan names for programming emission (response-programming-surface category (a)) therefore lands in a leaf sequenced after `H4a`, so `H4a`'s Ships-when self-check cannot be satisfied from `H4a`'s own deliverables. `H4b`'s Adds says it "extends `H4a`'s session-double fidelity contract" but `H4a` defines no emission primitive for `H4b` to extend, leaving the ownership boundary between the two leaves undefined.

The defect entered as a side effect of that single de-bundling edit.

## Solution approach

In `H4a` Adds, state that `H4a` itself provides a minimal response-emission capability for the double — its own deliverable, sufficient to drive self-check items (i) and (ii) — rather than deferring all emission to `H4b`. In the same place, frame `H4b`'s response-programming surface as *extending* this `H4a` emission capability into the full scripted-injection surface rather than introducing emission from scratch. In `H4b` Adds, make the existing "extends `H4a`'s session-double fidelity contract" antecedent concrete by pointing it at `H4a`'s minimal response-emission capability as the base the categories (a)–(g) surface widens.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the full scripted-injection surface — categories (a)–(g), covering `tool_use`/binder-response/cancellation-injection/invoke-child/subagent — remains owned by `H4b`; `H4a`'s minimal emission must cover only self-check items (i) and (ii).
- The spec is read-only for this fix; the change is internal to the two plan leaves.
