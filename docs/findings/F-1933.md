## Finding

`V14a` ships two algorithmically distinct phases under one **Ships when**. The first is a parse-time static-analysis phase grounded in `tool-calls.md`: the three argument-violation codes (`loom/parse/tool-arg-not-literal`, `tool-arg-arity`, `tool-arg-type-mismatch`, with arity checked before type), the closed `CodeToolError` enum, and the `.loom`-callable failure-surface routing via `Invoke*Error`. The second is a runtime `execute()` envelope-lowering phase grounded in `host-interfaces-core.md` §*Tool execution from loom code*: five accepted-path sub-cases (text-filter + single-`"\n"` join with silent non-text discard; `Ok("")` empty-result; `isError: true` → `Err(CodeToolError{cause:"execution"})` with 4096-byte code-point-boundary truncation; the fixed no-text message; and `execute()`-throw coercion under the same truncation rule) plus the two return-type-table lowering rows. The leaf additionally carries the `cka-47` pre-tool-call cancellation-checkpoint facet and the ERR-13 completed-callee-finality witness.

The two phases sit at different abstraction layers, cite different spec anchors, and require different test setups (driving parse-time diagnostics vs. driving a fake `execute()` envelope through the lowering path). `V14a`'s **Ships when** enumerates six independent assertion groups spanning both. The plan has already peeled `V14b`–`V14f` off `V14a` on exactly this atomicity rationale; the parse-vs-lower seam is the next clean cut and is sharper than several of the cuts already made.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** `77f2e88` — "pi-loom plan: resolve \"code-side tool-call envelope-lowering MUSTs unanchored\"" (2026-06-18)
**History:** The plan corpus is git-tracked. `git log --follow` over `docs/plan_topics/V14a-tool-calls.md` shows that commit `5a1915d` (2026-06-17, "resolve \"V14a bundles eight independently-testable concerns\"") had reduced `V14a` to a **parse-only** leaf — peeling the off-surface routing, host-denial, ceiling-#4, and swallowing-handler concerns into `V14c`–`V14f` — leaving a **Ships when** that asserted only the argument codes, the closed enum, the return-type rows, and the `.loom`-callable failure surface. The runtime `execute()` envelope-lowering phase was then re-introduced into `V14a` by commit `77f2e88` (`git show 77f2e88` shows the five-sub-case `host-interfaces-core.md` bullet and the matching **Ships when** clause added), which anchored the previously un-anchored envelope-lowering MUSTs onto the parse leaf rather than a dedicated lowering leaf — re-creating the two-phase coupling. The later `cka-47` facet (`849d926`) and ERR-13 witness (`7bd65a7`) added load but are not the source of the compile-time/runtime split; that coupling entered solely at `77f2e88`.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Split `V14a` along the parse/runtime seam, mirroring the `V14b`–`V14f` peel-offs already present, and split `V14a-T` the same way.

Retain in `V14a` (and `V14a-T`) the parse-time static-analysis phase: the three `loom/parse/tool-arg-*` argument codes (arity before type), the closed `CodeToolError` enum distinctness assertion, and the `.loom`-callable failure-surface routing via `Invoke*Error`. Keep the `cka-47` `V14a` tool-call-checkpoint facet and the ERR-13 delegated-witness bullet here unless the lowering surface is the more natural carrier — the fixer decides which sub-leaf owns each, but each must continue to be asserted by exactly one leaf.

Move to a new leaf (`<new>` impl + `<new>-T` tests; let the fixer/implementer allocate the real `V14`-slice ID per the plan's `V<slice><letter>` scheme and `no-invented-ids` policy) the runtime `execute()` envelope-lowering phase: the five accepted-path lowering sub-cases cited to `host-interfaces-core.md` §*Tool execution from loom code* and the two return-type-table lowering rows from `tool-calls.md`. Carry the `host-interfaces-core.md` §*Tool execution from loom code* **Spec** citation and the lowering **Ships when** clause onto the new leaf.

Reconcile `coverage-matrix.md`: retarget the `cka-46` closing-leaf cell (currently `V14a`) to the new lowering leaf, and update the `cka-13` row and any `V14a` mention so each envelope-lowering obligation maps to whichever sub-leaf now owns it. The new leaf's **Deps** should carry whatever `V14a` currently depends on for the lowering path (it consumes the same `execute()` surface). Treat the `host-interfaces-core.md` lowering MUSTs as read-only spec — this fix is internal to the plan files; do not edit the spec.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reviewer must context-switch between parse-time AST analysis and runtime envelope-lowering in a single pass, and the paired `-T`/impl TDD ritual lands one oversized red-to-green step instead of two independently observable ones; the larger leaf is harder to verify in one reading. The leaf is fully specified and implementable as written, so no implementer divergence results — the cost is reviewability and TDD granularity, the same cost the plan already paid down by splitting `V14b`–`V14f` out.
