## Problem

`V14a` (and its paired `V14a-T`) ships two algorithmically distinct phases under one **Ships when**: a parse-time static-analysis phase (the `loom/parse/tool-arg-*` argument codes, the closed `CodeToolError` enum, and the `.loom`-callable failure-surface routing, grounded in `tool-calls.md`) and a runtime `execute()` envelope-lowering phase (the five accepted-path lowering sub-cases and the two return-type lowering rows, grounded in `host-interfaces-core.md` §*Tool execution from loom code*). The two phases sit at different abstraction layers, cite different spec anchors, and require different test setups (driving parse-time diagnostics vs. driving a fake `execute()` envelope through the lowering path). The plan has already peeled `V14b`–`V14f` off `V14a` on this same atomicity rationale; the parse-vs-lower seam is the next clean cut.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** `77f2e88` — "pi-loom plan: resolve \"code-side tool-call envelope-lowering MUSTs unanchored\"" (2026-06-18)
**History:** The plan corpus is git-tracked. `git log --follow` over `docs/plan_topics/V14a-tool-calls.md` shows that commit `5a1915d` (2026-06-17, "resolve \"V14a bundles eight independently-testable concerns\"") had reduced `V14a` to a **parse-only** leaf — peeling the off-surface routing, host-denial, ceiling-#4, and swallowing-handler concerns into `V14c`–`V14f` — leaving a **Ships when** that asserted only the argument codes, the closed enum, the return-type rows, and the `.loom`-callable failure surface. The runtime `execute()` envelope-lowering phase was then re-introduced into `V14a` by commit `77f2e88` (`git show 77f2e88` shows the five-sub-case `host-interfaces-core.md` bullet and the matching **Ships when** clause added), which anchored the previously un-anchored envelope-lowering MUSTs onto the parse leaf rather than a dedicated lowering leaf — re-creating the two-phase coupling. The later `cka-47` facet (`849d926`) and ERR-13 witness (`7bd65a7`) added load but are not the source of the compile-time/runtime split; that coupling entered solely at `77f2e88`.

## Solution approach

Split `V14a` and `V14a-T` along the parse/runtime seam, mirroring the `V14b`–`V14f` peel-offs. Retain in `V14a`/`V14a-T` the parse-time static-analysis phase (the `loom/parse/tool-arg-*` argument codes, the closed `CodeToolError` enum, the `.loom`-callable failure-surface routing). Move the runtime `execute()` envelope-lowering phase — the five accepted-path lowering sub-cases cited to `host-interfaces-core.md` §*Tool execution from loom code* and the two return-type lowering rows from `tool-calls.md` — to a new paired lowering leaf plus its `-T`. Reconcile `coverage-matrix.md`: retarget the `cka-46` closing-leaf cell from `V14a` to the new lowering leaf, and update the `cka-13`, `cka-47`, and `ERR-13` rows so each obligation maps to whichever sub-leaf now owns it.

## Solution constraints

- The spec is read-only for this fix — the `host-interfaces-core.md` §*Tool execution from loom code* lowering MUSTs are not editable; the change is internal to the plan files.
- Allocate the new leaf ID per the plan's `V<N><letter>` scheme and the no-invented-ids policy (next free letter in the target phase; no retired-ID reuse), and keep `coverage-matrix.md` and `conventions.md` consistent so every envelope-lowering obligation remains asserted by exactly one leaf.
