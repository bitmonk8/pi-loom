## Finding

`spec_topics/invocation.md` labels the positional-`invoke()` type-checking paragraph **"Argument binding"** and exposes it under the stable HTML anchor `#argument-binding` (line 24, `<a id="argument-binding"></a>`). In every other corner of the corpus, the bare phrase *argument binding* names the LLM-driven slash-command binder: the dedicated subspec file `spec_topics/binder.md` is titled "Slash-Command Argument Binding"; the glossary entry for **binder** defines it as "the LLM-driven argument-binding mechanism"; the normative failure-mode templates in `binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md` render system notes of the form `argument binding needs more info` / `argument binding failed` / `argument binding cancelled`; and `frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-a.md` repeatedly uses "argument binding" in its `bind_*` field descriptions to mean the binder. The disambiguating sentence ("The LLM-driven binder used at the slash-command boundary … does not run here") lives in the paragraph body, not in the label that names the anchor.

The collision is observable at two surfaces. (a) Cross-file links: `diagnostics/code-registry-parse.md`, `frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-a.md`, `tool-calls.md`, and the plan leaves `V15f` / `V15f-T` all link to `invocation.md#argument-binding` with link text "Argument binding" or "Argument arity", reading from outside the file as references to the binder. (b) Author lookup: a reader who has just seen `loom /<name>: argument binding cancelled` in a system-note table and follows back to the canonical definition is presented with a paragraph about positional-`invoke()` typing that explicitly excludes the binder, with no signposting that the two senses share a name.

The wider scope worth flagging: the dominant phrase across the corpus is the binder sense, so the cleaner rename is on the `invocation.md` side. The body already characterises the operation as positional type-checking; the label should follow.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `53f5831` (2026-05-03, "spec: rewrite query primitive, system prompts, and schema syntax"); `83802db` (2026-05-04, "spec: collapse to single typed argument style; add LLM-driven slash-command binder"); `333ba2f` (2026-05-04, "spec: define static resolution for invoke/tools callees (Option C)")
**History:** `git log -S '**Argument binding.**'` shows the bolded paragraph **Argument binding** first entered `spec.md` in `53f5831` as the label for positional `invoke()` type-checking, before any binder existed; at that point the name was unambiguous. `83802db` then introduced the binder under the H2 "Slash-Command Argument Binding" and renamed the surrounding glossary/system-note vocabulary to the *argument binding* phrasing, at which point the two senses began coexisting in body prose — but `invocation.md` had no stable URL anchor yet, so the collision was confined to a single same-file paragraph label. `git log -S '<a id="argument-binding">'` shows the URL collision proper landed in `333ba2f`, which added the `<a id="argument-binding"></a>` anchor (and the back-reference from the new §"Static resolution") to support its eager-transitive-load redesign. From that commit onward the label is externally addressable and the cross-file links in `diagnostics/code-registry-parse.md`, `tool-calls.md`, `frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-a.md`, and plan leaves `V15f` / `V15f-T` all point at it. The defect is the joint product of the binder rename (which made *argument binding* the dominant sense) and the later URL-anchor introduction (which froze the `invocation.md` sense at the same name); no single commit considered both endpoints.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Rename the `invocation.md` paragraph label and HTML anchor to **"Argument type-checking"** / `#argument-type-checking`, and update every cross-file link to follow.

Concretely:

1. In `docs/spec_topics/invocation.md`:
   - Change `<a id="argument-binding"></a>` (line 24) to `<a id="argument-type-checking"></a>`. Preserve the old anchor alongside as `<a id="argument-binding"></a>` for one revision so external bookmarks survive; flag the old anchor for removal at the next deprecation sweep.
   - Change the paragraph lead **`Argument binding.`** (line 38) to **`Argument type-checking.`**.
   - Update the §"Static resolution" back-reference `[Argument binding](#argument-binding)` (line 20) to `[Argument type-checking](#argument-type-checking)`.
2. In `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/code-registry-parse.md` (rows for `loom/parse/invoke-arity-too-few` and `invoke-arity-too-many`, lines 111–112), update both `[Invocation — Argument arity](../invocation.md#argument-binding)` links to `…#argument-type-checking` (the link text **Argument arity** was already accurate; only the anchor target moves).
3. In `docs/spec_topics/frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-a.md` line 60, update `[Invocation — Argument arity](../invocation.md#argument-binding)` similarly.
4. In `docs/spec_topics/tool-calls.md` line 14, update `[Invocation — Argument arity](./invocation.md#argument-binding)` similarly.
5. In `docs/plan_topics/V15f-invoke-diagnostics.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V15f-T-invoke-diagnostics.md` (both line 8), update the link text and anchor: `[invocation.md — Argument binding](../spec_topics/invocation.md#argument-binding)` → `[invocation.md — Argument type-checking](../spec_topics/invocation.md#argument-type-checking)`.

Edge cases for the implementer:
- The §"Argument arity" paragraph (line ~55) keeps its own label and is unchanged — only the cross-file link *text* in code-registry-parse / frontmatter / tool-calls currently reads "Argument arity" while pointing at `#argument-binding`; the rename leaves the arity paragraph untouched and merely re-targets those four links to the (now-correctly-named) type-checking anchor, which is what they actually want.
- Do **not** rename the binder's H1 (`binder.md` — "Slash-Command Argument Binding"). It owns the dominant sense across the corpus and renaming it would cascade through the glossary, every system-note template, the `bind_*` frontmatter docs, and BNDR-3.
- Do **not** edit the system-note template strings (`argument binding cancelled`, etc.) in `binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md`. Those are normative wire text and the sibling finding "determinism-cancellation-failure.md — 'argument binder' vs 'argument binding' template naming inconsistency" owns any change to them.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader chasing the binder's `argument binding cancelled` system note backward through any of the cross-file links lands on positional-`invoke()` typing instead, and an editor adding a new binder cross-link can plausibly point at the wrong anchor. No implementer will produce wrong behaviour over this — the body text disambiguates — but the corpus carries two sites named the same and the canonical sense is the binder.
