## Problem

The `mode: subagent` bullet in overview.md's `### Scope of a loom file` states
that when a subagent loom finishes "the intermediate transcript is private to the
loom and is **not retained by the runtime** after the loom returns." The clause
bundles two assertions of different epistemic status: the privacy/isolation half
(the caller's conversation never receives the loom's intermediate turns, and the
`invoke` return carries only the typed final value) is externally observable and
testable, while "not retained by the runtime" is an internal-memory-lifetime claim
no loom-level API exposes and no test can witness. As written it reads as a
normative guarantee, inviting implementers to believe they owe an untestable
active free/zeroise step, while the genuinely checkable obligations are not flagged
as the conformance surface.

The unfalsifiable retention clause was therefore present in the sentence's first appearance.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the `mode: subagent` bullet's guarantee clause in overview.md's
`### Scope of a loom file` to assert only the externally observable properties:
the loom's intermediate query turns never appear in the caller's conversation, and
the `invoke` return surfaces only the typed final value (not the transcript).
Demote the unfalsifiable "not retained by the runtime" retention detail to a
non-normative aside or drop it.
