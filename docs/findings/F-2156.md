## Finding

The `loom 1.0 seam — `system:` expression sublanguage` blockquote in `frontmatter-fields-b-and-templates.md` carries two parts. The first is a genuine, observable normative obligation: *"A `${…}` body in `system:` MUST be a `Path` (the `Path` production above); any other body fires one of the four `loom/parse/system-interp-*` diagnostics listed under *Parse errors* below."* The second part is a self-labeled `*Non-normative implementation note.*` describing one internal realisation: loom 1.0 reuses the shared `${…}` expression entry point and applies a parser-level filter so the deferred richer-`system:`-expression extension "widens the filter rather than rewriting the parser," closing with "the implementation shape is not an observable conformance point."

That implementation note is cruft on two counts. First, it restates the *Parser entry point* paragraph that sits directly above the blockquote — that paragraph already says the `${…}` syntax is parsed by the same expression entry point, restricted at parse time by a parser-level filter rather than a hand-rolled `${param}` regex, and that the seam is what lets the deferred richer-expression extension land by widening the filter rather than rewriting the parser. The note's own text even points the reader back to it ("see *Parser entry point* above"). Second, the note explicitly disclaims conformance relevance ("not an observable conformance point"), so no conformance test can depend on it; an implementer is free to realise the Path restriction however they choose. Embedding a named non-normative note inside the seam callout adds reader load — a reader may hunt for a conformance implication that the note itself says does not exist — without constraining anything.

The fix removes only the implementation-note sentence. The normative MUST and the two anchor IDs (`#loom-1-0-seam-system-expression-sublanguage`, `#v1-seam-system-expression-sublanguage`) stay; the seam-rationale survives in the adjacent *Parser entry point* paragraph.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In the `loom 1.0 seam — `system:` expression sublanguage` blockquote, delete the `*Non-normative implementation note.*` and everything after it through "; the implementation shape is not an observable conformance point." Keep the leading `**loom 1.0 seam — `system:` expression sublanguage.**` label, both `<a id="…">` anchor tags, and the normative sentence ("A `${…}` body in `system:` MUST be a `Path` … fires one of the four `loom/parse/system-interp-*` diagnostics listed under *Parse errors* below."). The seam/filter/future-extension rationale is preserved verbatim in the *Parser entry point* paragraph immediately above the blockquote, so no information is lost. Do not delete the whole blockquote — the MUST and the two anchors are load-bearing.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

If shipped unfixed, the seam callout carries a non-normative implementation note that duplicates the preceding *Parser entry point* paragraph and disclaims its own conformance relevance. No implementer or runtime behaviour diverges; the cost is reader load and the risk of someone searching for a conformance implication that the note states does not exist.
