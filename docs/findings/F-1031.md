## Problem

`PIC-2. Prompt-mode sequential execution` lives in `subagent.md`, the integration-contract page for subagent-mode isolation, but its normative subject is prompt mode: the non-overlap of prompt-mode body windows within a single user session. Every premise it composes belongs to prompt mode, and its only mention of subagent mode is a negative delegation back to the concurrent-invocation-isolation paragraph. The rule therefore sits in a file whose subject does not bracket it. Readers scanning `subagent.md` for subagent-isolation obligations hit a prompt-mode lifecycle rule out of nowhere, while readers on the prompt-mode driver pages find nothing where they look.

**Verdict:** multi-commit interaction
**Introducing commits:** `40ffdc5` (2026-05-27, *spec: resolve "T19a — Replace session-model paragraph with eight SM-N sub-units"*) → `7d7bfe2` (2026-06-01, *spec: resolve "SM-7c/SM-8 authoritative-in-place relocation (T08/T10/T11)"*) → `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04, *docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set*)
**History:** `git log --reverse -G 'Prompt-mode sequential execution'` shows the rule first appearing as the `SM-7c` sub-unit in `docs/spec.md` (40ffdc5) under the session-model heading, where the prompt-mode subject and surrounding session-model material are in scope. Commit 7d7bfe2 then performed the "authoritative-in-place relocation" by introducing the `<a id="pic-2"></a>` paragraph into the (then-monolithic) `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md`, inserting it immediately after the `Concurrent-invocation isolation and concurrency disposition` paragraph and before `System notes` — i.e. inside the concurrency cluster rather than the prompt-mode-driver cluster. Commit f5e89f4's mechanical 100KB-cap split of `pi-integration-contract.md` then carried the paragraph into `subagent.md` along with the surrounding concurrent-invocation-isolation block; the split was content-preserving and made no scoping decision of its own. The scope defect is therefore the product of the in-place relocation choosing the wrong neighbour paragraph (concurrent-invocation-isolation rather than the prompt-mode driver section), with the size-cap split mechanically propagating that choice into the per-topic page that now violates scope most visibly.

## Solution approach

Move the `pic-2` paragraph from `subagent.md` into `conversation-drive.md` adjacent to the `pic-18` paragraph, where the prompt-mode body-lifecycle material lives. Add a forward-link at the former location in `subagent.md`'s `concurrent-invocation-isolation` block so the negative reference that `PIC-22` and that paragraph rely on still resolves. Repoint the `SM-7c` digest cross-reference in `docs/spec.md` to the relocated anchor.
