## Problem

PIC-1 clause (c)(i) describes the `validation` co-fire predicate as "the `validation` event when its `cause` is `"schema_validation"`", but the `RuntimeEvent` shape declared above PIC-1 carries no `cause` field — the `cause` sub-discriminator lives on `ValidationError`, evaluated at the origin site where the typed `QueryError` is in hand. Sibling clause (c)(ii) qualifies its parallel predicate as the `code_tool` event's "underlying `CodeToolError.cause`", so (i)'s bare phrasing is an asymmetric, misleading divergence. Two reasonable implementers split: one extends `RuntimeEvent` with `cause?` (a wire-shape change against the additive-only payload contract), the other correctly reaches through to `ValidationError.cause`. The predicate gates the only non-empty `masked` payload in loom 1.0 (`["ceiling#2"]`), so the divergence is observable.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `4ccc072` (2026-05-07, "Masked ceilings: no observability requirement for the unreported breach"); `7598f50` (2026-05-07, "CIO-6 hard-ceiling co-fire: predicate, test vector, and normative ownership")
**History:** `4ccc072` first introduced PIC-1's predicate prose in `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md`, in which the *only* co-fire-eligible runtime-event surface was the `validation` event keyed on `its `cause` is `"schema_validation"``. In that single-clause form the bare "its `cause`" was already a mismatch against the `RuntimeEvent` shape (added in the same commit, with no `cause` field), but with nothing to contrast against the asymmetry was latent. `7598f50` (later the same day) rewrote PIC-1 into clauses (a)–(g) and added clause (c)(ii) for the `code_tool` event with the explicit "underlying `CodeToolError.cause`" wording — at which point the asymmetry between (i)'s bare "its `cause`" and (ii)'s qualified phrasing became visible as a defect. The shard-split commit `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04) moved the paragraph verbatim into `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/runtime-event-channel.md`; subsequent edits (`0e2eb05`, `d75f2a8`) re-anchored CIO links and tightened clause (e) without touching the (c)(i) wording. The durable fix is the wording alignment in clause (c)(i), not a revert of either commit.

## Solution approach

Clarify PIC-1 clause (c)(i) so its sub-discriminator names its owning type, matching clause (c)(ii)'s "underlying `<Variant>Error.cause`" framing — qualify the predicate as the `validation` event's underlying `ValidationError.cause` being `"schema_validation"`. Leave the `RuntimeEvent` type block untouched (`cause` is not a wire field on the event). If clause (d)'s "V1 reachable predicate" shorthand (`with `cause: "schema_validation"``) reads ambiguously against the same referent, apply the qualifier consistently there.

## Solution constraints

- The `RuntimeEvent` payload shape MUST NOT gain a `cause` field — the fix is wording-only and must not widen the additive-only wire contract.
