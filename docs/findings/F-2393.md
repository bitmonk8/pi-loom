## Problem

The `active-invocation-registry.md` `loom`-field canonical-name resolver asserts that all three insertion arms read the callee's final-derived slash-name from a field the resolved catalog entry "already carries", but two of the three cited owners declare no such field. The `invocation.md#static-resolution` per-load-pass parse cache declares only the callee's parsed/lowered source and diagnostics ŌĆö no slash-name field ŌĆö and the `frontmatter-fields-a.md#tools` resolution snapshot declares its entry name as the underscore-remapped in-loom callable name (`./code-review.loom` ŌåÆ `code_review`), not the verbatim-stem slash-name the registry needs. The block also pins a read-vs-construct mechanism ("read from the resolved catalog entry's slash-name field rather than constructed at the insertion site") even though it concedes re-derivation "would produce the identical canonical form", so the choice is not observable. Two conforming implementations of the callee-only arms can therefore emit different `details.event.loom` values, splitting the operator dedup buckets the registry exists to keep aligned.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the `active-invocation-registry.md` `loom`-field resolver as a behavioural contract: all three insertion arms set `details.event.loom` to the same Discovery final-derived canonical form, obtained from the resolved callee ŌĆö the `LoomRegistry` `Map<slashName, parsedLoom>` key for slash-registered looms, and the Discovery final-derived form of the resolved callee for callee-only `.loom` files ŌĆö rather than from a presupposed slash-name field on the `invocation.md#static-resolution` cache entry or the `frontmatter-fields-a.md#tools` snapshot entry. Demote the read-vs-construct preference from a normative pin to a non-normative implementation note.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: `invocation.md` and `frontmatter-fields-a.md` ŌĆö neither owner page declares a new slash-name field.
