## Finding

`PIC-2. Prompt-mode sequential execution` is authored as the final paragraph of `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/subagent.md` (line 90), appended after `PIC-22` (subagent parallel spawn initiation) and the `concurrent-invocation-isolation` block whose closing sentence references it by contrast ("the three potential sources of in-session overlap therefore produce genuine concurrency only between subagent-mode invocations"). The rule itself is wholly about *prompt-mode* bodies — that within one user session at most one prompt-mode body holds an open `pi.setActiveTools` snapshot/restore window — and is *derived* from three premises, none of which lives on `subagent.md`: (i) Pi's per-session slash-handler serialisation at `tool-registration-lifetime.md#snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions`, (ii) the load-time rejection of prompt-mode `.loom` callees in `tools:` at `frontmatter-fields-a.md`, and (iii) the parent-suspending `invoke(...)` rule and closed `subagent → prompt` fan-out in `invocation.md`.

A reader navigating the prompt-mode invocation surface — opening `conversation-drive.md` (which already hosts `PIC-18`, the prompt-mode turn-lifecycle event subscription), `tool-registration-lifetime.md` (which already hosts `PIC-17` / `PIC-19`, the snapshot/restore protocol PIC-2 derives from), or `invocation.md` (cross-mode semantics and the parallel-`invoke` non-surface) — finds no PIC-2 on any of those pages and no forward pointer to it. The rule is reachable only by anchor from the orientation aggregator `session-model-and-appendix.md` (`SM-7c`), from `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` (GOV-30's enumeration), and from the four plan leaves `V9c` / `V9c-T` / `V15d` / `V15d-T`; topic-proximity navigation misses it entirely.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Move PIC-2 to `tool-registration-lifetime.md` after `PIC-19`

**Approach.** Relocate the `<a id="pic-2">` paragraph to `tool-registration-lifetime.md`, immediately after `PIC-19` (snapshot/swap-install-failure protocol).

**Spec edits.**
- Cut the PIC-2 paragraph from `subagent.md` (line 90).
- Insert it on `tool-registration-lifetime.md` after `PIC-19`, preserving the `<a id="pic-2">` anchor verbatim.
- Inside the moved paragraph, rewrite the closing back-reference from "per **Concurrent-invocation isolation and concurrency disposition** above" (same-page on `subagent.md`) to the cross-file form `[Subagent — Concurrent-invocation isolation](./subagent.md#concurrent-invocation-isolation)`. Also rewrite the same-page link to `snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions` as a same-page link rather than the current cross-file form (since the precondition now lives on the same page as PIC-2).
- Rewrite the closing sentence of `subagent.md`'s `concurrent-invocation-isolation` block from a same-file reference to PIC-2 to the cross-file form `[PIC-2](./tool-registration-lifetime.md#pic-2)`.
- Repoint the six inbound links (`session-model-and-appendix.md`'s `SM-7c`, GOV-30's enumeration in `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`, and the **Spec** lines of `V9c` / `V9c-T` / `V15d` / `V15d-T`) from `subagent.md#pic-2` to `tool-registration-lifetime.md#pic-2`.

**Pros.** PIC-2's load-bearing premise *is* `snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions` (b); co-locating the derived property with `PIC-17` / `PIC-19` closes the snapshot/restore contract on a single page, and a future editor of the preconditions sees the derived property in the same file and is reminded to re-audit it.

**Cons.** A reader arriving at the prompt-mode driver surface (`conversation-drive.md`) still does not find PIC-2 there; `tool-registration-lifetime.md` is the *mechanism* page, not the *prompt-mode driver* page.

**Risks.** Purely textual: one paragraph move, two link-direction rewrites on `subagent.md`, six inbound-link repoints.

### Option B — Move PIC-2 to `conversation-drive.md` after `PIC-18`

**Approach.** Relocate the `<a id="pic-2">` paragraph to `conversation-drive.md`, immediately after the existing `PIC-18` (prompt-mode turn-lifecycle event subscription) block.

**Spec edits.**
- Cut the PIC-2 paragraph from `subagent.md` (line 90).
- Insert it on `conversation-drive.md` after `PIC-18`, preserving the `<a id="pic-2">` anchor verbatim.
- Inside the moved paragraph, rewrite the closing back-reference from "per **Concurrent-invocation isolation and concurrency disposition** above" to the cross-file form `[Subagent — Concurrent-invocation isolation](./subagent.md#concurrent-invocation-isolation)`. The link to `snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions` remains cross-file (no change).
- Rewrite the closing sentence of `subagent.md`'s `concurrent-invocation-isolation` block to the cross-file form `[PIC-2](./conversation-drive.md#pic-2)`.
- Repoint the same six inbound callsites listed under Option A to `conversation-drive.md#pic-2`.

**Pros.** Topic proximity for the *driver* reader: the prompt-mode driver, the `pi.setActiveTools` consumer call site, the prompt-mode event subscription (`PIC-18`), and the prompt-mode sequential-execution conclusion (`PIC-2`) sit on one page. A reader looking for "what holds across prompt-mode bodies" finds it without a hop.

**Cons.** `conversation-drive.md` is already among the larger PIC topic pages; adding a derived REQ-ID widens its scope from "how the prompt-mode driver issues turns" to "and what concurrency property holds across drivers." The link from PIC-2 to its load-bearing premise (`snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions`) stays cross-file.

**Risks.** Same as Option A — purely textual: one paragraph move, one link-direction rewrite on `subagent.md`, six inbound-link repoints.

### Recommendation

**Option A** (`tool-registration-lifetime.md` after `PIC-19`). PIC-2 is *derived* from `snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions` (b) — co-locating the derived property with the premise closes the snapshot/restore contract on a single page and gives a future editor of either rule a local reminder of the other. The driver-reader concern Option B addresses is met by adding a one-sentence forward pointer near `PIC-18` on `conversation-drive.md` (e.g. "see PIC-2 at `tool-registration-lifetime.md` for the cross-body non-overlap derivation"), which is cheaper than hosting the full paragraph there. Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The `<a id="pic-2">` anchor string must be preserved verbatim across the move so the six inbound links — particularly the GOV-30 enumeration entry, which is the canonical aggregator pointer — continue to resolve under the new path.
- The closing back-reference inside PIC-2 (currently "per **Concurrent-invocation isolation and concurrency disposition** above") becomes a cross-file link; leaving it as a bold-text same-page reference would produce a dead reference under the new path.
- The four plan leaves' **Spec** lines all spell the path explicitly, so they need updating in the same commit as the spec move; a grep for `subagent.md#pic-2` across `docs/` after the edit should return zero hits.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Navigability defect only. The rule is fully stated and operative — every inbound citation reaches it by anchor — but a reader who arrives at the prompt-mode surface by topic does not find it where they would expect, and a future contributor editing the snapshot/restore protocol or the prompt-mode driver has no local reminder that PIC-2 depends on those rules. No observable behaviour changes; no two reasonable implementers would diverge.
