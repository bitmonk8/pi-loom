## Finding

`PIC-2. Prompt-mode sequential execution` is authored as the final paragraph of `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/subagent.md`, immediately after `PIC-22` (subagent parallel spawn initiation). The rule itself is wholly about prompt-mode bodies — that at most one prompt-mode body holds an open `pi.setActiveTools` snapshot/restore window per user session — and derives entirely from (i) Pi's per-session slash-handler serialisation pinned at `tool-registration-lifetime.md#snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions`, (ii) the load-time rejection of prompt-mode `.loom` callees in `tools:` (owned by `frontmatter-fields-a.md`), and (iii) the parent-suspending `invoke(...)` and closed `subagent → prompt` fan-out rules in `invocation.md`. None of those sources sits on `subagent.md`; the subagent page is touched only via the closing back-reference to `concurrent-invocation-isolation`, which uses PIC-2 as a contrast ("the three potential sources of overlap therefore produce genuine concurrency only between subagent-mode invocations").

A reader navigating the prompt-mode invocation surface — opening `conversation-drive.md`, `tool-registration-lifetime.md` (where `PIC-17`/`PIC-19` already govern the snapshot/restore protocol PIC-2 derives from), or `invocation.md` (which owns cross-mode semantics and the parallel-`invoke` non-surface) — finds no PIC-2 there and no forward pointer to it. Only the orientation aggregator `session-model-and-appendix.md` (`SM-7c`) and two plan leaves (`V9c`, `V15d`) name it by anchor; the contract is reachable from those callsites but not from topic-proximity navigation.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Move PIC-2 to `tool-registration-lifetime.md`

**Approach.** Relocate the `<a id="pic-2">` paragraph to `tool-registration-lifetime.md`, immediately after `PIC-19` (snapshot/swap-install-failure protocol).

**Spec edits.**
- Cut the PIC-2 paragraph from `subagent.md` (line 90).
- Insert it on `tool-registration-lifetime.md` after `PIC-19`, preserving the `<a id="pic-2">` anchor verbatim so inbound links resolve under the new path.
- Adjust the closing back-reference inside PIC-2 from `Concurrent-invocation isolation and concurrency disposition` (a same-page section on `subagent.md`) to the cross-file form `[Subagent — Concurrent-invocation isolation](./subagent.md#concurrent-invocation-isolation)`.
- Adjust the same-page link from `concurrent-invocation-isolation`'s closing sentence on `subagent.md` (currently "see PIC-2 below") to the cross-file form pointing at `tool-registration-lifetime.md#pic-2`.
- Repoint `session-model-and-appendix.md`'s `SM-7c`, GOV-30's enumeration in `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`, `V9c`, `V9c-T`, `V15d`, and `V15d-T` from `subagent.md#pic-2` to `tool-registration-lifetime.md#pic-2`.

**Pros.** PIC-2 is a property *of* the snapshot/restore protocol; co-locating it with `PIC-17`/`PIC-19` makes the snapshot-related contract self-contained. A contributor editing the snapshot/restore preconditions sees the derived property in the same page and is reminded to re-check it.

**Cons.** A reader looking for prompt-mode invocation rules under `conversation-drive.md` or `invocation.md` still needs a forward pointer; `tool-registration-lifetime.md` is the *mechanism* page, not the *prompt-mode driver* page.

**Risks.** Six cross-file repoints; one same-file edit replaced by a cross-file edit on `subagent.md`. All purely textual.

### Option B — Move PIC-2 to `conversation-drive.md`

**Approach.** Relocate the `<a id="pic-2">` paragraph to `conversation-drive.md`, after the existing `PIC-18` prompt-mode-event-subscription block.

**Spec edits.**
- Cut the PIC-2 paragraph from `subagent.md` (line 90).
- Insert it on `conversation-drive.md` after `PIC-18`, preserving the `<a id="pic-2">` anchor.
- Adjust the closing back-reference inside PIC-2 to the cross-file form `[Subagent — Concurrent-invocation isolation](./subagent.md#concurrent-invocation-isolation)`.
- Adjust the link on `subagent.md`'s `concurrent-invocation-isolation` closing sentence to the cross-file form pointing at `conversation-drive.md#pic-2`.
- Repoint the same six callsites listed under Option A to `conversation-drive.md#pic-2`.

**Pros.** Topic proximity: the prompt-mode driver, its `pi.setActiveTools` snapshot consumer, the prompt-mode event subscription (`PIC-18`), and the prompt-mode sequential-execution conclusion sit together. A reader arriving at the prompt-mode surface finds PIC-2 without a hop.

**Cons.** `conversation-drive.md` is already long; adding a derived REQ-ID paragraph widens its scope from "how the prompt-mode driver issues turns" to "and what concurrency property holds across drivers." The link to the snapshot/restore preconditions becomes cross-file rather than same-file.

**Risks.** Same as Option A — purely textual repoints.

### Recommendation

Option A (`tool-registration-lifetime.md` after `PIC-19`). PIC-2 is *derived* — its load-bearing premise is precondition (b) of `snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions`, which lives there alongside `PIC-17`/`PIC-19`; co-locating it makes the snapshot/restore contract closed. The reader-finding concern Option B addresses is met by leaving the existing forward links in `conversation-drive.md`'s and `invocation.md`'s prompt-mode prose unchanged and adding (at most) a one-sentence forward pointer in `conversation-drive.md` near `PIC-18` saying "see PIC-2 at tool-registration-lifetime.md for the cross-body non-overlap derivation." The implementer must watch that the `<a id="pic-2">` anchor string is preserved verbatim across the move so the six inbound links continue to resolve, and that the closing back-reference inside PIC-2 (to `concurrent-invocation-isolation`) is rewritten as a cross-file link rather than left as a stale same-page reference.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Navigability defect only. The rule is fully stated and operative — every inbound citation reaches it by anchor — but a reader who arrives at the prompt-mode surface by topic does not find it where they would expect, and a future contributor editing the snapshot/restore protocol or prompt-mode driver has no local reminder that PIC-2 depends on those rules. No observable behaviour changes; no two reasonable implementers would diverge.
