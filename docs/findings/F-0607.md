## Finding

The *Category-(1)/(3) inventory join key (per-category specialisation)* paragraph in `audit-resolution.md` specifies resolution as an **exact match between `<member>` and the rightmost dot-separated segment of an entry's `path` field**, against the entire `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY`. No clause restricts the candidate entry set to entries of the matching carrier/category. The category is determined by the access shape at the *source* site (a `pi.<member>` access is category (1); a `ctx.<member>` access is category (3)) but is not also required on the inventory side, even though the surrounding text already documents the convention that the leftmost dot-separated segment of `path` names the carrier (e.g. `pi.registerCommand`, `ExtensionContext.modelRegistry`, `ctx.modelRegistry`).

Two concrete consequences follow. First, a `pi.<member>` access resolves against any inventory entry whose rightmost segment matches `<member>`, including category-(3) entries — so a `pi.modelRegistry` access is silently authorised by the existing `ctx.modelRegistry` / `ExtensionContext.modelRegistry` entry, defeating the canonical-carrier rule the same chapter pins under *Target surface categories*. The same hole runs in the other direction for `ctx.<member>` against `pi.<member>` entries. Second, the uniqueness clause (*"the same uniqueness rule applies symmetrically on the rightmost dot-separated segment … `literal-read/duplicate-rightmost-segment`"*) is also unscoped: legitimate independent surfaces that happen to share a member name across carriers — e.g. `pi.shutdown` and `ctx.shutdown` — collide at literal-read time even though they describe disjoint accesses.

Both effects share one root cause: the join key consumes only the rightmost segment when both sides of the join carry the carrier information.

The defect is not the residue of a later refactor; the join key was designed without carrier scoping.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Scope both the resolution rule and the uniqueness rule by carrier on the inventory side, by treating the leftmost dot-separated segment of `path` as the carrier discriminator the same way the surrounding text already informally describes it.

Concretely, amend the *Category-(1)/(3) inventory join key (per-category specialisation)* paragraph so a category-(1) `pi.<member>` access resolves only against inventory entries whose `path`'s **leftmost** dot-separated segment is the literal `pi`, **and** whose rightmost dot-separated segment equals `<member>`; and a category-(3) `ctx.<member>` access resolves only against inventory entries whose `path`'s leftmost dot-separated segment is one of the literals `ctx`, `ExtensionContext`, or `ExtensionCommandContext` (the closed set the spec already pins as the canonical category-(3) carrier annotations), **and** whose rightmost dot-separated segment equals `<member>`. The `path` leftmost-segment carrier convention should be promoted from descriptive prose into a normative MUST on the per-kind shape: every category-(1) audit-participating entry's `path` MUST begin with `pi.`, and every category-(3) audit-participating entry's `path` MUST begin with one of `ctx.`, `ExtensionContext.`, or `ExtensionCommandContext.`. This makes the carrier predicate mechanically checkable at literal-read time under a new `literal-read/<symptom>` token sibling to `literal-read/empty-leftmost-segment`.

Amend the uniqueness clause in parallel: "the same uniqueness rule applies symmetrically on the rightmost dot-separated segment" becomes "the same uniqueness rule applies symmetrically on the rightmost dot-separated segment **within each category** (i.e. within the partition of category-(1) audit-participating entries, and independently within the partition of category-(3) audit-participating entries)". A `pi.shutdown` and a `ctx.shutdown` are then legal as disjoint entries; a second `pi.shutdown` (or a second `ctx.shutdown`) fires `literal-read/duplicate-rightmost-segment` as before. The implementation may keep a single `<symptom>` token or split into per-category variants per the existing "category-specific variant the implementation may coin" delegation.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The leftmost-segment carrier predicate is closed against exactly the literals enumerated above. A future entry with `path: "ExtensionAPI.foo"` (mirroring the category-(3) "type-name as leftmost segment" convention) is **not** authorised under this rule — either coin a category-(1) entry as `pi.foo` instead, or amend the carrier-literal enumeration in the same edit. The minimum-shape literal-read assertion catches the mismatch routably.
- The category-(2) leftmost-segment join key for `@earendil-works/*` named imports is unaffected and the *Category-(2) inventory join key* paragraph needs no edit other than (optionally) a one-sentence pointer noting that the category-(2) uniqueness check is over leftmost segments while category-(1)/(3) is over rightmost segments *within carrier partition* — the two partitions do not interact.
- The existing `type-union-snapshot` `path: "SessionShutdownEvent.reason"` entry resolves under category (2) (named import `SessionShutdownEvent`) and is governed by the leftmost-segment rule; it is not a category-(1)/(3) entry and therefore unaffected by the carrier-scoping edit.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

A reasonable implementer reading the current text will either build the unscoped rightmost-segment match (silently cross-authorising surfaces between `pi.` and `ctx.`, defeating category isolation) or notice the gap and ad-hoc invent the carrier predicate (diverging from any peer implementation). Either way, the inventory-closure audit no longer enforces what it advertises: an out-of-inventory `pi.shutdown` could be silently authorised by an existing `ctx.shutdown` entry, and a legitimate inventory state with both `pi.foo` and `ctx.foo` would fail the literal-read uniqueness gate spuriously.
