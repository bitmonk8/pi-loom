## Problem

Two distinct, independently-failable normative MUSTs about the loom extension's factory boundary in `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md` carry no per-obligation `PIC-N` anchor. (1) The factory never-throw MUST ("The factory MUST NOT throw out of `default function (pi: ExtensionAPI)`…") in the **Extension-bootstrap SDK failures** closing paragraph rides no REQ-ID and is a same-page restatement of `PIC-6` widened to the whole factory body. (2) The synchronous renderer-registration MUST (the Registration timing bullet under `#renderer-registration`, between `PIC-21` and the collision bullets) coins no `PIC-N`. Each fails independently — async registration satisfies (1) but violates (2), a non-renderer throw violates (1) only — and each is cited by separate plan leaves, yet a conformance test, AC line, coverage-matrix row, or `// allow-broad-catch:` token can only cite them by file-and-paragraph path, a GOV-22 non-conformance.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `2d2fc8e` (factory never-throw sentence, 2026-05-06) and `9eea3d9` (Registration timing bullet, 2026-05-06).
**History:** Both sentences were authored within hours of each other (2026-05-06) into the then-monolithic `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md`, before the per-topic split, both un-anchored. `841a085` (2026-06-06) later coined `PIC-21` for Renderer exception safety in the same block but did not extend coinage to its neighbour; the factory-never-throw restatement was left un-anchored when `PIC-6` was independently coined on the narrower probe scope. Neither sentence has been edited materially since; the atomicity gap is structural drift from the pre-PIC-N era, not a regression. The compound-MUST cluster is the predictable downstream effect: every page authored before PIC-N coinage became routine carries the same debt.

## Solution approach

Split the compound prose so each obligation stands alone and coin a sequential `PIC-N` REQ-ID at each defining site (the next free PIC indices after `PIC-23`, verified by `grep -rh 'a id="pic-[0-9]*"'` over `pi-integration-contract/`): one for the factory-body never-throw MUST, one for the synchronous renderer-registration bullet. Cross-reference `PIC-6` from the factory-never-throw anchor as the narrower probe-scope precursor. Register both in the per-page PIC count, add the corresponding coverage-matrix rows, and update each citing plan leaf's spec-reference.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the same-page `PIC-6` restatement placement concern is owned by the systemic PIC cluster — this finding only makes the wider-locus obligation citable.
- Do not retire `PIC-6`.
