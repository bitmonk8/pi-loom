## Finding

`inventory-audit-intro.md`, in the seven-capability cardinality paragraph (the paragraph anchored after `<a id="non-capability-pi-members">`, immediately above the `Inventory-closure audit` block), closes with a sentence that explains the spec's own authoring choice rather than constraining an implementer:

> "Describing the audit's behavioural contract here, rather than its concrete tooling, lets the implementation choose a static-AST walker, a TypeScript-program symbol-table walk, or any equivalent tooling that detects every recognised import/access shape per *Recognised import/access shapes* below and surfaces every violation under the family→discriminator obligations per *Failure-surface contract* below, and prevents the surface-set-closure guarantee … from depending on a tooling claim."

The clause "Describing the audit's behavioural contract here, rather than its concrete tooling, lets …" is meta-commentary about why the spec is written behaviourally; "prevents the surface-set-closure guarantee … from depending on a tooling claim" is design rationale. Neither imposes an obligation on an implementer.

The actual normative latitude it gestures at is already stated, normatively, in the very next paragraph (the `Inventory-closure audit` block): *"PIC pins the audit's behavioural contract; concrete tooling and literal discriminator strings are implementation-owned …"*. The tooling enumeration (static-AST walker / TypeScript-program symbol-table walk) is likewise restated as a normative `MAY` in `audit-recognised-shapes.md`. The rationale sentence therefore conveys nothing an implementer needs and restates latitude that lives elsewhere in normative form.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Delete the rationale sentence from the seven-capability cardinality paragraph. The normative tooling-choice latitude is already carried by the `Inventory-closure audit` paragraph's "concrete tooling and literal discriminator strings are implementation-owned" statement and by the `MAY`-variant clause in `audit-recognised-shapes.md`, so removal loses no obligation.

Edge case for the fixer: do not collapse the surrounding sentences that *do* carry obligations — the preceding clauses pin the `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS`-constant assertion and the markdown-enumeration↔constant↔prose contributor-discipline chain. Excise only the final "Describing the audit's behavioural contract here … from depending on a tooling claim." sentence.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

If shipped unfixed, no observer behaviour diverges and no implementer is misled into wrong behaviour. The cost is purely editorial: a reader of the cardinality paragraph encounters spec-authoring rationale interleaved with normative prose, and the tooling-choice latitude is stated in three places (here, the `Inventory-closure audit` paragraph, and `audit-recognised-shapes.md`), inviting drift.
