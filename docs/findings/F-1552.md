## Finding

The `#session-shutdown-details-conventions` block in `diagnostic-shape.md` is a single, unbroken paragraph of roughly 1,400 words that bundles five distinct, independently-enforceable normative sub-topics for the `session_shutdown` event payload:

1. the `{reason}` field-set closure at loom 1.0.0 (no new `SessionShutdownEvent` siblings without a spec edit);
2. the per-code `details.event.reason` value spaces (the wide `"quit" | "reload" | "new" | "resume" | "fork" | string | "<unreadable>"` union for `loom/runtime/cancelled-by-session-shutdown` versus the closed `{"new","resume","fork"}` set for `loom/host/session-shutdown-runtime-degraded`, which consumers MUST validate per-code, not uniformly);
3. the runtime-constructed sibling carve-out for `details.event.loom` and `details.event.invocation_id` (exempt from the strict-validation entitlement; MUST be present on every structured emission);
4. the presence-entitlement scope that distinguishes structured emissions from the two fallback-path token shapes; and
5. the runtime construction obligation (MUST construct `details.event` as a fresh object with explicit static property names — no `...event` spread, no `Object.assign`, no key-copy loop sourced from the SDK event).

The block carries multiple MUST-bearing rules, yet they are demarcated only by inline italic run-in labels (`*Per-code …*`, `*Runtime-constructed sibling carve-out.*`, `*Presence-entitlement scope …*`, `*Runtime construction obligation.*`) embedded mid-paragraph. The surrounding file uses anchored bold run-in leads as its sub-section device (`**Located-site fields …**`, `**Transient toasts …**`, `**Serialised content format.**`), so a reader or auditor scanning the document's structure sees this entire API/wire-contract specification as a single opaque paragraph behind one lead. The construction obligation in particular — arguably the most consequential MUST in the block, since a spread-style refactor would silently leak future SDK fields onto the wire — is buried at the far end of the paragraph where structure-scanning will not surface it.

This is a wire-contract specification (how implementers construct `details.event.*` objects and how consumers validate them), not a description of a delivery channel. It should read as a headed, navigable section.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** commit-tracked
**Introducing commits:** `52b9101` (2026-05-11) — `pi-loom spec: resolve "T24 — Fork-reason watcher closure leaves the extension in an unspecified, silently degraded state"`
**History:** The *Runtime construction obligation* paragraph was authored in `52b9101` as part of the T24 fix, which introduced the `loom/host/session-shutdown-runtime-degraded` row and the new `details.event.{reason}` nesting convention; the paragraph was added together with the closed-`{reason}` strictness pin it defends, on what was then `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics.md`. The forbidden-construct enumeration ("no `...event` spread, no `Object.assign(..., event, ...)`, no per-key copy loop over `Object.keys(event)`") and the "explicit static property name" positive prescription were present at first appearance, not introduced by a later refactor. The paragraph was carried over to `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md` unchanged in content (only the path changed) by `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04) when the 100KB / 30KB size cap split `diagnostics.md` into an index plus child pages. The misplacement therefore predates the split: the rule was authored on the shape page in `52b9101` while the sibling *Construction-site wrap* / *Hoist obligation* clauses landed on the PIC `diagnostic-emission-isolation` page, and the over-prescriptive framing is the form the rule has had since its single authoring commit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Restructure in place into anchored sub-sections

- **Approach:** Split the single paragraph into discrete anchored bold-lead sub-sections matching the file's existing structural convention — one per the five sub-topics already marked by italic run-in labels (field-set closure, per-code value spaces, runtime-constructed sibling carve-out, presence-entitlement scope, runtime construction obligation). Keep the existing `<a id="session-shutdown-details-conventions"></a>` anchor on the lead sub-section so all inbound cross-links continue to resolve.
- **Spec edits:** Edit only `diagnostic-shape.md`. Introduce sub-section headings/anchors; move no content out of the file; preserve every MUST verbatim.
- **Pros:** Zero inbound-link churn (the 6 cross-references in `code-registry-host.md`, `code-registry-runtime.md`, `active-invocation-registry.md`, `diagnostic-emission-isolation.md`, `session-only-degraded-state.md`, and the in-file `located-site-classification` reference all keep resolving); satisfies anchor-stability governance without repointing; smallest diff.
- **Cons:** Leaves `diagnostic-shape.md` at ~37 KB, still over the 30 KB recommended size cap (though comfortably under the 100 KB hard cap).
- **Risks:** Low. The only risk is leaving the recommended-cap overage in place — an independent, pre-existing condition.

### Option B — Extract to a sibling topic file

- **Approach:** Move the block to a new sibling, e.g. `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/session-shutdown-payload-conventions.md`, headed and sub-sectioned, and replace the in-`diagnostic-shape.md` paragraph with a one-line pointer.
- **Spec edits:** Create the sibling; repoint the `#session-shutdown-details-conventions` anchor's 6 inbound cross-links to the new file; update the in-file `located-site-classification` reference.
- **Pros:** Brings `diagnostic-shape.md` back under the 30 KB recommended cap; gives the wire-contract spec a first-class home distinct from the delivery-channel page.
- **Cons:** Requires repointing ≥6 inbound anchor cross-links across two directories; higher diff surface and more review risk.
- **Risks:** A missed inbound link silently dangles. Anchor-stability governance must be honoured during the move.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A**: restructure in place into anchored bold-lead sub-sections, retaining the `#session-shutdown-details-conventions` anchor on the lead sub-section. It resolves the discoverability defect with no inbound-link churn and no anchor-stability handling. The 30 KB overage is a pre-existing, recommended-only concern independent of this finding and is better addressed as its own size-cap pass. Watch the lead-anchor placement so the six external cross-links still land on the field-set-closure material they cite.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The normative rules are all present and correct, so a careful full-paragraph read yields a working implementation. The risk is discoverability: an implementer or auditor scanning by document structure can miss one of the buried MUSTs — most critically the "fresh object, no spread/assign" construction obligation, whose violation would silently leak future SDK fields onto the wire under a later refactor — or apply one code's `details.event.reason` value space to the other and over- or under-validate.
