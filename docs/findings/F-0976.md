## Problem

DISC-2's *clean leaf-`ENOENT`* rule on `discovery-sources.md` classifies discovery-source outcomes by branching on Node errno strings (`ENOENT` for the missing/unreadable split; `EACCES` / `EPERM` / `ENOTDIR` or any other ancestor-chain code for an unreadable-source warning). Read alone, the bullet presupposes two facts about the `FileSystem` seam without citing where they are established: that `readdir` and `lstat` rejections carry Node-style `.code` values matching those predicates, and that `FakeFileSystem` reproduces those rejections so the ancestor walk is test-callable through the seam. PIC-13 on `host-interfaces-services.md` establishes both facts, but DISC-2 never points at it, so a future edit to PIC-13's `.code` surface carries no textual signal back to the discovery page. The predicates are correct; the defect is the missing cross-page anchor.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `5b772b6` (2026-05-04) — "pi-loom spec: resolve 'Discovery source failure-mode policy is unspecified'" — introduced the **Failure modes** section and *Implementation notes* bullets in `spec_topics/discovery.md` against an `fs.readdir` errno frame, without anchoring to a seam.
- `bc6b182` (2026-05-06) — "pi-loom spec: resolve 'Discovery: clean leaf-ENOENT undefined under Windows ENOENT-aliasing'" — rewrote the implementation note into the present *clean leaf-`ENOENT`* paragraph, locking in the `EACCES` / `EPERM` / `ENOTDIR` branch predicates and naming the `FileSystem` seam exposure of `readdir`/`lstat` but not the `.code` contract per se.
- `8a6d15f` (2026-05-06, ~18 minutes later) — "pi-loom spec: resolve 'FileSystem seam interface signature lives outside the spec corpus'" — landed PIC-13's `.code` JSDoc and the *Rejected `.code` values map onto the discovery rules verbatim* sentence on `pi-integration-contract.md`, supplying the contract DISC-2 had been assuming.

**History:** The defect is the *interaction* of these three commits, not any one of them. `5b772b6` wrote the policy first; `bc6b182` hardened the predicates against errno strings while routing them through the seam; `8a6d15f` then formalised the seam-side `.code` contract — but the discovery page was not edited in `8a6d15f` to back-cite the contract it now relies on, so the predicates and the contract that justifies them live on separate pages without a textual link. The split-spec reorganisation in `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04) and the DISC-1/2/3/4 anchor introduction in `da4bd57` reshaped the surface but did not alter the relationship. Subsequent commits on `discovery-sources.md` did not touch DISC-2's predicate list.

## Solution approach

Add a forward-link from the *clean leaf-`ENOENT`* bullet's errno-predicate point on `discovery-sources.md` to PIC-13 (`#pic-13`) on `host-interfaces-services.md`, anchoring the two load-bearing facts the predicates rely on: the Node-style `.code` rejection shape of `readdir` / `lstat`, and the `FakeFileSystem` mirror that makes the ancestor walk test-callable through the seam.

## Solution constraints

- Do not re-enumerate the errno codes in `discovery-sources.md`; defer to PIC-13's "any other code on the chain is an unreadable-source failure" closure rule so a future broadening of PIC-13's `.code` surface needs no parallel edit here.
- Out of scope: DISC-3/DISC-4 `realpath` anchoring, owned by F-0977.
