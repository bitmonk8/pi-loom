## Problem

In `surface-extensions.md`, the `### Surface extensions without a dedicated topic-page seam` and `### Surface extensions gated on an open design decision` sub-buckets each open with a multi-sentence preamble whose purpose is to justify why their items are excluded from the "Forward-compatibility seams" tally on the Scope page. That is GOV-31 accounting bookkeeping for a count owned elsewhere, not a forward-compatibility obligation, and the inline rationale drifts whenever the seam count changes.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** 0e12140 — pi-loom spec: resolve "Non-goals are not consolidated into a single section" (2026-05-08, Thomas Andersen); 02d7ca5 — pi-loom spec: resolve "T02 surface-extension seam obligations not consistently marked" (2026-05-08, Thomas Andersen); 528cef4 — pi-loom spec: resolve "Surface extensions → Automatic context escalation — embedded open decision" (2026-06-03, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The three cruft sites entered separately. The concurrent-sessions detection-gap prose was seeded when the non-goals were consolidated (0e12140, 2026-05-08) and then accreted across later edits (e.g. the build-time SDK surface-inventory assertion detail arrived with 0229468, 2026-06-06). The "not counted in the tally" exclusion preamble was added with the seam-marking pass (02d7ca5, 2026-05-08). The embedded open-decision item was introduced when the Automatic-context-escalation decision was moved into the surface-extensions page (528cef4, 2026-06-03). The 81bf52a collapse of `spec/` into `spec_topics/` (2026-06-11) relocated all three but did not introduce them.

## Solution approach

Delete the tally-exclusion preamble paragraph under `### Surface extensions without a dedicated topic-page seam` and the parallel exclusion preamble under `### Surface extensions gated on an open design decision` in `surface-extensions.md`. Retain at most a brief note that these sub-buckets' items are not inventoried seams and are therefore excluded from the tally per GOV-31, letting the Scope-page tally definition and GOV-31 own the accounting.

- Out of scope: relocating the "Automatic context escalation" open-decision item itself — that move is owned by F-2535; this finding deletes only the tally-exclusion preamble in its sub-section.
