## Finding

The seven items in `pi-integration-contract/capability-inventory-items.md` are each authored as "Pi MUST expose `<signature>`" (items 1–6) or "Non-bypass looms MUST be able to resolve … via `ctx.modelRegistry`" (item 7). The MUST is grammatically directed at the external owner — Pi — rather than at the loom runtime that consumes the surface. The substance every item carries is loom's *consumption presupposition*: "loom requires these Pi surfaces and refuses to load if they are absent."

The silent-wrong risk is materially neutralised: absence of any factory-probable member is converted to `loom/load/host-incompatible` by `capability-probe.md` Step 0 (c), and `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS` / `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` build-time assertions (owned by `V18a`) close over the set. So the page does not silently assert ownership it lacks; the obligation just *sounds* like it does. It is a mood issue, not structural overreach. The repair is to rephrase in loom's own voice — "loom requires Pi to expose `<signature>`; absence surfaces as `loom/load/host-incompatible` per Step 0 (c)".

The phrasing propagated unchanged: `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04) relocated the items into `capability-inventory-items.md` verbatim, and later edits (`aa334c2`, `5d55c12`, `6932599`, `252acda`, `9ced9f1`) extended items 3 and 5 but reused the `Pi MUST expose …` opening.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Reword items 1–6 so the modal subject is loom, not Pi: replace each leading "`**<name>.**` Pi MUST expose `<signature>` …" with "`**<name>.**` Loom requires Pi to expose `<signature>`; absence surfaces as `loom/load/host-incompatible` per [Step 0 (c)](./capability-probe.md#entry-capability-probe)." Item 7 is already in loom's voice and does not change. Preserve every `<a id="sdk-cap-…">` anchor (inbound cross-references from `spec.md`, PIC-15, plan leaves cite these) and every forward-link tail. Item 5's two sub-clauses (AbortSignal supply + `AgentSession.abort`) are distinct presuppositions — reword both consistently and retain the cross-axis-identity paragraph.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader auditing the page in isolation may read "Pi MUST expose …" as the spec attempting to bind an external project, even though the surrounding probe + audit machinery makes clear the obligation is loom's. No implementer behaviour changes; no test/contract changes. Purely authorial voice at the lens-flagged boundary.
