## Problem

The `pi.on(...)`-surface `Tests` bullet in `V9k` (and its `V9k-T` pair) describes the post-failure observable as "the remaining steps do not execute," a position-independent phrasing that covers three subscriptions registered at three different positions in the sequence (`resources_discover` at step 1, `session_start` at step 3, `session_shutdown` at step 4 per `registration-steps.md`). The set of "remaining steps" differs per event, so the assertion is undecidable as written. For the `session_shutdown` case the phrasing is worse than vague: the Factory-ordering pin requires step 4's subscription to follow step 5's registry/watcher construction, so a literal "remaining steps do not execute" would assert that step 5 must not run — contradicting the plan's own ordering. The sibling `pi.registerFlag` bullet avoids this by pinning the concrete set ("registration steps 2–5 do not execute"); the `pi.on` bullet dropped that concreteness without a per-event substitute.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** 6df646a — pi-loom plan: resolve "extension-bootstrap SDK-failure rule has no closing leaf" (2026-06-12, plan author)
**History:** The `V9k` and `V9k-T` leaves were both created in commit 6df646a, which authored the `pi.on(...)` test bullet with the ambiguous "the remaining steps do not execute" phrasing from the outset. `git log -S "the remaining steps do not execute"` localises the phrase to that single introducing commit; the two later commits that touched these files (cb8737a, the over-bundling resolution, and f264d3a, the `getCommands` drain-state-dependency fix) did not alter the phrasing.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the `pi.on(...)`-surface `Tests` bullet in both `V9k` and `V9k-T` so its abort observable uses position-relative framing instead of the position-independent "the remaining steps do not execute." Express the observable as: no subsequent `pi.register*` / `pi.on` call executes after the failing subscription — matching the spec's `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md` `pi.on(...)` rule ("the factory skips every subsequent `pi.register*` and `pi.on` call"). This framing is well-defined regardless of which of the three events (`resources_discover` / `session_start` / `session_shutdown`) threw and sidesteps the step-numbering-vs-execution-order mismatch created by the step-5 Factory-ordering pin.

## Solution constraints

- The spec is read-only for this fix — the `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md` `pi.on(...)` rule already carries the correct "subsequent call" framing; align the plan bullets to it rather than amending the spec.
