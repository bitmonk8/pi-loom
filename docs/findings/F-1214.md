## Finding

Three independent editorial residues sit in the PIC audit-cluster / capability-probe pages. None of them is load-bearing — each is a paragraph fragment that PIC's own normative rules elsewhere already supersede — but together they read as inattentive prose and one of them (the illustrative member list) is mildly dangerous because it lives inside a strict-textual category-(3) carrier paragraph.

1. **Illustrative `ExtensionContext` member list inside normative category-(3) text.** `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-target-categories.md` (the *Target surface categories* paragraph) names thirteen illustrative members — `waitForIdle`, `cwd`, `ui`, `model`, `modelRegistry`, `isIdle`, `getContextUsage`, `hasPendingMessages`, `compact`, `shutdown`, `getSystemPrompt`, `sessionManager`, `signal` — and is forced to immediately disclaim them with "this listing is illustrative only and the audit's category-(3) closed set MUST NOT be seeded from it". The very next sentence pins the SDK type declarations at `dist/core/extensions/types.d.ts` as "the single source of truth for category (3)'s closed in-scope set" and obligates the implementation to derive entries from them rather than from any prose enumeration. The list contributes no normative content; its presence inside a paragraph whose entire point is that prose enumerations MUST NOT seed the inventory is itself a category-(3) seeding trap a hasty implementer could fall into.

2. **Disjoint-diagnostic obligation appears twice, with different modal force.** The relationship between the `loom/load/host-incompatible` and `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved` diagnostics is pinned twice in PIC:
   - `capability-probe.md` step (c) closes with "The two diagnostic surfaces (`loom/load/host-incompatible` and `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved`) MUST stay disjoint."
   - `audit-wire-and-canary.md`, in the item-5 / item-7 capability-verification paragraph, restates the same fact as bare prose: "the two diagnostic surfaces (`loom/load/host-incompatible` and `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved`) are disjoint."
   Two sites; only one carries the MUST. That asymmetric duplication invites drift on the next edit to either page, and the `audit-wire-and-canary.md` statement adds no information the `capability-probe.md` MUST does not already pin.

3. **"Deferred past loom 1.0" rationale clauses on closed soft-surface acknowledgments.** Three paragraphs trail their normative conclusion with a "this is a PIC edit deferred past loom 1.0" tail clause:
   - `audit-resolution.md` *Audit scope* — Generated-output-in-src limitation: "…widening the inventory-closure audit's family-set to subsume this check is a PIC edit deferred past loom 1.0."
   - `audit-resolution.md` *Exemption mechanism* — Citation-token grammar: "…widening the audit to validate citation resolution against a registry is a PIC edit deferred past loom 1.0."
   - `audit-failures.md` *Stale-citation limitation*: "…widening the audit to validate citation resolution would require attaching a registry of valid REQ-IDs and PIC anchors to the audit input, which is a PIC edit deferred past loom 1.0."
   Each of the three already states its closed normative conclusion (the family-set is exhaustive at five, citation grammar is purely lexical, no audit discriminator is owed). The "deferred past loom 1.0" tail clauses are roadmap commentary about hypothetical post-1.0 work — they neither change today's behaviour nor live in the deferral page that owns such roadmap entries (`future-considerations/`), so they read as decision-log residue on what is otherwise a closed obligation.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Apply three pinpoint deletions in PIC; no normative content changes.

1. In `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-target-categories.md`, *Target surface categories* paragraph, category-(3) clause: delete the sentence beginning *"Illustrative members at the loom 1.0 pin include `waitForIdle`, `cwd`, …, and `signal`; this listing is illustrative only and the audit's category-(3) closed set MUST NOT be seeded from it."* in its entirety. The immediately following sentence (which pins `dist/core/extensions/types.d.ts` as the single source of truth and the obligation to derive entries from the SDK declarations rather than from any prose enumeration) is preserved unchanged and stands on its own.

2. In `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-wire-and-canary.md`, item-5 / item-7 capability-verification paragraph: drop the trailing clause *"and the two diagnostic surfaces (`loom/load/host-incompatible` and `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved`) are disjoint"* (and its preceding "; it is **not** factory-probable" conjunction — keep the "not factory-probable" claim as its own sentence). Replace with a single back-reference to the canonical MUST in `capability-probe.md` step (c) so the disjointness obligation has one canonical site. The MUST in `capability-probe.md` is the keeper.

3. In `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-resolution.md` (two occurrences) and `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-failures.md` (one occurrence), delete the tail clauses "…is a PIC edit deferred past loom 1.0" (and "…widening the audit to validate citation resolution would require attaching a registry of valid REQ-IDs and PIC anchors to the audit input, which is a PIC edit deferred past loom 1.0"), keeping the normative conclusions that immediately precede them ("…but PIC does not require it." / "…and is **not** owed an additional audit discriminator in loom 1.0." / the analogous sibling). The closed family-set rule, the purely-lexical citation-grammar rule, and the explicit "not owed an additional audit discriminator" conclusion already pin the loom 1.0 behaviour without the post-1.0 roadmap commentary.

Edge cases for the implementer:

- The illustrative listing's deletion must be a clean deletion of the entire sentence — leaving a stub of the list (e.g. just "`waitForIdle`") would be worse than leaving the whole listing, since it would still seed without the disclaimer.
- For item 2, do **not** delete the disjointness statement from both sites. The MUST in `capability-probe.md` step (c) is the keeper; only the bare-prose copy in `audit-wire-and-canary.md` is the duplicate.
- For item 3, the three deletions are pure tail-clause trims; the sentences before the "; widening …" / "which is a PIC edit deferred past loom 1.0" tails are the surviving normative content and must not be touched.
- No `<a id>` anchors, REQ-IDs, or cross-references are affected by any of the three deletions (the affected spans are pure mid-paragraph prose, not anchor sites or named clauses anything else links to). A `grep -rn` sweep over `docs/` after the edit should still resolve cleanly.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Item 1 carries a small misreading risk (an implementer could attempt to "be helpful" and hard-code the thirteen members into `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY`, contradicting the next sentence); items 2 and 3 are pure editorial dross. None of the three changes any observable behaviour, diagnostic shape, audit record, or test fixture. Leaving them in ships a working system that simply reads as inattentive in three places.
