## Problem

`V7d` (and its paired `V7d-T`) bundles three independently-testable deliverables in one leaf: the core `loom-system-note` delivery surface (the `sendMessage` envelope with renderer registration, per-`.loom` multi-error batching, re-scan re-emit, and the producer-facing diagnostic-emission seam), the `PIC-21` renderer-throw trap, and the `PIC-54` `system-note-delivery-failed` fallback chain. Both resilience paths are red-testable in complete isolation from the core delivery and do not depend on the batching/seam machinery being exercised in the same leaf. `conventions.md` §3 defines a vertical leaf as "the smallest feature that can ship and be tested independently", and `coverage-matrix.md` already tracks `PIC-21` and `PIC-54` as their own rows — confirming they are distinct closing obligations rather than facets of the core delivery.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-at-leaf-inception
**Introducing commits:** `692cc8c` ("pi-loom plan: resolve \"V7a bundles diagnostics primitive with loom-system-note channel\"")
**History:** `git log --follow docs/plan_topics/V7d-system-note-channel.md` shows the leaf was created in `692cc8c` (2026-06-17) when `V7d` was peeled off an over-large `V7a`. The new `V7d` was authored from the outset bundling the core delivery surface, the `PIC-21` renderer-throw trap, and the `PIC-54` fallback chain in a single `Adds`/`Tests` block — the bundling was present at the leaf's first commit, not introduced by a later edit. The only subsequent change (`47915a2`) added the `runtime-event-channel.md#pic-54` anchor to the existing fallback-chain bullet and did not alter the bundling.

## Solution approach

Narrow `V7d` and `V7d-T` to the core delivery path — the `loom-system-note` envelope and renderer registration, multi-error batching, re-scan re-emit, and the producer-facing diagnostic-emission seam — and narrow `V7d`'s `Ships when` to the batched-envelope delivery criterion. Add a new `V7`-phase leaf (with its paired `-T`) under §V7 in `docs/plan.md` owning the `PIC-21` renderer-throw trap and the `PIC-54` fallback chain, with `Deps` including `V7d` and `H4a`, and move the `PIC-21` and `system-note-delivery-failed` `Tests` bullets onto it. Retarget the `PIC-21` and `PIC-54` closing-leaf cells in `coverage-matrix.md` to the new leaf's ID, and add the new leaf to `H5b`'s coverage-producing `Deps` set.

## Solution constraints

- The existing `V7d` consumers (`V1a`, `V9a`, `V9d`, `V9k`, `V9p`, `V9q`) depend on the producer-facing seam that stays on `V7d`; leave their `Deps` edges unchanged.
- The new leaf ID follows the `V<N><letter>` scheme (next free letter in the `V7` phase) and must not reuse a retired ID, per `## Plan rules`.
