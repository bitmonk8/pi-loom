## Problem

The opening **Provider error mapping** paragraph in `provider-error-mapping.md` carries a version-coupled MUST ("MUST be re-validated on each upgrade"), but its only delegated verification routes to SHOULD-level item (i) (`bump-checklist-provider-overflow-wording`) of the editorial-review checklist, and V18c's "Ships when" predicate treats a recorded `fail` / `not-performed` on the SHOULD-level (f)–(ak) range as a conformant, non-blocking bump outcome. There is therefore no observable artefact whose absence or red state can declare the MUST unmet: two contributors can record opposite conformance verdicts on the same Pi minor bump. The sibling **Provider seed-field mapping** paragraph (`provider-seed-field-mapping`) carries identical version-coupled-MUST wording but is backed by a build-time `Api`-coverage assertion plus a fixture rerun gated by V18c; the error-mapping side has the wording without the apparatus.

## Solution approach

Give the re-validation MUST a falsifiable predicate by mirroring the enforcement model the **Provider seed-field mapping** paragraph (`provider-seed-field-mapping`) already uses two paragraphs below in the same file: a mechanical bump-procedure gate that reruns the provider-error fixtures, paired with the captured-error-body corpus refresh obligation. Promote or replace SHOULD-level item (i) (`bump-checklist-provider-overflow-wording`) so the re-validation obligation becomes MUST-level and observable. Rewrite the *Re-validation gate (loom 1.0.0)* clause on `provider-error-mapping` to point at the new mechanical gate instead of deferring it.

## Solution constraints

- The build-time gate obligation is out of scope for the `V9j` plan leaf — it owns the runtime classifier, not the bump procedure.
- Promoting item (i) to MUST-level must not break V18c's "Ships when" conformance predicate, which currently keys MUST-level vs SHOULD-level on the (a)–(e) / (f)–(ak) item-index range.
