## Finding

The opening paragraph of `provider-error-mapping.md` reads:

> The matching rules are version-coupled to `@earendil-works/pi-ai` and MUST be re-validated on each upgrade.

The page then routes "re-validation" to the *Re-validation gate (loom 1.0.0)* clause, which explicitly defers a mechanical CI-wired gate to post-loom 1.0.0 maintenance and points instead at item (i) of the *Editorial-review checklist for unpinned host presuppositions* in `version-bump-step2.md`. Item (i) is SHOULD-level — the contributor "SHOULD re-run the provider-error fixtures … and confirm each overflow signature still matches" — and the V18c "Ships when" predicate states that on items (f)–(ak) "a recorded `fail` / `not-performed` … is a conformant non-blocking outcome." A contributor can therefore land a Pi minor bump that records item (i) as `not-performed`, or that records `fail` after the fixtures red, and still ship a conformant bump.

The MUST on the spec page and its only delegated verification mechanism are jointly unfalsifiable: there is no observable artefact whose absence or red state can be cited to declare the MUST unmet, and two reasonable contributors can record opposite conformance verdicts on the same bump. The companion **Provider seed-field mapping** paragraph two paragraphs below in the same file demonstrates the contrasting model — its own version-coupled MUST is backed by the build-time `Api`-coverage assertion plus the step-6 fixture rerun, both gated by V18c. The provider-error-mapping side carries the same wording but none of the apparatus.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Withdraw the loom 1.0.0 deferral and wire the fixture rerun as a MUST-level bump-procedure step

**Approach.** Mirror the existing **Provider seed-field mapping** treatment on the error-mapping side. Add a new step to `version-bump-triggers.md` ("**Re-validate the provider-error fixtures.** Run the provider-error fixtures against the candidate `@earendil-works/pi-ai` minor; the build asserts a green run before merge"), define the captured-error-body corpus refresh as the contributor's same-commit obligation, and promote item (i) of `version-bump-step2.md` from SHOULD to MUST (or remove it in favour of the new step). On the spec page, delete the *Re-validation gate (loom 1.0.0)* clause and replace it with the same sentence shape the seed-field paragraph uses: "the mechanical gate is the provider-error fixture run rerun as step N of [Pi version bump procedure] below."

**Spec edits.** `provider-error-mapping.md` *Re-validation gate (loom 1.0.0)* paragraph; `version-bump-triggers.md` numbered-step list (insert new step); `version-bump-step2.md` item (i) (promote to MUST or delete); `V9j` / `V18c` leaf bodies in the plan to surface the new gate (V18c `Tests.` and `Ships when.`).

**Pros.** Restores joint falsifiability of the MUST against an observable build-output state. Aligns the two version-coupled MUSTs in the same file under the same enforcement model. The fixture infrastructure already exists in `npm test`, so the gate is a wiring change rather than a new test surface.

**Cons.** Increases the merge-blocking surface of every Pi minor bump (a regressed fixture now blocks merge instead of being a recorded `fail`). Promotes a fixture corpus whose freshness depends on out-of-band provider behaviour into the gating path; a provider rewording between captures and bump can produce false-red merges. Reverses an explicit prior decision that the deferral was a `correctness` finding the project would defer past 1.0.0.

**Risks.** The captured-error-body corpus has no automated freshness signal; the gate's correctness still rests on contributor discipline at the corpus-refresh boundary that item (i) already names. Wiring a MUST against a corpus that the spec itself describes as contributor-maintained transfers the unfalsifiability from one site to another unless the bump procedure also pins how the corpus is re-captured. The fix MUST state both halves: the fixture-run gate and the corpus-refresh trigger.

### Option B — Downgrade the spec sentence to SHOULD to match the existing SHOULD-level verification

**Approach.** Keep the loom 1.0.0 deferral intact and resolve the falsifiability gap by aligning the obligation level: rewrite the spec-page sentence as "The matching rules are version-coupled to `@earendil-works/pi-ai` and SHOULD be re-validated on each upgrade per item (i) of the *Editorial-review checklist for unpinned host presuppositions* in [Pi version bump procedure](./version-bump-intro.md#pi-version-bump-procedure) below." The *Re-validation gate (loom 1.0.0)* clause then becomes the rationale for the SHOULD level rather than a deferral attached to a stronger MUST.

**Spec edits.** `provider-error-mapping.md` opening paragraph (MUST → SHOULD); the *Re-validation gate (loom 1.0.0)* clause rephrased as rationale; no `version-bump-step2.md` change required.

**Pros.** Smallest edit; preserves the deliberate post-1.0.0 deferral the corpus has already chosen. RFC 2119 SHOULD is satisfied by item (i)'s mandatory-recording shape (the contributor records `pass`/`fail`/`N/A`/`not-performed` for every item; a `not-performed` SHOULD is conformant), so MUST and verification become jointly consistent without expanding the merge gate.

**Cons.** Demotes the version-coupled obligation across two visually parallel paragraphs in the same file — the **Provider seed-field mapping** paragraph below retains its MUST plus the `Api`-coverage gate, producing an asymmetry where the spec treats two functionally analogous version-couplings as different obligation strengths. A future reviewer may re-raise the asymmetry as a separate finding.

**Risks.** The downgrade may understate the load-bearing role of the provider-error classifier (a silent rewording produces a real downgraded-error misclassification). If the project later restores the mechanical gate, the SHOULD must be re-promoted to MUST in the same edit, increasing churn.

### Recommendation

Option A. The asymmetry against the sibling **Provider seed-field mapping** treatment is the deciding factor: the spec already encodes the answer for the same class of obligation two paragraphs down, and adopting that template removes the falsifiability gap without inventing new apparatus. Implementer edge cases the fixer must watch:

- The new bump-procedure step is the third version-coupled MUST in `version-bump-triggers.md` (joining the snapshot/type-equality step 5 and the seed-field step 6); insert it as step 6.5 (renumbering downstream) or as a new step 8, and update every "step N" cross-reference in `V18c` / `V18c-T` / `version-bump-step2.md` item (i).
- Item (i) in `version-bump-step2.md` is currently a SHOULD-level editorial-checklist item, and `V18c`'s "Ships when" predicate distinguishes MUST-level items (a)–(e) from SHOULD-level items (f)–(ak) by index range. Promoting (i) to MUST requires either moving it out of the (f)–(ak) range or changing V18c's predicate to key on per-item level rather than index range.
- The fixture-rerun step must name the captured-error-body corpus as the same-commit refresh trigger so the gate's correctness does not transfer the unfalsifiability to corpus freshness; word the step in the same shape as step 6's "consult pi-ai release notes" sentence so the obligation is concrete.
- The plan-side `V9j` leaf's **Adds** / **Ships when** lines name the classifier table; the new gate belongs on `V18c` (which already owns the seed-field assertion), not on `V9j`. Do not add a build-time gate obligation to `V9j` — it owns the runtime classifier, not the bump procedure.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

The textual MUST cannot be confirmed met or unmet from any artefact the spec defines, so two implementers can reach opposite conformance verdicts on the same Pi minor bump. Operationally, a silent provider-error rewording downgrades a real `ContextOverflowError` to `TransportError` with null token counts (the spec acknowledges this in the *Provider-owned-wording presupposition*); the missing falsifiability means no automated signal exists to surface that degradation at the bump that ought to catch it.
