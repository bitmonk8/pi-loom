## Finding

The §*Recovery path and prohibitions* section of [`docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/session-only-degraded-state.md`](../../docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/session-only-degraded-state.md) carries four independently-satisfiable MUST NOT obligations as an unannotated bullet list — "MUST NOT un-drain the registry", "MUST NOT re-subscribe or re-establish the closed `discoveryWatcher` / `settingsWatcher`", "MUST NOT poll for `.loom` or settings changes after watcher closure", and "MUST NOT introduce any other discovery, re-scan, or self-recovery path". None of the four carries a REQ-ID anchor, and the enclosing section's only anchor is the GitHub-auto-slug `#recovery-path-and-prohibitions`. The four obligations land in the coverage matrix as a single GOV-22-residue token (`cka-27`), bundled with three other unrelated obligations on the same page.

The defect is the per-obligation surface: a future test (or a future spec edit) that touches only the un-drain rule, only the polling rule, or only the open-ended "no other self-recovery path" rule has no fragment-link target to cite, and the [`coverage-matrix.md`](../../docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md) `cka-27` aggregator gives no way to assert that an individual bullet is closed without claiming closure of all four. GOV-9's `#prefix-n` cross-link contract is therefore unreachable for these obligations until they are coined as REQ-IDs under the page's prefix, which is the residue-class GOV-22 was added to drain.

The original framing's suggestion to register a fresh `DEGR` prefix is incorrect: this page already lives under the `pi-integration-contract/` subtree, whose [REQ-ID prefix-table](../../docs/spec_topics/governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-a.md) row binds the prefix `PIC` (live IDs `PIC-1`…`PIC-22`). The correct coinage is four new `PIC-N` anchors (next-free integers, currently `PIC-23`…`PIC-26`) under the existing row.
