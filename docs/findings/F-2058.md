## Finding

The terminal-outcomes aggregator paragraph in `overview-and-orientation.md` (anchor `#terminal-outcomes-aggregator`) introduces the capitalised term **"Failure-cause enumeration"** inline — "the following hard-ceiling cases are excluded from the Failure-cause enumeration: (a) … (b) …" — without rendering it as a link or otherwise defining it at the point of first use. The term denotes the *Failure* arm of the closed terminal-outcomes set, which is owned and enumerated on a different page: [Errors and Results — Terminal outcomes](../errors-and-results/error-model.md#terminal-outcomes).

A reader of this paragraph cannot tell, at the moment the exclusions are stated, *which* enumeration the exclusions modify. The first resolving link to `error-model.md#terminal-outcomes` appears only later, buried inside exclusion item (a) ("per the binder argument-binding failure item in [Errors and Results — Terminal outcomes]"), and exclusion item (b) ("ceiling #4's *in-loop* model-driven tool-call args row") carries no link to the owning enumeration at all — it links only to the `in-loop` glossary entry. The capitalised styling signals a defined term of art, but the orientation paragraph neither anchors it nor points at its owner up front.

The owning page itself uses the same term in prose within the **Failure** bullet of *Terminal outcomes*; it has no dedicated anchor, so the correct resolution target for any link is the section anchor `#terminal-outcomes`.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 8168ea8 — pi-loom spec: resolve "T30 ceiling #4 model-driven row trichotomy carve-out" (2026-05-10, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The terminal-outcomes aggregator paragraph and its "excluded from the Failure-cause enumeration: (a) … (b) …" exclusion list were added in 8168ea8; the capitalised term was coined inline there with the resolving `Terminal outcomes` link already buried inside item (a) and absent from the in-loop exclusion item, so the defect is present in the introducing commit. Later commits moved the content (file split 81bf52a, 2026-06-11) and reworded the exclusion items but never linked the term at first use.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In the `#terminal-outcomes-aggregator` paragraph, render the first occurrence of "Failure-cause enumeration" as a link to [Errors and Results — Terminal outcomes](../errors-and-results/error-model.md#terminal-outcomes) (the owning *Failure* bullet of the closed terminal-outcomes set). Additionally, add the same link to exclusion item (b) ("ceiling #4's *in-loop* model-driven tool-call args row") so both excluded items point an implementer back at the enumeration they narrow. Use the existing section anchor `#terminal-outcomes`; the term has no dedicated anchor on the owning page and one is not required.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader following the orientation paragraph must scan ahead into the exclusion items to discover which enumeration is being narrowed, and exclusion item (b) gives them no anchor at all. The enumeration is ultimately resolvable (the paragraph links its owner at the end), so no divergent implementation results; the cost is reading friction and reduced discoverability in an entry-point aggregator.
