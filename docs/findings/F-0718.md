## Problem

Version-bump checklist item (a) (`#bump-checklist-instance-survival` in `version-bump-step2.md`, mirrored in `host-prerequisites.md` clause (a) at `#degraded-state-host-prerequisites`) is a MUST-level gate that requires the contributor to record an "authoritative determination" of whether loom's package-loaded `extensions/index.ts` is governed by Pi's documented teardown-and-rebind lifecycle or "provably differs" from it ŌĆö but neither qualifier is defined. The text names no acceptable evidence, no actor empowered to issue the determination, and no acceptance criterion that distinguishes a conformant record from a non-conformant one, so two contributors can record opposite verdicts on the same Pi minor and both be formally conformant. Because item (a)'s outcome flips the session-only degraded-state branch (SM-3b / SM-4 / SM-5 / SM-6) on or off, the missing evidentiary standard is load-bearing rather than incidental.

## Solution approach

Clarify item (a) at `#bump-checklist-instance-survival` (and its mirror in `host-prerequisites.md` clause (a) at `#degraded-state-host-prerequisites`) so the gate names an evidentiary standard: what evidence makes a determination conformant, who is the actor of record, and what acceptance criterion separates a conformant record from a non-conformant one. Add the item-(a) recording convention to *Outcome recording conventions* (`#bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions`). Add forward cross-references from the affected branch leaves ŌĆö SM-4 in `session-model-and-appendix.md` and the degraded branch in `session-only-degraded-state.md` ŌĆö so an implementer reading there sees that an item (a) outcome gates the branch.

- Out of scope: deciding item (a)'s actual verdict for any given Pi minor ŌĆö the underlying lifecycle contradiction is resolved by the contradiction-resolution finding; this finding defines only the shape a conformant record must take.
