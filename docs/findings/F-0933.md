## Finding

The V9l leaf's **Deps** field carries two structurally identical "(a)" references in a single sentence: "this leaf is **blocked** until the **version-bump editorial-review checklist item (a)** authoritatively resolves the **host-prerequisites clause (a)** open contradiction." The two "(a)" labels denote different artefacts at different anchors — "version-bump editorial-review checklist item (a)" is a procedural audit step (`version-bump-step2.md#bump-checklist-instance-survival`), while "host-prerequisites clause (a)" is the normative presupposition / open-contradiction statement (`host-prerequisites.md#degraded-state-host-prerequisites`). Neither reference carries its anchor link inline, so a reader has only the bare "(a)" tokens to disambiguate them.

The same parallel "(a)" structure is repeated in `plan.md` §Blocked obligations, where the V9l bullet says the leaf is "blocked on the host-prerequisites clause (a) open contradiction … owned by version-bump editorial-review checklist item (a)." A reader resolving the block could conflate the two and misidentify which artefact must change before the block lifts (the spec clause records the contradiction; the checklist step is where the authoritative resolution is performed and recorded). The phrase "open contradiction" is also left uncharacterised at the V9l leaf except via the forward link to §Blocked obligations.

The block mechanism itself is sound — V9l's `Deps` edge on `V18c` correctly gates pickability — so execution does not break; this is a readability gap in the prose that explains the block.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Disambiguate the two references by giving each a distinct name and pinning each to its anchor, in both locations that carry the parallel structure:

- In `docs/plan_topics/V9l-session-only-degraded-branch.md`, **Deps** field — rewrite the blocking clause so the two "(a)" tokens read as distinct named artefacts: refer to the procedural step as the **version-bump checklist step (a)** linked to `../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md#bump-checklist-instance-survival`, and to the normative clause as the **host-prerequisites.md clause (a)** linked to `../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md#degraded-state-host-prerequisites`. The corrected sentence states that the leaf is blocked until the version-bump checklist step (a) authoritatively resolves the host-prerequisites.md clause (a) open contradiction.
- In `docs/plan.md`, §Blocked obligations (V9l bullet) — apply the same two distinct names so the bullet and the leaf agree: the host-prerequisites.md clause (a) is the open contradiction; the version-bump checklist step (a) is the deciding resolution's owner.

Both spec anchors already exist (`#degraded-state-host-prerequisites` and `#bump-checklist-instance-survival`), so this is a wording-and-link change only; no spec edit and no change to the block mechanism (V9l's `Deps` edge on `V18c`).

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader determining whether the V9l block has lifted could conflate the procedural checklist step with the normative spec clause and inspect or edit the wrong artefact. Pickability is unaffected (the `Deps` edge on `V18c` gates it correctly) and the forward links resolve, so no implementer produces a wrong leaf — the cost is reader friction at the block-resolution boundary.
