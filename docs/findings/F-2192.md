## Problem

The **Closure** paragraph in `placeholder-rendering-a.md` routes every *Message*-column `<…>` token through five buckets; bucket (a) covers placeholders enumerated in categories 1–7, "rendered byte-identically per its category's rule". `<required>` is enumerated in category 4, so the closure classifies its `loom/load/host-incompatible` occurrence as a bucket-(a) token governed by category 4's numeric rule. But the §4 *Scope of `<required>` / `<provided>`* note removes `<required>` on `loom/load/host-incompatible` from that numeric rule and pins it per `kind` (a SemVer range, a tilde-range pin, or a closed literal) at `placeholder-rendering-b.md#host-incompatible-observed-required`. The closure therefore asserts a rendering rule for that token that the body of §4 directly disclaims, leaving the build-time-enforced closure surface internally inconsistent for it.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commit:** `fa9f2d5` — "pi-loom spec: resolve \"host-incompatible per-kind observed/required rendering\"" (2026-06-02)
**History:** `fa9f2d5` introduced the §4 *Scope of `<required>` / `<provided>`* note that removed `<required>` on `loom/load/host-incompatible` from category 4's numeric rule and redirected it to the new per-`kind` `<observed>` / `<required>` enumeration. That commit did not add a matching carve-out clause to the **Closure** statement, so the closure continued to assert (via bucket (a)) that the token renders "per its category's rule." A later closure-completeness fix, `72e2c31` — "Placeholder-rendering closure omits live placeholders" (2026-06-05) — added the parallel parsed-scalar `<observed>` carve-out as clause (e) but left the analogous `<required>` gap unaddressed. The inconsistency has persisted since `fa9f2d5`.

## Solution approach

Add a carve-out clause to the **Closure** paragraph in `placeholder-rendering-a.md` for `<required>` on `loom/load/host-incompatible`, and qualify bucket (a) to exclude that usage — parallel to the existing clause (e) / bucket (b) treatment of the parsed-scalar `<observed>` usage. Have the clause point at the per-`kind` enumeration anchor `host-incompatible-observed-required` in `placeholder-rendering-b.md` as the source of truth, mirroring the redirect §4 already makes.

## Solution constraints

- Keep the per-`kind` enumeration at `placeholder-rendering-b.md#host-incompatible-observed-required` as the single source of truth; do not duplicate the per-`kind` `<required>` values into the closure clause.
- `<required>` must remain enumerated in category 4 for the arity sites (`loom/parse/invoke-arity-too-few` / `loom/parse/invoke-arity-too-many`); the build-time closure admission check is unchanged.
