## Problem

The glossary's `in-loop` entry (`docs/spec_topics/glossary.md`, `<a id="in-loop">`) exceeds the glossary's self-declared descriptive role. Beyond reminding the reader what *in-loop* means, it states full normative ceiling-#4 routing: the no-`Err`-at-firing / no-`loom-system-note`-at-firing prescription, the `validation`-runtime-event suppression on the model-driven row, and the `tool_loop_exhausted` surface that becomes reachable only at ceiling-#2 exhaustion. That routing's canonical home is the `#ceiling-4-table-reconciliation` paragraph on `docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md`, and ceiling #2's section owns the `tool_loop_exhausted` exhaustion surface. The descriptive surface thus duplicates a normative rule that already lives at its declared canonical site, inviting drift if one is edited and the other is not.

The defect is thus present since the entry's inception, not introduced by a later edit.

## Solution approach

Narrow the `in-loop` glossary entry back to a descriptive reminder: keep the one-sentence definition of *in-loop* and the V1-instance note (ceiling #4's model-driven tool-call args row, a `maxDepth` schema check), and defer the routing rule to its canonical page. Delete the normative routing prose — the no-`Err` / no-`loom-system-note`-at-firing prescription, the `validation`-runtime-event suppression clause, and the ceiling-#2 `tool_loop_exhausted` exhaustion routing. Re-point the entry's `See:` link at `./hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md#ceiling-4-table-reconciliation` so readers reach the canonical statement directly.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: do not add the removed routing prose to `ceilings-3-and-4.md` — `#ceiling-4-table-reconciliation` already states it and ceiling #2's section owns the `tool_loop_exhausted` exhaustion surface.
- Preserve the `<a id="in-loop">` anchor and the entry's alphabetical position; cross-page links (e.g. `ceilings-3-and-4.md` set-level invariants resolve `../glossary.md#in-loop`) depend on the anchor surviving.
