## Finding

The `## SDK capability inventory` section in `inventory-audit-intro.md` (anchor `sdk-capability-inventory`, PIC-15) is named after a single concept — the enumeration of the seven named SDK capabilities — but its body actually establishes three distinct, independently-anchored obligations:

- the seven-capability enumeration plus the non-capability `pi.<member>` surfaces (`#non-capability-pi-members`);
- the **inventory-closure audit** (`#sdk-cap-inventory-closure-audit`), a negative-direction build-time check that resolves every Pi-side surface reference against `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY`, which the section itself states is "strictly broader than the seven capabilities"; and
- the **strict-capability absence-under-the-probed-name arm** (`#strict-capability-absence-under-probed-name`), a literal-read assertion on the model-registry surface.

A reader navigating the corpus by heading expects a capability list and finds two further normative MUSTs whose subject (a surface-closure audit, a rename-detection gate) is broader than capability cardinality. The heading therefore underadvertises the section's normative weight: someone scanning the table of contents for the closure-audit contract or the strict-capability gate has no heading-level signal that they live here, and the closure audit's own name ("inventory-closure", over the broader `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY`) actively cuts against the capability framing of its parent heading.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `e5cd514` (2026-05-09, "pi-loom spec: resolve T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit") + `6829a79` (2026-06-06, "pi-loom spec: resolve Pi-SDK hidden-assumption cluster (strictCapable absence, decode steps, pi.on origin)")
**History:** The two contested phrasings were not introduced together. `e5cd514` first coined "the positive-direction literal-read assertion that owns items 1–7" inside the new *Inventory-closure audit* paragraph (then on the monolithic `pi-integration-contract.md`), at the point the negative-direction closure audit was added as a sibling of the seven-capability inventory. A month later `6829a79` (the Pi-SDK hidden-assumption-cluster resolution that pinned the strictCapable-absence claim and added its absence-under-the-probed-name MUST) authored "the existing `strict-capability-probe` literal-read assertion (per [step 2(a)](…))" — placed in `audit-target-categories.md` (then `audit-recognised-shapes.md`), naming the assertion via the entry-kind name in order to point at the existing step-2(a) gate the new MUST extends, without reconciling against the *Inventory-closure audit* paragraph's "positive-direction" phrasing. `bf92fe6` (2026-06-25, F-1290) then relocated the MUST sentence from `audit-target-categories.md` to `inventory-audit-intro.md` so it sits in the same file as the *Inventory-closure audit* paragraph, which is the configuration the current finding observes; the relocation was scoped to placement and did not reword either phrasing. The defect is therefore the interaction between the two earlier coining commits, surfaced into one-page co-location by the later relocation rather than introduced by it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Keep `## SDK capability inventory` as the section heading covering PIC-15's capability enumeration paragraph, and introduce two subordinate `###` headings inside the section so navigation matches scope:

- `### Inventory-closure audit` immediately before the `<a id="sdk-cap-inventory-closure-audit">` block, and
- `### Strict-capability absence-under-the-probed-name arm` immediately before the `<a id="strict-capability-absence-under-probed-name">` block.

Leave the existing `<a id>` anchor tags in place on the bold lead lines so every inbound deep-link continues to resolve. This surfaces all three obligations in the table of contents without renaming the stable `sdk-capability-inventory` anchor or touching any cross-reference.

Edge case for the implementer: if the related finding "The term \"reachable\" is not defined anywhere in the shard." is resolved by relocating the strict-capability arm out to `version-bump-step2.md`, drop the second `###` heading and add it there instead — do not add a subordinate heading for content that will leave this section.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The section's three obligations are all fully specified in the body and carry their own anchors, so no implementer who reads the section is misled. The cost is navigational: the heading and table-of-contents entry advertise a capability list while two normative audit obligations hide beneath it, raising the chance a reader scanning by heading overlooks the closure-audit and strict-capability contracts.
