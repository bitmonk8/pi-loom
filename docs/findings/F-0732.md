## Finding

The GOV-15 paragraph — restated in the **Source-language stability** NFR bullet at `spec_topics/overview-and-orientation.md` (anchor `source-language-stability`) and owned at `spec_topics/governance/source-language-stability.md` (anchor `gov-15`) — uses the phrase *"the exclusion list"* in a sentence with no unambiguous antecedent:

> *"Observable (c)'s equivalence relation, not the exclusion list, governs variation that is embedded as a placeholder sub-field inside a content string."*

The same paragraph names two distinct lists, and the sentence is grammatical against either:

1. The **variable-placeholder normalisation list** introduced one sentence earlier — the per-invocation identifiers (`<invocation-id>` / `<uuid>`), the wall-clock-derived sub-fields (`<ms>`), and the category-8 host-derived freeform tails — classified by [Diagnostics — Placeholder rendering](../spec_topics/diagnostics/placeholder-rendering-a.md#placeholder-rendering-normative). These are *normalised away* before observable (c)'s byte-identicality test; they are not excluded from the equivalence promise.
2. The **independent-observables exclusion list** named explicitly two sentences later as *"the wall-clock / token-count / log-line-volume exclusion list"*, which carves wall-clock timing, token counts, and log-line volume *out of* the GOV-15 equivalence promise as independent observables in their own right.

The two readings produce different scopes for observable (c):

- Under reading (1), the sentence is the *correct* gloss the surrounding prose is reaching for — "byte-identicality up-to-normalisation governs the variable placeholder sub-fields embedded inside a content string; do not appeal to the independent-observables exclusion list (which is a different mechanism for a different concern)." This is the only reading consistent with the immediately-following sentence, which *contrasts* observable (c)'s normalised sub-fields with the wall-clock / token-count / log-line-volume exclusions as "independent observables in their own right — distinct from the wall-clock-derived sub-fields that observable (c) normalises within a content string."
- Under reading (2), the sentence asserts that observable (c) governs variation *embedded as a placeholder sub-field* without appeal to the wall-clock / token-count / log-line-volume exclusion list — true, but trivially so (those three things are observables of their own, not placeholder sub-fields), and the sentence carries no normative work.

A reviewer who picks reading (2) will conclude the sentence is filler; a reviewer who picks reading (1) will conclude it is the load-bearing bridge between the two mechanisms. The same ambiguity propagates into the orientation aggregator copy in `overview-and-orientation.md`, where the surrounding context is even thinner, so reading (2) becomes more attractive there.

The defect is purely a missing modifier on the noun phrase; the surrounding paragraph already carries the disambiguating evidence, so the fix is local.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (of the disambiguation sentence)
**Introducing commit:** `1406d57` (2026-06-01) — *"pi-loom spec: resolve 'loom-system-note equivalence undefined for variable sub-fields'"*
**History:** The sentence *"Observable (c)'s equivalence relation, not the exclusion list, governs variation that is embedded as a placeholder sub-field inside a content string."* was introduced in `1406d57`, the same commit that added the variable-placeholder normalisation list (per-invocation IDs, `<ms>` wall-clock sub-fields, category-8 tails) to GOV-15 and to the `spec.md` NFR bullet, in mirrored form. The pre-existing paragraph already named *"Wall-clock timing, token counts, and log-line volume are explicitly excluded from this expectation"* — the *independent-observables exclusion list*. `1406d57` therefore added a second list (the normalisation list) and, in the same commit, a sentence referring to *"the exclusion list"* with the definite article — without renaming, numbering, or qualifying either of the now-two candidate antecedents. The ambiguity is intrinsic to that edit; no later commit has altered the sentence. Subsequent commits (`494f7fc`, `fe8aa5c`, `5a772e3`) re-edited the surrounding paragraph for unrelated concerns, the topic split (`81bf52a`, 2026-06-11) relocated GOV-15 to `governance/source-language-stability.md`, and `7995d3c` (2026-06-05) extracted GOV-15 from a retired-prefix-table file — all carried the defective sentence forward verbatim. There is no later commit that "broke" a previously-clear sentence; the defect is co-extensive with the sentence's existence.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In both edit sites (the GOV-15 owner paragraph at `governance/source-language-stability.md#gov-15` and the orientation-aggregator NFR bullet at `overview-and-orientation.md#source-language-stability`), replace the bare noun phrase *"the exclusion list"* with *"the independent-observables exclusion list"*, and replace *"governs variation"* with *"governs the variation"* for grammatical flow. The disambiguated sentence reads:

> *"Observable (c)'s equivalence relation, not the independent-observables exclusion list, governs the variation that is embedded as a placeholder sub-field inside a content string."*

Justification embedded in the surrounding paragraph: the sentence that follows already names *"the wall-clock / token-count / log-line-volume exclusion list"* as the **independent-observables** list ("Wall-clock timing, token counts, and log-line volume remain excluded from this expectation as independent observables in their own right"). Promoting *independent-observables* into the disambiguating sentence binds it to that named role; the *variable-placeholder normalisation list* keeps no name because it is not a normative construct that needs one — it is the sub-field set classified by Placeholder rendering, which the prior sentence already delegates to by reference.

Notes for the implementer:

- Both sentences must land in the **same commit** per the GOV-30 lock-step rule that the GOV-15 paragraph itself names (and which the orientation aggregator at `overview-and-orientation.md` is bound by, per the lock-step pattern this section instantiates).
- Do **not** introduce a new proper name for the variable-placeholder normalisation list (e.g. "the normalisation list"). The paragraph already enumerates the list inline by category and forward-links the authoritative classification; coining a third named list increases the very ambiguity this fix removes.
- Do **not** edit the standalone *"wall-clock / token-count / log-line-volume exclusion list"* phrasing later in the paragraph — that named construct is what the fix-side adjective binds against, and is also referenced (with the same wording) from the *closed-at-loom-1.0.0* clause.
- Do **not** alter `placeholder-rendering-a.md` or any other downstream owner; the fix is local to the two edit sites named above.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Two reviewers reading the GOV-15 paragraph independently can derive different scopes for observable (c)'s equivalence relation: one that includes embedded variable placeholder sub-fields under (c)'s normalised byte-identicality, one that treats them as outside (c) and unhandled by either list. The second reading silently re-introduces the very gap commit `1406d57` ("loom-system-note equivalence undefined for variable sub-fields") closed, by routing the placeholder sub-fields back to the wrong mechanism. Implementation is not blocked — GOV-15 is a release-process goal, not a runtime obligation — but the sentence whose job is to disambiguate the two mechanisms is itself ambiguous.
