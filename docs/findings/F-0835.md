## Finding

`version-bump-step2.md` item (a) cites the three blocked obligations as the compressed token "SM-4/5/6" — twice in the same paragraph, with neither occurrence linked. The same paragraph in the sibling `host-prerequisites.md` clause (a) introduces the trio as "[SM-4 / SM-5 / SM-6](../session-model-and-appendix.md#sm-4-session-only-degraded-state)" — a single link aimed at the SM-4 anchor, followed by an unlinked "SM-4/5/6" recurrence. SM-5 and SM-6 are never individually addressable from either site even though `session-model-and-appendix.md` publishes the per-obligation anchors `sm-4-session-only-degraded-state`, `sm-5-tag-transition-predicate`, and `sm-6-diagnostic-emission-predicate` for exactly this kind of inbound link.

The citation style is also inconsistent with how the surrounding spec corpus cites SM-N obligations. `language-and-architecture.md` cites SM-7a … SM-7e as five separate `[SM-7a](…#sm-7a-…)` links with their names spelled out; `model-changes-and-non-goals.md` cites SM-7c as `[spec.md — Session model — SM-7c prompt-mode sequential execution](…#sm-7c-prompt-mode-sequential-execution)`; SM-3b is cited as a named link in the same `host-prerequisites.md` paragraph. The "SM-4/5/6" compression therefore reads as ad-hoc shorthand against the established convention, and `governance/anchor-scheme-and-retired.md` GOV-23 explicitly directs that "an inbound citation that pins a single sub-obligation SHOULD target the specific `sm-N-…` (or sub-letter) anchor" — which a compound `SM-4/5/6` token, linked only to SM-4's anchor, structurally cannot do.

The defect obscures that three distinct obligations with three distinct scopes are being blocked: SM-4 is the degraded-state premise, SM-5 is the broader tag-transition predicate (which also covers snapshot-failure and unknown-reason paths), and SM-6 is the strictly narrower diagnostic-emission predicate. A reader following the single SM-4 link does not arrive at SM-5 or SM-6, and the blast radius of the host-prerequisites clause (a) open contradiction onto SM-5's snapshot-failure / unknown-reason paths and SM-6's diagnostic-channel scope is not navigable from the citing prose.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

At each of the four citation sites — the two occurrences in `version-bump-step2.md` item (a) and the two occurrences in `host-prerequisites.md` clause (a) — expand "SM-4/5/6" into three individual links with the obligation names spelled out: `[SM-4 — Post-teardown degraded state](../session-model-and-appendix.md#sm-4-session-only-degraded-state)`, `[SM-5 — Tag-transition predicate scope](../session-model-and-appendix.md#sm-5-tag-transition-predicate)`, `[SM-6 — Diagnostic-emission predicate scope](../session-model-and-appendix.md#sm-6-diagnostic-emission-predicate)`. Match the existing citation convention in `language-and-architecture.md` (`[SM-7a](…)` … `[SM-7e](…)`) and satisfy GOV-23's "pin a single sub-obligation" directive. The path prefix is relative to the citing file: `../session-model-and-appendix.md#…` from the `pi-integration-contract/` siblings.

Sweep the same shape into the second-occurrence prose ("SM-4/5/6 must be reworked", "before [SM-4/5/6] are implemented") so every mention is linked to its specific anchor; leave the adjacent `[SM-3b](…#sm-3b-session-swap-semantics)` link unchanged (it already follows the convention). If the `docs/plan.md` §Blocked obligations restatement of the same rework list (line 234) is touched in the same editorial sweep, apply the same expansion there for consistency, but a plan-side update is not required by the spec-side fix.

Implementer edge cases: the obligation names above are the H2-bold tokens published at each `sm-N-…` anchor in `session-model-and-appendix.md` — quote them verbatim rather than paraphrasing, so a future name edit shows up as a one-shot sweep rather than as silent drift. Do not retarget either of the existing `[SM-3b](…)` links or the `[loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin](…)` link in the same paragraph.

## Consequence

A reader who follows the single SM-4 link reaches SM-4 only; SM-5 and SM-6 are not navigable from the bump-checklist or host-prerequisites citations, and the per-obligation distinction is not visible at the citation site. No observable runtime behaviour is at stake — but the editorial sweep that lands the host-prerequisites clause (a) resolution will need to know exactly which obligations to rework, and a non-navigable, undifferentiated `SM-4/5/6` compresses that information out.
