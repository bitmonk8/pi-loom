## Problem

The `schema slug` entry in `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` carries a MUST-level terminology rule and designates itself the source of truth for a future synonym-avoidance grep gate. The glossary preamble declares every entry descriptive-only and states that the canonical page wins on disagreement, so the rule sits where the spec's own precedence clause denies it force. The canonical home — `docs/spec_topics/schema-subset.md`'s `## Canonical schema hash` subsection — defines `schema slug` but does not restate the terminology MUST or its synonym list, so an implementer or grep-gate author reading the canonical page finds the definition without the rule that governs the term's use.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 9d99fe6 — pi-loom spec: resolve "Schema-hash identifier is referred to by six surface names" (2026-05-07, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The `schema slug` glossary entry was added in 9d99fe6 as part of the six-surface-names rename resolution, and it carried the MUST terminology rule and the "future grep gate uses this entry as its source of truth" designation from its first appearance. The glossary's descriptive-only preamble predates that commit, so the placement conflict was introduced in this single commit when the normative rule was written into the descriptive page rather than onto `schema-subset.md`.

## Solution approach

Move the normative terminology MUST, the synonym-avoidance list, and the grep-gate source-of-truth designation from glossary.md's `schema slug` entry onto schema-subset.md's `## Canonical schema hash` subsection. Narrow the glossary `schema slug` entry to its descriptive role and its existing `See:` pointer, dropping the relocated normative content.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the parallel `.loom callable` MUST glossary entry (F-2056's territory).
- The grep-gate source-of-truth designation MUST name exactly one site after the move; do not leave a duplicate or dangling reference in the glossary.
