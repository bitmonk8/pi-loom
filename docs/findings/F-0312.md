## Problem

The closing sentence of the *No globals, statics, singletons* bullet in `docs/plan_topics/conventions.md` asserts that the two mechanical scans (the `H2a` architectural test and the `H3a` identifier-keyed scan) and release-time residue-inspection checklist items 1 and 2 "partition the space with no third gap between them." This is a completeness claim over a non-mechanical half that is a human reviewer reading `src/**`, where each checklist item opens with an enumeration of example forms. A reviewer treating those enumerations as the closed catalogue will miss a violation form that is neither statically detectable nor among the named examples. Best-effort manual review against an example list cannot deliver the gap-free partition the sentence asserts.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit-introduction (carried forward by a later fix)
**Introducing commits:** `f005760` (2026-06-11); over-claim reworded but preserved by `e94ca10` (2026-06-16)
**History:** The "partition the space with no third gap" completeness claim entered the *No globals, statics, singletons* bullet in `f005760` ("Architectural / ambient-access scan blind spots have no named compensating review gate"), which first routed the singleton and indirect-ambient-read residue to a named manual review and asserted that the mechanical scans plus that review "partition the space with no third gap between them." `e94ca10` ("GOV-15 release-time reviewer inspection has no enumerated residue checklist") rewrote the surrounding sentence to point at the now-enumerated release-time checklist items 1 and 2, but retained the "no third gap" over-claim verbatim. Intervening edits (`80f7c51`, `358b5df`, `c6824f2`) touched the bullet's allow-list and banned-primitive wording without altering the partition claim. The over-claim has therefore been continuously present since `f005760`.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the closing sentence of the *No globals, statics, singletons* bullet in `docs/plan_topics/conventions.md` so it no longer asserts an exhaustive gap-free partition over best-effort human review. State instead that the `H2a` and `H3a` scans catch the violation forms they can statically detect, and that release-time residue-inspection checklist items 1 and 2 are the best-effort human review of the residue those scans concede — governed by each item's **Clean when** condition rather than by its illustrative example list, so a residue form outside both the scans' reach and an item's named examples is still caught when it breaches that item's **Clean when** condition.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: re-narrowing checklist items 1 and 2's **Clean when** conditions — leave them as the broad catch-all conditions they already are.
- Out of scope: the banned-primitive-list lockstep-drift hazard — a separate finding's concern, not this wording fix.
