## Finding

`V9k` is titled "Extension-bootstrap SDK-failure **abort** surfaces" (in both `V9k-extension-bootstrap-failures.md` and the paired `V9k-T-extension-bootstrap-failures.md`), but the leaf's own **Adds** field names them "the two **whole-extension abort** surfaces" and its coverage-matrix row (`cka-16`) likewise reads "whole-extension `registerFlag`/`pi.on` abort closure." The "whole-extension" qualifier is load-bearing: it distinguishes V9k's two surfaces (a `registerFlag` or `pi.on` failure aborts the *entire* extension) from the surfaces V9k's split-out sibling `V9p` owns â€” renderer-failure degrade, per-loom `registerCommand` drop, and `getCommands()` read-failure drop â€” which are also "aborts" in the colloquial sense (a single loom or renderer is dropped) yet are explicitly *not* whole-extension aborts.

`V9p` carries the qualifier on its side consistently: its title is "Extension-bootstrap SDK-failure **non-abort** surfaces" and its Adds says "the three **non-abort** surfaces." The result is an asymmetric pair â€” `non-abort` (qualified) against bare `abort` (unqualified) â€” where the bare "abort" title reads as the complement of "non-abort" (i.e. "everything that aborts something"), which over-claims V9k's scope, since V9p's per-loom and renderer drops are themselves a form of aborting a unit of work.

No execution breaks â€” the leaf bodies, `Deps`, and `cka-16` mapping all carry the correct "whole-extension" scoping â€” so this is a title/label asymmetry only, internal to the plan corpus.
