## Problem

DISC-5 (`#disc-5` in `package-and-settings.md`) names `minimatch` as the glob engine and pins three knobs â€” the `!`/`+`/`-` override order, the three match targets, and `nocase: off` â€” but leaves every other result-affecting minimatch option at its silent default (`dot`, `noglobstar`, brace expansion, `matchBase`) and pins no minimatch major version. Two conforming implementers can therefore install different majors with different default flags and produce divergent match sets for the same `pi.looms` array, each passing its own unit tests. Separately, DISC-5's normative text grounds the contract by naming the `minimatch` library and citing Pi's unexported `dist/`-internal symbols `applyPatterns` and `matchesAnyExactPattern`, which are not importable from the SDK inventory yet read as part of loom's contract surface.

## Solution approach

Pin the minimatch major version in `implementation-notes.md` `#loom-package-implementation-dependencies-loom-1-0`. Move the `dist/core/package-manager.js` / `applyPatterns` / `matchesAnyExactPattern` citations out of DISC-5's normative text into a non-normative note, leaving the enumerated option set, the override order, and the three match targets as the normative contract.
