## Problem

The glossary distinguishes two foundational concepts — `loom` (the file unit) and `Loom` (the scripting language) — solely by letter case. Case is an unreliable disambiguator in CommonMark prose: sentence-initial, list-bullet-initial, heading, and table-cell positions force capitalisation regardless of which sense the author intends, so a reader cannot recover the intended sense from a citation, a quoted fragment, or a heading-anchored cross-link, and neither `grep "Loom"` nor `grep "loom"` can audit one sense without silently matching or missing the other. The collapse is already live across `glossary.md`, `influences.md`, `grammar.md`, and `runtime-value-model.md`, and for normative prose (e.g. runtime-value-model.md's "Loom code observes `Result` only…") the ambiguity is observable in what implementers build and test. This is the only remaining glossary overload that relies on case alone; every other ambiguous-when-bare term carries a distinct alternative name.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (for the file-unit / language split as a *two-entry, case-distinguished* pair)

**Introducing commit:** `53b8970` — *pi-loom spec: resolve "loom / Loom / pi-loom — three referents, no glossary disambiguation"* (Tue May 5 22:33:43 2026 +0200). This commit added the `loom (file unit)` and `Loom (language)` entries to `spec_topics/glossary.md` in their current form, including the rule "Capitalised when used as a proper noun … the lowercase form is reserved for sense 1 above." The commit also retitled `overview.md`'s `### Scope of a Loom File` → `### Scope of a loom file` to enforce the new rule, demonstrating that the case-only disambiguator was load-bearing from its first commit.

**History:** Before `53b8970` the glossary contained no entries for either sense (`git show 53b8970^:spec_topics/glossary.md` lists `binder`, `callable set`, `coercion`, `loom-side name`, `prompt mode`, `Pi tool` / `.loom callable`, `top-level loom` — no `loom` or `Loom` entry). The two glossary entries and the rule that distinguishes them were introduced together by `53b8970`; the prose collision documented above all pre-dates the rule and was inherited rather than created, so the rule did not introduce the collision so much as fail to resolve it. No later commit has attempted a stronger disambiguator on either sense.

## Solution approach

Give the language sense a structurally-distinct name so the disambiguator survives every position. Rename the language sense to the fixed forms `the Loom language` when standing alone and `Loom-language <noun>` (e.g. `Loom-language expression`, `Loom-language AST`, `Loom-language type`) when used adjectivally, updating the `Loom (language)` glossary entry and the language-sense occurrences in `influences.md`, `grammar.md` (including the `## Loom literal sublanguage` heading), `runtime-value-model.md`, `expressions.md`, `bindings.md`, and `lexical.md`. Reserve bare `loom` for the file-unit sense in every position; where a heading, table cell, or sentence-initial position would force its capitalisation, restructure or pluralise (`looms`) rather than capitalise, and record that usage note under the `loom (file unit)` glossary entry. The rename must be applied per intended sense, judged occurrence by occurrence.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the `loom/...` diagnostic-code namespace (governance-locked).
- Must not be applied as a mechanical find-and-replace — a blind pass would silently rewrite legitimate sentence-initial file-unit uses and change their referent.
