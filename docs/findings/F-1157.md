## Finding

Three independent wording defects sit in the discovery pages, all minor in isolation but each capable of leading an implementer astray.

1. **"non-bypass loom" is used as load-bearing jargon without a definition.** The `looms.binderModel` description in `package-and-settings.md` §"Keys read" (line 64) says the setting is *"Required when any non-bypass loom is in scope"* and the **Top-level shape validation** paragraph and the `binder-model-unresolved` row of `diagnostics/code-registry-load.md` repeat the phrase. The glossary defines `no-params bypass` and `single-string bypass` as a paired entry (and `binder bypass` as a generic), but **never** defines the negation `non-bypass loom`. A reader who has not already internalised the bypass-cases page (`binder/binder-bypass-and-envelope.md`) cannot tell from the use site whether "non-bypass" means "neither static bypass condition applies", "the bypass decision is `false` at runtime", or something else. Capability-inventory item `sdk-cap-binder-llm-model` and the host-prerequisites bullet handle the same predicate by spelling out *"Non-bypass looms"* alongside an explicit cross-reference to *Bypass cases*; the settings page does not.

2. **"after `pi.looms` mapping" implies a slash-name remap that `pi.looms` does not perform.** `discovery-sources.md` line 83 (the paragraph following DISC-4) says collision detection runs *"on the final derived name (after `pi.looms` mapping, with the filename stem taken verbatim for sources that have no `pi.looms` mapping)"*. But per DISC-5, `pi.looms` is a `string[]` of glob patterns selecting which files contribute looms; the slash name is always the filename stem verbatim (per the *Filename validity* rule, no case-folding or substitution). There is no `pi.looms` "mapping" step that produces a different name — the parenthetical's two halves are contradictory ("after mapping" vs "stem taken verbatim where no mapping"), because the same verbatim-stem rule applies in both cases. The phrase is a fossil of an earlier draft (`b87d4a6`) in which DISC-4 collision detection nominally ran after `pi.looms` mapping, `as` rename, *and* basename hyphen-normalisation; the `as` rename and hyphen-normalisation steps were struck (commits `f2a82ad` and `0ff2faf`), but the lead phrase survived without its supporting clauses.

3. **"ERR-7 elevates the two failure outcomes" is a verb with two readings.** `package-and-settings.md` §"Watcher-time reload failures" (the paragraph introducing the two-bullet list) says *"ERR-7 elevates the two failure outcomes of the debounced re-parse-and-swap"*. "Elevates" reads as either (a) promotes the severity of, or (b) lifts the two outcomes into a category / treats them as the surface of. The intended reading is (b) — ERR-7 is the defined surface that channels both outcomes onto the `loom-system-note` channel — but no severity change is implied (a registry-swap throw and a re-parse diagnostic each carry their own registered severity from the load/runtime code registries). The verb is the only ambiguous word in an otherwise pinned paragraph.

A single editor pass over those four sites resolves all three. The defects are clarity-only — no behavioural rule, no acceptance criterion, no diagnostic surface changes.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Apply three localised wording edits, all within the existing paragraphs (no new sections, no anchor changes, no diagnostic-code changes):

1. **Define "non-bypass loom" once at the glossary and cross-reference from the use site.** Extend the existing `no-params bypass` / `single-string bypass` paired glossary entry with a third clause introducing `non-bypass loom` as "a loom for which neither static bypass condition holds at load time — `params:` declares at least one field, and either the field count is >1, the single field's declared type is not `string`, or the field carries a default". On the `package-and-settings.md` `looms.binderModel` bullet, change *"Required when any non-bypass loom is in scope"* to *"Required when any non-bypass loom is in scope (per [Binder — Bypass cases](../binder/binder-bypass-and-envelope.md#bypass-cases))"*. Mirror the same cross-reference into the `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved` row of `code-registry-load.md`.

2. **Replace "after `pi.looms` mapping" with the operation `pi.looms` actually performs.** In `discovery-sources.md` line 83, change *"Detection runs on the **final derived name** (after `pi.looms` mapping, with the filename stem taken verbatim for sources that have no `pi.looms` mapping), not the source filename."* to *"Detection runs on the slash name derived from the filename stem per *Filename validity* above (the slash name is the filename stem taken verbatim under all five sources, including files contributed by a package's `pi.looms` glob — `pi.looms` selects which files contribute, not what they are named), not the source filename."* This removes the false "mapping" framing and pins the rule on the existing verbatim-stem invariant.

3. **Replace "elevates" with a verb whose meaning is unambiguous.** In `package-and-settings.md` §"Watcher-time reload failures", change *"ERR-7 elevates the two failure outcomes of the debounced re-parse-and-swap"* to *"ERR-7 is the defined surface for the two failure outcomes of the debounced re-parse-and-swap"*. The replacement explicitly signals categorisation, not severity promotion, and matches the language used by the cross-referenced ERR-7 anchor in `error-model.md`.

Edge case the implementer must watch: when extending the glossary in (1), keep the new `non-bypass loom` clause inside the **existing** paired entry rather than creating a new top-level entry, so the cross-referenced anchor remains stable; the `binder/binder-bypass-and-envelope.md` "Bypass cases" definitions remain the normative source — the glossary clause is the negation shorthand, not a fresh definition.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Each defect is a small reading-fluency hit rather than a behavioural ambiguity: the `binder-model-unresolved` failure code documents what fires when the predicate trips, DISC-4's *Loom-vs-loom (same priority)* arm pins the collision rule independent of the "mapping" parenthetical, and the two-bullet list under "Watcher-time reload failures" enumerates the two outcomes regardless of which sense of "elevates" the reader picks. The risk is that an implementer reading the settings page in isolation may either over-apply `binderModel` (treating it as universally required) or, conversely, omit it under a misunderstanding of which looms qualify as "non-bypass"; the binder-load probe catches both at load time. None of the three is a correctness blocker.
