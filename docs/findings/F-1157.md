## Problem

Three independent wording defects sit in the discovery pages. (1) `package-and-settings.md` §"Keys read" (the `looms.binderModel` bullet), the **Top-level shape validation** paragraph, and the `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved` row of `diagnostics/code-registry-load.md` use "non-bypass loom" as load-bearing jargon, but the glossary defines only `no-params bypass` / `single-string bypass` / `binder bypass` and never the negation — a reader cannot tell whether it means "neither static bypass condition holds", "the bypass decision is `false` at runtime", or something else. (2) `discovery-sources.md`'s paragraph following DISC-4 says collision detection runs on the derived name "after `pi.looms` mapping", but per DISC-5 `pi.looms` is a `string[]` of globs selecting which files contribute looms, not a name-remap; the slash name is always the filename stem verbatim per *Filename validity*, so the parenthetical contradicts itself. (3) `package-and-settings.md` §"Watcher-time reload failures" says "ERR-7 elevates the two failure outcomes", where "elevates" reads ambiguously as either promoting severity or categorising — the intended sense is categorisation, with no severity change.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `0fa81fa` (2026-05-05), `b87d4a6` (2026-05-04), `d488eaa` (2026-06-06)
**History:** The three defects each entered the corpus through a distinct commit and share only their location/topic, not their origin.
- "non-bypass loom" entered as undefined jargon in `0fa81fa` ("spec: pin binder model resolution; document envelope two-arm rationale (Decisions #5, #6)"), which both introduced the `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved` code and rewrote the `looms.binderModel` settings descriptor to "Required when any non-bypass loom is in scope." That commit added no glossary entry for the negated term; the `no-params bypass`/`single-string bypass` glossary pair was authored separately and never extended to cover the negation.
- "pi.looms mapping" entered earlier, in `b87d4a6` ("Add consolidated enriched spec review"), as part of a longer collision-detection clause that originally read *"after `pi.looms` mapping, as rename, and basename hyphen-normalisation"*. Subsequent edits stripped the surrounding steps — `f2a82ad` (2026-06-06) removed `basename hyphen-normalisation` per F-restored-DISC-4-vs-Filename-validity contradiction work, and `0ff2faf` (2026-06-22) struck the dangling `as rename` clause per F-0978 — leaving the lead phrase standing alone, where it is now self-contradicted by its own parenthetical clarification.
- "elevates the two failure outcomes" entered in `d488eaa` (2026-06-06, "pi-loom spec: resolve 'ERR-7 defining anchor + collision payload field'"), which added the entire `watcher-time-reload-failures` paragraph and its two-bullet list. The verb has not been touched since.
The defect surface as a triple is the product of these three independent edits accreting on the same page neighbourhood over a six-week window without a clarity pass over the result.

## Solution approach

Define `non-bypass loom` once by extending the existing `no-params bypass` / `single-string bypass` paired glossary entry, and add a cross-reference to [Binder — Bypass cases](../binder/binder-bypass-and-envelope.md#bypass-cases) from the `looms.binderModel` use site in `package-and-settings.md` §"Keys read" and from the `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved` row of `code-registry-load.md`. In `discovery-sources.md`'s paragraph following DISC-4, rewrite the "after `pi.looms` mapping" parenthetical to ground the derived name on the verbatim filename-stem rule (*Filename validity*) and to state that `pi.looms` selects which files contribute rather than what they are named. In `package-and-settings.md` §"Watcher-time reload failures", replace "elevates" with a verb that signals categorisation rather than severity promotion (e.g. "is the defined surface for"), matching the ERR-7 anchor's language in `error-model.md`.

## Solution constraints

- Extend the existing paired glossary entry in place (no new top-level entry) so the cross-referenced anchor stays stable; the normative bypass definitions remain in `binder/binder-bypass-and-envelope.md` "Bypass cases" — the glossary clause is negation shorthand, not a fresh definition.
- Out of scope: the DISC-6 / DISC-5 / DISC-1 testability gaps in the same file, owned by F-1155.
