## Finding

`H4a`'s **Adds** field carries a ~700-word governance protocol for the manual real-host smoke run: trigger conditions (version bump; any merge touching the four fidelity-contract axes), named owners for each trigger, a live-host precondition narrative, a five-part pass/fail criterion (a)–(e), the un-runnable-gate vs behavioural-divergence distinction, the commit-message evidence-recording convention, the "contributor obligation, not CI-enforced gate" caveat, and the merge-blocker / revert path. This is process and governance addressed to the bump/merge contributor and the `H6a` release-gate auditor — a different audience from the `H4a` implementer, who builds the factory shell, the end-to-end harness, and the session-double fidelity contract.

The leaf-format convention (`conventions.md` §Leaf format) defines **Adds** as one descriptive sentence naming what the leaf introduces in the codebase; binding obligations live in **Tests** REQ-ID bullets and **Ships when**. The smoke-governance block describes no codebase artifact `H4a` produces — `H4a`'s own Tests/Ships-when explicitly exclude the manual smoke from the harness self-check and from `npm test`. The protocol's normative obligations (the triggers, owners, and merge-blocker rule) therefore sit in the one field the convention marks non-binding, where the `H4a` implementer will read past them and the bump/merge contributor who is the actual audience has no reason to look.

The protocol's effective home is the release-gate / version-bump machinery: `H6a` already restates the release-gate acceptance and audits the recorded evidence, and `V18d` owns the version-bump revert / pin-restore path the merge-blocker references. The governance block sits in `H4a` only because that is where the smoke run's fixture-fidelity rationale was first authored, not because `H4a` enforces it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

The defect (governance prose mis-sited in a descriptive field) is one obligation, but it admits two materially different destinations.

### Option A — Extract to a dedicated governance section / note

**Approach:** Move the smoke-governance protocol out of `H4a` Adds into a dedicated home addressed to its real audience — e.g. a new release-gate / version-bump governance section in `docs/plan.md`'s Release gate area, or a sibling note under `docs/plan_topics/`. `H4a` Adds retains a one-sentence pointer naming the factory, harness, and fidelity contract, plus a cross-link to the governance home.

**Plan edits:** Strike the two governance paragraphs from `H4a` Adds; author the protocol (triggers, owners, live-host precondition, pass criteria (a)–(e), evidence-recording convention, merge-blocker / revert) at the new home; update `H4a`'s Tests bullet that currently says the smoke "is established as a governance protocol in **Adds** above" to point at the new home; update `H6a`'s "see `H4a`" back-references to the new home.

**Spec edits:** None.

**Pros:** Single home addressed to the bump/merge contributor and release-gate auditor; `H4a` shrinks to its codebase scope; no governance content duplicated inside another leaf's release-gate prose.

**Cons:** Introduces a new plan surface that the implementer/fixer must place and that future cross-links must track.

**Risks:** A dangling cross-reference if any of the `H4a`/`H6a`/`V18d` links to the protocol is missed during the move.

### Option B — Fold the protocol into H6a

**Approach:** Relocate the governance protocol into `H6a`, the release-gate leaf that already restates the release-gate acceptance and audits the recorded evidence, then reduce `H4a` Adds to its one-sentence codebase scope plus a pointer to `H6a`.

**Plan edits:** Strike the governance paragraphs from `H4a` Adds; place the protocol body in `H6a` (Adds or a dedicated release-gate sub-section); reconcile it against `H6a`'s existing **Release-gate acceptance (manual real-host smoke)** Tests bullet so the trigger/owner/merge-blocker rules and the audit step read as one coherent statement rather than two overlapping ones; update `H4a`'s Tests cross-reference and any `H6a` "see `H4a`" links.

**Spec edits:** None.

**Pros:** Uses an existing leaf whose audience already includes the release-gate auditor; no new plan file.

**Cons:** `H6a` already restates the release-gate acceptance, so folding the full triggers/owners/pass-criteria block risks overlap that the reconciliation must resolve; the two trigger-(1)/trigger-(2) owners and the pre-merge merge-blocker are not purely release-time concerns, so housing them under the terminal release-gate leaf understates that they fire throughout the `H7a`-onward window.

**Risks:** Duplicated or drifting statements of the smoke pass criterion between `H6a`'s existing acceptance bullet and the relocated protocol.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A**: extract the protocol to a dedicated release-gate / version-bump governance home and reduce `H4a` Adds to a one-sentence codebase description (factory + harness + fidelity contract) with a cross-link. The protocol's triggers fire across the whole `H4a`→`H6a`→version-bump lifecycle, so a standalone home addressed to the bump/merge contributor serves its audience better than burying it inside the terminal `H6a` release-gate leaf, and it avoids the overlap Option B must reconcile against `H6a`'s existing acceptance bullet. The fixer must keep all inbound references consistent — `H4a`'s Tests bullet, `H6a`'s "see `H4a`" links, and `V18d`'s revert-path reference — so no link dangles after the move. The spec is read-only for this fix; the relocation is internal to plan files. Honour the leaf-ID scheme and `<new>`/`no-invented-ids` discipline if a new note file is created.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The smoke run's normative governance — its triggers, named owners, and merge-blocker rule — is the only statement of a contributor obligation that has no mechanical gate, yet it lives in a field the leaf-format convention marks descriptive and non-binding, addressed to an audience (the `H4a` implementer) that is not its target. The bump/merge contributor and release-gate auditor who must act on it have no structural reason to read `H4a`'s Adds, so the obligation can be missed; meanwhile the oversized block inflates the `H4a` review surface around content the implementer does not act on. No `H4a` implementer is blocked — the factory, harness, and fidelity contract remain implementable from the Tests/Ships-when bullets.
