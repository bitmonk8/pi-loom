## Problem

`H4a`'s **Adds** field carries a ~700-word manual real-host smoke governance protocol — trigger conditions, named owners, a live-host precondition, a five-part pass/fail criterion, the un-runnable-gate vs behavioural-divergence distinction, the commit-message evidence convention, the contributor-obligation caveat, and the merge-blocker / revert path. Its audience is the bump/merge contributor and the `H6a` release-gate auditor, not the `H4a` implementer who builds the factory shell, end-to-end harness, and session-double fidelity contract. The leaf-format convention (`conventions.md` §Leaf format) defines **Adds** as one descriptive sentence naming what the leaf introduces; binding obligations live in **Tests** REQ-ID bullets and **Ships when**. The protocol's normative obligations therefore sit in the one field the convention marks non-binding, where the actual audience has no structural reason to read them.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `328ba4d` (2026-06-10, "resolve real-host verification gap" — first real-host smoke content), `15eb44c` (2026-06-11, "resolve real-host divergence pre-merge gate (posture i)" — added the pre-merge gate with triggers/owners), `ad23856` (2026-06-16, "resolve H4a manual real-host smoke governance misplaced in Tests (F-0041+F-0042)" — relocated the block from Tests into Adds)
**History:** `git log -S` over `docs/plan_topics/H4a-factory-shell-and-harness.md` shows the smoke-governance content entered as real-host-verification prose (`328ba4d`), grew the pre-merge gate's triggers, owners, and merge-blocker (`15eb44c`), and accreted prerequisites and pass criteria across roughly a dozen later commits. A prior review found the block misplaced in **Tests** and `ad23856` moved it wholesale into **Adds** — which closed the Tests-placement defect but landed a ~700-word governance block into a field the convention reserves for one descriptive sentence. The current oversized-Adds state is the cumulative result of that progressive accretion followed by the wholesale relocation, not a single authoring slip.

## Solution approach

Move the manual real-host smoke governance protocol out of `H4a`'s **Adds** into a release-gate / version-bump governance home addressed to the bump/merge contributor and the `H6a` release-gate auditor — either a new release-gate section in `docs/plan.md` or a sibling note under `docs/plan_topics/`. Reduce `H4a`'s **Adds** to a single descriptive sentence naming the factory shell, the end-to-end harness, and the session-double fidelity contract, with a cross-link to the new home. Update the inbound references so no link dangles: `H4a`'s Tests bullet (which currently says the smoke "is established as a governance protocol in **Adds** above"), `H6a`'s "see `H4a`" back-references, and `V18d`'s revert-path reference.
