## Problem

The *Source-language stability* bullet under `id="source-language-stability"` (in the *Non-functional requirements* section of `overview-and-orientation.md`) restates the entire substantive content of GOV-15 inline before naming GOV-15 as the owner: the (a)/(b)/(c) observables, the byte-identity equivalence relation with placeholder-sub-field normalisation, the wall-clock / token-count / log-line-volume exclusion list, and the ceiling-set and diagnostic-registry carve-outs. The same material appears verbatim or near-verbatim on the GOV-15 owner page (`governance/source-language-stability.md`). The bullet's stated role is *informative orientation* that forward-links the normative owner, but the inline duplication defeats that role and inflates the GOV-30 lock-step surface to the size of GOV-15, so every future GOV-15 wording edit becomes a reviewer-judged two-file co-edit.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `2ff03c2` — `pi-loom spec: resolve "Source-language stability bullet leaves equivalence undefined at the aggregator and silently contradicts the open-set ceiling clause"` (2026-05-10) — expanded the aggregator bullet on `docs/spec.md` from a one-sentence summary-plus-forward-link to a paragraph that inlined the (a)/(b)/(c) observables, the exclusion list, and the ceiling-set carve-out pointer.
- `1406d57` — `pi-loom spec: resolve "loom-system-note equivalence undefined for variable sub-fields"` (2026-06-01) — added the byte-identity equivalence relation, the placeholder-normalisation clause, and the *Observable (c)'s equivalence relation, not the exclusion list, governs variation…* sentence to **both** the aggregator bullet and GOV-15 in the same commit.
- `b78063e` — `pi-loom spec: resolve "Cross-cutting NFR bullets lack a discoverable home in spec.md"` (2026-06-03) — moved the bullet from *Scope* into a new *Non-functional requirements* sub-section, carrying the duplicated body across unchanged.
- `81bf52a` — `docs: collapse spec/ section pages into spec_topics/` — relocated the bullet to its present home at `docs/spec_topics/overview-and-orientation.md`.

**History:** the duplication is not the fault of any single commit. `2ff03c2` first elevated the aggregator from summary to substantive restatement to resolve a contradiction-against-the-open-set-ceiling finding; `1406d57` then deepened the duplication by landing the new byte-identity equivalence clauses on the aggregator and on GOV-15 simultaneously rather than only on the owner; later commits preserved the shape on each move. The defect is the cumulative interaction — each commit individually defensible, jointly producing a near-verbatim second copy of GOV-15 in the orientation aggregator.

## Solution approach

Narrow the *Source-language stability* bullet at `id="source-language-stability"` so it owns no GOV-15 content inline — remove the inlined (a)/(b)/(c) observables, the byte-identity equivalence relation, the placeholder-normalisation clause, the exclusion list, and the ceiling-set and diagnostic-registry carve-out text — leaving an orientation summary that forward-links GOV-15 (`#gov-15`, including its loads-cleanly predicate at `#gov-15-loads-cleanly`) as the normative owner. Keep the bullet inside the GOV-30 aggregator's referent-identity scope so lock-step still applies on add/remove/rename of the NFR bullets but stops binding every GOV-15 wording edit.

## Solution constraints

- Preserve the `id="source-language-stability"` anchor (cross-referenced from the GOV-15 diagnostic-registry carve-out and the *Future Considerations — loom 1.0 non-goals* index).
- Out of scope: the sibling *Trust boundary* and *Runtime observability* NFR bullets — this finding trims only the *Source-language stability* bullet.
