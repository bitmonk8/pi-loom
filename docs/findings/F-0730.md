## Finding

The *Source-language stability* bullet under [`overview-and-orientation.md` — Non-functional requirements](../../docs/spec_topics/overview-and-orientation.md#non-functional-requirements) restates the entire substantive content of GOV-15 inline before naming GOV-15 as the owner: the three observables (a)/(b)/(c), the byte-identity equivalence relation for observable (c) (UTF-8 substrate, normalised placeholder sub-fields per the diagnostics placeholder-rendering categories, literal-bytes-byte-identical), the *Observable (c)'s equivalence relation, not the exclusion list, governs variation…* clarification, the wall-clock/token-count/log-line-volume exclusion list, the ceiling-set carve-out and its sub-pointers, and the diagnostic-registry carve-out. The same paragraphs appear verbatim — or near-verbatim with only stylistic substitutions — as the first paragraph and as the *Diagnostic-registry carve-out* paragraph of [GOV-15 on `governance/source-language-stability.md`](../../docs/spec_topics/governance/source-language-stability.md#gov-15).

The orientation prose around the bullet correctly labels it *informative orientation* and points GOV-30 at it as the aggregator-vs-source lock-step convention, but the bullet's own body is sized like a normative owner. The result is two side-by-side authoritative-seeming statements of the same equivalence relation, exclusion list, and carve-out shapes; under GOV-30's lock-step trigger, every future edit to any of those clauses on `governance/source-language-stability.md` must be co-edited here in the same commit, with semantic equivalence verified by reviewer judgement (GOV-30 reserves no mechanical witness). The bullet's stated role — forward-link the topic page that owns the normative contract — already promises that delegation; the inline duplication defeats it. The sibling *Trust boundary* bullet has the same shape and is potentially affected by the same pattern, but is out of scope for this finding.

The defect is the cumulative interaction — each commit individually defensible, jointly producing a near-verbatim second copy of GOV-15 in the orientation aggregator.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace the bullet body with a one-sentence summary plus forward-links, owning *no* observable list, equivalence relation, exclusion list, or carve-out text inline. A working shape:

> - <a id="source-language-stability"></a> **Source-language stability.** A `.loom` or `.warp` file that [loads cleanly](./governance/source-language-stability.md#gov-15-loads-cleanly) under loom 1.0.0 is expected to load and produce equivalent observable behaviour under every loom 1.x release — a release-process goal, not a normative obligation. The equivalence relation (observables (a)/(b)/(c), the byte-identity rule for `loom-system-note` content strings with placeholder-sub-field normalisation, and the wall-clock / token-count / log-line-volume exclusion list), the ceiling-set and diagnostic-registry carve-outs, the loads-cleanly predicate, and the attribution test are all owned by [Governance — GOV-15](./governance/source-language-stability.md#gov-15); the companion review-posture note (formerly GOV-14) is retired and demoted to a non-normative editorial note (see [Retired REQ-IDs](./governance/anchor-scheme-and-retired.md#retired-req-ids)). Migration across major versions is out of loom 1.0 scope per [Future Considerations — loom 1.0 non-goals](./future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md#loom-1-0-non-goals).

Edge cases the implementer must keep in mind:

- The `<a id="source-language-stability"></a>` anchor is referenced from elsewhere in the corpus (e.g. the cross-link inside the GOV-15 *Diagnostic-registry carve-out* paragraph back to the orientation aggregator) and from the *Future Considerations — loom 1.0 non-goals* index; preserve the anchor.
- The bullet is registered under GOV-30 as one of the *multi-source-page* aggregator items ("the three cross-cutting non-functional-requirement dispositions"); the summary above keeps it inside that aggregator's referent-identity scope without re-stating GOV-15's content, so GOV-30 lock-step continues to apply on add/remove/rename of the three NFR bullets but stops binding every GOV-15 wording tweak.
- Do not remove the forward-link to `governance/source-language-stability.md#gov-15-loads-cleanly` — it is what makes the input set's selector discoverable from the orientation page.
- The ceiling-set carve-out is *also* cited by name from the *Hard ceilings* aggregator's closing parenthetical (which lives under the *Scope* sub-section, not under *Non-functional requirements*); leave that cross-reference intact. The summary above intentionally folds the carve-out under the generic phrase "the ceiling-set and diagnostic-registry carve-outs" rather than re-anchoring each carve-out separately, since both are owned by GOV-15 and a single forward-link to GOV-15 suffices.
- The parenthetical clarification *Observable (c)'s equivalence relation, not the exclusion list, governs variation…* and the antecedent ambiguity it carries (the subject of the *separate* finding [`overview-and-orientation.md — Source-language stability ("the exclusion list" antecedent)`](#)) live only on the GOV-15 owner page after this edit, which narrows the surface that finding needs to touch.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader hitting the orientation bullet learns the full GOV-15 contract twice and must compare the two copies to confirm they agree before trusting either; that defeats the bullet's *informative orientation* role and inflates the GOV-30 lock-step surface to the size of GOV-15 itself, so every future GOV-15 edit becomes a two-file co-edit with reviewer-judged paraphrase parity as the only check. No implementer is misdirected today (both copies currently agree), but the duplication is a steady drift hazard.
