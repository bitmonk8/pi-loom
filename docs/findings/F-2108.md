## Problem

`docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings/ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` carries two paragraphs under *Audit methodology* — ***Routing obligation for future ceilings.*** and ***Release gate.*** — whose audience is future spec editors, not runtime implementers. They specify how a spec editor must structure a ceiling introduction (three enumerated cases), rewrite a per-axis entry on retirement (four forms (i)–(iv)), format the SHA-or-tag retirement pointer, name the three required components of every per-axis entry, and what the GOV-15 reviewer-inspection / GOV-30 release gate must confirm. None of this constrains the loom runtime; it is spec-maintenance process whose natural home is the governance cluster it already cites. Embedding it in a runtime-ceiling normative page inflates the implementer-facing normative surface with MUST/SHALL directives that have no observable runtime consequence and that an implementer may misread as conformance requirements.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commit:** `2ff03c2`
**History:** The ***Routing obligation for future ceilings.*** and ***Release gate.*** paragraphs were net-added (`+`) to `docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings.md` in commit `2ff03c2` (2026-05-10, "pi-loom spec: resolve 'Source-language stability bullet leaves equivalence undefined at the aggregator and silently contradicts the open-set ceiling clause'"). They entered as a large governance block embedded in the ceiling content from the moment of introduction — the defect is co-located with their creation, not the result of later drift. The page was subsequently split into `hard-ceilings/ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` (`81bf52a`), the `<a id="release-gate">` anchor and surrounding structure were elaborated (`6ff9971`), and the GOV-12 references were renamed to GOV-30 and reflowed under a size-cap edit (`f5e89f4`); none of these changed the placement defect, they only carried it forward. The introducing commit was resolving an unrelated source-language-stability finding and added the editorial procedure to the ceiling page rather than to the governance cluster it cites.

## Solution approach

Move the editor-facing procedure — the three introduction cases, the four post-retirement rewrite forms (i)–(iv), the retirement-pointer bookkeeping, the three-component per-axis-entry requirement, and the ***Release gate.*** acceptance criteria — out of `ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` into the governance cluster at `docs/spec_topics/governance/source-language-stability.md` (under the `#ceiling-set-carve-out` anchor, or a dedicated governance sub-page linked from GOV-15/GOV-30). Retain on the ceiling page the four-axis closure list, the `NOCEIL-1` … `NOCEIL-4` claims, and the closure-invariant sentence stating that any future runtime resource must route to one of the four axes or a documented `NOCEIL-N` fall-through, with a forward-link to the relocated procedure. Repoint the page's existing GOV-30 lock-step co-edit statement at the relocated procedure so the obligation is not orphaned.
