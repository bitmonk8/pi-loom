## Problem

`H2a` ("Cross-cutting lint and architectural gates") packages four mechanically distinct enforcement surfaces into one leaf behind one `Ships when` gate: the `no-broad-catch` ESLint rule (*Specific exception types only*), the `no-restricted-syntax` sequential-by-default ban on `Promise.all`/`race`/`allSettled`/`any` (*Sequential by default*), the `no-restricted-syntax` blocking-runtime ban on `*Sync`-suffixed callees (also *Sequential by default*), and the `src/**` architectural test that rejects a module-level mutable binding (*No globals, statics, singletons*). Each has its own rule body and fixture and shares no implementation with the others; a red `npm test` on one does not implicate the rest, and no single gate can ship independently. The four collapse into one execution unit only by editorial grouping, not by a shared mechanism that forces them to land together.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** `c6a664e` (2026-06-10, "pi-loom plan: build/update plan for spec.md + review"); bundle widened from three gates to four at `c33d309` (2026-06-17, "resolve 'Blocking-runtime ban concedes enforcement to manual review'")
**History:** The first plan-build commit `c6a664e` already authored `H2a` as a single leaf bundling three distinct gates (`no-broad-catch`, the `no-restricted-syntax` sequential ban, and the singleton architectural test). The fourth gate — the `*Sync` blocking-runtime ban — was added later at `c33d309` (refined at `8561a7c`, 2026-06-18), widening the bundle but not introducing the multi-gate bundling premise, which was present from inception. Subsequent commits on the file (`d56d65f`, `3d64f7f`, `506d71c`, `cf47e6f`, `30ee609`, `33f9ee5`) refined individual gate bodies and never revisited the bundling.

## Solution approach

Split `H2a` into separate leaves, one per cohesive convention, so each gate ships and reds independently: a leaf owning the `no-broad-catch` rule (*Specific exception types only*), a leaf owning **both** `no-restricted-syntax` rules — the sequential-by-default `Promise.all`/`race`/`allSettled`/`any` ban and the `*Sync` blocking-runtime ban — since both belong to *Sequential by default* and share the `no-restricted-syntax` engine, and a leaf owning the module-level-mutable-binding architectural test (*No globals, statics, singletons*). Each resulting leaf keeps `Deps. H1a` and wires its own assertions into `npm test`; the implementer may repurpose `H2a` itself as one of the three groups to reduce reference churn. Retarget every existing `H2a` reference to whichever split leaf now owns the cited gate: `H3a`'s `Deps. H2a`, the `docs/plan.md` §Horizontal-phases listing entry, the gate-attribution prose / §"mechanical gates scoped to `src/**`" sentence / residue-inspection items 1/3/6 in `conventions.md`, and the `H1a` lint-toolchain test bullet.
