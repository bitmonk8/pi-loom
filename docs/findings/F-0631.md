## Finding

The *Slash-command entry* bullet under **Forwarding into `loomAbort`** in `cancellation.md` makes three first-order claims about Pi's runtime behaviour and presents all three as flat, inline assertions about how Pi behaves — neither cross-referenced nor framed as loom-side presuppositions:

1. Pi delivers a specific closed five-event set (`tool_call`, `tool_result`, `message_update`, `turn_end`, `agent_end`) to the runtime's subscribed handlers during the active user turn.
2. The per-handler `ctx.signal` observed inside those handlers reflects the latest abort state for the turn (so `ctx.signal.aborted` is observable to the forwarding listener).
3. An `agent_end` event "reporting a user-cancelled turn" fires, distinguishably enough that the runtime can use it as an independent cancellation trigger.

Two of these claims have been pinned, but cancellation.md does not say so. Claims (1) and (2) are exactly presupposition (v) of *Pi-side slash-handler promise lifecycle (consumption posture)* in `pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md` (`#pi-slash-handler-promise-lifecycle-presupposition`), with bump-checklist item (v) in `pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md` (`#bump-checklist-turn-lifecycle-event-delivery`) carrying the editorial re-audit obligation, and the event-subscription mechanism is owned by **PIC-18** in `pi-integration-contract/conversation-drive.md` (`#pic-18`). Cancellation.md cites neither anchor at the assertion site; a reader of `cancellation.md` alone has no way to discover that the load-bearing assumptions live in PIC.

Claim (3) — the `agent_end`-as-user-cancellation-indicator path — has no PIC owner at all. Presupposition (iv) covers Pi *eventually* delivering a terminal `agent_end` (turn-liveness), and presupposition (v) covers delivery of the five turn-lifecycle events with fresh `ctx.signal`; neither covers Pi's commitment to *report user cancellation through* an `agent_end` event in a way distinguishable from a normal turn end. This matters because **CNCL-4** *Abort-reason propagation* later in the same file then carves out the `agent_end`-driven trigger as having "no source `AbortSignal`" and synthesises the reason `"loom cancelled by agent_end"` — the synthesised reason is the load-bearing observable, and the trigger that produces it is unanchored.

This is the same defect class neighbouring claims in cancellation.md were already cleaned up for: Pi's tolerance of `undefined` `ctx.signal` at slash-handler entry is anchored to its JSDoc citation in PIC, `complete()`'s `signal` honouring is anchored to the binder's complete() presupposition, and the single-macrotask abort flip is anchored to presupposition (vi). The slash-command forwarding bullet is the only one of the four whose Pi-behavioural assertions remain inline and uncited.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction

**Introducing commits:**
- `8395c03` (2026-05-04, "pi-loom spec: resolve 'Cancellation source ctx.signal is undefined when a slash-handler starts'") — rewrote the *Signal sources* bullet list into the current **Forwarding into `loomAbort`** structure and authored the inline slash-command bullet with the five-event list and the "`agent_end` event reporting a user-cancelled turn aborts `loomAbort`" clause, both as flat assertions.
- `7f07a96` (2026-06-05, "pi-loom spec: resolve 'Cancellation forwarding — turn-lifecycle event delivery not in SDK capability inventory'") — extended `pi-slash-handler-promise-lifecycle-presupposition` from four to five presuppositions, pinning the five-event delivery + per-handler `ctx.signal` freshness claim as presupposition (v) in host-interfaces-core.md, but did not back-link the inline assertion site in cancellation.md to the new anchor.

**History:** `git log -G "user-cancelled" -- "*.md"` over the cancellation.md history shows the inline `agent_end`-reporting clause was introduced by 8395c03 and has not been edited since. The companion PIC anchor for two of the three claims was added a month later by 7f07a96, which expanded the lifecycle-presupposition paragraph from four to five members; that same commit added the version-bump checklist item (v) but did not touch cancellation.md to insert the cross-reference. The third claim (`agent_end` as a user-cancellation indicator distinguishable from turn-end) has no introducing-anchor commit — `git log --all -S "user-cancelled turn"` returns only the original 8395c03 prose and downstream review/plan files that quote it. The defect is therefore the *interaction*: 8395c03 introduced three inline assertions; 7f07a96 retroactively anchored two of them in PIC without back-linking; the third was never anchored. No single revert undoes the interaction.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Edit the *Slash-command entry* bullet under **Forwarding into `loomAbort`** in `docs/spec_topics/cancellation.md` so that all three Pi-behavioural assertions are cross-referenced rather than asserted inline:

1. After "`tool_call`, `tool_result`, `message_update`, `turn_end`, `agent_end`)", append a parenthetical of the form *"(this five-event delivery + per-handler `ctx.signal` freshness is loom-side presupposition (v) of [Pi-side slash-handler promise lifecycle (consumption posture)](./pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md#pi-slash-handler-promise-lifecycle-presupposition); the runtime's subscription mechanism is pinned by [PIC-18](./pi-integration-contract/conversation-drive.md#pic-18))"*. This anchors claims (1) and (2) at the assertion site and eliminates the back-link gap commit `7f07a96` left.
2. For the `agent_end`-as-user-cancellation clause ("an `agent_end` event reporting a user-cancelled turn aborts `loomAbort`"), add a new loom-side presupposition (vii) to the `pi-slash-handler-promise-lifecycle-presupposition` paragraph in host-interfaces-core.md covering "*Pi reports a user-cancelled turn through the terminal `agent_end` event in a way distinguishable from a normal turn end*", then back-reference it from cancellation.md at the assertion site. Add the matching editorial-review checklist item to version-bump-step2.md on the same footing as items (j), (v), and (ad).

Implementer edge cases: do not promote any of the three claims to a PIC-N normative MUST — they are unpinned Pi behaviours observed against the loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin, and the *consumption posture* framing is the established treatment for exactly this case. Keep the new presupposition (vii) parallel in voice to (iv) and (v) so the editorial-review item (v) under version-bump-step2.md can be cloned with a matching anchor (`bump-checklist-agent-end-user-cancellation-reporting` or equivalent). The CNCL-4 synthesised-reason carve-out for the reason-less `agent_end`-driven trigger continues to live in cancellation.md unchanged; only the *existence* of the trigger needs the new PIC anchor, not its reason-propagation behaviour.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader of `cancellation.md` cannot trace the three Pi-behavioural assumptions to their owners and may either (a) reinvent unanchored presuppositions or (b) miss the editorial re-audit obligation when Pi bumps. The `agent_end`-as-user-cancellation claim in particular has no PIC home, so a future Pi minor that changed how user cancellation is reported in `agent_end` would silently degrade the synthesised-reason trigger without falsifying any named presupposition. Implementation is not blocked — V17a and V9c already encode the runtime mechanics correctly — but the version-bump audit gate has a hole.
