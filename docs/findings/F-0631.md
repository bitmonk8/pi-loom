## Problem

The *Slash-command entry* bullet under **Forwarding into `loomAbort`** in `docs/spec_topics/cancellation.md` makes three Pi-behavioural claims inline with no cross-reference: the closed five-event delivery set (`tool_call`, `tool_result`, `message_update`, `turn_end`, `agent_end`), the per-handler `ctx.signal` freshness observed inside those handlers, and the `agent_end` event reporting a user-cancelled turn. The first two are already pinned as presupposition (v) of the *Pi-side slash-handler promise lifecycle (consumption posture)* paragraph (`#pi-slash-handler-promise-lifecycle-presupposition`) with the subscription mechanism owned by PIC-18 (`#pic-18`), but cancellation.md links to neither. The third claim has no PIC owner at all, even though CNCL-4's synthesised `agent_end`-driven reason depends on that trigger existing.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction

**Introducing commits:**
- `8395c03` (2026-05-04, "pi-loom spec: resolve 'Cancellation source ctx.signal is undefined when a slash-handler starts'") — rewrote the *Signal sources* bullet list into the current **Forwarding into `loomAbort`** structure and authored the inline slash-command bullet with the five-event list and the "`agent_end` event reporting a user-cancelled turn aborts `loomAbort`" clause, both as flat assertions.
- `7f07a96` (2026-06-05, "pi-loom spec: resolve 'Cancellation forwarding — turn-lifecycle event delivery not in SDK capability inventory'") — extended `pi-slash-handler-promise-lifecycle-presupposition` from four to five presuppositions, pinning the five-event delivery + per-handler `ctx.signal` freshness claim as presupposition (v) in host-interfaces-core.md, but did not back-link the inline assertion site in cancellation.md to the new anchor.

**History:** `git log -G "user-cancelled" -- "*.md"` over the cancellation.md history shows the inline `agent_end`-reporting clause was introduced by 8395c03 and has not been edited since. The companion PIC anchor for two of the three claims was added a month later by 7f07a96, which expanded the lifecycle-presupposition paragraph from four to five members; that same commit added the version-bump checklist item (v) but did not touch cancellation.md to insert the cross-reference. The third claim (`agent_end` as a user-cancellation indicator distinguishable from turn-end) has no introducing-anchor commit — `git log --all -S "user-cancelled turn"` returns only the original 8395c03 prose and downstream review/plan files that quote it. The defect is therefore the *interaction*: 8395c03 introduced three inline assertions; 7f07a96 retroactively anchored two of them in PIC without back-linking; the third was never anchored. No single revert undoes the interaction.

## Solution approach

Add forward-links from the *Slash-command entry* bullet in cancellation.md to `#pi-slash-handler-promise-lifecycle-presupposition` and `#pic-18`, anchoring the five-event delivery and per-handler `ctx.signal` freshness claims at the assertion site. Add a new loom-side presupposition to the *Pi-side slash-handler promise lifecycle (consumption posture)* paragraph in `host-interfaces-core.md` covering Pi's reporting of a user-cancelled turn through the terminal `agent_end` event distinguishably from a normal turn end, and forward-link the cancellation.md bullet to it. Add a matching editorial-review checklist item to `version-bump-step2.md` alongside item (v) (`#bump-checklist-turn-lifecycle-event-delivery`).

## Solution constraints

- Do not promote any of the three claims to a normative PIC-N MUST — they are unpinned Pi behaviours under the consumption-posture framing, not loom obligations.
- Out of scope: CNCL-4's synthesised-reason carve-out for the reason-less `agent_end`-driven trigger; only the trigger's existence needs the new anchor.
