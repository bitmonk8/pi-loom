## Problem

`V18c`'s editorial-review acceptance gate ranges only over checklist items `(a)`–`(aq)` in both its `Adds.` field and its `Ships when.` clauses, but the spec checklist it gates against in [`version-bump-step2.md`](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md) enumerates two further items the leaf never names. Item `(ar)` is a MUST-level per-bump inbound-reference enumeration-delta reconciliation confirmation; item `(as)` is a SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record item (same footing as `(f)`–`(aq)`). Because the leaf's gate stops at `(aq)`, the acceptance check passes while the MUST-level `(ar)` reconciliation was never recorded, and the leaf's bump-blocking MUST-level set (`(a)`–`(e)`) omits `(ar)` even though `(ar)` is MUST-level.

**History:** F-2264 (1a59ccf) added MUST-level spec item `(ar)` to `version-bump-step2.md` and routed its loom-1.0 enforcement to the editorial-review checklist, but did not extend the `V18c` leaf's range. The next day F-2308 (5c33dab) corrected the `V18c` range from `(al)` to `(aq)` — yet `(ar)` already existed in the spec by then, so that re-sync pulled the leaf forward but stopped one item short, leaving `(ar)` orphaned. F-1797 (d3a1687) then appended spec item `(as)` without touching the `V18c` range, widening the gap to two items.

## Solution approach

Extend `V18c`'s editorial-review acceptance range from `(a)`–`(aq)` to the full `(a)`–`(as)` enumeration in [`version-bump-step2.md`](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md), across both the `Adds.` field and the `Ships when.` clauses. Reflect the spec's normative levels — MUST-level for `(a)`–`(e)` and `(ar)`, SHOULD-audit-but-MUST-record for `(f)`–`(aq)` and `(as)` — and add `(ar)` to the `Ships when.` clause (2) bump-blocking MUST-level set alongside the existing `(a)`–`(e)` condition. Consider phrasing the recorded-outcome range against the checklist the spec enumerates rather than a hardcoded terminal letter, so a future enumerated item is covered without a further leaf edit.
