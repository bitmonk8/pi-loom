## Problem

For a runtime-panic `loom-system-note` (the `loom/runtime/*` codes,
including `loom/runtime/internal-error`), `diagnostic-shape.md`'s
**Serialised content format** paragraph states the
`"<file>:<line>:<col>: <code>: <message>"` line format unconditionally
as the `content` of "each `loom-system-note`", with no carve-out for
panic notes and no link to the reconciliation that already exists in
`runtime-event-channel.md`. Three distinct strings exist for the same
emission — `Diagnostic.message` (the DIAG-4 template), the serialised
`content` line, and the bespoke `"loom /<name> aborted: <message>"`
framing in `code-registry-runtime.md` — and a reader inside the
diagnostics shard cannot tell which is on the wire as `content` for a
panic note. Two implementers reading that shard alone will diverge on
the panic `content` for every panic-source row, and both can honestly
cite the spec.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `ac8c3af` (2026-05-04) — *resolve "LoadExtensionsResult.errors is the wrong delivery channel for loom diagnostics"*; `f8f4813` (2026-05-05) — *resolve "Panic routing has no defined path for unexpected interpreter exceptions"*; `5c8d782` (2026-05-06) — *resolve "Panic routing through loom-system-note is under-specified across two pages"*
**History:** `ac8c3af` introduced the **Serialised content format**
paragraph in `spec_topics/diagnostics.md`, stating the
`"<file>:<line>:<col>: <code>: <message>"` line format
unconditionally as the `content` of "each `loom-system-note`". The
next day `f8f4813` added the `loom/runtime/internal-error` registry
row carrying, in its Trigger prose, the bespoke slash-surface string
`"loom /<name> aborted with internal error: <message>"` — a third
string for the same emission, with no edit to the **Serialised
content format** paragraph above. The day after, `5c8d782`
performed the cross-page reconciliation in
`spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md`: it scoped the
serialised-line format to parse / load / type batches in the
`details`-variant list, added the runtime-panic row to the
per-variant `display` / `content` matrix with the `"loom /<name>
aborted…"` framing, and rewrote the panic-emission bullet under
**Deduplication and lifetime rules** to pin the framing as the
panic `content`. That reconciliation was never back-ported into
`diagnostic-shape.md`'s **Serialised content format** paragraph,
which still reads as unconditional today (carried forward through
the `spec_topics/diagnostics.md` → `spec_topics/diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md`
split). The defect is the omitted edit at the diagnostics shard's
own source-of-truth paragraph, not any single one of the three
commits in isolation.

## Solution approach

In `diagnostic-shape.md`'s **Serialised content format** paragraph,
narrow the `"<file>:<line>:<col>: <code>: <message>"` line format to
parse / load / type batches and pin that a runtime-panic note (a
single-element `Diagnostic[]` carrying a `loom/runtime/*` code)
instead carries the user-facing framing as its `content`. Forward-link
the runtime-panic clause to `runtime-event-channel.md`'s per-variant
`display` / `content` matrix and its panic-emission bullet under
**Deduplication and lifetime rules** rather than restating the framing
rule. In `code-registry-runtime.md`, delete the parenthetical
wire-format prose in the `internal-error` and panic-source rows that
restates the framing, replacing it with a pointer to that same matrix.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the framing-string definition — `runtime-event-channel.md`'s per-variant matrix and `errors-and-results.md` **Runtime panics** remain its single owner; do not restate the framing literal in `diagnostic-shape.md`.
- Out of scope: the five `console.error`-routed teardown-handler codes (`reload-teardown-timeout` and the four `loom/host/session-shutdown-*`) — their wire shape is owned by **Diagnostic-emission isolation**, not the **Serialised content format** paragraph.
