## Finding

The governance subtree coins two distinct anchor concepts that both lead with the qualifier `dual-`, and neither has a glossary entry or in-prose disambiguator pointing at the other:

- **GOV-1's "dual-form" pair** (`req-id-prefix-table-active-a.md` *Canonical form* / *Dual-form layout* / *Per-page progressive normalisation* / *Edge cases*, also referenced from `stable-inline-labels.md` and `req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` GOV-22) — a co-located pair *on a single source line*: an `<a id="prefix-n"></a>` HTML token immediately followed by the inline `**PREFIX-N.**` marker. The word "dual" here means *two anchor forms (HTML + inline-bold) for one REQ-ID at one site*; its witness regex is `<a id="prefix-n"></a> \*\*PREFIX-N\.\*\*`.

- **GOV-25–GOV-29's "dual-anchored" heading or paragraph** (`release-version-naming.md`, intensional definition pinned by GOV-27) — *two `<a id>` tags* (a canonical `loom-1-0-…` arm and an alias `v1-…` arm) co-located at one heading or paragraph so that inbound cross-links resolving against either fragment land at the same target. The word "dual" here means *two anchor slugs (canonical + alias) for one heading*.

The two concepts touch disjoint mechanics — REQ-ID anchor *layout* vs. legacy-token alias *back-compat* — but a reader doing `grep dual-` across `governance/`, or skimming the section structure, sees adjacent governance prose that uses overlapping vocabulary for orthogonal rules. The conflict is sharpened by the fact that GOV-27's "dual-anchored" sites *also* satisfy GOV-1 (they need REQ-ID anchors in dual-form), so a single sentence can legitimately need both qualifiers. The glossary defines neither term, so there is no canonical place that distinguishes them.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction

**Introducing commits:**
- `ce1af28` — "pi-loom spec: resolve 'GOV-1 leaves anchor placement ambiguous in two ways H6 cannot mechanically resolve'" (2026-05-06) — first introduced GOV-1's *Dual-form layout* sub-bullet and the term `dual-form` for the co-located inline-marker + HTML-anchor pair.
- `4a7afbf` — "pi-loom spec: introduce release-version naming + legacy token aliases (GOV-19/20/21)" (2026-05-28) — introduced GOV-21's "dual-anchor convention" and the predicate "*dual-anchored* iff it carries both a `<a id=\"v1-…\">` anchor and a `<a id=\"loom-1-0-…\">` anchor", overlapping the `dual-` qualifier with the pre-existing GOV-1 vocabulary.
- `8e3ccb4` — "pi-loom spec: resolve 'GOV-21 bundles five independently testable sub-clauses'" (later) — split GOV-21 into GOV-25..GOV-29 and pinned the term in GOV-27's official title "dual-anchored intensional definition", elevating the colliding qualifier to a named REQ-ID.

**History:** The collision is a multi-commit interaction. The GOV-1 dual-form terminology landed first as part of a layout-clarification fix; three weeks later, GOV-21 was authored out-of-band (the commit message explicitly notes "bypasses the spec-review pipeline") and chose `dual-anchor` for an unrelated alias-pair concept without checking the existing `dual-`-prefixed vocabulary. The later GOV-21 split into GOV-25..29 preserved the colliding term in GOV-27's name. Neither author of the two `dual-` coinages appears to have noticed the other; the lens that would have flagged it (cross-page naming hygiene in `governance/`) was not run on either commit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Rename GOV-25..29's "dual-anchored" → "alias-paired" (or "twin-slug")

**Approach.** Replace the qualifier `dual-anchored` (and the section-header phrase `dual-anchor convention`) on `release-version-naming.md` with a term that does not lead with `dual-`. The original finding suggested "dual-slug / alias-paired anchor"; "alias-paired" is the cleanest single-word swap. Update GOV-27's title to "alias-paired intensional definition" (or similar), update the four sibling rules (GOV-25, GOV-26, GOV-28, GOV-29) to use the new qualifier, update the inline references on `anchor-scheme-and-retired.md`'s retired-prefix row, and add a glossary entry for **alias-paired heading or paragraph** that points at GOV-27. Add a one-line "*Not to be confused with GOV-1 dual-form anchor (a single REQ-ID's HTML + inline-marker pair).*" disambiguator under the new term in the preamble.

**Spec edits.**
- `release-version-naming.md`: ~6 occurrences of `dual-anchored` and 2 of `dual-anchor convention` in the preamble + GOV-25..29 rule bodies.
- `anchor-scheme-and-retired.md`: 1 occurrence in the GOV-21 retired-prefix row cell.
- `glossary.md`: add **alias-paired heading or paragraph** entry citing GOV-27 as canonical home.

**Pros.** Removes the `dual-` collision at its source; "alias-paired" reads accurately (the canonical arm is not an alias; the *pair* exists *for* the alias). GOV-1's terminology — older, broader downstream surface in `req-id-prefix-table-active-a/-b.md` and `stable-inline-labels.md` — stays untouched, so the rename's blast radius is bounded to one topic file plus one row in the retired-prefix table.

**Cons.** GOV-27's official title changes, which is a visible rename of a coined REQ-ID title (not the ID itself). Reviewers familiar with the `dual-anchored` phrase from recent commits will need to re-learn the term.

**Risks.** Low. The term "dual-anchored" does not appear in `plan.md`, `plan_topics/`, or any non-governance spec file, so the rename does not propagate beyond the governance subtree plus the one glossary entry.

### Option B — Glossary-only disambiguation, keep both names

**Approach.** Leave both `dual-form` and `dual-anchored` in place and add two glossary entries — **dual-form REQ-ID anchor** (citing GOV-1) and **dual-anchored heading or paragraph** (citing GOV-27) — each carrying a one-sentence "not to be confused with the other" disambiguator. Optionally add an inline cross-link in the GOV-27 preamble that explicitly distinguishes from GOV-1.

**Spec edits.**
- `glossary.md`: two new entries.
- `release-version-naming.md`: one inline disambiguator sentence in the preamble.
- No other file edits.

**Pros.** Smallest possible blast radius; no rename of any coined REQ-ID title. Both terms remain greppable in their current form.

**Cons.** The `dual-` collision survives; a grep for `dual-` still pulls both. Glossary disambiguation is reactive — it helps the reader who already noticed the confusion and looked the term up, not the reader who silently mis-cited.

**Risks.** Low; the defect persists in the same shape it has today, and future contributors continue to pay the same clarity tax.

### Recommendation

Adopt **Option A** (rename GOV-25..29's qualifier to **alias-paired**) and also add the glossary entry. GOV-1's `dual-form` terminology is older, more widely cited (three governance files plus internal back-references on `req-id-prefix-table-active-a.md`), and structurally more central — it is part of the witness regex contracted by GOV-9 and GOV-22 — so renaming it would have a much larger blast radius than renaming the GOV-27 family, which is bounded to one topic file plus one retired-prefix row. Resolve in this order: (1) update the preamble + GOV-25..29 prose on `release-version-naming.md`; (2) update the retired-prefix row on `anchor-scheme-and-retired.md`; (3) add the **alias-paired heading or paragraph** glossary entry citing GOV-27 as canonical. Watch for: the GOV-27 REQ-ID identifier itself stays as `GOV-27` — only its descriptive title changes, so no `#gov-27` cross-link breaks.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A future contributor grepping `dual-form` or `dual-anchor` across `governance/` retrieves both rule families and must read each hit's surrounding paragraph to decide which "dual" is at play. Misreading is unlikely to produce wrong runtime behaviour (the two rules' mechanics are syntactically disjoint), but mis-citation in commit messages, review comments, or new spec prose is easy: a sentence saying "the dual anchor at this site is missing" is ambiguous between a missing HTML form and a missing alias arm. The defect is a clarity tax on every reader who touches governance, not a behavioural divergence risk.
