## Problem

The governance subtree coins two distinct anchor concepts that both lead with the qualifier `dual-`: GOV-1's `dual-form` pair (an `<a id="prefix-n"></a>` HTML token co-located with an inline `**PREFIX-N.**` marker for one REQ-ID at one site, on `req-id-prefix-table-active-a.md`) and GOV-25–GOV-29's `dual-anchored` / `dual-anchor convention` (two `<a id>` arms — a canonical `loom-1-0-…` arm and an alias `v1-…` arm — co-located at one heading or paragraph, on `release-version-naming.md`). The two concepts govern disjoint mechanics — REQ-ID anchor layout vs. legacy-token alias back-compat — yet a reader grepping `dual-` across `governance/` retrieves both families and must read surrounding prose to tell which is meant; the collision is sharpened because a GOV-27 dual-anchored site also satisfies GOV-1. Neither term has a glossary entry or in-prose disambiguator pointing at the other.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction

**Introducing commits:**
- `ce1af28` (2026-05-06) — first introduced GOV-1's *Dual-form layout* sub-bullet and the term `dual-form`.
- `4a7afbf` (2026-05-28) — introduced GOV-21's "dual-anchor convention" and the predicate "*dual-anchored* iff it carries both a `<a id=\"v1-…\">` anchor and a `<a id=\"loom-1-0-…\">` anchor", overlapping the `dual-` qualifier with the pre-existing GOV-1 vocabulary.
- `8e3ccb4` — split GOV-21 into GOV-25..GOV-29 and pinned the term in GOV-27's official title "dual-anchored intensional definition", elevating the colliding qualifier to a named REQ-ID.

**History:** The collision is a multi-commit interaction. The GOV-1 dual-form terminology landed first; three weeks later GOV-21 was authored out-of-band (the commit message explicitly notes "bypasses the spec-review pipeline") and chose `dual-anchor` for an unrelated alias-pair concept without checking the existing vocabulary. The later GOV-21 split into GOV-25..29 preserved the colliding term in GOV-27's name.

## Solution approach

Rename the GOV-25–GOV-29 qualifier (`dual-anchored` and `dual-anchor convention`) to a non-`dual-`-leading term across `release-version-naming.md`'s preamble and the GOV-25–GOV-29 rule bodies, including GOV-27's descriptive title. Add a glossary entry for the renamed concept on `glossary.md` citing GOV-27 as canonical home, and add an in-prose disambiguator under the renamed term that distinguishes it from GOV-1's `dual-form`.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: renaming GOV-1's `dual-form` terminology; its witness regex `<a id="prefix-n"></a> **PREFIX-N.**` is contracted by GOV-9 and GOV-22.
- Preserve the `GOV-27` REQ-ID and its `#gov-27` anchor unchanged; only GOV-27's descriptive title is renamed.
