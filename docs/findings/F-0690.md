## Finding

`drain-state-contract.md` defines three operator-visible system-note templates that the slash-command handler may return in lieu of dispatching a loom:

- arm (b) — `"loom /<name>: extension shutting down"`
- arm (c) — `"loom /<name>: extension degraded; /reload to recover"`
- *Superseded-entry dispatch* (a sub-case of arm (a)) — `"loom /<name>: superseded; /reload to refresh"`

The two arm-(b)/arm-(c) templates are explicitly governed by a *deliberate disjointness* clause in `patch-skew-degradation.md` sub-step 1, which pins the substrings `"shutting down"` and `"degraded"` as the grep keys operator tooling MUST extend its match to after the legacy single-template surface was split. The *Superseded-entry dispatch* paragraph, added later, frames its template as "modelled on arm (c)'s `…degraded; /reload to recover` note" yet substitutes `refresh` for `recover` — and the disjointness analysis is not extended to cover this third operator-visible string.

Two consequences follow. (1) Operator tooling that grepped on the `recover` substring (the operator-action keyword the disjointness clause spotlights) will silently miss the superseded path's note even though the operator action is the same `/reload`. (2) The choice between `refresh` and `recover` is now a coin-flip for an editor extending either template — there is no rule pinning which verb belongs with which note — so a future edit that aligns one toward the other will look equally well-grounded as the current divergence.

The plan corpus already pins the superseded note's verbatim text on `V9m`'s acceptance criteria, so the divergence has propagated outward unchallenged.

Neither commit individually authored the defect; the durable fix lives at the seam.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Align "refresh" to "recover"

**Approach.** Replace `"loom /<name>: superseded; /reload to refresh"` with `"loom /<name>: superseded; /reload to recover"` in `drain-state-contract.md`'s *Superseded-entry dispatch* paragraph, and update the matching parenthetical clause ("the operator action that re-runs the extension factory and refreshes the slash command list") to drop the verb-justification. Sweep the two `V9m` plan leaves that quote the template verbatim.

**Spec edits.**
- `drain-state-contract.md` — change the template literal and rephrase the surrounding parenthetical.
- `plan_topics/V9m-drain-state-contract.md`, `plan_topics/V9m-T-drain-state-contract.md` — update the verbatim template quotation in the Tests bullets and the *Ships when* sentence.

**Pros.**
- Strengthens the existing disjointness contract by collapsing the operator-action keyword to a single substring (`recover`).
- Smaller surface: one shared verb across both notes is one fewer thing for tooling and editors to track.
- Aligns with the *Superseded-entry dispatch* paragraph's own framing ("modelled on arm (c)").

**Cons.**
- `refresh` was arguably the more precise verb for the superseded case (the slash-command list, not the degraded runtime, is what is restored), and the change drops that nuance.

**Risks.**
- The plan files pin the template verbatim and must be edited in lockstep; missing one leaves a plan↔spec drift.

### Option B — Document "refresh" as deliberately disjoint

**Approach.** Extend the existing *deliberately disjoint* clause in `patch-skew-degradation.md` sub-step 1 (or add a parallel sentence in `drain-state-contract.md`'s *Superseded-entry dispatch* paragraph) to pin all three operator-action substrings — `"recover"` and `"refresh"` for the `/reload`-prompting notes plus the bare `"shutting down"` for the transient case — as the closed set of grep keys operator tooling MUST extend its match to.

**Spec edits.**
- `patch-skew-degradation.md` sub-step 1 — extend the "deliberately disjoint at the substrings …" sentence to enumerate the third template's distinguishing substring, or add a back-reference sentence pointing to `drain-state-contract.md`'s superseded paragraph.
- `drain-state-contract.md` *Superseded-entry dispatch* paragraph — add a one-sentence cross-reference noting that `refresh` is the third pinned operator-action keyword.

**Pros.**
- Preserves the more-precise `refresh` verb for the superseded case.
- The disjointness rule grows to cover the surface it was always meant to cover, making future template additions easier to land correctly.

**Cons.**
- Expands the operator-tooling contract surface from two substrings to three.
- The disjointness rule now lives split across two pages unless the back-reference is carefully maintained.

**Risks.**
- Any future fourth template would require another spec edit; the closed enumeration becomes a recurring maintenance touchpoint.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A**. The disjointness contract exists to give operator tooling a single, stable per-action grep key; the superseded path's operator action is `/reload` — the same as arm (c)'s — so a divergent verb actively works against that contract for no behavioural gain. The nuance Option B preserves (`refresh` reading slightly more precisely than `recover` for the registration-list case) is a thin justification against the cost of a third pinned substring and the split-page rule it produces. Implementer edge case: the two `V9m` plan leaves quote the template verbatim and MUST be edited in the same commit, or `H5a`-class plan↔spec drift gates will redden.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The runtime behaviour is unaffected — `/reload` is the operator action under both notes — but operator tooling that buckets the loom-system-note surface by the action-keyword substring will silently drop one of the three note categories, weakening the in-band signal the disjointness clause was authored to preserve. Without a normative pin the divergence is also a recurring small-edit attractor for downstream contributors.
