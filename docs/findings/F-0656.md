## Finding

Two presuppositions in `pi-integration-contract/conversation-drive.md` —
`#complete-forced-tool-presupposition` and
`#complete-retry-and-cancellation-presupposition` — explicitly state that
they govern the binder's `complete()` call as well as the typed-query
forced respond turn, and both cross-reference
`binder-inference.md#binder-inference-call` in their bodies. The
`complete()` entry point itself, the bullet enumeration of every
`options` field it takes (`temperature`, `signal`, `seed`, `onResponse`)
and the binder's full failure-classification surface all live on
`binder-inference.md`; `conversation-drive.md` does not otherwise own
this entry point.

The retry/cancellation presupposition has no inbound link from
`binder-inference.md` at all: the binder's `options.signal =
loomAbort.signal` bullet asserts cancellation wiring without pointing at
the `options.signal`-honouring presupposition, and the trailing
"delegating per-attempt backoff and any server-requested wait … to this
call's `StreamOptions.maxRetries` / `StreamOptions.maxRetryDelayMs`"
sentence asserts the retry-timing delegation without pointing at the
retry-timing presupposition. The forced-tool presupposition does have a
single inbound link from `binder-inference.md`'s `context.tools` bullet,
but it is buried mid-paragraph next to the typed-query forced-respond
discussion, so a reader of the binder page still ends up navigating to
the typed-query section of a different file to find the contract that
governs the binder's own `complete()` call.

The result is that `binder-inference.md` reads as if its `complete()`
usage is fully pinned in place, when in fact two of the behavioural
properties the binder relies on — forced-tool selection via
`options.toolChoice`, and `options.signal`-honouring cancellation plus
internal retry-timing — live on a different page, under a section whose
title (*Provider compatibility for typed queries*) advertises the
typed-query forced-respond turn and not the binder. An auditor walking
the binder call's contract surface will miss them, and the corresponding
version-bump checklist items (u), (aa), (ab) accordingly read as
typed-query-only obligations even though their loss would also break
every binder inference.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Relocate both presuppositions to `binder-inference.md`, immediately after
the `complete(model, context, options)` bullet enumeration (where
`options.signal`, `StreamOptions.maxRetries` /
`StreamOptions.maxRetryDelayMs`, and the forced-tool delivery mechanism
are already discussed), preserving the `#complete-forced-tool-presupposition`
and `#complete-retry-and-cancellation-presupposition` anchor ids verbatim
so existing inbound links from `version-bump-step2.md` items (u), (aa),
(ab) and from `docs/findings/F-0537.md` continue to resolve. Replace the
two paragraphs in `conversation-drive.md` with one forward-pointer
sentence under *Provider compatibility for typed queries* stating that
the per-provider `complete()` forced-tool, retry-timing, and
cancellation behavioural properties the typed-query forced-respond turn
depends on are pinned on `binder-inference.md` at those two anchors.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- `binder-inference.md` already cross-references
  `#complete-forced-tool-presupposition` from its `context.tools`
  bullet. After relocation, rewrite that cross-reference to a same-file
  link (`#complete-forced-tool-presupposition` on `binder-inference.md`)
  rather than leaving the cross-file form pointing at the now-stub
  paragraph on `conversation-drive.md`.
- The binder's `options.signal` bullet and the trailing
  retry-delegation sentence currently have no anchor to
  `#complete-retry-and-cancellation-presupposition`; add the two local
  anchor links in the same edit, so the relocation also closes the
  related "binder retry timing delegates backoff/`Retry-After` to pi-ai
  without verification" finding rather than leaving it live with the
  presupposition merely renamed.
- The forced-tool presupposition's bodies reference
  `#pic-typed-query-noncompliance` (the typed-query diagnostic
  limitation) which lives on `conversation-drive.md`. Keep the
  reference as a cross-file link after relocation; do not move the
  diagnostic-limitation block.
- `version-bump-step2.md` items (u), (aa), (ab) link to the two
  anchors. The anchor ids stay verbatim, so the items need no edit, but
  re-read each item's introductory link text — phrased as
  "[Conversation drive — `complete()` …]" — and rephrase to
  "[Binder inference — `complete()` …]" so the breadcrumb prose matches
  the new host page.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer or reviewer working from `binder-inference.md` to wire up
the binder's `complete()` call can miss the two behavioural properties
the call depends on (named-tool forcing via `options.toolChoice`,
`options.signal`-honouring cancellation, internal retry/`Retry-After`
timing) and the corresponding version-bump editorial-review items, since
the binder page does not anchor them locally. Specifically, the binder's
own `options.signal` and retry-timing bullets stand without any inbound
link to the presupposition that justifies them, so a Pi minor that
regressed either property would not be caught by anything the binder
page itself points at.
