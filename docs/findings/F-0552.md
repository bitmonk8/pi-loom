## Finding

`V11e` (`docs/plan_topics/V11e-system-note-determinism.md`) delivers two logically independent concerns in a single leaf. Arm (a) is the binder **system-note rendering** discipline: the five line-rules from `binder/defaulting-system-note-echo.md` §System-note rendering — single-line collapse/trim, the 120-code-point truncation-with-`…` cap, the prefix/suffix demarcation grammar, empty-model-content→malformed-envelope classification, and `ambiguous.candidates` non-surfacing (tracked as coverage row `cka-41`). Arm (b) is the binder **determinism contract** from `binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md` §Determinism — `temperature:0` on every call plus FNV-1a seed derivation (tracked as coverage row `cka-42`).

The two arms share neither spec page, failure mode, nor test fixture. A rendering defect is a string-processing bug observed against a reference rendering; a determinism defect is a non-deterministic-call bug observed by asserting seed/temperature stability across repeated inputs. The leaf's single **Ships when** gates both at once, so a reviewer evaluates two unrelated verification strategies in one pass, and the paired `V11e-T` carries both fixture families.

This is an organisational defect, not an execution blocker — the leaf is small enough to land in one TDD pass, and arm (b) partially overlaps `V9j`, which already owns the `complete()` call's `temperature:0` and seed-field mapping (`V11e`'s distinct determinism content is the binder-side FNV-1a seed *derivation*). The bundling is an advisory granularity concern.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Keep `V11e` whole

**Approach:** Leave the leaf as a single rendering+determinism unit.
**Plan edits:** None.
**Spec edits:** None.
**Pros:** Zero churn; no new leaf ID, no coverage-matrix retarget, no `Deps` propagation. Both arms are small and land in one TDD pass.
**Cons:** A reviewer still evaluates two unrelated verification strategies together; a regression in either arm reopens the joint `Ships when`.
**Risks:** None beyond the standing advisory concern.

### Option B — Split rendering off; fold determinism into `V11f`

**Approach:** Retain `V11e`/`V11e-T` as the system-note **rendering** leaf (rules 1–5, `cka-41`) only, and move the determinism contract (`temperature:0`, FNV-1a seed derivation, `cka-42`) into `V11f`, which already cites `binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md`, depends on `V9j`, and owns adjacent binder-call mechanics.
**Plan edits:**
- In `V11e-system-note-determinism.md` and `V11e-T-system-note-determinism.md`: remove the `determinism-cancellation-failure.md — binder determinism` Tests bullet and the determinism clause from `Adds`/`Ships when`/`Spec`; retitle to the rendering scope.
- In `V11f-binder-retry-taxonomy.md` and `V11f-T-binder-retry-taxonomy.md`: add the FNV-1a-seed / `temperature:0` determinism Tests bullet and the corresponding `Adds`/`Ships when` clause (the `Spec` field already lists `determinism-cancellation-failure.md`).
- In `coverage-matrix.md`: retarget `cka-42`'s closing-leaf cell from `V11e` to `V11f`.
**Spec edits:** None.
**Pros:** Clean separation by failure mode; determinism lands with `V9j`-adjacent binder-call concerns; no new leaf ID.
**Cons:** Touches four leaf files plus the coverage matrix; enlarges `V11f`, itself flagged elsewhere as already dense.
**Risks:** `cka-42` retarget must land atomically with the leaf edits or the H5d/H5e closing gates see a determinism row pointing at a leaf that no longer asserts it.

### Recommendation

Given the advisory severity, **Option A is acceptable as-is** — do not split solely on account of this finding. If the fixer is already restructuring the V11 binder slice, prefer **Option B**: keep `V11e` as the rendering leaf and fold the determinism MUSTs (`cka-42`) into `V11f`, since `V11f` already cites `determinism-cancellation-failure.md` and sits with the binder-call mechanics. Whichever is chosen, the `cka-42` coverage row and its closing leaf must stay in agreement, and any renumbering must honour the existing `V<n><letter>` scheme (no invented final IDs — use `<new>` until the implementer assigns one).
