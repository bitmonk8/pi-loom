## Problem

The §V9 and §V11 interleave notes in `docs/plan.md` declare the seam `V9b → V11a → V9c`/`V9i`/`V9j` and state that `V11a` is a prerequisite of `V9c`. For `V9i` and `V9j` this holds — both list `V11a` directly in their `Deps`. For `V9c` it does not: `V9c`'s `Deps` are `V9c-T, V9a, V8a, V17a` and `V9c-T`'s are `V9a, V8a, V17a`, naming neither `V11a` nor `V9b`, with no transitive path to `V11a`. `V9c` does not consume binder-model resolution — the `stopReason`/trailing-turn seams it exposes are consumed downstream by `V9n`, which owns the binder-classifier coupling. The notes (narrative) and the `Deps` (the canonical build-order authority) disagree, risking a maintainer "fixing" the contradiction by re-coupling the deliberately decoupled `V9c`.

**History:** Commit b9b427d (2026-06-10) introduced the §V9 and §V11 interleave notes naming `V9c`/`V9i`/`V9j` as `V11a`-prerequisites; at that point the claim held transitively, because `V9c`'s `Deps` then read `V9c-T, V9a, V9j, V8a` and `V9j` carries a direct `V11a` edge (`V9c → V9j → V11a`). Commit 0c3a2a2 (2026-06-17) split `V9c`'s concern clusters and removed `V9j` from `V9c`'s `Deps` (the transport-error mapping moved to `V9n`), severing the only `V9c → V11a` path. The interleave notes were not updated to match, leaving them stale relative to the post-split `Deps`.

## Solution approach

Correct the §V9 and §V11 interleave notes in `docs/plan.md` so `V9c` no longer appears in the `V11a`-prerequisite set or the seam arrow, leaving the `V9i`/`V9j` references intact. Leave `V9c`/`V9c-T`'s `Deps` unchanged — they are accurate. Preserve the rest of each note (the `V11a`-lands-mid-V9 point, the `V18c` clauses, the `V11e`/`V11h` inversion).

## Solution constraints

- Do not add `V11a` or `V9b` to `V9c`/`V9c-T`'s `Deps`; the binder-classifier coupling is owned by `V9n`.
- Out of scope: the inversion-note additions to the §V9 note owned by F-1114 — coordinate so both edits to that note land consistently.
