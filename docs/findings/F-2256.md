## Problem

The Pi version-bump procedure is an ordered list whose step 1 lives in `version-bump-intro.md`, while `version-bump-step2.md` opens with the *Editorial-review checklist for unpinned host presuppositions* block before the numbered `2.` line (*Re-run the SDK surface-inventory tests*) that defines sub-step `(a)` (the positive-direction seven-capability literal-read assertion, anchor `id="bump-step-2-positive"`). The checklist therefore renders between step 1 and the numbered step 2, carries no list number, and — indented as a list continuation — reads as a trailing part of step 1 rather than a step of its own. Roughly forty SHOULD-level checklist items close with "on the same footing as the seven-capability literal-read assertion under step 2(a) above", but step 2(a) physically appears below the checklist, so every "above" reference resolves downward. The checklist's execution order relative to the numbered steps 1–7 is consequently unstated, and its only positional self-reference contradicts its rendered location.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `e5cd514` (2026-05-09, "T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit"), `52b9101` (2026-05-11, "T24 — Fork-reason watcher closure …"), and the later SHOULD-level-item additions beginning `8c930fe` (2026-05-17, "T22c …") and `e89e42e` (2026-05-17, "T21 …").
**History:** The corpus is git-tracked. `git log -S 'step 2(a) above'` shows the `step 2(a)` referent and the `2(a)`/`2(b)` split of the SDK-surface-inventory step were introduced first, at `e5cd514` — before any editorial-review checklist existed. Two days later `52b9101` introduced the editorial-review checklist (then only items `(a)`–`(d)`, none of which referenced step 2(a)) and placed it physically ahead of the numbered `2.` line; at that point the unnumbered/mis-positioned structure was already present but carried no backward reference. The wrong-direction "step 2(a) above" references then accreted incrementally as SHOULD-level items `(f)`–`(aq)` were added across many later commits (`8c930fe`, `e89e42e`, and subsequent item-addition commits), each appending the "on the same footing as the seven-capability literal-read assertion under step 2(a) above" clause. `git log --follow` confirms the inverted ordering survived the `81bf52a` (2026-06-11) split of the integration-contract hub into `version-bump-*.md` sub-pages. No single commit owns the defect: the `step 2(a)` referent, the checklist's placement before it, and the "above" citations to it each entered in different commits, and the durable fix is the structural ordering of the checklist relative to the numbered step-2 block rather than a revert of any one commit.

## Solution approach

Move the *Editorial-review checklist for unpinned host presuppositions* block in `version-bump-step2.md` so it follows the numbered `2.` block's `(a)`/`(b)` sub-steps (anchors `id="bump-step-2-positive"` / `id="bump-step-2-negative"`), and restructure it as an explicitly-labelled trailing portion of step 2. Add a sentence pinning its execution order relative to the numbered steps. With step 2(a) physically preceding the checklist, the existing "step 2(a) above" references resolve correctly without editing them.

## Solution constraints

- Keep the checklist inside step 2; do not promote it to a new top-level step — renumbering cascades into the cluster's "step 3" / "step 5 below" / "step 2(b)" cross-references in `version-bump-intro.md`, `version-bump-step2b.md`, and `version-bump-triggers.md`.
- Preserve the checklist's `(a)`–`(aq)` item labels; their relabel is owned by F-2255 and must be reconciled with this fix rather than applied here.
