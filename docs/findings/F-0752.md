## Finding

`query-escapes-stringification.md` is scoped, by its title and by the contents of its first two sections (`## Escapes`, `## Stringification of interpolated values`), to two things: the escape grammar inside an `@`...`` template body, and the per-Loom-static-type rendering of `${expr}` interpolations into prompt text. The third and fourth top-level sections — `## Discarded query results are a parse error (`loom/parse/discarded-query-result`)` and `## Observability of discarded results` (with its `### Panics during interpolation are not caught by let _ =` subsection) — instead define a distinct query expression form (the bare `@`...`` expression-statement and its `let _ = …` / `void`-tail discard variants), the `loom/parse/discarded-query-result` parse error that gates it, and the operator-channel `RuntimeEvent` that preserves a discarded `Err`. None of that is about escapes or value-to-text rendering.

The mis-placement has live consequences on the cross-references that point into this file. `code-registry-parse.md`'s row for `loom/parse/discarded-query-result` already routes readers to `[Query — Discarded query results](../query.md)` — i.e. it deliberately avoids naming `query-escapes-stringification.md`, because that filename does not describe the content. `glossary.md`'s `query-terminating` entry, in contrast, links to `./query/query-escapes-stringification.md#observability-of-discarded-results`, which surfaces the same dissonance to the reader following the link. `query-tool-loop.md` cites this file as the source for "post-interpolation, post-newline-trim, post-dedent" — the rendering pipeline — confirming that the file's expected scope is the rendering pipeline, not the discard rule. The discard form is a sibling of the other forms documented in `query-forms.md` (untyped, typed, explicit) and the observability section is a contract between the query primitive and the always-log channel — both belong with the form-level material in `query-forms.md`, leaving `query-escapes-stringification.md` to own only what its title claims.

The `### Panics during interpolation are not caught by let _ =` subsection is genuinely cross-cutting (panics arise from `${expr}` evaluation, so they touch interpolation; their non-containment by the discard form is a property of the discard form), and the right home is whichever file the parent `## Observability of discarded results` section ends up in, with a one-line `See also` from the interpolation-pipeline notes pointing at it.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-split

**Introducing commits:** `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04, *docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set*).

**History:** Both sections were authored in `53f5831` (2026-05-03, *spec: rewrite query primitive, system prompts, and schema syntax*) as `## Discarded query results are a parse error` and `## Observability of discarded results` adjacent to the escapes/stringification sections inside a single monolithic `spec_topics/query.md` (later `docs/spec_topics/query.md`). At that point the placement was unobjectionable — they were all sections of one Query topic. The misplacement was introduced when `f5e89f4` mechanically split `query.md` into four child files by **line-range partitioning** (per `docs/_tools/split_spec.py`'s `PLANS["spec_topics/query.md"]` entry, which assigns lines 136–192 to a child slug `query-escapes-stringification`). Lines 136–192 of the pre-split file happened to contain `## Escapes`, `## Stringification of interpolated values`, `## Discarded query results are a parse error`, and `## Observability of discarded results` contiguously, so all four landed in the same child file even though the latter two are not about escapes or stringification. The split was driven by file-size caps, not topical grouping, and no review pass re-grouped the four resulting query/ children afterward. The `b3bc4ce` commit (2026-05-04, *spec: pin failure-observability surface*) is the earlier rewrite that promoted the observability paragraph from an inline parenthetical to its own `## ` section in the monolithic file; it predates the split and so did not introduce the misplacement — only enlarged the surface that the split later misallocated.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move both sections — `## Discarded query results are a parse error (loom/parse/discarded-query-result)` and `## Observability of discarded results`, including the latter's nested `### Panics during interpolation are not caught by let _ =` subsection — verbatim from `query-escapes-stringification.md` to `query-forms.md`, appended after the existing `### Explicit form` / `## Multi-line templates` block (i.e. as new top-level sections of `query-forms.md`, alongside the untyped / typed / explicit form sections). Preserve the existing anchor structure: `## Discarded query results are a parse error (loom/parse/discarded-query-result)` keeps its slug-derived anchor `discarded-query-results-are-a-parse-error-loomparsediscarded-query-result` (or whichever slug the toolchain currently emits — verify by grep of incoming links before and after the move); `## Observability of discarded results` keeps `observability-of-discarded-results`; the `### Panics during interpolation are not caught by let _ =` subsection keeps `panics-during-interpolation-are-not-caught-by-let-_-`.

After the move, in `query-escapes-stringification.md`, append a one-line `See also: [Query forms — Panics during interpolation are not caught by let _ =](./query-forms.md#…)` cross-reference under the `## Stringification of interpolated values` notes, because the panic subsection is the one piece of relocated content with a genuine pull on the interpolation pipeline.

Repoint the four existing cross-references:

- `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` — change `./query/query-escapes-stringification.md#observability-of-discarded-results` to `./query/query-forms.md#observability-of-discarded-results`.
- `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/code-registry-parse.md` — tighten the `loom/parse/discarded-query-result` row's link from the bare `../query.md` to `../query/query-forms.md#discarded-query-results-are-a-parse-error-loomparsediscarded-query-result` (or whichever anchor the move preserves); the existing bare-index link is a workaround that is no longer needed once the section is in a topically-coherent file.
- `docs/plan_topics/V13g-query-discard-observability.md` and `V13g-T-query-discard-observability.md` — replace `query-escapes-stringification.md` in the `Spec.` field with `query-forms.md`.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- Run `docs/_tools/check_links.py` (the corpus link/anchor validator added in `f5e89f4`) after the move to confirm no incoming link is dangled and the four repointed targets resolve. The author-intent residual dangling refs from the original split commit are unrelated to this finding and should be left alone.
- Anchor slugs in the existing file may include the parenthesised diagnostic code in the heading; verify the resulting slug at the destination matches what incoming links spell, and adjust the heading wording (not the link) if the toolchain emits a different slug at the destination than it did at the source. The safest path is to keep the heading text byte-identical across the move.
- `query-tool-loop.md`'s citation `[Query escapes and stringification](./query-escapes-stringification.md)` remains correct after the move — that citation is about the rendering pipeline, which stays in `query-escapes-stringification.md`. Do not touch it.
- The `Pi Integration Contract — Runtime event channel` page contains the sentence "The symmetric statement on the consumer side is owned by [Query — Observability of discarded results](../query.md)" — the bare-index link continues to resolve to the file that contains the section after the move (since `query.md` is the index for the `query/` subdirectory). No change required, but the link can be tightened to the new path if the implementer prefers a section-anchored target.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The normative content is intact and unambiguous; an implementer who reads `query-escapes-stringification.md` to the end finds the discard rule and the observability contract regardless. The cost is reader-orientation: an implementer of V13g who opens the file named after escapes and stringification, expecting to find no discard rule there, is wasted; the inverse — an implementer opening `query-forms.md` to find every form of the construct — is misled into thinking the discard form does not exist. The diagnostics registry's choice to link to `../query.md` (the index) rather than this file is a workaround already paying for the misplacement.
