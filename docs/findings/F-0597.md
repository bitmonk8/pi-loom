## Problem

Three `glossary.md` entries restate a closed enumeration or live range owned by another page, with the restatement narrower or staler than the owner. The `always-log set` entry lists three discard dispositions (`Err` match, `?` propagation, `let _ =`) and omits the void-tail-expression discard form that the canonical `runtime-event-channel.md` "Discard-site disposition" paragraph and the neighbouring `query-terminating` glossary entry both recognise. The `GOV-N (governance rule)` entry embeds the literal "currently running through `GOV-23`" while the live GOV set on `governance.md` now runs through `GOV-31`, self-contradicting its own "cite `governance.md` rather than a fixed upper bound" directive; the `.warp` file entry enumerates three permitted top-level forms (`schema`, `enum`, `fn`) where `imports.md` enumerates five (it also permits `import` and `export`). None reaches a runtime, but each costs a reader-friction round of "which page wins", and the `always-log set` case can mislead an implementer working from the glossary alone into a silent operator-observability gap on void-tail-discarded `Err` values.

**History:** `52219c4` introduced both the `always-log set` glossary entry (with the three-form discard enumeration) and the runtime-event-channel "Discard-site disposition" paragraph (which already named the `void`-tail-expression form) in a single change — the glossary's narrower enumeration was born at the same commit as the broader rule; subsequent edits (`8455d5e`, `038dab3`) left the discard-form list untouched, and the neighbouring `query-terminating` entry (`8168ea8`, 2026-05-10) modelled the discard clause correctly, making the asymmetry observable from then on. `96cfc08` rewrote the `GOV-N` entry and introduced the "currently running through `GOV-23`" literal alongside the "cite `governance.md` … rather than a fixed upper bound" sentence, internally inconsistent from the moment it landed; `1d773d6` (`GOV-24`) made the literal numerically stale, and `fe8aa5c` (`GOV-30`/`GOV-31`) widened the gap to eight rules. `d54b426` added the `` `.warp` file `` entry already in its current 3-form shape, two days after `imports.md` had been raised to 5 forms at `b0b9a14` (2026-05-05). In every case the glossary embedded a restatement while the owner page moved on, and no later commit re-synced the glossary.

## Solution approach

Re-sync each entry to its canonical owner by deferring or mirroring rather than restating the value, so the next owner-page edit does not re-stale the glossary. In the `always-log set` entry, clarify the discard enumeration to include the void-tail-expression discard form, mirroring the `query-terminating` entry's phrasing. In the `GOV-N (governance rule)` entry, delete the `GOV-23` upper-bound literal, leaving the existing "cite `governance.md`" directive and the retired-IDs link standing. In the `.warp` file entry, rewrite the opening clause to defer to `imports.md#permitted-top-level-forms` rather than re-list the top-level forms.
