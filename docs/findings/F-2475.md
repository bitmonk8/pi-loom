## Problem

The `## Acceptance criteria` section of `diagnostic-emission-isolation.md` enumerates fixtures only for the construction-site catch arm (PIC-26, building on PIC-25), and its own framing scopes it to "the construction-site catch-arm obligation". Two normatively-pinned main-path behaviours carry no acceptance fixtures: the PIC-25 serialiser-throw fallbacks — the bare-code `console.error(code)` form for flat-`details` codes and the two-token `` console.error(`${code} ${detailsEventReason}`) `` form for the two nested-shape codes, including the `"<unreadable>"` sentinel on the sub-step-2 stamp-throw clean-cancel path — and the PIC-27 handler-isolation swallow on the main path. Because the construction-site path and the wrapped serialise-and-emit path are mutually exclusive, the existing construction-site fixtures do not transitively cover these. A regression in those branches (a re-throw on serialiser failure, or a lost sentinel collapsing per-reason dedup buckets) would pass acceptance.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** 9559e19 (2026-05-07, `"<unreadable>"` sentinel), 75a9626 (2026-05-09, bare diagnostic-code serialiser-throw fallback), 52b9101 (2026-05-11, two-token form + `MUST be swallowed`), 8008b91 (2026-06-25, F-1217, added the `## Acceptance criteria` section)
**History:** The normative emission forms accreted across several finding-resolution commits, each adding an obligation without a paired fixture: the `"<unreadable>"` sentinel (9559e19), the bare-code serialiser-throw fallback (75a9626), and the two-token form plus the handler-isolation swallow (52b9101). The forms then moved verbatim into this file when the oversized `pi-integration-contract.md` was split (f5e89f4). The `## Acceptance criteria` section was introduced later by F-1217 (8008b91), but scoped deliberately to the construction-site catch-arm (PIC-26) only — entrenching the gap rather than closing it for the main-path PIC-25 fallbacks and the PIC-27 swallow. The defect is the interaction between the obligation-adding commits and the partial acceptance block, not any single revert.

## Solution approach

Extend the `## Acceptance criteria` section of `diagnostic-emission-isolation.md` with main-path fixtures covering the PIC-25 serialiser-throw bare-code fallback for flat-`details` codes, the PIC-25 two-token fallback for the two nested-shape codes (`loom/host/session-shutdown-runtime-degraded` and `loom/runtime/cancelled-by-session-shutdown`) including the `"<unreadable>"` sentinel variant on the sub-step-2 stamp-throw clean-cancel path, and the PIC-27 handler-isolation swallow on the main path. Key the fixtures on the codes already in scope and mirror the construction-site block's byte-exact / branch-coverage discipline.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the terminal `console.error` swallow of the `sendSystemNote` fallback chain — it is owned by PIC-54 at its own definition site (`runtime-event-channel.md`); reference it rather than restating its swallow here.
- The new fixtures MUST NOT introduce a new diagnostic code or wire field (no-invented-ids).
