## Finding

Three items in step 2's editorial-review checklist drive a `dist/*.js` body read or diff against the baseline tree the [Baseline acquisition](../../docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md#bump-baseline-acquisition) paragraph materialises: item (e) (the `AgentSession.prototype.prompt` body in `dist/core/agent-session.js`), item (r) (the `convertToLlm` transformer in `dist/core/messages.js`), and item (aj) (the `SessionManager.inMemory` in-memory-session branch's JS body in `dist/core/session-manager.js`). Item (e) anticipates that a Pi minor may have minified, re-bundled, renamed, or restructured the `dist/` tree so the cited body is no longer textually locatable, and routes that case explicitly: the **Textual-locatability with no equivalent site** fail condition triggers when the loom 1.0-cited evidence trail cannot be identified, and the **conservative-posture rule** forbids recording `N/A` in any case where non-overlap cannot be positively demonstrated.

Items (r) and (aj) carry no equivalent disposition. Both items state only "diff the cited body against the baseline" / "read the cited branch's JS body" with no defined outcome when that body cannot be located, diffed, or read against the candidate minor — the same drift scenarios item (e) anticipates apply to them by construction (they read against the same baseline tree via the same Baseline acquisition block, and the same minification / re-bundling / rename / restructuring shapes apply to `dist/core/messages.js` and `dist/core/session-manager.js`). A contributor who cannot locate the cited body has no spec-defined outcome to record: they may silently choose `pass`, `fail`, or `N/A`, with `pass` being the dangerous default since "I could not find what would falsify this" is easily mistaken for "I could not falsify this." The asymmetry is unprincipled: the same defensive posture that item (e) reasons explicitly about applies to every body-read item on the page.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `5d73cc3` (2026-06-07), `ccc6e38` (2026-06-07), `9ced9f1` (2026-06-08)
**History:** `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md` is git-tracked. `git log -S 'Textual-locatability with no equivalent site' --all` localises the introduction of item (e)'s drift-handling fail-trigger and conservative-posture rule to `5d73cc3` ("Item (e) fail predicate operator-precedence ambiguity and buried sub-outcomes"); at that commit, item (e) was the only `dist/*.js` body-diff in the checklist, so the fallback's scope-to-(e) authoring was coherent. `git log -S 'bump-baseline-acquisition' --all` and `git log -S 'bump-checklist-convert-to-llm-context-entry' --all` show `ccc6e38` ("Item (e)/(r) dist/*.js baseline source unspecified") landing later the same day, factoring the **Baseline acquisition** block out of item (e) and binding item (r) to it; that commit extended the shared infrastructure to (r) but did not propagate (e)'s drift-handling fail-trigger to (r). `git log -S 'bump-checklist-inmemory-transcript-discard' --all` shows `9ced9f1` ("subagent transcript-discard not routed to bump checklist") added item (aj) the following day, again binding it to the Baseline acquisition block without inheriting (e)'s drift-handling pattern. The defect is the cumulative interaction: each commit was scoped to the obligation it was adding, and the conservative-posture/textual-locatability rule was left attached to item (e) rather than promoted to a step-level rule the new body-read items would inherit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Promote drift handling from an item-(e)-local rule to a step-level paragraph that governs every `dist/*.js` body read or diff on the page, so items (e), (r), and (aj) all inherit it uniformly. The cleanest placement is a new short paragraph adjacent to the **Baseline acquisition** block (whose scope already enumerates items (e), (r), (aj)) titled something like *Body-read drift handling*, stating:

- A `fail` outcome is recorded on any `dist/*.js` body-read / body-diff item when the cited body cannot be located, diffed, or read against the candidate minor — i.e. the loom 1.0-cited path, symbol, or surrounding evidence (function name, class name, method name, file path) is no longer textually locatable because Pi has minified, re-bundled, renamed, or restructured the `dist/` tree.
- The conservative-posture rule applies: when the body cannot be positively verified against the candidate minor, the outcome MUST be `fail`, never `N/A` and never `pass`.
- Item (e)'s **textual-locatability with no equivalent site** condition remains the worked example; its "equivalent reachable site" carve-out stays scoped to item (e) (since (r) and (aj) have no analogous reachability-based equivalence) and items (r)/(aj) carry no such carve-out — drift on either is an unconditional `fail`.

Edges the implementer must watch:

- Item (e)'s existing fail-trigger language and conservative-posture rule should be re-anchored to reference the shared paragraph rather than restating it, so the two do not drift out of sync on the next edit.
- The merge-gate disposition for a recorded `fail` on (r)/(aj) is the existing SHOULD-level rule (conformant, non-blocking); the new paragraph must not silently re-classify (r)/(aj) `fail` outcomes as MUST-level blockers.
- The promotion must not change item (e)'s status as a MUST-level blocker; the shared rule is about *how* a body-read drift outcome is recorded, not about *which* gate level the item carries.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A contributor whose candidate minor has re-bundled or minified `dist/core/messages.js` or `dist/core/session-manager.js` has no spec-defined outcome to record for items (r)/(aj). The most likely failure mode is a silent `pass` ("I did not see the change I was watching for") on what is really an un-runnable audit, masking a real regression of the `convertToLlm` LLM-context-inclusion invariant or the in-memory transcript-discard privacy guarantee — both of which the cited paragraphs explicitly call out as having no SDK surface-inventory signal.
