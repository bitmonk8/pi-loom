## Finding

The `**Baseline acquisition (the canonical "from" side for every `dist/*.js` diff on this page).**` paragraph in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md` is a step-level prerequisite that applies to three checklist items — (e), (r), and (aj) — and carries the only MUST / MUST NOT obligations on the page (the contributor *MUST* materialise the pinned `dist/*.js` tree, the scratch tree *MUST NOT* be committed, and the acquisition *MUST* either precede step 1 or pass the explicit `@<pinned-version>` specifier). It is currently rendered as a second-level bullet nested inside item (e)'s body, so structurally it reads as if it were a sub-rule of the per-session-slash-dispatch audit rather than a precondition of every `dist/*.js` body read on the page.

Items (r) and (aj) reach the block only by following the in-line `[Baseline acquisition](#bump-baseline-acquisition)` anchor in their own body text. An auditor working item (r) or item (aj) in isolation — the normal mode, since each item is a separate audit with its own pass/fail row — sees a one-clause hyperlink in the middle of a long paragraph and has no structural signal that the link conceals MUST/MUST NOT obligations they are bound by. The block's own opening phrase ("the canonical 'from' side for every `dist/*.js` diff on this page") already advertises page-wide scope, but its placement contradicts that scope.

The introduction of item (aj) (which added a third consumer of the block) compounded the problem without addressing it: the block stayed nested under (e) and item (aj)'s reachable-via-anchor framing was simply copied from item (r).

**Verdict:** present-at-introduction-of-block, compounded by later expansion

**History:** The block was authored with the page-wide scoping language already in its opening sentence, but rendered as an item-(e) sub-bullet — so the placement / scope mismatch is original to `ccc6e38` rather than emerging through later edits. `9ced9f1` widened the scope to a third item without restructuring, which made the placement-vs-scope contradiction more acute but did not introduce it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Promote the `Baseline acquisition` paragraph to a top-level sub-section of step 2 placed in the step-2 preamble — between the existing `*Editorial-review checklist for unpinned host presuppositions.*` opening paragraph and the `(a)`–`(ak)` items list — under a labelled heading such as `**Baseline acquisition for `dist/*.js` body reads (applies to items (e), (r), (aj)).**`. Keep the existing `<a id="bump-baseline-acquisition"></a>` anchor on the new heading verbatim so the in-line `[Baseline acquisition](#bump-baseline-acquisition)` links in items (e), (r), (aj) still resolve. Delete the nested copy under item (e); item (e)'s body then opens directly with the `Diff the cited AgentSession.prototype.prompt body …` paragraph that currently follows the block. The page-wide-scope opening sentence ("the canonical 'from' side for every `dist/*.js` diff on this page") stays — it now matches the structural placement.

Implementer-relevant edge cases:

- The step-2 preamble already contains a separate `Outcome recording conventions` block that the prior `Item (e) recovery over-prescribes mutex; N/A and recording conventions buried` resolution moved out of item (e); place the new `Baseline acquisition` sub-section **before** the items list and the existing `Outcome recording conventions` block (which sits after the items list per its current discoverability rationale), so both extracted blocks bracket the items list at their natural reading positions.
- Do not rewrite the obligation text; the MUST / MUST NOT clauses and the registry-retrievability framing carry through verbatim. The change is purely structural.
- Item (e)'s body must keep its existing `[Baseline acquisition](#bump-baseline-acquisition)` reference (in the `Diff the cited …` paragraph) so an auditor working item (e) still has a forward link to the prerequisite they just walked past in the preamble.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The MUST/MUST NOT obligations are still reachable via the anchor, so a conscientious auditor can find them; an auditor who works items (r) or (aj) in isolation, however, is one missed hyperlink away from committing a scratch tree or running a diff against the wrong "from" side. The defect degrades reviewability and the discoverability of bump-time obligations rather than rendering any audit unrunnable.
