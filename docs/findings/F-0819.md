## Problem

The `Baseline acquisition` block (anchor `#bump-baseline-acquisition`) makes `npm pack @earendil-works/pi-coding-agent@<pinned-version>` the sole channel for materialising the canonical "from" side that items (e), (r), (aj), and (am) diff and read against, making registry retrievability of the pinned version an implicit, unstated precondition for those four items. The procedure never states what outcome to record when acquisition fails (yanked or unpublished pin, offline contributor, mirror without the pinned version, corrupt download, registry auth failure), so two contributors hitting the same failure can defensibly record `fail`, `not-performed`, `N/A`, or a silent skip — each mapping to a different merge disposition under the Ships-when gate. Compounding this, only item (e) carries the `conservative-posture rule`; siblings (r), (aj), and (am) read against the same baseline tree under the same authoring template but inherit no catch-all disposition. The block is also nested inside item (e) as a sub-bullet even though its opening sentence scopes it page-wide, so an implementer reading (r)/(aj)/(am) in isolation has no signal that they depend on the same baseline acquisition or inherit its failure shape.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-at-introduction-of-block, compounded by later expansion

**Introducing commits:**
- `ccc6e38` — *pi-loom spec: resolve "Item (e)/(r) dist/*.js baseline source unspecified"* (2026-06-07). Added the `Baseline acquisition` block and immediately nested it as a sub-bullet inside item (e), with item (r) reaching it via the `#bump-baseline-acquisition` anchor.
- `9ced9f1` — *pi-loom spec: resolve "subagent transcript-discard not routed to bump checklist"* (2026-06-08). Added item (aj) as a third consumer of the block; extended the block's opening sentence to enumerate items (e), (r), and (aj), but left the structural placement under (e) unchanged.

**History:** The block was authored with the page-wide scoping language already in its opening sentence, but rendered as an item-(e) sub-bullet — so the placement / scope mismatch is original to `ccc6e38` rather than emerging through later edits. `9ced9f1` widened the scope to a third item without restructuring, which made the placement-vs-scope contradiction more acute but did not introduce it.

## Solution approach

Promote the `Baseline acquisition` block out of item (e) to a step-level position preceding item (a), so items (e), (r), (aj), and (am) all reference it as a shared prerequisite, and retain its `#bump-baseline-acquisition` anchor and `MUST NOT be committed` rule. Add a registry-retrievability precondition stating that the pinned version must be resolvable on the contributor's configured registry at bump time. Add a failure-disposition arm directing that when the pinned tarball cannot be retrieved or extraction yields a corrupt/truncated tree, the auditor record `fail` on every dependent item — (e), (r), (aj), and (am) — never `N/A`, `not-performed`, or an omitted row, aligning with item (e)'s `conservative-posture rule` and the fail-closed posture at `audit-wire-and-canary.md#audit-infrastructure-failure-handling`.

## Solution constraints

- Preserve the `#bump-baseline-acquisition` anchor across the promotion; items (e)/(r)/(aj)/(am) link to it by anchor, and the move must not break those inbound links.
