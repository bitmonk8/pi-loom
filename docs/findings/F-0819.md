## Problem

The `Baseline acquisition` block (anchor `bump-baseline-acquisition`) in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md` is a step-level prerequisite that applies to checklist items (e), (r), and (aj) and carries the only MUST / MUST NOT obligations on the page (materialise the pinned `dist/*.js` tree, do not commit the scratch tree, order the acquisition before step 1 or pass the explicit `@<pinned-version>` specifier). It is rendered as a second-level bullet nested inside item (e)'s body, so it structurally reads as a sub-rule of the per-session slash-dispatch audit rather than a precondition of every `dist/*.js` body read on the page. Items (r) and (aj) reach the block only by following the in-line `[Baseline acquisition](#bump-baseline-acquisition)` link in their own text; an auditor working (r) or (aj) in isolation — the normal per-item audit mode — has no structural signal that the link conceals obligations they are bound by. The block's opening phrase already advertises page-wide scope, contradicting its placement.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-at-introduction-of-block, compounded by later expansion

**Introducing commits:**
- `ccc6e38` — *pi-loom spec: resolve "Item (e)/(r) dist/*.js baseline source unspecified"* (2026-06-07). Added the `Baseline acquisition` block and immediately nested it as a sub-bullet inside item (e), with item (r) reaching it via the `#bump-baseline-acquisition` anchor.
- `9ced9f1` — *pi-loom spec: resolve "subagent transcript-discard not routed to bump checklist"* (2026-06-08). Added item (aj) as a third consumer of the block; extended the block's opening sentence to enumerate items (e), (r), and (aj), but left the structural placement under (e) unchanged.

**History:** The block was authored with the page-wide scoping language already in its opening sentence, but rendered as an item-(e) sub-bullet — so the placement / scope mismatch is original to `ccc6e38` rather than emerging through later edits. `9ced9f1` widened the scope to a third item without restructuring, which made the placement-vs-scope contradiction more acute but did not introduce it.

## Solution approach

Promote the `Baseline acquisition` block out of its nesting under item (e) to a top-level position in the step-2 preamble, ahead of the items list, so items (e), (r), and (aj) all read it as a shared precondition. Keep the `<a id="bump-baseline-acquisition"></a>` anchor verbatim on the promoted block so the existing `[Baseline acquisition](#bump-baseline-acquisition)` links in items (e), (r), and (aj) still resolve, and retain item (e)'s own in-line forward link to the prerequisite. This is a structural relocation only — the MUST / MUST NOT obligation text carries through unchanged.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the `Outcome recording conventions` block (anchor `bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions`) — its placement is a separate cluster finding; do not relocate it as part of this edit.
- Out of scope: the `comparator baseline` / `Baseline acquisition` naming collision inside item (e) prose — a separate cluster finding owns it; do not rename during the promotion.
