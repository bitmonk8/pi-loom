## Problem

Lookup-failure-to-discriminator routing arm (1) in `unknown-reason-rule.md` (under PIC-47, anchor `#unknown-reason-rule-handler-trycatch`) pins the exact guard expression `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY === undefined` as a MUST before invoking the iteration primitive, alongside the already-stated observable requirement that an undefined `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` route to discriminator `"missing-entry"` (never a `"throw:…"` value). Read as a MUST, the explicit-check mandate needlessly forbids equivalent conforming undefined-guard expressions and grows the normative surface that maintainers and conformance fixtures must track, with no observable divergence between conforming implementations.

## Solution approach

Demote the explicit `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY === undefined`-check mandate in routing arm (1) to a non-normative note, retaining the existing parenthetical warning that a naive `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY.find(…)?.literals` pattern misroutes the case to the throw arm. Keep the observable obligation normative: an undefined `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` MUST yield discriminator `"missing-entry"`, never a `"throw:…"` value.
