## Problem

The `*§7 closed-enum `<reason>` row co-update (convention).*` paragraph at the tail of step 5 in `version-bump-triggers.md` (anchor `#bump-step-5-section7-reason-row`) assigns two incompatible modal weights to the same edit in adjacent sentences. The first sentence states the contributor `SHOULD` update the §7 `<reason>` table in the same commit as the snapshot edit (RFC 2119 `SHOULD`: deviation is reviewable and requires justification); the next sentence relegates it to a "contributor-discipline convention, not a mechanically-enforced co-edit" (weaker than `SHOULD` — nothing for a reviewer to flag against). The reconciliation tail naming the "next page-touching editorial sweep" is consistent only with the weaker convention reading. Two contributors — and two reviewers — will land divergent behaviour on whether a missing §7 update blocks a bump commit.

The commit message records that it folded two predecessor review findings — T54 (the missing step-5 co-edit obligation) and T55 (the missing mechanical gate, with an explicit "or demote to a SHOULD-level convention" alternative) — into a single resolving edit. The resolution attempted to honour both halves: the `SHOULD update` clause satisfies T54's "state the obligation at step 5" ask, and the *"convention, not a mechanically-enforced co-edit"* clause satisfies T55's "demote to convention" alternative. Because the two halves were resolved by *additive* prose rather than by picking one modal weight, the resulting paragraph carries both weights in adjacent sentences.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the `#bump-step-5-section7-reason-row` paragraph so the §7 `<reason>` row co-update carries a single coherent modal weight, removing the contradictory "convention, not a mechanically-enforced co-edit" framing. Reconcile the "next page-touching editorial sweep" tail and the in-prose-illustrative-restatement comparison to that chosen weight rather than to the convention reading. The MUST-weight inline-triplet sweep one paragraph above operates on the same `SessionShutdownEvent['reason']` snapshot surface and is available as the calibration point for where the §7 row's weight should sit.
