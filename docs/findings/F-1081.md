## Finding

`V18c`'s `Adds.` field describes the contributor editorial-review checklist as "MUST-level for `(a)`–`(e)` and SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record for `(f)`–`(al)`." The hyphenated compound "SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record," read as a single severity token, does not make plain which obligation is SHOULD and which is MUST. Two readings survive: (A) performing the audit of `(f)`–`(al)` is SHOULD-level, but recording an outcome (e.g. `not-performed`) for each such item is MUST regardless of whether the audit ran; (B) both auditing and recording are merely SHOULD, the compound being emphasis only.

The leaf's own `Ships when.` field resolves the ambiguity in favour of reading (A): it requires "the bump commit message records a per-item outcome for every item `(a)`–`(al)`" and treats "a recorded `fail` / `not-performed` on a SHOULD-level item `(f)`–`(al)`" as a conformant non-blocking outcome. The authoritative spec text agrees — `version-bump-step2.md` makes the audit SHOULD-level for `(f)`–`(al)` while the Outcome-recording conventions require a per-item `pass`/`fail`/`N/A`/`not-performed` row for every item. The defect is confined to the `Adds.` prose: a reader who stops at `Adds.` cannot tell whether silently omitting the recorded outcome for a `(f)`–`(al)` item is conformant, even though the rest of the leaf and the spec say it is not.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit introduction
**Introducing commits:** `f1cda48`
**History:** The compound phrase was first authored in `f1cda48` (2026-06-11, "pi-loom plan: resolve \"V18c mislabels editorial-review checklist as a static build-time gate\"") in `docs/plan_topics/V18c-version-bump-checklist.md`. That commit carved the editorial-review checklist out of the build-time-gate sentence — resolving an unrelated placement finding — and in doing so compressed the MUST/SHOULD distinction into the hyphenated compound "SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record for `(f)`–`(ak)`" now flagged. `git log -S 'SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record'` reports `f1cda48` as the earliest commit containing the text. A later commit `1ca13ec` (2026-06-19) extended the upper bound from `(ak)` to `(al)` but left the compound phrase intact; the ambiguity predates that range change.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V18c-version-bump-checklist.md`, `Adds.` field, replace the clause "SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record for `(f)`–`(al)`" with prose that separates the two obligations: performing the audit of `(f)`–`(al)` is SHOULD-level (recommended, not required), while recording an outcome for each such item — including `not-performed` when the audit is skipped — is MUST regardless of whether the audit ran. For example: "MUST-level for `(a)`–`(e)`; for `(f)`–`(al)` performing the audit is SHOULD-level (recommended but not required), but recording an outcome for each — including `not-performed` when the audit is skipped — is MUST." This makes `Adds.` self-consistent with the leaf's existing `Ships when.` text and with `version-bump-step2.md`'s Outcome-recording conventions; no `Ships when.`, `Deps.`, or spec edit is required.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer reading only the `Adds.` field could conclude that recording an outcome for a `(f)`–`(al)` item is optional and silently omit it. The same leaf's `Ships when.` editorial-review gate would catch the omission (it requires a per-item outcome for every `(a)`–`(al)` item), so a leaf authored against the whole leaf still ships correctly; the gap is in `Adds.` guidance read in isolation, not in the executable acceptance criteria.
