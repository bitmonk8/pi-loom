## Problem

`H4a` produces three deliverables of materially different kinds in a single leaf: the production-code extension factory entry point (under `src/**`) plus the out-of-tree `extensions/index.ts` delegating shim and its never-fault / synchronous-arm boundary; the reusable in-process session-double test harness with its in-memory fixture-supply and minimal response-emission capability; and the session-double fidelity contract with the four-axis harness self-check. The first is production code; the latter two are test infrastructure consumed by `H4b`/`H4c`/`H7a`/`M` and many `V` leaves. This is a review-granularity defect, not a behavioural one — partial completion of any one deliverable is not separately observable, since the leaf's three `Tests` bullets are addressed as a group.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (subsequently accreted)
**Introducing commit:** `c6a664e` (2026-06-10) — initial plan build; `H4a` was created bundling the factory entry point and the end-to-end harness in one leaf.
**History:** The plan corpus is git-tracked. `git log --follow` on `docs/plan_topics/H4a-factory-shell-and-harness.md` shows the leaf bundled "the Pi extension factory entry point … and a reusable end-to-end test harness" from its first commit (`c6a664e`). The bundle then accreted further co-located deliverables rather than splitting: the fidelity contract / self-check landed the same day (`eeb0014`/`07403da`, 2026-06-10), the in-memory fixture-supply mechanism on 2026-06-11 (`2d52ad1`), the `extensions/index.ts` shim on 2026-06-16 (`e7c1b1e`), and the minimal-emission capability on 2026-06-20 (`32ea081`, F-0477). A prior atomicity fix (`fd6169b`, 2026-06-12, "resolve 'H4a bundles three independent units into one leaf'") peeled the scripted-injection response-programming surface out into `H4b` but deliberately left the factory + harness + fidelity-contract three-way bundle in place. The defect is therefore original to the leaf's structure and was narrowed, not eliminated, by the June-12 split.

## Solution approach

Resolve the bundling in `docs/plan_topics/H4a-factory-shell-and-harness.md` by recording an explicit cohesion rationale on the leaf for keeping the production factory, the harness, and the fidelity contract co-located, following the plan's standing accepted-bundle precedent (`V2b`, `V5f`, `V3a`); the leaf's three separate `Tests` bullets already isolate the three review concerns. Alternatively, split the test-infrastructure deliverables (harness, fixture-supply, minimal-emission capability, fidelity contract, and self-check) into a new leaf that `Deps` on the factory leaf, mirroring the `H4b`/`H4c` decomposition, and retarget consumer `Deps` from `H4a` onto the new harness leaf — recognising this is a broad sweep whose benefit is review-time only.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the response-programming surface owned by `H4b`/`H4c`, the `V9a` capability-probe refusal logic, and the manual-smoke governance owned by `real-host-smoke-gate.md` — a rationale note must not re-introduce content already moved out to those leaves.
- If the split path is taken, allocate the new leaf ID per the `H<n><letter>` scheme in `## Plan rules` (next free letter, never reuse a retired ID) and keep `conventions.md` and `coverage-matrix.md` consistent; do not pin a specific ID here.
