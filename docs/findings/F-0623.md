## Finding

The second paragraph of `## Binder model` in `binder/binder-model-and-context.md` opens with a one-clause statement that `looms.binderModel` hot-reload re-resolves on next load, then — without any sub-heading, callout, or topic break — proceeds to specify a full operational-notification feature: the verbatim `loom watcher: looms.binderModel changed; …; run /reload to retry: <names>` template, the `details: { recovery: { looms: string[] } }` payload, a claim that this is "the fourth shape on the disjoint-by-key `loom-system-note` channel," `recovery.looms` content rules (final derived slash names, ascending Unicode code-point order, narrowed to the set that re-resolves under the changed setting), `<N>` and `<names>` substitution rules, the verbatim-literal `loom(s)` / `looms.binderModel` / `/reload` invariants, the `recovery.looms.length === 0` suppression rule, and the 250 ms settings-file debounce coalescing rule.

That body is a recovery-notification feature in its own right, not a clarifying detail about binder-model resolution. The `## Binder model` subsection is structured around load-time resolution and the strict-capability probe; the surrounding prose gives the reader no signpost that an operator-facing notification surface is about to be defined, and the recovery-note material is too large to fit comfortably as a parenthetical on the resolution path. A reader scanning for "how do I know when settings changes unblock previously-failed looms?" cannot find this content by heading or by cross-reference target name — the only handle is the inline `<a id="binder-model-hot-reload">` anchor.

The shard inherits this from `binder.md` and `spec.md`, which carry no `## Goals` / `## Scope` / `## Non-goals` inventories the omitted feature could have been listed against; the literal "goals/scope inventory" framing in the finding's wording does not match the corpus's organising convention. The real defect is structural rather than missing-from-an-inventory: a coherent operational feature is wedged into a paragraph whose declared subject is something else, and gives a downstream `runtime-event-channel.md` shape table a back-reference target buried mid-paragraph rather than a discoverable section.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `0fa81fa` (2026-05-05, "spec: pin binder model resolution; document envelope two-arm rationale (Decisions #5, #6)") seeded the hot-reload sentence as a one-line tail to the binder-model paragraph; `319228c` (2026-05-05, "pi-loom plan: resolve \"V16e bad looms.binderModel silent loom drop and recovery\"") appended the unstructured "single consolidated `loom-system-note` listing the affected slash names and prompting the user to run `/reload`" clause; `f88698e` (2026-06-05, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Hot-reload looms.binderModel recovery note under-specified loom-system-note\"") expanded that clause into the present operational-feature paragraph (verbatim template, `recovery.looms` payload, "fourth shape" claim, substitution rules, debounce coalescing) without re-shaping the enclosing `## Binder model` subsection to declare or contain it.
**History:** the original sentence at `0fa81fa` was small enough to live as a parenthetical inside `## Binder model`; the placement was fine for what it said. The placement defect emerged not at any single commit but from the interaction of (a) the original embedding decision in `0fa81fa`, (b) the `319228c` append that grew the surface from "re-resolves on next load" to "emits a note," and (c) the `f88698e` rewrite that pinned a full operational-notification feature in the same paragraph. Each commit individually resolved a real, narrowly-scoped review finding; collectively they overflowed the implicit scope of the paragraph they kept landing in. A later edit (`97430aa`, 2026-06-06) softened the over-promise about `/reload` but did not revisit the placement.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Promote the recovery-note body to its own `### Hot-reload recovery note` sub-section

**Approach.** Split the current `## Binder model` second paragraph at the existing `<a id="binder-model-hot-reload">` anchor. Keep the first clause ("Hot-reload of Pi settings (`looms.binderModel` changed at runtime) re-resolves on the next loom load; it does not retroactively fix already-failed loads.") in `## Binder model`. Move every byte from "When the change would let a previously…" through "…no `loom/load/*` code is registered for it." into a new `### Hot-reload recovery note` heading immediately after the truncation-rule sub-section, retaining the inline `<a id="binder-model-hot-reload">` anchor verbatim on the new heading.

**Spec edits.** One file: `binder/binder-model-and-context.md`. No edits to `runtime-event-channel.md` (its `details: { recovery }` row already back-links via the existing anchor, which the move preserves). No edits to `V11a` or `coverage-matrix.md` (the fragment they cite is unchanged).

**Pros.** Smallest blast radius. Preserves locality — recovery-note resolution is conceptually a tail of binder-model resolution. Makes the feature discoverable in the file's heading structure and the `binder.md` Contents-list rendering. Zero plan / coverage churn.

**Cons.** The cross-shard concern raised by the sibling finding *binder-model-and-context.md — hot-reload "fourth shape" of loom-system-note (cross-shard)* — that the shape count is asserted from the wrong shard — is not addressed by this option; it remains a separate fix.

**Risks.** A future contributor who reads `runtime-event-channel.md`'s "fourth shape" row first still has to chase a back-link into the binder shard for the template bytes. This is the status quo, just better-organised.

### Option B — Move the normative template to `runtime-event-channel.md` and leave a back-reference here

**Approach.** Move the verbatim template, the substitution rules, the `recovery.looms` content rules, and the `recovery.looms.length === 0` suppression rule into `runtime-event-channel.md`'s `details: { recovery }` shape entry (which currently back-links here for the template). Leave only a forward-link from `binder/binder-model-and-context.md` saying that a hot-reload that re-resolves a previously-unresolved loom emits the channel's recovery-note shape (with link to `runtime-event-channel.md#system-note-details-shapes`). Keep the trigger condition, the 250 ms-window coalescing rule, and the link-out in the binder shard, since they are about binder-model resolution timing, not channel shape.

**Spec edits.** `binder/binder-model-and-context.md` (shrink), `runtime-event-channel.md` (absorb the template + payload spec + substitution rules). `V11a-T` and `coverage-matrix.md` row `cka-38` re-target the new anchor in `runtime-event-channel.md`. `V11a`'s Spec link list gains a reference to `runtime-event-channel.md`.

**Pros.** Co-locates the recovery-note shape with the other three `loom-system-note` shapes, making the "fourth shape" claim verifiable in-place. Addresses both this finding and the sibling cross-shard finding in one edit.

**Cons.** Larger surface than Option A. Splits a single feature across two shards (trigger here, payload there), which adds a follow-link cost to readers approaching from the binder side. Touches the V11a leaf's Spec list and the coverage matrix.

**Risks.** `runtime-event-channel.md` is already a busy shard; importing a domain-specific template body grows it further. Care needed to keep `recovery.looms` ordering / suppression rules anchored at one site only.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A** first. It resolves this finding with a single-file edit, preserves every existing cross-shard anchor, and creates the heading the `runtime-event-channel.md` shape row implicitly assumed existed. Then consider Option B's cross-shard rehoming under the sibling finding *binder-model-and-context.md — hot-reload "fourth shape" of loom-system-note (cross-shard)* — that is the right venue for the channel-side enumeration concern, and treating it separately avoids bundling two distinct decisions into one diff. Edge case the implementer must watch: keep the inline `<a id="binder-model-hot-reload">` anchor on the new heading verbatim so `V11a-T-binder-model-resolution.md`, `V11a-binder-model-resolution.md`, `coverage-matrix.md` row `cka-38`, and `runtime-event-channel.md`'s `details: { recovery }` back-link all continue to resolve.
