## Finding

The `Baseline acquisition` block (anchor `#bump-baseline-acquisition`) makes `npm pack @earendil-works/pi-coding-agent@<pinned-version>` the sole channel for producing the canonical "from" side that items (e), (r), and (aj) diff/read against, and it explicitly forbids retaining the extracted tree as an in-repo snapshot (*"The scratch directory and the extracted tarball are ephemeral, are not retained as an in-repo snapshot, and MUST NOT be committed."*). Together this makes registry retrievability of the pinned version an implicit precondition for three audit items, but the precondition is never stated and the procedure never says what outcome to record when acquisition fails.

The failure modes are not exotic — they are the routine failure modes of `npm pack`: the pinned version has been unpublished or yanked, the contributor is offline or behind a registry mirror that does not carry that version, the tarball downloads corrupt, or registry auth fails. On any of these, items (e)/(r)/(aj) become unrunnable. Item (e)'s elaborate fail-trigger machinery (the *conservative-posture rule*, the textual-locatability arm, the pass-evidence rule) all key off the auditor being unable to *locate* evidence inside the baseline tree — they presuppose a baseline tree exists. Item (r)'s diff and item (aj)'s body read have the same hidden assumption. Two reasonable contributors hitting an unretrievable baseline will diverge: one records `not-performed` (legal only on (f)–(aj) SHOULD-level items and even there only with a one-line rationale that does not name baseline unavailability as an outcome), one records `fail` and blocks the bump on item (e)'s MUST gate, one records `N/A` (which on item (e) has a specific superseded-by-Pi-mechanism definition that does not cover this), and one silently skips and merges.

The gap also collides with the `npm test` audit's fail-closed posture next door in [`audit-wire-and-canary.md` §"Infrastructure-failure handling"](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-wire-and-canary.md#audit-infrastructure-failure-handling): the build-time surface-inventory audit treats infrastructure-level read failures as fail-closed reds with a stable `audit/infra/<family>/<symptom>` discriminator, while the editorial baseline-acquisition step in the same procedure has no equivalent disposition.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Extend the `Baseline acquisition` block (anchor `#bump-baseline-acquisition`) with two adjacent sentences, kept inside the same block so items (e)/(r)/(aj) inherit them by reference rather than each repeating the disposition:

1. **State the precondition.** *"Acquisition presupposes the pinned `@earendil-works/pi-coding-agent@<pinned-version>` tarball is registry-retrievable from the contributor's environment — i.e. the registry (or the contributor's configured mirror) still serves that exact version, the host can reach it, and the downloaded tarball is intact."* This makes the previously-implicit dependency a named precondition that the disposition clause can bind against.

2. **Define the failure disposition.** When the precondition does not hold (the pinned version is unpublished or yanked from every reachable registry/mirror, the host is offline, the tarball download or extraction fails, or integrity verification fails after a reasonable retry), the contributor MUST record `fail` on items (e), (r), and (aj) with a one-line rationale that names the failure mode (e.g. `fail — pinned version unpublished from npm`, `fail — registry unreachable`, `fail — tarball extraction failed`), and the bump MUST NOT merge until either (a) the baseline tree has been obtained from a named alternate source — a registry mirror, a previously-published artefact already on the contributor's machine, a peer contributor's machine, or any other source where the tree is byte-identical to the registry-published tarball for `<pinned-version>` — and items (e)/(r)/(aj) re-run against it to a non-`fail` outcome, or (b) the [loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin](./host-prerequisites.md#pi-sdk-pin) is moved forward to a still-retrievable version under this same procedure (which makes the unretrievable version no longer the "from" side). The `MUST NOT be committed` rule is preserved unchanged — the alternate-source tree is still ephemeral.

Notes for the implementer:

- Place the two sentences as a new paragraph at the end of the existing block, before items (e)/(r)/(aj) read the anchor; do not duplicate the disposition into each item.
- The `fail` arm aligns with item (e)'s *conservative-posture rule* (when the auditor cannot positively demonstrate the property, record `fail` not `N/A`) and with the [`audit-wire-and-canary.md` §"Infrastructure-failure handling"](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-wire-and-canary.md#audit-infrastructure-failure-handling) fail-closed posture used by the build-time arm of step 2.
- Do not introduce a `not-performed` arm for this case: items (e)/(r) are MUST-level on (e) and SHOULD-level-but-MUST-record on (r)/(aj), and `not-performed` already has a defined meaning (the SHOULD-level audit was skipped) that does not match "the audit was attempted and infrastructure prevented it from running."
- The `N/A` arm is unaffected: item (e)'s `N/A` is reserved for the superseded-by-Pi-mechanism case at the `Outcome recording conventions` anchor and continues to mean only that.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

A bump performed offline, or a bump performed after the pinned `pi-coding-agent` version has been unpublished, currently has no defined audit outcome for items (e)/(r)/(aj). Two reasonable contributors will record different outcomes (`fail`, `not-performed`, `N/A`, or a silent skip) and reach different merge dispositions for the same external state, defeating the point of the per-item recording obligation.
