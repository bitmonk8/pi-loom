## Problem

ERR-8 and ERR-9 are stated purely as prohibitions: the loom runtime MUST NOT truncate, re-write, replace, remove, or inject Pi-committed conversation surfaces after a mid-stream cancellation, and ERR-10/ERR-11/ERR-12 inherit these obligations by cross-reference. None of these clauses names an observation point or a positive, checkable invariant — they describe an *absence* of mutation, which cannot be falsified by inspecting a single trace. Because the prohibition is not falsifiable as written, two implementers can claim ERR-8/ERR-9 compliance against incompatible behaviours and no conformance test can distinguish them.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 2180f59 — pi-loom spec: resolve "Mid-stream cancellation: no observable rule for loom-runtime conversation behaviour" (2026-05-08, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The conversation-state rule entered in commit 2180f59, which resolved a prior finding titled "Mid-stream cancellation: no observable rule for loom-runtime conversation behaviour." That resolution's own proposal sketched a positive observable assertion (a byte-identical committed-transcript snapshot asserted at the next driver send, with no interleaving Pi-mutating call), but the committed diff landed only the negative MUST-NOT prose and dropped the observable predicate. Commit 07f72e5 (2026-06-04) later split that paragraph into the ERR-8…ERR-12 REQ-ID anchors without adding any observability, so the testability gap has been present in the rule's text since its introduction.

## Solution approach

Add a positive, observable predicate to ERR-8 (ERR-9 follows by the same edit) that pins the observation window and invariant: after a mid-stream cancellation, the conversation the prompt-mode driver next reads (the outbound transcript for the next `@`-query / driver send) MUST equal the transcript Pi had committed at the instant the stream was interrupted, with no Pi-conversation-mutating call issued between the cancellation observation and that next driver send. Scope the equality to the pre-interruption committed baseline — the assistant tokens, tool-call cards, and system notes Pi had already committed. For ERR-12, state the same invariant against the spawned subagent `AgentSession`'s committed-turn set rather than the caller's conversation.

## Solution constraints

- The observable MUST NOT assert anything about whether the partial fragment appears in the conversation — partial-fragment visibility is owned by the Partial-append carve-out and the slsh-2 visibility rule (`../slash-invocation.md#slsh-2`).
