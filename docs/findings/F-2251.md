## Finding

Step 1 of the version-bump procedure (`version-bump-intro.md`, "Re-typecheck against the new package") closes with a single ~400-word sentence that enumerates, site by site, where the closed `SessionShutdownEvent['reason']` set is restated in prose. It names five locations as *"illustrative restatements of that constant pinned at write time, not independent normative copies"* — the sub-step-4 intro paragraph, the Unknown-reason rule (with its parenthesised re-listing), the `session-only-degraded-state.md` *Edge cases* bullet, the *Per-invocation operator visibility (clean-cancel path)* bullet, and the `spec.md` SM-2 listing — and then, in a follow-on sentence, identifies the diagnostics §7 `<reason>` closed-enum row as a *sixth* site whose co-update is owned by step 5. (The original finding's "six illustrative restatements" miscounts: there are five carve-out sites plus one normatively-co-updated site.)

The defect is organisational, not substantive. This inline inventory is doing real normative work — it delimits exactly which prose the snapshot carve-out leaves transiently stale (no update needed) versus which prose is a normative literal that *must* be swept (the inline `{"new","resume","fork"}` triplet at the `loom/host/session-shutdown-runtime-degraded` emission site, and the §7 row). But that load-bearing distinction is encoded as deeply nested parentheticals inside a run-on sentence, where a contributor performing a reason-union snapshot edit must parse the whole sentence to recover the illustrative-versus-normative partition. The risk is a mis-read in either direction: sweeping a site the carve-out deliberately leaves stale, or missing the triplet/§7 row that genuinely require the co-edit.

The original suggestion — collapse the per-site list to a single category statement such as *"prose enumerations of the closed reason set elsewhere are illustrative restatements"* — must **not** be taken literally: a blanket statement would wrongly sweep the normative triplet and the §7 row into the "illustrative" bucket and silently drop their co-update obligations. The correct repair preserves the precise per-site partition while moving it out of the inline run-on.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Lift the per-site restatement inventory out of the step-1 run-on into a short structured block (a labelled note or a small table) adjacent to step 1, and reduce step 1's inline text to a one-line pointer plus the carve-out rule. The structured block must preserve the exact partition, not collapse it:

- **Illustrative restatements (no snapshot-driven update; reconciled by the next page-touching editorial sweep):** the sub-step-4 intro paragraph; the Unknown-reason rule (including its parenthesised re-listing); the `session-only-degraded-state.md` *Edge cases* closed-set listing — **excluding** the inline `{"new","resume","fork"}` triplet at the `loom/host/session-shutdown-runtime-degraded` emission site; the *Per-invocation operator visibility (clean-cancel path)* widening listing; and the `spec.md` SM-2 closed-set listing.
- **Normative literal — MUST be swept on a reason-union change:** the inline `{"new","resume","fork"}` triplet at the `runtime-degraded` emission site (subject to the step-5 inbound-reference-sweep obligation).
- **Co-update site owned elsewhere:** the diagnostics §7 `<reason>` closed-enum row, whose co-update is a contributor-discipline convention owned by `version-bump-triggers.md` step 5 (no build-time gate).

Keep the carve-out's normative rule (the runtime consumes the pinned snapshot constant, not the prose; the step-5 type-equality assertion is the gate; stale illustrative prose has no runtime-correctness impact) in step 1. Do **not** replace the enumeration with a blanket "all prose enumerations are illustrative" statement — that would erase the triplet exclusion and the §7 co-update obligation.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The procedure is correct and complete; a careful reader recovers the full illustrative-versus-normative partition. But the inline run-on raises the risk a contributor doing a reason-union snapshot edit either sweeps a site the carve-out deliberately leaves stale, or misses the `runtime-degraded` triplet / §7 row that genuinely require co-editing. No runtime behaviour diverges either way; this is a guidance-density gap, not a correctness fault.
