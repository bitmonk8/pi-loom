## Finding

In `diagnostic-shape.md` ("Persistent diagnostics (default)") the sole constraint tying renderer registration to the discovery scan reads: *"The renderer MUST be registered synchronously inside the extension factory **before** the first discovery scan kicks off, so the first batch of scan diagnostics renders through the loom-specific renderer rather than as raw fallback text."* The phrase "the first discovery scan kicks off" names no concrete boundary. The factory-time discovery walk is actually step 1 of the numbered factory sequence in `registration-steps.md` (the walk of the five discovery sources at factory time), and parsing is step 2 — but the diagnostic-shape sentence never anchors "the first discovery scan" to those steps, nor does the numbered factory sequence place `pi.registerMessageRenderer` relative to step 1 at all. An implementer reading the authoritative factory ordering finds no slot for renderer registration; reading the diagnostic-shape prose finds only the informal "kicks off."

The vagueness is compounded by the fact that the rationale ("so the first batch of scan diagnostics renders … rather than as raw fallback text") cannot be satisfied during the factory at all: per `registration-steps.md` step 2, every session-state action method — including `pi.sendMessage`, the delivery sink for these diagnostics — is bound to a throwing closure on the pre-bind runtime and only becomes callable at `session_start`. The factory-time scan therefore cannot deliver any diagnostic synchronously; the "first batch" is delivered on or after `session_start`, by which point the renderer (registered anywhere inside the factory body) is already in place. The operative, sufficient timing rule already exists in PIC's **Renderer registration → Registration timing** bullet: *"MUST call `pi.registerMessageRenderer(…)` synchronously inside the factory body, before the factory returns."* The diagnostic-shape sentence restates that obligation in weaker, informal language ("kicks off") that is simultaneously imprecise and stronger-sounding than the boundary that actually matters.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In the `diagnostic-shape.md` "Persistent diagnostics (default)" paragraph, replace the standalone timing prescription with a cross-reference to PIC's authoritative boundary. Drop the informal "before the first discovery scan kicks off" clause and instead state that the renderer is registered synchronously inside the extension factory per the PIC **Registration timing** rule (`extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md` → Renderer registration → Lifecycle → Registration timing), which pins "before the factory returns." Retain a brief non-normative note that, because `pi.sendMessage` is unavailable on the pre-bind runtime (`registration-steps.md` step 2), the first batch of scan diagnostics is delivered no earlier than `session_start`, by which point the factory-registered renderer is already in place — so the diagnostics render through the loom renderer rather than as raw fallback text. This removes the unpinned "kicks off" boundary, keeps the single normative timing rule in PIC, and leaves `diagnostic-shape.md` describing only the wire format and delivery sink.

Edge case for the implementer: the renderer registration must still precede any `session_start`-time delivery; since both registration and the scan occur inside the factory and delivery is deferred to `session_start`, registering the renderer at any point in the factory body satisfies the contract.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer cannot derive a precise conformance boundary from "before the first discovery scan kicks off," and the numbered factory sequence does not place renderer registration relative to the discovery walk. The behavioural risk is low because PIC's "before the factory returns" rule is the authoritative, sufficient boundary and the scan's diagnostics are not deliverable until `session_start` regardless; the cost is reader confusion and a redundant, looser restatement of an already-pinned rule.
