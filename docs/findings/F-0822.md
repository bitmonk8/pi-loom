## Problem

The closing self-maintenance paragraph of `version-bump-step2.md`'s editorial-review checklist binds its MUST on presuppositions whose detection "routes to editorial review under this procedure," but no source-side presupposition note carries any textual or formal marker signalling that it opts into that routing. The presupposition paragraphs items (a)–(ak) cite appear under a free mix of headings (*consumption posture*, *behavioural presupposition*, *open question*, *Host prerequisite*, *premise*), none of which is a load-bearing routing signal, so a contributor adding a new presupposition cannot mechanically decide whether the MUST applies. The gate therefore collapses to the contributor-recall failure mode its own "non-conformant with this step" conformance clause declares out of bounds: a new presupposition can land, be omitted from the checklist, and the omission is detectable only by reviewer intuition.

The defect was therefore latent from the day the gate was authored — the editorial-review checklist's self-maintenance clause never had a textual signal pinning which source-page paragraphs trigger it.

## Solution approach

Rebind the closing self-maintenance paragraph's MUST so it keys on a textually-decidable source-side signal rather than the undecidable "routes its detection to editorial review under this procedure" predicate. Introduce that signal as a marker convention the source-side presupposition notes carry, defined on `version-bump-step2.md`. Backfill the marker onto the existing presupposition paragraphs items (a)–(ak) reference so the convention is closed at introduction rather than left transitional.
