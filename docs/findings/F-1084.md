## Problem

The `V9l` bullet in `docs/plan.md` §Blocked obligations carries a "Pre-designed fallback:" clause that prescribes a concrete branch-unreachable outcome — collapse to full teardown + `/reload` with no degraded tag write, plus spec-side rework of `session-only-degraded-state.md` and `SM-4`/`SM-5`/`SM-6`/`SM-3b`. That outcome is contingent on the version-bump editorial-review checklist item (a) resolution owned by `V18c`, which has not yet been made. An implementer could build to the pre-design as if settled without re-verifying it against the resolution actually reached; if item (a) lands on different terms, the pre-design is vacuous or misleading. The blocked-on-clause-(a) statement and the spec-recorded note that the resolution "may find the branch unreachable as written" already carry the normative content the plan needs at this point.

## Solution approach

Delete the "Pre-designed fallback:" sentence from the `V9l` bullet in `docs/plan.md` §Blocked obligations, retaining the rest of the bullet (the blocked-on-clause-(a) statement, the `version-bump-step2.md#bump-checklist-instance-survival` / `V18c` resolution-owner pointer, the spec-recorded "may find the branch unreachable as written" note, and the trailing unknown-reason / closed-set / snapshot-failure sentence). Strike the now-dangling trailing parenthetical from the Ships-when paragraph of `docs/plan_topics/V9l-session-only-degraded-branch.md`, since it cites the removed block.

## Solution constraints

- The contingent design and any `SM-`-rework directive belong in the `V18c` clause-(a) resolution commit or in `V9l` when it becomes pickable — do not relocate them elsewhere in the plan.
- Out of scope: the `Deps` references to §Blocked obligations in `V9l` and `V9l-T`, which point at the section generally and stay valid.
