## Problem

The editorial-review checklist's preamble in `version-bump-step2.md` declares items (a)–(e) MUST-audits whose `fail` outcome blocks the bump (the gate `V18c`'s **Ships when** clause restates this). Items (a) and (e) carry first-class audit machinery — dispositive verdicts, an evidentiary standard, and a pass-evidence rule that invalidates a no-site `pass` rationale. Items (b), (c), and (d) are bare title-plus-pointer entries: their pointer targets state the presupposition but specify no audit method, no evidence a conformant `pass` must cite, and no `pass`/`fail` discriminator the merge gate can key on. Two contributors auditing the same candidate minor can therefore record divergent verdicts with equally bare one-line rationales, silently weakening three of the five MUST audits.

**History:** Commit 52b9101 introduced items (a)–(d) of the editorial-review checklist into the consolidated `pi-integration-contract.md` (the topic split at f5e89f4 carried the prose forward into `version-bump-step2.md` unchanged). All four items landed as bare title-plus-pointer lines; (b)/(c)/(d) have remained in that shape since. Item (a) gained its authoritative-determination recording shape and evidentiary standard at commit 80fcd63 (2026-06-20, F-0718+F-0823), and item (e) gained its fail predicate, conservative-posture rule, and pass-evidence rule at commit c0259ca (2026-06-07, "four obligations have no verifiable acceptance criterion") and 7447d1e (2026-06-10); neither enrichment touched (b)/(c)/(d).

## Solution approach

For each of items (b), (c), and (d) in `version-bump-step2.md`, add an audit clause naming the concrete property the contributor must confirm against the candidate minor, plus a pass-evidence rule requiring the `pass` rationale to cite a specific candidate-minor code/doc site — modelled on item (e)'s pass-evidence rule. Apply item (e)'s conservative-posture rule symmetrically, so an outcome that cannot positively cite the required evidence is recorded `fail`, not `N/A`.

## Solution constraints

- Do not extend item (e)'s `N/A — superseded by <named Pi mechanism>` carve-out to items (b)/(c)/(d): none of the three has a superseded-by pathway under the present contract, so an `N/A` for them has no defined shape.
