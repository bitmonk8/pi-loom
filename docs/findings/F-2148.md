## Finding

The recap paragraph that follows the eight-item pre-evaluation failures list in `errors-and-results/error-model.md` closes with a spec-maintenance instruction aimed at editors, not at implementers: *"Any insertion or reordering of items, and the `the eight below` count assertion in the preceding paragraph, moves in lock-step with the list under the same co-edit obligation."* This sentence describes how a future spec edit must be sequenced (keep the count word, the list, and its owner pages in one commit); it constrains the spec's own change-management process, not the runtime an implementer builds.

The same paragraph carries adjacent governance/rationale prose of the same character: the clause *"a future leaf that introduces one updates this list and the new failure's owner page in the same commit per the GOV-30 lock-step convention extended to this paragraph"* is a co-edit directive, and the multi-sentence explanation of *why* each item gets its own `ERR-N` REQ-ID anchor and *why* the anchors are deliberately non-contiguous (`ERR-1`–`ERR-7` then `ERR-16`) is maintainer-facing commentary. The per-item `ERR-N` anchors already make every failure surface independently citable; sibling pages bind to those anchors without consulting any of this editorial text.

Two pieces in the paragraph do carry implementer value and should not be swept up in the removal: the closed-set assertion *"No additional pre-evaluation failure surface applies in loom 1.0"* (it tells an implementer the list is exhaustive) and the short note that the numbering is non-contiguous because `ERR-8`–`ERR-15` are allocated elsewhere (it pre-empts a reader concluding the list is truncated). The cruft is the co-edit/lock-step instruction and the anchor-allocation rationale, not the exhaustiveness statement or the brief non-contiguity pointer.

## Issue introduction

Commit f5e89f4 (2026-06-04) carried the paragraph verbatim into the newly split `error-model.md` during the spec size-cap reflow, and 3fe040c (2026-06-05) later expanded the recap with the non-contiguous-numbering explanation and bumped the count word from "seven" to "eight"; none of these changed the editorial-commentary character the original commit introduced.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In the recap paragraph following the eight-item list, delete the trailing co-edit instruction (*"Any insertion or reordering of items, and the `the eight below` count assertion in the preceding paragraph, moves in lock-step with the list under the same co-edit obligation."*) and the lock-step co-edit clause (*"— a future leaf that introduces one updates this list and the new failure's owner page in the same commit per the GOV-30 lock-step convention extended to this paragraph"*). Drop the maintainer-facing rationale explaining why each item carries a separate `ERR-N` anchor.

Retain the load-bearing content: the exhaustiveness statement *"No additional pre-evaluation failure surface applies in loom 1.0"* and a single brief note that the `ERR-N` numbering is intentionally non-contiguous (`ERR-1`–`ERR-7` then `ERR-16`) because `ERR-8`–`ERR-15` are allocated to other obligations — this pointer prevents a reader from misreading the list as truncated. If the per-item-anchor rationale is judged worth preserving for maintainers, relocate it to a Governance note rather than leaving it inline in the normative page; do not retain it in `error-model.md`.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A maintenance instruction and anchor-allocation rationale sit inside a normative spec page, adding reading burden and inviting an implementer to treat editorial process as a runtime requirement. No runtime observer is affected; the closed list and its per-item anchors already carry the implementable content.
