## Problem

`docs/plan.md` §Spec coverage links to the coverage matrix with the trailing clause "Empty today; populated as leaves are authored." That state-claim is stale: `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md` is fully populated — it maps every numbered REQ-ID prefix range and named obligation to one or more closing leaves, and no longer describes itself as empty. The two files now disagree about whether the matrix is authoritative, so a maintainer wiring the `H5a` / `H5d` closing gates (which treat the populated matrix as the source of truth) is told by plan.md that the table is an unfilled stub. The defect is a stale pointer-prose claim in the plan's index, not a defect in the matrix.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** 15f69aa — pi-loom plan: finish scaffold/template re-pivot from commit 657ee76 (2026-05-26, thomasa); c6a664e — pi-loom plan: build/update plan for spec.md + review (later, thomasa)
**History:** Commit 15f69aa wrote the "Empty today; populated as leaves are authored" clause into plan.md §Spec coverage when coverage-matrix.md genuinely was an empty stub (its table held only `_(none yet)_` rows and its header said "The matrix is empty"). Commit c6a664e and subsequent plan-build/review commits populated coverage-matrix.md row-by-row and dropped its self-describing "empty" header, but the pointer prose in plan.md was never re-synced — so the two files diverged through the interaction of the inception clause and the later population of the matrix.

## Solution approach

In `docs/plan.md` §Spec coverage, delete the stale `Empty today; populated as leaves are authored.` state-claim from the matrix-link bullet. Optionally replace it with a clause that matches reality — that the matrix is maintained per How-to-use step 5 and activated at the loom 1.0 release gate by `H6a`.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md` is read-only for this fix; reconcile the divergence by editing plan.md prose only.
