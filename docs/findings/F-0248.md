## Finding

`conventions.md` §Leaf format defines **Ships when** as "a concrete, externally observable change." The V4c **Ships when** field carries two sentences. The first states the observable gate (`npm test` proves the enumerated `ERR-8`…`ERR-13` cases hold — committed turns survive cancellation and `?`-propagation unmodified, and a completed callee stays final after a downstream terminal event). The second sentence — "The no-rollback guarantee is architectural: it rests on the runtime containing no compensating/rollback path, and the tests witness it on the enumerated cases rather than exhaustively." — is design justification for *why* the guarantee holds, not an observable condition the implementer can check at the exit gate.

The architectural rationale it restates is already normative in the spec: [`error-model.md` ERR-13 §No rollback](../spec_topics/errors-and-results/error-model.md#err-13) and the accompanying "Loom has no implicit transactional layer…" narrative carry the "no compensating/rollback path" framing in full. Duplicating it inside the gate field adds nothing the implementer needs to observe and dilutes the field's single job.

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commit:** `db918a2e84e2715685249f2be495c6eae553b5b2` (2026-06-11) — "pi-loom plan: resolve \"ERR-13 no-rollback / completed-callee finality over-claimed in V4c\""
**History:** The pre-`db918a2` Ships-when read simply "`npm test` proves committed turns survive cancellation and `?`-propagation unmodified, with no rollback." — a single observable sentence. `db918a2`, resolving an over-claim finding, expanded the gate to the enumerated-cases form and appended the "The no-rollback guarantee is architectural… rather than exhaustively." justification sentence in the same edit (`git log -S "tests witness it on the enumerated cases"` and `git show db918a2` confirm the addition). The de-over-claim fix correctly scoped the test bullet but carried its rationale into the gate field as a side effect.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V4c-terminal-outcomes.md`, **Ships when** field, strike the second sentence:

> The no-rollback guarantee is architectural: it rests on the runtime containing no compensating/rollback path, and the tests witness it on the enumerated cases rather than exhaustively.

Retain the first sentence unchanged as the gate. The struck rationale is already normative at [`error-model.md` ERR-13 §No rollback](../spec_topics/errors-and-results/error-model.md#err-13), so no relocation is required; nothing is lost. This edit is confined to the **Ships when** field of `V4c` — the parallel rationale in the **Adds** field and the `ERR-13` test bullet are separate findings and are out of scope here.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The observable exit gate (sentence one) is intact, so no implementer is blocked or misdirected; the trailing justification is redundant prose that the spec already owns. Leaving it in keeps a non-observable claim inside a field whose contract is "observable change only."
