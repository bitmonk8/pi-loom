## Problem

In `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-services.md` the DI-seam blocks split their heading vocabulary: PIC-11 (`SchemaValidator`), PIC-12 (`Clock`/`FakeClock`), PIC-13 (`FakeFileSystem`/`FileSystem`), and PIC-14 (`FakeFileWatcher`/`FileWatcher`) introduce themselves as "… interface." while sibling blocks PIC-10, PIC-16, and PIC-20 say "… seam." All four "interface"-headed bodies nonetheless describe themselves as DI seams injected at construction time and carry the GOV-18 arm (a) disclaimer that the TypeScript shape is non-normative, and the surrounding spec enumerates them as seams. The split is non-load-bearing residue of authoring order, and it presents a false two-tier taxonomy that invites readers to guess at a distinction that does not exist.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `bc50e69` (2026-05-05) "pi-loom spec: resolve 'H2 Clock seam — watcher debounce, scanPackagesTimeoutMs, RuntimeEvent occurred_at'" — adds **`Clock` / `FakeClock` interface.**
- `52219c4` (2026-05-06) "Address all 41 spec-review findings" — adds the `SchemaValidator` block (further reshaped by `1ea0455`, 2026-06-04, "DI-seam vs reference-implementation placement boundary", which kept the "interface" heading word)
- `8a6d15f` (2026-05-06) "pi-loom spec: resolve 'FileSystem seam interface signature lives outside the spec corpus'" — adds **`FakeFileSystem` / `FileSystem` interface.** and **`FakeFileWatcher` / `FileWatcher` interface.**

**History:** Each of PIC-11/12/13/14 was authored in May 2026 around the same review pass that promoted the TypeScript `interface X { ... }` shape into the spec; the heading word "interface" pointed at that artefact. PIC-10's `Checkpoint` block (`033b8e3`, 2026-05-05) was authored a day earlier in the same review pass and already used "seam", because it had no `interface X { ... }` body to name after. The corpus then drifted toward the GOV-18 arm (a) posture — bodies were edited to demote the TS shape to a non-normative illustration (`e228dfa`, `1ea0455`, both 2026-06-04) — but the four headings were never back-edited. The two seams added after the posture had settled (PIC-16 `TokenEstimator` in `1f37b15`, 2026-06-05; PIC-20 `IdSource` in `e9b0597`, 2026-06-05) consistently used "seam", producing the present split. No single commit introduced the inconsistency; it is the residue of the corpus-wide vocabulary shift not being applied to the four headings that predated it.

## Solution approach

Rename the lead bold phrase of PIC-11, PIC-12, PIC-13, and PIC-14 from `interface.` to `seam.`, matching PIC-10/16/20 and the bodies — e.g. `**PIC-11. \`SchemaValidator\` seam.**` and `**PIC-12. \`Clock\` / \`FakeClock\` seam.**`. The rename is heading-only; the bodies and inline self-references already pair the noun "seam" with the `-interface` anchors and need no co-edit.

## Solution constraints

- Preserve the `<a id="schemavalidator-interface">`, `<a id="clock--fakeclock-interface">`, `<a id="fakefilesystem--filesystem-interface">`, and `<a id="filewatcher-interface">` anchors byte-for-byte; do not add `…-seam` aliases or rename the existing anchors (they are consumed by ~32 cross-reference sites across `docs/`).
