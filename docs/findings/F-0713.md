## Problem

PIC-10's `loop-iter` checkpoint requires `before(...)` to release the event loop for one macrotask turn before resolving (echoed in cancellation.md §Granularity), but neither location names the primitive that achieves the yield. The obvious candidates — bare `setTimeout(fn, 0)`, `setImmediate(fn)`, and `Clock.setTimeout(fn, 0)` — are not interchangeable: bare `setTimeout` trips the PIC-12 ambient-primitive ban (`#clock--fakeclock-interface`) at build time, `setImmediate` is on neither the ban list nor the `Clock` seam, and routing through `Clock.setTimeout` couples the yield to the `FakeClock` advance model. The seam therefore mandates a behaviour with no spec-sanctioned implementation, so two implementers diverge on the load-bearing Esc-during-compute-bound-loop cancellation guarantee.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `bc50e69` (2026-05-05, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"H2 Clock seam — watcher debounce, scanPackagesTimeoutMs, RuntimeEvent occurred_at\"") and `8cd3c4a` (2026-06-03, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Loop-iteration checkpoint does not yield the event loop\"").
**History:** `bc50e69` introduced the `Clock` seam in the consolidated `pi-integration-contract.md` together with the ambient-primitive ban — "The runtime MUST NOT call `Date.now`, `performance.now`, `Date.prototype.getTime`, or the global `setTimeout` / `clearTimeout` outside the `WallClock` adapter" — and the grep-test enforcing exactly that closed surface. At that point the `Checkpoint` seam's `loop-iter` kind did not yet require a macrotask yield (production wiring was a uniform "already-resolved promise" across all kinds). `8cd3c4a` then added the macrotask-yield requirement to both PIC-10 and `cancellation.md` §Granularity without naming the primitive and without revisiting the PIC-12 ban surface added a month earlier; the durable defect is the *interaction* between those two edits rather than either commit in isolation. Subsequent edits (`8362824` demoting the grep-test to non-normative on 2026-06-04; `fdc5115` pinning the single-macrotask abort-flip presupposition on 2026-06-06; the `spec_topics/` shard split that moved the prose to `host-interfaces-services.md`) re-touched both paragraphs without closing the gap.

## Solution approach

Name `Clock.setTimeout(fn, 0)` — the existing PIC-12 timer seam — as the primitive the `loop-iter` `before(...)` uses to realise its macrotask yield, in PIC-10's production-wiring rule. Clarify in PIC-12's `FakeClock` paragraph (`#clock--fakeclock-interface`) that a 0-ms seam timer fires under `advance(0)`, so the `V8a` / `V17c` conformance tests assert the yield property by driving `clock.advance(0)` between the abort-flip and the next signal-check. Add a forward-link from cancellation.md §Granularity to the PIC-10 anchor.

## Solution constraints

- Do not add `setImmediate` as a bare primitive or as a new `Clock` member; widening the closed PIC-12 timer surface breaks the ambient-primitive ban invariant and forces parallel updates to the `H3a` scan and the `V8d` Tests-field allow-list.
