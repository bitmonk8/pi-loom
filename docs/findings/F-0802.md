## Finding

`subagent.md` asserts as a flat fact that "the in-memory `SessionManager` is the mechanism that prevents subagent output from reaching Pi's user-facing UI" and points readers at `slash-invocation.md` SLSH-2 for user-visible behaviour. Capability inventory item 3 (`sdk-cap-subagent-isolated-session`) leans on the same property when it promises the capability returns "a disposable `AgentSession` whose transcript is private when `SessionManager.inMemory(cwd)` is supplied." Neither of these claims is mechanically probed at load time, neither is encoded in `CreateAgentSessionOptions` or in `SessionManager.inMemory`'s declared signature, and the loom asserts no behavioural check of its own.

The two editorial-review checklist items that point at the in-memory factory cover adjacent but strictly narrower properties. Item (o) (`bump-checklist-subagent-spawn-satellite-types`) re-validates the *signature* `static inMemory(cwd?: string): SessionManager` and the `ResourceLoader` member shape. Item (aj) (`bump-checklist-inmemory-transcript-discard`) re-validates that the in-memory session *discards rather than persists* the transcript when the loom returns — a retention property about disk / cross-session stores, not a stream-suppression property about Pi's TUI. The cross-reference in `subagent.md`'s satellite-type-pins paragraph cites only item (o); item (aj) is reached only from capability inventory item 3.

The UI-non-surfacing property therefore has no re-validation hook in the corpus at all. A Pi minor that preserved `SessionManager.inMemory`'s signature (passing item (o)) and continued to discard the transcript on return (passing item (aj)) while newly streaming the in-memory session's assistant tokens or tool-call cards into the user-facing TUI in real time would silently falsify both the `subagent.md` "mechanism that prevents subagent output from reaching Pi's user-facing UI" claim and capability item 3's transcript-privacy guarantee, with no signal at load time, at the SDK surface-inventory assertion, or at any bump-checklist item.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:**

- `e466d1f` (2026-05-04, *pi-loom spec: resolve "Streaming visibility of query responses is unspecified"*) — introduced the original *"User-visible streaming — subagent mode"* paragraph in `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` with the load-bearing claim "The subagent's `AgentSession` uses an in-memory `SessionManager`; nothing it produces flows to Pi's user-facing UI." No re-validation hook was attached because no bump-checklist existed yet at that revision.
- `af6e5f6` (2026-05-05, *pi-loom spec: resolve "Prompt-mode streaming edge cases live on the wrong page"*) — moved the user-visible-streaming prose to `slash-invocation.md` and minted the current `subagent.md`-resident phrasing "The in-memory `SessionManager` is the mechanism that prevents subagent output from reaching Pi's user-facing UI; user-visible behaviour is specified in [Invocation from Pi — User-visible streaming](./slash-invocation.md)." This is the present-day defect-token site. No re-validation hook accompanied the move.

**History:** `git log -G` against the spec corpus shows the UI-suppression claim has been carried in the same form (with no behavioural re-validation hook) since the two May-2026 commits above. Subsequent maintenance built up the adjacent hooks without covering this property: `5d55c12` (2026-06-05) introduced item (o) for satellite-type *signatures*, `252acda` (2026-06-05) annotated capability 3 with "the loom asserts no independent behavioural check that the in-memory session discards rather than persists the transcript", and `9ced9f1` (2026-06-08) added item (aj) for the *transcript-discard* (retention) behaviour and routed capability 3's prose to it. None of those commits introduced an item covering the in-memory ⇒ no-UI-surfacing property, and `subagent.md`'s satellite-type-pins paragraph still cites only item (o). The defect is therefore present-since-inception of the UI-suppression claim, with later commits adding *adjacent-but-not-overlapping* hooks rather than ever closing this specific gap.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add a new SHOULD-level editorial-review checklist item under *Editorial-review checklist for unpinned host presuppositions* in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md`, modelled on item (aj), that audits the in-memory session's UI-non-surfacing behaviour on each Pi minor bump. Concretely:

- Append the new item directly after item (ak), giving it the next free letter (`(al)` at the time of writing). Anchor it as `bump-checklist-inmemory-no-ui-surfacing` and title it *`SessionManager.inMemory(cwd)` in-memory-session UI-non-surfacing behaviour*.
- Word it on the same SHOULD-level footing as (aj) / (ak): the contributor SHOULD confirm against the candidate `@earendil-works/pi-coding-agent` minor — by reading the `SessionManager.inMemory` in-memory-session branch's JS body against the [Baseline acquisition](#bump-baseline-acquisition) tree, plus the `AgentSession` event-emission path the in-memory session takes — that the in-memory session does *not* stream assistant tokens, tool-call cards, or system notes from the spawned subagent's queries to Pi's user-facing TUI. Note explicitly that this is the behavioural complement to item (o)'s signature audit and item (aj)'s retention audit: a Pi minor passing both (o) and (aj) while newly surfacing the in-memory session to the TUI would falsify the `subagent.md` UI-suppression claim and capability item 3's privacy guarantee, and is caught only here. Carry the same SHOULD-with-mechanical-escalation clause the surrounding items use.
- Update the checklist preamble's range from `(a)`–`(ak)` to `(a)`–`(al)` (the same one-letter widening `9ced9f1` did for (aj)), update the [Baseline acquisition](#bump-baseline-acquisition) paragraph's enumeration of items requiring JS-body reads from `(e)`, `(r)`, `(aj)` to also include `(al)`, and update the trailing closure rule that says "any future presupposition added … MUST be added to this checklist in the same edit" is satisfied by this edit itself.
- In `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/subagent.md`, extend the *Spawn-block satellite type pins* paragraph's "MUST be re-validated … per item (o)" clause to also point at the new item, so the cross-reference from the load-bearing UI-suppression sentence routes to a checklist obligation rather than to a signature-only audit. The cleanest form is the same one (aj) uses at capability item 3: "splits across editorial-review checklist items below: item (o) confirms the factory *signature*, item (aj) audits the no-persistence *behaviour*, and item (al) audits the no-UI-surfacing *behaviour*."
- In `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/capability-inventory-items.md` item 3, add a parallel pointer to item (al) alongside the existing item (o) / (aj) cross-references, so the capability-side presupposition statement names the bump-time detection mechanism for each of the three properties it asserts.
- Update `docs/plan_topics/V18c-version-bump-checklist.md` to widen the `(a)`–`(ak)` range to `(a)`–`(al)` in both the `Adds.` clause and the `Ships when.` clause, so the build-time gate that enforces per-item recording covers the new item.

Edge cases the editor must watch: (1) keep the new item SHOULD-level rather than MUST-level — escalating to MUST would force the bump to fail on any contributor who skips the JS-body read, whereas (aj)'s SHOULD-with-must-record posture is the established analogue; (2) the item must phrase the property as "does not stream events to Pi's user-facing TUI" rather than "is not observable to the operator" — the latter overlaps with the cancellation system-note rendering and the `loom-system-note` channel, which are independently observable and out of this item's scope; (3) the new item must not subsume item (aj) — they audit distinct properties of the same factory (retention vs. surfacing) and a single combined item would obscure which property a `fail` outcome diagnosed.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

A Pi-side change that began surfacing in-memory `SessionManager` events to the user-facing TUI would silently break two normative claims the spec rests on — the `subagent.md` UI-suppression sentence and capability item 3's transcript-privacy guarantee — and would surface to operators only as a user-visible privacy leak (subagent assistant tokens, tool-call cards, or system notes appearing in the user transcript). The signature-only audit at item (o) and the retention-only audit at item (aj) are both unable to detect this regression at bump time, so the loom 1.0 detection mechanism for it is contributor recall alone — exactly the failure mode the editorial-review checklist's closing rule ("any future presupposition … MUST be added to this checklist in the same edit; landing such a presupposition without extending the checklist leaves the detection mechanism reliant on contributor recall and is non-conformant with this step") forbids.
