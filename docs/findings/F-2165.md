## Problem

PIC-20 (`IdSource` seam) states a normative MUST NOT against calling `crypto.randomUUID()` (or any other ambient UUID source) outside the production adapter, but attaches no conformance criterion. Its two structural siblings each mandate one: PIC-12 requires a build-time check rejecting direct lexical references to the timing primitives and names that check as the conformance gate, and PIC-13 reuses PIC-12's criterion by reference for the environment-variable ban. PIC-20 is the third member of this ban family left without the verification mechanism the other two require, so an implementer cannot tell whether the omission is intentional or an oversight. The deterministic-test premise the `IdSource` seam exists to protect depends on no stray ambient UUID source existing, yet nothing in the spec gates that.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 4556623 — pi-loom spec-plan: fix F-1178 — PIC-12 timing-primitive ban gets observable build-time conformance criterion (2026-06-25, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The PIC-20 ban on `crypto.randomUUID()` outside the production adapter was introduced on 2026-06-05 in e9b0597 ("resolve invocationId has no injectable id-source seam"); at that point none of PIC-12, PIC-13, or PIC-20 carried a build-time-check criterion, so no asymmetry existed. The defect entered on 2026-06-25 in 4556623 (F-1178), which added the build-time-check conformance criterion to PIC-12 and extended it by reference to PIC-13 but did not touch PIC-20 — leaving the third member of the ban family without the verification mechanism the other two now mandate.

## Solution approach

Add to PIC-20, after its `MUST NOT call crypto.randomUUID()` sentence, a build-time-check conformance criterion that reuses [PIC-12](#pic-12) by reference exactly as PIC-13 does, scoped to direct lexical references to `crypto.randomUUID` and any other ambient UUID source outside the `CryptoIdSource` adapter.
