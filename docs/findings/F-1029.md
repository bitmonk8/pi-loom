## Problem

`subagent.md`'s `subagent-untyped-query-ok-extraction` paragraph encodes a three-part protocol claim about `AgentSessionEvent.agent_end.willRetry`: an `agent_end` with `willRetry: true` precedes an automatic SDK retry and MUST NOT resolve the query; exactly one `agent_end` with `willRetry: false` eventually arrives and MUST resolve it; and `agent_end` is the sole terminal-event channel for the driven turn. The runtime relies on all three â€” deviation either resolves on the wrong event (premature `Ok("")`) or hangs until cancellation. Only the eventual-delivery part is currently surfaced as a named presupposition and audited: `host-interfaces-core.md`'s `pi-slash-handler-promise-lifecycle-presupposition` (iv) and `version-bump-step2.md` item (j) cover turn-liveness, while the event-classification half (retry-vs-terminal discrimination and the no-alternative-terminal-channel claim) is asserted in prose but not anchored as a presupposition or routed to the per-bump editorial-review checklist. A future Pi minor that still delivers an eventual terminal `agent_end` (passing item (j)) but reuses `willRetry: false` on a non-terminal step, or introduces a sibling terminal-event variant, would break the runtime with no audit signal.

## Solution approach

Add a named event-classification presupposition anchored in `host-interfaces-core.md`, parallel to `messages-chronological-order-presupposition`, capturing the retry-vs-terminal discrimination and sole-terminal-channel claims that `pi-slash-handler-promise-lifecycle-presupposition` (iv) does not cover. In `subagent.md`, name the `subagent-untyped-query-ok-extraction` paragraph as a consumer of the new presupposition, matching its existing `AgentMessage[]`-chronological-ordering consumer sentence. Add a new editorial-review checklist item to `version-bump-step2.md` parallel to (h), pointing at the new anchor, and cross-reference it from item (j) so a reader on (j) is directed to where classification is audited.
