## Finding

In `version-bump-step2.md`, the `<a id="bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions"></a>` **Outcome recording conventions** appendix sits at the very end of step 2's checklist (line 103), *after* the full enumeration of items (a)–(an) plus the closing "Any future presupposition…" co-edit obligation. The appendix is the normative source of truth for three pieces of recording shape that earlier items forward-reference: item (a)'s authoritative-determination verdicts (`governed-by-rebind` / `provably-differs`), item (e)'s `N/A` definition and `N/A — superseded by <named Pi mechanism>` form, and item (e)'s two-sub-outcome recording convention covering (e.i)/(e.ii).

Both forward references appear in items (a) and (e) — the very first and fifth bullets of the list — so a reader following the spec in document order encounters every appeal to the appendix's definitions long before reaching the definitions themselves. The same reader also passes ~40 intervening items, each ~one screen of prose, while the unresolved anchors hang. The intent of pulling the conventions out of item (e)'s body in commit `c5f97f6` was specifically to make them "discoverable apart from the item bodies' inline prose"; placing the resulting appendix below those item bodies undercuts that goal because the appendix is now strictly less discoverable than the inline prose it replaced — an in-body restatement is co-located with the reader's question, whereas an end-of-list appendix requires the reader to know it exists and to scroll past every other item to find it.

The conventional placement rule for normative definitions consumed by a checklist is to state them at or above the first site that appeals to them. The fix is to move the appendix up.

The commit positioned the new appendix immediately after the closing "Any future presupposition…" sentence — i.e. below every enumerated item — so item (a)'s and item (e)'s forward references have been below-target since the appendix existed. Later edits (`80fcd63`, 2026-06-20, adding the item (a) authoritative-determination recording shape into the same appendix; `2123dc9`, 2026-06-21, adding item (an); `29f4cba`, extending the preamble's dash-form range to (f)–(am)) preserved the end-of-list placement and added a third forward reference (from item (a)) without revisiting it. The defect is the appendix's position, not the content of any one commit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move the entire `<a id="bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions"></a>` **Outcome recording conventions** paragraph from its current position (after the "Any future presupposition…" sentence) to immediately after the step-2 preamble and before item (a). Keep its `<a id="bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions"></a>` anchor verbatim so every existing forward reference (`#bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions` from item (a)'s authoritative-determination paragraph and from item (e)'s `N/A`-definition / two-sub-outcome / pass-evidence-rule paragraphs) continues to resolve without edits to the linking sites. Leave the closing "Any future presupposition…" sentence in its current end-of-list position — it is a meta-obligation on the checklist as a whole, not a recording convention, and its placement at the foot of the list is correct.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The appendix paragraph contains the only authoritative definitions of (i) item (e)'s `N/A` outcome, (ii) the `N/A — superseded by <named Pi mechanism>` recording shape, (iii) the two-sub-outcome recording convention with the inline `<a id="bump-checklist-slash-dispatch-serialisation-i"></a>` and `<a id="bump-checklist-slash-dispatch-serialisation-ii"></a>` anchors, and (iv) the item (a) authoritative-determination recording shape including its evidentiary standard and acceptance criterion. All four sub-blocks must move together as a single unit; do not split them.
- The two inline `<a id="bump-checklist-slash-dispatch-serialisation-i"></a>` and `bump-checklist-slash-dispatch-serialisation-ii` anchors inside the appendix are also targets of references from item (e)'s own body (the "two atomic sub-outcomes below" sentence) and from item (an)'s body via `[(e.ii)](#bump-checklist-slash-dispatch-serialisation-ii)`. Preserving the anchors verbatim during the move keeps every cross-reference valid.
- The preamble's own forward reference to `[Outcome recording conventions](#bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions)` (inside the long paragraph beginning "*Editorial-review checklist for unpinned host presuppositions.*") now becomes a forward reference of one paragraph rather than ~100 lines; verify the link still renders and update no other prose.
- No other spec file links into `#bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions` (`grep -rn` confirms the anchor is only referenced from within `version-bump-step2.md` itself), so the move is self-contained to this file.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A first-time reader of step 2 hits item (a)'s forward reference to "Item (a) authoritative-determination recording shape" and item (e)'s forward references to the `N/A` definition and the two-sub-outcome convention well before the definitions appear, and must either trust the link will resolve later or scroll past every remaining item to read the convention before continuing. The normative content is correct and resolvable; only the reading-order ergonomics degrade.
