## Finding

`V9b` delivers four independently-reviewable concerns inside one leaf: (1) the registration pipeline — steps 1–5 (`registerFlag('loom')` → walk, pending-registration list, `session_start` collision + `registerCommand`, `session_shutdown` subscribe, and the watcher + `LoomRegistry` build-aside-publish swap with its step-5 structural-change `loom-system-note`); (2) the test-only `ReloadFailureInjector` interface (declared once here, with the registry-swap and `.loom`/`.warp` re-parse arms wired in this leaf); (3) the structural-change watcher-note debounce behaviours (empty-window suppression and same-window-rename emission); and (4) the model-reference-matcher production wiring (constructing Pi's `findExactModelReferenceMatch` against `ctx.modelRegistry.getAvailable()` and injecting it into `V6a`'s parser seam).

Concern (4), the matcher wiring, is orthogonal to the swap/debounce/failure-injection concerns — it shares no code path with them and is coupled only by living in the same load pass. The `Ships when` gate enumerates six distinct verification conditions spanning all four concerns, so a reviewer must evaluate four unrelated behavioural areas in one pass.

The leaf remains executable and reviewable in a single session; this is a review-granularity / verification-surface concern, not a correctness defect. No two reasonable implementers diverge because of the bundling.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Group the `Ships when` conditions by behavioural area

**Approach:** Leave the leaf intact; annotate `V9b`'s `Ships when` so the six conditions are grouped under their behavioural area (registration/swap, read-only collision pass, structural-change debounce note, matcher wiring), making the four concerns scannable without changing the DAG.
**Plan edits:** Edit only `V9b-registration-reload-wiring.md`'s `Ships when` bullet to label the condition groups by area.
**Spec edits:** None.
**Pros:** No new leaf; no `Deps` retargeting; no coverage-matrix change; minimal diff.
**Cons:** All four concerns still land and are verified in one leaf.
**Risks:** None beyond leaving the bundle in place.

### Option B — Extract the matcher wiring into its own leaf

**Approach:** Move concern (4) — the model-reference-matcher production wiring and its single `Ships when` condition — into a new leaf (placeholder `<new>`) that depends on `V9b`'s load-pass walk. Retarget `V11a`'s matcher-reconciliation `Deps` edge from `V9b` to the new leaf.
**Plan edits:** Remove the matcher-wiring paragraph from `V9b`'s Adds, the matcher `Tests` bullet, and the matcher clause of `Ships when`; author the new leaf (and its `-T` partner) carrying that content with `Deps` on `V9b`; link it under §Vertical slices V9 in `docs/plan.md`; update the `V11a` / `V11a-T` `Deps` edge and the `cka-23` closing-leaf cell in `coverage-matrix.md` if the matcher facet moves.
**Spec edits:** None.
**Pros:** Each leaf carries a single coherent concern; smaller per-leaf review surface.
**Cons:** Adds a leaf and a `-T` partner; widens the DAG; forces a `V11a` `Deps` retarget and a coverage-matrix touch; the other three concerns (registration, failure-injection, debounce) still co-reside in `V9b`.
**Risks:** Mis-targeted `Deps` after the split could leave `V11a` pointing at a leaf that no longer owns the matcher instance; the coverage-matrix `cka-23` facet attribution must follow the moved `Tests` bullet.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A**. The leaf is executable and reviewable in one session, the four concerns do not interact, and no implementer diverges, so the marginal review-granularity gain from extraction does not justify a new leaf plus a `V11a` `Deps` retarget and a coverage-matrix edit. Group the six `Ships when` conditions by behavioural area in `V9b-registration-reload-wiring.md` so a reviewer can navigate the concerns without restructuring the DAG. Honour the project's `no-invented-ids` rule — if Option B is taken instead, leave the new leaf's ID as `<new>` for the implementer to allocate, and retarget `V11a`'s matcher edge in the same change so it never references a leaf that no longer owns the matcher instance.

The leaf still produces a working implementation; no implementer divergence results, so nothing downstream breaks if it ships as-is.
