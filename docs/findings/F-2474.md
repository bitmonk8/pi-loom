## Problem

The Diagnostic-emission-isolation paragraph and its sibling Pi-integration-contract pages (`drain-state-contract.md`, `tool-registration-lifetime.md`, `unknown-reason-rule.md`, `session-only-degraded-state.md`) lean on the term-of-art "leaf" / "leaf-owned" to mark what the spec delegates rather than pins, yet "leaf" has no glossary entry and no first-use gloss anywhere in the corpus. Its intended meaning — the downstream implementation unit the corpus deliberately does not bind per GOV-18 — is only recoverable by a reader who already carries that governance context. A reader without it cannot reliably distinguish "implementation-chosen / unconstrained here" from "constrained by some other lower-level document I must go find," so a load-bearing term that gates the read of adjacent normative MUSTs stands with no definition.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-introduction
**Introducing commits:** `75a9626` (2026-05-09, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"SessionShutdownEvent.reason closed set has no build-time pin against the SDK type\"")
**History:** The corpus is git-tracked. `git log -S 'leaf-owned' -G 'leaf[- ]own'` over `docs/spec.md docs/spec_topics` bottoms out at `75a9626` (2026-05-09), which introduced the "leaf-owned" / "the leaf owns" ownership idiom into the Pi-integration-contract session-shutdown prose already carrying its normative MUSTs. `git log -S 'leaf' -- docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` returns no commits, confirming the glossary has never contained a "leaf" entry across the corpus's history. The Diagnostic-emission-isolation file itself was later carved out of `pi-integration-contract.md` by the size-cap split `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04), which carried the undefined term forward. A subsequent corpus-direction sweep `9e53613` (2026-05-26, "drop H1 / plan_topics / plan-leaf-ID references from spec corpus") removed explicit plan-leaf-ID references but left the generic "leaf-owned" ownership idiom in place without a definition. The defect — a normative-adjacent term-of-art with no glossary anchor — has therefore been present since the term first entered the corpus and was never subsequently defined.

## Solution approach

Add a glossary entry to `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` for **leaf** (with **leaf-owned** as a sub-form), defining it as the downstream implementation unit the spec corpus does not bind and stating that "leaf-owned" marks an implementation-chosen point the spec leaves unpinned at that site, in contrast to the normative MUSTs beside it. Cross-reference GOV-18 (`#gov-18` on `corpus-direction-and-scope.md`) as the binding-scope authority for why such choices fall outside the corpus's normative reach. Existing bare uses of "leaf-owned" need no per-site edit once the anchor exists.

## Solution constraints

- Define "leaf" by its role (the downstream unit the spec's obligations are consumed by), not by naming `plan_topics/**` file shapes or coverage-matrix internals — naming third-party internals trips GOV-18's internals-prohibition.
