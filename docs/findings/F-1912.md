## Finding

`H7a`'s third `Tests.` bullet (the **committed permitted-code list** bullet) re-states, at length, provenance that already lives verbatim in the leaf's `Adds.` paragraph: that the list is a *manually-maintained, best-effort* union of each Deps slice's emittable codes, kept current by a per-Deps-slice provenance obligation rather than verified against an emittable-code registry; that it is deliberately broader than the golden diagnostics list and intended as a *superset*; and that its completeness "is **not** mechanically verified, so a stale union can omit a benign code a slice emits." The same sentences appear in `Adds.` after the `f340c3b` edit.

The bullet then trails a rationale clause — "that gate is what guarantees the in-process gate and the real-host smoke cannot disagree…" and "It is the single committed, reviewed reference set the `H4a` manual real-host smoke run's pass criterion (e) and the `H6a` release-gate evidence record check the live run's emitted codes against…" — that explains *why* the artefact exists rather than naming an assertable behaviour. That cross-reference is owned elsewhere: `real-host-smoke-gate.md` (pass criterion (e)) and `H6a`'s Ships-when each restate it.

The only non-duplicative normative content the bullet carries is that the permitted-code list artefact is checked in alongside the fixture `.loom`, and that it is a superset of the golden diagnostics list. The actual mechanical requirement — the `golden ⊆ permitted` containment check — is asserted by the *next* (fourth) bullet, not this one. As written, an implementer must read past the provenance and rationale to find the one-line artefact-existence requirement.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-later-edit
**Introducing commits:** `f340c3b` ("pi-loom plan: resolve \"permitted-code-list cannot-disagree guarantee unenforced\"", 2026-06-17)
**History:** The permitted-code-list Tests bullet first appeared in `052b019` (2026-06-11, "…names a permitted code set with no committed source"), already carrying superset/reference-set rationale. `f340c3b` then added the *manually-maintained, best-effort* / *completeness not mechanically verified* provenance to **both** the `Adds.` paragraph and this Tests bullet, and split the `golden ⊆ permitted` containment assertion out into a new fourth bullet — leaving the third bullet as provenance duplicated against Adds plus a rationale tail, with its actionable gate now living in the next bullet. The duplication this finding names dates to `f340c3b`.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/H7a-integration-acceptance.md`, trim the third `Tests.` bullet to its minimal normative content: a committed permitted-code list is checked in alongside the fixture `.loom` (next to the committed golden transcript and golden diagnostics list), and it is a superset of the golden diagnostics list whose `golden ⊆ permitted` containment the following bullet gates.

Strip from the bullet the provenance sentences that already appear in `Adds.` — the "manually-maintained, best-effort union…", "kept current by a per-Deps-slice provenance obligation rather than verified against an emittable-code registry", and "its completeness … is **not** mechanically verified, so a stale union can omit a benign code a slice emits" clauses. Strip the rationale tail — "that gate is what guarantees the in-process gate and the real-host smoke cannot disagree…" and "It is the single committed, reviewed reference set the `H4a` manual real-host smoke run's pass criterion (e) and the `H6a` release-gate evidence record check…", which are owned by `real-host-smoke-gate.md` criterion (e) and `H6a`'s Ships-when.

Leave the fourth bullet (the `golden ⊆ permitted` containment check) and the `Adds.` paragraph unchanged. If any detail in the stripped prose is unique to the bullet and not already in `Adds.`, fold it into `Adds.` rather than retaining it in the Tests bullet. Do not edit `real-host-smoke-gate.md`, `H6a`, or the spec for this fix.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The duplicated provenance and rationale obscure the bullet's one actionable requirement (the permitted-code list artefact is checked in), and the mechanical gate the bullet's *superset* wording relies on already lives in the next bullet. No implementer would diverge on observable behaviour; the cost is reader friction and a maintenance hazard where the Adds and the bullet can drift out of sync.
