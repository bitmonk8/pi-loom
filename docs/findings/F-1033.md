## Finding

The `PIC-22` block in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/subagent.md` (anchor `pic-22`) carries, after its actual behavioural obligation (parallel `createAgentSession` initiation for N≥3 subagent-mode parallel tool calls) and its conformance-test sketch, a long tail beginning *"Beyond the absence of an admission cap and scheduler pinned above, the loom adds no per-class threshold and no cross-sibling aggregation, throttling, or storm-detection over the resources concurrent loom invocations consume…"* through the closing *"Widening or narrowing this rule is a major-version concern."* That tail is a concurrency **non-goal** (and, in its last sentence, a governance disposition): it states which resource classes the loom contract does **not** own (V8 heap, OS descriptors/sockets/child-process slots, provider rate-limit/quota), who does own them (host runtime / host OS / provider), and that no per-class threshold or cross-sibling aggregation/throttling/storm-detection surface is added — together with the per-call surfacing routing for catchable host throws, provider 429s, and uncatchable host fatals.

Two distortions follow from attaching that tail to PIC-22. First, PIC-22's actual normative scope is the parallel-spawn-initiation MUST and the witnessing conformance test; bundling a resource-ownership disposition under the `pic-22` anchor makes "what PIC-22 obliges" larger and fuzzier than the closing-leaf acceptance in `plan_topics/V9i-subagent-isolation.md` (which witnesses only the N=3 spawn behaviour). Second, the same prose is *already* carried by the consolidated non-goals aggregator in `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md` under the "No admission cap on in-flight loom invocations" entry, which back-references the subagent.md site — so the two copies have to be kept in lockstep, and a reader looking up the non-goals via the canonical aggregator (per `language-and-architecture.md` §V1 non-goals → `model-changes-and-non-goals.md#loom-1-0-non-goals`) finds the same content in two places with no signal as to which is canonical.

The tail also rides on the "Concurrent-invocation isolation and concurrency disposition" paragraph immediately above PIC-22, which already owns the `no-invocation-cap` anchor and the no-admission-cap / no-scheduler disposition — that paragraph, not the PIC-22 block, is the natural normative home for the resource-ownership statement (the non-goals aggregator can then continue to back-reference a single anchor instead of straddling two).

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Detach the resource-ownership / no-aggregation tail from the `PIC-22` block and reattach it to the "Concurrent-invocation isolation and concurrency disposition" paragraph immediately above it in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/subagent.md` (the paragraph that already owns `concurrent-invocation-isolation` and `no-invocation-cap`). Concretely:

- Leave the `PIC-22` block as: anchor, "Parallel subagent spawn initiation" heading, the N≥3 spawn-initiation MUST, the conformance-test sketch with the fake `AgentSession`, and the upstream-serialisation reminder. Stop there. The block then carries exactly the obligation `V9i-T-subagent-isolation` witnesses.
- Move the sentences starting *"Beyond the absence of an admission cap and scheduler pinned above…"* through *"…on the same footing as any other engine fatal."* into the preceding `concurrent-invocation-isolation` paragraph, immediately after the existing `no-invocation-cap` sentence. Keep the wording verbatim; only the attachment point changes.
- Move the final sentence *"Widening or narrowing this rule is a major-version concern."* with the resource-ownership tail (it is the major-version disposition for the same no-aggregation rule, not for PIC-22 specifically). The related cruft finding suggests relocating this sentence to governance; either landing is consistent with this finding, but if it is left in subagent.md it MUST sit with the no-aggregation tail in the `concurrent-invocation-isolation` paragraph, not under `pic-22`.
- Update the "No admission cap on in-flight loom invocations" entry in `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md` so its back-reference to `subagent.md#no-invocation-cap` still resolves: the target anchor `no-invocation-cap` is preserved (it already lives on the destination paragraph), so no anchor rename is required; verify the back-reference still lands on the resource-ownership prose after the move.
- Do not duplicate the resource-ownership text into `language-and-architecture.md`. That file's `### V1 non-goals` is an aggregator that routes to `model-changes-and-non-goals.md#loom-1-0-non-goals`; the canonical non-goals entry already lives there. The original "Move to language-and-architecture.md §V1 non-goals" framing pre-dates the consolidation done at commit `0e12140` and would re-fragment the non-goals.

Implementer edge cases:

- The `pic-22` anchor MUST be preserved (it is cited by `V9i-subagent-isolation`, `V9i-T-subagent-isolation`, `coverage-matrix.md`, and the runtime allow-list comment `// allow: PIC-22 — pi-integration-contract/subagent.md`). The fix shrinks what lives under the anchor; it does not rename it.
- The `no-invocation-cap` anchor MUST be preserved on the destination paragraph (it is back-referenced from `model-changes-and-non-goals.md` and from `spec.md`'s session-model forward link).
- Do not collapse the resource-ownership content out of subagent.md on the theory that the non-goals aggregator carries it: the consolidation in `model-changes-and-non-goals.md` was authored to back-reference a normative anchor in the pi-integration-contract surface, not to be the primary site.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Implementers reading PIC-22 are led to believe the resource-ownership disposition is part of the PIC-22 obligation a conformance test must witness, when in fact the only witnessable PIC-22 obligation is the parallel spawn-initiation MUST. Reviewers maintaining the non-goals also have to keep two near-identical paragraphs (subagent.md and `model-changes-and-non-goals.md`) in sync; drift between them is a future-edit hazard rather than a present-day correctness break.
