## Problem

The *Wire serialisation* clause pins every emitted record's leading segment to a `path:line` pair and is precise about the `line` integer's encoding (tab-free, joined by the right-most ASCII colon), but never states its indexing base — whether `line` is the 0-based or 1-based physical line number. The record is an explicitly-pinned cross-system contract, so a CI parser or contributor mapping a record back to source cannot resolve the line unambiguously without the base. The surrounding corpus offers no disambiguating default: it carries a zero-based byte offset for `loom/load/invalid-encoding` while the `line` field's own *originating line* concept is described purely in physical-line terms with no base attached. Two reasonable implementers will make opposite choices and emit well-formed records that point readers at different lines.

The defect has been present since the contract first existed.

## Solution approach

Clarify the `line` field's indexing base on the *Wire serialisation* clause of `audit-wire-and-canary.md`, where the `path:line` segment is defined, pinning it as the 1-based physical line number of the *originating line* fixed by `audit-resolution.md` *Multi-line surface placement*. 1-based is the universal editor/CI line-number convention, so consumers map `<path>:<line>` to a source location without conversion.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the zero-based byte-offset convention for `loom/load/invalid-encoding` — a distinct coordinate (also touched by F-1788); the new wording must not alter it.
