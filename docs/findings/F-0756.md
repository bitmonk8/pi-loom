## Problem

`query-tool-loop.md` carries one normative worked-example transcript (depth-6 forced respond at `max_rounds`, parameterised at `tool_loop.max_rounds: 2`) and pins two further normative behaviours only in prose: the structurally-different `max_rounds: 0` dispatch arm and the forced-respond right-trim rule (which keeps the prompt-to-instruction gap at exactly one U+000A). Neither has a normative input/output vector to assert against. A conformant-looking implementation could issue a spurious zero-tool free-phase call on the `max_rounds: 0` path, or omit/mistrim the U+000A separator, and still satisfy every prose MUST and pass the depth-6 vector. `V13c-T` cites the `max_rounds: 0` boundary but has no normative target behind it.

## Solution approach

Add two normative input/output vectors to `query-tool-loop.md`, both observable at the provider boundary. Add a second worked-example transcript sibling to the depth-6 vector (`#worked-example-depth-6-forced-respond`) covering the `max_rounds: 0` boundary, pinning the single forced-respond provider call: its user-message body, the forced `tool_choice` to the respond tool, CIO-4 firing at initialisation with no free-phase provider call, and `masked` omitted on a raised `validation` event. After the right-trim sentence in `#typed-queries-are-tool-loop-shaped` step 2, add a normative rendered-prompt→forced-respond-body vector in the shape of the `query-forms.md` rendered-text vectors, distinguishing zero, one, and multiple trailing U+000A and a trailing non-U+000A whitespace case. Cite both new vectors from the `Tests` bullets of `V13c` and `V13c-T`.
