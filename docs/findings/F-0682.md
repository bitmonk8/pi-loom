## Finding

The intro paragraph of `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics.md` asserts that "the diagnostics defined here all fire after the extension is already live (during scan, watcher reload, or slash-command execution)." That universal claim is contradicted by at least two diagnostic rows in this same topic:

- `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed` (in `diagnostics/code-registry-load.md`) is explicitly triggered by "a factory-time or `session_start`-time SDK registration or subscription call" — i.e. before the factory finishes binding `pi.*`, or at the host-driven `session_start` callback. Both windows are before the extension is "live" in the scan / watcher-reload / slash-command sense the intro names.
- `loom/load/host-incompatible` (same file) is emitted from the extension-factory capability probe (Step 0), strictly before any `pi.register*` / `pi.on` call, and so is also a factory-time diagnostic.

The intro's own qualifier ("Such bootstrap failures surface on Pi startup") refers only to the failures Pi's loader would otherwise push into `LoadExtensionsResult.errors`; it does not retract the "all fire after the extension is already live" claim that immediately follows. The result is a self-contradiction within one paragraph: the topic introduces itself with a window that excludes two of the diagnostic codes it then defines.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace the universal "all fire after the extension is already live" clause in the intro with a two-window statement that names the factory-time / `session_start`-time exceptions. Concretely, the second half of the paragraph should read something like:

> The diagnostics defined here fire in two windows: a small set of **factory-time / `session_start`-time** codes — `loom/load/host-incompatible` and `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed` — surface during extension bootstrap, before the first scan; all other codes fire **after the extension is live** (during scan, watcher reload, or slash-command execution).

Notes for the implementer:

- The exception set is the two `load`-phase codes that are emitted from inside the capability probe or the bootstrap registration `try`/`catch` envelopes. Do not extend the set to other `load`-phase codes (e.g. `loom/load/invalid-encoding`, `loom/load/unreadable-source`, `loom/load/discovery-slow`, `loom/load/package-read-timeout`): those fire during the post-bootstrap scan and watcher-reload windows and are correctly described by the "after the extension is live" branch.
- Keep the existing `LoadExtensionsResult.errors` framing in the first half of the paragraph unchanged; it is independently correct and the related finding "`extension-bootstrap-failed` / `LoadExtensionsResult.errors` interplay asserted without backing" owns any further work on it.
- The per-code Trigger cells in `diagnostics/code-registry-load.md` already pin the timing windows authoritatively; this edit is intro-only and should not duplicate or restate those triggers.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The authoritative per-code triggers in `code-registry-load.md` are correct and pin the timing windows precisely, so an implementer who reads the rows ships the right behaviour. The contradiction is a reader-orientation defect: a contributor scanning the intro to decide whether bootstrap-failure handling belongs in this topic gets the wrong answer, and a future reviewer auditing "all" claims will trip over it again.
