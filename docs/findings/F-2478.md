## Problem

The handler-internal `try`/`catch` clause in `unknown-reason-rule.md` (PIC-47), the *Hoist obligation* / two-token-fallback clauses in `diagnostic-emission-isolation.md` (PIC-25), and the *Predicate split* / *Seam minimality* clauses in `session-only-degraded-state.md` pin the handler-scoped variable names `capturedEventReason`, `pinnedConstantReadOk`, and `detailsEventReason`. None of these identifiers appears in observable output — they are internal implementation locals. Pinning them as normative names needlessly forbids conforming implementations that use equivalent local identifiers, against PIC-1 clause (e)'s observable-only stance, and grows the normative surface future maintainers and conformance fixtures must track.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the variable-name references in `unknown-reason-rule.md`'s handler-internal `try`/`catch` clause (PIC-47), `diagnostic-emission-isolation.md`'s *Hoist obligation* and two-token-fallback clauses (PIC-25), and `session-only-degraded-state.md`'s *Predicate split* and *Seam minimality* clauses to name each binding by its observable/behavioural role rather than its runtime identifier — the handler-entry captured-reason value defaulting to `"<unreadable>"`, the snapshot-read-success flag set on the snapshot lookup-and-`literals` success arm, and the hoisted reason value feeding the two-token catch arm. Where a clause must refer to a binding as a normative seam, name it conceptually or mark the identifier explicitly non-normative-referent-only.
