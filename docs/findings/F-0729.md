## Problem

The combined glossary entry **no-params bypass** vs. **single-string bypass** in `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` describes the single-string case as setting the param "to the entire slash-argument string (whitespace-trimmed)," but the bare `(whitespace-trimmed)` parenthetical is the only corpus occurrence of this normalisation that does not link to its canonical definition. Every other site that mentions the same trim — the bypass rule, the system-prompt user-arguments line, and `SLSH-1` — links to the anchored definition `slash-argument-whitespace` on `binder/binder-bypass-and-envelope.md`, which pins the set to ASCII whitespace and excludes the regex `\s` class. A reader who consults the glossary first sees a normalisation term with two plausible interpretations (ASCII vs. Unicode `\s`) and no cue that a normative pin exists elsewhere, failing the corpus convention of routing each coined term to its anchored definition.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `b4fbfac` (2026-05-04), `9aeb318` (2026-05-05), `f14a45e` (2026-06-05)
**History:** The defect token entered as `(whitespace-trimmed)` in `b4fbfac` ("pi-loom spec: resolve 'no central glossary'"), which created the central `glossary.md` with a single combined `binder` / `binder bypass` entry. `9aeb318` ("Glossary entry for binder bypass omits the no-params case") split that entry into the present `binder` entry plus the combined `no-params bypass` vs. `single-string bypass` entry, carrying the bare `(whitespace-trimmed)` parenthetical through verbatim into the single-string half. At that point every callsite in the corpus used informal "leading and trailing whitespace" wording, so the glossary was no worse than its peers. `f14a45e` ("empty-template / slash-argument whitespace set unpinned") then introduced the anchored `slash-argument whitespace` definition on `binder-bypass-and-envelope.md` and rewrote every other callsite — the two bypass clauses, the user-arguments line in the system-prompt MUSTs, and `SLSH-1` — to link to it. The glossary entry was not updated in that pass, leaving its bare parenthetical as the sole unlinked use. The defect is the *interaction*: the original parenthetical was acceptable at authoring time; the later precision-tightening commit raised the bar at every other site but did not propagate into the glossary.

## Solution approach

In `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md`, add a forward-link from the single-string clause's `(whitespace-trimmed)` parenthetical to the canonical `slash-argument-whitespace` definition on `binder/binder-bypass-and-envelope.md`, using the relative-link form the sibling callsites already use.

## Solution constraints

- Touch only the single-string clause of the combined entry; the no-params half performs no string trimming and must not gain a whitespace link.
- Do not restate the ASCII-set trim rule inline in the glossary — the canonical page owns it, and an inline duplicate is a drift hazard.
