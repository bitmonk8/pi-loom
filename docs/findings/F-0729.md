## Finding

The combined glossary entry **no-params bypass vs. single-string bypass** in `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` describes the single-string case as setting the param "to the entire slash-argument string (whitespace-trimmed) and skips the binder," but the bare parenthetical `(whitespace-trimmed)` is the only occurrence of this normalisation that does not link to its definition. Every other site in the spec corpus that mentions the same trim — the bypass rule itself, the system-prompt user-arguments line, and `SLSH-1` no-params overflow — links to the anchored definition `slash-argument whitespace` on `docs/spec_topics/binder/binder-bypass-and-envelope.md#slash-argument-whitespace`, which pins the trim set to ASCII whitespace (per System-note rendering rule 1) and explicitly excludes the regex `\s` class.

A reader who consults the glossary first therefore sees a normalisation term with two plausible interpretations (ASCII set vs. Unicode `\s`) and no cue that a normative pin exists elsewhere. Within the spec the descriptive-vs-canonical disclaimer at the top of `glossary.md` ("if a glossary entry and its canonical page disagree, the canonical page wins") covers correctness, but the glossary entry still fails the lighter clarity bar the sibling entries meet by routing each coined term through one explicit `See:`/inline link to its canonical anchor.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `b4fbfac` (2026-05-04), `9aeb318` (2026-05-05), `f14a45e` (2026-06-05)
**History:** The defect token entered as `(whitespace-trimmed)` in `b4fbfac` ("pi-loom spec: resolve 'no central glossary'"), which created the central `glossary.md` with a single combined `binder` / `binder bypass` entry. `9aeb318` ("Glossary entry for binder bypass omits the no-params case") split that entry into the present `binder` entry plus the combined `no-params bypass` vs. `single-string bypass` entry, carrying the bare `(whitespace-trimmed)` parenthetical through verbatim into the single-string half. At that point every callsite in the corpus used informal "leading and trailing whitespace" wording, so the glossary was no worse than its peers. `f14a45e` ("empty-template / slash-argument whitespace set unpinned") then introduced the anchored `slash-argument whitespace` definition on `binder-bypass-and-envelope.md` and rewrote every other callsite — the two bypass clauses, the user-arguments line in the system-prompt MUSTs, and `SLSH-1` — to link to it. The glossary entry was not updated in that pass, leaving its bare parenthetical as the sole unlinked use. The defect is the *interaction*: the original parenthetical was acceptable at authoring time; the later precision-tightening commit raised the bar at every other site but did not propagate into the glossary.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md`, replace the bare `(whitespace-trimmed)` in the `no-params bypass` vs. `single-string bypass` entry with a hyperlinked reference to the canonical anchor — e.g. "the runtime sets the param's value to the entire slash-argument string (with leading and trailing [slash-argument whitespace](./binder/binder-bypass-and-envelope.md#slash-argument-whitespace) trimmed) and skips the binder". Do not restate the ASCII-set rule inline; the canonical definition lives on the bypass page and a duplicated inline statement would be a future drift hazard.

Edge cases for the implementer:

- Use the relative path `./binder/binder-bypass-and-envelope.md#slash-argument-whitespace`, matching the link form already used by `slash-invocation.md` `SLSH-1` and by the bypass page's self-link from clause 5 of the system-prompt MUSTs.
- Touch only the single-string clause inside that combined entry. The leading "no-params bypass" half of the same entry does not perform any string trimming and must not gain a misleading whitespace link.
- The `binder` entry (the prior bullet) intentionally no longer mentions the trim — leave it alone.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader landing in the glossary entry first cannot tell whether non-ASCII whitespace (e.g. `U+00A0`) is trimmed without navigating to the bypass page. The risk of an actual divergent implementation is small (the canonical bypass page is unambiguous and is what implementers cite), but the glossary entry violates the corpus convention of always routing a coined term to its anchored definition.
