## Problem

The auxiliary transient-toast surface's drain-state-tag non-writer rule lives only on the diagnostics-shape page, not with the drain-state contract that owns the tag's write-site set. The contract's `*Fields.*` closure for `drainStateTag` ("never otherwise mutated") is normatively complete, but it does not name the toast surface — the most plausible accidental writer, run from watcher callbacks and invariant-check sites outside the teardown path — as a deliberate non-writer, and carries no back-reference to the explicit prohibition on the toast page. Discoverability is therefore one-directional: an implementer or auditor reading the contract to enumerate tag-write sites and verify the teardown short-circuit reaches only "never otherwise mutated", with no pointer to where the toast-surface write is forbidden and why it would let a later `session_shutdown` skip teardown.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** c2e278f — pi-loom spec-plan: fix F-1398 — pin per-arm post-failure disposition for transient toasts (2026-06-25, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** Commit c2e278f added the **Transient toasts (auxiliary)** paragraph's per-mode post-failure-state enumeration to `diagnostic-shape.md`, and with it the normative "None of the three arms writes the `LoomRegistry` drain-state tag …" sentence with a forward-link to `drain-state-contract.md`'s short-circuit predicate. The same commit did not add a reciprocal back-reference or a restated MUST NOT to `drain-state-contract.md`, so the prohibition has lived only in `diagnostic-shape.md` since it was authored. `drain-state-contract.md`'s own *Non-normative editorial convention* ("no other writer or reader of either field is permitted") was authored separately in commit 52b9101 (2026-05-11, "T24 — Fork-reason watcher closure …") and was not revised when c2e278f introduced the toast-paths rule.

## Solution approach

In `drain-state-contract.md`, at the `*Fields.*` "never otherwise mutated" closure for `drainStateTag`, add a clause naming the auxiliary transient-toast surface as a deliberate non-writer with a forward-cross-reference to `diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md#transient-toasts`, noting that a toast-surface write would satisfy the handler-entry short-circuit predicate (`snapshot.tag !== undefined`) and cause a later `session_shutdown` to skip teardown. This makes the cross-reference bidirectional — the toast paragraph already forward-links the contract — so a reader auditing tag-write sites from either page reaches the complete picture.

## Solution constraints

- The normative prohibition and the three-arm behavioural disposition (subsystem teardown states, `readDrainState` arm-(a) dispatchability, `/reload` recovery) remain single-sourced in `diagnostic-shape.md`; the contract clause is a back-reference plus the existing closure, not a relocated or duplicated copy of the prohibition.
