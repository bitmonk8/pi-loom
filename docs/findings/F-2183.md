## Finding

The INV-4 summary sentence reads: "The interpreter caps the nesting depth of an `invoke` chain at **32**, counting both direct `invoke(...)`, `.loom` callable calls through `tools:`, and cross-file `.warp` `fn` calls." The quantifier "both" promises a two-element list, but three coordinated items follow the comma. The sentence is self-contradictory on cardinality.

A reader resolving the mismatch in favour of "both" can group the three items into two — one compound item ("direct `invoke(...)` plus `.loom` callable calls through `tools:`") and one simple item ("cross-file `.warp` `fn` calls") — and may then infer that the compound pair counts as a single frame class or that only one of its two members is a countable frame. The intended reading is three independent, co-equal countable-frame classes, each contributing to the same per-chain counter.

The elaborating paragraph immediately below the summary states the three-way enumeration unambiguously ("a countable frame is any direct `invoke(...)` call, any `.loom` callable call dispatched through a `tools:` entry, or any cross-file `.warp` `fn` call"), so the corpus does disambiguate itself for a reader who continues. The defect is confined to the one summary sentence, where the word "both" is simply wrong for a three-item list.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** present-since-rule-introduction

INV-4 (the "Invocation depth bound" rule) first entered the spec in commit `833d8d5`, which added the sentence "counting both direct `invoke(...)`, registered-loom calls through `tools:`, and `.warp` `fn` invokes" — already a three-item list governed by "both". Walking the file's history (`git log --follow` across the `spec_topics/invocation.md` → `docs/spec_topics/invocation.md` rename at `31ff060`, plus per-commit `git show | grep`) confirms the parent commit `6750c81` did not contain the phrasing and every descendant does. Later edits relabelled the individual items (`registered-loom` → `.loom callable`, `.warp fn invokes` → `cross-file .warp fn calls`) but never reconciled the "both"-versus-three-items cardinality. The defect was born with the rule and has been carried unchanged ever since.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace "counting both direct `invoke(...)`, `.loom` callable calls through `tools:`, and cross-file `.warp` `fn` calls" with "counting all three of direct `invoke(...)` calls, `.loom` callable calls through `tools:`, and cross-file `.warp` `fn` calls" — or simply drop "both" so the sentence reads "counting direct `invoke(...)` calls, `.loom` callable calls through `tools:`, and cross-file `.warp` `fn` calls". Either form makes the summary agree with the three-way enumeration in the elaborating paragraph and the `V15b` leaf. Keep the parenthetical "(the count is per-chain, not per-process …)" unchanged.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The summary sentence is internally inconsistent on count, but the immediately following elaboration paragraph enumerates the three countable-frame classes unambiguously, so a reader who continues past the summary recovers the intended rule. No two reasonable implementers who read the full INV-4 entry would diverge; the defect is a wording slip with no behavioural observer.
