## Problem

Arm (1) of the *Lookup-failure-to-discriminator routing.* clause on `unknown-reason-rule.md` routes an uninitialised `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` (named as resolving to `undefined`) to the `"missing-entry"` discriminator and mandates that the handler guard the lookup with an explicit `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY === undefined` check before the iteration primitive. Under the runtime's pinned ES-modules-only model, that circular-init / live-binding-gap scenario cannot produce `=== undefined`: ESM bindings are live and TDZ-protected, so reading the `const`-shaped pinned-constants block before its initialiser runs throws `ReferenceError: Cannot access 'SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY' before initialization`, not the falsy value the guard tests for. The guard is therefore dead code, and the TDZ `ReferenceError` lands in arm (3) `"throw:<String(error)>"` — exactly the mis-routing the guard exists to prevent. The four-arm exhaustiveness claim and the `V9h` fixture floor for `"missing-entry"` rest on a premise the pinned ESM-only runtime cannot exhibit.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** indeterminate
**Introducing commits:** none identified with confidence
**History:** The *Lookup-failure-to-discriminator routing* clause and its `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY === undefined` guard mandate were authored in `75a9626` (2026-05-09, *"pi-loom spec: resolve 'SessionShutdownEvent.reason closed set has no build-time pin against the SDK type'"*) into the then-monolithic `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md`. Before that commit, the **Unknown-reason rule** paragraph existed but only covered the closed-set membership check and the `event.reason` read; the snapshot lookup, its four-arm discriminator routing, and the `=== undefined` guard did not exist. `75a9626` introduced both the four-arm enumeration and the guard sentence in the same diff hunk, so the defect (the mismatch between arm (1)'s premise and the runtime's pinned ESM module model) is present-since-inception of the routing clause itself rather than introduced by a later edit.

The defect is also `multi-commit-interaction`-shaped in a weaker sense: the ESM-only pin (`extension-esm-type-module`) was relocated and refined in commits prior to and after `75a9626` (e.g. `ce48e09` 2026-06-22 — *"fix F-0961 — relocate ESM type:module obligation to host-prerequisites"*), so the routing clause was authored against an ESM-only premise that has only hardened since. No single commit "introduces" the contradiction — `75a9626` introduces a routing whose arm (1) premise was already incompatible with the ESM pin at authoring time, and the ESM pin has only become more load-bearing since. Subsequent edits to `unknown-reason-rule.md` after the `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04) split (`a7d2a80`, `c326bfa`, `aa2f9d7`, `a37d4ef`, `a9fb344`, `8008b91`) refined the discriminator grammar, the `pinnedConstantReadOk` seam, sub-anchors, and the `"literals-shape-invalid"` four-sub-case fixture obligation, but none of them edited arm (1)'s premise text or the `=== undefined` guard mandate. Verdict left `indeterminate` because the "introducing commit" is the same commit that introduced the routing arm; there is no prior state in which the arm existed without the defect, so the defect is co-extensive with the clause rather than a regression.

## Solution approach

Rewrite arm (1)'s trigger in the *Lookup-failure-to-discriminator routing.* clause so it covers the ESM TDZ / circular-init / live-binding-gap case — a `ReferenceError` thrown by the bare-identifier read of `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` at handler entry — alongside the off-spec `undefined` read, keeping arm (1)'s `"missing-entry"` discriminator. Delete the `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY === undefined` guard mandate and replace it with a direction to route a caught `ReferenceError` from the bare-identifier read to `"missing-entry"` rather than to the `"throw:<String(error)>"` arm. Clarify the four-arm exhaustiveness sentence and PIC-47 sub-anchor (iii) to reflect the widened arm (1). Add a fixture under PIC-47 / `V9h-T` that drives a three-module ESM cycle into the pre-initialisation read and asserts `details.failure === "missing-entry"` with no `"throw:"` prefix.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: arm (3)'s `"throw:<String(error)>"` routing — a per-entry `.kind` or `.path` read that throws during iteration is not TDZ-class and MUST NOT collapse into `"missing-entry"`.
- Reuse the existing `"missing-entry"` / `"literals-shape-invalid"` / `"throw:<String(error)>"` discriminator literals (PIC-48 substring (d) anchor-stable contract surface); do not coin a new discriminator value, alter the `details.failure` TypeScript union, or edit the `diagnostics.md` row.
