## Problem

The *Stop-reason classification* section (`#stop-reason-classification` in `provider-error-mapping.md`) classifies an HTTP-200 terminator by the resolved `AssistantMessage.stopReason` value, relying on pi-ai surfacing length-truncation as `stopReason: "length"` and surfacing the other abnormal terminators it keys on as recognisable `stopReason` values. This is an unpinned pi-ai behavioural dependency with no editorial-review audit route: the *Editorial-review checklist for unpinned host presuppositions* in `version-bump-step2.md` has no item covering the stop-reason value space. Item (m) covers only the `stopReason: "error"` member; no item covers `"length"` or the abnormal-terminator value space. A future pi-ai change to how length-truncation or abnormal terminations surface would silently misclassify them with no audit signal.

## Solution approach

Add a routing sentence to the `#stop-reason-classification` section recording that its `stopReason` value-space reliance routes to editorial review on the same footing as the `#provider-overflow-wording-presupposition`. Add a corresponding item to the *Editorial-review checklist for unpinned host presuppositions* in `version-bump-step2.md` at the next free checklist letter, carrying the SHOULD-level audit obligation and escalation-to-build-time-pin clause its sibling items carry. Update the checklist preamble's item-range bound to the new upper letter.
