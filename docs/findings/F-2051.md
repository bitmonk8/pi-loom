## Finding

The glossary `caller` entry (`docs/spec_topics/glossary.md`, `<a id="caller">`) coins two disambiguated sub-terms and elevates them with a SHOULD: *"Authors and implementers SHOULD use the disambiguated terms `slash caller` and `invoke parent` whenever the bare word `caller` is ambiguous in context."* SHOULD-level guidance at corpus scope effectively promotes both to spec-coined terms, yet neither `slash caller` nor `invoke parent` has its own glossary heading, anchor, or index entry — they exist only as bolded inline fragments inside the `caller` entry's running prose.

Both terms are in fact reused on more than one page, several times with load-bearing normative weight: SLSH-3 (`slash-invocation.md`) hinges on "a slash caller and no invoke parent", the `display: true` cascade in `pi-integration-contract/runtime-event-channel.md` repeats the same condition, and `invoke parent` is a column header / cause-surface label in `errors-and-results/error-model.md`, `schema-subset.md`, and `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md`. This is exactly the reuse pattern the glossary preamble names as the trigger for a dedicated entry ("Add new entries here when the spec coins a new term that is reused on more than one page").

A reader encountering `slash caller` or `invoke parent` in body prose therefore has no direct glossary target. Where the prose links at all it points at `#caller` (e.g. SLSH-3's "see [Glossary — caller]"); the sub-terms are only discoverable by reading the `caller` entry in full, and several prose uses carry no link at all. The spec coins terms under a SHOULD that it does not register.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** d54b426 — pi-loom spec: resolve "Glossary cluster: six independent defects in spec_topics/glossary.md" (2026-05-07, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The `caller` entry's two-sense framing, the bolded `slash caller` / `invoke parent` sub-terms, and the SHOULD-use-the-disambiguated-terms sentence all entered together in d54b426; that commit authored the entry with the sub-terms inline and never gave them their own anchors. The defect has been present from the entry's first form (the only subsequent edit broadened "prompt-mode loom" to "in either prompt or subagent mode" and did not add anchors).

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Register both sub-terms with their own glossary anchors

- **Approach:** Give `slash caller` and `invoke parent` discoverable targets. Either add two slim alphabetical glossary entries (`<a id="slash-caller">**slash caller** — …`, `<a id="invoke-parent">**invoke parent** — …`), each a one-line reminder that cross-refers to the `caller` entry for the co-occurrence rule, or attach anchored sub-bullets (`<a id="slash-caller">`, `<a id="invoke-parent">`) to the two senses already inside the `caller` entry.
- **Spec edits:** `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` only — add the two anchors plus (for standalone entries) one descriptive line each. Optionally repoint existing prose links from `#caller` to the new sub-anchors where the prose names a specific sense.
- **Pros:** Keeps the SHOULD intact; matches the glossary's own "reused on more than one page → register it" convention; gives every prose use a direct target.
- **Cons:** Adds two entries/anchors to maintain; mild duplication with the `caller` entry's sense list.
- **Risks:** If standalone entries drift from the `caller` entry's co-occurrence rule, the "two senses cannot co-occur" invariant could be stated inconsistently — mitigate by making the sub-entries point at `#caller` for that rule rather than restating it.

### Option B — Demote the SHOULD to a non-normative parenthetical

- **Approach:** Reword the `caller` entry so it no longer issues SHOULD-level guidance to prefer the two terms; present them descriptively (e.g. "these two senses are referred to as the *slash caller* and *invoke parent*") so the spec is not coining registered vocabulary.
- **Spec edits:** `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` `caller` entry — replace the "SHOULD use the disambiguated terms" sentence with a descriptive aside.
- **Pros:** Removes the coin-without-register tension with no new anchors.
- **Cons:** Weakens guidance for terms that are already used with normative weight in SLSH-3 and the cascade condition; prose that already leans on the disambiguation loses its backing.
- **Risks:** Existing normative prose ("a slash caller and no invoke parent") would then rest on terms the glossary only mentions in passing, which is the weaker end-state.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A** and register `slash caller` and `invoke parent` as anchored sub-bullets within the `caller` entry (or slim standalone entries). The terms are already reused across `slash-invocation.md`, `runtime-event-channel.md`, `error-model.md`, `schema-subset.md`, and `ceilings-3-and-4.md` with normative effect, which is precisely the glossary's stated trigger for registration; demoting the SHOULD would undercut prose that already depends on them. Watch one edge case: the "two senses cannot co-occur for a single invocation" rule must live in exactly one place — keep it in the `caller` entry and have the sub-anchors defer to it rather than restating it.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader who meets `slash caller` or `invoke parent` in normative prose cannot jump to a definition; they must already know to open the `caller` entry. The terms still resolve with effort, so no implementation diverges — the cost is wayfinding friction and a SHOULD that coins vocabulary the glossary does not register.
