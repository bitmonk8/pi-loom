## Problem

PIC-12 carries a runtime `MUST NOT` â€” the runtime must not call `Date.now`, `performance.now`, `Date.prototype.getTime`, or the global `setTimeout` / `clearTimeout` outside the `WallClock` adapter â€” but the same sentence disclaims its only named enforcement (a build-time grep-test) as a non-normative implementation choice that is "not an observable conformance point." The MUST therefore stands with no normative acceptance criterion: no behavioural observation through `FakeClock`, no static-analysis obligation any conforming implementation must run, and no diagnostic that fires on violation. Because the `FakeClock` seam is what makes the watcher debounce, the `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS` await, the `looms.scanPackagesTimeoutMs` deadline math, and the `loop-iter` yield deterministically testable, a violation outside the adapter silently corrupts a timing assertion at a consuming test rather than surfacing at its own site. The defect is symmetrical with the `process.env.HOME` ban under PIC-13's `homedir()` surface, which carries the same self-disclaimed enforcement.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the closing sentence of the PIC-12 paragraph so the build-time check is a normative conformance criterion stated on a conforming implementation rather than a self-disclaimed implementation note: pin that such a check exists and what it must reject (references to the timing primitives outside the `WallClock` adapter), while leaving how the check is realised to the implementer. Apply the same acceptance-criterion shape to the parallel `process.env.HOME` ban under PIC-13's `homedir()` surface, or make PIC-12 the canonical statement and have PIC-13 cross-reference it so the two bans do not drift apart.

- Scope the criterion to direct lexical references only; indirect forms (aliased, destructured, computed, helper-wrapper, or re-export reads) remain conceded to plan-side review and out of scope for the spec criterion.
