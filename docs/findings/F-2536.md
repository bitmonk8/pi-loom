## Problem

The `surface-set closure` glossary entry (`docs/spec_topics/glossary.md`) states the property is enforced at loom 1.0 by the "positive-direction" SDK surface-inventory literal-read assertions. "Positive-direction" is load-bearing terminology: it distinguishes the literal-read assertions that confirm each inventory entry exists with the required shape from the negative-direction inventory-closure audit that scans the source tree for unlisted surfaces. The glossary entry uses the bare adjective with no inline gloss and no anchor to where the distinction is defined, and its lone `See:` link points at the audit's intro anchor rather than at the positive/negative contrast. The glossary's contract is definitional self-containment, so a reader cannot recover what makes the assertions "positive-direction" without hunting across the PIC pages.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 74633aa — pi-loom spec: reconcile inventory-closure audit apparatus to post-1.0 framing across 9 pages (2026-06-28, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The `surface-set closure` glossary entry was introduced at 589d9a4 (2026-06-05) and then read "The property the *inventory-closure audit* enforces" — with no "positive-direction" qualifier. Commit 74633aa (2026-06-28) re-attributed loom-1.0 closure enforcement from the audit to the step-2(a) literal-read assertions for the post-1.0 framing, introducing the phrase "positive-direction SDK surface-inventory literal-read assertions" into the entry without carrying in a co-located gloss or anchor for the new qualifier.

## Solution approach

Make the `positive-direction` qualifier self-explaining at its point of use in the `surface-set closure` glossary entry — either clarify it with an inline gloss that contrasts the literal-read assertions against the negative-direction inventory-closure audit, or add a forward-link to its defining site `version-bump-step2.md#bump-step-2-positive`.

## Solution constraints

- Reuse the canonical `positive-direction … literal-read assertions` / `negative-direction … audit` phrasing the PIC pages already use; do not coin a third phrasing for the distinction.
