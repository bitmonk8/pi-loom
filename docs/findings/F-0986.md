## Problem

The intro paragraph to `## loom 1.0 non-goals` in `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md` reads "a future revision adding the disposition is not anticipated by loom 1.0 and will require a migration." Two clauses earlier in the same sentence, "the disposition" denotes the scope decision itself, so "adding the disposition" parses as adding a new documented non-decision — an editorial change — which is the opposite of the intended reading. The intended reading, made explicit by each bullet's closing sentence (e.g. "A future per-loom capability model is not anticipated…"), is a future revision that adds the deferred capability.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** `0e12140` (2026-05-08, "pi-loom spec: resolve 'Non-goals are not consolidated into a single section'")
**History:** The phrase "a future revision adding the disposition is not anticipated by V1 and will require a migration" was introduced verbatim by commit `0e12140`, the same commit that created the consolidated V1 non-goals section in `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations.md` and the matching one-line aggregator in `docs/spec.md`. The same wording was carried unchanged through the V1 → loom 1.0 rename (`a6d82db`) and the `spec/` → `spec_topics/` collapse / file split (`81bf52a`, which moved the section into its own `model-changes-and-non-goals.md` topic file). `git log -L` over the paragraph and `git log -G "adding the disposition"` over `docs/` agree: the wording is original to the section and has never been revised on its merits. The defect is therefore best understood as a phrasing slip baked in at the moment the consolidation was authored rather than an artefact of later evolution.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the second clause of the intro paragraph's first sentence so the noun phrase names the deferred capability rather than reusing "the disposition" — e.g. "a future revision that adds the deferred capability is not anticipated by loom 1.0 and will require a migration" — mirroring the closing-sentence pattern each bullet already uses.
