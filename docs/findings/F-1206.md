## Finding

GOV-29 (cross-page canonical-arm uniqueness) closes with a worked example: *"The known instance at the time of these rules' authoring is `v1-non-goals` (present on both `docs/spec.md` and `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations.md`); future collisions are handled by the same rule without amendment."* Neither cited file carries the colliding alias anchor today: `docs/spec.md` is a 9-line table-of-contents stub, and `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations.md` is a 25-line hub. The two sites that actually carry byte-identical `<a id="v1-non-goals">` anchors live one directory below them: the normative-owner site at `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md` (line 19, paired with the bare canonical arm `loom-1-0-non-goals`) and the aggregator copy at `docs/spec_topics/language-and-architecture.md` (line 5, paired with the disambiguated canonical arm `loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator`).

The miscitation is the only thing wrong with GOV-29 here — the rule's prescribed disambiguation (bare canonical arm on the normative-owner site, `-aggregator`-tailed arm on the secondary site) is in fact correctly applied at the two real sites. But because the example is the reader's anchor for validating the rule against the corpus, citing files that do not carry the collision invites the reader to either conclude the example is fictional, hunt for the collision in the wrong files, or copy the stale citation forward into derivative material. The defect surface is the worked example only; no normative obligation under GOV-29 needs to move.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Update GOV-29's closing sentence on `docs/spec_topics/governance/release-version-naming.md` so the parenthetical cites the two files that actually carry the `v1-non-goals` collision under the current layout. Replace:

> The known instance at the time of these rules' authoring is `v1-non-goals` (present on both `docs/spec.md` and `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations.md`); future collisions are handled by the same rule without amendment.

with text naming `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md` (the normative-owner site that retains the bare canonical arm `loom-1-0-non-goals` per GOV-29) and `docs/spec_topics/language-and-architecture.md` (the aggregator site that authors the site-distinguishing canonical arm `loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator`). The example may additionally name the disambiguated canonical arms each site carries — making the example a self-contained walkthrough of the rule's application rather than only a citation to two paths — and may drop the *"at the time of these rules' authoring"* clause (no longer load-bearing once the example tracks the current layout). The "future collisions are handled by the same rule without amendment" sentence remains.

Edge cases to watch:

- A second live collision under GOV-29 exists at `tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact` (see *Related Findings* below). Do not silently fold it into the GOV-29 example by listing it as a second "known instance" — that overlaps with the separate finding that rejects the duplication outright, which would have the aggregator-side anchor disambiguated rather than indexed under GOV-29.
- Do not introduce a third anchor arm or rename either live anchor while editing the example — both arms are pinned by GOV-26 (alias permanence) and GOV-29 itself; the only edit needed here is the prose citation in `release-version-naming.md`.
- The two anchor sites are on different topic pages but share the orientation-aggregator-vs-normative-owner relationship GOV-30 pins. Keep the example wording consistent with GOV-30's terminology (*"normative-owner site"* and *"aggregator site"*) so the GOV-29 example reads as a worked instance of GOV-30's lock-step convention.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The normative rule (canonical-arm uniqueness with bare-arm-on-owner / tailed-arm-on-aggregator disambiguation) is intact and is correctly applied at the live collision sites. The stale example degrades the rule's pedagogical surface — a reader who follows the citation to verify the rule against the corpus finds nothing, and a future contributor invoking GOV-29 on a new collision has no working model to copy. No implementer can derive a wrong runtime behaviour from the defect, but reviewers cannot use the example as a self-check.
