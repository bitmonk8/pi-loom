## Problem

The *Deliberate deviation from Pi's bundled-package convention* sub-paragraph of `host-prerequisites.md` §`#pi-sdk-pin` justifies pinning the four `@earendil-works/*` peers at the tilde range instead of Pi's `"*"` convention on a single behavioural claim: that pnpm (default `node-linker=isolated`) and yarn do not auto-deduplicate transitive peer-dep ranges and therefore fail the install loudly on minor skew. The same claim recurs in the "belt-and-braces" sentence of the item-1 lock-step paragraph and is the only load-bearing benefit cited for accepting the deviation's documented alignment cost. The claim carries no citation, no pnpm/yarn version or mode qualifier, and no fixture or build-time regression test, so if either tool dedupes more aggressively than asserted the promised install-time skew-detection never materialises and the deviation buys nothing in exchange for the alignment cost it documents. The deviation's other gates — the Step 0 (d) runtime peer-dep probe and the build-time `peerDependencies` literal-read assertion — are positioned as consequences of the four-anchor invariant, not as substitutes for the rationale, so the rationale paragraph can neither be deleted nor stand unverified.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the load-bearing package-manager-behaviour sentence in the *Deliberate deviation from Pi's bundled-package convention* sub-paragraph of `#pi-sdk-pin` to demote install-time package-manager enforcement to a non-load-bearing observation, and foreground the four-anchor invariant — the property the build-time `peerDependencies` literal-read assertion ([`version-bump-intro.md` step 2](./version-bump-intro.md#pi-version-bump-procedure)) and the Step 0 (d) runtime peer-dep probe ([`capability-probe.md`](./capability-probe.md#entry-capability-probe)) are built around — as the deviation's load-bearing benefit. Demote the parallel PM-behaviour claim in the "belt-and-braces" sentence of the item-1 lock-step paragraph in the same edit so the framing is consistent across the page.
