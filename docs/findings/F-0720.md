## Problem

The `**Loom-package implementation dependencies (loom 1.0).**` bullet in `implementation-notes.md` (anchors `loom-package-implementation-dependencies-loom-1-0` / `loom-package-implementation-dependencies-v1`) attaches a per-package normative carve-out to each of `chokidar` and `yaml`, asserting that each package is out of scope for the inventory-closure audit's *Target surface categories* and needs no `// allow-pi-surface:` exemption marker. That consequence is already fixed by the general rule at the audit's `#audit-target-surface-categories` anchor in `audit-recognised-shapes.md`, where the in-scope categories are closed at the four `@earendil-works/*` peer packages plus `typebox`; any other package is out of scope by construction. The per-package paragraphs re-derive that consequence by hand with near-verbatim duplicated prose, so each new runtime dependency pays a spec-edit tax to attach derivable boilerplate and the duplicated paragraphs form a drift surface.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `4cfe858` (2026-06-04, *pi-loom spec: resolve "chokidar named but never declared as a dependency"*) — introduced the original `chokidar` dependency paragraph in `pi-integration-contract.md`, including the first per-package "out of scope … no `// allow-pi-surface:` marker" sentence. The carve-out language was authored in the same paragraph as the dependency declaration rather than as a cross-reference to the general scope rule.
- `1ea0455` (2026-06-04, *pi-loom spec: resolve "DI-seam vs reference-implementation placement boundary"*) — moved the paragraph (with its per-package carve-out shape unchanged) from `pi-integration-contract.md` into `implementation-notes.md` under the loom-package-implementation-dependencies anchor, locking in the structure on the implementer-hints page.
- `1064946` (2026-06-10, *pi-loom plan: resolve "YAML frontmatter parsing mechanism is never declared as a dependency"*) — added the `yaml` dependency by copy-pasting the `chokidar` paragraph's shape, including a near-verbatim duplicate of the carve-out sentence ("Like `chokidar`, `yaml` is neither one of the four `@earendil-works/*` peer packages nor `typebox`…"). This is the multi-commit-interaction marker: the first commit established the per-package-named pattern; the third commit demonstrated the foreseeable maintenance tax by reproducing it on the next dependency that came up.

**History:** `git log -S "is out of scope for the [inventory-closure audit"` on `docs/spec_topics/` returns exactly the three commits above. `git log -S "chokidar" -- docs/spec_topics/implementation-notes.md` corroborates the move-in-place at `1ea0455` and the no-touch propagation at `1064946`. The defect is not attributable to any single commit: `4cfe858` chose a defensible local framing in a single-package context, and only the `1064946` duplication made the per-package shape's cost visible. The durable fix is to address the *pattern* (collapse the per-package claims into a single cross-reference to the existing general rule), not to revert any commit.

## Solution approach

Delete the two per-package "out of scope … no `// allow-pi-surface:` marker" sentences (the `chokidar` and `yaml` ones) from the `loom-package-implementation-dependencies-loom-1-0` bullet. Replace them with a single cross-reference sentence that points at `#audit-target-surface-categories` in `audit-recognised-shapes.md` and states the audit scope as a positive per-category rule: a runtime dependency outside the enumerated categories needs no exemption marker. The dependency declarations themselves (`semver`, `chokidar`, `yaml`, their roles, and `@types/*` notes) stay.

## Solution constraints

- Preserve both `loom-package-implementation-dependencies-loom-1-0` and `loom-package-implementation-dependencies-v1` anchors verbatim — citations from H1a, V6a, and other leaves resolve against them.
- The replacement cross-reference must name the category set via the `#audit-target-surface-categories` anchor, not enumerate it inline; inline enumeration re-creates the drift surface this finding removes.
