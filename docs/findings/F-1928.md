## Finding

`V9g` bounds its `session_shutdown` sub-step-3 `Promise.allSettled` over every in-flight entry's `disposeBarrier` by `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS`, and both `V9g` and `V9g-T` assert that `loom/runtime/reload-teardown-timeout` "fires at the cap". A deterministic at-cap assertion is a time-bounded await whose timeout must be made to elapse on demand; under real wall-clock time it is either flaky or slow, and a raw `setTimeout`/`Date.now` implementation violates the *No globals, statics, singletons* ambient-timing ban in `conventions.md` (enforced by the `H3a` identifier-keyed scan). The only project-sanctioned way to drive a cap deterministically is the injected `Clock` seam owned by `V8d`, advanced through its `FakeClock` test double.

Neither leaf names that seam. `V9g` Deps are `V9g-T, V9a, V9e, V9h, V17a`; `V9g-T` Deps are `V9e, V9h, V17a` — `V8d` appears in neither, and neither leaf's Tests/Adds prose states the at-cap race is driven through `FakeClock`. This diverges from every analogous time-bounded leaf: `V10d` (250 ms reload debounce) and `V10b` (per-read deadline) both list `V8d` directly in their Deps and state the timing is driven through the `FakeClock` seam (`Clock.setTimeout`/`Clock.clearTimeout`, advancing virtual time). `V17a`, which owns the cancellation-runtime *semantics* of `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS`, likewise does not list `V8d`. The `Clock` seam is reachable for `V9g` only incidentally — `V9e` (a direct dep) lists `V8d` — so pickability is not blocked, but nothing in `V9g`/`V9g-T` tells the implementer to enforce or exercise the cap through it.

With the mechanism unpinned, two reasonable implementers diverge: one drives the at-cap assertion through `FakeClock` (matching the `V10b`/`V10d` convention); another reaches for a raw `setTimeout` and real elapsed time, producing a flaky test that also trips the ambient-timing architectural gate.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** `c6a664e` (2026-06-10) — `pi-loom plan: build/update plan for spec.md + review`
**History:** `V9g`/`V9g-T` were authored in the initial plan-build commit `c6a664e` already carrying the `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS` cap and the "fires at the cap" assertion, with Deps `V9g-T, V9e, V9h, V17a` (impl) and `V9e, V9h, V17a` (tests) — `V8d` absent from the first version. The later edit `a6e2ecb` added the `V9a` Deps edge but never the `Clock` seam. The defect — a deterministic cap assertion with no enumerated `Clock` seam and no `FakeClock` test-mechanism statement — has been present since the leaf pair first existed; it was not introduced by any later interaction.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add `V8d` to the Deps of both `docs/plan_topics/V9g-session-shutdown.md` (`**Deps.** V9g-T, V9a, V9e, V9h, V17a, V8d`) and `docs/plan_topics/V9g-T-session-shutdown.md` (`**Deps.** V9e, V9h, V17a, V8d`), matching how `V10d`/`V10b`/`V9e` cite the seam directly rather than relying on the incidental `V9e → V8d` path.

State, on the `reload-teardown-timeout` / sub-step-3 Tests bullet in `V9g-T` (and mirrored in `V9g`), that the await cap is driven through the injected `Clock` seam's `FakeClock` test double — advancing virtual time so the `Promise.allSettled` exceeds `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS` deterministically and `loom/runtime/reload-teardown-timeout` fires at the cap — mirroring the `V10d` reload-debounce wording (`Clock.setTimeout`/`Clock.clearTimeout`, deterministic virtual-time advance). This keeps the cap enforcement off raw ambient timing, consistent with the `conventions.md` No-globals ambient-timing ban.

If, on inspection, the intended design enforces the cap without the `Clock` seam, state in `V9g` how the cap is enforced and tested deterministically without ambient timing instead.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

The "fires at the cap" assertion has no enumerated deterministic mechanism, so implementers diverge: a `FakeClock`-driven build (correct, matching `V10b`/`V10d`) versus a raw-`setTimeout`/real-time build that is flaky and trips the `H3a` ambient-timing gate. The cap behaviour can ship under-verified or red the architectural lint depending on which reading the implementer picks.
