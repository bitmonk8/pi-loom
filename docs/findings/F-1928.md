## Problem

`V9g` bounds its `session_shutdown` sub-step-3 `Promise.allSettled` by `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS`, and both `V9g` and `V9g-T` assert that `loom/runtime/reload-teardown-timeout` "fires at the cap" ŌĆö a time-bounded await that needs a deterministic way to make the cap elapse on demand. Neither leaf names the injected `Clock` seam owned by `V8d` (advanced via its `FakeClock` double): `V8d` is absent from both Deps fields, and no Tests bullet states the at-cap race is driven through `FakeClock`. This diverges from the analogous time-bounded leaves `V10b` and `V10d`, which list `V8d` directly and state the timing is driven through the seam. With the mechanism unpinned, an implementer may instead reach for a raw `setTimeout`/real-time build that is flaky and trips the `conventions.md` No-globals ambient-timing ban (the `H3a` scan).

## Solution approach

Add `V8d` to the Deps field of both `docs/plan_topics/V9g-session-shutdown.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V9g-T-session-shutdown.md`, matching how `V10b`/`V10d`/`V9e` cite the seam directly rather than relying on the incidental `V9e ŌåÆ V8d` path. On the `reload-teardown-timeout` / sub-step-3 Tests bullet in `V9g-T` (mirrored in `V9g`), state that the await cap is exercised through the injected `Clock` seam's `FakeClock` double ŌĆö advancing virtual time so the `Promise.allSettled` exceeds `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS` deterministically. If inspection shows the intended design enforces the cap without the `Clock` seam, instead state in `V9g` how the cap is enforced and tested deterministically without ambient timing.

## Solution constraints
