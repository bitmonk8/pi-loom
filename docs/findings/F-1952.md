## Finding

`V18c` Adds closes with: "…the contributor's editorial-review checklist (items `(a)`–`(al)`): a manual per-bump audit of each host presupposition against the candidate Pi minor, MUST-level for `(a)`–`(e)` and SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record for `(f)`–`(al)`." The clause "MUST-level for `(a)`–`(e)` and SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record for `(f)`–`(al)`" assigns a normative severity classification to the checklist items.

Per `conventions.md` §Leaf format, the `Adds.` field is **descriptive by default** — its prose "does not, on its own, bind the implementer"; the binding obligations live in the `Tests.` REQ-ID bullets and the `Ships when` gate. V18c's `Ships when` already states the same classification authoritatively and in more precise terms: "no MUST-level item `(a)`–`(e)` carries a `fail` outcome … a `fail` on any MUST-level item `(a)`–`(e)` blocks the bump, whereas a recorded `fail` / `not-performed` on a SHOULD-level item `(f)`–`(al)` is a conformant non-blocking outcome," with the spec source `version-bump-step2.md` cited.

The same MUST/SHOULD partition therefore appears in two fields of one leaf: once non-normatively in Adds, once normatively in Ships-when. The two can silently drift on future edits (the gating prose has already moved once — the blocking distinction was added to Ships-when after the severity preview already existed in Adds), and a reader scanning Adds first sees a normative-looking severity assignment in a field the convention declares non-binding.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `f1cda48` ("resolve 'V18c mislabels editorial-review checklist as a static build-time gate'", 2026-06-11); `828b7d3` ("resolve 'V18c editorial-checklist MUST/SHOULD blocking distinction missing from exit gate'", 2026-06-16)
**History:** `git log -S` over `docs/plan_topics/V18c-version-bump-checklist.md` locates the severity-classification phrase ("MUST-level for `(a)`–`(e)` and SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record for `(f)`–`(al)`") entering the **Adds** field in `f1cda48`, when the editorial-review checklist was split out of the build-time gates. At that point Ships-when required only per-item outcome recording, not a severity split, so no duplication existed. The duplication arose in `828b7d3`, which added the authoritative MUST/SHOULD blocking distinction ("a `fail` on any MUST-level item `(a)`–`(e)` blocks the bump…") to **Ships when** — fully restating in the normative field the classification that already lived in the descriptive Adds field. Neither commit alone is the defect; the interaction of the two produced the duplicate.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V18c-version-bump-checklist.md`, edit the final sentence of the **Adds** field to drop the severity classification, leaving Adds as a non-normative pointer to the checklist's existence. Strike the clause `, MUST-level for `(a)`–`(e)` and SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record for `(f)`–`(al)`` so the sentence ends after "a manual per-bump audit of each host presupposition against the candidate Pi minor." The MUST/SHOULD severity split and the blocking-vs-non-blocking distinction remain solely in the **Ships when** gate (and its cited source `version-bump-step2.md`), which already state them in full.

Leave the **Ships when** text unchanged. Do not edit the spec — `version-bump-step2.md` is the canonical source and stays read-only for this fix.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Nothing currently breaks: the convention resolves authority in Ships-when's favour, so no implementer diverges today. The defect is duplication of a normative classification into a non-normative field, which risks the two copies drifting apart on a later edit and presents a severity assignment in a field the project declares non-binding.
