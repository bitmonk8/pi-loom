## Problem

The closed-set closure governing which diagnostics the `session_shutdown` teardown handler may emit — the five permitted codes plus the MUST-NOT *"No other diagnostic code may be emitted from this handler"* — sits at the tail of an unanchored Edge-cases prose paragraph in `active-invocation-registry.md`, with no dedicated section anchor of its own. The per-code registry pages that own those codes (`code-registry-host.md`'s four `loom/host/session-shutdown-*` rows and `code-registry-runtime.md`'s `loom/runtime/reload-teardown-timeout` row) carry no back-reference to a single authoritative allowlist, and `diagnostic-shape.md` enumerates the same five codes around the `console.error` delivery sink rather than the closure. The negative-closure conformance obligation therefore exists at exactly one buried, unanchored site, creating discoverability and drift risk when the teardown diagnostic set evolves or a conformance test needs a normative citation.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-commit
**Introducing commit:** `9559e19` ("pi-loom spec: resolve \"session_shutdown unknown reason: no closed-set validation, no fail-safe\"", 2026-05-07)
**History:** `git log -S "No other diagnostic code may be emitted from this handler"` first introduces the closure at `9559e19`, inside the then-monolithic `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md`, where it was authored as the tail sentence of the Edge-cases prose paragraph and listed two codes. It was authored buried — with no dedicated anchor and no mirror in the diagnostics pages — from inception. Subsequent commits grew the list (`14424b4` reached the present count) and the corpus-split commit `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04) relocated the paragraph verbatim into `active-invocation-registry.md`; neither changed the burial. The placement defect is therefore co-extensive with the closure's original introduction, not a product of the split.

## Solution approach

Lift the closed-set closure out of the Edge-cases bullet in `active-invocation-registry.md` into a dedicated normative statement carrying its own stable HTML anchor, listing the five permitted codes and stating the MUST-NOT. Add forward-links to that anchor from each of the four `loom/host/session-shutdown-*` rows in `code-registry-host.md` and the `loom/runtime/reload-teardown-timeout` row in `code-registry-runtime.md`, and from `diagnostic-shape.md`'s "five carved-out exceptions" enumeration so it cites the closure as the authoritative closed set rather than restating it.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the `console.error`-sink and `sendSystemNote`-prohibition prose in the Edge-cases bullet (owned by the active-invocation-registry over-prescription same-cluster finding) — only the allowlist closure relocates.
- `loom/runtime/cancelled-by-session-shutdown` is emitted from the per-invocation `finally`, not the teardown handler, and MUST stay outside the allowlist.
