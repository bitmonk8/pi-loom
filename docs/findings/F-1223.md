## Finding

PIC-2 (`<a id="pic-2"></a>`, *Prompt-mode sequential execution*) is authored as the final paragraph of `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/subagent.md`, appended after PIC-22 and the `concurrent-invocation-isolation` block. Its normative subject is prompt mode: the non-overlap of `pi.setActiveTools` snapshot/restore windows held by prompt-mode bodies within a single user session. The rule's premises also live elsewhere — Pi's per-session slash-handler serialisation at `tool-registration-lifetime.md#snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions`, the load-time rejection of prompt-mode `tools:` callees in `frontmatter-fields-a.md`, and the parent-suspending `invoke(...)` rule in `invocation.md`. The only subagent-mode mention is a negative delegation back to `concurrent-invocation-isolation` above it.

The shard's filename and surrounding content define `subagent.md` as the integration-contract page for subagent-mode isolation and lifecycle. PIC-2 therefore sits in a file whose subject does not bracket it: a reader scanning the subagent shard hits a prompt-mode lifecycle rule out of nowhere, while a reader on the prompt-mode driver pages (`conversation-drive.md`, `tool-registration-lifetime.md`) finds nothing at the natural location and must follow cross-references back to the subagent shard. The page-level `Contents` of `pi-integration-contract.md` lists topic pages (not REQ-IDs), so the discoverability gap is structural rather than a TOC-attribution mismatch, but the effect on a reader navigating top-down is the same.

The `pic-2` anchor is stable and every inbound citation (`session-model-and-appendix.md` §SM-7c, `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` §GOV-30, the plan leaves `V9c`/`V9c-T`/`V15d`/`V15d-T`) resolves through it, so this is not a broken-link defect; it is a placement defect.

**Pros.**

**Risks.**

### Recommendation

Take **Option A** (relocate to `tool-registration-lifetime.md`).

PIC-2 is explicitly a *derived* property of the snapshot/restore protocol — its body states this directly, citing `snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions` and PIC-17/PIC-19 as the premises it composes. Adjacency to the premise is the dominant maintenance concern: when the snapshot/restore contract is touched (e.g. the recovery-mutex carve-out in the same shard), PIC-2 must be re-read for consistency, and a same-file scroll is the cheapest way to enforce that. The size concern on `tool-registration-lifetime.md` is real but secondary; PIC-2 is a single paragraph and does not push the shard near the size cap. PIC-18 on `conversation-drive.md` is about cancel-forwarding, not about prompt-mode body non-overlap, so Option B's adjacency claim is weaker than it first appears.

Implementer notes:

- Preserve the `pic-2` anchor string verbatim across the move. Every inbound citation resolves through it (the SM-7c digest, the GOV-30 forward-link inventory, the V9c plan leaves) and the short-id is also cited by V15d/V15d-T as "`PIC-2` cross-body non-overlap (owned by `V9c`)".
- The Pi behavioural preconditions paragraph on `tool-registration-lifetime.md` is large and already discusses prompt-mode `invoke(...)` LIFO nesting in its closing sentences; PIC-2 reads naturally as the *normative statement* of the conclusion that paragraph's closing sentences derive informally. Insert directly after that block.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Inbound citations resolve correctly via the stable `pic-2` anchor, so no implementer is blocked. The cost is navigational: a reader doing top-down reading of `subagent.md` hits an out-of-subject prompt-mode rule, and a reader looking for prompt-mode body-lifecycle obligations on the natural shards (`conversation-drive.md`, `tool-registration-lifetime.md`) finds only cross-references back. Maintenance cost rises because edits to the snapshot/restore protocol on `tool-registration-lifetime.md` (PIC-17/PIC-19/PIC-8), from which PIC-2 is derived, must cross-edit a different file.
