## Problem

The glossary entry for `caller` (anchor `id="caller"` in `glossary.md`) admits the term is used in two distinct senses — *slash caller* (the user whose slash-command turn dispatched a loom) and *invoke parent* (the loom or `.warp` `fn` whose `invoke(...)` call spawned the current loom) — and then defers resolution to a SHOULD-level author discipline ("Authors and implementers SHOULD use the disambiguated terms `slash caller` and `invoke parent` whenever the bare word `caller` is ambiguous in context"). A glossary entry whose body recommends using *other* terms instead of defining its head term inverts the glossary's role as the canonical-term page. The bare word `caller` is then used across the corpus in both senses — and in a third *Pi session* quasi-sense (`frontmatter-fields-a.md` `model` row, `binder-model-and-context.md`'s `caller-session`) — without enforcement.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (of the entry)
**Introducing commit:** `d54b426` (2026-05-07) "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Glossary cluster: six independent defects in `spec_topics/glossary.md`\""
**History:** No glossary entry for `caller` existed before `d54b426`; that commit's G6 sub-fix introduced the entry verbatim with the two-sense framing and the SHOULD-level "Authors and implementers SHOULD use the disambiguated terms" workaround. The only later edit to the entry, `0033898` (2026-06-09, "subagent-mode top-level Err at slash boundary unsurfaced"), widened the *slash caller* sense from "a `prompt`-mode loom" to "a loom (in either prompt or subagent mode)" — that edit preserved the SHOULD wording unchanged. The collision-acknowledged-but-not-resolved shape has therefore been present since the entry first existed.

## Solution approach

Restructure the `caller` glossary entry (`id="caller"` in `glossary.md`) from a SHOULD-level workaround into a disambiguation pointer, and add standalone glossary entries for `slash caller` and `invoke parent`, each with its own `See:` link. Rewrite the bare `caller` uses across `invocation.md`, `overview.md`, `errors-and-results/error-model.md`, `subagent.md`, and `binder-model-and-context.md` to the disambiguated forms, resolving the *Pi session* quasi-sense uses (the `frontmatter-fields-a.md` `model` row and `binder-model-and-context.md`'s `caller-session`) to *Pi session* rather than a third glossary sense. Narrow the `operator` entry's parenthetical (`id="operator"`) to a `caller` cross-reference.
