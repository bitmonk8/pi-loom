## Finding

The glossary entry for `caller` (`docs/spec_topics/glossary.md`, line 13) explicitly tells the reader that the term is used in two distinct senses — *slash caller* (the user whose slash-command turn dispatched a loom) and *invoke parent* (the loom or `.warp` `fn` whose `invoke(...)` or `.loom`-callable call spawned the current loom) — and then defers resolution to an author-side discipline: "Authors and implementers SHOULD use the disambiguated terms `slash caller` and `invoke parent` whenever the bare word `caller` is ambiguous in context." The collision is acknowledged on the canonical page and pushed back into prose, not resolved.

The bare word `caller` is then used across the spec corpus in both senses without enforcement. `invocation.md` uses `caller` consistently for invoke-parent contexts (the cross-mode matrix rows; "the caller's current conversation"; "the caller's settings are not inherited"); `slash-invocation.md` introduces the variant `slash caller` (per the SHOULD); `errors-and-results/error-model.md` mixes both — the failure-cause table uses the column header *Slash caller (user-visible)* alongside *invoke parent*, but neighbouring prose says "the caller observes only the `Err` envelope" where `caller` is whichever of the two the cause routes to. `binder-model-and-context.md` uses `caller-session` for the user session, `frontmatter-fields-a.md` says "the loom inherits the caller's model" (which means the *Pi session* — neither of the two glossary senses). The glossary entry's own *See* points at the two normative pages, but neither of those pages enforces the disambiguated forms either, so a reader resolving `caller` by following the link does not land somewhere stricter.

A glossary entry whose body is a recommendation is upside-down: the glossary is the canonical-term page, so an entry that says "use *these other* terms instead" is admitting that the head term should not be a glossary entry at all but a disambiguation pointer to two glossary entries that do exist on their own (`slash caller`, `invoke parent`). The current entry achieves the worst of both shapes — it keeps `caller` admissible as a glossary term while undermining its own authority.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (of the entry)
**Introducing commit:** `d54b426` (2026-05-07) "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Glossary cluster: six independent defects in `spec_topics/glossary.md`\""
**History:** No glossary entry for `caller` existed before `d54b426`; that commit's G6 sub-fix introduced the entry verbatim with the two-sense framing and the SHOULD-level "Authors and implementers SHOULD use the disambiguated terms" workaround. The only later edit to the entry, `0033898` (2026-06-09, "subagent-mode top-level Err at slash boundary unsurfaced"), widened the *slash caller* sense from "a `prompt`-mode loom" to "a loom (in either prompt or subagent mode)" — that edit preserved the SHOULD wording unchanged. The collision-acknowledged-but-not-resolved shape has therefore been present since the entry first existed.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Replace the entry with a pure disambiguation pointer; sweep call sites

**Approach.** Add standalone glossary entries `slash caller` and `invoke parent` (each with its own canonical definition and `See:` link). Replace the current `caller` entry's body with one sentence that says `caller` is not a coined term: it is shorthand for one of the two entries below, and spec prose MUST use the disambiguated form except where the context names both (e.g. a sentence true of both, such as "the caller observes the `Err` envelope" in `error-model.md`, which can keep `caller` provided the surrounding paragraph has just enumerated both senses). Sweep `invocation.md`, `overview.md`, `error-model.md`, `subagent.md`, and `binder-model-and-context.md` so every bare `caller` either becomes one of the disambiguated terms or is justified by a both-senses-apply context. Leave the third-sense uses (`caller-session`, `caller's model` denoting the Pi session) to a separate cleanup — they are not what this finding is about.

**Spec edits.** Three glossary entries (`caller` becomes a disambiguator; two new entries are added — `slash caller`, `invoke parent`); approximately 15–25 prose rewrites across the five files listed above; the `operator` entry's parenthetical loses its embedded redefinition.

**Pros.** Removes the upside-down-glossary problem; makes the SHOULD enforceable as a MUST and gives a future grep gate (`grep -nE '\bcaller\b' docs/spec_topics`) something to check against; aligns with the existing precedent in `pi-integration-contract/runtime-event-channel.md` and `slash-invocation.md` SLSH-3 that already write `slash caller and no invoke parent` in full.

**Cons.** Largest edit footprint of the three options; the "both senses apply" carve-out for sentences like "the caller observes the `Err` envelope" is a judgement call that re-introduces a small disciplinary surface.

**Risks.** A sweep that misses one ambiguous site leaves the corpus in a worse place than today (a MUST with admitted violations). Mitigated by a one-shot grep over `docs/spec_topics/**` after the sweep.

### Option B — Strengthen the SHOULD to MUST without restructuring the entry

**Approach.** Keep the current entry's two-sense framing, but change "SHOULD use" to "MUST use" and add a one-sentence carve-out for sentences true of both senses. Sweep only the call sites where bare `caller` is genuinely ambiguous.

**Spec edits.** One-word change in `glossary.md`; ~5–8 prose rewrites at the ambiguous sites.

**Pros.** Minimal footprint; preserves the canonical entry shape; lower risk of sweep-miss.

**Cons.** Does not fix the upside-down-glossary problem — the canonical entry for `caller` still tells the reader to prefer other terms; `slash caller` and `invoke parent` remain pseudo-entries inside the `caller` body rather than top-level glossary entries one can link to via `glossary.md#slash-caller`; the linkability gap matters because several non-glossary pages would otherwise be able to cross-reference the disambiguated terms directly.

**Risks.** A future "MUST" with no anchor to point at for each sense is hard to enforce; the next reviewer hits the same finding under a different wording.

### Option C — Accept the current entry; downgrade the finding

**Approach.** Treat the SHOULD-level guidance as adequate. No edits.

**Pros.** Zero cost.

**Cons.** Leaves a glossary entry that admits ambiguity as the canonical term page; the next sweep-style review re-discovers this finding; the precedent of canonical-term entries that defer to other terms weakens later naming-lens findings against similar drift (e.g. the sibling `loom` / `Loom` finding in this same review).

**Risks.** None operationally, but the finding is a recurring-review attractor.

### Recommendation

**Option A.** The cost of the sweep is bounded (five files, low-tens of edits) and the structural win is what the finding is really asking for: a glossary in which the canonical-term page actually owns the canonical term, with linkable anchors per sense. The precedent already exists in the corpus (`runtime-event-channel.md`, SLSH-3) and the `errors-and-results/error-model.md` failure-table column header already uses *Slash caller* in full, so the disambiguated forms are not new vocabulary — Option A is mostly hoisting that discipline into the glossary and propagating it back into the prose that lags.

**Edge cases for the implementer.**
- The sentences "the caller observes only the `Err` envelope" under *Failure* and *Cancelled* in `error-model.md` are true under both senses; rewriting them as "the *slash caller* and the *invoke parent* each observe only the `Err` envelope" is more accurate than collapsing to one term. Leaving bare `caller` here is also defensible **iff** the surrounding `Per-cause caller surfaces` table has just enumerated both — the carve-out in Option A's body covers exactly this case.
- The bare `caller` in `frontmatter-fields-a.md` `model` row ("the loom inherits the caller's model") and the `caller-session` token in `binder-model-and-context.md` denote the *Pi session* (the slash caller's session, but at a layer below the user), not either of the two glossary senses; these are a third quasi-sense and are best resolved by replacing them with *Pi session* / *Pi session's model* rather than by either of the two disambiguators. Flag these in the sweep but resolve them as the obvious local rewrite rather than by inventing a third glossary entry.
- The `operator` entry's parenthetical "(which may be another loom via `invoke(...)` or a Pi prompt-mode user turn)" embeds a definition of `caller` that the standalone entries will subsume; reduce it to a `[caller](#caller)` cross-reference.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The spec is internally coherent in context — at every concrete use site one of the two senses is determinable by the surrounding paragraph — so an implementer does not actually diverge. The cost is reader friction: the canonical-term page admits ambiguity and then defers, which both weakens the glossary's authority and makes spec-prose drift (the `caller-session` / `caller's model` third-sense uses) hard to catch with a grep gate. Left in place, every future spec edit that adds a `caller` use perpetuates the SHOULD-level workaround.
