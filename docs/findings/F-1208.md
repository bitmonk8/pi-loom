## Finding

The *Custom-message channel persistence and LLM-context entry* paragraph in `runtime-event-channel.md` sits in the normative body alongside the present-tense MUSTs that govern the `loom-system-note` channel. Its trailing sentence ends with an inert clause: "‚Ä¶a future Pi minor that adds such a field would be picked up by the `pi-version-bump-procedure` re-validation gate, at which point the runtime SHOULD set the opt-out on the canonical `sendSystemNote` call." The SHOULD is conditioned on Pi shipping a `CustomMessage.excludeFromContext` field that does not exist at the [loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md#pi-sdk-pin) ‚Äî the same sentence states this two clauses earlier ‚Äî so a loom 1.0 implementer reading the paragraph has no action to take: the canonical `sendSystemNote` call has no `excludeFromContext` parameter to set, and the version-bump-step2.md item (r) editorial-review hook already owns the re-audit obligation on each Pi minor bump.

The SHOULD therefore reads as a normative obligation in V1 but resolves to a no-op in V1, and its conditional escalation is already redundantly carried by the editorial-review machinery. It clutters the V1 normative surface without changing any V1 behaviour, and slightly degrades the reader's ability to scan the page for obligations that actually fire today.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Demote the conditional future clause to a labelled non-normative note in the same paragraph, drop the SHOULD modal, and rely on version-bump-step2.md item (r) for the binding obligation. Concretely, replace the trailing sentence "‚Ä¶would be picked up by the `pi-version-bump-procedure` re-validation gate, at which point the runtime SHOULD set the opt-out on the canonical `sendSystemNote` call." with a parenthetical pointer along the lines of: "(If a future Pi minor adds such a field, version-bump editorial-review item (r) above flags the change; the disposition ‚Äî including whether `sendSystemNote` should set the opt-out ‚Äî is decided at that bump, not pinned by loom 1.0.)"

This keeps the analogy-referent + no-opt-out claim that surrounding text depends on (the `convertToLlm` durable-context invariant rests on the field being absent at the pin), removes the unactionable V1 SHOULD, and avoids duplicating the escalation obligation already owned by item (r). The closing sentence linking to item (r) remains as-is.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:
- Do not move the surrounding paragraph (or the analogy-referent sentence) to future-considerations: the no-opt-out fact is load-bearing for the V1 durable-context claim in the preceding sentences, which other PIC pages (`session-shutdown-semantics.md`, `diagnostics/code-registry-runtime.md`) cite by anchor.
- Preserve the `custom-message-context-entry-presupposition` anchor exactly ‚Äî version-bump-step2.md item (r) targets it by id.
- The `sendSystemNote` helper signature stays unchanged; no plan leaf needs to learn a new parameter.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A V1 implementer scanning the page for SHOULDs to honour finds one they cannot satisfy at the pinned SDK; the page invites either a confused no-op implementation gesture or a brief detour to confirm the field really is absent. No V1 behaviour changes either way, and the version-bump-step2.md item (r) editorial-review hook already binds the future-minor obligation, so removing the SHOULD costs no carry-through coverage.
