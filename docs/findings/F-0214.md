## Problem

The `H5a` H1 heading (`REQ-ID / diagnostic-code closing-gate automation`) enumerates only two of the leaf's three co-equal closing-gate reconciliation surfaces, omitting the un-anchored normative MUST/MUST-NOT surface that the leaf's `Adds.` body, the `H5b` canary, and `coverage-matrix.md` all carry as a third live surface. The identical title string also appears as the Horizontal-phases TOC link for `H5a` in `plan.md`, so both renderings under-advertise the leaf's scope. A reader triaging `H5a` by its heading under-scopes the leaf and can miss that the un-anchored-MUST surface is owned here, eroding the heading's value as a scope summary against the corpus's canonical three-surface `closing-gate` framing.

## Solution approach

Amend the `H5a` H1 heading so it enumerates the un-anchored-MUST surface alongside the REQ-ID and diagnostic-code surfaces it already names, using the corpus's existing short form for that third arm (the body and `coverage-matrix.md` call it the "un-anchored normative MUST/MUST-NOT" / "un-anchored-obligation" surface). Apply the identical title text to the Horizontal-phases TOC link for `H5a` in `plan.md` so the two strings do not diverge.
