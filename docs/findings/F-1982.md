## Finding

"SHOULD" in a spec normatively connotes RFC 2119 "recommended but may be omitted with justification." Here it governs a contributor process obligation (re-running test fixtures during a version bump), not a conformance property of loom implementations. An implementer or contributor reading "SHOULD be re-run" as RFC 2119 SHOULD might conclude that skipping the re-run is permissible if they document a rationale, when the intent appears to be an unconditional process step. The second occurrence in the retry-timing presupposition reinforces this ambiguity.
