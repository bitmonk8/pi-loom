## Finding

Items (f) through (ak) of `version-bump-step2.md` each close with a near-identical sentence of the form *"This obligation is SHOULD-level only at loom 1.0; it escalates to a build-time pin (on the same footing as the seven-capability literal-read assertion under step 2(a) above) once Pi exposes a typed <X> contract that step 2(a)'s surface-inventory probe can mechanically verify, at which point the editorial audit prescribed here is superseded by the mechanical gate."* The sentence appears 31 times ã once per checklist item ã and in every occurrence it leaves three load-bearing questions unanswered.

First, **no actor is named.** The surrounding step-2 preamble pins the contributor as the auditor for the editorial obligations and step 2(b) branch (2) names the contributor as the owner of `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` / `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS` promotion edits, but the escalation clause itself never says who detects that "Pi exposes a typed <X> contract," who decides the surface-inventory probe can mechanically verify it, or who performs the supersession. Two contributors auditing the same Pi minor could disagree on whether the trigger has fired.

Second, **no edit shape is specified.** "Superseded by the mechanical gate" implies the checklist row is removed and a new `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` entry (plus, where the obligation is a capability, a `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS` entry and an integer-literal bump per step 2(b) branch (2)) takes its place, but the prose never says so. The plan-side `V18c` ships-when literally enumerates "(a)ã(ak)" and `V18c-version-bump-checklist.md` records the MUST/SHOULD split using that fixed range, so an unannotated removal of a row would silently invalidate those acceptance criteria.

Third, **the closing paragraph carries only the addition half of the invariant.** *"Any future presupposition added to this page or to `future-considerations.md` that routes its detection to editorial review under this procedure MUST be added to this checklist in the same edit"* states the addition MUST, but there is no symmetric removal MUST when the escalation trigger fires: nothing forbids leaving a stale SHOULD-level row in the checklist after the same obligation has been promoted to an `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` entry, so a contributor who promotes the surface without removing the row leaves the audit double-recorded (once mechanically, once editorially) without violating any stated rule.

The three gaps compound: a step that names neither owner, nor edit, nor symmetric removal lets two contributors legitimately disagree on whether the trigger fired, what edit it warrants, and whether removing the now-redundant row is even permitted.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace the 31 per-item escalation sentences with a single shared escalation clause stated once in the step-2 preamble (or in a new sub-section adjacent to *Outcome recording conventions*), and pair it with a symmetric removal MUST. Each per-item row keeps only a short back-reference to that clause. The shared clause MUST state:

1. **Actor.** The contributor performing the bump is the detector and owner of the escalation edit. The trigger fires when the candidate `@earendil-works/pi-coding-agent` minor's `.d.ts` declarations expose a typed contract that mechanically witnesses the per-item obligation under step 2(a)'s surface-inventory probe shape (the per-item *typed <X> contract* wording is preserved as the per-row qualifier).
2. **Edit.** When the trigger fires, the contributor performs the escalation in a single commit, co-edited with the bump per step 2(b) branch (2)'s promote arm (and its capability-row sub-case when the obligation is a capability ã extending `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS`, bumping the integer literal in step 2(a)'s count-equality assertion, and extending the markdown items-1ãN enumeration). The escalation commit MUST: (a) add the new `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` entry (and `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS` entry where applicable), (b) remove the now-superseded checklist row, and (c) cite the row's anchor ID in the commit message so the audit trail records which editorial obligation the new gate replaces.
3. **Removal symmetry.** State explicitly that the row MUST be removed in the same commit that lands the mechanical gate; landing the mechanical gate without removing the row is non-conformant (symmetric to the existing addition MUST in the closing paragraph). Extend the closing paragraph to state both directions: *additions* of editorial-routed presuppositions MUST extend the checklist; *escalations* to a mechanical gate MUST shrink it.
4. **Range invariance for downstream leaves.** Because `V18c` / `V18c-T` enumerate "(a)ã(ak)" as a literal range, the shared clause MUST also state that the per-bump escalation does NOT re-letter surviving rows ã the row is removed by anchor ID, the surviving anchor IDs stay stable, and the literal "(a)ã(ak)" in `V18c` is updated only when the range's bounds (the alphabetically first or last surviving anchor) change. Alternatively, re-anchor `V18c` / `V18c-T` to name the items by anchor-ID set membership rather than by alphabetic range, so spec-side escalations do not propagate as plan-side literal edits; either choice is conformant, but the choice MUST be stated.

Implementer edge cases to watch: a Pi minor that exposes a typed contract too narrow to fully witness the per-item obligation (the mechanical gate covers only part of the editorial check) ã the shared clause SHOULD say the escalation fires only when the typed contract mechanically witnesses the obligation in full, and a partial-coverage typed contract leaves the row in place with an updated rationale citing the partial-coverage Pi surface; and a bump in which two items escalate simultaneously ã the escalation commit lands both edits together, with both anchor IDs cited.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two contributors performing the same Pi minor bump can legitimately disagree on (i) whether a Pi-side typed contract has appeared, (ii) what edit the trigger demands, and (iii) whether the now-mechanically-gated row may or must be removed from the checklist. The result is either a stale double-audited row that wastes review effort and risks divergent recording shapes, or a silent row removal that invalidates `V18c`'s ships-when acceptance criterion (which counts the items by name).
