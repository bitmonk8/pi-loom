## Problem

The escalation clause repeated across items (f)–(ak) of `version-bump-step2.md` ("This obligation is SHOULD-level only at loom 1.0; it escalates to a build-time pin … once Pi exposes a typed <X> contract … at which point the editorial audit prescribed here is superseded by the mechanical gate") leaves three load-bearing questions unanswered. It names no actor who detects the trigger, decides the surface-inventory probe can verify it, or performs the supersession. It specifies no edit shape — "superseded by the mechanical gate" implies the row is removed and replaced by an `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` (and, for capability obligations, `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS`) entry, but the prose never says so. And the closing addition-MUST paragraph carries only the addition half of the invariant: nothing forbids leaving a stale SHOULD-level row after the same obligation has been promoted to a mechanical gate, so an escalation that does not remove the row leaves the audit double-recorded without violating any stated rule.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** `8c930fe` (2026-05-17, *pi-loom spec: resolve "T22c — Pi version-bump procedure has no step for the session-binding contract"*).
**History:** Commit `8c930fe` introduced the first instance of the escalation sentence as the closing clause of item (f) — the *Session-binding contract* row — and shipped it in its present actor-less, edit-shape-less form. Every later commit that added a new SHOULD-level row to the checklist (items (g) through (ak), landed across 2026-05-17 → 2026-06-10 in commits `e89e42e`, `aebec4e`, `79fa3b9`, `d16c23a`, `cb3a2f2`, `d37c563`, `aa334c2`, `5d55c12`, `1f37b15`, `b077c07`, `257c545`, `158ed09`, `e63ba6c`, `7f07a96`, `4ea0544`, `bfef26e`, `6caacfe`, `113fb60`, `3683345`, `fdc5115`, `0229468`, `6829a79`, `60fba43`, `ce4e476`, `44782e9`, `9ced9f1`, `85fc906`, and several refinement commits in between) copied the same boilerplate verbatim, so the defect propagated through repetition rather than mutating across edits. The closing addition-MUST paragraph ("Any future presupposition added to this page … MUST be added to this checklist") was authored separately (commit `52b9101`, 2026-05-11, predating the first escalation sentence) and was never paired with a symmetric removal MUST when the boilerplate later introduced an escalation path that implies row removal. The defect is therefore *present-since-inception* of the escalation clause and orthogonal to the addition MUST that landed earlier.

## Solution approach

Factor the escalation clause repeated across items (f)–(ak) into a single shared statement — in the step-2 preamble or a sub-section adjacent to `#bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions` — that resolves the three gaps Problem names: the actor who detects the trigger and owns the escalation edit, the resulting `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` / `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS` edit (and its relation to step 2(b) branch (2)'s promote arm at `#bump-step-2b-promote`), and removal symmetry; leave each item a back-reference. Extend the closing addition-MUST paragraph ("Any future presupposition added to this page …") with the symmetric removal direction, so an escalation to a mechanical gate MUST shrink the checklist as well. Address the plan-side coupling: `V18c` enumerates the literal `(a)–(ak)` range, so state how surviving anchor IDs and that range stay stable when a row is removed.
