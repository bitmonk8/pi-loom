## Finding

The seven-step *Pi version bump procedure* is fragmented across four sibling pages whose filenames and TOC entries do not describe their actual contents, with the baseline-acquisition prerequisite nested inside one audit item's body, and step 1's normative text duplicating step 5's TS-error-code routing rules:

1. **`version-bump-step2b.md` carries steps 2b, 3, and 4.** The filename, the H1 (`# Version bump step2b`), and the parent index entry (`- [Version bump step2b](./pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2b.md)`) all advertise a step-2b-only page. The body opens with step 2(b) but then runs through numbered steps 3 (engines.node floor re-confirmation) and 4 (peerDependencies pin update + `Deliberate deviation` re-write). A reader following the TOC to find step 3 or step 4 finds nothing under those titles and must guess which page they landed in.

2. **Step 1 lives entirely in `version-bump-intro.md`.** The TOC names the page `Version bump intro`, suggesting an overview / preamble. The body is in fact the procedure's preamble paragraph *plus the full normative text of step 1* (re-typecheck, brand-string carve-out, TS230x-family routing). The step-1 body is the single largest paragraph in the four-page set and is the load-bearing first step; it is hidden under a title that does not name it.

3. **`Baseline acquisition` is nested inside item (e)'s body.** The `bump-baseline-acquisition` anchor block is a step-wide prerequisite — its own opening sentence reads "the canonical 'from' side for every `dist/*.js` diff on this page" — and four checklist items inbound-link to it: (e) per-session slash-command dispatch serialisation, (r) `convertToLlm` conversion, (aj) `SessionManager.inMemory` transcript-non-retention, and (am) `createAgentSession` `tools`-allowlist suppression. It is currently authored as a paragraph inside item (e)'s bullet, so three of the four consuming items have to forward-link into a sibling item's body to reach a prerequisite that logically precedes all four. A reader scanning item (e) sees a prerequisite that visually belongs to (e) alone; a reader scanning (r) / (aj) / (am) sees a `[Baseline acquisition](#bump-baseline-acquisition)` link with no in-page parent.

4. **Step 1 in `version-bump-intro.md` duplicates step 5's TS-error-code routing prose.** The step-1 paragraph spells out the `loom/typecheck/session-shutdown-reason-snapshot` brand-string discriminator, the `TS2305 / TS2307 / TS2614 / TS2724` closed-family routing through step 2(a), the per-assertion (not per-invocation) carve-out semantics, the illustrative-restatement enumeration, and the §7 closed-enum row co-update — all of which are restated, in equivalent or stronger form, in `version-bump-triggers.md` step 5 (which is the actual normative owner of the brand string, the closed family, and the trigger (i)/(ii) routing). A Pi-side rename of the family list (the same finding that already produced a single-edit drift in commit `6f898a9`) must now be applied in two places; an implementer reading step 1 has no signal that step 5 is the authoritative restatement.

The four defects are independent in their fix surface but share a single root cause: when commit `f5e89f4` ran `docs/_tools/split_spec.py` to enforce the 30KB-recommended / 100KB-hard size cap on the spec set, the pre-shard monolithic `pi-integration-contract.md` was sliced by line ranges and the `title_from_slug` helper mechanically generated each child's H1 from its slug. The split chose to put steps 2b–4 in `version-bump-step2b.md` for size reasons, but did not adjust the filename or the H1 to describe the result; nothing in the splitter could detect that "step2b" is no longer the page's actual scope.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** `f5e89f4` (`docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set`, 2026-06-04)
**History:** None of the four pages existed before `f5e89f4`; that commit ran the one-shot `docs/_tools/split_spec.py` driver, whose `PLANS` table for `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` declares the four children `version-bump-intro` (lines 902–908), `version-bump-step2` (909–922), `version-bump-step2b` (923–930), and `version-bump-triggers` (931–935) by line range. The line ranges were chosen to keep each child under the 30 KB recommended cap, not to align with the procedure's step boundaries — so step 1's body landed in `version-bump-intro.md` because step 1 happened to start at line 902, and steps 3–4 landed in `version-bump-step2b.md` because they happened to fit in the size budget after step 2(b). The H1 of each child was generated mechanically by `title_from_slug(slug) = slug.replace("-", " ")[:1].upper() + slug.replace("-", " ")[1:]` (line 115 of `split_spec.py`) — so `version-bump-step2b` produced `Version bump step2b` and `version-bump-intro` produced `Version bump intro`, neither describing the body's actual scope. The TOC entries in the parent index (`pi-integration-contract.md` `## Contents`) were generated by the same routine in the splitter's index-page emission pass and copied the same titles. The `Baseline acquisition` block, the step-1 / step-5 routing-prose duplication, and the items (a)/(b) sub-label conflict were carried over verbatim from the pre-split monolithic `pi-integration-contract.md`; the splitter did not author them. Two later commits touched the affected region without addressing the page-layout defect: `ccc6e38` (2026-06-07, "Item (e)/(r) dist/*.js baseline source unspecified") authored the `bump-baseline-acquisition` anchor block but placed it inside item (e)'s body — at the time, only items (e) and (r) consumed it — and subsequent commits (`9ced9f1` adding (aj), `077afb5` adding (am)) routed new consumers through the same in-(e) anchor without hoisting it. `6f898a9` (2026-06-05) edited only step 1's TS-family routing wording, demonstrating the duplication's drift risk concretely. The same `title_from_slug` defect produces the sibling H1-vs-canonical-term mismatches called out elsewhere in this review (`unknown-reason-rule.md`, `queryerror-variants.md`, the `audit-*.md` cluster).

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

Each option below is an independent obligation. Apply in the recommended order so the smaller / scope-bounding edits land first and the wider structural moves resolve on a stable baseline.

### Option A — Hoist `Baseline acquisition` to a step-2 prerequisites block

**Approach.** Move the `<a id="bump-baseline-acquisition">` paragraph out of item (e)'s bullet body and place it as a step-2 prerequisite block immediately after the step-2 preamble paragraph, before the bulleted item list begins. Re-word the opening sentence from "Before running the `dist/*.js` diffs and JS-body reads items (e), (r), and (aj) prescribe …" to a step-scoped framing such as "Before any of the `dist/*.js` diffs or JS-body reads the items below prescribe …", and add a one-line back-reference at the head of items (e), (r), (aj), and (am) to the now-sibling block (the existing `[Baseline acquisition](#bump-baseline-acquisition)` links continue to resolve).

**Spec edits.** `version-bump-step2.md` only: move the `Baseline acquisition` paragraph to a position between the preamble and item (a); the `bump-baseline-acquisition` anchor is preserved verbatim; the four existing inbound forward-links stay byte-equal.

**Pros.** Smallest fix surface; preserves every inbound anchor and link; no rename, no TOC change. Resolves defect (3) outright. Compatible with every other option below.

**Cons.** Does not address defects (1), (2), or (4).

**Risks.** Negligible — the move is anchor-preserving and within a single file.

### Option B — Replace step 1's TS-family / brand-string duplication with a forward reference

**Approach.** Trim step 1's body in `version-bump-intro.md` to the action ("Install the candidate minor, run `npm run typecheck`, confirm no loom-load-bearing Pi-imported types have changed shape") plus a one-sentence summary of the two carve-outs (the `SessionShutdownEvent['reason']` snapshot widening and the module-import-resolution family), each forward-linking to its authoritative restatement in step 5 (the `#bump-step-5-trigger-ii` anchor for the snapshot carve-out, a step-5-anchored target for the TS230x-family routing). Delete the in-line repetition of the brand string, the four-code family with its illustrative TS2305 wording, the per-assertion-vs-per-invocation carve-out semantics, the illustrative-restatement enumeration, and the §7 closed-enum-row co-update prose. Keep step 1's *triage rule* ("a typecheck red that does not carry the brand string is owned by step 1, not step 5") since step 5 itself depends on this disambiguation to define its scope.

**Spec edits.** `version-bump-intro.md` step 1 paragraph (shrink to triage + forward-link); `version-bump-triggers.md` step 5 (add anchor on the brand-string-introducing sentence if one is not present, so the forward-link target is stable). No anchor renames.

**Pros.** Removes the lock-step drift hazard the `6f898a9` commit illustrated. Halves the cognitive load on step 1 (currently the largest single paragraph in the four-page set). The brand string and TS-error-code family stay defined exactly once.

**Cons.** Slightly more involved than Option A — requires identifying which sentences in step 1 are the load-bearing triage rule versus the duplicated routing prose, and confirming step 5's restatement carries every clause step 1 currently states.

**Risks.** A clause present in step 1 but absent in step 5 would silently disappear under a naive deletion; the contributor MUST diff the two paragraphs before deleting and either trim only the overlap, or co-edit step 5 to absorb any step-1-only clauses (e.g. the TS2314 disambiguation example is currently in step 1 only).

### Option C — Rename / re-split the four pages to match their contents

**Approach.** Two sub-variants:

- **C1 (rename-only, no re-split):** Rename `version-bump-step2b.md` → `version-bump-steps-2b-3-4.md` (or similar), rename `version-bump-intro.md` → `version-bump-step1.md`, rename `version-bump-triggers.md` → `version-bump-steps-5-7.md`, and re-write each H1 to match the body's actual scope. Re-point the parent index TOC entries to the new paths and labels. The procedure's preamble paragraph (currently in `version-bump-intro.md` ahead of step 1) moves either to the parent index page (`pi-integration-contract.md`) under the `Pi version bump procedure` section, or stays in `version-bump-step1.md` with a sub-heading separating it from step 1's body.
- **C2 (re-split):** Re-cut the four pages along step boundaries — one page per step, or one page per logical grouping (`step1.md`, `step2.md`, `steps-3-4.md`, `steps-5-7.md`) — keeping each child under the 30 KB recommended cap (step 5 alone is already close to the cap, so it cannot easily share a page with step 6 or 7).

**Spec edits.** Four file renames (or splits) plus a corpus-wide cross-reference sweep: 1571 cross-references were repointed by `split_spec.py` at the original split, so the inbound surface to these four files is non-trivial and includes plan-topic citations (`V18c`, `V18d` `Spec.` lines), `docs/findings/` entries, and `docs/plan.md` blocked-obligation prose. The `<a id>` anchors inside each file (`bump-step-2-positive`, `bump-step-2b-promote`, `bump-step-5-trigger-i`, `bump-baseline-acquisition`, etc.) must be preserved verbatim across the rename so existing `#anchor` link targets do not break.

**Pros.** Resolves defects (1) and (2) at the source — filename and H1 describe what the reader will find. The TOC becomes navigable for a contributor who knows the step number they need but not which page it lives on.

**Cons.** Largest fix surface of any option. Cross-reference sweep is corpus-wide and must be done atomically; a partial rename leaves broken links. `V18c` and `V18d`'s `Spec.` citation lists are affected.

**Risks.** The same `title_from_slug` mechanical-rename trap that produced the original H1s applies in reverse — a contributor renaming the file is responsible for the H1 not being derived back from the new slug by the same routine on a subsequent split, which means the H1 must be hand-authored (it is already, post-split; this is a process risk rather than a tooling one). If `docs/_tools/split_spec.py` is ever re-run (the commit message frames it as one-shot, but the file is retained), the renamed pages would not be regenerated correctly unless `PLANS` is updated; the script's one-shot framing should be re-confirmed before relying on it.

### Recommendation

Apply A first, then B, then C — in that order — and treat them as three independent commits.

A is the smallest scope-bounding edit: it moves the `Baseline acquisition` block within a single file with no anchor change, so subsequent options land on a baseline where the prerequisite has the right structural position. B then collapses the step-1 / step-5 duplication on top of A; doing B before C means the smaller wording edit is not entangled with a file rename. C is the largest and most cross-reference-heavy edit, and should land last so its corpus-wide sweep is the only outstanding change.

For C, prefer **C1** (rename-only) over C2 (re-split) at loom 1.0 — the rename addresses the navigability defect without re-litigating the line-range layout the splitter chose for size reasons, and C2's per-step pages would each be smaller than the 30 KB recommendation target without a corresponding readability gain. Edge cases the implementer must watch under C1: (i) `docs/findings/F-0813.md` already cross-references "version-bump-step2b.md — title/filename advertise only step 2b but content includes steps 3–4" and "version-bump-triggers.md — filename `version-bump-triggers.md` vs title `Version bump steps 5–7`" as same-cluster siblings of the `unknown-reason-rule.md` H1 finding; those finding-side mentions are descriptive and need not be edited, but if the rename lands, the finding records on disk (under `docs/findings/`) should be re-checked for stale path strings in their `location` frontmatter; (ii) the `<a id>` anchors inside the renamed files are the inbound contract surface (per the same logic that argues against renaming `unknown-reason-rule.md`) and MUST NOT change; (iii) the parent index page (`pi-integration-contract.md`) carries forward-narrative prose referring to "the contributor checklist … is owned by [Pi version bump procedure](./pi-integration-contract/version-bump-intro.md#pi-version-bump-procedure) at the end of this page" — that link target must be re-pointed to whichever child page hosts the procedure's preamble after the rename.

If only a subset of the three options can land at this pass, A alone is the highest-value single edit (it removes the only structural defect that makes a step-wide prerequisite appear to belong to a single bullet); B alone is the highest-value single edit for drift-prevention (it removes a paragraph-pair already demonstrated to drift); C alone is the highest-value single edit for first-time-reader navigability.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A contributor performing a Pi minor bump must navigate four pages whose filenames and TOC entries do not predict their contents, locate step 1 under "Version bump intro" and steps 3–4 under "Version bump step2b", and discover the step-wide `Baseline acquisition` prerequisite by following a forward-link from one of three audit items whose body texts are not its logical parent. The duplicated step-1 / step-5 routing prose means a Pi-side rename of the TS-error-code family or the brand string must be applied in two paragraphs in lock-step or the two paragraphs drift; the spec corpus already shows a single-commit drift of this exact paragraph (`6f898a9`). None of the defects changes a gate, a discriminator, or an obligation — the procedure is navigable as written and the build-time gates fire on the same conditions — but each adds a recurring contributor-side cost on every bump.
