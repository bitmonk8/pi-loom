## Problem

The `loom/host/session-shutdown-pinned-constant-unreadable` row in
`docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/code-registry-host.md`, and the source-of-truth
clause it quotes inside the `#unknown-reason-rule` paragraph of
`docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/unknown-reason-rule.md`, carry a
`MUST NOT` directed at operator triage tooling — that tooling "MUST NOT
silently extend [`details.observed`'s] semantics to this code". The
obligation binds an externally-owned actor whose conformance loom cannot
observe: the runtime's only verifiable behaviour is the producer-side fact
that this diagnostic carries `details.failure` rather than `details.observed`.
The underlying naming asymmetry between the two keys is correct and worth
keeping; it is the operator-directed prescriptive `MUST NOT` that is
unfalsifiable from loom's surface.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 75a9626 — pi-loom spec: resolve "SessionShutdownEvent.reason closed set has no build-time pin against the SDK type" (2026-05-09, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The `loom/host/session-shutdown-pinned-constant-unreadable` row and the Unknown-reason-rule catch-arm-discriminator paragraph were authored together in `75a9626`, which first introduced the diagnostic into the then-monolithic `diagnostics.md` and `pi-integration-contract.md` topic files; that same commit already carried the operator-directed `MUST NOT silently extend [details.observed]'s semantics` clause and its `details.failure`-vs-`details.observed` naming rationale at both sites. The diagnostic code did not exist in the corpus before `75a9626`, so the unfalsifiable operator-tooling `MUST NOT` entered alongside the code rather than being grafted onto a pre-existing row. The later `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04) only sub-split those topic files into the cited `diagnostics/code-registry-host.md` and `pi-integration-contract/unknown-reason-rule.md` paths, relocating the clause verbatim without changing it.

## Solution approach

Demote the operator-directed `MUST NOT` to a non-normative note at both
sites, preserving the naming-asymmetry rationale that `details.failure`
carries an internal closed-set discriminator while `details.observed`
carries an externally-observed SDK value. Rewrite the clause in the
`code-registry-host.md` pinned-constant-unreadable Trigger column and the
matching clause inside `unknown-reason-rule.md`'s `#unknown-reason-rule`
catch-arm-discriminator paragraph.

## Solution constraints

- The three discriminator literals `"missing-entry"`,
  `"literals-shape-invalid"`, and `"throw:<String(error)>"` (anchor-stable
  contract surface (d) of `#unknown-reason-rule`) MUST remain intact in the
  rewritten text at both sites.
- Both site edits MUST land in the same commit, per the existing cross-page
  "the two pages MUST stay aligned on the predicate text" pin.
- Out of scope: the surrounding producer-side discriminator-grammar and
  deduplication-bucket `MUST`/`MUST NOT` statements, which are
  runtime-observable and stay normative.
