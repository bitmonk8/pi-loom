## Finding

The `loom/host/session-shutdown-pinned-constant-unreadable` row in
`docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/code-registry-host.md` contains a parenthesised
normative obligation directed at a third party:

> *…operator triage tooling that keys on `details.observed` for
> `session-shutdown-reason-unknown` MUST NOT silently extend that key's
> semantics to this code, per PIC's **Unknown-reason rule**.*

The MUST NOT binds an externally-owned actor — operator-side log-triage
tooling that consumes loom's emitted diagnostics. Nothing the loom runtime
emits or the loom test suite can observe will distinguish a conformant
triage tool from a non-conformant one; the runtime's only verifiable
behaviour on this row is the producer-side fact that this diagnostic
carries `details.failure` (drawn from the closed `"missing-entry" |
"literals-shape-invalid" | "throw:<…>"` discriminator set) rather than
`details.observed`. Whether downstream tooling then re-uses the
`details.observed` key for this code is opaque to loom by construction.

The same wording exists upstream in
`docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/unknown-reason-rule.md` (the
last clause of the long catch-arm-discriminator paragraph), which
`code-registry-host.md` quotes via the parenthetical "per PIC's
**Unknown-reason rule**". The defect is therefore co-located: the
upstream sentence is the source-of-truth statement, and the registry-row
quote is its restatement under the contract-surface (d) carve-out.
Either site, taken alone, is unfalsifiable from loom's surface.

The substantive content underneath the MUST NOT — the deliberate naming
asymmetry between `details.failure` (closed internal discriminator) and
`details.observed` (externally-observed SDK value) — is correct and
worth keeping. It is the prescriptive *MUST NOT* directed at the
external consumer that is the testability defect, not the underlying
rationale.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Demote the operator-directed MUST NOT to a non-normative parenthetical
note at both sites, preserving the underlying naming-asymmetry rationale:

- In `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/code-registry-host.md`, edit the
  parenthetical in the `loom/host/session-shutdown-pinned-constant-unreadable`
  *Trigger* column from
  *"(operator triage tooling that keys on `details.observed` for
  `session-shutdown-reason-unknown` MUST NOT silently extend that
  key's semantics to this code, per PIC's **Unknown-reason rule**)"*
  to a non-normative note such as
  *"(the field is named `failure` rather than `observed` because the
  two payloads carry different kinds of value — `details.observed`
  carries an externally-observed SDK value on the sibling row, whereas
  `details.failure` carries an internal closed-set discriminator
  here; downstream consumers therefore see two distinct keys rather
  than two overloaded `details.observed` populations.)"*
- Apply the matching demotion in
  `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/unknown-reason-rule.md`
  to the corresponding clause inside the long catch-arm-discriminator
  paragraph (the source-of-truth sentence the registry row quotes).
  Use the same non-normative wording so the two sites continue to
  read consistently and the existing "the two pages MUST stay aligned"
  cross-page pin holds.

The producer-side wire-format MUSTs surrounding the demoted clause
stay normative and unchanged: the closed three-arm
`details.failure` discriminator set, the `"throw:<String(error)>"`
template grammar (256-UTF-16-code-unit truncation, control-character
escape table, literal-colon and inner-`"throw:"` passthrough,
`"throw:<unreadable>"` coercion-failure fallback), and the
`(code, details.failure)` deduplication-bucket fact.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- `diagnostics.md`'s anchor-stable contract-surface clause (d) pins
  the three discriminator literals (`"missing-entry"`,
  `"literals-shape-invalid"`, `"throw:<String(error)>"`) as
  inbound-reference-stable substrings; the demotion MUST leave those
  three literals intact in the rewritten note so the (d) substring
  audit and the `H5e` un-anchored-MUST gate continue to pass.
- The cross-page "MUST stay aligned on the predicate text" pin
  between `code-registry-host.md` and
  `unknown-reason-rule.md` applies to the composite-predicate
  sentence, not to the demoted clause — but the two edits MUST land
  in the same commit anyway so the two sites do not drift in the
  interim.
- Do not also demote the surrounding producer-side MUST/MUST-NOT
  sentences (the discriminator-grammar pins, the
  bucket-collapse-avoidance fact). Those are runtime-observable from
  the emitted `details.failure` string and are correctly normative.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The MUST NOT cannot affect any conformant implementation's behaviour —
loom emits `details.failure` either way, and what consumers do with it
is outside loom's reach. Carrying it as normative text pollutes the
un-anchored-MUST text-scan surface the `H5e` closing gate audits, and
invites a reviewer to mark the obligation "satisfied" on grounds that
are not loom-observable. Demoting it to a non-normative note removes
the unfalsifiable obligation while preserving every producer-side
fact already pinned elsewhere in the same row and in the upstream
rule.
