## Finding

`H4a`'s **Adds** names a single input-side capability as the leaf's own deliverable — the **minimal response-emission capability** for the session double — and scopes it explicitly to "drive self-check items (i) and (ii)": emit streamed assistant tokens observable before `ctx.waitForIdle()` resolves, and append one streamed response as a single prompt-mode turn. The full scripted-injection response-programming surface, including category (e) abort/cancellation injection, is deferred to `H4b` (which depends on `H4a`).

`H4a`'s **Tests** self-check, however, asserts all four fidelity-contract axes: (i) streamed-token order, (ii) single-turn append, **(iii) the `pi.on` cancel-forward subscription**, and **(iv) cancellation propagation (CNCL-4)**. Items (iii) and (iv) cannot be observed without firing a host cancel / `ctx.signal` abort into the double — a triggering affordance that appears nowhere in `H4a`'s enumerated deliverables and is instead deferred to `H4b`'s category (e).

`H4a`'s **Ships when** condition requires this self-check to pass, and `H4a`'s only **Dep** is `H3a`, so it cannot borrow the affordance from `H4b` (which sits downstream). The leaf therefore asserts a gate it has no enumerated means to drive.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit introduction
**Introducing commits:** `32ea081` (2026-06-20, "fix F-0477 — define H4a minimal emission capability + H4a/H4b boundary")
**History:** The four-axis self-check (enumerating (i)–(iv), including the `pi.on` cancel-forward subscription and cancellation propagation) already existed in `H4a`'s **Tests** at the parent of `32ea081`. That commit added the "minimal response-emission capability … sufficient only to drive self-check items (i) and (ii)" framing and the "self-checkable from its own deliverables" claim, narrowing `H4a`'s owned input-side capability to (i)/(ii) while leaving the self-check asserting (iii)/(iv) and deferring the full scripted-injection surface (including cancellation injection) to `H4b`. The mismatch between the scoped deliverable and the four-item self-check entered at that commit.

## Solution Space

### Recommendation

In `H4a`'s **Adds**, alongside the minimal response-emission capability sentence, name a minimal host-cancel / `ctx.signal`-abort triggering affordance as `H4a`'s own deliverable, and change the self-check coverage statement from "sufficient only to drive self-check items (i) and (ii)" to cover items (i)–(iv). Reconcile `H4a`'s **Tests** and **Ships when** wording so the self-check drives axes (iii) (the `pi.on` cancel-forward subscription) and (iv) (cancellation propagation, CNCL-4) against this affordance. No spec edits.

Keep the `H4a` trigger's scope explicitly narrower than `H4b` category (e): name it as a single fire-a-host-cancel affordance, not the pre-call / in-flight / during-retry injection-point matrix. Pin category (e) as `H4b`-owned so the two cancellation surfaces stay delimited and do not duplicate or drift.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable implementers diverge: one invents an ad-hoc cancel trigger inside `H4a` (going off-script, with no boundary against `H4b`'s category (e)), another defers the (iii)/(iv) self-check to `H4b` and ships `H4a` with a self-check narrower than its **Tests** prose claims. As written, `H4a`'s fidelity-contract self-check — part of its Ships-when gate — cannot be satisfied from `H4a`'s own deliverables.
