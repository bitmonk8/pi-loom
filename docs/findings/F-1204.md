## Finding

GOV-20's *Closure heuristic* paragraph in `docs/spec_topics/governance/release-version-naming.md` closes with:

> Default on ambiguity: `loom 1.0.0` — frozen-baseline is the stronger commitment, and design-scope claims subsume frozen-baseline claims.

The two clauses after the em-dash justify the stated default in opposite directions. The first clause ("frozen-baseline is the stronger commitment") argues for the narrower, more-specific spelling: when the closure test is hard to apply, prefer `loom 1.0.0` because binding a sentence to the closed-inventory sense over-promises less than binding it to the whole design scope. The second clause ("design-scope claims subsume frozen-baseline claims") points the other way: GOV-19 establishes `loom 1.0 ⊇ loom 1.0.0`, so by that containment relation a claim that binds the design scope *already* binds the frozen baseline — which is the canonical reason to default to the **broader** alias (`loom 1.0`) and let containment do the rest. An implementer running the closure heuristic at an ambiguous callsite latches onto whichever half matches the sentence in front of them and commits the opposite spelling from a peer reading the other half.

The defect is local to the tail of one sentence — the rule's mechanical arm (the GOV-19 *closed enumeration* intensional test) is intact, and only the tie-breaker fallback is ambiguous — but it falls on the only rule the spec offers for ambiguous-closure callsites, so two converting contributors can permanently disagree about which alias-mapped spelling to commit. The stated default itself (`loom 1.0.0`) is unambiguous; what is missing is a single coherent justification that does not invite the reverse reading.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace the *Default on ambiguity* sentence's post-em-dash justification with a single, direction-consistent rationale that matches the stated default (`loom 1.0.0`). Concretely, rewrite the closing sentence as:

> Default on ambiguity: `loom 1.0.0` — the frozen-baseline literal is the narrower / more-conservative spelling at a callsite the closure test does not decisively classify, and committing the narrower spelling never over-promises by GOV-19's `loom 1.0 ⊇ loom 1.0.0` containment (a sentence that turned out to bind the design scope still holds under the narrower spelling, but not the reverse).

This keeps the existing default, drops the "design-scope claims subsume frozen-baseline claims" clause as a free-standing justification (its containment content is folded into the parenthetical, where it correctly supports — rather than contradicts — picking the narrower spelling), and ties the rationale to GOV-19's already-pinned containment relation rather than to an undefined "stronger commitment" notion. No other GOV-20 clause moves, no anchor changes, no callsite-conversion outcomes change — the rule's mechanical arm (the closed-enumeration intensional test) and its stated default are preserved verbatim; only the tie-breaker justification is rewritten so it points in one direction.

Implementer-relevant edge case: the rewrite MUST stay inside the *Closure heuristic* paragraph (not migrate to GOV-19) so the containment citation remains co-located with the tie-breaker it justifies; and the parenthetical "a sentence that turned out to bind the design scope still holds under the narrower spelling, but not the reverse" is load-bearing for the direction argument and MUST NOT be elided.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two contributors converting the same ambiguous-closure callsite (a sentence that neither clearly asserts exhaustive membership per GOV-19 nor clearly does not) can read the two halves of the tie-breaker and pick opposite alias-mapped spellings — one committing `loom 1.0.0` on the "stronger commitment" reading, the other committing `loom 1.0` on the "design-scope claims subsume" reading. The divergence is in committed spec prose, not runtime behaviour, but it permanently splits the converted-callsite inventory the GOV-20 row's eventual retirement (GOV-7 / GOV-8 lifecycle) depends on.
