## Problem

GOV-20's *Closure heuristic* paragraph in `release-version-naming.md` closes with a *Default on ambiguity* sentence whose stated default (`loom 1.0.0`) is unambiguous, but whose post-em-dash justification carries two clauses that argue in opposite directions. "Frozen-baseline is the stronger commitment" points at the narrower frozen-baseline spelling, while "design-scope claims subsume frozen-baseline claims" — via GOV-19's `loom 1.0 ⊇ loom 1.0.0` containment — is the canonical reason to default to the broader alias `loom 1.0`. Two contributors converting the same ambiguous-closure callsite can latch onto opposite halves and commit opposite alias-mapped spellings, permanently splitting the converted-callsite inventory the GOV-20 row's eventual retirement depends on. The rule's mechanical arm (the GOV-19 closed-enumeration intensional test) is intact; only the tie-breaker justification is defective.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the *Default on ambiguity* tail of GOV-20's *Closure heuristic* sentence so its justification points in a single direction consistent with the stated `loom 1.0.0` default, grounding the rationale in GOV-19's `loom 1.0 ⊇ loom 1.0.0` containment rather than the undefined "stronger commitment" notion. Drop the contradicting "design-scope claims subsume frozen-baseline claims" clause as a free-standing justification. Keep the stated default and the mechanical closed-enumeration intensional test unchanged.

## Solution constraints
