## Problem

Inside `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/`, the two audit-cluster sibling files have their bodies, H1 headings, and `<a id>` anchors transposed against their basenames. `audit-recognised-shapes.md` carries the H1 `# Audit target categories` and the *Target surface categories* paragraph (anchors `audit-target-surface-categories` and `strict-capability-absence-pin`); `audit-target-categories.md` carries the H1 `# Audit recognised shapes` and the *Recognised import/access shapes* paragraph (anchor `audit-recognised-shapes`). A maintainer who navigates by filename lands on a page whose H1 advertises the opposite topic, and the parent index `pi-integration-contract.md` masks the inversion by pairing H1-matching link text with the mismatched filename URL. The inversion has leaked into every cross-reference: the intra-shard "above" link and the external citation sites (`overview-and-orientation.md`, `host-prerequisites.md`, `inventory-audit-intro.md`, `capability-probe.md`, `version-bump-step2.md`, `version-bump-step2b.md`) point at URLs whose filename and anchor name disagree on topic.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**

- `f5e89f4` — *docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set* (Thomas Andersen, 2026-06-04). `docs/_tools/split_spec.py` materialised both files at once from the pi-integration-contract monolith. Its `PLANS` table allocates source-line 826 to slug `audit-recognised-shapes` and source-line 827 to slug `audit-target-categories`, but the monolith content at those line positions was the inverse: line 826 was the *Target surface categories* paragraph, line 827 was the *Recognised import/access shapes* paragraph. The script synthesised each child's H1 from its slug (`title_from_slug`), so on commit the H1s matched the basenames but neither matched the paragraph body the file actually contained.
- `7e3e94e` — *pi-loom spec: resolve "audit-cluster files have H1 headings inverted"* (Thomas Andersen, 2026-06-05). A targeted fix to the same review item swapped the two H1 headings (and the link-text on the parent index) so each page's H1 now matched its body — but left the basenames, the surviving `<a id="audit-recognised-shapes">` anchor, and every cross-reference pointing at the names assigned by the split. This resolved the heading↔body half of the inversion and re-pointed the heading↔filename half into the present state.

**History:** `git log --follow` over both files shows two commits touching either H1: `f5e89f4` (the split) and `7e3e94e` (the H1 swap). `git log --all -S 'audit-recognised-shapes' -- docs/_tools/split_spec.py` confirms the split-time slug table is the original source of the slug↔body mismatch. The defect cannot be attributed to either commit alone: `f5e89f4` introduced the slug↔content mismatch from the wrong line-range table, and `7e3e94e` chose the swap-headings arm over the rename-files-or-swap-bodies arm, locking the slug↔H1 inversion into the corpus. The durable fix addresses that arm choice rather than reverting either commit.

## Solution approach

Realign each file's body to its existing basename rather than renaming the basenames: move the *Recognised import/access shapes* paragraph (anchor `audit-recognised-shapes`) into `audit-recognised-shapes.md` under H1 `# Audit recognised shapes`, and move the *Target surface categories* paragraph (anchors `audit-target-surface-categories` and `strict-capability-absence-pin`) into `audit-target-categories.md` under H1 `# Audit target categories`. In `pi-integration-contract.md`'s Contents block, rewrite each audit-page entry so its visible link text matches its URL basename. Sweep every cross-reference to the moved anchors — the intra-shard "above" reference and the external citation sites named in the Problem — and re-point each to the file the anchor now lives in.

## Solution constraints

- Preserve every `<a id>` anchor identifier verbatim — `audit-recognised-shapes`, `audit-target-surface-categories`, and `strict-capability-absence-pin` are cited externally; relocating them is the point, but each keeps its existing identifier text.
- Out of scope: the substantive audit-category, recognised-shape, capability-probe, and strict-capability-probe prose owned by F-0959, F-0961, and F-0960 — this is a content move plus a link sweep, not a prose edit.
