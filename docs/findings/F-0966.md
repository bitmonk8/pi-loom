## Finding

Step 0 (c) opens by enumerating the factory-probable subset of the seven SDK capabilities as "items 1, 2, 3, 4, and 6 (five capabilities, nine function members — capability 1 contributes one, capability 2 contributes one, capabilities 3 and 6 each contribute two, and capability 4 contributes three)". Capability 5 (cancellation propagation) is excluded from both the item list and the per-capability member tally.

Two later sentences in the same step contradict that exclusion. The narrative immediately states that "Item 5 (cancellation propagation) is covered jointly by step (b) above ... and step (c) below (the `AgentSession.prototype.abort` member listed alongside `createAgentSession`)", and the `AgentSession.prototype.abort` bullet itself is annotated "(capability 3 / capability 5)". So one member in step (c)'s loop is normatively *also* contributing to capability 5's coverage, yet the parenthetical's cardinality summary attributes that bullet entirely to capability 3 and re-lists capability 5 only under "covered jointly".

An implementer wiring the partition `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES` (the constant `V9a` exports and `V18a` reconciles against `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS`' factory-probed flags) cannot tell from this paragraph whether capability 5 belongs in that set. Reading the item list and per-capability tally gives a 5-element answer `{1, 2, 3, 4, 6}`; reading the dual-coverage sentence and the bullet annotation gives a 6-element answer `{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}`. The same ambiguity infects diagnostics framing: an `sdk-capability-missing` failure on the `AgentSession.prototype.abort` member is simultaneously a capability-3 failure and a capability-5 failure on one reading, but a capability-3-only failure on the other.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit-regression
**Introducing commit:** `5c8e630` (2026-05-07) — *pi-loom spec: resolve "Subagent cancellation wiring depends on a non-existent createAgentSession({ signal }) option"*
**History:** Prior to `5c8e630` step (c) read "...items 1, 2, 3, 4, and 6 (five capabilities, seven function members — capabilities 4 and 6 each contribute two function members). Item 5 (cancellation propagation) is covered by step (b) above." — internally consistent: capability 5 was excluded from the factory-probable subset and step (b) alone was credited with covering it. `5c8e630` added a new factory-time probe for `AgentSession.prototype.abort` (to surface a missing handle-side cancellation primitive as `loom/load/host-incompatible` rather than a spawn-time `TypeError`), annotated the new bullet "(capability 3 / capability 5)", and rewrote the orientation sentence to "Item 5 (cancellation propagation) is covered jointly by step (b) above ... and step (c) below". The same commit bumped the function-member count from seven to eight but did **not** update the item list `{1, 2, 3, 4, 6}` or the per-capability contribution tally to reflect that capability 5 now contributes a probed member at step (c); the contradiction between the parenthetical's "five capabilities" / `{1, 2, 3, 4, 6}` framing and the new dual-coverage prose dates from that commit. Subsequent edits to the paragraph (`3e6ce2e` 2026-06-05 removed `ExtensionCommandContext.waitForIdle` and bumped the count to nine; `aa334c2` and `d8d5ad0` expanded surrounding text) preserved the contradiction unchanged.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Treat capability 5 as factory-probable; partition becomes `{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}`

**Approach.** Acknowledge that step (c) does in fact probe a load-bearing capability-5 member (`AgentSession.prototype.abort`) and place capability 5 inside the factory-probable subset alongside the other five. The dual-coverage prose stays — step (b)'s `AbortSignal` shape check and step (c)'s `AgentSession.prototype.abort` check together discharge the capability — but capability 5 is no longer "covered jointly from outside the set"; it is a member of the set and is covered jointly by two of the set's own checks.

**Spec edits.** In step (c)'s opening: replace "items 1, 2, 3, 4, and 6 (five capabilities, nine function members — capability 1 contributes one, capability 2 contributes one, capabilities 3 and 6 each contribute two, and capability 4 contributes three)" with "items 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6 (six capabilities, nine function members — capability 1 contributes one, capability 2 contributes one, capability 3 contributes two, capability 4 contributes three, capability 5 contributes one (`AgentSession.prototype.abort`, shared as the second function member of capability 3), and capability 6 contributes two; the shared member is counted once)". Reword the "covered jointly" sentence to "Capability 5 (cancellation propagation) is itself factory-probable: it is discharged jointly by step (b) (the WHATWG `AbortSignal` / `AbortController` shape probe) and by the `AgentSession.prototype.abort` member in step (c) below, which the listing also attributes to capability 3." Leave the `AgentSession.prototype.abort` bullet's "(capability 3 / capability 5)" annotation as-is — it is now consistent with the partition.

**Pros.**
- Eliminates the "covered jointly from outside the set" framing, which is the actual source of confusion: a capability cannot be both excluded from the factory-probable subset and probed at factory time.
- Reflects the spec's own behavioural reality: if `AgentSession.prototype.abort` is missing the probe routes to `loom/load/host-incompatible` with `details.member = "AgentSession.prototype.abort"`, which *is* a capability-5 failure surfacing at factory time.
- The 9-member tally is unchanged (the shared member is counted once), so step (c)'s loop and `V9a`'s pinned-constants block need no count adjustment.

**Cons.**
- `V18a`'s `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS` partition flag for item 5 flips from "verified-otherwise" to "factory-probed", which changes the partition `V9a`'s `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES` enumerates and `V18a-T` asserts against.
- Requires touching `capability-inventory-items.md` item 5 to say capability 5 is factory-probable via dual coverage (it currently only forward-links to step (b) and step (c) without claiming factory-probable status).

**Risks.** A future contributor reading "shared as the second function member of capability 3" may try to double-count the member into the per-capability tally and trip the build-time count assertion. The "counted once" clause must stay explicit.

### Option B — Treat capability 5 as verified-otherwise; partition stays `{1, 2, 3, 4, 6}`

**Approach.** Keep capability 5 outside the factory-probable subset and recharacterise the `AgentSession.prototype.abort` bullet as a probe of capability 3's handle surface that *incidentally* discharges a portion of capability 5's obligation. The shared annotation is downgraded from a partition claim to a cross-reference note.

**Spec edits.** In step (c)'s opening keep the item list `{1, 2, 3, 4, 6}` and the "five capabilities" / nine-member tally; reword the joint-coverage sentence to "Item 5 (cancellation propagation) is **not** itself factory-probable — its obligation is discharged by step (b) (the `AbortSignal` / `AbortController` shape probe) plus the spawn-time presence of `AgentSession.prototype.abort`, the latter probed at factory time as part of capability 3's handle surface (see the `AgentSession.prototype.abort` bullet below). The dual mechanism is owned by [SDK capability inventory item 5](./capability-inventory-items.md#sdk-cap-cancellation-propagation)." Rewrite the bullet annotation to "(capability 3; also the cancellation primitive the runtime invokes ...)" and move the capability-5 cross-reference into the bullet body.

**Pros.**
- Preserves `V18a`'s existing item-5 partition flag ("verified-otherwise") and `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES`' existing 5-element shape; no leaf needs to flip its assertion direction.
- Keeps the per-capability tally arithmetic trivial (no "shared member counted once" clause).

**Cons.**
- The factory-probable/verified-otherwise partition no longer cleanly tracks "which capabilities have a factory-time check"; capability 5 has one but is classified as verified-otherwise. The classification becomes a convention rather than a behavioural property.
- Asymmetric with how capability 3's handle-surface coverage is described: the same `AgentSession.prototype.abort` probe is treated as a full partition claim for capability 3 but only an incidental contribution for capability 5.

**Risks.** A future bump of `AgentSession`'s handle surface that splits `abort` into capability-5-only territory (e.g. moves it off the `AgentSession.prototype` to a sibling type) would need to re-author both the bullet and the inventory entry to keep the convention; under Option A the same change is just a partition re-flag.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A**. The cleaner invariant is "the factory-probable subset is exactly the set of capabilities the factory probe runs at least one check for", and Option A is the only reading that preserves that invariant under the current step-(c) bullet list. Resolve in this order:

1. Edit `capability-probe.md` step (c) per Option A (item list to `{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}`; "six capabilities, nine function members" with the shared-member-counted-once clause; reworded joint-coverage sentence).
2. Edit `capability-inventory-items.md` item 5 to state capability 5 is factory-probable via dual coverage (step (b) + the `AgentSession.prototype.abort` bullet of step (c)).
3. Flip `V18a`'s `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS` item-5 partition flag from "verified-otherwise" to "factory-probed" and the matching `V18a-T` assertion; update `V9a`'s `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES` literal to `{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}`.

Edge case for the implementer: `V9a`'s build-time per-capability member-count assertion must explicitly state that capability 3 and capability 5 *share* the `AgentSession.prototype.abort` function member, so naïve `sum(per_capability_counts) === FUNCTION_MEMBER_COUNT` becomes `6+1 ≠ 9`. Either encode the partition as `{capability → members[]}` and assert `union(members).size === 9` (preferred — no special case), or keep a numeric tally and add an explicit `SHARED_MEMBERS = [{ member: "AgentSession.prototype.abort", capabilities: [3, 5] }]` carve-out the count assertion subtracts.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable implementers will partition `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES` differently — one writes `{1, 2, 3, 4, 6}` from the item list and member tally, the other writes `{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}` from the dual-coverage sentence and the bullet annotation. The `V18a` partition assertion that consumes `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES` will accept whichever choice was made (it is a self-consistency check, not an external pin), so the divergence silently propagates from the spec into `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS`' factory-probed/verified-otherwise flags and from there into the `loom/load/host-incompatible` diagnostic interpretation when an `AgentSession.prototype.abort`-missing host fails the probe.
