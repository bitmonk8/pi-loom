## Finding

`binder-inference.md` justifies its load-bearing assertion that `options.signal` is `loomAbort.signal` and *always defined* with the phrase "see **Cancellation source** below". `capability-inventory-items.md` item 5 (*Cancellation propagation*) likewise sources the loom-side `AbortController` wrapping rule and the listener-and-`abort()` mechanism to "**Cancellation source** and **Subagent session lifecycle** above". In both files these are bold, unlinked spans with directional cues ("below" / "above") that read as same-page references.

Neither target section is on those pages. The **Cancellation source** paragraph lives in the sibling file `host-interfaces-core.md` (it carries an anchor, `#cancellation-source`), and **Subagent session lifecycle** is `PIC-9` in `subagent.md` (anchor `#pic-9`). The directional words therefore resolve to nothing on-page, and the bold spans offer no link to the actual owning files. A reader of the binder-call page cannot reach the paragraph that establishes the non-optional-`signal` invariant (the only place that states "Because `loomAbort.signal` is always defined …"), and a reader of inventory item 5 cannot reach the wrapping-rule and disposal-mechanism text, without grepping the corpus by hand.

The anchors needed to repair the references already exist, so the fix is a mechanical link-and-wording correction; there is no missing normative content.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `8395c03` (2026-05-04, *resolve "Cancellation source ctx.signal is undefined when a slash-handler starts"*); `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04, *docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set*)
**History:** `git log -S` traces the bold spans to `8395c03`, which authored the **Cancellation source** section and the "see **Cancellation source** below / above" references while the entire Pi Integration Contract was one file (`docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md`); at that point the bold spans were valid same-page references (unlinked, but the directional words resolved). `f5e89f4` ran `docs/_tools/split_spec.py` to shard the corpus under the size cap, relocating the **Cancellation source** paragraph into `host-interfaces-core.md` and **Subagent session lifecycle** into `subagent.md` while copying the referencing prose verbatim into the new `binder-inference.md` and `capability-inventory-items.md` (`git show f5e89f4` confirms the "see **Cancellation source** below" line was carried over unchanged). The defect is the interaction: a mechanical split turned the in-page bold directionals into stale cross-file references without rewriting them into links. The durable fix is to make the references resolvable links, not to revert either commit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Convert the bold cross-references into hyperlinks to the anchored owning sections and drop the now-incorrect directional words:

- In `binder-inference.md`, replace `see **Cancellation source** below` with a link to `host-interfaces-core.md#cancellation-source` (e.g. `see [Cancellation source](./host-interfaces-core.md#cancellation-source)`).
- In `capability-inventory-items.md` item 5, replace `in **Cancellation source** and **Subagent session lifecycle** above` with links to `host-interfaces-core.md#cancellation-source` and `subagent.md#pic-9` respectively.

Both anchors already exist (`<a id="cancellation-source">` in `host-interfaces-core.md`; `<a id="pic-9">` on **PIC-9. Subagent session lifecycle** in `subagent.md`), so no new anchors are needed.

Edge cases for the implementer: the same stale-bold-with-wrong-directional pattern recurs at `capability-probe.md` ("see **Subagent session lifecycle** and **Cancellation source** below"), which is also a cross-file reference; it is outside this finding's named scope but resolves with the identical link substitution. By contrast, the in-file `**Cancellation source**` references within `host-interfaces-core.md` itself (the `signal` field comments and the *Pi-side slash-handler promise lifecycle* paragraph) are genuinely same-page, so their directional words are correct and only an optional intra-page link would change.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer working from `binder-inference.md` or inventory item 5 cannot follow the "below"/"above" cue to the section that establishes the "`loomAbort.signal` always defined" invariant and the `AbortController` wrapping/disposal mechanism, because those sections are in sibling files and the references are not links. No two implementers diverge on runtime behaviour, but verifying the non-optional-`signal` precondition costs a manual corpus search.
