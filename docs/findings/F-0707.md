## Finding

The Block-expressions section of `grammar.md` ends its statement-form-block paragraph with a single-form carve-out:

> A present tail `Expr` is evaluated and its value is discarded (the block produces no value), except that a tail `Expr` of the `@…?` form still triggers `?` early-return on failure, because `?` desugars to `return Err(e)` per [Return Statement](./return.md).

Read literally, this restricts the early-return behaviour to the query-with-postfix-`?` shape and leaves the spec silent on the other postfix-`?` operands the grammar otherwise admits. The `?` operator is universal across `Result<_, QueryError>`-typed operands: `return.md` states *"the `?` operator's `Err`-arm desugaring is literally `return Err(e)`; no separate magic is needed"*, and `errors-and-results/error-model.md` §ERR-18 enumerates the admissible operands as *"a `@`-query, an `invoke(...)`, or an explicit `Ok(...)` / `Err(...)`"* (with `tool-calls.md` and `invocation.md` confirming `?` on tool calls and on `invoke` children). A careful reader who cross-checks `return.md` and ERR-18 reconciles the carve-out to the universal rule; a reader who treats the grammar paragraph as authoritative concludes that `invoke(child)?`, `tool_call(...)?`, or `f(...)?` in the tail position of a statement-form `if` / `for` / `while` body does *not* propagate `Err`, contradicting the universal desugaring.

The defect is a wording one — the carve-out picks one concrete tail-`Expr` shape (`@…?`) as if it were the only one that could carry `?`, rather than naming the postfix-`?` operator as the trigger and leaving the operand set to ERR-18.

**Introducing commit:** `816852b` (2026-06-05) — *pi-loom spec: resolve "Block expressions — no grammar production for statement-form if/for/while bodies"*
**History:** Before `816852b`, `grammar.md` distinguished only *expression-position blocks* and *body blocks*; statement-form `if` / `while` arm bodies were lumped under "expression-position blocks" and no statement-form-block paragraph existed. `816852b` introduced the `StmtBlock`, `IfStmt`, `ElseClause`, `WhileStmt`, and `ForStmt` productions and the new paragraph carrying the tail-`Expr`-discarded rule together with the *"except that a tail `Expr` of the `@…?` form still triggers `?` early-return on failure"* clause. The clause has been verbatim since that commit; subsequent edits to `grammar.md` (`057159c`, `5c8041a`, `c3b7c87`, `e1f5c2a`) did not touch this paragraph. `git log --all -G '@…\?' -- docs/spec_topics/grammar.md` and `git log --all -S 'still triggers \`?\` early-return'` both surface `816852b` as the sole introducing commit. The defect is inception-state in this paragraph: it entered the corpus already restricting the carve-out to the `@…?` shape, rather than being a regression from a broader earlier wording.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Generalise the carve-out clause from naming one concrete tail-`Expr` shape to naming the operator that drives the behaviour. Replace:

> A present tail `Expr` is evaluated and its value is discarded (the block produces no value), except that a tail `Expr` of the `@…?` form still triggers `?` early-return on failure, because `?` desugars to `return Err(e)` per [Return Statement](./return.md).

with:

> A present tail `Expr` is evaluated and its value is discarded (the block produces no value). A tail `Expr` whose outermost form is the postfix `?` operator (over any operand admitted by [Errors and Results — `?` operator](./errors-and-results/error-model.md#err-18) — a `@`-query, an `invoke(...)`, a tool-call, or an explicit `Ok(...)` / `Err(...)`) is the one exception: it still early-returns `Err` from the enclosing function or top-level loom on failure, because `?` desugars to `return Err(e)` per [Return Statement](./return.md). This is not a `StmtBlock`-specific exception — it is the universal `?` desugaring restated at the tail-discarded rule's boundary so the discard rule does not appear to override it.

Edge cases the implementer must keep in mind when wiring the rule:

- The exception fires on *any* `?`-bearing tail expression, not only on `@…?`. `invoke(...)?`, `f()?` where `f` returns `Result<_, QueryError>`, and `Ok(x)? / Err(e)?` all early-return identically.
- The exception is about the *outermost* form being `?`. A tail `Expr` that contains a `?` deeper inside (e.g. `g(h()?)`) still propagates on `h()?` per the universal rule, but the discard-vs-propagate distinction at the `StmtBlock` boundary is about whether the tail's *value* is discarded; an inner `?` early-returns before the outer expression's value is computed, so the boundary rule is moot for inner `?`s.
- The enclosing scope still has to be `Result<_, QueryError>`-compatible per ERR-18 / `loom/parse/question-outside-result-fn`; this rule does not relax that precondition. A `?` at the tail of a `StmtBlock` inside a non-`Result`-returning function still fires `loom/parse/question-outside-result-fn` at parse time, before any runtime evaluation question arises.
- No change is needed to `return.md`, ERR-18, or the diagnostic registry; this is a wording fix on one paragraph that brings `grammar.md` into agreement with the rules those pages already pin.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two implementers acting on the spec diverge on what runs at the tail of a statement-form `if` / `for` / `while` body. An implementer who treats the `grammar.md` carve-out as authoritative emits the tail `Expr` of `invoke(child)?` or `f()?` as an `Ok`-unwrap-and-discard (no early-return on `Err`), violating ERR-18 and `return.md`'s universal desugaring. An implementer who reads `return.md` / ERR-18 as authoritative early-returns on any `Result<_, QueryError>` operand. Authors writing `for area in areas { invoke(reviewer, area)? }` to short-circuit the loop on the first child failure get correct behaviour from one implementation and silent error-suppression from the other.
