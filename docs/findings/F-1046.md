## Problem

Item (e)'s enumeration paragraph (anchor `bump-checklist-slash-dispatch-serialisation`) closes by calling a newly-discovered glob-matching `.js` file "audit-positive evidence of a new driver mode" but states no consequence at that point — the reader cannot tell whether that evidence flips the verdict, is purely informational, or requires further checks. The phrase "audit-positive evidence" is defined nowhere in `version-bump-step2.md`. The actual consequence — that such a file is a fail trigger — appears only as the third bullet of the `**fail** outcome` list several paragraphs later, far enough that an auditor recording an `(e.ii)` verdict can miss it. Two reasonable auditors reading only the enumeration paragraph can therefore record divergent verdicts on `(e.ii)` for the same candidate minor.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit-introduction
**Introducing commits:** `5d73cc3` (2026-06-07) — *"pi-loom spec: resolve 'Item (e) fail predicate operator-precedence ambiguity and buried sub-outcomes'"*
**History:** The phrase "audit-positive evidence of a new driver mode" entered the corpus in commit `57d8235` (2026-05-11, *"pi-loom spec: resolve 'T23 per-session slash-handler serialisation citation'"*), which authored the original item (e). In that authoring the phrase was followed *inline*, in the same paragraph and with no intervening blank line, by the consequence: *"...load-tested against; a fail outcome — the auditor cannot demonstrate that the candidate minor's `prompt` body still establishes the precondition (b) behavioural property..."* — so the trigger and its consequence read as one continuous sentence and the ambiguity this finding describes did not exist.

Commit `5d73cc3` (the bump-procedure restructure that split the item (e) wall-of-text into discrete fail-condition bullets to address the operator-precedence ambiguity in the original run-on sentence) severed the adjacency. The "audit-positive evidence of a new driver mode" phrase was left at the tail of the enumeration paragraph; the consequence was moved into a separately-introduced *"A **fail** outcome is recorded when **any** of the following conditions holds"* bullet list three paragraphs later. The restructure resolved its named scope (precedence ambiguity, sub-outcome burial) but did not re-read the enumeration paragraph against the new structure to check whether any of its trigger phrases still depended on the now-removed run-on continuation for their consequence. The 2026-06-11 collapse commit (`81bf52a`, *"docs: collapse spec/ section pages into spec_topics/"*) moved the page from `pi-integration-contract.md` to `version-bump-step2.md` without further editing the affected text. Subsequent item-(e)-touching commits (`5dfc9aa`, `1ca13ec`, `80fcd63`) further extended the page but did not revisit this paragraph either.

## Solution approach

Clarify item (e)'s enumeration paragraph (anchor `bump-checklist-slash-dispatch-serialisation`) so the closing `audit-positive evidence of a new driver mode` clause names its consequence — that the file is a fail trigger for `(e.ii)` — at the point the trigger is described, rather than leaving the consequence reachable only via the `**fail** outcome` bullet list below. Keep the third `**fail** outcome` bullet (the newly-discovered glob-match trigger) aligned with the enumeration-paragraph wording so the two describe one trigger, not two.

## Solution constraints

- Do not break the paragraph chain: the enumeration paragraph must remain reachable to the downstream *known-fragile-evidence* / *conservative-posture* prose that anchors on it — do not resolve this by lifting the trigger out of the enumeration paragraph entirely.
