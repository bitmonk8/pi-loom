## Finding

The opening paragraph of `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics.md` makes a load-bearing claim about Pi's extension loader: that Pi "would otherwise push" factory-time bootstrap registration / subscription failures into `LoadExtensionsResult.errors`, and that loom emits its own `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed` for that same class instead. The claim carries no anchor citation. Every other concrete Pi-shape claim in the corpus is backed by a `host-interfaces-core.md` or `audit-recognised-shapes.md` pin (the [loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin](../pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md#pi-sdk-pin) site, the `Model<Api>` pin, the `ExtensionAPI` pin, etc.); this one stands alone, which leaves a reader no anchor to re-validate on the next Pi minor bump.

The interplay claim also depends on a no-double-report invariant — Pi must not also be writing the same failure into `LoadExtensionsResult.errors` while loom emits `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed`. That invariant is, in fact, already pinned in the corpus: `pi-integration-contract/extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md` requires "The factory MUST NOT throw out of `default function (pi: ExtensionAPI)`; per-call `try`/`catch` around each step keeps the failure local to its granularity rule above," and the underlying-error coercion clause guards against a synchronous `TypeError` escaping the per-call `try`/`catch`. Because every factory-time and `session_start`-time SDK call is wrapped, no throw ever reaches Pi's `loadExtensions(...)` `try`/`catch` — so Pi's `errors` array stays empty for these failures by construction. `diagnostics.md` never says so. A reader of the intro has to derive that conclusion themselves from a different topic file, which defeats the purpose of asserting the interplay in the first place.

Both gaps live in a single intro paragraph and resolve through a single edit: anchor the Pi-side behavioural claim and cross-link the no-double-report invariant to the swallow contract that already exists in `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md`.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Rewrite the second sentence of `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics.md`'s opening paragraph to (a) cite the Pi-side surface and (b) name the swallow contract that makes the no-double-report invariant hold. A worked phrasing:

> Pi's own `LoadExtensionsResult.errors` field is **not** used: that field is the return-shape of Pi's extension loader (`loadExtensions(...)` in `@earendil-works/pi-coding-agent`'s `dist/core/extensions/loader.js` at the [loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin](../pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md#pi-sdk-pin)), which only populates an entry when the extension's `import()` or its factory `await` throws past loom's per-call `try`/`catch` wrappers. The loom extension factory's **never-throw boundary** (per [Pi Integration Contract — Extension-bootstrap SDK failures](../pi-integration-contract/extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md)) guarantees that does not happen for the factory-time and `session_start`-time SDK registration / subscription failures in scope here: every such call is wrapped in a per-call `try`/`catch` that swallows the throw and emits [`loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed`](./diagnostics/code-registry-load.md) through the System-notes fallback chain, so Pi's `errors` array stays empty for this class by construction and there is no double-reporting hazard.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- **Citation home.** If a future audit prefers all Pi-shape pins to live in one anchor file, move the `loadExtensions` shape claim into `pi-integration-contract/audit-recognised-shapes.md` (or `host-interfaces-core.md`) and have `diagnostics.md` cite that anchor instead of citing the dist path directly. The intro paragraph is the wrong home for a long-lived shape pin; a citation hop is correct.
- **Pin bump procedure.** The bump checklist at `pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md` already enumerates host-shape re-validations; add a one-line item for the `LoadExtensionsResult` shape (or note that it is covered transitively by the chosen citation anchor) so that the next Pi minor bump verifies the `errors: Array<{ path, error }>` shape and the population rule together.
- **No diagnostic-shape change.** The `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed` row in `diagnostics/code-registry-load.md` already pins the trigger, payload, and channel; do not duplicate that detail in the `diagnostics.md` intro. The intro's job is the channel-ownership framing and the citation; the diagnostic detail stays at its owner row.
- **Do not retract the claim.** Demoting the sentence to non-normative would re-open the channel ambiguity the finding's ancestor (`ac8c3af`) was originally resolving. The fix is to cite, not to weaken.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The Pi-shape claim is correct against the [loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin](../pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md#pi-sdk-pin) (`pi-coding-agent`'s `dist/core/extensions/loader.js` `loadExtensions(...)` only pushes into `errors` when the extension's `import()` or factory `await` throws past loom's per-call wrappers, and the swallow contract guarantees that does not happen). What the gap costs is **re-validation cost on each Pi minor bump**: a reader checking the bump checklist has no anchor for "does Pi still populate `errors` only on factory throw?" and no signpost to the swallow contract that makes the invariant hold. Two implementers could ship correct code while still disagreeing about whether the spec asserts a Pi behavioural claim (re-validate it) or a loom design claim (no re-validation needed).
