## Problem

Step 7 of `version-bump-triggers.md` is the sole spec-side definition of how the rename-detection arm of the binder strict-capability probe fires, yet its English description — the SDK surface-inventory assertion "catches 'field present on `Model<Api>` namespace but not under the probed name'" — is internally contradictory: a name-keyed surface inventory cannot observe a member that exists under a name other than the probed `strictCapable`. Two reasonable implementers therefore build incompatible gates: one a name-presence check that collapses the arm into a duplicate of the absence-under-the-probed-name gate, the other an unspecified semantic detector (allow-list, type-aware walk, or per-bump fixture — each with a materially different scope). Downstream consumers (`strict-capability-absence-pin` in `audit-target-categories.md`, the `strict-capability-probe` row of `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY`, and the `V18c` / `V18c-T` two-arm enumeration) all defer to step 7 for the mechanism, so the ambiguity propagates. Under the name-presence reading the rename catch-window is silently lost — a renamed Pi indicator leaves the probe bound to `strictCapable`, returns `undefined`, and degrades to the `loom/load/binder-model-strict-capability-unknown` branch with no build-time signal.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit-introduction
**Introducing commits:** `52219c4` (2026-05-06) — *Address all 41 spec-review findings*
**History:** `git log --all -G "field present on .*Model<Api>"` against the spec corpus returns `52219c4` as the introducing commit; the diff at that commit shows step 7's *Edge cases* bullet in `plan_topics/v16-binder.md` and the parallel step-7 paragraph in `spec.md` both grow the literal phrase "field present on `Model<Api>` namespace but not under the probed name" in the same edit, and no earlier history contains it (`288f191` and `fecb504`, the preceding spec-set commits, do not). Subsequent edits — `54c4e39` (anchor the `Model<Api>` type to the model-registry pin), `bfe08cf` (drop the dual-package attribution), `5e73072` (rename the npm scope), `f5e89f4` (split the monolithic spec into `spec_topics/`), `f10c915` and the per-finding sweeps cited in `git log --follow` on `version-bump-triggers.md` — preserve the contradictory phrase verbatim across the split into `version-bump-triggers.md`. The defect entered with the phrase itself: the spec-review-driven rewrite that landed the rename-detection-arm cross-reference at the same commit it pinned the assertion's English description introduced the ambiguity between the arm's *name* (rename-detection) and the *mechanism* the prose suggests (name-presence under the probed name).

## Solution approach

Rewrite step 7's mechanism description in `version-bump-triggers.md` so the rename-detection arm and the absence-under-the-probed-name arm become separately-specified gates, each naming its detection mechanism directly rather than describing its effect. Eliminate the self-contradictory "field present on `Model<Api>` namespace but not under the probed name" phrasing, which suggests a name-keyed assertion can observe a differently-named member. Align the rewritten arms and the `strict-capability-absence-pin` paragraph in `audit-target-categories.md` on a single source of truth for the probed identifier rather than independently restating the literal `strictCapable`.
