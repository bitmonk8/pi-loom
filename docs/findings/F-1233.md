## Finding

Step 7 of the Pi version-bump procedure pins a single English description of the gate that catches a renamed `strictCapable` indicator: the SDK surface-inventory assertion "catches 'field present on `Model<Api>` namespace but not under the probed name'". The sentence is the sole spec-side definition of *how* the rename-detection arm fires; every downstream consumer (the `strict-capability-absence-pin` paragraph in `audit-target-categories.md`, the `strict-capability-probe` row in `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY`, the two-arm test enumeration in `V18c-T` / `V18c`) names the arm but defers to step 7 for the mechanism.

Read literally, "field present on `Model<Api>` namespace but not under the probed name" is internally inconsistent: a *field present under a different name* is, by construction, **not** observable by reading the probed name `strictCapable` — there is no syntactic check on a name-keyed surface inventory that can detect "a member exists whose semantics correspond to strict-capability but whose name is not `strictCapable`". Two reasonable implementers therefore diverge on what the gate actually is:

- *Reading A — name-presence on the probed constant.* The assertion checks that `Model<Api>` exposes a member named exactly `strictCapable`. Under this reading the arm fires on **absence** under the probed name, not on a rename — making the arm a duplicate of the `strict-capability-absence-pin` "absence-under-the-probed-name gate" rather than the rename-detection arm `audit-recognised-shapes.md` explicitly cites step 7 as completing.
- *Reading B — semantic detection of a renamed indicator.* The assertion somehow recognises a strict-capability-like field under a non-`strictCapable` name (an enumerated alternative-names allow-list, a TypeScript-type-aware property walk, a contributor-supplied fixture-of-renamed-indicator per bump). The mechanism is unspecified and the candidate mechanisms have materially different scopes — an allow-list pins a closed set of permitted rename targets, a type-aware walk commits to a TypeScript-program-load tooling choice PIC otherwise declines to require, and a per-bump fixture punts the detection to contributor discipline.

The plan-side leaf prose (`V18c` / `V18c-T`) papers over the ambiguity by re-describing the arm as "a reachable `Model<Api>` declaration exposes a strict-capability indicator under a name other than the probed `strictCapable`", which begs the same question — *how* the gate recognises an indicator under a different name — and consumes the `strict-capability-probe` entry's `probedName` payload field, which only names the probed identifier and carries no rename-target enumeration.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Rewrite the step-7 mechanism sentence to (1) name the detection mechanism directly rather than describe its effect, and (2) split the two arms so the rename-detection arm and the absence-under-the-probed-name arm have separately-specified gates whose payloads cite a single source-of-truth field on the `strict-capability-probe` row of `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY`.

Concretely, replace the current single English sentence with two anchored sub-clauses keyed on a `probedName` payload field (already named in `audit-target-categories.md`'s `strict-capability-absence-pin` — the same payload field per F-0603's resolution applies here):

- *Absence-under-the-probed-name arm.* The SDK surface-inventory assertion (step 2(a)'s literal-read branch table arm for the `strict-capability-probe` `kind`) fails red when the imported `Model<Api>` type does not expose a member whose name equals the `probedName` payload field on the `strict-capability-probe` row of `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY`. This is the gate the `strict-capability-absence-pin` paragraph cites as the completion of the rename-detection arm.
- *Rename-detection arm.* The rename-detection arm is **not** an automatic semantic-detection gate — PIC does not commit `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` to a closed enumeration of permitted rename targets nor to a TypeScript-type-aware property walk. Detection is mechanically backed by a per-bump contributor-authored *renamed-indicator fixture* under the editorial-review checklist of [`version-bump-step2.md`](./version-bump-step2.md): a fixture whose body declares a `Model<Api>` whose strict-capability indicator is named other than the `probedName` (e.g. `strictModeCapable`), against which the surface-inventory assertion is run; a red fail proves the gate fires. The contributor MUST author such a fixture when the candidate `@earendil-works/pi-ai` minor exposes the indicator under a different name (the trigger condition step 7 already states), in the same edit that renames `probedName`. The arm's standing name in spec / plan prose is "rename-detection arm" but its mechanical backing is the fixture-plus-assertion pair, not an automatic detector.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The rewrite MUST keep the `probedName` payload field as the single source of truth for the probed identifier — `version-bump-triggers.md` step 7 and `audit-recognised-shapes.md`'s `strict-capability-absence-pin` operative clause MUST cite the same payload field rather than independently restating the literal `strictCapable`, on the same footing F-0603 already pinned for the absence side.
- The two-arm split MUST be reflected in `V18c-T`'s test enumeration so that the rename-detection arm's negative fixture is named as a contributor-authored per-bump artefact rather than as a standing test in the test suite — `V18c-T`'s current prose treats both arms as standing negative fixtures, which is consistent with the rename-detection arm being fixture-backed but should be tightened to name the per-bump contributor obligation that authors the fixture.
- The arm-name "rename-detection" remains accurate as the arm's *purpose*, but the spec text MUST avoid the phrase "field present on `Model<Api>` namespace but not under the probed name" — it suggests a name-keyed assertion can observe a differently-named field, which is the contradiction this finding flags.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two implementers reading step 7 at face value will build incompatible gates — one a name-presence check on `strictCapable` (collapsing the rename-detection arm into a duplicate of the absence arm), the other a semantic detection of unknown shape. The rename-detection arm is the gate that catches a Pi `@earendil-works/pi-ai` minor that ships strict-capability under a renamed field; under Reading A that catch-window is silently lost — a Pi rename ships, the loom-side probe stays bound to `strictCapable`, returns `undefined`, and the runtime degrades to the universal-`W` `loom/load/binder-model-strict-capability-unknown` branch in production with no build-time signal that a rename occurred.
