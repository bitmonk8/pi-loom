## Finding

The paragraph beginning `***loom 1.0.0 rejected-candidate record (closed).***` in `docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings/ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` enumerates three ceiling candidates that were *considered and rejected during loom 1.0.0* — regex backtracking on regex-bearing built-ins, JSON-Schema width pathologies, and pattern-match recursion depth — and declares itself "a closed record of loom 1.0.0 worked examples" that future spec-editors "MUST NOT extend." This is historical deliberation content: it records what the loom 1.0.0 authors discussed and chose not to include, not a rule the runtime or any future editor must apply.

It is wedged between two paragraphs that are unambiguously normative: the `Routing obligation for future ceilings` paragraph immediately before it (which carries the four-axis closure MUST and the introduction / retirement / relaxation forms) and the `Release gate` paragraph immediately after it (which makes GOV-15 reviewer-inspection block release on a closure defect). A reader walking the four-axis methodology cannot tell from typography or surrounding prose that this paragraph is descriptive-of-history rather than prescriptive-of-conformance, and the explicit "do not extend" instruction reads as a normative authoring rule on its own — but the only obligation the paragraph actually creates is "future editors don't append here," which is bookkeeping about the appendix itself, not about the spec subject.

The three closed examples remain useful as worked rationale for why the four-axis closure was deemed sufficient at the loom 1.0.0 boundary; they belong in a design-rationale appendix that the methodology paragraph cross-references, not inline inside the methodology.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move the `***loom 1.0.0 rejected-candidate record (closed).***` paragraph out of `ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` into a design-rationale destination, replace it in place with a one-sentence pointer, and drop the "do not extend" sentence (it is bookkeeping about the appendix and stops being needed once the appendix is a separate page that future editors can simply not edit).

Concretely:

- **Destination.** Create `docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings/ceiling-design-rationale.md` as a new sibling page under the existing `hard-ceilings/` subdirectory. Preferred over `related-work.md` (which is reserved for situating loom against external prior art, not for internal design-history) and over `future-considerations/` (which holds *deferred* work — items the spec might add later — not *rejected* candidates the spec deliberately did not add). The new page carries an H1 (e.g. "Hard ceilings — design rationale"), a one-paragraph preamble naming it as a non-normative companion to `ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md`, and the three rejected-candidate bullets reformatted into a short subsection per candidate (regex backtracking, JSON-Schema width, pattern-match recursion). The two inline-link references (`expressions.md` for the no-regex stance, `schema-subset.md` for the *Explicitly not supported* enumeration) move with the bullets.
- **Replacement in place.** In `ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md`, between the *Routing obligation* paragraph and the *Release gate* paragraph, insert a single sentence: *"For three ceiling candidates considered and rejected during loom 1.0.0 and the rationale for treating each as a closure-list fall-through rather than a separate ceiling, see [Hard ceilings — design rationale](./ceiling-design-rationale.md)."* No additional content; no "do not extend" wording (the new page is itself the design-rationale file, and future editors performing the four-axis check supply their own per-edit rationale per the *Routing obligation* paragraph that already says so).
- **Cross-references.** Update `V16a` and `V16a-T` in `docs/plan_topics/` to cite the new page alongside `ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` in their `Spec.` line. No other plan leaf cites the affected paragraph, and no other spec page links into the closed-record sentence (verified via `grep -rn "rejected-candidate record" docs/`).
- **Edge cases.** (i) The new page is non-normative; its preamble must say so explicitly so the GOV-15 reviewer-inspection step does not treat it as part of the ceiling-set closure. (ii) The pointer sentence stays inside the same `## No additional loom 1.0 runtime ceiling applies` section so the reader walking the four-axis methodology hits the pointer in narrative order. (iii) Do not move the *Five-site list co-edit obligation* paragraph that follows the *Release gate* — that paragraph is normative co-edit machinery for ceiling #4's per-boundary table and is unrelated to the rejected-candidate record.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

No runtime behaviour changes. The cost is reader-load on the normative methodology paragraph and a permanent invitation to misread the "do not extend" sentence as a methodology rule rather than appendix bookkeeping. Implementers do not diverge.
