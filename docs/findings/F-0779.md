## Finding

The opening **System notes** paragraph of `runtime-event-channel.md` describes the registered renderer as one that "formats these as one-line, dim-styled notes in the transcript." That sentence pins concrete visual styling — line count and a colour treatment — on a page whose contract is the channel envelope: the `pi.sendMessage` call shape, the four `details` payload variants, the per-variant `display`/`content` matrix, the always-log set, the `RuntimeEvent` shape, and the cascade-twin / dedup rules. None of those load-bearing requirements depend on whether the renderer emits one line or many, nor on whether it dims the text.

The renderer's contract is owned a page over, in `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md` under **Renderer registration**, which already specifies that styling MUST be sourced from the `theme: Theme` parameter ("dim text colour, etc.") rather than hard-coded. That clause is the right home for any styling commitment; restating "one-line, dim-styled" on the channel page duplicates the topic onto a non-owner and pre-commits a presentation choice the renderer page is more equipped to revise (e.g. multi-line expanded rendering keyed on `options.expanded`, which the renderer page already reserves as a future revision).

The load-bearing requirements the channel-page sentence does carry — that the notes persist in the session transcript for replay and `/tree` navigation rather than appearing transiently — are independent of the styling claim and would survive its removal.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** `fae85d3` (2026-05-04, *Tighten spec for implementation-plan readiness*)
**History:** The sentence "A `pi.registerMessageRenderer("loom-system-note", ...)` formats them as one-line, dim-styled notes in the transcript" was authored in `fae85d3` as part of the original **System notes** paragraph in monolithic `spec.md`. `fecb504` (2026-05-04) moved the paragraph verbatim into `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/runtime-event-channel.md` during the per-topic split; later commits — `9eea3d9` (pinned the `pi.registerMessageRenderer` signature) and `b077c07` / `78a2f94` (carved out the **Renderer registration** section in `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md` and parenthesised the cross-reference) — established the renderer page as the styling owner without removing the duplicate prescription from the channel page. The defect therefore exists from the moment the channel-page paragraph was written; later restructuring made it redundant rather than introducing it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace "formats these as one-line, dim-styled notes in the transcript" with a behavioural phrasing that names only what the channel page is the owner of — persistence and the cross-reference to the styling owner. Concrete edit: change the sentence to read along the lines of "A `pi.registerMessageRenderer("loom-system-note", renderer)` registered by the loom extension factory (see [Renderer registration](./extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md#renderer-registration)) renders these notes in the transcript; the rendered form (line count, theme-sourced styling) is owned by the renderer-registration section." Leave the following sentence — "The custom-type approach (rather than `ctx.ui.notify(...)`) is chosen because notes need to persist in the session transcript for replay and `/tree` navigation, not just appear transiently." — untouched; it carries the load-bearing persistence rationale and is on-topic for this page.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- Do **not** delete or modify the **Renderer registration** › `theme` clause in `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md`; it remains the sole authority for styling and references `dim` as an illustrative example of theme-sourced colour.
- Preserve the existing parenthetical link `(see [Renderer registration](./extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md#renderer-registration))` so the cross-reference still resolves.
- The downstream `display`/`content` matrix, the `RuntimeEvent` shape, PIC-1, PIC-21, the fallback chain, and the per-variant `display: false` / `content: ""` rules are all untouched by this edit; no other paragraph on the page mentions line count or dim styling.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The styling claim is observably redundant with `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md`'s theme-sourced styling rule and harmless to implementers, who follow the renderer-page contract. The only concrete risk is presentation drift: if a future revision widens the renderer (e.g. an `options.expanded` branch that renders multi-line) the channel-page sentence becomes silently stale and contradicts the renderer page.
