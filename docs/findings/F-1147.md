## Finding

The typed-query bullet in `pi-integration-contract/conversation-drive.md` defines the loop as a "two-phase tool-loop conversation" and enumerates the phases inline as `(1)` *free phase* and `(2)` *forced respond turn*. The phase-2 clause names exactly one trigger for the forced respond turn — "once the model emits a plain text turn" — and is structured as the definition of when phase 2 fires. The literal reading is that phase 2 is reachable only after a free-phase plain-text turn, which would make it unreachable at `max_rounds: 0` (where no free-phase turn is ever issued) and would conflict with CIO-4's `max_rounds`-final branch, which dispatches the forced respond turn at `slot_count == max_rounds` whether or not the model has had a free-phase opportunity to emit plain text.

The very next sentence of the same bullet does pin the CIO-4 entry point — "the forced respond turn is the exempt-routed terminator dispatched by CIO-4's `max_rounds`-final branch … the runtime issues it on every typed query reaching that branch — including `max_rounds: 0` where it is the only turn" — so the bullet as a whole is internally consistent and the `query-tool-loop.md` two-phase enumeration explicitly pins the `max_rounds: 0` boundary case in its phase-2 step. The defect is local: the phase-2 enumeration sentence in `conversation-drive.md` is the operative *definition* of when the forced respond turn fires, and reading it in isolation gives the wrong trigger set. An implementer who treats the enumeration as the contract and the follow-up sentence as commentary will skip the `max_rounds: 0` and `max_rounds`-final dispatches.

The fix is to harmonise the phase-2 trigger clause with the rest of the bullet (and with `query-tool-loop.md` item 2) so that the enumeration itself names both entry points: the free-phase plain-text turn *and* the CIO-4 `max_rounds`-final branch (degenerating to "the only turn" at `max_rounds: 0`). The follow-up "exempt-routed terminator" sentence then becomes the cross-reference it was clearly intended to be rather than the sole place the second trigger is mentioned.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Rewrite the phase-2 clause so the enumeration itself lists both entry points, and demote the existing "exempt-routed terminator" follow-up sentence to a pure cross-reference. Concretely, replace the current phase-2 opening — "Once the model emits a plain text turn (provider stop reason `end_turn` / `stop`), the runtime issues one additional follow-up user turn" — with wording of the form: "The runtime issues the forced respond turn at the first of the following points to occur: (a) the model emits a plain text turn (provider stop reason `end_turn` / `stop`) during the free phase, or (b) CIO-4's `max_rounds`-final branch fires (see [Hard Runtime Ceilings — CIO-4](../hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md#cio-4)) — including at `max_rounds: 0`, where (b) holds at typed-query start and the forced respond turn is the only turn issued."

Keep the follow-up "exempt-routed terminator" sentence in place but trim it to the cross-reference and slot-accounting facts it uniquely carries (the turn is not debited against `tool_loop.max_rounds`); it no longer needs to re-establish the `max_rounds: 0` case because the enumeration covers it.

Implementer notes: (i) keep the `(end_turn / stop)` stop-reason gloss on entry (a); (ii) preserve the existing "respond-repair follow-ups restart the whole two-phase loop with a fresh `tool_loop` budget" sentence verbatim — it is unaffected; (iii) the symmetric phase-2 enumeration in `query-tool-loop.md` already names both triggers and the `max_rounds: 0` boundary, so no edit is required there beyond confirming the wording in `conversation-drive.md` remains consistent with it.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer who reads the phase-2 enumeration as the operative trigger definition and treats the follow-up "exempt-routed terminator" sentence as commentary will never dispatch the forced respond turn at `max_rounds: 0` and never dispatch it on the `max_rounds`-final branch, leaving typed queries on those paths stuck waiting for a free-phase plain-text turn that the runtime is not permitted to wait for. The bullet's later sentence and the cross-referenced `query-tool-loop.md` item 2 both correct this, so an implementer who reads the whole bullet plus the cross-reference produces the right behaviour; the defect is a phrasing trap, not a contradiction.
