## Problem

The binder inference call's option enumeration at `binder-inference.md#binder-inference-call` pins `model`, `context.*`, `options.temperature`, `options.signal`, the seed key, and `options.onResponse`, but never sets `options.maxRetries` or `options.maxRetryDelayMs`. The section's closing sentence nonetheless delegates per-attempt backoff and any server-requested `Retry-After` wait to those two `StreamOptions` fields, presupposing loom configures them. This leaves three things undefined: whether loom sets the fields at all and to what values; whether an SDK-internal wire retry counts against the HC3 "≤3 binder LLM calls per slash invocation" budget (HC3-d, at `determinism-cancellation-failure.md#per-invocation-retry-budget`); and whether loom issues one outer `complete()` call or one per HC3 slot. Two conformant implementations diverge on attempt count, latency, and `Retry-After` handling, and the HC3-d conformance test cannot be written until the call-counting semantics are fixed.

## Solution approach

Add an `options.maxRetries` bullet (and a companion disposition for `options.maxRetryDelayMs`) to the `complete()` option enumeration at `binder-inference.md#binder-inference-call`, pinning loom's SDK-level retry configuration explicitly rather than leaving it to pi-ai defaults. Rewrite the section's closing delegation sentence so it states the chosen configuration and its consequence for the HC3 budget instead of reading as an open delegation. In `determinism-cancellation-failure.md#per-invocation-retry-budget`, clarify HC3-d's "≤3 binder LLM calls" so the unit of counting (outer `complete()` invocation vs wire attempt) is unambiguous and consistent with the pinned configuration.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: HC3-d's interleaving "consumes a slot" semantics at `determinism-cancellation-failure.md#per-invocation-retry-budget` (which class's budget a cross-class retry consumes) — this finding fixes only the call-shape count boundary, i.e. what counts as one binder LLM call.
