## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/binder/binder-bypass-and-envelope.md` § *System-prompt structure (normative)* item 8 reads:

> The prompt MUST contain **a single line** that includes both the literal substring `defaulted` (case-sensitive) and at least one of the directive substrings `Do not`, `omit`, or `skip` (case-sensitive). The rest of the wording is non-normative.

The phrase "a single line" admits two incompatible readings:

1. *Exactly-one* — there MUST be exactly one matching line; a prompt that emits the directive on two lines (e.g. one for `defaulted` parameters and one for `defaulted` array elements) is non-conforming.
2. *At-least-one* — there MUST be at least one matching line; additional matching lines are permitted.

The two readings yield different conformance verdicts and therefore different conformance tests. The neighbouring items in the same list use disambiguated cardinality phrasing — item 1 ends "Exactly one such line per prompt," item 3 says "MUST appear exactly once," item 4 enumerates "one per-field line per declared field" — so the bare "a single line" in item 8 sits as the lone unpinned cardinality in the section. Because the matching-substring set (`defaulted` plus a directive token) is plausibly emissible by an alternative renderer on more than one line (e.g. once for the parameter-defaulting policy, once for an array-element-default note), the divergence is not hypothetical: it can arise from a renderer that satisfies item 8's information-content goal but trips an exactly-one reading.

The `V11d` / `V11d-T` test leaves assert "all eight structured items (1–8)" — without a pinned cardinality, the test author has to pick exactly-one or at-least-one and a second implementer's conforming-by-the-other-reading renderer would fail or pass that test for the wrong reason.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace "a single line" with "**at least one line**" in item 8. The intent of the rewrite that introduced the pinned-token form was testability of *presence*, not exclusivity; matching the at-least-one cardinality used implicitly by items 5 and 7 (which assert presence without forbidding repetition) keeps item 8 in line with its siblings and leaves renderers free to emit the directive on multiple lines if their information design wants to.

The pinned obligation should read:

> **No-invent-defaults instruction.** The prompt MUST contain **at least one line** that includes both the literal substring `defaulted` (case-sensitive) and at least one of the directive substrings `Do not`, `omit`, or `skip` (case-sensitive). The rest of the wording is non-normative.

Edge cases for the V11d / V11d-T test author:

- The conformance assertion is now "≥ 1 line of the prompt, split on `\n`, contains both `defaulted` and at least one of {`Do not`, `omit`, `skip`}". A trivially-conforming reference is the example prompt's `Do not invent values for defaulted parameters that the user did not specify; omit them.` (one line, both predicates).
- A prompt that emits the directive on two lines (e.g. one for parameter-level defaulting and one for array-element defaulting) is conforming; the test must not assert exactly-one.
- A prompt that puts `defaulted` on one line and a directive token on a different line is **not** conforming — the predicates must co-occur on the same line. This was true under the original wording and remains so; no further pin is needed.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable implementers, each reading "a single line" the way English most naturally permits, will produce different conformance tests for item 8 and may classify a third implementer's renderer in opposite directions. Because item 8 is one of only eight structural obligations in the binder system prompt and the only one whose subject (defaulting policy) is genuinely model-behavioural, the divergence is felt on the most-likely-to-be-silently-dropped rule of the set.
