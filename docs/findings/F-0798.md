## Problem

GOV-15's *Tighten* clause permits *Tighten* within loom 1.x only when no input in the loom 1.x conformance fixture suite "once it ships" is made to fail, and forbids *Tighten* "until that suite ships," but neither sentence defines what "ships" means ŌĆö a merged `main` commit, a tagged loom 1.x release containing the suite, a wired CI gate, or some other observable. A maintainer evaluating whether a proposed *Tighten* edit is admissible in a future loom 1.x release cannot mechanically determine whether the gate has been crossed, and two maintainers can reasonably disagree. The same vagueness reaches the trailing `Once *Tighten* becomes permitted` clause that inherits the GOV-30 lock-step and *Audit methodology* release-gate obligations onto *Tighten*.

## Solution approach

Clarify the *Tighten* clause in `source-language-stability.md`'s `id="operational-definitions"` paragraph by defining a single named, release-anchored predicate for when the loom 1.x conformance fixture suite counts as shipped, keyed to the existence of a loom 1.x release tag whose tree contains the suite rather than a bare `main` commit. Rewrite the two undefined-`ships` sentences and the trailing `Once *Tighten* becomes permitted` inheritance clause to use that one named predicate so all three sites share a crisp, mechanically-checkable condition.
