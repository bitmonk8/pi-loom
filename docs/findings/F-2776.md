## Problem

`V9f` and its paired `V9f-T` both carry the Tests bullet *"The callable set's visibility tracks the invocation."* — the only Tests bullet on the pair that names no concrete observable and pins no REQ-ID, while its siblings each cite `PIC-8`, `PIC-19`, `PIC-44`, and the `ToolDefinition.label` derivation (`cka-17`) with a pinned assertion. The bullet only restates the headline design property of `tool-registration-lifetime.md`, but the concrete observables that realise it are already owned and gated elsewhere: the `PIC-17` prompt-mode active-set snapshot/restore facet by `V9c` (with the prompt→prompt restore facet on `V15d`), and the `PIC-9` isolated-subagent facet by `V9i`. The bullet is not referenced by `V9f`'s Ships-when, so it gates nothing. An implementer cannot tell what to assert for it and risks re-implementing a redundant copy of the `PIC-17` active-set check `V9c` already owns.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commit:** `c6a664e` (2026-06-10, "pi-loom plan: build/update plan for spec.md + review")
**History:** The bullet entered both `docs/plan_topics/V9f-tool-registration-lifetime.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V9f-T-tool-registration-lifetime.md` at the leaf-pair's creation commit `c6a664e`, originally worded "The callable set's visibility tracks the invocation; `registration-cache-collision` disambiguates a slug clash." (`git log --diff-filter=A --follow` confirms `c6a664e` created both files; `git log -S "visibility tracks the invocation"` returns only `c6a664e`). A later edit, `47915a2` (2026-06-27, coverage-matrix REQ-ID mapping pass), split the `registration-cache-collision` clause out into the dedicated `PIC-44` byte-equality bullet, leaving the bare unanchored "visibility tracks the invocation" sentence. The defect — an observable-free, REQ-ID-free clause — was present at inception; the later split only made its orphaned state more conspicuous.

## Solution approach

Delete the Tests bullet *"The callable set's visibility tracks the invocation."* from both `docs/plan_topics/V9f-tool-registration-lifetime.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V9f-T-tool-registration-lifetime.md`. The visibility observable is already pinned and gated by the leaves that own it (`V9c`/`V15d` for the `PIC-17` facet, `V9i` for the `PIC-9` facet), so the bullet names no observable `V9f` uniquely owns; `V9f`'s gated obligations and Ships-when are unaffected.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the `PIC-17` active-set visibility observable owned by `V9c`/`V15d` and the `PIC-9` isolated-subagent observable owned by `V9i` — do not re-implement them on `V9f`/`V9f-T`.
- The spec is read-only for this fix.
