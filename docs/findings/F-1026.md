## Problem

The `## Session Model` section of `docs/spec_topics/session-model-and-appendix.md` lists its eight SM-N sub-obligations as a single flat bullet sequence. Four of them — `SM-3b`, `SM-4`, `SM-5`, `SM-6` — are blocked at the loom 1.0 pin, each gated on the open contradiction recorded at `pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md#degraded-state-host-prerequisites` clause (a); the other four are immediately implementable. No structural cue distinguishes the two groups — the gate is delivered only as a trailing *Implementation gate:* sentence at the tail of each blocked bullet, so an implementer scanning the list encounters the blocked units as ordinary normative obligations. This is also out of step with the plan side, which groups the gated leaf under a dedicated `## Blocked obligations` section.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction

**Introducing commits:**

- `40ffdc5` (2026-05-27, "pi-loom spec: resolve 'T19a — Replace session-model paragraph with eight SM-N sub-units'") — split the prior single session-model paragraph into the eight SM-N bulleted sub-units in `docs/spec.md`. The `SM-4`/`SM-5`/`SM-6` bullets were authored at this commit as ordinary normative obligations, with no contingency marker, no grouping, and no preamble distinguishing them from the immediately-implementable peers. The clause-(a) contradiction was either latent or already documented at the host-prerequisites premise site at this point, but the list structure introduced here did not reflect it.
- `81bf52a` ("docs: collapse spec/ section pages into spec_topics/") — relocated the Session Model section into the current file at `docs/spec_topics/session-model-and-appendix.md`, preserving the flat list structure from `40ffdc5` verbatim. No structural change.
- `b8f004f` (2026-06-19, "pi-loom spec-plan: fix F-0719 — SM-4/5/6/3b degraded-state implementation-gate contingency markers") — appended the trailing *Implementation gate:* sentences to `SM-3b`, `SM-4`, `SM-5`, `SM-6` forwarding to clause (a). This was a partial mitigation: it inserted the textual gate at each bullet's tail but did not regroup the bullets, did not add a subsection header, and did not add a top-of-section preamble — so the four blocked sub-units remained visually peer with the implementable ones. The residual defect this finding flags is precisely the part of the mitigation that `b8f004f` did not perform.

**History:** The peers-of-implementables presentation is the original list shape from `40ffdc5`; the contingency was retrofitted inline by `b8f004f` without reshaping the list. The defect is the interaction of those two waves — the structural choice plus the chosen mitigation surface — rather than a single commit's local mistake.

## Solution approach

Restructure the `## Session Model` list so the gated sub-units (`SM-3b`, `SM-4`, `SM-5`, `SM-6`) are visually demarcated from the immediately-implementable ones — for example by moving them under a labelled sub-block introduced by a one-sentence preamble that forward-links to `pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md#degraded-state-host-prerequisites` clause (a). Rewrite the Session Model intro paragraph's "eight sub-obligations below" framing to describe the resulting two-group structure.

## Solution constraints

- The `session-model` umbrella anchor and the `sm-N-…` page-local anchors are inbound-cited (e.g. by the concurrency-model aggregator and future-considerations links) and stable per GOV-23 — do not renumber the SM-N units or change any anchor ID.
- The preamble must be a pointer to clause (a), not a restatement — do not duplicate clause (a)'s prose (single-source discipline).
