## Finding

Category 7 of the placeholder-rendering surface (`placeholder-rendering-b.md` §7) bundles `<callee>`, `<enum>`, `<schema>`, `<method>`, `<model>`, `<provider>`, `<slash-name>`, `<slug>`, `<invocation-id>`, `<uuid>`, `<name1>`, `<name2>`, and `<X>` into the "Identifier-shaped" sub-bucket whose rule is "rendered unquoted in identifier form per [Lexical — Identifiers](../lexical.md)". The Lexical identifier production is the strict `[A-Za-z_][A-Za-z0-9_]*` (case-sensitive, no hyphens, no leading digit) pinned at `lexical.md` line 13 and reused as the `<key>` predicate in §5.

Several of the values these placeholders actually carry cannot satisfy that production. The slash-name regex pinned at `discovery/discovery-sources.md` line 74 is `^[a-z0-9][a-z0-9_-]*$` — hyphens are admitted (`code-review.loom`) and a leading digit is admitted (`42-things.loom`), and the `loom/runtime/reload-teardown-timeout` row in `code-registry-runtime.md` explicitly renders `<slash-name>` by reading `entry.loom` verbatim (the canonical-key form pinned at `active-invocation-registry.md`, "post-`pi.looms` mapping, stem taken verbatim, with no leading `/`"). `<slug>` (used in `__loom_respond_<slug>` etc.) is derived from slash names and inherits the same shape. `<model>`, `<provider>`, and `<method>` are host-supplied identifiers (e.g. `claude-3-5-sonnet`) that routinely contain hyphens and digits. `<uuid>` and `<invocation-id>` are 8-4-4-4-12 hyphenated hex strings explicitly named that way two clauses later in the same bullet. Every one of these violates the strict Lexical identifier production the sub-rule cites.

The DIAG-4 byte-identical contract makes the ambiguity material: one implementer can read "identifier form per Lexical" as "pass the field's source bytes through unchanged" (the rendering this corpus expects, and what the `<list>` row's "read verbatim" prose enforces locally); another can read it as "the value must match `[A-Za-z_][A-Za-z0-9_]*` — reject or escape characters that don't", which would refuse to emit `/code-review:<uuid>` at all or substitute an escaped form. The two readings produce different bytes.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** f930818 — pi-loom spec: resolve "placeholder rendering exemption open-ended" (2026-05-07, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The "Identifier-shaped" sub-rule was introduced verbatim in its current form by f930818, which authored the eight-category placeholder-rendering surface — that commit placed `<slash-name>`, `<slug>`, `<invocation-id>`, `<uuid>`, `<model>`, `<provider>`, `<method>` together under "rendered unquoted in identifier form per [Lexical — Identifiers]" without reconciling them against the broader slash-name regex (`[a-z0-9][a-z0-9_-]*`) already pinned in discovery or the hyphenated UUID form named later in the same bullet. Subsequent edits (`bf7c69e` on 2026-06-05, `f5e89f4` on 2026-06-04) refined the `<list>` row's verbatim wording and re-split the page but never revisited the sub-rule itself; the conflict has been latent in the spec from the moment §7 was first authored.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace the §7 "Identifier-shaped" sub-rule's appeal to *Lexical — Identifiers* with a rule that matches what the values actually are: render the field's source bytes verbatim (the canonical-key string for `<slash-name>` and `<slug>`, the host-supplied id for `<model>` / `<provider>` / `<method>`, the canonical lowercase 8-4-4-4-12 hex form for `<uuid>` and `<invocation-id>`, the loom-side identifier for `<enum>` / `<schema>` / `<name1>` / `<name2>` / `<X>` — which actually *do* conform to Lexical), with no escaping, no case-folding, and no shape rejection. The existing `<callee>`, `<uuid>`, `<invocation-id>`, and `<slash-name>` clauses already in the bullet should be retained — they pin the per-placeholder shape — but the umbrella "identifier form per Lexical — Identifiers" phrase that ties the sub-bucket together is the defect and must be reworded to "rendered unquoted, verbatim from the producing site's field".

Concretely, name the producing site per placeholder in the rewritten sub-rule:

- `<slash-name>` and `<slug>` — the canonical-key form pinned in [Discovery — Filename validity](../discovery/discovery-sources.md) (`^[a-z0-9][a-z0-9_-]*$`).
- `<model>`, `<provider>`, `<method>` — the host-supplied id (which may contain hyphens and digits) read verbatim from the binder model handle / provider descriptor.
- `<uuid>`, `<invocation-id>` — the canonical lowercase 8-4-4-4-12 hex form (the clause already in the bullet, kept).
- `<enum>`, `<schema>`, `<name1>`, `<name2>`, `<X>` — the loom-side identifier per [Lexical — Identifiers](../lexical.md) (these genuinely *are* Lexical identifiers; the citation belongs here and nowhere else).

Add one normative test vector to §7 exercising the hyphenated-slash-name case end-to-end: a `reload-teardown-timeout` whose `entry.loom` is `code-review` renders the `<list>` entry as `/code-review:<uuid>` (already implicit in the `/plan` / `/review` vector immediately below the bullet, but a vector with a hyphen forecloses the wrong reading at conformance-test time). The §7 closure-of-bucket and §1–§6 / §8 cross-references are unaffected; no GOV-7/GOV-8 surface widens (the placeholder set is unchanged), so no closure-versioning obligation fires.

Implementer edge case: the `<callee>` fall-through to category 5's `<path>` rule (the existing *Category 7 `<callee>` fallback* paragraph) stays intact — it concerns the path-form fallback, not the identifier-form primary, and is orthogonal to this fix.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two conformant implementers will disagree on rendering `<slash-name>`, `<slug>`, `<model>`, and the other §7 "Identifier-shaped" tokens whenever the field's bytes contain a `-` or a leading digit — a routine case (`code-review`, `claude-3-5-sonnet`). One follows the §7 sub-rule literally and rejects or sanitises the value; another follows the row-level "read verbatim" prose and emits the bytes as-is. Either reading defeats DIAG-4's byte-identical contract on every panic message that interpolates one of these placeholders, and breaks the `<list>` rendering of `loom/runtime/reload-teardown-timeout` (where the row's verbatim contract is explicit, but the §7 sub-bucket the row defers to says otherwise).
