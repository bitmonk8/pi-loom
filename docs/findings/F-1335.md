## Problem

Step 4 of the Pi version-bump procedure (`docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2b.md`) bundles two independently-failable normative MUSTs into one unnumbered paragraph: the **joint-move MUST** (move all four `peerDependencies` entries together with the loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin literal in one commit, plus the byte-equal assertion shape) and the **deviation re-write trigger MUST** (with its (a)/(b) criterion). Neither carries the dual-form `PIC-N` anchor GOV-1 mandates, and both are absent from `coverage-matrix.md`'s *Code-keyed obligation areas* table. Downstream pages can therefore cite them only by anchor-less path quotation, and a GOV-8 lifecycle edit on either side forces a retire-and-re-add of the whole compound. The two MUSTs are temporally and logically distinct — the first binds every bump, the second is conditional — which is the GOV-8 *Split* criterion.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `b0cdf1b` (joint-move MUST), `d8da74b` (deviation re-write MUST, 2026-05-08), `c0259ca` and `5dfc9aa` (strengthenings without coining a REQ-ID), and `4f588e0` (GOV-22 introduction, 2026-06-03).
**History:** `git log -S "Move all four \`peerDependencies\` entries"` shows the joint-move MUST in place at `b0cdf1b` (2026-05-06) and unchanged in substance since. `git log -S "Deliberate deviation from Pi's bundled-package convention"` shows the deviation sub-paragraph coined at `d8da74b` (2026-05-08), the re-write MUST attached the same day. The MUST was strengthened to "re-written" with its (a)/(b) criterion at `c0259ca` (2026-06-07); the byte-equal assertion shape added at `5dfc9aa` (same day). GOV-22 was introduced at `4f588e0` (2026-06-03) after both MUSTs existed, so they fell into the standing pre-existing residue class GOV-22 leaves to progressive drainage. The coverage-matrix backstop has admitted neighbouring un-anchored MUSTs (`cka-19`, `cka-21`) but never these two; the gap is a missed coverage-matrix authoring pass overlaid on a pre-GOV-22 standing-residue defect.

## Solution approach

Split the bundled step-4 paragraph by coining two atomic `PIC-N` REQ-IDs at the defining site using GOV-1's dual-form layout, allocating the next two free integers under the already-registered `PIC` prefix per GOV-3. Anchor the joint-move MUST (the "move all four … do not split" content plus the byte-equal assertion shape, including the `typebox`-excluded carve-out) as one REQ-ID, and the conditional deviation re-write trigger MUST (through its (a)/(b) criterion and four-artefact co-edit list) as the other. Scan `docs/spec_topics/**` and `docs/plan_topics/**` for prose-only citations of either MUST and repoint each to the new anchors in the same commit, satisfying GOV-22's same-commit coinage obligation.

## Solution constraints

- Do not add `cka-N` coverage-matrix rows for these two MUSTs — coining the REQ-IDs removes them from the un-anchored residue class the *Code-keyed obligation areas* table tracks.
