## Finding

Two paragraphs in `schema-subset.md` (the opening paragraph and the `Draft` bullet of the subset list) are prefaced with the parenthetical label `*(Non-normative provenance.)*` and then mix authoring history with sentences that are *not* in fact provenance — they are forward-looking, loom-owned governance constraints that bind future maintainers:

- Opening paragraph: "…that provider-alignment rationale is design context, not a present-tense claim about any provider's live capabilities, **and the subset is not re-derived from those capabilities if they later drift.**"
- `Draft` bullet: "It was chosen at loom 1.0 authoring time as the draft both supported providers' strict modes accepted…; **the choice is loom-owned and is not a live claim about either provider's current requirements.**"

The bolded clauses are normative pinning rules: loom's permitted-subset enumeration and its Draft 2020-12 selection are not allowed to auto-track upstream provider capabilities, even if those providers later widen or shift them. That is a real obligation on a maintainer who, on seeing an OpenAI/Anthropic capability change, might otherwise reasonably propose "we should regenerate the subset / bump the draft." Today that maintainer is asked to read past a `(Non-normative provenance.)` banner — which, by spec convention elsewhere in the corpus, implementers and reviewers are entitled to skip — to discover the rule that prohibits exactly the action they were about to propose.

The defect is structural, not factual: the normative constraint is correctly stated, just filed under a label that signals "skip me." The fix is to lift the loom-ownership clauses out from under the provenance banner into adjacent normative sentences (next to the "fixed, loom-defined subset" declaration and the "loom fixes this draft" sentence), and to confine the `(Non-normative provenance.)` text to actual history ("chosen at loom 1.0 authoring time because…").

That commit replaced the opening's prior provider-coupled framing ("lowest common denominator of OpenAI Structured Outputs … and Anthropic tool-use `input_schema`") with the current loom-owned framing plus the two `*(Non-normative provenance.)*`-tagged sentences (one in the opening paragraph, one on the **Draft** bullet, which previously read `JSON Schema Draft 2020-12 (required by Anthropic; compatible with OpenAI)`). The provenance prose is therefore an artefact of decoupling the subset from live provider capabilities: the decoupling itself is normatively useful, but the historical justification was inlined rather than externalised.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Restructure both passages so that each `(Non-normative provenance.)` block contains *only* authoring history, and the loom-ownership pinning is stated as a normative sentence adjacent to the corresponding declaration. Concretely:

- **Opening paragraph.** Keep "Loom's `schema` keyword does **not** target the full JSON Schema standard. It targets a **fixed, loom-defined subset** of JSON Schema, pinned by this spec and listed below." Immediately follow it with a brief normative sentence such as: "The subset is loom-owned: it is not re-derived from provider capabilities if those later drift." Then open the `(Non-normative provenance.)` parenthetical, which now contains only the authoring-history sentence ("The subset was chosen at loom 1.0 authoring time to sit within what the loom 1.0-supported providers' strict modes — OpenAI Structured Outputs and Anthropic tool-use `input_schema` — could enforce on a grammar-constrained decoder, so one lowered schema is accepted across the supported provider set."). Drop the "design context, not a present-tense claim…" hedge — it is meta-commentary about the label itself, which the label is now strict enough to make unnecessary.
- **`Draft` bullet.** Keep "JSON Schema Draft 2020-12. loom fixes this draft for the schemas it emits. All occurrences of 'validates' / 'is accepted by the validator' in normative prose are evaluated against JSON Schema 2020-12 semantics; lowered schemas are evaluated under that draft both by loom and by any conforming validator." Append one normative sentence: "The draft is loom-owned: loom 1.0 does not re-pin it to track provider strict-mode requirements that may later shift." Then open the `(Non-normative provenance.)` parenthetical, which now contains only the authoring-history sentence ("Draft 2020-12 was chosen at loom 1.0 authoring time as the draft both supported providers' strict modes accepted — Anthropic's strict `input_schema` required it; OpenAI Structured Outputs was compatible with it.").

Edge cases for the fixer:

- Apply the same pattern to the `Depth` bullet's "it is loom-owned and does not track later changes to any provider's cap" clause — it is not currently wrapped in `(Non-normative provenance.)`, but it is the same loom-ownership pattern and should be left as-is (already correctly placed) and pointed at as the template.
- Do not delete the `(Non-normative provenance.)` parentheticals outright; the authoring-history sentences are still worth keeping as design context. The fix is relocation of the normative clauses, not deletion of provenance.
- Preserve the existing forward links inside the affected paragraphs (the `[Lowering Algorithm](#lowering-algorithm)` reference in the Nullability bullet is unaffected; the `Depth Enforcement` link in the `Depth` bullet is unaffected; nothing in the two edited paragraphs links onward).

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A loom 1.0 implementer building the reject gate or the lowering pass is not misled — the bullet list and the Draft sentence already pin the observable behaviour. The risk lands on the maintenance horizon: a future reviewer presented with "OpenAI now accepts Draft 2020-13" or "Anthropic widened its keyword set" is positioned to propose tracking the upstream change, having skipped past the `(Non-normative provenance.)` banners that hold the prohibition. The spec's stability claim — that the subset and the draft are loom-owned and do not drift with providers — is correct but is currently filed where readers are taught to skip.
