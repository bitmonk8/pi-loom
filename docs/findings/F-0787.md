## Finding

The opening paragraph of `docs/spec_topics/schema-subset.md` and the **Draft** bullet each carry a sentence explicitly tagged `*(Non-normative provenance.)*` that recounts why the loom 1.0 subset and the JSON Schema Draft 2020-12 choice were made at authoring time (alignment with OpenAI Structured Outputs and Anthropic strict `input_schema`). Both sentences end with a defensive clause clarifying that the rationale is design context — not a present-tense claim about any provider's live capabilities — and that the subset is "loom-owned" and is not re-derived if providers drift.

Because the sentences are self-declared non-normative and bound to no observable behaviour, they constrain no implementation and no test. They exist purely as historical rationale for a decision the spec has already pinned in the surrounding normative prose (the subset listing immediately below, and `Draft: JSON Schema Draft 2020-12` itself). The "loom-owned, not re-derived" hedge is a meta-disclaimer about the rationale rather than a normative obligation; without the rationale it has nothing to qualify.

Their location inside the normative subset page adds noise on the path every implementer of the lowering/validation surface reads, and invites future editors to extend the design-history narrative inline.

The provenance prose is therefore an artefact of decoupling the subset from live provider capabilities: the decoupling itself is normatively useful, but the historical justification was inlined rather than externalised.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Delete both `*(Non-normative provenance.)*`-tagged sentences from `docs/spec_topics/schema-subset.md` (the one in the opening paragraph and the one on the **Draft** bullet).

Concretely:

1. In the opening paragraph, drop the entire sentence beginning `*(Non-normative provenance.)* The subset was chosen at loom 1.0 authoring time …` through `… if they later drift.` The remaining paragraph still establishes the normative claim ("loom targets a fixed, loom-defined subset … pinned by this spec and listed below … The normative subset loom emits and enforces is:").
2. On the **Draft** bullet, drop the entire sentence beginning `*(Non-normative provenance.)* It was chosen at loom 1.0 authoring time …` through `… current requirements.` The bullet still carries its normative content (`JSON Schema Draft 2020-12. loom fixes this draft for the schemas it emits. All occurrences of "validates" / "is accepted by the validator" in normative prose are evaluated against JSON Schema 2020-12 semantics; lowered schemas are evaluated under that draft both by loom and by any conforming validator.`).
3. The loom-owned framing already present in the normative sentences ("a fixed, loom-defined subset … pinned by this spec", "loom fixes this draft for the schemas it emits") fully carries the decoupling intent the provenance sentences were hedging. Do not migrate the rationale to a new ADR / design-rationale appendix: the corpus has no such appendix today, and creating one solely to absorb two sentences trades cruft in one place for scope creep in another. If a design-rationale collection materialises later (e.g. `hard-ceilings/ceiling-design-rationale.md` is mooted by a related finding), the deleted text remains recoverable from commits 29e4faa / 7f217a8.

Implementer watch-outs:

- Leave the **Depth** bullet's "conservative ceiling loom fixes for itself … chosen at loom 1.0 authoring time …" prose alone; that sentence is not tagged `*(Non-normative provenance.)*` and is the subject of a separately-scoped concern, not this finding.
- Do not touch the trailing-clause normative addition on the **Draft** bullet (the `All occurrences of "validates" …` sentence introduced in 7f217a8); only the provenance sentence is deleted.
