## Finding

`H1a`'s Adds justifies the `minimatch` runtime dependency with the clause "`minimatch` belongs in loom's own `dependencies` as the glob matcher engine for `pi.looms` resolution â€” the `!`/`+`/`-` override ordering and exact-path operand treatment â€” grounded in `discovery/package-and-settings.md` Â§DISC-5 (consumed in `V10b`)". The em-dash appositive attaches the override ordering and exact-path operand treatment to what `minimatch` is described as providing, reading as though the library delivers DISC-5's `!`/`+`/`-` resolution.

`minimatch` is a single-pattern glob matcher. DISC-5 is explicit that the cross-pattern-list logic is loom's own consumer-side responsibility, not the library's: "The matcher engine and the `!`/`+`/`-` override ordering are pinned here rather than deferred to Pi, because the extension resolves these patterns itself," and it specifies the four-class fixed order (plain includes â†’ `!` drops â†’ `+` re-admits â†’ `-` removes with final precedence) plus that `+`/`-` operands are exact paths compared against the relative path, basename, and POSIX-normalised absolute path (mirroring Pi's `matchesAnyExactPattern`), "not glob patterns". `V10b`'s Adds correctly scopes minimatch to per-pattern matching and lists the `!`/`+`/`-` ordering as loom-implemented.

The defect is confined to the H1a dependency-justification sentence: it over-states minimatch's surface relative to the spec it cites. The downstream implementing leaf (`V10b`) carries the correct split, so the inflated claim does not by itself drive a wrong implementation; it is a guidance inaccuracy in the scaffold leaf's prose.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory
