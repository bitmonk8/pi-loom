## Finding

The slash-command-entry bullet under **Forwarding into `loomAbort`** asserts two distinct things that neither this page nor any forward-linked PIC page actually pins:

1. *`agent_end`-driven trigger without a detection predicate.* The text says "equivalently, an `agent_end` event reporting a user-cancelled turn aborts `loomAbort`", but it gives the runtime no way to recognise such an event. `AgentSessionEvent`'s terminal `agent_end` variant is pinned in [`pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md`](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md) only for its `messages: AgentMessage[]` field (chronological-order / leading-`user`-message presuppositions) and its terminal `willRetry: false` shape (presupposition (iv) of the slash-handler promise-lifecycle paragraph); no field, value, `stopReason`, or shape on the payload is documented as the user-cancellation indicator. Two implementers — one that treats every terminal `agent_end` as cancellation, one that gates on a guessed `stopReason: "cancelled"` or `reason` field — will diverge silently, and CNCL-4's byte-exact `"loom cancelled by agent_end"` reason then fires in different conditions on different builds. The `Suggested fix` margin of "or forward-link a PIC section" does not apply: no PIC section currently documents this predicate.

2. *Unanchored idle-non-turn-context premise.* The parenthetical "Pi documents `ctx.signal` as `undefined` in idle, non-turn contexts (which is exactly when the slash-command handler fires)" splits into two sub-claims. The first sub-claim *is* anchored — host-interfaces-core.md quotes the verbatim JSDoc "current abort signal, or undefined when the agent is not streaming" — but the second sub-claim, that the slash-command handler is dispatched *only* from such an idle/non-turn context, is asserted as Pi-documented fact with no anchor. The nearest related presupposition, `operator-always-present-presupposition` in [`host-prerequisites.md`](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md), pins operator presence inside an interactive TUI session but does not pin the dispatch-from-idle predicate. Without that anchor, a Pi minor that dispatched a slash handler mid-turn would silently regress the load-bearing "tolerate `undefined`" obligation with no surface to fail against.

Both defects sit in the same bullet but resolve independently — one demands a new detection-predicate specification (or an admission that the trigger is identifying *terminal* `agent_end` regardless of cancellation, paired with the matching CNCL-4 wording), the other demands one new named presupposition (or forward-link).

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Resolve the slash-command-entry bullet's two unpinned claims by first anchoring the idle-dispatch premise, then specifying the `agent_end`-driven detection predicate against that stabilised baseline.

**Anchor the idle-non-turn-context premise.** Insert one new presupposition paragraph into the (i)–(vi) sequence under [Pi-side slash-handler promise lifecycle (consumption posture)](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md#pi-slash-handler-promise-lifecycle-presupposition) in `host-interfaces-core.md` — it becomes presupposition (vii); the lifecycle paragraph already enumerates six and is its natural home — and give it its own anchor `slash-handler-idle-dispatch-presupposition`. The paragraph names the source of truth (the `ExtensionContext.signal` JSDoc *"current abort signal, or undefined when the agent is not streaming"* is the symptom; the dispatch-from-idle predicate is the unstated cause) and records the failure mode (a Pi minor that dispatched a slash handler mid-turn would route the loom's "tolerate `undefined`" obligation past every signal-defined assumption). In `cancellation.md`'s slash-command-entry bullet under **Forwarding into `loomAbort`**, replace the unanchored parenthetical "Pi documents `ctx.signal` as `undefined` in idle, non-turn contexts (which is exactly when the slash-command handler fires)" with the forward link `(see [slash-handler idle-dispatch presupposition](./pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md#slash-handler-idle-dispatch-presupposition))`.

**Specify the `agent_end`-driven detection predicate.** Read the loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin's `dist/core/agent-session.d.ts` to determine what `AgentSessionEvent`'s terminal `agent_end` variant carries as a user-cancellation indicator (the spec already pins this variant for `messages` and `willRetry: false`, but not for any cancellation indicator). In `cancellation.md`'s slash-command-entry bullet, replace "an `agent_end` event reporting a user-cancelled turn aborts `loomAbort`" with the predicate named verbatim from the pinned payload field/value (e.g. a `stopReason: "cancelled"` value if `StopReason` carries one at the pin, or a dedicated `cancelled: true` / `reason: "user"` field). If the pinned `agent_end` payload carries no cancellation indicator, the only sound predicate is "any terminal `agent_end` whose `ctx.signal` reads aborted from inside the handler" — state that explicitly so the trigger is plainly redundant with the `ctx.signal`-aborted trigger and CNCL-4's synthesised-reason rule degrades to a fallback, and delete or reframe the bullet's "equivalently, …" clause as a belt-and-braces backstop rather than keep the legacy wording. Add a parallel pin to `host-interfaces-core.md` under the `AgentSessionEvent` paragraph naming the same field/value (or noting its absence).

**Audit and test wiring.** Add two per-bump checklist rows to `version-bump-step2.md`: one mirroring item (j) so a Pi minor that renames or restructures the cancellation indicator surfaces at bump time, and one parallel to item (j) / (v) covering the new idle-dispatch presupposition. Adjust `V17a`'s CNCL-4 reason-identity Tests bullet so the synthesised-reason test asserts the predicate that landed in the spec rather than the legacy "any terminal `agent_end`" implicit reading, and adjust `V9c`'s `pi.on` cancel-forward wiring so it asserts against the named predicate.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable implementers will diverge on when the `agent_end`-driven trigger fires: one wires every terminal `agent_end` to `loomAbort.abort(new Error("loom cancelled by agent_end"))`, surfacing spurious `cancelled` results on every clean turn-end; the other gates on a guessed `stopReason` and misses real user cancellations whose payload differs. The unanchored idle-dispatch claim is a slower-burn correctness risk — a future Pi minor that dispatched a slash handler mid-turn would silently invalidate the "tolerate `undefined`" obligation without surfacing against any named presupposition.
