## Problem

`V3a`'s **Adds** field promises bare-identifier resolution in the four-tier shadow order `local > fn > import > callable` and the no-match outcome `loom/parse/unknown-identifier`, both defined in `expressions.md` §Identifier resolution (and the latter in `diagnostics/code-registry-parse.md`). Its **Tests** field asserts neither: the bullets cover only precedence/associativity, short-circuit observability, equality/ordering/arithmetic widening, and the `loom/parse/non-boolean-condition` reject. Because `V3a` is the closing leaf for the EXPR area, it can ship green with resolution mis-ordered or with `unknown-identifier` never emitted, and no gate witnesses the gap.

**History:** The `V3a` leaf was first authored in c6a664e with the **Adds** field already claiming "identifier resolution (local > fn > import > callable)", but its **Tests** field never carried a bullet for resolution ordering or for `unknown-identifier` — `git log -S'unknown-identifier'` over the leaf returns no commit, and the resolution-precedence Adds string traces back to c6a664e and was never paired with a test. Later edits (8704946 scope narrowing, cb8737a re-bundling, the `non-boolean-condition` bullet addition) reshaped surrounding content but never closed this coverage gap. The defect is original to the leaf, not introduced by a later edit.

## Solution approach

Add **Tests** bullets to `V3a` — with paired failing assertions in `V3a-T` — that assert the four-tier shadow order `local > fn > import > callable` and that a bare identifier matching no tier fires `loom/parse/unknown-identifier`, citing `expressions.md#identifier-resolution`. Extend `V3a`'s **Ships when** to name these two behaviours alongside the existing proofs.

- Out of scope: the EXPR closing-leaf mapping in `cka-3` / `coverage-matrix.md` — the obligation already belongs to `V3a` and must not be re-mapped.
