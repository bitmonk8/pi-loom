## Finding

`version-bump-step2b.md`'s step 2(b) (the inventory-closure-audit resolution arm of the version-bump procedure) carries two normative obligations on an actor it calls *the reviewer*: "reviewers MUST check that fit on the bump-commit diff" governs every branch (1)/(2)/(3) choice, and the (1)∧(2) overlap — branch (1) delete vs branch (2) promote — is resolved only as "a reviewer-discretion call on the bump-commit diff … the contributor records the call and a reviewer confirms it or pushes back on the diff." Both clauses bind the bump-merge gate: a bump-commit diff for which "a reviewer" has not confirmed the call is, on its face, not yet conformant with step 2(b).

The corpus nowhere introduces this role. The version-bump procedure (`version-bump-intro.md`, `version-bump-step2.md`, `version-bump-step2b.md`, `version-bump-triggers.md`, `host-prerequisites.md`) names the *contributor* as the sole actor at every other step, and no spec page declares a review precondition for a bump commit, names a reviewer-distinct-from-contributor requirement, or describes a review process that a bump must pass through. The "reviewer" referent is also used in passing across `GOV-15`, `GOV-22`, `GOV-30`, `GOV-31`, and the audit canary (`audit-wire-and-canary.md`), all as an undefined editorial-process actor; the spec corpus elsewhere lives with that ambient framing because those sites cast reviewer behaviour as an SHOULD-flag, an editorial-convention backstop, or a per-commit coinage check — none of them are merge gates on a bump. Step 2(b) is the one place where reviewer action is hoisted onto a `MUST` and onto the (1)∧(2) overlap's only stated resolution path.

For the only deployment shape the rest of the spec describes — a single contributor performing a Pi minor bump — both clauses are unsatisfiable as written. The "reviewers MUST check that fit" MUST has no actor distinct from the contributor to perform the check, and the (1)∧(2) overlap reaches no terminating resolution because the contributor's recorded call awaits a confirmation the spec gives no one the standing to issue. A reader cannot tell whether step 2(b) is silently asserting an unstated review-process precondition (bump commits require a second actor), tolerating a self-review collapse (the contributor records and confirms their own call), or expecting the overlap to be unresolved until some downstream process the corpus does not describe. The plan-side `V18c` *Ships when* clause inherits the same gap: it requires "editorial review" to confirm the per-item outcome shape but does not say whose review counts.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**

- `e5cd514` — *"pi-loom spec: resolve 'T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit'"* (2026-05-09): first introduced the "reviewers MUST check that fit on the bump-commit diff" clause as the merge-gate on every branch (1)/(2)/(3) choice.
- `e785f77` — *"pi-loom spec: resolve 'Version-bump procedure six clarity/scope gaps (T061)'"* (2026-06-07): added the (1)∧(2) overlap paragraph and its "reviewer-discretion call … the contributor records the call and a reviewer confirms it or pushes back on the diff" resolution, making the reviewer role load-bearing for the overlap's only stated terminating path.

**History:** The "reviewer" referent existed in the corpus before either commit (it appears in GOV-15/22/30/31 and `audit-wire-and-canary.md` as an undefined editorial-process actor), and the role-declaration gap is by GOV-14's retirement note an intentional editorial posture: reviewer behaviour is held outside the arm-(a)/arm-(b) coverage of GOV-18 and admits no mechanical witness. `e5cd514` first crossed that ambient framing into a `MUST` on a merge-blocking gate, and `e785f77` then made the same undeclared role the only stated terminating path for the (1)∧(2) overlap. Neither commit declared the role; no later commit on either file does so. The defect is therefore not localised to either introducing commit individually but is the interaction between them and the standing absence of a review-process precondition for the version-bump procedure, which is the form the durable fix must address.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Narrow the obligations to the contributor as sole actor

**Approach.** Recast both reviewer-bound clauses in step 2(b) as contributor obligations: the "reviewers MUST check that fit on the bump-commit diff" sentence becomes "the contributor MUST record, on the bump-commit diff, the fit-check (per-branch criterion satisfied) for each branch (1)/(2)/(3) resolution"; the (1)∧(2) overlap paragraph becomes "the contributor records the delete-vs-promote determination on the bump-commit diff, citing the runtime-dependence evidence that placed the reference under branch (1) or branch (2)". The "mechanical reviewer-disagreement failure mode" sentence is rewritten as the post-merge correction loop ("a post-merge dispute is resolved by re-applying the tie-break against the offending bump-commit diff and routing the correction through the next bump's step 2(b) pass") with no reviewer dependency.

**Spec edits.** `version-bump-step2b.md` step 2(b) preamble and the (1)∧(2) overlap paragraph; sweep "reviewer" tokens in the same step (currently three sites). `version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a)'s "reviewer SHOULD flag" cardinality-drift sentences may stay as editorial-convention SHOULDs (they are not merge gates).

**Pros.** No new project-process precondition is asserted; the rest of the version-bump procedure already names the contributor as sole actor, so the alignment is local; the post-merge correction loop the spec already pins becomes the unique resolution path on every overlap, removing the terminating-path gap.

**Cons.** Loses the explicit second-pair-of-eyes posture the (1)∧(2) overlap argument leans on (the choice "turns on the substantive question of whether the runtime depends on the surface", which is a judgement call); a sloppy or adversarial contributor self-confirms.

**Risks.** A contributor reading the rewritten clause may still want a reviewer; the spec needs to be explicit that none is required so the omission does not read as oversight. `V18c`'s *Ships when* clause must drop or re-target its "editorial review" phrasing on the same commit.

### Option B — Declare a reviewer-distinct-from-contributor precondition for the bump commit

**Approach.** Add a precondition paragraph to `version-bump-intro.md` (or to `version-bump-step2.md`'s preamble) declaring that a Pi minor bump merges only when a named reviewer distinct from the bump contributor has confirmed the bump-commit diff's step 2(b) resolutions and the per-item outcomes step 2 records. The named reviewer is recorded in the bump commit message — the same evidence-record convention `H6a`'s manual real-host smoke gate and the release-time residue inspection already use ("the named owner who ran the gate"). Step 2(b)'s existing reviewer-MUST and (1)∧(2) reviewer-discretion clauses then have a defined actor, and the post-merge correction loop becomes a fallback rather than the sole path.

**Spec edits.** New precondition paragraph in `version-bump-intro.md`; cross-reference from `version-bump-step2.md`'s preamble and from `version-bump-step2b.md` step 2(b); the existing reviewer-MUST language is unchanged but now resolves against a declared role. `V18c`'s *Ships when* clause adds the named-reviewer record as part of the editorial-review acceptance shape.

**Pros.** Preserves the second-pair-of-eyes posture that branch (2) vs branch (1) substantively wants; aligns the bump-commit evidence-record shape with the analogous manual-gate conventions `H6a` and `real-host-smoke-gate.md` already pin; gives `V18c` a concrete record to check.

**Cons.** Asserts a project-process requirement (two distinct actors per bump) that no other spec page asserts, expanding the spec's scope beyond runtime/build-time contracts; conflicts with GOV-14's retired posture that reviewer behaviour is outside the corpus's witness model; raises questions the spec then has to answer (who is eligible to review, what happens when no second contributor exists at a given release, whether an automated review tool counts).

**Risks.** Adopting this option without a corresponding `V18c` edit leaves the plan-side gate silently advancing on a commit the spec marks non-conformant; the precondition must be added in the same commit as `V18c`'s acceptance-shape update.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A**. The version-bump procedure already names the contributor as sole actor at every other step, and the (1)∧(2) overlap's substantive content — "the choice turns on whether the runtime depends on the surface" — is recoverable as a contributor obligation to cite the runtime-dependence evidence on the bump commit, which makes the call auditable on the diff without depending on a second actor. The fallback the spec already pins ("a post-merge dispute is resolved by re-applying the tie-break against the offending bump-commit diff and routing the correction through the next bump's step 2(b) pass") then becomes the unique terminating path on every overlap, removing the gap.

Choose Option B only if maintainers want to formally assert a two-actor review-process precondition on bump commits — a substantive project-process expansion the rest of the corpus has avoided per the GOV-14 retirement posture. If Option B is taken, the precondition paragraph and the `V18c` *Ships when* edit must land in the same commit; the editorial-record shape should follow the named-owner / date / outcome convention `H6a` and `real-host-smoke-gate.md` already pin, and the precondition must explicitly address solo-contributor bumps (defer, escalate to a named release-time auditor, or otherwise) rather than leaving the gap re-introduced under a new clause.

Edge cases the implementer must watch under either option: the "reviewer SHOULD flag" sentences in `version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a) and across GOV-30/GOV-31 are editorial-convention SHOULDs and remain conformant under either option without rewording; the version-bump procedure's other `MUST` clauses (item (a)/(e) merge gates, the step-2 per-item recording obligation, the runtime-evidence acceptance gate) name no reviewer and are not in scope.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable bump contributors will resolve a (1)∧(2) overlap on opposite sides: one self-confirms their delete/promote call and treats step 2(b) as cleared; the other reads the reviewer language strictly and is blocked with no one to clear the gate. A solo-contributor bump is, on the strict reading, non-conformant on every (1)∧(2) overlap commit. The runtime effect is contained — the bump itself still lands or stalls — but the merge-blocking shape of step 2(b) is undefined for the dominant deployment shape, and the plan-side `V18c` acceptance gate inherits the same gap.
