## Problem

Step 2(b) of the version-bump procedure in `version-bump-step2b.md` binds two normative obligations on an actor it calls *the reviewer*: a `MUST` that "reviewers MUST check that fit on the bump-commit diff" governing every branch (1)/(2)/(3) choice, and the (1)∧(2) overlap (branch (1) delete vs branch (2) promote) whose only stated resolution path is "the contributor records the call and a reviewer confirms it or pushes back on the diff." The spec corpus nowhere introduces a reviewer role — the version-bump procedure names the *contributor* as the sole actor at every other step and declares no review precondition for a bump commit. For the single-contributor bump the rest of the spec describes, both clauses are unsatisfiable: the `MUST` has no actor distinct from the contributor, and the overlap never terminates because the contributor's recorded call awaits a confirmation no one has standing to issue. The plan-side `V18c` *Ships when* clause inherits the same gap by requiring an unattributed "editorial review."

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**

- `e5cd514` — *"pi-loom spec: resolve 'T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit'"* (2026-05-09): first introduced the "reviewers MUST check that fit on the bump-commit diff" clause as the merge-gate on every branch (1)/(2)/(3) choice.
- `e785f77` — *"pi-loom spec: resolve 'Version-bump procedure six clarity/scope gaps (T061)'"* (2026-06-07): added the (1)∧(2) overlap paragraph and its "reviewer-discretion call … the contributor records the call and a reviewer confirms it or pushes back on the diff" resolution, making the reviewer role load-bearing for the overlap's only stated terminating path.

**History:** The "reviewer" referent existed in the corpus before either commit (it appears in GOV-15/22/30/31 and `audit-wire-and-canary.md` as an undefined editorial-process actor), and the role-declaration gap is by GOV-14's retirement note an intentional editorial posture: reviewer behaviour is held outside the arm-(a)/arm-(b) coverage of GOV-18 and admits no mechanical witness. `e5cd514` first crossed that ambient framing into a `MUST` on a merge-blocking gate, and `e785f77` then made the same undeclared role the only stated terminating path for the (1)∧(2) overlap. Neither commit declared the role; no later commit on either file does so. The defect is therefore not localised to either introducing commit individually but is the interaction between them and the standing absence of a review-process precondition for the version-bump procedure, which is the form the durable fix must address.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the reviewer-bound clauses in `version-bump-step2b.md` step 2(b) so the contributor is the sole actor here as at every other step of the procedure: recast the "reviewers MUST check that fit" obligation and the (1)∧(2) overlap resolution as contributor obligations recorded on the bump-commit diff, and recast the mechanical reviewer-disagreement failure-mode sentence as a post-merge correction loop with no reviewer dependency. State explicitly that no reviewer distinct from the contributor is required for a bump, so the omission does not read as oversight. Re-target the "editorial review" phrasing in the plan-side `V18c` *Ships when* clause so it no longer asserts a reviewer-distinct-from-contributor confirmation.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the "reviewer SHOULD flag" cardinality-drift SHOULDs in `version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a) and across GOV-30/GOV-31 — they are editorial-convention SHOULDs, not merge gates, and must not be swept by the contributor recast.
