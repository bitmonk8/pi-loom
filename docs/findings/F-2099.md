## Problem

The *Race semantics — late-settlement discard at the tool-call checkpoint* paragraph in `cancellation.md` opens the discard window with "once a `tool-call` checkpoint has **surfaced** `Err(QueryError { kind: "code_tool", cause: "cancelled", ... })`," and pins the discriminator as "whether cancellation has already been **surfaced** at the checkpoint." The verb "surfaced" is never bound to a concrete instant and admits two readings: the runtime's internal commit point (the cancelled outcome is fixed at the checkpoint, before it reaches loom code), or the delivery point (the `Err` has resolved the call site's `await`). The same page's *Surfacing* section uses "surfaces as `Err`" to mean delivery to loom code, so importing that sense leaves a settlement arriving between commit and `await`-resolution ungoverned — CNCL-1/CNCL-2/CNCL-3 would not bind it, and an implementer could rebind the call site or emit a second event. Surrounding text (the checkpoint-attached swallowing handler, the total side-channel suppression obligation) leans toward the commit-point reading, but the spec never states it.

None of these touched the meaning of "surfaced." The corpus is git-tracked; the verdict is well-determined.

## Solution approach

Clarify the late-settlement discard paragraph's opening sentence and its discriminator sentence so the discard window opens at the runtime's internal commit instant — when the checkpoint observes the abort and the call site's `cause: "cancelled"` outcome is fixed — and state that this instant precedes the loom-visible `await` resolution. Distinguish this trigger from the `surfacing` section's "surfaces as `Err`" delivery sense so the verb "surfaced" is not read uniformly across the page. Any settlement of the underlying Promise at or after the commit instant, including one landing in the gap before `await` resolves, is then discarded under CNCL-1/CNCL-2/CNCL-3.
