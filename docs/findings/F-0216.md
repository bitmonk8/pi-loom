## Finding

Across the corpus the warn-only live-corpus gate-rehearsal step is named with the noun **"canary"**: the `H5b` heading (`Warn-only live-corpus canary (pre-activation pre-flight)`), `plan.md` §Release gate ("A warn-only live-corpus canary (`H5b`) runs the `H5a` closing gate…") and its leaf-list entry, `H6a`'s body ("the warn-only `H5b` canary"), and `H5b`'s own *Tests* and *Ships when* bullets. In those canonical uses "pre-flight" appears only as a verb or descriptor (e.g. conventions.md's "the canary pre-flights … against incomplete coverage"; `H5a`'s "the `H5b` canary consumes this mode to pre-flight live coverage").

Inside `H5b-warn-only-canary.md` two places instead use **"pre-flight" as the noun for the thing itself**: the **Convention** bullet ("the warn-only live-corpus pre-flight of the closing gate") and the **Adds** body ("A warn-only live-corpus pre-flight that runs `H5a`'s closing gate…" and "so the pre-flight emits no spurious finding…"). The Adds body then switches mid-paragraph to "This canary surfaces live coverage gaps…", so the same paragraph names the step two different ways.

The heading parenthetical equates the two terms ("canary (pre-activation pre-flight)"), so no reader is genuinely misled, but the noun-level term drift is gratuitous against an otherwise uniform vocabulary and reads as if "pre-flight" might be a distinct artefact.

The term drift therefore entered the corpus with the leaf's first authoring and no later edit introduced or corrected it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/H5b-warn-only-canary.md`, make "canary" the noun in the two places that currently use "pre-flight" as the noun, leaving "pre-flight" as a descriptor/verb (matching the heading parenthetical and the usage in `H5a`/conventions.md):

- **Convention bullet:** change `the warn-only live-corpus pre-flight of the closing gate` to `the warn-only live-corpus canary of the closing gate`.
- **Adds body, first sentence:** change `A warn-only live-corpus pre-flight that runs [\`H5a\`](./H5a-closing-gate-automation.md)'s closing gate` to `A warn-only live-corpus canary that runs [\`H5a\`](./H5a-closing-gate-automation.md)'s closing gate`.
- **Adds body, last sentence:** change `so the pre-flight emits no spurious finding` to `so the canary emits no spurious finding`.

Leave the heading parenthetical ("pre-activation pre-flight") and any verb/descriptor uses of "pre-flight" untouched.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The two terms are equated by the heading and the descriptor uses elsewhere are correct, so no implementer diverges; the only cost is a momentary "is the pre-flight a separate thing from the canary?" reading of `H5b`'s Adds body before the parenthetical resolves it. Pure terminology consistency within one leaf.
