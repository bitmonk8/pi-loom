## Finding

`V9i` (Subagent-mode session isolation and lifecycle) carries three concerns that share no implementation surface and no test harness:

1. **Core lifecycle** — the private `AgentSession` (no shared transcript or `tools:` table), the spawn sequence, the pre-spawn binder-model guard, `dispose()`-in-`finally` idempotency, the final-value-contract propagation, and the `subagent-model-unresolved`/`subagent-dispose-failure` codes (`PIC-9`, plus the isolation and final-value bullets).
2. **PIC-22 parallel-spawn** — the N≥3 parallel-spawn step that dispatches `createAgentSession` for all N before any returns, asserted against a fake `AgentSession` whose `sendUserMessage` blocks until explicitly released.
3. **Swallowing-handler attachment** — the cancellation-side assertion that the subagent `AgentSession.abort()` Promise attaches its swallowing handler at construction time and suppresses a late settlement across three side channels (no `unhandledRejection`, no second `RuntimeEvent`, no diagnostic), driven through the `V8a` `Checkpoint` seam.

Each concern needs a distinct test rig: the core lifecycle drives dispose paths and the model guard; PIC-22 needs a release-gated fake session that proves concurrency; the swallowing-handler test needs a harness observing three independent side channels with a `Checkpoint`-seam late settlement. A reviewer must hold all three rigs in one pass, and the single compound `Ships when` mixes the dispose-idempotency gate with the three-channel suppression gate.

## Solution Space

### Recommendation

Peel the swallowing-handler concern out of `V9i` into its own leaf pair, leaving core lifecycle and PIC-22 parallel-spawn together in `V9i`.

- In `V9i-subagent-isolation.md` and `V9i-T-subagent-isolation.md`, strike the swallowing-handler `Tests` bullet ("Swallowing-handler attachment at this site …") and the swallowing-handler clause of `Ships when`.
- Create a new `<new>` / `<new>-T` leaf pair owning the three-channel suppression assertion at the `Checkpoint` (`V8a`) seam: assert that the subagent `AgentSession.abort()` Promise attaches its swallowing handler at construction time and suppresses a late settlement across all three side channels (no `unhandledRejection`, no second `RuntimeEvent`, no diagnostic). Carry its `Deps` on `V8a`, `V17a`, and `V9i`.
- In `coverage-matrix.md`, leave the `PIC-9, PIC-22 | V9i` row intact; add the swallowing-handler obligation against the new leaf, landed on the same baseline as finding 31's coverage-matrix swallowing-handler row so the new leaf and the matrix row are consistent.
- Use the `<new>` placeholder per the `no-invented-ids` policy until the implementer allocates a real ID under the `V9<letter>` scheme. Keep the spec read-only — this fix is internal to the plan corpus.
