## Problem

`V9i` (Subagent-mode session isolation and lifecycle) carries three concerns that share no implementation surface and no test harness: the core lifecycle (private `AgentSession`, spawn sequence, pre-spawn model guard, `dispose()`-in-`finally` idempotency, final-value contract, `PIC-9`), the `PIC-22` parallel-spawn step, and the swallowing-handler attachment test (three-channel suppression of a late `AgentSession.abort()` settlement at the `V8a` `Checkpoint` seam). Each needs a distinct test rig, and the single `Ships when` mixes the dispose-idempotency gate with the three-channel suppression gate, so a reviewer must hold all three rigs in one pass and the most subtle concern is most likely under-scrutinised.

## Solution approach

Split the swallowing-handler concern out of the `V9i` / `V9i-T` leaf pair into its own new `V9<letter>` leaf pair, leaving core lifecycle and `PIC-22` parallel-spawn together in `V9i`. Strike the swallowing-handler `Tests` bullet and the swallowing-handler clause of `Ships when` from both `V9i-subagent-isolation.md` and `V9i-T-subagent-isolation.md`. Create the new leaf pair owning the three-channel suppression assertion at the `V8a` `Checkpoint` seam, carrying `Deps` on `V8a`, `V17a`, and `V9i`. Update `coverage-matrix.md` to add the swallowing-handler obligation against the new leaf while leaving the `PIC-9, PIC-22 | V9i` row intact.
