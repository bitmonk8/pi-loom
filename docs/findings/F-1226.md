## Finding

Two unrelated normative slots in `subagent.md` use undefined editorial terms where the surrounding spec is otherwise rigorous about RFC-2119 modals and defined-term referents.

The first slot is in the **Six rules** block, rule 4. After granting that `DefaultResourceLoader.systemPromptOverride` is "a conformant alternative channel the loom MAY use", the same sentence then says the channel "is discouraged" and the rejection rationale is restated in the satellite-types paragraph as "the conformant-but-discouraged construction channel the loom MAY use". The corpus uses RFC-2119 modals (`MUST`, `MUST NOT`, `SHOULD`, `SHOULD NOT`, `MAY`) as its normative vocabulary (see `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` GOV-22's enumeration of defining-obligation tokens); "discouraged" is not in that vocabulary. The text leaves an implementer unsure whether the channel is `SHOULD NOT` (a normative obligation a conformant runtime takes on) or a `MAY` with editorial preference attached (so picking it remains conformant). Two reasonable implementers will diverge on whether a runtime that uses `systemPromptOverride` ships as conformant.

The second slot is in the **Subagent state-isolation matrix**, in the row that reads "other non-overridden `ExtensionContext` members per **`ExtensionContext` (member surface loom touches)** below". The term *non-overridden* does not appear in that referenced section's vocabulary. `host-interfaces-core.md` §"Per-mode override semantics" partitions `ExtensionContext` members into *overridden* (the three rows of the override table: `signal`, `sessionManager`, `abort`) and *forwarded* (everything else, enumerated in the per-mode bullets that follow). The matrix's "non-overridden" forces the reader to infer that "non-overridden" means "forwarded per the per-mode bullets in `host-interfaces-core.md`", but a literal reading could also include `signal` / `sessionManager` / `abort` *in subagent mode* on the grounds that the matrix already accounts for them in its other rows. The defect is small individually but the matrix is the canonical enumeration of what crosses the subagent boundary, so the imprecise referent at this exact site is load-bearing.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `fc54626` — *pi-loom spec: resolve "systemPromptOverride channel status made normative"* (Sat Jun 6 2026) — introduced "conformant alternative … but discouraged" in rule 4 and "conformant-but-discouraged construction channel" in the satellite-types paragraph, replacing earlier phrasing ("an alternative channel but is **not** the recommended construction" / "the not-recommended construction channel").
- `52219c4` — *Address all 41 spec-review findings* (Wed May 6 2026) — introduced the state-isolation matrix into `pi-integration-contract.md`, with the second-column third row reading "other non-overridden `ExtensionContext` members" against the override semantics added in the same commit's series.
**History:** The two defects share a surface (`subagent.md`'s spawn block + state-isolation matrix) but were authored independently. The matrix row carrying "non-overridden" predates the rule-4 wording by a month and survived an intervening normalising commit (`0e080df`, *ExtensionContext member surface lacks signatures and per-mode override semantics*) that introduced the *overridden* / *forwarded* partition the matrix cell should have been retrofitted to. The rule-4 "discouraged" wording landed afterwards, in a commit whose stated intent was to upgrade the channel's status to normative; that commit selected "discouraged" rather than `SHOULD NOT`, leaving the modal gap the finding now flags. Neither commit is individually wrong about the conclusion it draws; the defect lives in the choice of terminology each made against the corpus's defined-term and RFC-2119 vocabularies.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Rule 4 modal clarification

**Approach.** Replace "but it is discouraged" in rule 4 and "the conformant-but-discouraged construction channel the loom MAY use" in the satellite-types paragraph with an RFC-2119 modal. Two equally-acceptable shapes:

- A1: Promote to `SHOULD NOT`: rule 4 becomes "`DefaultResourceLoader`'s `systemPromptOverride: (base) => string | undefined` constructor option is an alternative channel; the loom SHOULD NOT use it: it still runs all of `DefaultResourceLoader`'s discovery side-effects against the parent `cwd` to compute `base`, only to ignore the result, so the custom-adapter form is the only construction the runtime uses in practice." Satellite-types paragraph mirrors: "`DefaultResourceLoaderOptions.systemPromptOverride` is the `SHOULD NOT`-use construction channel (per rule 4 above)".
- A2: Drop the soft prohibition entirely and keep `MAY` clean. Rule 4 becomes: "`DefaultResourceLoader`'s `systemPromptOverride: (base) => string | undefined` constructor option is a conformant alternative channel the loom MAY use; the runtime nevertheless uses the custom-adapter form to avoid invoking `DefaultResourceLoader`'s parent-`cwd` discovery side-effects only to ignore the result." Satellite-types paragraph mirrors: "`DefaultResourceLoaderOptions.systemPromptOverride` is a conformant alternative construction channel the loom MAY use (per rule 4 above)".

**Spec edits.** Two sentences in `subagent.md`: rule 4's final sentence, and the rejection-rationale clause in the *Spawn-block satellite type pins* paragraph.

**Pros.** Restores an RFC-2119 modal at a load-bearing decision point; either A1 or A2 closes the conformance question.

**Cons.** A1 is a soft normative widening (an A1-conformant runtime that ships with `systemPromptOverride` is a defect; an A2-conformant runtime is fine). A2 weakens the deterrent the commit `fc54626` was trying to introduce.

**Risks.** Conformance tests under V9i may need a corresponding adjustment if A1 is chosen and any test currently treats `systemPromptOverride` use as observable (the V9i and V9i-T leaves do not currently assert anything in this region, so the risk is forward-looking).

### Option B — State-isolation matrix referent fix

**Approach.** Replace "other non-overridden `ExtensionContext` members" in the matrix's second column, third row, with a referent that names the established partition. Concretely: "other `ExtensionContext` members forwarded per the per-mode bullets in [`ExtensionContext` (member surface loom touches)](./host-interfaces-core.md#extensioncontext-interface) below". The receiving partition is already named *forwarded* in `host-interfaces-core.md` §"Per-mode override semantics" ("Every other member from the signature block above forwards unchanged to the live host"), so the replacement reuses an existing defined term rather than coining a new one.

**Spec edits.** One matrix cell in `subagent.md`'s **Subagent state-isolation matrix**.

**Pros.** Mechanical, single-cell edit; aligns the matrix vocabulary with the surface it cross-links to; removes the literal-reading hazard that would re-forward `signal` / `sessionManager` / `abort` in subagent mode.

**Cons.** None of consequence — the change is a defined-term swap.

**Risks.** None observed.

### Recommendation

Resolve in order: **Option B first** (the smaller scope-bounding edit; one matrix cell, no normative shift), then **Option A** (the modal choice; recommend **A1** — promote to `SHOULD NOT` — because the surrounding paragraph's discovery-side-effects rationale frames the channel as a conformance hazard, and the only reason `fc54626` did not write `SHOULD NOT` outright appears to be vocabulary drift rather than an intent to keep the channel `MAY`-conformant). Edge case for the implementer of A1: the matrix cell touched by Option B is not part of this clause and must stay aligned with the *forwarded* terminology after both edits land.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

For rule 4: a runtime author reading "discouraged" without an RFC-2119 modal can ship a `systemPromptOverride`-based adapter and reasonably claim conformance (since the surrounding clause grants `MAY`), defeating the rule's intent that the custom-adapter form be the only one observed in practice and re-exposing the parent `cwd` discovery side-effects the rule was written to suppress. For the matrix: an implementer who reads "non-overridden" literally may forward the parent's `signal` / `sessionManager` / `abort` in subagent mode in addition to the three matrix rows that handle them, contradicting the override table and silently widening what subagent mode inherits.
