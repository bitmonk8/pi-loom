## Problem

Two independent normative slots in `subagent.md` use undefined editorial terms where the surrounding corpus is rigorous about RFC-2119 modals and defined-term referents. Rule 4 of the **Six rules** block (and its mirror in the *Spawn-block satellite type pins* paragraph) calls the `DefaultResourceLoader.systemPromptOverride` channel "discouraged" / "conformant-but-discouraged" — terms outside the corpus's RFC-2119 modal vocabulary — leaving an implementer unsure whether the channel is `SHOULD NOT` (a conformance obligation) or a `MAY` with editorial preference attached, so two implementers diverge on whether a runtime that uses it ships conformant. Separately, the **Subagent state-isolation matrix**'s second-column third row refers to "other non-overridden `ExtensionContext` members", a term that does not appear in the referenced `host-interfaces-core.md` §"Per-mode override semantics", which partitions members into *overridden* and *forwarded*; a literal reading could re-forward `signal` / `sessionManager` / `abort` in subagent mode in addition to the matrix rows that already handle them. The matrix is the canonical enumeration of what crosses the subagent boundary, so the imprecise referent at this site is load-bearing.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `fc54626` — *pi-loom spec: resolve "systemPromptOverride channel status made normative"* (Sat Jun 6 2026) — introduced "conformant alternative … but discouraged" in rule 4 and "conformant-but-discouraged construction channel" in the satellite-types paragraph, replacing earlier phrasing ("an alternative channel but is **not** the recommended construction" / "the not-recommended construction channel").
- `52219c4` — *Address all 41 spec-review findings* (Wed May 6 2026) — introduced the state-isolation matrix into `pi-integration-contract.md`, with the second-column third row reading "other non-overridden `ExtensionContext` members" against the override semantics added in the same commit's series.
**History:** The two defects share a surface (`subagent.md`'s spawn block + state-isolation matrix) but were authored independently. The matrix row carrying "non-overridden" predates the rule-4 wording by a month and survived an intervening normalising commit (`0e080df`, *ExtensionContext member surface lacks signatures and per-mode override semantics*) that introduced the *overridden* / *forwarded* partition the matrix cell should have been retrofitted to. The rule-4 "discouraged" wording landed afterwards, in a commit whose stated intent was to upgrade the channel's status to normative; that commit selected "discouraged" rather than `SHOULD NOT`, leaving the modal gap the finding now flags. Neither commit is individually wrong about the conclusion it draws; the defect lives in the choice of terminology each made against the corpus's defined-term and RFC-2119 vocabularies.

## Solution approach

Replace the undefined editorial terms in both slots with the corpus's established vocabulary. In rule 4 of the Six rules block and in the *Spawn-block satellite type pins* paragraph, rewrite the `systemPromptOverride`-channel clause to use the RFC-2119 modal `SHOULD NOT` in place of "discouraged" / "conformant-but-discouraged". In the state-isolation matrix's second-column third row, rename the "non-overridden" referent to the *forwarded* partition already defined in `host-interfaces-core.md` §"Per-mode override semantics" (`#extensioncontext-interface`), reusing its forwarded-per-mode-bullets terminology rather than the undefined term.

## Solution constraints

- Strengthening rule 4's `systemPromptOverride` clause from editorial preference to a `SHOULD NOT` modal on this non-narrative page engages GOV-22 progressive-coinage: if the rule-4 site carries no co-located `PIC-N` REQ-ID anchor in the post-edit text, mint the next free `PIC-N` anchor under the page's registered prefix in the same commit.
