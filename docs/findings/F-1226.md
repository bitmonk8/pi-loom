## Finding

Two unrelated normative slots in `subagent.md` use undefined editorial terms where the surrounding spec is otherwise rigorous about RFC-2119 modals and defined-term referents.

The first slot is in the **Six rules** block, rule 4. After granting that `DefaultResourceLoader.systemPromptOverride` is "a conformant alternative channel the loom MAY use", the same sentence then says the channel "is discouraged" and the rejection rationale is restated in the satellite-types paragraph as "the conformant-but-discouraged construction channel the loom MAY use". The corpus uses RFC-2119 modals (`MUST`, `MUST NOT`, `SHOULD`, `SHOULD NOT`, `MAY`) as its normative vocabulary (see `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` GOV-22's enumeration of defining-obligation tokens); "discouraged" is not in that vocabulary. The text leaves an implementer unsure whether the channel is `SHOULD NOT` (a normative obligation a conformant runtime takes on) or a `MAY` with editorial preference attached (so picking it remains conformant). Two reasonable implementers will diverge on whether a runtime that uses `systemPromptOverride` ships as conformant.

The second slot is in the **Subagent state-isolation matrix**, in the row that reads "other non-overridden `ExtensionContext` members per **`ExtensionContext` (member surface loom touches)** below". The term *non-overridden* does not appear in that referenced section's vocabulary. `host-interfaces-core.md` ┬¦"Per-mode override semantics" partitions `ExtensionContext` members into *overridden* (the three rows of the override table: `signal`, `sessionManager`, `abort`) and *forwarded* (everything else, enumerated in the per-mode bullets that follow). The matrix's "non-overridden" forces the reader to infer that "non-overridden" means "forwarded per the per-mode bullets in `host-interfaces-core.md`", but a literal reading could also include `signal` / `sessionManager` / `abort` *in subagent mode* on the grounds that the matrix already accounts for them in its other rows. The defect is small individually but the matrix is the canonical enumeration of what crosses the subagent boundary, so the imprecise referent at this exact site is load-bearing.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Make two independent terminology fixes in `subagent.md`, both replacing undefined editorial terms in normative slots with the corpus's established vocabulary.

First, fix the **Subagent state-isolation matrix** referent. In the matrix's second column, third row, replace "other non-overridden `ExtensionContext` members per **`ExtensionContext` (member surface loom touches)** below" with "other `ExtensionContext` members forwarded per the per-mode bullets in [`ExtensionContext` (member surface loom touches)](./host-interfaces-core.md#extensioncontext-interface) below". This reuses the *forwarded* partition already defined in `host-interfaces-core.md` ┬¦"Per-mode override semantics" ("Every other member from the signature block above forwards unchanged to the live host") rather than the undefined term *non-overridden*, and removes the literal-reading hazard that would re-forward `signal` / `sessionManager` / `abort` in subagent mode in addition to the three matrix rows that already handle them.

Second, restore an RFC-2119 modal in **rule 4** of the Six rules block and in the *Spawn-block satellite type pins* paragraph. Rewrite rule 4's final sentence to read: "`DefaultResourceLoader`'s `systemPromptOverride: (base) => string | undefined` constructor option is an alternative channel; the loom SHOULD NOT use it: it still runs all of `DefaultResourceLoader`'s discovery side-effects against the parent `cwd` to compute `base`, only to ignore the result, so the custom-adapter form is the only construction the runtime uses in practice." Mirror this in the satellite-types paragraph's rejection-rationale clause: "`DefaultResourceLoaderOptions.systemPromptOverride` is the `SHOULD NOT`-use construction channel (per rule 4 above)". `SHOULD NOT` (not the undefined "discouraged") is the correct modal because the discovery-side-effects rationale frames the channel as a conformance hazard the runtime must avoid.

Keep the matrix cell aligned with the *forwarded* terminology after both edits land; the matrix cell is not part of the rule-4 clause but shares the surface.

Conformance tests under V9i and V9i-T do not currently assert anything in this region, but a future test that treats `systemPromptOverride` use as observable must align with the `SHOULD NOT` obligation.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

For rule 4: a runtime author reading "discouraged" without an RFC-2119 modal can ship a `systemPromptOverride`-based adapter and reasonably claim conformance (since the surrounding clause grants `MAY`), defeating the rule's intent that the custom-adapter form be the only one observed in practice and re-exposing the parent `cwd` discovery side-effects the rule was written to suppress. For the matrix: an implementer who reads "non-overridden" literally may forward the parent's `signal` / `sessionManager` / `abort` in subagent mode in addition to the three matrix rows that handle them, contradicting the override table and silently widening what subagent mode inherits.
