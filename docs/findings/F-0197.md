## Problem

`coverage-matrix.md` maps every obligation family to its closing leaf in one of its two tables (Numbered REQ-IDs; Code-keyed obligation areas), except the inline-label families `HC3-a … HC3-e` and `NOCEIL-1 … NOCEIL-4`. Their closing-leaf mappings appear only inside the trailing "`CIO` / `HC3` / `NOCEIL` / `CEIL` closure rationale" paragraph, interleaved with design-motivation prose. A reader performing a prefix-keyed lookup ("what closes `NOCEIL-1`?") scans the tables, finds no row, and must know to fall through to the prose to recover the answer. The placement is inconsistent with how every other prefix is presented, making the lookup path for these two families non-obvious.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** 2565ddd
**History:** The `HC3-a … HC3-e` and `NOCEIL-1 … NOCEIL-4` closure data entered `coverage-matrix.md` in 2565ddd (2026-06-10, "resolve 'V16a NOCEIL Adds. claim with no backing Tests'") as a prose paragraph appended to the Governance section. It has been prose ever since; aa1b666 (2026-06-17, "resolve 'Governance section carries runtime/ceiling closure notes under disclaiming heading'") relocated the paragraph out of the Governance section to follow the Numbered REQ-IDs table but preserved its prose form. No commit ever rendered this closure data as table rows, so the prose-not-table shape is present since the data's inception.

## Solution approach

Surface the inline-label closing-leaf mappings as table rows in `coverage-matrix.md` so a prefix-keyed reader finds them where every other family lives. The mappings to render are `HC3-a … HC3-e` → `V11f`, `NOCEIL-1` → `V6a`, `NOCEIL-2` → `V4d`, `NOCEIL-3` → `V4b`, `NOCEIL-4` → `V15b`; the `CIO-1 … CIO-6` rows already exist in the Numbered REQ-IDs table and need no new row. Leave in the rationale paragraph only the residual content that does not reduce to a table cell (the distributed-seam and GOV-15 release-time audit caveat).
