## Finding

The orientation-aggregator section in `docs/spec_topics/language-and-architecture.md` carries the heading `### V1 non-goals` (line 6), while the two `<a id>` anchors immediately above it (line 5) are `loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator` and `v1-non-goals`, and the section body (line 8 onward) refers to "the consolidated loom 1.0 non-goals list". This is the only `V1`-spelled heading anywhere in the spec corpus: every other reference to this section — in `overview-and-orientation.md`, `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md`, `governance.md`, and the body of this same file — uses the canonical "loom 1.0 non-goals" spelling.

GOV-20 (`governance/release-version-naming.md`) makes `V1` a governed alias that maps to "loom 1.0", and explicitly permits the corpus to carry mixed spellings during incremental conversion, so the heading is not a governance violation. It is, however, the lone unconverted callsite for a section whose surroundings have all been converted, leaving an internal inconsistency: a reader who lands on the heading sees `V1 non-goals` while the canonical anchor, the body prose, and every inbound cross-reference name it "loom 1.0 non-goals".

The conversion is mechanical and carries no anchor risk. GOV-25's *incidental auto-id prohibition* already anticipates this exact rename — it uses the heading `### loom 1.0 non-goals` as its worked example and confirms the dual-anchor pair (`loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator` + `v1-non-goals`) is the stable citation surface, so a heading-text change does not disturb any citable anchor.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction

**Introducing commits:**
- `0e12140` (2026-05-08) — `pi-loom spec: resolve "Non-goals are not consolidated into a single section"` — coined the `### V1 non-goals` aggregator heading (then on `docs/spec.md`) with body prose that already used `V1` throughout.
- `a6d82db` (2026-05-29) — `pi-loom spec: resolve "Prose V1 / V1.0 / V1.x rename to canonical spelling at non-closure callsites"` — converted the surrounding body prose at this aggregator (and across the corpus) from `V1` to `loom 1.0` but left the ATX heading text on this section unconverted, producing the present heading-vs-body mismatch. The companion dual-anchor commit `2e0dad2` (same day) added `<a id="loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator"></a>` alongside the existing `<a id="v1-non-goals"></a>`, so the site is dual-anchored and the heading rename is governance-ready.

**History:** The defect is not present-since-inception of the aggregator: at `0e12140` the heading and body were internally consistent (both `V1`). It is the partial prose-rename in `a6d82db` — which deliberately scoped itself to non-closure prose callsites and stopped short of ATX heading text — that introduced the visible inconsistency. The intervening relocation commits `f5e89f4` (size-cap split) and `81bf52a` (collapse `spec/` into `spec_topics/`) only moved the section across files without editing the heading or its body. The fix therefore discharges work that the May-29 rename pass deliberately deferred.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Change the heading text on line 6 of `docs/spec_topics/language-and-architecture.md` from `### V1 non-goals` to `### loom 1.0 non-goals`, completing GOV-20's incremental conversion for this callsite. Leave the `<a id="loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator">` and `<a id="v1-non-goals">` anchor pair on line 5 unchanged — it already provides both the canonical citation arm and the `v1-` back-compat alias, and per GOV-25 the renderer's incidental auto-id for the new heading text is not a citable anchor, so no inbound cross-reference is affected.

A reader or maintainer encounters `V1 non-goals` at the heading while the anchors, body, and all cross-references say "loom 1.0 non-goals", a single-callsite spelling inconsistency. No runtime behaviour, anchor resolution, or implementer decision diverges.
