## Finding

`V18c` Adds previews the contributor editorial-review checklist as "MUST-level for `(a)`–`(e)` and SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record for `(f)`–`(al)`." Read in isolation, the compound coinage "SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record" admits two readings:

- **(A)** The audit is discretionary (SHOULD); *if* it is performed, the outcome MUST be recorded. Under this reading, skipping an item and recording nothing is conformant — there is no outcome to record.
- **(B)** The audit is recommended (SHOULD), but recording a per-item outcome — including an explicit `not-performed` when the audit is skipped — is mandatory for every item, and omitting the row is non-conformant.

The authoritative spec resolves this unambiguously to **(B)**: `version-bump-step2.md` states that for items `(f)` onward "the contributor MUST record one of `pass` / `fail` / `N/A` with a one-line rationale when the SHOULD-level audit was performed, or `not-performed` with a one-line rationale when the SHOULD-level audit was skipped — omitting the row is non-conformant". `V18c`'s own Ships-when also encodes (B) ("the bump commit message records a per-item outcome for every item `(a)`–`(al)`"). Only the Adds preview is ambiguous: the compound coinage carries the (B) obligation in a form that reads naturally as (A).

The two readings produce divergent contributor behaviour — items omitted entirely versus every item carrying a recorded outcome. Because the binding statement lives in Ships-when and the spec (both unambiguous), this is a guidance gap in a non-normative preview rather than a divergence in what `V18c` implements.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `f1cda48` (Thu Jun 11 2026, "resolve 'V18c mislabels editorial-review checklist as a static build-time gate'") and `828b7d3` (Tue Jun 16 2026, "resolve 'V18c editorial-checklist MUST/SHOULD blocking distinction missing from exit gate'")
**History:** `git log -S "SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record"` shows the coinage first entered the Adds field in `f1cda48`, which split the editorial-review checklist out of the static-build-time-gates list. At that point the coinage was immediately followed by a disambiguating sentence ("Its done condition is not `npm test` but the per-item outcome recording the spec mandates … `pass` / `fail` / `N/A` / `not-performed` + one-line rationale …"), which pinned reading (B). `git log -S "Its done condition is not"` shows that disambiguating sentence was removed in `828b7d3`, which relocated the MUST/SHOULD blocking distinction into Ships-when and trimmed the Adds preview — leaving the compound coinage standing alone in Adds with no in-field clarification. The current ambiguity is the product of the second commit stripping the disambiguator the first commit had paired with the coinage.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V18c-version-bump-checklist.md`, in the Adds field, replace the trailing clause `MUST-level for \`(a)\`–\`(e)\` and SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record for \`(f)\`–\`(al)\`.` with wording that mirrors `version-bump-step2.md`'s recording rule, splitting the level of the audit from the level of the recording. For example:

> MUST-level for `(a)`–`(e)`; for `(f)`–`(al)` the per-bump audit is SHOULD-level, but the contributor MUST record a per-item outcome for every one of those items — `pass` / `fail` / `N/A` with a one-line rationale when the audit was performed, or `not-performed` with a one-line rationale when it was skipped (omitting a row is non-conformant), per [`version-bump-step2.md`](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md).

Keep the existing `(f)`–`(al)` item range the leaf already uses (do not import the spec's wider range). The `pass` / `fail` / `N/A` / `not-performed` outcome vocabulary and the "omitting the row is non-conformant" clause both come straight from `version-bump-step2.md`, so the preview stays faithful to the authoritative source.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A contributor reading only the Adds preview could adopt reading (A) — skip an unaudited item and record nothing — while the binding Ships-when and `version-bump-step2.md` require a recorded outcome (including `not-performed`) for every item, with omission being non-conformant. The binding obligation is stated unambiguously elsewhere, so the gate still fires correctly; the risk is a contributor mis-following the preview rather than a divergent implementation of `V18c`.
