## Problem

The INV-2 and INV-3 seam blockquotes in `docs/spec_topics/invocation.md` each open with an indicative-voice claim about internal program structure under a REQ-ID anchor: INV-2 asserts the invocation AST node carries an explicit `style: "positional" | "named"` discriminator, and INV-3 asserts the invoke primitive's runtime-internal options record is an open struct rather than a closed positional record. Neither claim is reachable from any conformance vantage — the `"named"` arm has no loom 1.0 surface syntax, and the options record is never exposed to author code, test fixtures, or wire-level inspection. Each blockquote's own *Non-normative implementation note* concedes the shape is "a maintainability convention, not an observable conformance point," so the REQ-ID-anchored head and its footnote assert opposite things. The plan's `V15a` accordingly lists INV-2 and INV-3 as Tests against a property the spec itself flags as not a conformance point.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit interaction
**Introducing commits:** `09c42c0` (2026-05-05) introduced the "Argument-binding style" and "Options surface" sub-paragraphs in `spec_topics/invocation.md` while resolving *Surface-extension Seam annotations carry V1 obligations from non-goals page*; the original prose already combined an indicative AST/struct-shape claim with a MUST on internal consumers (`MUST switch on the discriminator exhaustively`; `consumers MUST tolerate unknown fields`). `52219c4` (2026-05-06, *Address all 41 spec-review findings*) reformatted the two paragraphs as `> **V1 seam — …**` blockquotes without changing their content. `d510a55` (2026-06-04, *resolve "Seam-blockquote MUSTs lack co-located REQ-ID anchors"*) promoted them by attaching the `INV-2` / `INV-3` REQ-IDs and `<a id="inv-2">` / `<a id="inv-3">` anchors, turning a styled paragraph into a normative anchored seam. `8362824` (2026-06-04, *resolve "Demote over-prescriptive and non-testable MUSTs on unobservable implementation structure"*) then bolted on the trailing *Non-normative implementation note* explicitly labelling the AST-discriminator and open-struct factoring as *"a maintainability convention, not an observable conformance point"*, but left the indicative-voice head paragraph intact. The contradiction the present finding describes is exactly the residue of that last commit: the demotion landed on the tail but the head still asserts the unobservable shape declaratively under a REQ-ID anchor.
**History:** `git log --follow` over `docs/spec_topics/invocation.md` (and its pre-relocation path `spec_topics/invocation.md`) plus `git log -S 'open struct'`, `git log -S 'Named-argument-invocation seam'`, and `git show` on each of the four commits above.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the INV-2 and INV-3 blockquote heads so the normative claim binds the observable loom 1.0 surface and the additive-extension guarantee, and demote each head's internal-shape sentence — the AST `style` discriminator for INV-2, the open-struct options record for INV-3 — into the existing *Non-normative implementation note* tail. Keep the `id="inv-2"` / `id="inv-3"` anchors and REQ-ID labels in place. Apply the same rewrite to the parallel per-call-timeout seam blockquote in `docs/spec_topics/tool-calls.md`, and realign the INV-2 / INV-3 Tests bullets in `docs/plan_topics/V15a-invocation-core.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V15a-T-invocation-core.md` to assert against the new observable heads.
