## Finding

In `docs/spec_topics/invocation.md`, the INV-2 ("Named-argument-invocation seam") and INV-3 ("Per-call-timeout seam") blockquotes each anchor a REQ-ID and open with a declarative claim about an internal program structure: INV-2 says *"The invocation AST node carries an explicit `style: "positional" | "named"` discriminator on each argument list"*, and INV-3 says *"The runtime-internal options record passed into the invoke primitive … is an **open struct**, not a closed positional record."* Both are stated in indicative-MUST voice inside a normative seam blockquote that carries its own `INV-N` ID.

Neither claim is reachable from any conformance vantage. The `"named"` arm has no loom 1.0 surface syntax (the same paragraph states `"positional"` is the only defined arm), and the options record is internal to the invoke primitive and never exposed to author code, test fixtures, or wire-level inspection. The trailing *Non-normative implementation note* on each blockquote concedes exactly this: the AST discriminator and the open-struct shape are *"a maintainability convention, not an observable conformance point."* A normative paragraph and its own footnote thus assert opposite things — the first as a REQ-ID-anchored MUST, the second as a non-normative concession that the MUST is untestable.

The downstream cost is visible in the plan: `V15a` lists `INV-2` and `INV-3` as **Tests** ("the AST arg list carries `style:"positional"|"named"`"; "the invoke-options record is an open struct"), so the leaf has to write conformance tests against a property the spec itself says is not a conformance point. The same pattern recurs verbatim in `tool-calls.md` for the tool-call options record (flagged separately and folded in below).

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit interaction
**Introducing commits:** `09c42c0` (2026-05-05) introduced the "Argument-binding style" and "Options surface" sub-paragraphs in `spec_topics/invocation.md` while resolving *Surface-extension Seam annotations carry V1 obligations from non-goals page*; the original prose already combined an indicative AST/struct-shape claim with a MUST on internal consumers (`MUST switch on the discriminator exhaustively`; `consumers MUST tolerate unknown fields`). `52219c4` (2026-05-06, *Address all 41 spec-review findings*) reformatted the two paragraphs as `> **V1 seam — …**` blockquotes without changing their content. `d510a55` (2026-06-04, *resolve "Seam-blockquote MUSTs lack co-located REQ-ID anchors"*) promoted them by attaching the `INV-2` / `INV-3` REQ-IDs and `<a id="inv-2">` / `<a id="inv-3">` anchors, turning a styled paragraph into a normative anchored seam. `8362824` (2026-06-04, *resolve "Demote over-prescriptive and non-testable MUSTs on unobservable implementation structure"*) then bolted on the trailing *Non-normative implementation note* explicitly labelling the AST-discriminator and open-struct factoring as *"a maintainability convention, not an observable conformance point"*, but left the indicative-voice head paragraph intact. The contradiction the present finding describes is exactly the residue of that last commit: the demotion landed on the tail but the head still asserts the unobservable shape declaratively under a REQ-ID anchor.
**History:** `git log --follow` over `docs/spec_topics/invocation.md` (and its pre-relocation path `spec_topics/invocation.md`) plus `git log -S 'open struct'`, `git log -S 'Named-argument-invocation seam'`, and `git show` on each of the four commits above.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Rewrite the INV-2 and INV-3 blockquote heads so the normative portion talks about *user-visible loom 1.0 surface*, and move every claim about AST node shape or options-record cardinality into the existing *Non-normative implementation note* tail.

Concretely, in `docs/spec_topics/invocation.md`:

- **INV-2.** Replace the head paragraph with a statement of behaviour: loom 1.0's only `invoke` argument-binding surface is positional (`invoke("./path.loom", a, b, c)`); a deferred named-argument / `key=value` invocation surface is reserved for a minor revision per *Future Considerations*, and additive emergence of that surface MUST NOT break call sites authored against loom 1.0. Move the existing AST-node sentence (*"carries an explicit `style: "positional" | "named"` discriminator"*) and the "every consumer MUST switch on the discriminator exhaustively" sentence into the *Non-normative implementation note* tail, where the spec already calls that factoring a maintainability convention.
- **INV-3.** Same shape: the normative head becomes "loom 1.0 exposes no per-call timeout knob on `invoke(...)`; a deferred per-call-timeout extension is reserved per *Future Considerations*, and additive emergence MUST NOT break call sites or test fixtures authored against loom 1.0." Move the "open struct, not a closed positional record" and "consumers MUST tolerate unknown fields" sentences into the non-normative note.
- Keep the `<a id="inv-2">` / `<a id="inv-3">` anchors and REQ-ID labels in place — the seam still exists, it just now binds an observable additivity property rather than an unobservable shape claim.
- Apply the same rewrite to the parallel `loom 1.0 seam — per-call timeout` blockquote in `docs/spec_topics/tool-calls.md` so the two pages stay symmetric (see *Related Findings*).

Then in `docs/plan_topics/V15a-invocation-core.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V15a-T-invocation-core.md`, replace the `INV-2` and `INV-3` Tests bullets with assertions against the new observable head (e.g. for INV-3: a call site authored without a timeout option compiles and runs unchanged when a future fixture adds an unused option field; for INV-2: a loom whose `invoke(...)` site uses the only legal positional form parses, and an attempt at named/`key=value` invocation surface is a parse error). The `coverage-matrix.md` row `INV-1, INV-2, INV-3 | V15a` stays intact — the seams still close at `V15a`, only the test scope shifts.

Edge cases the implementer must watch: do not weaken the deferred-surface cross-links in `future-considerations/surface-extensions.md`; those entries currently lean on the seam wording, and the rewrite must keep a normative anchor for the additivity guarantee they depend on.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The user-visible loom 1.0 surface is unaffected — INV-2's `"positional"` arm and INV-3's "no per-call timeout knob" already pin everything an external observer can see. The cost is implementability and test discipline: `V15a` is required to author conformance assertions for properties the spec itself flags as not-a-conformance-point, and a future reader of either REQ-ID has to reconcile the indicative head with the non-normative tail before deciding what is binding.
