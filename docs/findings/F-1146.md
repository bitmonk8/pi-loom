## Problem

The prompt-mode untyped-query bullet's two transport-failure mappings — the narrow secondary synchronous-throw mapping and the primary `stopReason: "error"` mapping under the `prompt-mode-error-detection` anchor — are stated indicatively ("is mapped to" / "maps to"), not normatively, and the negative halves ("not to `loom/runtime/internal-error`") are descriptive rather than prohibitive. With no RFC-2119 force, an implementation that wraps the synchronous throw as `loom/runtime/internal-error`, lets it propagate as an uncaught throw, or swallows it as `Ok("")` is not unambiguously non-conforming — the spec merely states what the mapping is, not what the implementer must produce. Because the rule governs the user-visible `QueryError` shape returned from prompt-mode untyped queries (and the free phase of typed queries), the ambiguity is observable to loom authors and to the runtime-event-channel always-log emission for `kind: "transport"`.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (regressed by a later rewrite)
**Introducing commits:**
- `2d2fc8e` — *pi-loom spec: resolve "Pi SDK capability calls have no failure contract"* (2026-05-06): introduced the original sentence "A thrown or rejected `pi.sendUserMessage` … **is mapped to** `Err(QueryError { kind: "transport", … })` — not to `loom/runtime/internal-error`." The indicative phrasing and the descriptive negative half are present in this first authored form.
- `8c6cfec` — *pi-loom spec: resolve "pi.sendUserMessage returns void; prompt-mode transport-error detection path is mis-described"* (2026-05-07): rewrote the paragraph to split the surface into primary (`AgentSession.errorMessage` probe, later replaced by the `stopReason: "error"` trailing-`assistant` probe) and secondary (synchronous throw), and added the parallel primary-surface "maps to …" sentence. Both halves carried the indicative phrasing forward unchanged.
- `7f75947` and `d37c563` (post-`8c6cfec` rewrites of the *Error detection* surface) preserved the indicative phrasing across the `AgentSession.errorMessage` → `stopReason: "error"` migration; neither tightened the rule.

**History:** The defect is original to the first authored form of the synchronous-throw mapping (`2d2fc8e`) and survived every subsequent rewrite of the surrounding paragraph. No commit ever upgraded the clause to RFC-2119 normative force, and the parallel primary-surface sentence introduced in `8c6cfec` inherited the same indicative phrasing rather than fixing it.

## Solution approach

Rewrite both prompt-mode transport-failure mappings in `conversation-drive.md` — the narrow secondary synchronous-throw clause and the primary `stopReason: "error"` clause under `id="prompt-mode-error-detection"` (including its absent-`errorMessage` fallback) — to carry RFC-2119 normative force, pairing each positive MUST with explicit MUST NOTs against the misroutings the current indicative phrasing leaves open. Preserve the synthesised `QueryError` shape and the `provider`-from-resolved-`model:` sourcing unchanged.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: which value the `provider:` field should carry — owned by F-1145. Keep the current resolved-`model:` sourcing rule intact.
- Strengthening these clauses to normative-modal force adds defining-obligation sites on a non-narrative page; per GOV-22 the same commit MUST co-locate a `PIC-N` REQ-ID anchor at each strengthened site that lacks one.
