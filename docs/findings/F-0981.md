## Problem

The `<a id="loom-flag-namespace"></a>` paragraph in `discovery/discovery-sources.md` states two behavioural claims about `pi.registerFlag('loom', …)` at the loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin as bare fact, anchored only to the pin URL: that the call does not throw on a flag-name overlap, and that registration is first-load-wins so a later sibling extension registering `'loom'` is silently shadowed while loom's own `pi.getFlag('loom')` still observes loom's registration. Neither claim is encoded in the typed `ExtensionAPI.registerFlag` surface; both are unpinned host behaviours of exactly the kind `host-prerequisites.md` otherwise records as a named presupposition routed to an editorial-review item on the `version-bump-step2.md` (a)–(an) checklist. Here they carry no presupposition framing, no `bump-checklist-…` re-audit hook, and no known-fragile-evidence citation, so a Pi minor that changed the overlap semantics would silently invert the load-bearing "no `loom/load/...` collision diagnostic" decision with no build-time signal. The paragraph also conflates two separable mechanisms — the non-throwing `registerFlag` write and the shared per-name flag-value slot that `getFlag` reads — which can each regress independently.

The originating review item (T04 in the then-`spec-review.md`, deleted in the same commit) framed the addition as scoped to the [loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin] and constrained the new prose to be **observational** rather than citing the bundled `loader.js` symbol as load-bearing; the resolving commit followed that constraint, which is the same editorial choice that produced the now-flagged gap: the prose anchors to the pin URL but to no presupposition framing or version-bump checklist item. No subsequent commit on `docs/spec_topics/discovery/discovery-sources.md` between `37b0428` and `HEAD` touches the paragraph (`git log 37b0428..HEAD -- docs/spec_topics/discovery/discovery-sources.md` lists six commits, none of which alter the `loom-flag-namespace` block per inspection of each diff). The defect therefore entered with the paragraph itself and has been stable since.

## Solution approach

Promote the two behavioural claims to a named presupposition block under `host-prerequisites.md`, on the same footing as the `model-registry-population-presupposition`, `settings-write-back-preservation-presupposition`, and `operator-always-present-presupposition` blocks, distinguishing the non-throwing-overlap behaviour from the shared per-name flag-value-slot read behaviour. Add a corresponding editorial-review item to `version-bump-step2.md`'s (a)–(an) presupposition checklist that routes detection to the new block. Rewrite the `loom-flag-namespace` paragraph in `discovery/discovery-sources.md` to cite the new host-prerequisite anchor by location rather than restating the behavioural claims.

## Solution constraints

- The new presupposition block MUST stay observational — a point-in-time observation at the pin rather than a load-bearing claim about stable shape across patches — matching the known-fragile-evidence discipline of the sibling presupposition blocks on the page.
- The new checklist item MUST be SHOULD-level, so a recorded `fail` is conformant non-blocking under `version-bump-step2.md`'s outcome-recording rules, not MUST-level like items (a)–(e).
