## Finding

`H3a`'s **Adds** declares each host-seam interface's member signatures and anchors the `Checkpoint` contract at [`host-interfaces-services.md#checkpoint-seam`](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-services.md#checkpoint-seam). It then qualifies that anchor with a parenthetical: "even though `V8a` lists `Checkpoint` under `host-interfaces-core.md`."

That parenthetical is false. `V8a`'s **Spec** field cites `host-interfaces-services.md` (plus `implementation-notes.md`) — the same page, and same section, `H3a` anchors. The two leaves agree on the source; there is no divergence to reconcile. Worse, the parenthetical points a reader at `host-interfaces-core.md`, which does not define the `Checkpoint` seam at all: `PIC-10` and the `#checkpoint-seam` anchor live in `host-interfaces-services.md`.

The interface shape is non-binding per `GOV-18` arm (a) (the seam's member set and `CheckpointKind` union are explicitly a non-normative reference; only the behavioural rules bind), so this is not a contract conflict that would split two implementers — it is a stale cross-reference that asserts a contradiction that no longer exists and sends a reader to the wrong page. The clause should be struck.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `592219d` (pi-loom plan: resolve "H3a seam interfaces cite no source for member shapes", 2026-06-11) authored the parenthetical; `86ab9af` (pi-loom plan: resolve "V8a / V8a-T Spec field points at pages that do not define PIC-10", 2026-06-16) invalidated it.
**History:** When `592219d` added the H3a clause "even though `V8a` lists `Checkpoint` under `host-interfaces-core.md`," it was accurate — at that revision `V8a`'s Spec line read `host-interfaces-core.md`. Five days later `86ab9af` retargeted `V8a` (and `V8a-T`) from `host-interfaces-core.md` to `host-interfaces-services.md` to fix a separate finding (PIC-10 is not defined on core.md), but did not update H3a's parenthetical, which still asserts the now-removed core.md citation. The defect is the residue of that un-propagated edit, not present at either commit in isolation.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/H3a-di-seam-skeleton.md`, the **Adds** parenthetical currently reads:

> (the `Checkpoint` contract is anchored at [`host-interfaces-services.md#checkpoint-seam`](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-services.md#checkpoint-seam), even though `V8a` lists `Checkpoint` under `host-interfaces-core.md`)

`V8a` now cites `host-interfaces-services.md`, matching the anchor, so the reconciliation clause has no referent. Strike the "even though `V8a` lists `Checkpoint` under `host-interfaces-core.md`" clause, leaving the anchor statement: "(the `Checkpoint` contract is anchored at [`host-interfaces-services.md#checkpoint-seam`](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-services.md#checkpoint-seam))". Do not edit `V8a` — its Spec citation is already correct. The spec pages are read-only for this fix.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader of `H3a` is told `V8a` cites `host-interfaces-core.md` for `Checkpoint` and may go looking there for the seam contract, which is not defined on that page. Because `V8a` actually cites the same `host-interfaces-services.md` page `H3a` anchors, and the interface shape is non-binding (`GOV-18` arm (a)), no implementer divergence on the real contract follows — the harm is a misleading, self-contradictory note, not a build defect.
