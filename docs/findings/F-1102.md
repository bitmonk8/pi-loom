## Problem

`V17a` (Cancellation core) still bundles the three-channel swallowing-handler side-channel suppression substrate at the `Checkpoint` seam alongside its `loomAbort` controller, the three forwarding paths, abort-reason and downward-only propagation, the late-settlement discard rules, and the `CNCL-1..6` outcome assertions — even after `V17b` (forwarding-listener throw-trap) and `V17c` (checkpoint granularity) were peeled out. The substrate meets the same separability test those peels used: it is observable through the `V8a` `Checkpoint` seam independent of the forwarding contract, it carries a distinct `H4b` dependency the core does not otherwise require, and it owns its own coverage-matrix facet — the `V17a` facet of row `cka-33`. Because it is co-resident with the core, an implementer can satisfy most of `V17a`'s `Ships when` bullets (controller, forwarding, `CNCL`) while leaving the substrate suppression incomplete, and a reviewer must evaluate both in a single pass.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** cb8737a (2026-06-12, "resolve 'Systemic leaf over-bundling across the plan corpus'" — peeled the forwarding-listener throw-trap into `V17b`), 5b95143 (2026-06-18, "resolve 'V17a bundles forwarding contract and checkpoint-site wiring'" — peeled the checkpoint-granularity surface into `V17c`, leaving `V17a` in its current shape)
**History:** The swallowing-handler substrate test entered `V17a` in 52a6819 (2026-06-10, "resolve 'cancellation test bullet conflates four obligations'"). `V17a` was subsequently de-bundled twice: cb8737a peeled the forwarding-listener throw-trap into `V17b`, and 5b95143 peeled the checkpoint-granularity surface into `V17c`. Both passes used the "distinctly complex, independently-verifiable sub-mechanism" / "observable independently" rationale yet left the swallowing-handler substrate resident in `V17a`. The residual-largeness condition this finding flags is the interaction of those passes: once `V17b` and `V17c` were removed, the substrate became the remaining surface meeting that same separability rationale, with no peel applied to it.

## Solution approach

Peel the `Checkpoint`-seam swallowing-handler suppression substrate out of `V17a` into a new sibling leaf in the V17 slice with a paired `-T` tests leaf, mirroring the `V17b`/`V17c` peel precedent; move the swallowing-handler substrate clauses out of `V17a` (`Adds`, `Tests`, `Ships when`) and `V17a-T` (`Tests`) into the new pair, leaving the controller, forwarding paths, propagation, late-settlement rules, and `CNCL-1..6` in `V17a`. Give the new leaf `Deps` on `V8a` and `H4b` plus its own `-T`, and link it into `plan.md`'s V17 slice listing. Retarget the `V17a` facet of `coverage-matrix.md` row `cka-33` to the new leaf, and update the four per-site routing leaves (`V14f`, `V13f`, `V15h`, `V9o`) — including `V9o`'s `Deps` edge — to delegate to the new substrate leaf instead of `V17a`. Use the literal `<new>` placeholder for the new leaf ID.
