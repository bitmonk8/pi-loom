## Problem

The Non-empty-scan canary asserts both counters strictly greater than zero, treating any zero as a misconfigured walker, but never states the precondition that makes "zero ⇒ misconfiguration" valid: that the audited source tree always contains at least one in-scope `.ts` file and at least one recognised Pi reference. That invariant holds for the loom runtime by construction (via Orientation — Prerequisites and the surface-set-closure property) but is not pinned at the floor. Two reasonable implementers diverge on a Pi-free tree — one reds the floor universally, one assumes the precondition silently — so canary behaviour differs on the same edge tree.

Both are omission/interaction defects present since the apparatus's inception, not introduced by a later edit.

## Solution approach

At the `audit-non-empty-scan-canary` paragraph, either state the precondition invariant the `> 0` floor relies on — that the audited source tree, by the loom runtime's construction, always carries at least one in-scope `.ts` file and at least one recognised Pi reference — and forward-link the entry-point obligation that guarantees it (`overview-and-orientation.md#orientation` Prerequisites), or narrow the canary's normative statement to a tree known to contain Pi surface.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the `> 0` floor value itself (it stays as written) and the addition of any separate parse/load diagnostic.
