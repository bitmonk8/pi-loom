## Finding

The `loom/runtime/reload-teardown-timeout` *Message* template in `code-registry-runtime.md` is `reload teardown timed out after <ms>ms; <N> invocation(s) still in flight: <list>`, where its own *Trigger* prose pins `<N>` as "the count of still-in-flight invocations" with `<N> >= 1`. The byte-level rendering of `<N>`, however, is not pinned anywhere.

The §-level closure paragraph at the head of `placeholder-rendering-a.md` is strict: every `<…>` token in any registry *Message* column "is either (a) a placeholder enumerated in categories 1–7 (rendered byte-identically per its category's rule), (b) a §8 placeholder…, (c) the bespoke `<list>` placeholder in `loom/runtime/reload-teardown-timeout`…, (d) the `<failure>` carve-out…, or (e) the `<observed>` carve-out…", with "No other placeholders are admitted; this closure is enforced at build time." `<N>` matches none of those arms: §4's numeric *Placeholders* list explicitly enumerates `<i>`, `<length>`, `<depth>`, `<offset>`, `<count>`, `<index>`, `<required>`, `<provided>`, `<max>` and stops there, and §7's *Numeric* sub-rule lists `<ms>`, `<deadline>` only. So `<N>` is a registry-row placeholder that the corpus's own closure invariant simultaneously requires to be categorised and leaves uncategorised.

Operationally this leaves the byte form of `<N>` undefined under DIAG-4. For `<N> = 1` two implementers agree by coincidence; at larger counts they may diverge on thousands separators, leading zeros, sign of zero, etc. — the very questions §4's integer rule was written to settle. The `reload-teardown-timeout` row's own test vector (`<N> = 2`) does not bound the rule for arbitrary `<N>`.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `5e8b499` (2026-05-06, *resolve "Hot-reload teardown contract for the loom extension instance is unspecified"*) — first authored the `loom/runtime/reload-teardown-timeout` row, introducing `<N>` into the *Message* template alongside `<ms>` and `<list>`; `f930818` (2026-05-07, *resolve "placeholder rendering exemption open-ended"*) — replaced the pre-existing **V1.0 closure scope (non-normative)** escape-hatch paragraph (which explicitly named `<ms>` and `<N>` as numerics admitted under "obvious extension of the closest matching category") with the strict **Closure.** paragraph that admits only categories 1–7, §8, the bespoke `<list>`, and the `<failure>` carve-out — sweeping `<ms>` into §7's numeric sub-rule but not `<N>`.
**History:** When `5e8b499` introduced the row, both `<N>` and `<ms>` were covered by the open-ended "Several registry rows ... use additional placeholder names ... numeric (`<ms>`, `<N>`) ... do not yet carry byte-identical rendering rules" hatch in §placeholder-rendering. The next-day `f930818` closure rewrite tightened that hatch into the current strict closure and pinned `<ms>` / `<deadline>` under §7's *Numeric* sub-rule, but did not extend the numeric enumeration in §4 or §7 to include `<N>`. Subsequent edits (the `f5e89f4` directory split and later row reworkings) preserved the row text unchanged; the gap is the artefact of those two commits' interaction, not of any single later edit. Defect is `present-since` the `f930818` closure tightening.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add `<N>` to the §4 *Numeric placeholders* list in `placeholder-rendering-a.md`, alongside `<count>` (which it most closely mirrors — both render unbounded non-negative integer counts of registry-derived entities). The edit is a single token insertion into the §4 **Placeholders.** bullet and a same-commit refresh of any sibling enumerations (e.g. the *Numeric* sub-rule in `placeholder-rendering-b.md` §7 names `<ms>` / `<deadline>` only because those are the §7-scope numerics — leave that list unchanged; `<N>` belongs to §4 directly).

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The `loom watcher: <N> file(s) added or removed …` informational `loom-system-note` template in `binder/binder-model-and-context.md` and `pi-integration-contract/registration-steps.md` also uses `<N>`; both templates already pin their own substitution rule (base-10, no thousands separator, no leading zero, no sign) inline. Once `<N>` lands in §4, drop those inline restatements or convert them to a reference to §4 to avoid a dual source of truth — but do not introduce a third, conflicting rule.
- The `reload-teardown-timeout` row's own *Trigger* prose ("`<N>` is the count of still-in-flight invocations … `<N> >= 1`") states the *semantics* but not the *rendering*; once §4 admits `<N>`, the row's rendering is fully determined and no row-local rendering note is needed.
- Update the V7c / V7c-T leaves' DIAG-4 test vector enumeration to include `<N>` at boundaries that exercise the integer rule (e.g. `<N> = 1`, `<N> = 10`, `<N> = 1000` for the no-thousands-separator check, and the `-0` / `Infinity` / `NaN` guards §4's *Rule* already pins — though those are unreachable here, the same `loom/runtime/internal-error` escape applies).

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two conformant implementations may emit byte-divergent `reload-teardown-timeout` messages once the in-flight count enters the range where integer-rendering policy matters (separators, leading zeros, signed zero), violating DIAG-4's byte-identical contract for this row. The closure paragraph's "enforced at build time" claim is also unverifiable as written: any closure-checker run today would flag `<N>` as an admitted placeholder with no admitting clause.
