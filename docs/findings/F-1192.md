## Problem

The `loom/runtime/reload-teardown-timeout` *Message* template uses `<N>` (the count of still-in-flight invocations, pinned `<N> >= 1` by its own *Trigger* prose), but `<N>`'s byte-level rendering is not pinned anywhere. The **Closure.** paragraph at the head of `placeholder-rendering-a.md` requires every `<…>` token in any registry *Message* column to match one of its enumerated arms (categories 1–7, §8, the bespoke `<list>`, `<failure>`, or `<observed>`), yet `<N>` matches none: §4's *Numeric placeholders* enumeration lists `<i>`…`<max>` and the §7 *Numeric* sub-rule in `placeholder-rendering-b.md` lists `<ms>` / `<deadline>` only. So `<N>` is a registry-row placeholder the corpus's own closure invariant simultaneously requires categorised and leaves uncategorised, leaving its byte form undefined under DIAG-4 wherever integer-rendering policy (thousands separators, leading zeros, signed zero) matters.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `5e8b499` (2026-05-06, *resolve "Hot-reload teardown contract for the loom extension instance is unspecified"*) — first authored the `loom/runtime/reload-teardown-timeout` row, introducing `<N>` into the *Message* template alongside `<ms>` and `<list>`; `f930818` (2026-05-07, *resolve "placeholder rendering exemption open-ended"*) — replaced the pre-existing **V1.0 closure scope (non-normative)** escape-hatch paragraph (which explicitly named `<ms>` and `<N>` as numerics admitted under "obvious extension of the closest matching category") with the strict **Closure.** paragraph that admits only categories 1–7, §8, the bespoke `<list>`, and the `<failure>` carve-out — sweeping `<ms>` into §7's numeric sub-rule but not `<N>`.
**History:** When `5e8b499` introduced the row, both `<N>` and `<ms>` were covered by the open-ended "Several registry rows ... use additional placeholder names ... numeric (`<ms>`, `<N>`) ... do not yet carry byte-identical rendering rules" hatch in §placeholder-rendering. The next-day `f930818` closure rewrite tightened that hatch into the current strict closure and pinned `<ms>` / `<deadline>` under §7's *Numeric* sub-rule, but did not extend the numeric enumeration in §4 or §7 to include `<N>`. Subsequent edits (the `f5e89f4` directory split and later row reworkings) preserved the row text unchanged; the gap is the artefact of those two commits' interaction, not of any single later edit. Defect is `present-since` the `f930818` closure tightening.

## Solution approach

Add `<N>` to the §4 *Numeric placeholders* enumeration in `placeholder-rendering-a.md`; `<N>` renders an unbounded non-negative integer count, the class §4's integer rule already governs (e.g. `<count>`). Reconcile the inline `<N>` rendering restatements in the `loom-system-note` templates in `binder/binder-model-and-context.md` and `pi-integration-contract/registration-steps.md` against the new §4 entry so there is a single source of truth.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: do not add `<N>` to the §7 *Numeric* sub-rule in `placeholder-rendering-b.md` — `<N>` is a §4-scope numeric, and a second categorisation would re-open the closure ambiguity.
