## Finding

`query/query-tool-loop.md` carries the *Worked example: depth-6 forced respond at `max_rounds`* block (anchor `#worked-example-depth-6-forced-respond`). The block is normative: it pins the only V1-reachable hard-ceiling co-fire, fixes a concrete `RuntimeEvent` payload (including `masked: ["ceiling#2"]`, `loom: "/depth-6-co-fire"`, the depth-6 JSON document, `occurred_at`), and is the witness that PIC-1's V1 reachable predicate fires exactly where the predicate says it does.

That payload, the closed `masked` identifier set, the canonical-absence rule, the per-site reachable mask domain, the V1 reachable predicate, the pure-read MUST, the verbatim-copy obligation on cascade-twin re-emission, and the dedup-key non-inclusion rule are all normatively owned by **PIC-1** in `pi-integration-contract/runtime-event-channel.md`. PIC-1's closing sentence even points back at this worked example as *the* fixture exhibiting a non-empty `masked` payload: "A worked example exhibiting a non-empty `masked` payload is at [Query — Tool-call loop bound, *Worked example: depth-6 forced respond at `max_rounds`*]". The conformance vector and its normative owner therefore live on different pages — readers of `runtime-event-channel.md` who want to see the wire shape exercised must follow a cross-shard hop into a query-tool-loop subsection, and the page that owns the shape carries no resident witness for it.

The block's natural home is the page that owns the shape. The query-tool-loop section it currently sits in motivates the `tool_loop` slot-counting context the predicate reads, but that motivation is one link away, whereas the `RuntimeEvent` wire-shape conformance the block actually exhibits has to traverse a link in the opposite direction.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move the entire *Worked example: depth-6 forced respond at `max_rounds`* block — heading, `<a id="worked-example-depth-6-forced-respond"></a>` anchor, loom source fixture, mock-provider transcript, the `Err(QueryError { … })` payload, and the `RuntimeEvent` payload with `masked: ["ceiling#2"]` — out of `docs/spec_topics/query/query-tool-loop.md` and onto `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/runtime-event-channel.md`. Place it inside the PIC-1 block, immediately after clause (g) and before the sentence that currently reads "The wire location of `masked` … A worked example exhibiting a non-empty `masked` payload is at [Query — Tool-call loop bound, *Worked example …*]." The block is the vector that closing sentence points at, so the closing sentence collapses to "The wire location of `masked` (`details.event.masked` for runtime-event surfaces, `details.masked` for diagnostic-shape surfaces) is owned by [Hard Runtime Ceilings — `masked` field](../hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md#masked-field)." with the worked-example pointer dropped.

Retain a one-sentence cross-reference at the block's old location in `query/query-tool-loop.md`, immediately after the `tool_loop.max_rounds` paragraph that motivates the predicate's slot-count read, of the form: "*The V1-reachable hard-ceiling co-fire that exercises a depth-6 forced-respond turn at `max_rounds` is worked through at [PIC — Runtime event channel, *Worked example: depth-6 forced respond at `max_rounds`*](../pi-integration-contract/runtime-event-channel.md#worked-example-depth-6-forced-respond).*" — the anchor moves with the block, so the slug stays `#worked-example-depth-6-forced-respond` and no rewrite of unrelated incoming links is required.

- `docs/plan_topics/V13c-query-tool-loop.md` — the fourth `Tests.` bullet ("[query-tool-loop.md — Worked example: depth-6 forced respond at `max_rounds`](../spec_topics/query/query-tool-loop.md)") retargets to `../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/runtime-event-channel.md#worked-example-depth-6-forced-respond`.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

PIC-1's existing cross-reference keeps the vector reachable, so no implementer is blocked. The cost is a normative seam: the conformance witness for the `masked` wire shape is one shard away from the rule that defines that shape, the page that defines the shape carries no resident example, and the page that hosts the example is not the page a PIC-conformance test author would read first. The split also concentrates the `["ceiling#2"]` reachable-predicate evidence on a page whose primary subject is the tool loop, weakening the PIC-1 owner page as a self-contained normative source.
