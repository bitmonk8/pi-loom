## Problem

GOV-31 (`id="gov-31"`) in `docs/spec_topics/governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` opens with a single sentence whose subject — the set of aggregators the rule governs — is buried inside three nested levels of parentheses before the governing verb is reached. The outermost parenthetical qualifies the *integer-count* shape; inside it a `currently:` enumeration lists six governed aggregators; and inside the first item a ~100-word aside explains the lone single-bullet aggregator's dual role and its non-registration under GOV-30. Because that `currently:` enumeration is the normative scope of a MUST-bearing rule, a maintainer who loses the thread can mis-identify the governed set and mis-apply the lock-step edit. The content is complete and recoverable, but recovering it requires manual paren-matching across a sentence too long and too nested to parse on a single read.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** fe8aa5c — pi-loom spec: resolve "GOV-12 bundles ≥3 independently-testable sub-obligations" (2026-06-05, spec author)
**History:** GOV-31 was authored in fe8aa5c as the successor rule when the bundled GOV-12 was split; the `<a id="gov-31">` anchor and the three-level-nested opening sentence (including the ~100-word single-bullet-aggregator parenthetical) both appear in that first commit. The rule never existed in an un-nested form. Later edits to the enumeration (e.g. adjusting the seam-count item and the source-page link targets) layered additional qualification onto the already-nested sentence but did not originate the nesting.

## Solution approach

Restructure GOV-31's opening sentence (`id="gov-31"`) so the six-item `currently:` aggregator set reads as a flat, linear enumeration rather than a nested parenthetical, keeping the governing literal-preservation MUST adjacent to that enumeration. Demote the single-bullet aggregator's dual-role explanation out of the first item so it no longer separates the subject from its obligation. This is a presentation-only edit.

## Solution constraints

- Presentation-only: retain every governed aggregator item, the single-bullet caveat, and the GOV-30 (`#gov-30`) cross-references in meaning — no change to the literal-preservation MUST or its scope.
- Preserve the aggregator names verbatim (e.g. "the two Scope bullets", "the three Host runtime preconditions"); the rule's later source-set definitions reference them by name and must stay resolvable.
