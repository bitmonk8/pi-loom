## Problem

`session-shutdown-semantics.md` describes the scope of sub-step 2's per-entry `try`/`catch` two irreconcilable ways. The detailed sub-step 2 prose states the `try`/`catch` wraps both the `shutdownReason` stamp and the `loomAbort.abort()` call, so a stamp throw skips that entry's `abort()` and continues — keeping the no-throw-escapes-the-handler invariant. The **Per-step isolation** paragraph contradicts this, describing sub-step 2 as a per-entry `try`/`catch` "around `loomAbort.abort()`" only (the word "existing" reinforces the abort-only reading from before the stamp was added). Since the stamp can throw (frozen entry, proxied entry, write-rejecting setter, OOM), an implementer who follows the abort-only summary lets a stamp throw escape sub-step 2's loop and defeat the invariant, while one who follows the detailed prose is safe — materially different teardown behaviour.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 75a9626 ("pi-loom spec: resolve \"SessionShutdownEvent.reason closed set has no build-time pin against the SDK type\"", 2026-05-09)
**History:** The spec corpus is git-tracked. The **Per-step isolation** paragraph was introduced by 6ce416d (2026-05-07, "Teardown sub-steps 1, 4, and 5 lack a per-step isolation rule") describing sub-step 2 as "per-entry `try`/`catch` around `loomAbort.abort()`" — at that point sub-step 2 performed only the abort, so the abort-only framing was internally consistent. Commit 75a9626 (2026-05-09) then added the `shutdownReason` stamp to sub-step 2 and authored the detailed "wraps both the stamp and the `abort()` call" prose (`git log -S "wraps both the stamp"`, `git log -S "stamp MUST happen before"` both first appear there), but did not sweep the Per-step-isolation summary's abort-only wording. The inconsistency is therefore an incomplete-sweep defect introduced by 75a9626. Later commits c3de47a (relocation of the teardown sequence into `session-shutdown-semantics.md`) and 4e531dc (F-2231, sub-step 3 Clock isolation) reworked the surrounding text but carried the stale abort-only summary forward unchanged.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the **Per-step isolation** paragraph's sub-step 2 sentence so it matches the detailed sub-step 2 prose: the per-entry `try`/`catch` wraps both the `shutdownReason` stamp and `loomAbort.abort()`, a stamp throw skips that entry's `abort()` and continues to the next entry, and sub-step 2 emits no `teardown-step-failed` on either the stamp-throw or abort-throw arm. Drop the word "existing".

## Solution constraints

- The detailed sub-step 2 prose (stamp+abort scope) is the correct reading; align the summary to it, do not edit the detailed prose toward abort-only.
- Out of scope: the registry contract's `abort()`-throw bullet in `active-invocation-registry.md` — no behavioural change there is required.
