## Finding

The `Adds.` field description in `conventions.md` §Leaf format defines Class-1 binding for an `Adds.`-named mechanism as, among other things, "a named normative step on a **code-keyed obligation page** per [`coverage-matrix.md`] "Code-keyed obligation areas (no numbered REQ-IDs)"". "Code-keyed obligation **page**" is not a defined term anywhere in the corpus. The canonical term, defined by the *Sequential by default* cross-cutting rule and carried by the `coverage-matrix.md` table title, is "**code-keyed obligation area**": *Sequential by default* introduces it as "a **code-keyed obligation area**: a named normative step on a page enumerated under [`coverage-matrix.md`] *Code-keyed obligation areas (no numbered REQ-IDs)*", and the broad-catch token-resolution arm pins the unit to a single `cka-<n>` row of that table.

The two terms are not interchangeable: a *page* is a `spec_topics/**` file, whereas an *area* is one `cka-<n>` row, and a single page can host more than one area. Calling the binding unit a "page" widens it from "the specific cka-keyed normative step" to "any normative step on the whole file." The same `Adds.` sentence then silently switches to the correct term in its parenthetical — "alongside its mandating REQ-ID or **code-keyed obligation area**" — so the bullet uses both spellings of the same concept in one sentence without signalling that they denote the same thing.

The malformed term was present from the clause's first authoring — it is not a later regression. `git log -S 'code-keyed obligation page' -- docs/plan_topics/conventions.md` returns only `b737beb`; subsequent edits to the bullet (`058ac36`, `9b8964f`) reshaped the Class-1/2/3 taxonomy around it but never corrected the "page"/"area" mismatch.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `conventions.md` §Leaf format, `Adds.` bullet, replace the phrase "a named normative step on a **code-keyed obligation page**" with "a named normative step on a **code-keyed obligation area**". This aligns the first mention with both the canonical definition in *Sequential by default* and the parenthetical's existing use of "code-keyed obligation area" in the same sentence. No other text in the bullet changes; the citation to `coverage-matrix.md` "Code-keyed obligation areas (no numbered REQ-IDs)" is already correct and stays.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader of the `Adds.` binding rule meets a term ("code-keyed obligation page") that is defined nowhere, alongside the defined term ("code-keyed obligation area") in the same sentence. The mis-named "page" framing could be read as widening Class-1 binding from a single `cka-<n>` area to a whole spec file, though the immediate citation to the `coverage-matrix.md` table and the parenthetical's correct term let an attentive reader resolve it. No downstream leaf inherits the wrong term, so no implementer diverges in practice.
