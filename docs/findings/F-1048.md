## Finding

Items (f) through (an) of the version-bump editorial-review checklist each close with a templated escalation clause of the form *"on the same footing as the seven-capability literal-read assertion **under step 2(a) above** once Pi exposes a typed contract that step 2(a)'s surface-inventory probe can mechanically verify"*. The phrase `under step 2(a) above` repeats verbatim 34 times across these items (one occurrence per item, lines 36–99 of `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md`).

The directional adverb is wrong. The editorial-review checklist (items (a)–(an)) is enumerated under step 1's preamble; step 2(a) — *"Positive direction — literal-read of the imported namespace"* — is defined inside step 2's body at line 106 of the same file, i.e. **after** every item that cites it. The correct adverb is `below`, not `above`. A reader scrolling upward in search of "step 2(a)" lands on nothing (the editorial-review preamble does not define it), then either gives up or learns to ignore the spatial cue across the rest of the checklist.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception, then mechanically propagated
**Introducing commits:**
- `8c930fe` (2026-05-17, *T22c — Pi version-bump procedure has no step for the session-binding contract*) — added the first item, `(f)`, with the wording `under step 2(a) above`. At that commit the monolithic `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` placed item `(f)` at line 803 and step 2's `(a) Positive direction` sub-section at line 808, so the adverb was already directionally wrong on the introducing edit.
- Items `(g)`–`(an)` were appended across roughly twenty subsequent commits (e.g. `aebec4e`, `aa334c2`, `5d55c12`, `1f37b15`, `b077c07`, `257c545`, `e63ba6c`, `4ea0544`, `bfef26e`, `61b4063`, `113fb60`, `3683345`, `fdc5115`, `0229468`, `44782e9`, `ce4e476`, `6829a79`, `60fba43`, `9ced9f1`, `85fc906`, `1ca13ec`, `077afb5`, `2123dc9`), each cloning the item-(f) escalation-clause template — including the wrong adverb — verbatim.
- `81bf52a` (2026-06-11, *docs: collapse spec/ section pages into spec_topics/*) split the monolithic page into the current per-file layout. The new file `version-bump-step2.md` preserves the same in-file ordering (editorial-review checklist above, step 2(a) below), so the directional error survives the split unchanged.

**History:** the defect is the joint product of (i) an introducing edit that copied "above" from a working draft where the layout was different, and (ii) two-plus dozen subsequent items cloning the template without re-checking the adverb against the live layout. No commit ever flipped step 2(a) above the checklist; the relative ordering has been items-then-step-2(a) since `8c930fe`.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace `under step 2(a) above` with `under step 2(a) below` in each of items (f) through (an) of `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md` — a one-shot find-and-replace across the 34 occurrences on lines 36–99. The clause text is otherwise unchanged; no anchors or links are touched.

Implementer notes:
- Only the bare `above` adverb in the phrase `under step 2(a) above` needs flipping. Adjacent constructions in the same items use a different shape (e.g. `that step 2(a)'s surface-inventory probe can mechanically verify`, 34 occurrences; `step 2(a)'s seven-capability literal-read assertion`, 16 occurrences) and carry no directional cue — leave them alone.
- The original heading additionally claimed "one capitalised `Step 2(a)`" should be lowercased. Verification against the current file shows the three `Step 2(a)` occurrences in items (f)–(an) range are all sentence-initial uses inside step 2(a)'s own body (line 106), where capitalisation is correct. There is no stray capitalised reference inside items (f)–(an) themselves. Skip the casing change; the adverb flip is the sole edit.
- Leave the directionally-correct uses elsewhere on the page alone: line 106 contains `the literal-read assertion of step 2(a) above` inside step 2(a)'s body, which refers back to its own preceding mention and is correct as written.

## Consequence

A contributor walking the checklist and trying to find the cited "step 2(a)" by scrolling up wastes a beat before realising the reference is downward. No normative obligation changes; the bump procedure produces the same artefacts either way.
