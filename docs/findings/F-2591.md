## Finding

`overview.md` coins **query template** in bold in the Query-and-Await section ("The primitive that crosses code → model is the **query template** — an `@`-prefixed backtick template"). Bolding is the corpus's coining marker, yet there is no glossary entry for the term, and the canonical feature page it would point to is titled simply "Query" (`query.md`), whose own opening line uses "query template" in plain prose with no anchor or definition. The glossary's stated admission rule — "Add new entries here when the spec coins a new term that is reused on more than one page" — is squarely met: the bolded form recurs across at least ten pages (`expressions.md`, `grammar.md`, `lexical.md`, `influences.md`, `implementation-notes.md`, the `frontmatter/*` and `query/*` topic files, and the parse-diagnostic registry), so the omission is a glossary-completeness gap, not a one-off.

Compounding the gap, the construct is referred to by three different names with no declared equivalence: **query template** (the bolded coinage and the dominant form), **query** (the page title and much of `overview.md`'s own prose), and **query expression** (`query/query-forms.md`, and the `loom/load/typed-query-unsupported-provider` diagnostic). A reader meeting the bolded coinage cannot tell whether "query template" is the formal name of the `@`...`` construct, an informal descriptor for the back-tick literal that introduces a "query", or a synonym for "query expression". Sibling coinages from the same `overview.md` section — *woven artifact*, *type sink* — both received glossary entries; this one was skipped.

**Verdict:** present-since-inception

The bolded coinage itself predates the glossary by one day, introduced in `53f5831` (`spec: rewrite query primitive, system prompts, and schema syntax`, 2026-05-03).
**History:** `git log --follow -G 'query template'` on `overview.md` (and its pre-split `spec.md` ancestry) traces the bolded coinage to `53f5831`. `git show b4fbfac -- glossary.md` confirms the glossary was authored with sibling `overview.md` coinages *woven artifact* and *type sink* but never `query template`, so the entry has been absent for the glossary's entire existence. Subsequent commits added many glossary entries to resolve identical coined-term-without-entry findings (e.g. *surface-set closure*, *PIC*, *type sink* refinements) without ever adding this one.

## Solution Space

### Recommendation

Treat "query template" as the formal name of the `@`-prefixed backtick construct. Add a `query template` entry to `glossary.md` in its alphabetised position, with a `See:` pointer to `query.md`. Add an explicit anchor plus a one-line definition at the opening of `query.md` for that glossary entry to target. Standardise on "query template" for the construct, reserving "query" for the act or result of issuing one. In the same edit, reconcile the stray "query expression" occurrences (`query/query-forms.md` and the `loom/load/typed-query-unsupported-provider` diagnostic) to "query template", or note them as equivalent, so no third name lingers.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader cannot determine whether "query template" is a formal term, an informal descriptor, or a synonym for "query"/"query expression", and has no single definition site to resolve the three competing names against. Implementers can still build a working construct because its semantics are fully pinned on `query.md`; the cost is reader friction and continued terminology drift, against a corpus that otherwise gives every reused coinage a glossary entry.
