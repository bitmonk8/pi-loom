## Problem

`overview.md` coins **query template** in bold (the corpus's coining marker) in its Query-and-Await section, yet `glossary.md` has no entry for it even though the term recurs on more than one page, which is exactly the condition `glossary.md`'s admission rule names. The canonical feature page (`query.md`) uses "query template" in plain prose at its opening with no anchor or definition for a glossary `See:` reference to target. The construct also goes by three undeclared-equivalent names — "query template", "query" (the `query.md` title and much of `overview.md`'s prose), and "query expression" (`query/query-forms.md` and the `loom/load/typed-query-unsupported-provider` diagnostic) — so a reader cannot tell whether these are the same construct. Sibling `overview.md` coinages *woven artifact* and *type sink* both received glossary entries.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** `b4fbfac` (`pi-loom spec: resolve "no central glossary"`, 2026-05-04) — created `glossary.md` with nine entries and omitted `query template`. The bolded coinage itself predates the glossary by one day, introduced in `53f5831` (`spec: rewrite query primitive, system prompts, and schema syntax`, 2026-05-03).
**History:** `git log --follow -G 'query template'` on `overview.md` (and its pre-split `spec.md` ancestry) traces the bolded coinage to `53f5831`. `git show b4fbfac -- glossary.md` confirms the glossary was authored with sibling `overview.md` coinages *woven artifact* and *type sink* but never `query template`, so the entry has been absent for the glossary's entire existence. Subsequent commits added many glossary entries to resolve identical coined-term-without-entry findings (e.g. *surface-set closure*, *PIC*, *type sink* refinements) without ever adding this one.

## Solution approach

Add a `query template` entry to `glossary.md` with a `See:` pointer to `query.md`, and add an anchor plus definition at the opening of `query.md` for that entry to target. Standardise on "query template" as the construct's name, reserving "query" for the act or result of issuing one. Reconcile the "query expression" occurrences in `query/query-forms.md` and the `loom/load/typed-query-unsupported-provider` diagnostic to the standard name.
