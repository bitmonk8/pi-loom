## Finding

The top-of-file *Implementation gate* banner in `session-only-degraded-state.md` scopes by clause rather than by page: it declares exactly two clauses **non-gated (buildable now)** — the *Drain-state tag write (state-independent)* paragraph (the `LoomRegistry.markRuntimeDegraded` setter contract) and the *Isolation* sub-clause under *Diagnostic emission* — and marks **every other clause** gated pending resolution of the host-prerequisites clause-(a) open contradiction, with a default-to-gated rule for any clause added later.

The body, however, carries no heading, callout, badge, or section break that distinguishes the two non-gated clauses from the gated remainder. The non-gated *Drain-state tag write* section sits as an ordinary `##` section between gated sections, and the non-gated *Isolation* clause is buried mid-way inside the otherwise-gated *Diagnostic emission* section. The branch-behaviour normative text (predicate split, co-emission, idempotent re-entry, residual gaps) and acceptance criteria (a)–(m) share the same flat structure. An implementer who reads linearly past the gate preamble — or who jumps to a section directly — encounters an unbroken normative document with no in-body signal of which clauses are buildable now, and may implement gated behaviour before the dependency resolves.

The gate preamble itself is complete and unambiguous; the defect is purely that the gated/non-gated boundary it declares is not observable anywhere in the body that the declaration governs.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Physical two-section split

- **Approach:** Restructure the body into two adjacent top-level sections, e.g. `## Buildable now — non-gated clauses` followed by `## Gated — blocked on host-prerequisites clause (a)`. Move the *Drain-state tag write (state-independent)* section and the *Isolation* sub-clause into the first; place the branch-behaviour text (predicate split, co-emission, idempotent re-entry, residual gaps) and acceptance criteria (a)–(m) in the second.
- **Spec edits:** Two new `##` headings; relocate the *Drain-state tag write* section; extract the *Isolation* sub-clause from the *Diagnostic emission* section into the buildable-now section (with a cross-reference left at its original site).
- **Pros:** The phase boundary is maximally observable; a reader scanning the table of contents sees it immediately. No reliance on backtracking to the gate preamble.
- **Cons:** Extracting the *Isolation* clause physically separates it from the *Diagnostic emission* contract it isolates (the `try`/`catch` cross-reference target), fragmenting a coupled contract across two sections. Renames/relocates sections that `V9l`'s Spec citations name, requiring those citations to be repointed.
- **Risks:** New section anchors must be swept for inbound references; the extracted *Isolation* clause may lose its local context next to the emission it wraps.

### Option B — Per-clause gate badges in place

- **Approach:** Keep the existing section order and add an explicit, uniform gate marker to each `##` section (and to the *Isolation* sub-clause), e.g. a leading bold tag `**[buildable now]**` / `**[gated — clause (a)]**` immediately under each heading, cross-linking back to the gate banner.
- **Spec edits:** One short badge line per section/sub-clause; no content moves.
- **Pros:** Makes the boundary observable at every section without tearing apart contextually-coupled contracts; the non-gated *Isolation* clause stays beside the emission it isolates. No section renames, so `V9l`'s Spec citations are untouched.
- **Cons:** The boundary is distributed rather than presented as one block; a reader must notice the per-section badge rather than two big sections. Adds a maintenance obligation (the default-to-gated rule means new sections must carry a badge).
- **Risks:** Badge wording must stay consistent with the gate banner's "non-gated / gated" vocabulary to avoid a third naming for the same partition.

### Recommendation

Adopt **Option B (per-clause gate badges)**. The two non-gated clauses are not contiguous and one of them (the *Isolation* sub-clause) is intentionally embedded inside the gated *Diagnostic emission* contract it isolates; a physical split (Option A) would fragment that coupling and churn the section anchors `V9l` cites. In-place badges make the gated/non-gated boundary observable at every reading entry point while preserving local contract coherence. Whichever option is chosen, ensure the badge/heading vocabulary matches the gate banner verbatim ("non-gated (buildable now)" / "gated"), and that the default-to-gated rule for future clauses is restated at the section level so a newly added clause without a badge still reads as gated.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer of the degraded-state branch who skips the gate preamble, or who navigates straight to a section, gets no in-body signal that all branch-behaviour text and fixtures (a)–(m) are gated. They may build gated behaviour early and incur rework when the host-prerequisites clause-(a) verdict lands (which may declare the branch unreachable as written). The gate banner is fully stated at the top, so a careful reader can still produce a correct build; the gap is wayfinding, not missing information.
