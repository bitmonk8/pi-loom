## Problem

`V10d` (`Reload debounce and settings-re-merge failure-injection arm`) carries two
substantively different state machines in one leaf. The first is the stateless 250 ms
watcher-event coalescer (`cka-36`): a drop-and-reschedule debounce driven through the
injected `Clock`/`FakeClock` seam. The second is the `PIC-49` cross-window
rebuild-serialisation concurrency guard, which defers a newly-fired rebuild while a
prior window's rebuild is still in flight against the live `LoomRegistry`, AJV
validator cache, and prompt-mode registration cache, and releases on that rebuild's
synchronous publish or its `loom/runtime/registry-swap-failed` discard. These are
independently red-testable concerns coupled only at the debounce→rebuild boundary;
bundling them (atop the leaf's settings-re-merge failure-injection arm) makes the
leaf larger and harder to red-test in isolation than its `V10a`/`V10b`/`V10c` siblings.

The debounce-vs-serialisation bundling did not exist at the leaf's inception; it entered when F-1891 closed PIC-49 onto the existing debounce leaf rather than a dedicated sibling.

## Solution approach

Peel the `PIC-49` cross-window rebuild-serialisation guard off `V10d` (and its paired
`V10d-T`) into a new sibling leaf with its own `-T` pair, leaving `V10d` as the 250 ms
reload-debounce coalescer plus its existing settings-re-merge failure-injection arm.
Strike the PIC-49 `Adds`/`Tests`/`Ships when` content from `V10d` and the PIC-49
`Tests` bullet from `V10d-T`, and have the new leaf own the deferral behaviour and
guard-release on the in-flight rebuild's publish or `loom/runtime/registry-swap-failed`
discard, with `Deps` on `V8d` (the `Clock` seam), `V9b` (the `PIC-36`
publish/discard completion signal), and `V10d`. Update `docs/plan.md` §V10 to link
the new leaf and record its post-`V10d` Deps edge in the interleave note, retarget the
`PIC-49` row in `coverage-matrix.md` from `V10d` to the new leaf, and add the new leaf
to the `V10a`–`V10d` Deps range in `H5b-warn-only-canary.md`.

## Solution constraints

- Retain `V9b` in `V10d`'s `Deps` for the settings-re-merge arm even after the PIC-49 coupling moves.
- New leaf IDs follow the plan's `V<N><letter>` scheme (`docs/plan_topics/conventions.md`); allocate the next free letter rather than reusing a retired ID.
