## Problem

The `<a id="audit-recognised-shapes">` *Recognised import/access shapes* paragraph in `audit-target-categories.md` embeds, inside its CJS-prohibition clause, two adoption-time obligations that are not detection rules of the inventory-closure audit: that `package.json` MUST set `"type": "module"`, and that a "land-green precondition (per the introductory paragraph above)" MUST verify both that `package.json#type` value and the absence of CJS-reach shapes before the audit's first run. The "introductory paragraph above" referent does not resolve — the closest candidate, `inventory-audit-intro.md`'s `<a id="sdk-cap-inventory-closure-audit">` Inventory-closure audit paragraph, pins only the `src/` source-tree sweep and states nothing about `package.json#type`. The verification obligation has no mechanical owner: `audit-resolution.md`'s `<a id="audit-scope">` scopes the audit to `src/**/*.ts` and excludes `.json`, `host-prerequisites.md`'s four host prerequisites omit the extension's ES-module declaration, and `H1a` authors `package.json` literal-read assertions for `peerDependencies` / `pi.extensions` / `engines.node` / `typescript` but none for `package.json#type`.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (of the carrier paragraph)
**Introducing commit:** `e5cd514` (2026-05-09) — *pi-loom spec: resolve "T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit"*
**History:** `git log -S '"type": "module"' -- docs/` returns exactly two commits: `e5cd514`, which authored the `*Recognised import/access shapes*` paragraph and embedded the `package.json#type: "module"` adoption mandate plus the "land-green precondition" cross-reference inside the CJS-prohibitions clause from the outset, and the later `f5e89f4` size-cap shard that split the original monolithic `pi-integration-contract.md` into per-topic files (the clause moved into `audit-target-categories.md` unchanged). No intermediate commit refined or relocated the mandate. The defect is structural to the paragraph as originally drafted: the adoption-time prerequisite was authored inside an audit-shape enumeration rather than under a host-prerequisites / scaffold heading, and the "introductory paragraph above" referent it appeals to was never authored to carry the verification obligation.

## Solution approach

Move the ESM adoption obligation (`package.json` MUST set `"type": "module"`) out of the `audit-recognised-shapes` paragraph and install it under `host-prerequisites.md` with a stable anchor a build-time gate can cite. Delete the embedded ESM mandate and the "land-green precondition … MUST verify … `package.json#type`" clause from `audit-target-categories.md`, replacing them with a forward-link to the new `host-prerequisites.md` anchor and leaving the CJS-shape prohibitions intact. Add a `package.json#type === "module"` literal-read assertion in `H1a` alongside its existing `package.json` assertions, sourced from the new anchor, as the obligation's mechanical owner.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope of this finding's edit to the `audit-recognised-shapes` paragraph: the family-(4) CJS-reach prohibition clauses (i)/(ii)/(iii) and the `createRequire(...).resolve()` Step 0 (d) carve-out — they are audit-shape rules that survive the ESM-clause excision unchanged.
