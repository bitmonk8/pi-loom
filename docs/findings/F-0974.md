## Finding

The defining sentence for *clean leaf-`ENOENT`* in DISC-2 says the candidate's ancestors must "all `lstat` successfully as directories the process can enter." The follow-on "Operationally:" sentence — which is what actually drives the implementation — performs no permission probe: it just walks the ancestor chain root-first, requires each `lstat` to succeed and the result to be a directory, and routes `EACCES` / `EPERM` / `ENOTDIR` / `ENOENT` rejections on ancestors to the unreadable-source branch. The "can enter" phrase therefore decorates the definition with a predicate the spec does not actually impose.

The decoration is operationally ambiguous in the one case a reader would consult it for: a POSIX ancestor with `--x` but not `r` (search/traverse permitted, enumerate denied). Such an ancestor `lstat`s successfully as a directory, satisfies the operational rule, and lets the walk continue — but it is also one common reading of "a directory the process *cannot* enter" (no read access, so `readdir` on it would fail with `EACCES`). A reader who treats "can enter" as load-bearing reaches the opposite classification from the operational sentence: they probe the ancestor's read-permission, see it denied, and route to unreadable; the operational sentence just calls `lstat`, sees success, and routes to missing — leaving the EACCES surfacing to the candidate's own `readdir`/`stat` first-attempt (which already failed with `ENOENT`, so the answer is "missing" after all). The defining sentence and the operational rule must agree.

The fix is a definition-side edit only: state the precondition in the terms the operational rule actually checks (`lstat` succeeds and the result is a directory), and drop "the process can enter." No behavioural change; no edit to the operational sentence, the seam contract, or the `EACCES`/`EPERM`/`ENOTDIR`/`ENOENT` branch.

Neither subsequent touch altered the wording; the defect is a single-commit slip in the resolver itself, not a multi-commit interaction or a present-since-inception ambiguity.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace the "as directories the process can enter" tail of the defining sentence with a predicate that matches the operational rule. Concretely, edit the first sentence of DISC-2's first implementation-notes bullet (line 66 of `docs/spec_topics/discovery/discovery-sources.md`) from:

> A *clean leaf-`ENOENT`* is an `ENOENT` from `readdir` or `stat` on the candidate path whose ancestors all `lstat` successfully as directories the process can enter.

to:

> A *clean leaf-`ENOENT`* is an `ENOENT` from `readdir` or `stat` on the candidate path whose ancestors all `lstat` successfully and resolve to directories.

Leave the rest of the bullet untouched — the operational sentence already pins the precise behaviour (`lstat` succeeds, result is a directory; otherwise `EACCES` / `EPERM` / `ENOTDIR` / `ENOENT` → unreadable), and the seam contract in PIC-13 already covers the `.code` shapes. The point of the edit is to make the two-sentence pair self-consistent, not to add new normativity.

Edge case the implementer must watch: a POSIX ancestor with `--x` but not `r` still satisfies the revised predicate (its `lstat` succeeds and it is a directory). Any `EACCES` arising from such an ancestor when the candidate's `readdir` actually crosses it surfaces on the candidate path itself, where the operational rule's "neither a clean leaf-`ENOENT` nor a successful read" arm classifies it as *unreadable* — exactly as today. No platform branch is introduced.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Two reviewers reading only DISC-2's defining sentence can plausibly diverge on whether an `--x`-only ancestor passes the precondition. In practice the operational sentence immediately below resolves the contract (no permission probe is performed), so an implementer who reads both sentences and treats the operational one as authoritative produces correct behaviour; the risk is confined to readers who fix on the defining sentence and infer a permission check that does not exist. No diagnostic emission, no observer-visible behaviour, and no test outcome changes under any reading.
