## Finding

The `session_shutdown` handler's short-circuit predicate is defined in `drain-state-contract.md`'s **idempotent** clause as exactly one expression — the disjunction `snapshot.drained === true || snapshot.tag !== undefined` — appearing twice in the same paragraph (once in the at-most-once-per-instance bound, once in the *Handler control-flow ordering* clause's step (I)) and cross-referenced verbatim from `session-only-degraded-state.md` and from the `loom/host/session-shutdown-runtime-degraded` row in `code-registry-host.md`. The clause and its cross-references label this expression "broadened" or "the broadened short-circuit predicate" in five places without ever stating what it was broadened from.

A first-time reader at step (I) of the control-flow ordering — the phrase "per the broadened predicate above" — has no way to resolve the comparative. The narrower form the label implies is referenced exactly once anywhere in the corpus, and only obliquely: acceptance criterion (j) of the `reason: "new" | "resume" | "fork"` edge-case bullet (`session-only-degraded-state.md`) closes with a parenthetical noting the test confirms "the *broader* short-circuit predicate (`drained || tag !== undefined`) … against an implementation that mistakenly keyed the predicate on `drained` alone, which would silently dispatch slash invocations and re-run the full handler on every subsequent `session_shutdown` whenever `drain` threw." That is the implicit narrower form — `snapshot.drained === true` alone — but it is buried in a test-rationale clause readers reaching step (I) have no reason to have read.

The defect is purely an editorial dangling comparative: the predicate itself is well-defined and consistent across every emission site. The label "broadened" is load-bearing only as a hint that the disjunct on `snapshot.tag` is deliberate (not an oversight that should be tightened to `drained` alone); without an anchor for the comparison it reads as a cross-reference to a definition the reader missed.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Drop "broadened" from the back-references

**Approach.** Strike the word "broadened" from each of the five sites that use it as an adjective for the short-circuit predicate, leaving "the short-circuit predicate above" / "the predicate above" / "the short-circuit predicate (`snapshot.drained === true || snapshot.tag !== undefined`)" as the back-reference.

**Spec edits.**
- `drain-state-contract.md` **idempotent** clause: replace "the broadened short-circuit predicate `snapshot.drained === true || snapshot.tag !== undefined` does not fire on re-entry" with "the short-circuit predicate `snapshot.drained === true || snapshot.tag !== undefined` does not fire on re-entry"; in the *Handler control-flow ordering* clause replace "per the broadened predicate above" with "per the short-circuit predicate above".
- `session-only-degraded-state.md` *All-three-throw corner-case interaction*: replace "the [**idempotent**](./drain-state-contract.md#idempotent-handler-clause) clause's broadened short-circuit predicate" with "the [**idempotent**](./drain-state-contract.md#idempotent-handler-clause) clause's short-circuit predicate".
- `code-registry-host.md` `runtime-degraded` row: replace "the broadened short-circuit predicate" with "the short-circuit predicate".
- `session-only-degraded-state.md` acceptance criterion (j): leave the inner "the *broader* short-circuit predicate (`drained || tag !== undefined`)" intact (it is anchored locally by the immediately-following "against an implementation that mistakenly keyed the predicate on `drained` alone"), or drop "broader" for symmetry — either is consistent with this option.

**Pros.** Single-keyword sweep across five sites; preserves every normative pin; no anchor restructuring; the verbatim disjunction at each emission site is its own definition. The "broader vs narrower" intuition is captured at the one site that needs it (acceptance criterion (j)) without leaking comparative framing into reader-facing prose.

**Cons.** Loses the hint that the disjunct on `snapshot.tag` is a deliberate widening over the obvious `drained`-only candidate. A future editor unaware of acceptance criterion (j) might propose "tightening" the predicate to `drained` alone.

**Risks.** Low. The "drain-throw recovery" obligation that requires the tag disjunct is independently pinned by acceptance criterion (j)'s test scope statement, which would fail under a `drained`-only implementation regardless of the label change.

### Option B — Add a one-sentence parenthetical at first definition

**Approach.** At the first site that names the short-circuit predicate as a unit (in the at-most-once bound of the **idempotent** clause), append a parenthetical naming the deliberate widening and pointing at acceptance criterion (j) as the test that pins it. Leave "broadened" in subsequent back-references — the parenthetical now provides the antecedent.

**Spec edits.**
- `drain-state-contract.md` **idempotent** clause: at the first occurrence of `snapshot.drained === true || snapshot.tag !== undefined`, append " — deliberately broadened from the naïve `snapshot.drained === true` form (which would silently dispatch slash invocations and re-run the full handler on every subsequent `session_shutdown` whenever sub-step 1's `LoomRegistry.drain` threw but `LoomRegistry.initDrainStateTag` or `LoomRegistry.markRuntimeDegraded` succeeded; the *Drain-throw recovery* acceptance criterion (j) of the [`reason: "new" | "resume" | "fork"` edge-case bullet](./session-only-degraded-state.md#session-only-reason-degraded-state) pins this widening as a test obligation)".
- `session-only-degraded-state.md` and `code-registry-host.md`: no edits — their "broadened" labels now resolve to the parenthetical in `drain-state-contract.md` via the existing cross-references to the idempotent-handler clause.

**Pros.** Records the rationale for the disjunct on `snapshot.tag` at the same site that defines it, so a future editor proposing to tighten the predicate is steered into the test obligation that would catch the regression. Preserves "broadened" as load-bearing prose rather than redundant adjective.

**Cons.** Lengthens an already-large paragraph (the idempotent clause). Introduces a second cross-reference into `session-only-degraded-state.md` from a clause that already cross-references it for the all-three-throw corner case.

**Risks.** Low. The parenthetical is non-normative editorial framing; an implementation that consumes only the predicate expression is unaffected.

### Recommendation

Take Option A. The "broadened" label adds no information at any of the five sites that use it — the predicate is stated literally as a disjunction at each site, and the deliberate-widening rationale already lives where it is testable (acceptance criterion (j)'s narrative). The fix is a one-keyword sweep across five sites with no anchor or cross-reference impact. Implementer edge case to watch: do not strike "broader" from acceptance criterion (j)'s own *inner* comparison ("the *broader* short-circuit predicate … against an implementation that mistakenly keyed the predicate on `drained` alone"); that comparative is locally anchored by the immediately-following clause and remains coherent on its own.
