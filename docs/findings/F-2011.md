## Finding

The notation `` `\n` `` is universally used in code and spec contexts to denote the newline character (U+000A), which **is** whitespace. A reader who does not catch the qualifier "two-character sequence" or "pre-escape" will conclude that `` `\n` `` is a rendered newline — still whitespace — and that the suppression hatch cannot work as described.
