## Problem

Five REQ-IDs (`PIC-24`…`PIC-28`) share one unbroken Markdown paragraph — a single physical source line — in `diagnostic-emission-isolation.md`, and four (`PIC-45`…`PIC-48`) share one paragraph in `unknown-reason-rule.md`. Because the line-resolved ID projection (`ids.md`) maps every ID to its source line, all five and all four siblings collapse to the same `file:line` location. A reviewer auditing conformance through the index cannot navigate to a specific ID's obligation: every sibling lands at the identical line, and the per-ID scope boundary is discoverable only by reading the whole paragraph and scanning for the next inline `**PIC-N.**` label. The in-document `<a id="pic-N">` anchors already exist; the defect is purely the prose packaging the line-based projection collapses.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced by specific commits
**Introducing commits:** `82856ab` (PIC-24…PIC-28), `a9fb344` (PIC-45…PIC-48), both 2026-06-25
**History:** The host paragraphs are older prose; the multi-ID-per-line collapse could not exist until the numbered IDs were coined into them. `git log -S 'pic-24'` on `diagnostic-emission-isolation.md` returns exactly `82856ab` ("coin PIC-24..28 anchors on diagnostic-emission-isolation.md"), and `git log -S 'pic-45'` on `unknown-reason-rule.md` returns exactly `a9fb344` ("coin PIC-45..48 on unknown-reason-rule.md"). `git show --stat 82856ab` shows a 2-line change to the page (`1 insertion, 2 deletions`) and `git show 82856ab^:…` confirms the line-3 paragraph already existed beforehand — the commit retrofitted the inline labels into the pre-existing unbroken paragraph rather than splitting it. The two commits are an hour apart and share the identical pattern (coin sibling IDs inline without breaking the host paragraph), so the defect entered at each of the two ID-coining commits independently rather than at a single point.

## Solution approach

Split the two host paragraphs so each `**PIC-N.**` inline label begins its own Markdown paragraph — hence its own source line — restoring per-ID line resolution in the `ids.md` projection. The two targets are the `#diagnostic-emission-isolation` paragraph in `diagnostic-emission-isolation.md` (housing `PIC-24`–`PIC-28`) and the `*Sub-anchors and pinned substrings.*` paragraph in `unknown-reason-rule.md` (housing `PIC-45`–`PIC-48`).

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: re-numbering or decomposing any PIC ID — coin no new `PIC-N` or sub-letter IDs. This fix is paragraph structure, not allocation; `PIC-25` and `PIC-48` keep their internal sub-clauses.
- Preserve every `**PIC-N.**` label and every `<a id>` anchor verbatim, including the sub-anchors `#unknown-reason-rule-membership-check`, `#unknown-reason-rule-constant-source`, `#unknown-reason-rule-handler-trycatch`, `#unknown-reason-rule-substring-a`…`-d`, `#pic-emission-swallow`, and `#pic-emission-visibility` — no content change.
