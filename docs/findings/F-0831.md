## Problem

The step-2 preamble mandates recording a per-item audit outcome (`pass` / `fail` / `N/A` / `not-performed` with a one-line rationale) for every item (a)–(ak) in the bump commit message and declares that "omitting the row is non-conformant", but never defines what the structural unit it calls a "row" is. There is no statement of the record's keying, field set, surface form, or ordering rule. Without a fixed schema, two contributors writing genuinely conformant commit messages can produce records a reviewer cannot mechanically compare for present/absent, and a missing row (e.g. for item (af)) is structurally undetectable. The plan-side V18c acceptance gate's check — "in the shape `version-bump-step2.md` mandates" — consequently has no mechanical predicate to evaluate against.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction (effectively present-since-inception)
**Introducing commits:**
- `52b9101` (2026-05-11) — *pi-loom spec: resolve "T24 — Fork-reason watcher closure leaves the extension in an unspecified, silently degraded state"* — first introduced the obligation "the contributor MUST audit … and MUST record the per-item audit outcome (pass / fail / N/A with one-line rationale) in the bump commit message", at the four-item (a)–(d) scope. The recording shape was already unspecified at this commit; with four items and one verdict triplet the gap was small enough not to bite.
- `9eea3d9` (2026-05-06, on the pre-split monolithic `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md`) — first edit that grew the version-bump procedure surface around this paragraph; the recording-shape gap was carried forward unchanged.
- `257c545` (2026-06-05) — expanded the SHOULD-level item range to (f)–(s) without specifying a row shape; the obligation surface grew from 4 items to ~19 with no schema added.
- `e785f77` (2026-06-07) — *"Version-bump procedure six clarity/scope gaps (T061)"* — added the `not-performed` verdict for the SHOULD-level arm and introduced the load-bearing phrase **"omitting the row is non-conformant"**, which is what elevates the schema-undefined defect from an editorial gap to a correctness defect: the rule now treats a structural unit ("the row") as a binary present-or-omitted predicate that the spec never structurally defines.
- Subsequent commits (`5d73cc3`, `c5f97f6`, `9ced9f1`) added item (e)'s `(e.i)`/`(e.ii)` sub-outcome shape, the `N/A — superseded by <named Pi mechanism>` form, the pass-evidence rule's code-site citation requirement, and the item (e) `fail`-arm second-pass row — each of which added a new sub-shape that compounds with the still-undefined parent row schema.

**History:** The recording obligation was introduced at `52b9101` already missing the schema specification; every subsequent edit that grew the item set, added a verdict, or introduced a sub-outcome shape stacked on top of that original gap without ever defining what the structural unit being added to is. The "omitting the row is non-conformant" sharpening at `e785f77` is what makes the original gap operationally undecidable — but the gap itself predates it.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the step-2 preamble's per-item recording obligation (the paragraph preceding the items list, paired with the `#bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions` block) to pin a single concrete record-block schema — the structural unit the "omitting the row is non-conformant" rule already references. The schema must resolve the record's keying, field set, surface form, and ordering so the omission predicate becomes mechanically decidable and V18c's "in the shape mandates" check reduces to a present-set comparison against the closed item enumeration (a)–(ak) the preamble owns. Co-locate item (e)'s two-sub-outcome recording (`#bump-checklist-slash-dispatch-serialisation`), its `N/A — superseded by <named Pi mechanism>` form, and the (e) `fail`-arm second-pass row with the schema as content constraints on the chosen fields rather than as free-standing prose.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: authoring a commit-message-lint gate that parses the record block — the schema pinned here is the prerequisite for such a gate, deferred to a future leaf, not the gate itself.
