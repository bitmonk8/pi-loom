## Problem

The **Forwarding-listener throw** clause in `cancellation.md` enumerates three steady-state forwarding triggers whose `loomAbort.abort(source.reason)` call may throw — the slash-command `ctx.signal`-aborted trigger, the tool-exposed `signal`-aborted trigger, and the `invoke`-parent derived-controller trigger — but pins only one observable surface in-line: the `cause: "internal_error"` arm of `InvokeInfraError` at an `invoke` parent. The slash-command surface is recoverable by following the cross-reference to `error-model.md#runtime-panics`, but the tool-exposed `.loom`-callable path has no named surface in either `cancellation.md` or the runtime-defect-surface section — it is determinable only by chaining through the operational-equivalence-with-`invoke` rule in `tool-calls.md`. A conformance author cannot enumerate the three observable surfaces from the clause and its linked section alone, so two reasonable implementers can diverge on what the tool-exposed forwarding-throw observably produces.

**History:** The **Forwarding-listener throw** paragraph was authored whole in f20ef73 (the commit resolving the per-call AbortController/AbortSignal defect-routing finding); it enumerated all three forwarding triggers but pinned only the `cause: "internal_error"` arm of `InvokeInfraError` "at an `invoke` parent" as the in-line observable surface. `git log -S` over the `arm of [\`InvokeInfraError\`]` phrasing confirms that clause has not changed since f20ef73, so the single-path surface naming has been present since the paragraph's inception (the file's later moves — fecb504 split, 31ff060 docs/ relocation — did not touch it).

## Solution approach

In `cancellation.md`'s **Forwarding-listener throw** clause, clarify the per-path observable-surface mapping so each of the three enumerated triggers has a named surface, not only the `invoke`-parent arm. Map the slash-command trigger to the runtime-defect surface's system-note channel at `error-model.md#runtime-panics` and the tool-exposed `.loom`-callable trigger to `InvokeInfraError`, grounding the latter in the operational-equivalence-with-`invoke` rule in `tool-calls.md` rather than re-deriving the surface.

## Solution constraints

- The slash-command system-note string format is singly-owned by `error-model.md#runtime-panics`; reference the runtime-defect surface's two channels rather than restating the string in `cancellation.md`.
- Do not weaken the existing non-swallowing guarantee: the trap routes only the defect throw, `source.signal.aborted` is unchanged, and the next `Checkpoint`-seam await still surfaces `Err(QueryError { kind: "cancelled" })`.
