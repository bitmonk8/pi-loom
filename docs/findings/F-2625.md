## Problem

SUBS-2 (`schema-subset.md#subs-2`) binds "Authors and implementers" and cites "a future grep gate," both of which GOV-18's `#gov-18-binding-prohibition` clause forbids: the spec corpus may bind only arm (a) (the implementation target) and arm (b) (the corpus itself), not a third party's choice of prose vocabulary, and verification-venue citations are stripped under that same clause. The cited grep gate has no corpus boundary, threshold, or implementation, so the MUST is unfalsifiable as written. The glossary `schema slug` entry carries a matching "source of truth for a future grep gate" clause with the same defect. The durable intent — that within the spec corpus the canonical-hash output is named `schema slug`/`slug` and the listed synonyms are not used as its name — is a legitimate arm-(b) invariant; only the binding subject and grep-gate citation overreach.

## Solution approach

Rewrite SUBS-2 on `schema-subset.md` (anchor `subs-2`) as a GOV-18 arm-(b) invariant about the corpus's own prose rather than a directive to people: remove the "Authors and implementers MUST use" subject and the "source of truth for a future grep gate" citation. Keep the `SUBS-2` REQ-ID and anchor in place. Update the `glossary.md` `schema slug` entry's matching grep-gate clause in lockstep so it no longer cites the gate.
