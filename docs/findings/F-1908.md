## Finding

`H5b`'s `Adds.` paragraph opens with "pre-flight" as the operative noun for the warn-only live-corpus gate ("A warn-only live-corpus **pre-flight** that runs `H5a`'s closing gate…"), and uses "pre-flight" again later ("so the **pre-flight** emits no spurious finding…"). Its `**Convention.**` field also keys the obligation as "the warn-only live-corpus **pre-flight** of the closing gate". By contrast, the leaf title leads with "canary" — *Warn-only live-corpus canary (pre-activation pre-flight)* — and every other reference to the gate uses "canary" as the primary noun: the `Tests.`, `Deps.` footnote, and `Ships when.` of this same leaf, plus `plan.md` (§Release gate prose and the H5b link text) and `conventions.md` (*Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance*, "the warn-only canary").

The two terms denote the same object — the leaf title fixes their equivalence by apposition with the `(pre-activation pre-flight)` parenthetical — so there is no semantic gap, only an inconsistent operative noun. A reader who enters at the `Adds.` field (the leaf's substantive description) meets "pre-flight" first and "canary" only mid-paragraph, the reverse of every sibling surface, with no inline statement tying the two together at the point of first use.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/H5b-warn-only-canary.md`, make "canary" the operative noun wherever the leaf currently leads with "pre-flight", matching the title and every sibling surface, and keep "pre-flight" only as a descriptor.

- `Adds.` opening: change "A warn-only live-corpus pre-flight that runs `H5a`'s closing gate…" so the subject noun is "canary" (e.g. "A warn-only live-corpus canary — the pre-activation pre-flight — that runs `H5a`'s closing gate…"), establishing the equivalence inline at first use.
- `Adds.` final sentence: change "so the pre-flight emits no spurious finding…" to use "canary" as the subject, consistent with the same paragraph's earlier "This canary surfaces…".
- `Convention.` field: align "the warn-only live-corpus pre-flight of the closing gate" to use "canary" as the primary noun, retaining "pre-flight" only as the parenthetical descriptor if desired.

`plan.md` and `conventions.md` already use "canary" consistently and need no edit. The fix is confined to the H5b leaf file.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader entering at `H5b`'s `Adds.` meets "pre-flight" as the operative noun while the title, the leaf's other fields, `plan.md`, and `conventions.md` lead with "canary"; the equivalence is recoverable from the title's parenthetical, so no implementer diverges on behaviour. The cost is reader friction and a momentary "are these two things distinct?" check.
