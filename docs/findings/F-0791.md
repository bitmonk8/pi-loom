## Finding

The `## Acceptance criteria` block in `session-only-degraded-state.md` decomposes "the rule above" (the `reason: "new" | "resume" | "fork"` degraded-state branch) into per-branch fixture obligations (a)–(m). Criterion (l) — "Read-failure fallback — slash-command call site" — does not match that framing: it pins the slash-command `handler`'s `try`/`catch` around `LoomRegistry.readDrainState`, the loom-1.0 fail-safe that returns the `"degraded; /reload to recover"` system note when the read itself throws. The trigger is a throw on **any** slash invocation regardless of `event.reason` history, the call site is the slash handler (not the `session_shutdown` handler), and the normative obligation it verifies is the *Read-failure fallback* clause hanging off `LoomRegistry.readDrainState`'s bullet in `drain-state-contract.md`, not any rule in this file. Within the file, criterion (l) is the only AC whose fixture has no session-shutdown event at all — every other AC in (a)–(m), including the companion handler-entry case (m), is triggered by a `session_shutdown` delivery whose `event.reason` selects the branch under test.

The downstream effect is a test-ownership mismatch. The plan routes degraded-branch acceptance through `V9l-T` (spec target `session-only-degraded-state.md`) and routes the `readDrainState` read-failure fail-safe through `V9m-T` (spec target `drain-state-contract.md`, which already lists the read-failure fail-safe in its **Tests** bullets). With criterion (l) sitting in `session-only-degraded-state.md`, an implementer following spec-to-leaf back-references lands on `V9l-T` for an obligation `V9m-T` already owns, and the `V9l` block (still blocked on the host-prerequisites clause-(a) resolution per `plan.md` §Blocked obligations) carries a fixture obligation that neither depends on that resolution nor is invalidated by the *branch unreachable* fallback.

Criterion (m) — "Read-failure fallback — `session_shutdown` handler-entry step (I) call site" — is materially different: its fixtures are explicitly `reason ∈ {"new","resume","fork"}` deliveries and the assertion is that the full five-sub-step teardown runs, which is the session-only-degraded-branch's territory. (m) therefore belongs in this file's AC block on the same footing as (i)/(j)/(k). Only (l) is out of scope.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move criterion (l) out of `session-only-degraded-state.md`'s `## Acceptance criteria` block into `drain-state-contract.md`, co-located with the *Read-failure fallback* clause that defines the obligation it verifies. Since `drain-state-contract.md` currently carries no `## Acceptance criteria` block, add one — initially with criterion (l) as its sole entry — placed immediately after the `LoomRegistry.readDrainState` bullet (which is where the *Read-failure fallback* clause hangs). Re-letter (l) to whatever identifier the new block uses (the natural choice is to keep the letter `(l)` since it preserves the back-reference shape but anchor it to the drain-state file; alternatively re-letter to `(a)` of a fresh block — either works as long as the back-references below sweep with it).

Concretely:

- In `session-only-degraded-state.md`: delete the `(l) Read-failure fallback — slash-command call site` bullet. Re-letter the surviving `(m)` to `(l)` so the file's AC block stays `(a)`–`(l)` contiguous. Sweep the file's two internal references to "(l)" in the prose (one in the diagnostic-emission isolation discussion of "the throwing arm is (k)" needs no change; verify no other bullet cites "(l)" or "(m)" by letter).
- In `drain-state-contract.md`: append a new `## Acceptance criteria` section after the `LoomRegistry.readDrainState` bullet (or as an anchored sub-clause under it), carrying the criterion-(l) text verbatim modulo the letter. The text already cross-references `drain-state-contract.md`'s *Read-failure fallback* clause via fragment link, so the link converts to a same-file `#read-failure-fallback` anchor.
- In `V9m-T-drain-state-contract.md`: the existing `Tests` bullet "`loom/host/session-shutdown-runtime-degraded`: a `readDrainState` read-failure fails safe (slash → degraded arm; handler-entry → steady-state full teardown)" already covers (l)'s slash-arm; add a parenthetical pointer to the new criterion's anchor so the leaf-to-AC trace is explicit.
- In `V9l-T-session-only-degraded-branch.md`: no `Tests`-bullet edit is required (none of the existing bullets enumerate (l) by letter), but verify the re-lettering of the surviving (m)→(l) does not orphan a leaf-side reference.

Edge cases for the implementer:

- Do **not** move criterion (m) — its fixtures are session-only `event.reason` deliveries (`{"new","resume","fork"}`) and the assertion shape (full five-sub-step teardown runs from the session-only branch) is genuinely owned by `session-only-degraded-state.md`. The asymmetry the *Read-failure fallback* clause itself pins (slash site short-circuits on arm (c); handler-entry site runs full teardown) is exactly the asymmetry the (l)/(m) split mirrors — (l) is universal to slash dispatch, (m) is session-only-branch-scoped.
- The "the throwing arm is (k)" parenthetical inside criterion (f) of `session-only-degraded-state.md` refers to criterion (k) (interstitial-seam tag-write throw), not (l)/(m); leave it alone.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A test author working from `session-only-degraded-state.md`'s AC list authors the slash-handler `readDrainState`-throw fixture inside the `V9l-T` suite (which is blocked on a separate clause-(a) resolution) rather than inside `V9m-T` (which is unblocked and already lists the read-failure fail-safe as one of its `Ships when` items). No behavioural divergence — two reasonable implementers reach the same runtime behaviour either way — but the misplacement either delays the test (if waited on `V9l-T`'s blocker) or splits the read-failure fail-safe coverage across two leaves with no shared owner.
