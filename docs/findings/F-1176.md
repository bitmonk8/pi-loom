## Finding

`glossary.md` opens with a binding doctrine: each entry is descriptive, the canonical normative definition lives on the feature page named in the entry's `See:` reference, and "if a glossary entry and its canonical page disagree, the canonical page wins." The `query-terminating` and `in-loop` entries violate this doctrine. Both entries carry substantive enforcement-point routing rules that have no canonical home elsewhere:

- `query-terminating` defines the per-ceiling routing-class resolution (ceiling #1 panics, ceiling #2 and ceiling #4's query-terminating arms return `Err`, none of ceiling #4's query-terminating points panic), the `Err`-unhandled → *fail* arm reachability rule (propagated via `?` / returned vs consumed by `match` vs discarded via `let _ = …`), the discard-site operator-channel behaviour, and the three-way partition (`query-terminating` ∪ `in-loop` ∪ slash-load `params` cross-routing) of ceiling #4's enforcement-point arms.
- `in-loop` defines the V1 enforcement-point inventory (only ceiling #4's model-driven tool-call args row), the silence-at-loom-code and silence-at-operator rules, and the cascade-via-ceiling-#2 reachability rule for both surfaces.

The `See:` targets do not own this material. `overview-and-orientation.md#hard-runtime-ceilings` is an aggregator paragraph and per GOV-30 forward-links into normative owners rather than carrying load-bearing definitions. `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md#ceiling-4-table` owns the per-boundary destination/surface table but not the prose about panic-vs-`Err` partitioning, `Err`-unhandled-reaches-*fail* semantics, or the three-way arm partition. The direction of citation confirms the inversion: `error-model.md`'s *Failure* bullet defers to "the per-ceiling resolution in the [`query-terminating` glossary entry]" for trichotomy classification, and the `ceiling-set-invariants` section in `ceilings-3-and-4.md` links both terms back to the glossary anchors to describe its own panic-uniqueness invariant. The glossary is being read as the normative owner; only the doctrine paragraph at the top of the page says it is not.

The consequence is doctrinal rather than mechanical: a reader following the "canonical page wins" rule cannot locate a winning canonical page, and a future edit to the routing logic has no fixed home to land on.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Relocate the per-ceiling routing-class prose, the `Err`-unhandled-reaches-*fail* rule, the discard-site operator-channel rule, and the three-way arm partition statement out of the `query-terminating` and `in-loop` glossary entries and into `docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md`. Place the routing-class material as a labelled paragraph inside the existing `#ceiling-set-invariants` section (whose ceiling-#1 panic-uniqueness invariant already depends on this prose and currently back-cites the glossary anchors) and place the three-way arm partition as a labelled paragraph anchored adjacent to the per-boundary table at `#ceiling-4-table`. Reduce the two glossary entries to one-paragraph descriptive reminders that name the concept, point readers at the new canonical anchors, and carry no normative MUSTs or routing rules of their own.

Repoint the inbound citations that currently land on the glossary anchors:

- `docs/spec_topics/errors-and-results/error-model.md` §Terminal outcomes *Failure* bullet — change "the per-ceiling resolution in the [`query-terminating` glossary entry]" to cite the new `ceiling-set-invariants` paragraph directly.
- `docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md` `ceiling-set-invariants` body — drop the back-citations to the glossary anchors now that the prose lives in-section.
- `docs/spec_topics/overview-and-orientation.md` `#terminal-outcomes-aggregator` — the aggregator's `[query-terminating](./glossary.md#query-terminating)` inline link should repoint to the new canonical anchor under `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md`, consistent with GOV-30's aggregator-forward-links-into-normative-owner pattern.

Edge cases for the implementer:

- The `query-terminating` entry's parenthetical that the discard-site observability of an unhandled `Err` "is governed by the always-log set per Pi Integration Contract — Runtime event channel" is itself a delegation paragraph rather than authoring prose; keep it on the canonical site, not in the glossary stub.
- `query-tool-loop.md` and `error-model.md` carry duplicated text about cascade-via-ceiling-#2 reachability that overlaps the `in-loop` material; the relocation MUST land before any de-duplication sweep, otherwise the de-duplicating editor will collapse onto the glossary anchor that this finding moves.
- A reader-facing forwarding link MUST stay on the glossary entries so that existing external cites (e.g. README, plan-side prose) do not break; the stubs being descriptive does not preclude them from forwarding the reader.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A motivated implementer can still derive ceiling-#4 routing by reading the glossary entries, because the entries are internally consistent and the per-boundary table backs the inventory. The harm is doctrinal: the preamble's "canonical page wins" tie-breaker has no winner on these two terms, lookup by reader who follows the doctrine fails, and future edits to the partition or to the `Err`-unhandled semantics have no fixed normative home to land on, inviting drift between the glossary entry and the per-boundary table.
