## Finding

`CodeToolError` is the schema name of the `QueryError` variant whose `kind` discriminator is `"code_tool"` â€” declared in `errors-and-results/queryerror-variants.md` under the **Code-side tool-call variant** sub-heading. The token is then reused as the name of that variant on at least sixteen pages of the corpus, including `tool-calls.md` (Failures + outcome-enumeration block), `pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md` (Tool execution from loom code + Outcome routing summary), `query/query-failure-and-repair.md` (where it appears in symmetry with `ModelToolError`), `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md`, `schema-subset.md`, `pi-integration.md`, `pi-integration-contract/tool-registration-lifetime.md`, `frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-b-and-templates.md`, `diagnostics/code-registry-parse.md`, `errors-and-results/error-model.md`, and `language-and-architecture.md`. The glossary already lists the sibling `InvokeInfraError` / `kind: "invoke_infra"` with both its loom-side schema name and its wire `kind` discriminant; `CodeToolError` is the only `QueryError` variant that is heavily cross-page-referenced and yet has no glossary entry of its own.

The closest the glossary comes is the `` `cause` `` entry, which mentions `CodeToolError.cause` in passing to illustrate the cross-variant `cause` field, and the **`InvokeInfraError`** entry, which mentions it as a partition partner. Neither anchors the term, neither pins the wire-kind discriminator `"code_tool"`, and neither helps a reader who lands on, say, `query/query-failure-and-repair.md` ("`ModelToolError` is the model-loop counterpart to `CodeToolError`â€¦") and tries the glossary as a terms index â€” the index-page collection policy that `glossary.md`'s preamble advertises ("Add new entries here when the spec coins a new term that is reused on more than one page") is the contract a reader is relying on at that moment, and it is silent on this term.

The inline gloss `host-interfaces-core.md` introduced in commit `a44ad72` â€” *"(this value is also referred to as `CodeToolError`; the two names denote the same `QueryError` variantâ€¦)"* â€” establishes the alias relationship at one site, but does not satisfy the index-page contract: a reader cannot find that parenthetical by searching the glossary, and the corpus has fifteen other reuse sites where the term simply appears bare.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add one glossary entry to `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md`, alphabetically placed (it sorts between `` `cause` `` and `canonical schema hash` under the existing backtick-folding ordering used for the `` `InvokeInfraError` `` entry), following that entry's template exactly:

```markdown
- **`CodeToolError`** / **`kind: "code_tool"`** â€” The code-side tool-call failure variant of `QueryError`, surfaced when loom code invokes a tool directly via `<name>(args)` and the call fails. The schema name carries a `Code` qualifier to partition it from `ModelToolError` (the model-driven `@`...`` tool-loop counterpart, which routes recoverable in-loop failures back to the model as tool-results rather than as `QueryError`s); the wire `kind` discriminant `"code_tool"` mirrors that partition. The `cause` sub-discriminator is closed at `"validation"` / `"execution"` / `"cancelled"` / `"unknown_tool"`. See: [Errors and Results â€” Code-side tool-call variant](./errors-and-results/queryerror-variants.md#code-side-tool-call-variant), [Tool Calls â€” Failures](./tool-calls.md).
```

Implementer notes:

- Reuse the wording shape of the existing `` `InvokeInfraError` `` entry verbatim so the glossary stays internally consistent â€” the partition partner (`ModelToolError`), the loom-side / wire-name pair, and the two-link `See:` footer are the load-bearing pieces.
- Do **not** restate the `cause` enum's semantic content (the canonical definition lives in `queryerror-variants.md`'s embedded `schema CodeToolError { â€¦ }`); listing the four cause tokens is fine because they appear there too and the glossary preamble already establishes the "canonical page wins" disagreement rule.
- Do **not** also touch `host-interfaces-core.md`'s inline alias gloss. That gloss was deliberately added in `a44ad72` to disambiguate at one heavy-traffic site; the glossary entry serves the index-page contract, not the inline-gloss role, and the two co-exist with the same pattern that already holds for `InvokeInfraError` (inline definition in `queryerror-variants.md`, partition note in `host-interfaces-core.md` prose, glossary entry as terms-index).
- The companion `ModelToolError` is not added in the same edit. The original finding cites `CodeToolError` only; `ModelToolError` has the same gap (also coined in `6750c81`, also reused on multiple pages, also not in the glossary), but is the subject of a separate same-cluster finding noted below and should be resolved on its own to keep each fix narrowly scoped.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Readers who first encounter `CodeToolError` on any of its fifteen non-canonical reuse sites and try the glossary as a terms index find nothing, violating the index-page collection policy the glossary's preamble explicitly states. This is a navigational/discoverability gap, not a correctness gap â€” the canonical definition exists and every reuse site cross-links to it â€” but it is the same gap the sibling `InvokeInfraError` glossary entry was specifically added to close (commit `17e4170`), so the absence reads as an oversight rather than a deliberate omission.
