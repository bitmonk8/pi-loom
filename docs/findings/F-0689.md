## Problem

The `*Superseded-entry dispatch.*` paragraph (anchor `superseded-entry-dispatch`) sits in `drain-state-contract.md`, but it pins slash-handler behaviour on an entry-table miss — it consumes the entry-table `Map<slashName, parsedLoom>` lookup, not the `drained`/`drainStateTag` fields or the closed four-method drain-state surface that file is scoped to. By its own framing the paragraph is a sub-case of arm (a)'s steady-state dispatch and introduces no fourth `readDrainState` arm. A reader asking what `/<name>` does after a DISC-4 supersession pass drops the loom's entry searches the slash-handler registration site or DISC-4, not the drain-state contract, so the rule's only home is reachable solely by cross-reference. The paragraph's presence in that file also forces the adjacent *Scope of the closed surface* paragraph to re-assert that the entry-table lookup falls outside the closed four-method surface.

That commit's diff shows the rule was authored together with the DISC-4-side `discovery-sources.md` cross-reference and the `registration-steps.md` step 3 re-wording (`drops the LoomRegistry entry … dispatch of the orphaned /<name> returns the fixed superseded note`). There is no prior version of the paragraph at a different location, no earlier draft, and no later relocation in history.

## Solution approach

Move the `superseded-entry-dispatch` paragraph out of `drain-state-contract.md` into `registration-steps.md`, co-located with the slash-handler registration (`#slash-handler-registration`) and the entry-table `Map<slashName, parsedLoom>` lookup it consumes. Preserve the `superseded-entry-dispatch` HTML anchor at the destination so existing inbound links keep resolving, and update the DISC-4 cross-reference in `discovery-sources.md` to target the new home. Reconcile `drain-state-contract.md`'s *Scope of the closed surface* paragraph, whose entry-table-exclusion call-out this paragraph instigated.
