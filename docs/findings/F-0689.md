## Finding

`drain-state-contract.md` carries a `*Superseded-entry dispatch.*` paragraph (the `<a id="superseded-entry-dispatch"></a>` clause) that pins the slash handler's behaviour on a *missing entry-table key*: when a `session_start` collision pass (Discovery — DISC-4) has dropped a previously-registered loom's `LoomRegistry` entry, the surviving `pi.registerCommand` registration still routes `/<name>` into the handler, the entry-table lookup misses, and the handler returns the fixed system note `"loom /<name>: superseded; /reload to refresh"`. The paragraph itself states this is "a sub-case of arm (a)'s steady-state dispatch" — reached only *after* `readDrainState` returns the steady-state tuple `(drained: false, tag: undefined)` and arm (a) is selected — and is explicit that it introduces no fourth `readDrainState` arm.

That is, by its own framing the paragraph does not consume the `drained` flag, the `drainStateTag` field, or any of the four-method drain-state surface; it consumes the entry-table `Map<slashName, parsedLoom>` lookup that registration-steps.md step 5 establishes and that the slash-handler closure registered at `registration-steps.md#slash-handler-registration` performs. The file it currently sits in is scoped — by its own opening sentence and by the explicit *Scope of the closed surface* paragraph at the end — to the two drain-state fields and their reader/writer methods. A reader hunting for "what does `/<name>` do when DISC-4 has dropped the entry?" looks in `registration-steps.md` (the slash-handler registration site) or `discovery-sources.md` DISC-4 (the supersession pass itself); the current home is reachable only by following a cross-reference from one of those two pages.

The placement also degrades the drain-state file's own coherence: the *Scope of the closed surface* paragraph immediately following must already exclude the entry-table lookup from the closed four-method surface, and the *Superseded-entry dispatch* paragraph is the local reason that carve-out has to be re-asserted. Relocating the paragraph removes the only mid-section call site that forces the scope re-assertion.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Relocate to `registration-steps.md` (slash-handler registration site)

**Approach.** Move the *Superseded-entry dispatch* paragraph into `registration-steps.md` as a clause appended to the step 3 `slash-handler-registration` anchor (or to step 5's `LoomRegistry` entry-table description, which is where the `Map<slashName, parsedLoom>` lookup lives). Leave a one-line cross-reference at the original location in `drain-state-contract.md` so the existing inbound link from `discovery-sources.md` and from the `*Drain-state-gated dispatch*` clause continues to find the rule.

**Spec edits.**
- Cut the `<a id="superseded-entry-dispatch"></a> *Superseded-entry dispatch.* …` paragraph from `drain-state-contract.md`.
- Paste it under `registration-steps.md` step 3, adjacent to the `slash-handler-registration` anchor, after the *Drain-state-gated dispatch* clause; relative links to `./registration-steps.md#structural-changes-no-unregister` become `#structural-changes-no-unregister`, and the reference to "arm (a) of `readDrainState`" stays as a cross-link to `./drain-state-contract.md#loom-registry-drain-state-contract` arm (a).
- Update the cross-reference in `discovery-sources.md` DISC-4 to target `../pi-integration-contract/registration-steps.md#superseded-entry-dispatch` (keep the same anchor name to minimise downstream churn).
- Optionally drop the *Scope of the closed surface* paragraph's now-redundant entry-table-exclusion call-out in `drain-state-contract.md` if removing the misplaced paragraph leaves it without a local instigator (verify against the rest of that paragraph's content before removing).

**Pros.**
- Co-locates the rule with the slash-handler `handler` value it constrains and the entry-table `Map` lookup it consumes.
- Reduces the drain-state file by one off-topic paragraph and removes the local trigger for the scope-restatement.
- Implementer reading `registration-steps.md` step 3 sees the full closed set of handler return paths (dispatch / `"shutting down"` / `"degraded"` / `"superseded"`) in one place.

**Cons.**
- `registration-steps.md` step 3 is already a single dense paragraph; adding another sub-clause grows it further.
- Two of the four handler return-note templates (`"shutting down"`, `"degraded"`) remain pinned in `drain-state-contract.md`'s arm-(b) / arm-(c) clauses, so the four-template family is now split across two files — a different kind of placement tension, traded for the current one.

**Risks.** Tooling or external pages may link to `drain-state-contract.md#superseded-entry-dispatch`; preserving the anchor in a one-line forwarding stub mitigates this. Internal references identified during this audit (the inbound link from `discovery-sources.md` DISC-4 paragraph) sweep cleanly.

### Option B — Relocate to `discovery-sources.md` DISC-4

**Approach.** Move the paragraph into `discovery-sources.md` adjacent to the DISC-4 paragraph that already describes the supersession pass and the `LoomRegistry`-side entry drop. The DISC-4 paragraph already mentions the superseded-note dispatch in passing; promote the full normative rule to live there.

**Spec edits.** Cut from `drain-state-contract.md` as in Option A; paste into `discovery-sources.md` immediately after the DISC-4 sentence that ends `"…via the [superseded-entry dispatch] sub-case of the slash handler's arm (a)."`; replace that sentence's cross-reference with the inline rule. Update the `registration-steps.md` step 3 cross-reference (in the *drops the LoomRegistry entry … fixed superseded note* clause) to point at the new home. Preserve the `superseded-entry-dispatch` anchor.

**Pros.** Co-locates the rule with the supersession pass that triggers it; the DISC-4 reader sees the full lifecycle (drop the entry → orphaned registration → handler returns the superseded note) in one paragraph.

**Cons.**
- The rule is a slash-handler behaviour, not a discovery-walker behaviour; `discovery-sources.md` is otherwise scoped to the discovery / collision pass, not to runtime slash-dispatch.
- Pulls a runtime-handler obligation across the discovery / pi-integration-contract topic boundary, which the existing file partition keeps clean.

**Risks.** Cross-topic placement is harder to find via the pi-integration-contract sub-tree, which is the more natural search surface for handler-return-note rules.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A**. The rule's behaviour is *what the slash handler returns when the entry-table lookup misses*, which is a registration-steps step-3 / step-5 concern; the discovery side already owns *why* the entry was dropped, and that cross-file lifecycle is well-served by the inbound cross-reference from DISC-4 to the new home. Implementers must additionally:

- Preserve the `superseded-entry-dispatch` HTML anchor at the destination so existing inbound links (DISC-4, the `Drain-state-gated dispatch` clause, plan leaves `V9b` / `V9m`) keep resolving.
- Move the `V9m` plan-leaf bullets that name "superseded-entry dispatch" (in both **Adds** and **Ships when**) and the `V9m-T` Spec-Pin bullet to `V9b` / `V9b-T` in the same commit, since the leaf-spec ownership tracks the spec home.
- After cut, re-read the `*Scope of the closed surface*` paragraph in `drain-state-contract.md`: if the entry-table-exclusion call-out is left without a local instigator, trim it to a one-sentence reminder rather than the current full restatement.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The behavioural rule is fully specified — two implementers will produce the same handler. The harm is navigation and durability: a reader who searches the registration / discovery surface for the superseded-dispatch rule does not find it locally, and the drain-state file accumulates a registration-side rule that forces its own scope paragraph to re-assert what it does not cover. Over future edits, the drain-state file is at slightly higher risk of accreting more entry-table-dispatch rules under the same precedent.
