## Problem

GOV-15's release-process goal is named with two distinct root words across the corpus. "Stability" appears in the owner page filename (`governance/source-language-stability.md`), its `# Source-language stability` H1, and the NFR aggregator bullet's `<a id="source-language-stability">` anchor/label, plus the glossary, `language-and-architecture.md`, and `model-changes-and-non-goals.md` references. "Equivalence" appears in the rule's own normative title and prose and in ~30 cross-page references across `diagnostics/`, `hard-ceilings/`, and the prefix tables. The words are not synonyms — "stability" names a release-over-release persistence posture while "equivalence" names the byte-identical-after-normalisation relation GOV-15's first paragraph actually defines — so a reader skimming for what the spec promises reaches two different conceptual answers. A `grep` on one form silently omits the other, and the asymmetric distribution of qualifiers between the two spellings (the diagnostic-registry carve-out was added only to the "equivalence" prose) is a slow drift surface future GOV-15 amendments must police by hand.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (multi-commit-interaction, same day)
**Introducing commits:** `6e5e373` (2026-05-06) — added the `**Source-language stability.**` orientation bullet to `spec.md`; `52219c4` (2026-05-06, same day) — coined the rule body as `**GOV-13 (V1.x source-language equivalence — no mechanical gate).**`
**History:** `git log -S "Source-language stability"` and `git log -S "source-language equivalence"` both bottom out on 2026-05-06. The two spellings entered the corpus on the same day in two separate commits. Subsequent migrations preserved the split intact: `c253233` (2026-05-07) renamed GOV-13→GOV-15 keeping the "equivalence" wording in the rule body; `7995d3c` (2026-06-05) moved the rule body into a new owner page and named that page `source-language-stability.md` / `# Source-language stability` — choosing the orientation-bullet spelling for the file/heading while leaving the rule's normative title and prose on the "equivalence" spelling, cementing the two-name pattern. No commit ever attempted to unify them.

## Solution approach

Unify the GOV-15 naming on *equivalence* across the corpus (the spelling already carried by the rule title, prose, and the dominant cross-page references). Rename the owner page `governance/source-language-stability.md` → `source-language-equivalence.md`, rewrite its `# Source-language stability` H1, and re-spell the `<a id="source-language-stability">` NFR aggregator bullet's label on `overview-and-orientation.md` to use *equivalence*. Update the `governance.md` TOC entry, the `glossary.md` GOV-N reference, the `language-and-architecture.md` top-of-file comment, and the `model-changes-and-non-goals.md` entry and `Recorded at:` line, along with every relative-link target across the spec corpus that points at the renamed page.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the independent content, layout, and wording issues on the GOV-15 owner page and the NFR aggregator bullet (the same-cluster siblings in Relationships) — change only the name/spelling, not GOV-15's substance.
- The owner-page rename and every dependent relative-link target update land in a single commit per GOV-30 lock-step.
