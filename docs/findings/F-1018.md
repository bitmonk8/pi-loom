## Problem

`schema-subset.md` — home of the JSON Schema subset, the lowering pass, and the canonical-hash → schema-slug recipe — asserts normative authority over tool-registration vocabulary owned by other pages. Step 5 (*Synthesised names*, `#synthesised-names`) enumerates four slug-bearing name forms and declares the list "the source of truth for the full set", but two of them (`__loom_callee_<slug>__<post-rename-name>` and `__loom_bind_<slug>`) are registration-protocol names whose authoritative definitions live in `tool-registration-lifetime.md` and `binder-inference.md`. The third bullet of *Schema-slug collision posture* (`#schema-slug-collision-posture`) restates the prompt-mode `pi.registerTool` registration-cache collision rule — naming the `loom/runtime/registration-cache-collision` diagnostic and the counter-suffix policy — duplicating a normative claim whose true owner is `tool-registration-lifetime.md`. The pages agree today, so there is no contradiction; the defect is ownership drift, where a future edit to the registration rule on its home page will silently desync schema-subset's restatement.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `c577ab3` (2026-06-06, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Glossary schema slug name enumeration cannot be complete\"") — replaced step 5 with the four-form enumeration and added the *"this list is the source of truth for the full set"* phrasing, pulling `__loom_callee_<slug>__<post-rename-name>` (a PIC name form) and reaffirming `__loom_bind_<slug>` (a binder name form) into the schema-subset page as its authoritative inventory.
- `2128e5f` (2026-06-07, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Schema-slug collision posture pinned only for registerTool cache\"") — added the *Schema-slug collision posture* paragraph and its three-bullet enumeration including the prompt-mode `pi.registerTool` registration-cache bullet, restating the `loom/runtime/registration-cache-collision` diagnostic and counter-suffix rule whose authoritative pinning already lived in `tool-registration-lifetime.md`.

**History:** Each commit individually resolved a real upstream review concern (incomplete name enumeration; collision posture covering only one of several slug-keyed sites). The scope-ownership defect emerges from the *combination*: c577ab3 promoted schema-subset to authoritative registry of all slug-bearing names, and 2128e5f then attached normative collision rules to the registration-layer rows of that registry. Neither commit introduced incorrect content; together they relocated PIC/binder normative surface onto a schema-lowering page. No subsequent commit has revisited this scope boundary, and the spec corpus moves under `docs/spec_topics/` (`81bf52a`) and the binder-rendering pin (`6892aa3`) are unrelated.

## Solution approach

Reframe the step 5 *Synthesised names* enumeration (`#synthesised-names`) from an authoritative registry into a non-normative convenience index: retain the four name forms as cross-referenced index entries, drop the "source of truth" framing, and point each registration-layer form at its owner page (`tool-registration-lifetime.md`, `binder-inference.md`). Rewrite the prompt-mode `pi.registerTool` bullet in *Schema-slug collision posture* (`#schema-slug-collision-posture`) as a bare cross-reference to the registration-cache section of `tool-registration-lifetime.md`, removing the restated diagnostic and disambiguation policy.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: `tool-registration-lifetime.md` and `binder-inference.md` receive no normative change; their existing pinnings remain the sole authoritative source for the registration-layer name forms and the registration-cache collision rule.
- The `loom/runtime/registration-cache-collision` diagnostic string remains normative only in `tool-registration-lifetime.md` (and the diagnostic-code registry); the schema-subset bullet may reference that section by cross-reference but must not quote the code string or restate the counter-suffixed disambiguation policy.
