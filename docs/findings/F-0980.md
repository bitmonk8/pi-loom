## Finding

The closing paragraph of `### File-extension namespace` in `docs/spec_topics/discovery/discovery-sources.md` reads:

> This check is a point-in-time observation, not a guarantee. The check has no REQ-ID, no per-leaf test obligation, and emits no diagnostic; it exists to record the namespace-clearance decision alongside the parallel `pi` manifest-namespace decision in the framing paragraph.

The paragraph itself names every property that disqualifies it as a normative spec statement: no REQ-ID, no per-leaf test obligation, no diagnostic — and then states its purpose explicitly as recording a past decision. Sitting under a `### ` heading inside a normative topic file, it imports decision-log discipline (history of why the namespace clearance was performed) into the implementation-target corpus, which per [GOV-18 arm (b)](../spec_topics/governance/corpus-direction-and-scope.md#gov-18-arm-b) is reserved for spec invariants. The corpus has no general convention for embedded ADR-shaped prose, and no other normative `###` subsection in `discovery-sources.md` carries a self-declared no-obligation disclaimer of this form.

The pattern is not isolated to this one paragraph: the two paragraphs above it (the `.loom`/`.warp` clearance assertion, the `--loom` flag-name clearance assertion, and the "Pi has no central file-extension registry" observation) are also point-in-time clearance observations rather than implementation obligations — the flagged paragraph is just the one that admits it. The fix therefore has to decide whether to remove the self-declaration alone or to recategorise the surrounding text it covers.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Delete the self-declaration paragraph only

**Approach.** Remove the flagged closing paragraph in its entirety. The three preceding paragraphs (`.loom`/`.warp` clearance, `--loom` flag clearance, "no central file-extension registry" observation) stay as-is; readers infer their non-obligational character from their content rather than from an explicit disclaimer.

**Spec edits.**
- `docs/spec_topics/discovery/discovery-sources.md`: delete the paragraph beginning "This check is a point-in-time observation, not a guarantee."
- No anchor changes, no cross-reference updates required.

**Pros.** Minimal diff. Preserves the `#file-extension-namespace` anchor and the inbound cross-references from `overview-and-orientation.md` and `host-interfaces-services.md`. Removes the most explicit invitation to read normative-section paragraphs as decision-log entries.

**Cons.** The three remaining paragraphs continue to carry the same non-normative character (point-in-time clearance observations) without an explicit label; a future cruft pass may flag them on the same grounds. The "no-REQ, no-test, no-diagnostic" property is no longer documented anywhere, so a future reader who wonders why the section is anchor-rich but has no acceptance criteria gets no answer.

**Risks.** Low — pure deletion of self-admitted decision-log prose.

### Option B — Demote the section to a non-normative editorial note in place

**Approach.** Keep the section in `discovery-sources.md` but reframe it as a non-normative editorial note using the same convention used on `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` and `governance/source-language-stability.md` (a leading italicised `*Editorial note (non-normative).*` marker on the opening paragraph). Delete only the now-redundant self-declaration sentence ("The check has no REQ-ID, no per-leaf test obligation, and emits no diagnostic; it exists to record the namespace-clearance decision …"); keep the "point-in-time observation, not a guarantee" sentence as the substantive claim.

**Spec edits.**
- `docs/spec_topics/discovery/discovery-sources.md`: prepend `*Editorial note (non-normative).*` to the first paragraph of `### File-extension namespace`; trim the closing paragraph to its first sentence.
- No anchor changes, no cross-reference updates required.

**Pros.** Preserves the cleared-namespace record (some future reader will ask "did we check this?" and the section answers). Uses an existing corpus convention rather than coining a new one. Resolves the underlying confusion (decision-log content in a normative section) for the whole section, not just the flagged paragraph.

**Cons.** Larger diff than Option A. Introduces the non-normative-editorial-note convention into a non-governance topic file for the first time, which weakens the convention's locality. The `### `-level heading still sits in the normative ToC.

**Risks.** Low — the convention exists and is anchored under GOV-8 *Pure rewording* worked examples in `req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`.

### Recommendation

Take Option A. The finding's narrow scope is the self-declaration paragraph, and Option A discharges it cleanly with zero anchor or cross-reference churn. Option B's preservation argument is weakened by the fact that the cleared-namespace observation is already encoded structurally in two stronger places: the `pi.looms` manifest-key list under `host-interfaces-services.md` (which enumerates the recognised Pi manifest keys) and the `pi.registerFlag('loom', …)` registration step under `pi-integration-contract/registration-steps.md`. Implementers needing to know "is `.loom` namespace-clear?" reach the answer through those normative anchors; the editorial-note paraphrase adds no information.

Edge case for the implementer: after deletion, verify that no other spec file cites the deleted paragraph by anchor (only the section anchor `#file-extension-namespace` is referenced externally — that anchor sits on the preceding `### ` line and is preserved).

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader encounters a paragraph that announces it carries no obligation while sitting inside a `###` subsection in a normative topic file, which trains them to scan for similar disclaimers elsewhere and weakens the default "everything under `spec_topics/` is normative unless marked otherwise" reading rule. No implementation behaviour or test is affected.
