## Problem

GOV-15's *Operational definitions* paragraph (`#operational-definitions` in `docs/spec_topics/governance/source-language-stability.md`) defines four ceiling-set verbs in one running paragraph: *Introduce*, *Retire*, *Relax* (all present-state, currently permitted), and *Tighten* (currently forbidden under loom 1.x, gated on the conformance-fixture suite shipping). The future-phase *Tighten* provisions — the GOV-30 lock-step and *Audit methodology* release-gate inheritance, the in-place-edit license, and the sub-case-closure obligations — ride inline behind a single "Once *Tighten* becomes permitted" sentence-opener, with no sub-heading, `(future)` marker, or other structural fence. A maintainer reading quickly or grepping for *Tighten* can mistake those future-unlocked obligations for present-day obligations the spec imposes today. The normative content is correct; only its layout fails to separate present-state operational definitions from future-phase inheritance.

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** `2ff03c2` (2026-05-10) — "pi-loom spec: resolve 'Source-language stability bullet leaves equivalence undefined at the aggregator and silently contradicts the open-set ceiling clause'"

The defect is therefore traceable to a single introducing commit; it is not a multi-commit interaction or a pre-inception artefact.

## Solution approach

Split the future-phase *Tighten* provisions — the GOV-30 lock-step and *Audit methodology* release-gate inheritance and the in-place-edit license, all contingent on the conformance-fixture-suite unlock — out of the *Operational definitions* paragraph (`#operational-definitions`) into a separate italic-heading sub-paragraph immediately after it, matching the page's existing `*Italic-heading.*` sub-paragraph style. Make the heading mark the deferred, future-conditional state. Leave the present-state material (four-verb naming, *Introduce*/*Retire*/*Relax* definitions, the *Tighten* deferral clause establishing it is forbidden today, and the closed-at-loom-1.0.0 sub-case enumeration) in *Operational definitions*.

## Solution constraints

- Presentation-only edit (GOV-8 *Pure rewording*): add no normative obligation and coin no new REQ-ID or anchor.
- Out of scope: the *Tighten* unlock-predicate wording (the conformance-fixture-suite condition), owned by the same-cluster "ships undefined" finding — relocate it verbatim, do not sharpen it.
