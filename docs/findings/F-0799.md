## Finding

GOV-15's *Operational definitions* paragraph in [`docs/spec_topics/governance/source-language-stability.md`](../../../docs/spec_topics/governance/source-language-stability.md) defines four ceiling-set verbs in a single running paragraph: *Introduce*, *Retire*, *Relax* (all present-state, currently permitted), then *Tighten* (currently forbidden — gated on the conformance-fixture suite shipping; until then deferred to loom 2.0). Immediately after the *Tighten* deferral, the same paragraph attaches a forward-looking clause — "Once *Tighten* becomes permitted (per the conformance-fixture-suite condition above), it falls under the same GOV-30 lock-step co-edit and *Audit methodology* release-gate obligations as *Relax*..." — and then resumes with sentences that read as present-state claims about both verbs jointly: "The Relax/Tighten sub-case enumeration above ... is closed at loom 1.0.0" and "Both *Relax* and *Tighten* are substantive ceiling-page edits in GOV-8 terms; they are licensed for in-place edit ... only by the present rule's *Ceiling-set carve-out* (for *Relax*) or by the present rule's *Tighten* permission clause (for *Tighten*)."

The future-conditional qualifier rides on a single "Once ... becomes permitted" sentence-opener; the surrounding present-tense prose treats *Tighten* as a co-equal operation. There is no `*Tighten future-phase provisions.*` sub-paragraph, no `(future)` marker, no fence-off into Future Considerations. A maintainer reading the paragraph quickly — or grepping for *Tighten* — can mistake the future-unlocked obligations (GOV-30 lock-step inheritance, *Audit methodology* release-gate inheritance, in-place-edit license, sub-case closure at loom 1.0.0) for present-day obligations the spec currently imposes, when in fact the entire *Tighten* operation is forbidden under loom 1.x today.

The defect is purely presentational: the normative content is correct; only its layout fails to separate present-state operational definitions from future-phase inheritance contingent on the conformance-fixture suite.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Lift the future-phase *Tighten* provisions out of the running *Operational definitions* paragraph into a clearly-labelled sub-paragraph, immediately after *Operational definitions* and before the *Diagnostic-registry carve-out*. Use the same `*Italic-heading.*` sub-paragraph style the page already uses for *Loads-cleanly predicate*, *Ceiling-set carve-out*, *Attribution test*, and *Operational definitions*. The new sub-paragraph header should make the future-conditional state explicit in the heading itself — for example `*Tighten future-phase provisions (deferred until the conformance-fixture suite ships).*` — and should carry:

- the "Once *Tighten* becomes permitted ... falls under the same GOV-30 lock-step co-edit and *Audit methodology* release-gate obligations as *Relax*" inheritance clause;
- the procedural-only / not-equivalence caveat that *Tighten*-affected inputs remain bound by the GOV-15 main-paragraph equivalence promise with no *Tighten*-specific carve-out;
- the "Both *Relax* and *Tighten* are substantive ceiling-page edits ... licensed for in-place edit only by the present rule's *Ceiling-set carve-out* (for *Relax*) or by the present rule's *Tighten* permission clause (for *Tighten*)" license clause, since its *Tighten* arm is itself contingent on the same unlock.

Leave in *Operational definitions* only the present-state material: the four-verb naming, the *Introduce* / *Retire* / *Relax* definitions (currently permitted), the *Tighten* deferral clause that establishes it is currently forbidden and gated on the conformance-fixture suite, the closed-at-loom-1.0.0 sub-case enumeration (which is a present-state claim about both verbs' shape and stays put), and the retirement-induced and routing-class-demotion-induced dead-`match`-arm clauses (which discuss *Retire* and *Relax* under present-day permissibility).

Implementer edge cases to watch: (i) the sub-case-enumeration closure sentence ("The Relax/Tighten sub-case enumeration above ... is closed at loom 1.0.0; a fourth widening or narrowing shape is forbidden in-place and requires loom 2.0 migration") is a present-state closure claim about both verbs' shape inventory and belongs in *Operational definitions*, not in the future-phase sub-paragraph; do not relocate it; (ii) the new sub-paragraph header introduces no normative obligation and no new REQ-ID — the edit is GOV-8 *Pure rewording* (presentation-only) and survives the rule's normative sentences unchanged; (iii) preserve every existing in-corpus cross-link target — none of the moved text carries a stable anchor today, so the relocation should not require GOV-30 lock-step at sibling pages, but a grep for `Tighten` across `docs/spec_topics/**` and `docs/plan_topics/**` should confirm no cross-link relies on the moved text's current source position.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Two implementers reading the *Operational definitions* paragraph could disagree on which *Tighten*-related obligations bind today versus only after the conformance-fixture suite ships. A careful reader resolves the ambiguity from the "Once ... becomes permitted" qualifier, but a quick reader, a grep-driven editor, or a maintainer authoring a *Tighten* attempt could conclude that the procedural inheritance (GOV-30 lock-step, *Audit methodology* release-gate) and the sub-case-enumeration closure already apply, license-in-place-edit included. No runtime behaviour is affected; the risk is to spec-corpus governance hygiene at the moment someone next attempts a ceiling-set edit.
