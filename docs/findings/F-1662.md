## Finding

The final paragraph of `audit-wire-and-canary.md` is a verification-detail narrative for SDK capability-inventory items 5 (cancellation propagation) and 7 (binder LLM model): it states that item 5 is verified jointly by the Step 0 (b) `AbortSignal` / `AbortController` shape probe and the Step 0 (c) `AgentSession.prototype.abort` member check, that item 7 is verified at per-loom load time and surfaces as `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved`, and that `spec.md`'s Orientation Prerequisites "name-links each item back to the obligation here so that downstream tests cite a single anchored source." None of this is about wire serialisation, the four-field record format, the infrastructure-failure surface, or the non-empty-scan canary — the four subjects the rest of the file owns. The paragraph is an orphan: it discusses the capability inventory, which lives in `capability-inventory-items.md` and `inventory-audit-intro.md`, not in the audit wire/canary file.

The content is also duplicative. Item 5's two-arm verification path is already stated at its own entry (`capability-inventory-items.md` item 5 — "enumerated in Step 0 (b) … `AgentSession.prototype.abort` is enumerated alongside `createAgentSession` under Step 0 (c)"), item 7's load-time path is already stated at its entry (item 7 — `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved`, owned by the binder strict-capability page), and the same joint-verification narrative is restated nearly verbatim in the Orientation aggregator (`overview-and-orientation.md`, Prerequisites precondition 3). The orphan paragraph carries no unique normative content.

Compounding the misplacement, the paragraph's self-reference is stale. It claims `spec.md`'s Orientation Prerequisites "name-links each item back to the obligation **here**" — but the Orientation Prerequisites bullets link items 5 and 7 to `capability-inventory-items.md#sdk-cap-cancellation-propagation` and `#sdk-cap-binder-llm-model`, not to this file. An auditor reading the paragraph would conclude `audit-wire-and-canary.md` is the canonical anchored source for items 5/7 verification and cite it in tests, when the actual anchored source is `capability-inventory-items.md`. The paragraph has no anchor id of its own.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Delete the orphan paragraph

- **Approach:** Remove the final paragraph of `audit-wire-and-canary.md`. The items-5/7 verification narrative already exists at the per-item entries (`capability-inventory-items.md` items 5 and 7) and is aggregated in the Orientation precondition-3 paragraph; the orphan adds no unique content and its self-reference is stale.
- **Spec edits:** Delete the paragraph beginning "Item 5 (cancellation propagation) is verified jointly by Step 0 item (b)'s …" from `audit-wire-and-canary.md`.
- **Pros:** Smallest diff; eliminates a third restatement of the same narrative; removes the false "the obligation here" self-reference; leaves the file owning only its wire/canary subject.
- **Cons:** PIC-15 in `inventory-audit-intro.md` still states only that items 1, 2, 3, 4, 6 are factory-probable and remains silent on how the two non-factory-probable items (5, 7) are verified — that complement is left implicit at the PIC-15 site itself (though it is explicit at each item entry).
- **Risks:** Minimal — the paragraph carries no anchor and is the target of no cross-reference or leaf citation.

### Option B — Relocate to PIC-15

- **Approach:** Move the verification narrative into `inventory-audit-intro.md` immediately after the PIC-15 sentence "Items 1, 2, 3, 4, and 6 are factory-probable …", reframed to state that items 5 and 7 are the two non-factory-probable capabilities and where each is verified. Drop the stale "name-links … the obligation here" wrapper, or repoint "here" to the `capability-inventory-items.md` item anchors.
- **Spec edits:** Delete the paragraph from `audit-wire-and-canary.md`; append a short block to PIC-15 covering item 5 (Step 0 (b) `AbortSignal` probe + Step 0 (c) `AgentSession.prototype.abort`) and item 7 (per-loom load, `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved`), cross-linking the `capability-inventory-items.md` anchors.
- **Pros:** Closes the factory-probable / non-factory-probable narrative at the single canonical PIC-15 site; keeps the verification map co-located with the capability inventory.
- **Cons:** Re-introduces a narrative that already exists at the per-item entries and the Orientation precondition-3 aggregator — a third restatement, and a wider diff than deletion.
- **Risks:** Must avoid re-creating a stale self-reference and must point cross-links at `capability-inventory-items.md` anchors rather than at the relocated prose.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A** (delete). Item 5's two-arm verification and item 7's load-time path are already carried verbatim at their own entries in `capability-inventory-items.md` and aggregated in `overview-and-orientation.md` precondition 3; the orphan paragraph adds no unique normative content, and its "the obligation here" claim is false because `spec.md`'s Orientation Prerequisites name-link items 5 and 7 to `capability-inventory-items.md`, not to this file. Before deleting, confirm no corpus cross-reference resolves to the paragraph (none found — it carries no anchor id).

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader of the audit wire/canary file encounters capability-verification commentary unrelated to the file's subject, and a reader of the capability inventory finds the same narrative scattered across three sites. The paragraph's stale "the obligation here" claim can mislead an auditor into citing `audit-wire-and-canary.md` as the anchored source for items 5/7 verification when `spec.md` actually links to `capability-inventory-items.md`. No runtime behaviour and no implementer divergence follows — the verification methods themselves are unambiguous and documented elsewhere.
