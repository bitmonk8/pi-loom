## Finding

`V4e`'s `ERR-7` Tests bullet (and the identical bullet in `V4e-T`) embeds the cross-leaf interface structure of the watcher-time reload failure-injection seam directly inside a normative test bullet: it states that the `ReloadFailureInjector` interface is "the single interface declared by `V9b`, from which `V4e` imports it," that `V9b` wires "the `loom/runtime/registry-swap-failed` registry-swap arm and the `.loom`/`.warp` re-parse arm," and that `V10d` contributes "the settings-re-merge arm." That is seam-ownership decomposition — who declares the interface and which leaf wires each arm — not a statement of what the `ERR-7` test exercises or asserts.

Per `conventions.md`, a Tests bullet is one bullet per REQ-ID describing the behaviour the leaf verifies; `Adds.` is the field that describes what a leaf introduces. The full seam decomposition is already owned canonically by `V9b`'s `Adds` — `V9b` is named there as "the single declaration site of the test-only `ReloadFailureInjector` interface for the whole watcher-time reload failure-injection seam," with the registry-swap and re-parse arms wired in `V9b` and the settings-re-merge arm contributed by `V10d` (also stated in `V10d`'s `Adds`). The narrative inside the `ERR-7` bullet therefore duplicates content that already lives in its authoritative home, and the same prose is duplicated again across both `V4e` and `V4e-T`.

The behavioural assertion the `ERR-7` bullet exists to state — a synthetic watcher-time reload failure injected through the seam routes pre-eval to `loom-system-note` with `triggerTurn:false`, exercising both the re-parse/re-merge diagnostic arm and the `loom/runtime/registry-swap-failed` registry-swap arm, without standing up a live watcher — survives intact once the ownership decomposition is stripped.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced by a single later commit, then carried forward

**Introducing commits:** `66acde6` — "pi-loom plan: resolve \"ERR-7 test injects at an undefined channel seam\"" (2026-06-11)

Commit `66acde6` introduced the seam-ownership decomposition ("owned by `V9b` (… registry-swap arm and … re-parse arm) and `V10c` (the settings-re-merge sub-arm)") into the test bullet while closing the undefined-channel-seam finding — an over-correction that embedded interface structure into a normative test bullet. Later commits reshaped but did not re-introduce the defect: `d64dce5` added the `ReloadFailureInjector.injectReloadFailure` reference, `fdce2a2` ("resolve \"ReloadFailureInjector seam has two owners\"") rewrote "owned by" into the "V9b wiring … V10d contributing" form, and `f14e929` renamed `V10c`→`V10d` after the settings-merge/reload-debounce split. The defect has been continuously present in the bullet since `66acde6`.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V4e-pre-evaluation-failures.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V4e-T-pre-evaluation-failures.md`, strike the cross-leaf seam-ownership clauses from the `ERR-7` Tests bullet — the "single interface declared by `V9b`, from which `V4e` imports it" attribution and the "`V9b` wiring … `.loom`/`.warp` re-parse arm and `V10d` contributing the settings-re-merge arm" decomposition — retaining the behavioural content the bullet asserts: a synthetic watcher-time reload failure injected via `ReloadFailureInjector.injectReloadFailure` at the watcher-time reload failure-injection seam, exercising both the re-parse/re-merge diagnostic arm and the `loom/runtime/registry-swap-failed` registry-swap arm, routes pre-eval to `loom-system-note` with `triggerTurn:false`, without standing up a live watcher.

The seam's declaration site and per-arm ownership already live authoritatively in `V9b`'s `Adds` (single declaration site; registry-swap and re-parse arms) and `V10d`'s `Adds` (settings-re-merge arm); do not restate that decomposition in the test bullet. `V9b` and `V10d` are read-only for this fix. If `V4e` is to carry the seam's Class-2 binding in its own `Adds` (the consumer naming the seam it consumes), that placement is the subject of the related Class-2-binding finding and belongs in `V4e`'s `Adds` field, not in the test bullet.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The `ERR-7` test bullet and `V9b`'s `Adds` both describe who declares the `ReloadFailureInjector` interface and which leaf wires each arm; the duplicated copy in the bullet (and its `V4e-T` twin) can drift from the authoritative `V9b`/`V10d` `Adds` as the seam evolves, leaving an implementer with two competing descriptions of the seam's ownership. The `ERR-7` behaviour remains testable, so this is organisational rather than blocking.
