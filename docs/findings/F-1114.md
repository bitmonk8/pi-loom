## Finding

The plan narrates its non-linear build order with per-slice "interleave notes" — blockquotes under a slice header that flag where slice/letter order diverges from the dep-driven order, each closing with "Sequence by **Deps**, not slice number." Notes are present on V9, V10, V11, V12, V14, and V15, but absent from other slices that carry equally-strong declared inversions, and the existing V9 note omits several of V9's own inversions.

The uncovered inversions are real and confirmed in the leaf `Deps.` fields:

- **V2** — `V2b` depends on `V5d`; `V2e` depends on `V5f` (cross-slice forward into V5).
- **V3** — `V3d` depends on `V4a` (cross-slice forward into V4).
- **V4** — `V4a` depends on `V4d` (intra-slice letter inversion); `V4d` depends on `V5d` (cross-slice forward into V5).
- **V6** — `V6c` depends on `V15a`/`V9f`; `V6d` depends on `V13a`; `V6e` depends on `V13c`/`V13d` (cross-slice forward into V9/V13/V15).
- **V8** — `V8a` depends on `V8d` (intra-slice letter inversion).
- **V9** — `V9c`/`V9g`/`V9i`/`V9n`/`V9o` depend on `V17a` (cross-slice into V17); `V9b` depends on `V10a`/`V10c` (cross-slice forward into V10); `V9g` depends on `V9h` and `V9p` depends on `V9m` (intra-slice letter inversions). None of these appear in the current V9 note, which covers only the `V9b → V11a → V9c/V9i/V9j` seam and the V18c gating.

Every edge above is correctly declared in `Deps.`, and the plan repeatedly states the canonical order is dep-driven, so execution is unaffected. The defect is narrative consistency: a reader who trusts the interleave-note convention to surface inversions will treat the un-annotated slices as letter-orderable and mis-sequence by slice/letter order.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan.md` §Vertical slices, add a `> **Interleave note.**` blockquote under each of the V2, V3, V4, V6, and V8 slice headers and extend the existing V9 note, mirroring the existing V9–V15 notes (each naming the inverted edges in `Deps.` terms and closing with "Sequence by **Deps**, not slice number"). No `Deps.` change.

The inversions to name:

- **V2 intro** — `V2b` → `V5d`, `V2e` → `V5f` (forward into V5).
- **V3 intro** — `V3d` → `V4a` (forward into V4).
- **V4 intro** — intra-slice `V4a` → `V4d`; cross-slice `V4d` → `V5d`.
- **V6 intro** — `V6c` → `V15a`/`V9f`, `V6d` → `V13a`, `V6e` → `V13c`/`V13d` (forward into V9/V13/V15).
- **V8 intro** — intra-slice `V8a` → `V8d`.
- **V9 note (extend)** — add: `V9c`/`V9g`/`V9i`/`V9n`/`V9o` depend on `V17a` (V17 slice); `V9b` depends on `V10a`/`V10c` (forward into V10); intra-slice `V9g` → `V9h` and `V9p` → `V9m`.

Edge cases for the implementer: the V9 note's current characterisation of `V9h` (needs only a `V18c` brand-string constant) and its `V9b → V11a → V9c` seam are themselves under separate review (see Related Findings); keep the extended V9 note consistent with whatever blocked-set and V9c-Deps reconciliations land, rather than re-asserting the disputed V9h/V9c claims here.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader relying on the interleave-note convention as the signal for "this slice is not letter-orderable" gets a false negative on V2/V3/V4/V6/V8 and on the V17a / V10 / intra-V9 edges, and could plan to pick leaves in letter order. Pickability is still gated mechanically by satisfied `Deps.`, so no leaf can actually be built out of order — the harm is reader mis-expectation and reduced trust in the convention, not a build defect.
