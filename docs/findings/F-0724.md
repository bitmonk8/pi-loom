## Finding

PIC-15 (`docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/inventory-audit-intro.md`, the opening paragraph anchored at `#sdk-capability-inventory` / `#pic-15`) is the citable owner anchor for the seven-capability inventory. It currently reads, in full: *"The runtime depends on the seven named SDK capabilities enumerated below. Items 1, 2, 3, 4, and 6 are factory-probable and verified by Step 0 (Capability probe) above (which on any failure skips every factory-time host-binding call — the four `pi.register*` calls and `pi.on` — and emits `loom/load/host-incompatible`)."*

Two gaps for a reader landing on this anchor:

1. **"factory-probable" is undefined here.** The term carries a specific, narrow meaning in the corpus — a member with a runtime value at extension-factory entry that can be checked by `typeof <path> === "function"` against a namespace-bound carrier — but that gloss lives only in `capability-probe.md` step (c) and `overview-and-orientation.md` precondition 3. PIC-15 uses the term as if already defined, and no inline definition or cross-reference points the reader at the carrying gloss.

2. **The 1/2/3/4/6 vs 5/7 partition is asserted without rationale.** Items 5 (cancellation propagation) and 7 (binder LLM model) are excluded from "factory-probable" by silent omission. The rationale ("item 5 is covered jointly by Step 0 (b)'s `AbortSignal` shape probe and Step 0 (c)'s `AgentSession.prototype.abort` member check; item 7 is verified at per-loom load time because `ctx.modelRegistry` is not available at extension-factory entry, surfacing as `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved`") exists in three other places (`capability-probe.md` step (c), `audit-wire-and-canary.md` trailing paragraph, `overview-and-orientation.md` precondition 3) but is absent from the inventory's own anchor, leaving a reader who follows a `spec.md` / `overview-and-orientation.md` cross-reference to `#sdk-capability-inventory` looking at an asserted partition with no in-page justification or pointer to the justification.

The partition is real and load-bearing (`V18a` asserts a `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS` partition flag against the `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES` constant `V9a` exports), and other pages reconcile it cleanly. PIC-15 is the only canonical surface where the partition appears without a definition of the partitioning predicate or a routing pointer for the excluded items.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commit:** `126ff8a` (2026-05-06) — *"pi-loom spec: Step 0 capability probe rewrite + V1.0 deferrals + Choice B"*
**History:** `git log -G "Items 1, 2, 3, 4, and 6 are factory-probable" -- docs/` shows the exact PIC-15 sentence first appearing in `126ff8a`'s rewrite of the inventory paragraph in `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md`. Prior to that commit, the inventory paragraph treated all seven items uniformly as load-bearing preconditions without partitioning them; the rewrite introduced the factory-probable partition (re-classifying item 7 out of Step 0 because `ctx.modelRegistry` is not factory-time-resolvable, and routing item 5 to step (b)'s `AbortSignal` shape probe). The commit added the gloss and the items 5/7 routing explanation to the elsewhere paragraphs (`capability-probe.md` step (c), the trailing paragraph that later landed in `audit-wire-and-canary.md`, and `overview-and-orientation.md` precondition 3) but did not include either in the inventory paragraph itself, so the inventory owner anchor has carried an unexplained partition since the partition was introduced. Subsequent commits (`81bf52a` splitting `pi-integration-contract.md` into per-page topic files; `9d914ef` adjusting the function-member count to nine; `5c8e630` adding the `AgentSession.prototype.abort` co-verification for item 5; `f985a17` / `ae529db` re-pinning ranges) modified neighbouring text but did not touch this paragraph.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Augment the PIC-15 paragraph in `inventory-audit-intro.md` with (a) an inline gloss for *factory-probable* — "members with a runtime value at extension-factory entry that the probe can check by `typeof <path> === "function"` against a namespace-bound carrier" — and (b) a sentence routing the two excluded items to their verification surfaces, mirroring the wording already present in `audit-wire-and-canary.md`'s trailing paragraph and `overview-and-orientation.md` precondition 3:

> *Item 5 (cancellation propagation) is covered jointly by [Step 0 (b)](./capability-probe.md#entry-capability-probe)'s `AbortSignal` / `AbortController` shape probe (the two Pi-side entry-point signals) and [Step 0 (c)](./capability-probe.md#entry-capability-probe)'s `AgentSession.prototype.abort` member check (the subagent-mode primitive). Item 7 (binder LLM model) is **not** factory-probable because `ctx.modelRegistry` does not exist at extension-factory entry; it is verified at per-loom load time and surfaces as `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved` per [Slash-Command Argument Binding — Strict-capability requirement](../binder/binder-model-and-context.md#strict-capability-requirement) (disjoint from the `loom/load/host-incompatible` surface above).*

Coordinate with the *items 5/7 verification-routing mapping misplaced* finding against `audit-wire-and-canary.md`: that finding's recommended target is either `capability-probe.md` or `inventory-audit-intro.md`. Landing the paragraph here as part of this fix lets `audit-wire-and-canary.md` drop the duplicate to a one-line cross-reference and resolves both findings in one edit. Pick this fix's edit first so the cross-reference in `audit-wire-and-canary.md`'s fix has a stable target. Take care not to duplicate the routing claim on three pages — fold `audit-wire-and-canary.md`'s trailing paragraph to a cross-link to the new home, and confirm `overview-and-orientation.md` precondition 3 keeps its existing aggregator forward-link to this anchor under GOV-30.
