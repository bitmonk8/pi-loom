## Finding

The normative artifact defined by the heading `### Failure-mode templates (normative)` in `binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md` (anchor `#failure-mode-templates-normative`) is referred to under two different names within the binder cluster. The canonical form — "failure-mode templates" / "Failure-mode templates (normative)" — is used in BNDR-2, BNDR-3, BNDR-9, the depth-walk and context-overflow classification prose, and every linked cross-reference (the anchor itself spells `failure-mode-templates-normative`). A second, unlinked variant — "failure-modes table" — is used in bare prose in three places:

- `defaulting-system-note-echo.md`: the System-note-rendering preamble and rules 4 and 5 (`"surface via the malformed-envelope row in the failure-modes table"`, `"the `ambiguous` row of the failure-modes table renders only …"`).
- `binder-bypass-and-envelope.md`: the envelope-schema `maxLength` paragraph (`"exercising the malformed-envelope row in the failure-modes table"`).
- `determinism-cancellation-failure.md`: the cancellation paragraph (`"the cancelled-binder system note in the failure-modes table below"`).

One artifact, two surface names, and the variant carries no link back to the canonical anchor. A reader who greps the corpus for the canonical term to find every site that drives a system-note row will miss the "failure-modes table" callsites; conversely a reader landing on a "failure-modes table" mention has no link to the defining section. (A third, mixed form — a canonical `[failure-mode templates](…)` link immediately followed by the bare word "table" — appears in BNDR-3 and the determinism classification prose; that form is linked and acceptable, and is not the target of this finding.)

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit-defect
**Introducing commit:** `c1e5d64` — *spec: REQ-ID infrastructure + Phase 12a heading promotion on five named pages (Decision #12, partial)* (Tue May 5 2026)
**History:** Phase 12a of Decision #12 promoted every bold-prefixed paragraph on `spec_topics/binder.md` (among four other pages) to a `##` or `###` sub-heading. In that edit `**Failure modes.**` became `### Failure-mode templates (normative)` — the section was renamed, not merely re-shaped. The three in-prose references calling the construct "the failure-modes table" were already in place at the moment of the rename — `git log -S "failure-modes table" -- spec_topics/binder.md` shows them landing in `0fa81fa` (system-note rendering rule 5, Tue May 5), `989b93e` (system-note rendering paragraph + rule 4, Mon May 4), and `7cb4cc3` (cancelled-binder note paragraph, Mon May 4), all authored against the pre-rename heading where "the failure-modes table" was the literal section name and the reference was self-consistent. The `c1e5d64` promotion changed the heading but did not sweep the in-prose wording, creating the drift. Later edits then compounded rather than introduced it: `1161fa0` (Sun May 31) authored BNDR-3 using the post-rename link text *plus* the legacy word "table" ("`[failure-mode templates](…) table MUST …`"), and `f5e89f4` (Thu Jun 4) split `binder.md` into the four-shard subdirectory layout, distributing the drifted references across `defaulting-system-note-echo.md`, `binder-bypass-and-envelope.md`, and `determinism-cancellation-failure.md` verbatim.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Standardise every bare-prose reference to the artifact on **"failure-mode templates"** (matching the section heading, the `#failure-mode-templates-normative` anchor, and the existing linked cross-references). Replace the five "failure-modes table" occurrences:

- `defaulting-system-note-echo.md` — the System-note-rendering preamble sentence and rules 4 and 5 (three occurrences).
- `binder-bypass-and-envelope.md` — the envelope `maxLength` paragraph (one occurrence).
- `determinism-cancellation-failure.md` — the cancellation paragraph, "in the failure-modes table below" → "in the failure-mode templates below" (one occurrence).

Where the surrounding sentence describes the artifact's tabular structure ("the malformed-envelope row in the failure-mode templates"), the substitution reads cleanly. Leave the already-canonical mixed form (`[failure-mode templates](…)` followed by "table") in BNDR-3 and the determinism classification prose untouched — it is linked and unambiguous. Adding the canonical anchor link to the newly-renamed prose mentions is optional polish, not required for consistency.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Pure wording inconsistency. The cross-reference links all resolve to the same section, so no implementer diverges on behaviour; the only cost is reduced grep/citation discoverability — a reviewer searching for the canonical term misses the unlinked "failure-modes table" callsites.
