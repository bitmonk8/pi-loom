## Problem

The `PIC-1` `Tests.` bullet in `V9d-runtime-event-channel.md` (and verbatim in its paired `V9d-T-runtime-event-channel.md`) ends with the fused clause "… is a pure read, and is cascade-copied verbatim with non-inclusion under dedup." That clause runs two orthogonal PIC-1 obligations together: the spec's clause (f) verbatim-copy obligation (the cascade-twin re-emission MUST carry `masked` copied verbatim, never re-derived) and the spec's clause (g) dedup-key non-inclusion rule (`masked` MUST be excluded from the `(kind, query_site, message, occurred_at)` dedup tuple). As phrased, "non-inclusion under dedup" reads as though `masked` is dropped from a deduped survivor — the literal reading directly contradicts clause (f). A test author encoding that reading would assert the opposite of the spec, and the wrong assertion would pass against a non-conforming implementation.

The fused phrasing has therefore been in the corpus since the leaf was created and was never the product of a later edit.

## Solution approach

In the `PIC-1` `Tests.` bullet, split the trailing fused clause into two committed assertions that map one-to-one onto spec PIC-1 clauses (f) and (g): one asserting `masked` is copied verbatim onto the cascade-twin boundary re-emission with re-derivation forbidden (clause (f)), and one asserting `masked` is excluded from the `(kind, query_site, message, occurred_at)` dedup tuple so emissions differing only in `masked` collapse as one occurrence (clause (g)). Apply the identical replacement to both `V9d-runtime-event-channel.md` and `V9d-T-runtime-event-channel.md` so the paired `Tests.` bullets stay byte-identical.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: leave the bullet's other `PIC-1` sub-clauses (closed id set, omit-when-empty, per-site reachable domain, pure read) and the leaf's `Deps.` / `Spec.` / `Ships when.` fields unchanged.
- The spec (`runtime-event-channel.md`, including clauses (f) and (g)) is read-only for this fix.
