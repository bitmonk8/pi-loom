## Problem

Two loose-label nits remain in the Session/runtime contract pages. In `conversation-drive.md`, the typed-query two-phase pairing names one part a "free phase" and the other a "forced respond turn" — "phase" versus "turn" — with no stated rationale for the asymmetry. In `drain-state-contract.md`, the `LoomRegistry` drain-state writer methods mix abstraction levels: `initDrainStateTag` names the field it sets, while `markRuntimeDegraded` names the resulting runtime state rather than the field. (The `drainStateTag`/`tag` split is excluded — it is intentionally documented.)

## Solution approach

For the phase/turn asymmetry, either clarify the distinction where the two terms are introduced in `conversation-drive.md` (cross-referencing `../query.md`), or rename the labels to be parallel. For the drain-state writer methods, either clarify the deliberate abstraction-level choice at the `#loom-registry-drain-state-contract` *Methods* surface, or rename `markRuntimeDegraded` / `initDrainStateTag` for consistency.

## Solution constraints

- If a drain-state method is renamed, sweep all four `LoomRegistry` method-name sites plus the `"loomRegistry.*"` `details.call` labels and prose references in the same edit (the page's existing rename-sweep obligation).
