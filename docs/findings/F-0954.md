## Finding

The watcher-time reload failure-injection seam has three contributing pieces, and its single declaration site `V9b` names them uniformly: "all three arms (registry-swap, `.loom`/`.warp` re-parse, settings re-merge) inject through the one `injectReloadFailure` method." `V4g`, the cross-shard consumer that exercises `ERR-7`, likewise calls the V10d piece "the settings-re-merge **arm**" throughout.

Two leaves break from that convention by calling the same piece a **"sub-arm"**: `V10c-settings-merge.md` (Adds — "the settings-re-merge **sub-arm** of the **watcher-time reload failure-injection seam** are carved out to V10d") and `V10d-reload-debounce.md` (Adds — "the settings-re-merge **sub-arm** of the **watcher-time reload failure-injection seam**"). Within the very same V10d Adds bullet the prose then reverts to "arm" ("`V10d` contributes this **arm** …", "the **arm** routes it onto …"), and the V10d / V10d-T titles and Deps notes use "arm" as well. The result is two terms for one component within a single bullet and across the two adjacent leaves.

"Arm" is the canonical term: it is what the seam's declaration site (`V9b`) and its consumer (`V4g`) use, and it correctly frames the settings-re-merge piece as one of the seam's three peer arms rather than a sub-component of an arm. The two stray "sub-arm" tokens are the outliers.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction (single origin, later widened)
**Introducing commits:**
- `fdce2a2` (2026-06-12) — "pi-loom plan: resolve \"ReloadFailureInjector seam has two owners\"": added the seam-contribution prose ("contributes this arm", "the arm routes", "settings-re-merge arm") to V10c while retaining the pre-existing "sub-arm" coinage, creating the first co-occurrence of both terms in one leaf.
- `f14e929` (2026-06-16) — "pi-loom plan: resolve \"split V10c into settings-merge and reload-debounce leaves\"": split V10c into V10c + V10d, propagating the mixed "sub-arm"/"arm" prose into the new V10d leaf and coining the V10d title "…settings-re-merge failure-injection **arm**", widening the divergence across two adjacent leaves.
**History:** The term "sub-arm" was the original coinage in V10c (present from `66acde6`, 2026-06-11, where only "sub-arm" appeared). The conflicting "arm" usage was introduced into the same leaf at `fdce2a2`; before that commit no leaf mixed the two terms. The V10c→V10c+V10d split at `f14e929` copied the mixed prose forward and added the "arm" title, so the cross-leaf inconsistency the finding describes is the cumulative product of those two commits, not a single edit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Standardise on **"arm"**, the term used by the seam's single declaration site (`V9b`) and its consumer (`V4g`), both of which enumerate the seam's three peer arms (registry-swap, `.loom`/`.warp` re-parse, settings re-merge).

Two edits, both replacing the one stray "sub-arm" token with "arm":

- In `docs/plan_topics/V10c-settings-merge.md` Adds, change `the settings-re-merge sub-arm of the **watcher-time reload failure-injection seam**` to `the settings-re-merge arm of the **watcher-time reload failure-injection seam**`.
- In `docs/plan_topics/V10d-reload-debounce.md` Adds, change `the settings-re-merge sub-arm of the **watcher-time reload failure-injection seam**` to `the settings-re-merge arm of the **watcher-time reload failure-injection seam**`.

After these edits V10c, V10d, V10d-T, V9b, V4g, and the `docs/plan.md` V10 index all refer to the piece as the settings-re-merge "arm". No title, Deps note, or test bullet needs further change — those already use "arm".

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Two names for one component invite a reader to wonder whether a "sub-arm" denotes a finer-grained unit than the seam's three "arms" when it does not. No implementer behaviour diverges — both terms point at the same `injectReloadFailure` contribution — so the impact is confined to readability.
