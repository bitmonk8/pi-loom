## Problem

`V2b`'s **Adds** field claims the type-compatibility engine introduces "a deferral marker for statically-unresolvable operands, consumed by the downstream runtime-validator sites (`V6b`, `V11g`)." Per the Class-2 seam rule in `conventions.md` §"Leaf format", a seam named in `Adds.` binds only when a consumer that lists `V2b` in its `Deps.` also names the seam in its own `Adds.` or `Tests.`. Neither `V6b` nor `V11g` lists `V2b` in `Deps.`, and neither references the marker, so the asserted producer/consumer contract is dangling. The spec's "Unresolvable operands" behaviour (`type-system.md`) is the ordinary runtime AJV validation already performed by `V5d`/`V8c`/`V11g`, not a distinct `V2b`-produced artifact.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 210ed9b — pi-loom plan: resolve "V2b ship-gate references the runtime AJV validator seam (V8a) outside its declared dependency closure" (2026-06-10, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** At the plan's first build (c6a664e, 2026-06-10) `V2b` Adds read "a runtime AJV safety-net for statically-unresolvable operands" — accurate and seam-free. Commit 210ed9b reworded it to "a deferral marker … consumed by the downstream runtime-validator sites (`V6b`, `V11d`)", introducing the cross-leaf consumer claim without adding the dependency edges or consumption assertions to the named consumers. A later over-bundling fix (cb8737a, 2026-06-12) retargeted the second consumer name `V11d` → `V11g` but did not close the dangling seam.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the **Adds** field in `docs/plan_topics/V2b-type-compat-engine.md` to strike the clause "and a deferral marker for statically-unresolvable operands, consumed by the downstream runtime-validator sites (`V6b`, `V11g`)" and restore a marker-free statement of the runtime-AJV safety net for statically-unresolvable operands. Align the **Ships when** prose ("marks unresolved operands for downstream runtime validation") to the same framing so it no longer gestures at a produced marker.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the `V6b` and `V11g` leaves — do not add `V2b` to their `Deps.` or name the marker in their `Adds.`/`Tests.` (that is the rejected promote-to-seam option); the spec is read-only.
- Do not coin a new `loom/…` code or REQ-ID for the deferral marker (`idAllocationPolicy: governed-by-spec`; the spec does not anchor one).
