## Finding

`overview-and-orientation.md` (the page `spec.md` lists in its Contents as the corpus entry point) opens its `## Overview` with:

> `pi-loom` is a [Pi Coding Agent](…) extension that **adds a domain-specific scripting language for prompts and agentic operations**.

`overview.md` (the per-topic conceptual model page, reached via the *Language and architecture* table of contents) opens its `## Overview` with:

> `pi-loom` is a [Pi Coding Agent](…) extension that **introduces a purpose-built scripting language for authoring parameterized, programmatic templates that target the code/model boundary**.

Both sentences are framed as the product-scope statement and both occupy the same structural slot (the first sentence under the file's `## Overview` heading). They differ on three points an implementer or downstream documenter will read as substantive: what loom *is* ("domain-specific" vs "purpose-built"), what it *does* ("prompts and agentic operations" vs "parameterized, programmatic templates"), and *where* it sits ("(unspecified surface)" vs "the code/model boundary"). Neither file cross-references the other, and the corpus designates no canonical owner for the one-sentence definition — `overview-and-orientation.md` is the entry the Contents links to and is the natural canonical site, but nothing in the text or in GOV-30 says so.

The defect is the divergence, not either wording in isolation: a reader who lands on `overview.md` from `language-and-architecture.md`'s "what a loom is" forward-link sees one framing; a reader who follows `spec.md`'s Contents sees the other. External documenters quoting "what pi-loom is" will pick one at random.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction

**Introducing commits:**
- `9a53d9c` (2026-05-01, *Initial scaffold: package.json, README, spec.md, extensions/*) — authored the original `## Overview` paragraph in `spec.md` with the *"introduces a purpose-built scripting language for authoring parameterized, programmatic templates…"* wording. This wording later migrated, unchanged in substance, into `spec_topics/overview.md` via the per-topic split `fecb504`.
- `d23263c` (2026-05-06, *pi-loom spec: resolve "spec.md opening paragraph — combined rewrite"*) — rewrote `spec.md`'s opening paragraph to *"adds a domain-specific scripting language for prompts and agentic operations"*, **without** touching the parallel sentence in `spec_topics/overview.md`. The divergence dates from this commit. The commit message frames the change as a `spec.md`-local rewrite; nothing in it acknowledges the sibling.
- `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04, *docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set*) and `81bf52a` (2026-06-11, *docs: collapse spec/ section pages into spec\_topics/*) — relocated the post-d23263c `spec.md` opening into `spec/overview-and-orientation.md` and then `spec_topics/overview-and-orientation.md`. These were mechanical moves with no content edits, but they **entrenched** the divergence by promoting the rewritten sentence from "the `spec.md` opening" to "a sibling file's `## Overview` opening", which is structurally parallel to `overview.md`'s `## Overview` opening and so reads as a competing canonical statement rather than an orientation paragraph above it.

**History:** The defect is a multi-commit interaction: `d23263c` introduced the textual divergence, and the later structural moves (`f5e89f4`, `81bf52a`) made the two sentences siblings of equal rank rather than parent-and-child. No single revert restores the invariant; the fix is to designate one canonical wording and reconcile the other site, regardless of which commit "caused" the drift.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Designate `overview-and-orientation.md`'s opening sentence the canonical one-sentence product-scope statement (it is the file `spec.md`'s Contents links to, and GOV-30 already treats that file as the corpus's orientation aggregator). Replace `overview.md`'s opening sentence with the byte-identical canonical sentence (preserving the surrounding "Where Pi's built-in `prompt` and `subagent` features…" expansion and the rest of the paragraph). Do **not** demote `overview.md` to a cross-reference: it is the conceptual-model page reached from `language-and-architecture.md`'s "what a loom is" TOC entry, and a reader landing there should not be bounced elsewhere for the one-sentence definition.

Implementer notes:

- The two sentences carry the same trailing markdown link (`[Pi Coding Agent](https://github.com/earendil-works/pi-mono)`). Copy verbatim so the link target stays in sync; if a separate finding repoints these to `https://pi.dev`, both sites move together.
- Do **not** add a GOV-30 entry for this sentence — GOV-30's aggregator framework governs paragraphs that enumerate items owned by topic pages; a one-sentence product definition is not an enumeration. The fix is editorial reconciliation, not a new normative rule.
- Leave `overview.md`'s second paragraph (the `.loom`-is-neither-TS-nor-Markdown elaboration with its forward-links to *Final value* and *Effects*) untouched; the divergence is confined to the first sentence.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

No implementer is blocked: both framings agree on the underlying architecture, and the normative obligations live on owner topic pages neither sentence purports to govern. The cost is documentary — external quoters and reviewers cannot answer "what is the canonical one-sentence definition of pi-loom" without picking, and the next editorial pass on either file is free to drift further because no canonicity rule exists.
