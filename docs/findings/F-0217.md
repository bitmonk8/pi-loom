## Finding

The plan corpus models the closing gate as **one gate in two distinct footings**, with two canonical, separately-named states: the **seeded-fixture footing** (the gate exercised against the permanent seeded fixtures at `H5a`) and the **live-corpus footing** (the gate reconciling the live `src/**` / `spec_topics/**` / test corpus, flipped on at the loom 1.0 release gate by `H6a`). This two-state vocabulary is used consistently everywhere the footings are named — `plan.md` ("flips that gate from its seeded-fixture footing to its live-corpus footing"), `H6a` (the flip narrative and its `Rollback` paragraph), `H5b` ("on the live-corpus footing ahead of `H6a`"), and `conventions.md` *REQ-ID discipline* ("one gate in two footings, not two gates").

`H5c`'s **Adds** breaks from that vocabulary. It states the broad-catch allow-list arm fails CI "on the same **seeded-fixture-then-live-corpus footing** as the surfaces the `H5a` closing gate owns." That hyphenated compound names a single footing that is purportedly both states at once, which contradicts the two-state model: the gate is on the seeded-fixture footing *until* the `H6a` flip and on the live-corpus footing *after* it — never on a fused "seeded-fixture-then-live-corpus" footing simultaneously. The compound appears nowhere else in the corpus.

The intended meaning is recoverable from the remainder of the same paragraph, which correctly describes the sequence ("its seeded `// allow-broad-catch:` violation fixtures reside under the same dedicated test-fixtures root… its live-corpus binding flips… at the loom 1.0 release gate… both footings are reconfigured by the single `H6a` gate-machinery flip"). The defect is the coined term in the lead clause, not the mechanism it describes.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/H5c-broad-catch-allow-list-gate.md` **Adds**, strike the coined compound `seeded-fixture-then-live-corpus footing` and restate the lead clause using the two canonical footing terms, matching the sequence the rest of the paragraph already describes. Replace the clause "…and an entry resolving to none is a CI failure, on the same seeded-fixture-then-live-corpus footing as the surfaces the `H5a` closing gate owns." with prose that says the arm is exercised against its seeded fixtures at `H5a` (the **seeded-fixture footing**) and binds against the live `src/**` allow-list at the loom 1.0 release gate (the **live-corpus footing**), on the same footing sequence as the surfaces the `H5a` closing gate owns. The `H6a` cross-reference for the live-corpus flip already exists later in the same paragraph ("both footings are reconfigured by the single `H6a` gate-machinery flip") and need not be duplicated. Keep the existing `H5a` cross-reference link intact.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader following the corpus's two-footing vocabulary hits a one-off compound that asserts a single footing being both states at once, which momentarily contradicts the "one gate in two footings" model. The surrounding sentence disambiguates the actual mechanism, so no implementer behaviour diverges; the cost is reader friction and vocabulary drift in a cross-leaf concept.
