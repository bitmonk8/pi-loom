## Problem

Two teardown-side diagnostics — `loom/runtime/subagent-dispose-failure` (raised when `AgentSession.dispose()` throws inside the per-invocation `finally`) and the teardown-side `loom/runtime/internal-error` (raised when the synchronous `session.abort()` throws) — are defined as advisory-only in PIC-9: on a normal-return teardown the loom still observes its `Ok` final value and the throw never promotes that `Ok` to an `Err`. But runtime-event-channel.md routes both through group B (neither is in the five-code `console.error` exclusion list), and the panic-emission rule then prescribes `display: true` with `content` fixed to the `"loom /<name> aborted: <message>"` framing. On a successful run this surfaces a `display: true` operator note falsely asserting the loom was "aborted", which Pi's `convertToLlm` transform injects durably into model context; the surfacing contract (`display`, `content`, target session) for an advisory teardown diagnostic on a non-aborting teardown is unpinned, leaving implementers free to diverge. A secondary tension corroborates the gap: the always-log set is described as `loom/runtime/*` "panic codes" while the group-B bullet says "every row", and `subagent-dispose-failure` is not a panic code.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `78a2f94` (broad group-B routing + panic-emission "aborted" content established on the relocated runtime-event-channel page), `7238428` (advisory-on-`Ok`-teardown semantics concretised on subagent.md / code-registry-runtime.md)
**History:** The corpus is git-tracked. The panic-emission `"loom /<name> aborted: <message>"` content for group-B diagnostic notes has been present since the spec was split into topic files (`f5e89f4`, 2026-06-04), and `78a2f94` (2026-06-04) relocated the runtime-event channel into its own page carrying the broad group-B membership "Runtime panics — every row of `loom/runtime/*` … except the five `console.error` codes." Independently, `7238428` (2026-06-06, "Teardown-side throw on the Ok path") sharpened the teardown diagnostics on subagent.md and code-registry-runtime.md to be explicitly *advisory only* — adding "on a normal-return teardown the parent still observes the loom's `Ok` final value … the disposal throw never promotes that `Ok` to an `Err`" for both `subagent-dispose-failure` and the teardown-side `internal-error`. That change reasoned about the *invocation result* (no masking of `Ok`) but not about the *operator-facing surfacing channel*, and never reconciled the advisory diagnostics against the broad group-B "aborted" framing on the runtime-event-channel page. Subsequent refinements of the group-A/B partition (`038dab3` "always-log set definition narrower than membership", `2e1888c` "Group B routing self-contradicts", `bf42043` qualifying the QueryError component, `d3b775e` correcting the `RuntimeEvent.kind` comment) repeatedly touched the partition without closing the advisory-teardown framing collision; the narrower "panic codes" phrasing in the always-log sentence versus the "every row" phrasing in the group-B bullet is a surviving residue of that incomplete reconciliation. The defect is the unresolved interaction between the two independently-reasonable rules rather than a single introducing edit.

## Solution approach

Carve `loom/runtime/subagent-dispose-failure` and the teardown-side `loom/runtime/internal-error` out of the panic-emission "aborted" framing in runtime-event-channel.md, keyed on diagnostic identity, in both the panic-emission rule under *Deduplication and lifetime rules* and the *Per-variant `display`/`content` pairings* matrix. Reconcile the always-log "panic codes" wording with the group-B "every row" wording so both agree these two codes are present-but-advisory rather than panics. State the resulting `display`/`content` disposition on the `loom/runtime/subagent-dispose-failure` and `loom/runtime/internal-error` rows in code-registry-runtime.md, and cross-reference the carve-out from PIC-9 (`#pic-9`) in subagent.md where the advisory teardown rule is stated.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: these two codes stay on the `loom-system-note` channel — do not move them into the five-code `console.error` exclusion list.
