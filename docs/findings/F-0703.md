## Finding

The `No parallel fan-out across prompt-mode .loom callees` bullet in `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md` is the longest item in the non-goals list and the bulk of its prose is verbatim or near-verbatim restatement of normative content owned by three other pages. It re-explains what "fan-out" means by quoting the `tool_loop` parenthetical "the runtime executing them all (in parallel where the provider supports parallel tool calls)" (twice ‚Äî once when introducing the term and again when carving out Pi-host tools); it asserts that prompt-mode `.loom` paths listed in another loom's `tools:` are rejected, which is the normative rule at `frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-a.md` (`loom/load/prompt-mode-callable`); and it pins "the per-sibling spawn shape ‚Äî own `AgentSession`, captured callable set, derived `AbortSignal`, private transcript" as the property that makes subagent-mode `.loom` callees the surviving concurrency surface, which is precisely the per-call spawn description normative at `tool-calls.md#concurrency` ("each invocation spawns its own `AgentSession` with its own captured callable set, its own derived `AbortSignal`, and its own private transcript").

The non-goal page's job is to record that loom 1.0 reserves no forward-compatibility seam for parallel fan-out across prompt-mode `.loom` callees, and to point at the normative owners of the rules that make that scope boundary fall where it does. Re-stating the owners' rules inline creates two divergence vectors: a future edit to the per-sibling spawn shape at `tool-calls.md#concurrency` or to the `loom/load/prompt-mode-callable` rule at `frontmatter-fields-a.md` will silently desync against this restatement, and a reader landing on either copy has no signal as to which is authoritative. The bullet also blurs the locus question the non-goals list is otherwise careful about (every other bullet on the page is one or two sentences plus a `Recorded at:` line).

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Trim the bullet to the non-goal statement plus citations, on the pattern the other non-goal bullets on the page already follow. Concretely:

- Keep the lead sentence ("No parallel fan-out across prompt-mode `.loom` callees") and one short sentence stating the scope decision: prompt-mode `.loom` callees are excluded from the parallel-fan-out surface, and lifting that exclusion is not anticipated by loom 1.0.
- Remove the in-bullet definition of "fan-out" (including both copies of the `tool_loop` parenthetical quote), the inline restatement of the prompt-mode-callable load-time rejection, and the four-tuple per-sibling spawn-shape clause. Each of those is owned at a page already cited and adds no scope-decision content.
- Keep the existing `Recorded at:` line pointing at `tool-calls.md#concurrency`, `frontmatter-fields-a.md#tools`, and `session-model-and-appendix.md#sm-7c-prompt-mode-sequential-execution`. Optionally move the SM-7c cross-link into the lead-paragraph cite-cluster so all three normative owners read uniformly.
- Preserve the bullet's anchor target if one is later added under the sibling finding *"non-goal bullets lack stable per-item anchors"* ‚Äî the slug should survive the trim.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:
- The two `tool_loop` quotes in the current bullet are the only places on the non-goals page that quote that clause; removing them is a pure deletion (no other bullet depends on the quote being present here).
- The bullet's lead sentence is the only place on the corpus that names "parallel fan-out across prompt-mode `.loom` callees" as a unitary scope item, so it must survive the trim verbatim or be paraphrased without losing the "prompt-mode `.loom` callees" qualifier ‚Äî a drift to "across `.loom` callees" would silently expand the non-goal to cover subagent-mode callees, contradicting `tool-calls.md#concurrency`.
- Do not touch `tool-calls.md#concurrency` or `frontmatter-fields-a.md#tools` ‚Äî the fix is one-sided, on the non-goals page only.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Two divergence vectors: an edit to the per-sibling spawn shape at `tool-calls.md#concurrency` (the four-tuple `AgentSession` / captured callable set / `AbortSignal` / private transcript) or to the `loom/load/prompt-mode-callable` rule will silently desync against this restatement, and a reader landing on either copy has no signal which is authoritative. No implementer is currently blocked ‚Äî the normative owners are easily reachable from the cited links ‚Äî but the page's contract (record the scope decision and cite its anchors) is undermined every time the bullet grows.
