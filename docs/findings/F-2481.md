## Problem

`diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md` (line 18) carries a normative ordering MUST: the renderer must be registered synchronously inside the extension factory **before** the first discovery scan kicks off, so the first batch of scan diagnostics renders through the loom renderer "rather than as raw fallback text." Three defects are bundled into the sentence. The rationale asserts a Pi host behaviour — that a `customType` with no registered renderer renders as raw fallback text — with no citation at the loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin and no version-bump checklist audit item, unlike every other pinned host behaviour (e.g. items (q) and (z)). The ordering MUST is mis-located away from its owner, the PIC §"Renderer registration" *Registration timing* bullet, which pins only the weaker "before the factory returns" rule; because the discovery scan runs at factory time (`registration-steps.md` step 1), a PIC reader can register after the scan yet before the factory returns, satisfying PIC while violating the diagnostics-shard ordering. The MUST also has no observable acceptance criterion — "raw fallback text" is undefined and there is no test anchor for the ordering.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commit:** `ac8c3af` — *pi-loom spec: resolve "LoadExtensionsResult.errors is the wrong delivery channel for loom diagnostics"*
**History:** The corpus is git-tracked. `git log -G "raw fallback"` and `git log -S "before the first discovery scan kicks off"` trace the renderer-registration sentence — including both the `before the first discovery scan` ordering MUST and the `rather than as raw fallback text` rationale — to a single `+26`-line edit to the then-monolithic `spec_topics/diagnostics.md` in `ac8c3af` (2026-05-04). That commit resolved an unrelated finding about the diagnostic *delivery channel* (`LoadExtensionsResult.errors`) and, in passing, authored the ordering rule and its uncited host-behaviour rationale directly in the diagnostics page rather than at the PIC renderer-registration ownership site. The text was subsequently carried verbatim through the `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04) size-cap shard split that moved `diagnostics.md` into `diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md`; no later commit added a citation, relocated the rule, or pinned an acceptance criterion. The defect has therefore been present since `ac8c3af` and is not present-since-inception.

## Solution approach

Move the before-the-scan ordering MUST from the `diagnostic-shape.md` renderer-registration sentence to its owner, the PIC §"Renderer registration" *Registration timing* bullet, strengthening that bullet to require registration before the factory-time discovery scan walk (`registration-steps.md` step 1); leave a forward-link in `diagnostic-shape.md` rather than restating the rule. Replace the undefined "raw fallback text" phrasing with the observable it stands for — first-scan diagnostics reaching the transcript without passing through the `loom-system-note` renderer. Cite the unregistered-`customType` host behaviour at the loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin as known-fragile evidence and add a version-bump editorial-review checklist item alongside (q)/(z), or reframe the rationale as a presupposition if the behaviour cannot be verified. Pin an observable acceptance criterion at the PIC site for the registration-before-first-scan ordering.
