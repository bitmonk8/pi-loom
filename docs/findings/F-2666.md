## Problem

PIC-52 bundles two distinct obligations under one undifferentiated MUST: an architectural prohibition against adding any sandbox / capability-filter / mediated-proxy channel in a 1.x release (verifiable only by architectural review, not at runtime), and a runtime-testable denial-routing observable. Because the two share a single anchor and modal, a test author cannot tell which clause is runtime-verifiable, and may either waste effort testing the unobservable prohibition or dismiss the whole rule — including the testable denial clause — as unverifiable. The denial clause also leaves its bound party implicit behind the passive "silent success on denial is forbidden."

## Solution approach

Split PIC-52 into two anchored obligations in `trust-boundary.md`: the design invariant (no interposed privilege layer; no additional access channels in 1.x; major-version concern), tagged as architectural-review-verified rather than runtime-testable; and the runtime-testable denial-routing observable, which names the loom runtime as the bound party and requires a thrown / `isError: true` host return to surface as `Err(QueryError { kind: "code_tool", cause: "execution", … })` and not be converted to `Ok`. Repoint `V14d` / `V14d-T` to the new denial-routing sub-anchor.
