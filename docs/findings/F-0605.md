## Finding

`audit-resolution.md` carries four ordering-relative pointers ("the *Malformed-marker discriminator* below" — twice, on the *Minimum justification* and *Stale-marker discriminator* paragraphs; "the (s2) sub-kind below" on *Inventory-first resolution*; and "the malformed-marker dual-emission rule below" on *Multi-line surface placement*) whose targets all live in `audit-failures.md`, a separate page. The "below" wording presupposes intra-file order, so each pointer dangles: a reader of `audit-resolution.md` who follows them finds nothing further on the page. The relationship is also structural rather than incidental — the *Malformed-marker discriminator* enumerates clauses (a)–(g) that the *Separator tolerance*, *Minimum justification*, *Citation-token grammar*, *Stale-marker discriminator*, and *Multi-line surface placement* paragraphs all hand off to ("fails the audit as *malformed* per the discriminator below", "fails malformed-marker clause (e)"), and the *Stale sub-kinds* sub-bullet on `audit-failures.md` reciprocally references the *Marker scope* / *Multi-line surface placement* paragraphs on `audit-resolution.md` as "above".

The same defect affects the analogous pointers in the other direction (`audit-failures.md` says "*Stale sub-kinds*…per *Marker scope* / *Multi-line surface placement* above"; "malformed-marker clauses (a)–(g) per *Malformed-marker discriminator* above"), which dangle symmetrically once the reader is on `audit-failures.md`. All of these are stranded by the loom 1.0 size-cap split, not by anything the original author intended.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** `f5e89f4` — *docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set* (2026-06-04)
**History:** `git log -S 'malformed-marker discriminator below'` and `git log -S 'Malformed-marker discriminator'` show the *Malformed-marker discriminator*, *Stale-marker discriminator*, *Minimum justification*, *Inventory-first resolution*, and *Multi-line surface placement* paragraphs all entered the corpus in a single edit (`e5cd514`, 2026-05-09, *"resolve T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit"*) inside the then-single `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` file, where every "below" pointer resolved intra-file. The 576KB-to-30-children size-cap split in `f5e89f4` placed *Malformed-marker discriminator* on the new `audit-failures.md` while leaving the *Minimum justification* / *Citation-token grammar* / *Stale-marker discriminator* / *Inventory-first resolution* / *Multi-line surface placement* paragraphs on `audit-resolution.md`, stranding the four pointers as cross-file "below" references that no longer compose. The commit message describes its rewrite as "1571 cross-references repointed across docs/" and "no content edits", confirming that the splitter was wired to fix `[link](path#anchor)` Markdown references but had no signal for naked prose pointers like "the *X* below". A `git blame` on the offending paragraphs in today's `audit-resolution.md` confirms the four "below" tokens are byte-for-byte the same strings the splitter inherited from `e5cd514`.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Convert the cross-file pointers to inter-file links

**Approach.** Leave the paragraph layout the size-cap split produced and edit only the broken pointers. On `audit-resolution.md`, replace the four "*X* below" / "below" tokens with explicit `[*Malformed-marker discriminator*](./audit-failures.md#audit-malformed-marker-discriminator)` / `[(s2) sub-kind](./audit-failures.md#audit-stale-sub-kinds)` / `[malformed-marker dual-emission rule](./audit-failures.md#audit-malformed-marker-discriminator)` links. Symmetrically, on `audit-failures.md`, replace "per *Marker scope* / *Multi-line surface placement* above" and "per *Malformed-marker discriminator* above" with the matching back-links into `audit-resolution.md`. Add a new `<a id="audit-malformed-marker-discriminator"></a>` anchor on `audit-failures.md` `*Malformed-marker discriminator*` (the only target without an explicit anchor today) and matching `<a id="audit-stale-sub-kinds"></a>` if needed.

**Spec edits.** ~6 prose substitutions across `audit-resolution.md`; ~4 across `audit-failures.md`; add 1–2 `<a id>` anchors.

**Pros.** Tiny diff; preserves the f5e89f4 size cap; does not perturb `V18b`/`V18b-T`'s **Spec** anchor citations; no risk of breaking the existing `coverage-matrix.md` row `cka-20` (which already enumerates both files).

**Cons.** Doesn't address the underlying structural coupling — *Stale-marker discriminator* will still depend on a definition that lives one file over.

**Risks.** None material; ordinary editorial change.

### Option B — Move *Malformed-marker discriminator* into `audit-resolution.md`

**Approach.** Lift the *Malformed-marker discriminator* paragraph (and its `*Stale sub-kinds*`, *Substitution limitation*, *Stale-citation limitation* dependents, if their cohesion warrants) out of `audit-failures.md` and re-seat them under the *Exemption mechanism* section of `audit-resolution.md`, immediately after the *Citation-token grammar* paragraph and before the *Stale-marker discriminator* paragraph. Restores intra-file "below" semantics for the four pointers. Leaves the *Failure-surface contract* and family→branch routing table on `audit-failures.md`.

**Spec edits.** Move ~1 (or ~4) paragraphs across files. Re-check `audit-resolution.md` against the 30KB recommended cap (currently 28.9KB; the malformed-marker paragraph alone is ~5KB, so the move pushes the page to ~34KB and breaches the cap). The plan's `V18b`/`V18b-T` **Spec** lists already cite both files, so no plan-side citation churn.

**Pros.** Collocates the exemption-marker grammar with its dependents, as the original finding suggests.

**Cons.** Breaches the 30KB recommended cap that motivated the f5e89f4 split in the first place; if *Stale sub-kinds* moves too, the breach widens; either re-splitting (a third file) or moving an unrelated block off `audit-resolution.md` becomes necessary.

**Risks.** Page-size regression against the documented soft cap; risk of cascading re-splits that re-introduce the same class of cross-file pointer elsewhere.

### Recommendation

Option A. The broken pointers are an editorial residue of the size-cap split, not a sign that the split was wrong; an in-place inter-file link discharges the structural-coupling concern without re-breaching the cap that the split was performed to enforce. Implementer watch-points: (1) add the explicit anchor on `audit-failures.md`'s *Malformed-marker discriminator* paragraph (and on `*Stale sub-kinds*` if back-linked) before referencing it; (2) check that every remaining "above"/"below" token in `audit-resolution.md` and `audit-failures.md` still resolves intra-file after the edit — a follow-up grep for `\b(above|below)\b` in both files catches any further f5e89f4 residue this finding does not name; (3) run `docs/_tools/check_links.py` to confirm the new anchors resolve.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A leaf author can still locate the *Malformed-marker discriminator* clauses (a)–(g) by grep across the `audit-*.md` pages, so `V18b`'s per-`<symptom>` fixture floor is reachable. But the audit's per-discriminator routability obligation is built out of these cross-paragraph references — a reader who takes "the discriminator below" at face value misses the clause they're being routed to, and the structural dependency the original finding flags (the stale-marker rule needs the malformed-marker grammar) becomes invisible at the page boundary.
