## Problem

In `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/`, the two audit pages have basenames that contradict their H1 headings and bodies: `audit-recognised-shapes.md` carries H1 `# Audit target categories` and the *Target surface categories* body, while `audit-target-categories.md` carries H1 `# Audit recognised shapes`, the *Recognised import/access shapes* body, and the `audit-recognised-shapes` anchor. Filename-based navigation (git history, grep, IDE jump-to-file, and every URL-bearing cross-reference) therefore lands on a page whose H1 advertises the opposite topic, and the intuitive link `audit-recognised-shapes.md#audit-recognised-shapes` does not resolve because that anchor lives in the sibling file. The parent index `pi-integration-contract.md` masks the inversion by pairing H1-matching link text with filename-matching URLs.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**

- `f5e89f4` — *docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set* (Thomas Andersen, 2026-06-04). Split the pi-integration-contract monolith via `docs/_tools/split_spec.py`. The `PLANS` table allocates source-line 826 to slug `audit-recognised-shapes` and source-line 827 to slug `audit-target-categories`, but the content at those line positions in the pre-split monolith was the inverse: line 826 was the *Target surface categories* paragraph and line 827 was the *Recognised import/access shapes* paragraph. The script's `title_from_slug` synthesised each child's H1 from its filename, so on commit the H1s matched the filenames but neither matched the content the child page actually contained.
- `7e3e94e` — *pi-loom spec: resolve "audit-cluster files have H1 headings inverted"* (Thomas Andersen, 2026-06-05). A targeted fix that swapped the two H1 headings (and the link-text on the parent index) so each page's H1 now matched its body — but left the basenames, the surviving `<a id="audit-recognised-shapes">` anchor, and every URL-bearing cross-reference pointing at the names from the split. The fix resolved the heading↔body half of the inversion and re-pointed the heading↔filename half into the present state.

**History:** `git log --follow` shows both files first appear in `f5e89f4` (the split) and the only subsequent rewrite touching either H1 is `7e3e94e`. The defect is not isolable to either commit alone: `f5e89f4` introduced the slug↔content mismatch from the wrong line-range table; `7e3e94e` chose the swap-headings arm over the rename-files arm, locking the slug↔H1 inversion into the corpus. The durable fix addresses that arm choice rather than reverting either commit.

## Solution approach

Realign each file's H1 and body to its existing basename rather than renaming the files. Move the *Recognised import/access shapes* paragraph (anchor `audit-recognised-shapes`) into `audit-recognised-shapes.md` under H1 `# Audit recognised shapes`, and move the *Target surface categories* paragraph (anchors `audit-target-surface-categories` and `strict-capability-absence-pin`) into `audit-target-categories.md` under H1 `# Audit target categories`. Update the parent index `pi-integration-contract.md` so each audit-page link's text and URL agree, and re-point the now-cross-file forward reference inside the *Recognised import/access shapes* paragraph.

## Solution constraints

- Relocate each paragraph body together with its `<a id>` anchors verbatim; do not edit the substantive audit-category / recognised-shape / capability-probe prose, which is owned by the sibling findings named in Relationships.
