## Problem

The category-(1) and category-(3) carrier-definition clauses in `audit-target-categories.md` §*Target surface categories* (anchor `audit-target-surface-categories`) contain a self-contradictory parenthetical: the canonical carrier annotation is defined as the literal `ExtensionAPI` / `ExtensionContext` / `ExtensionCommandContext` "qualified or unqualified, including `import type` re-imports, but resolved against the textual annotation rather than against an inferred type". "Qualified or unqualified" admits a namespace-qualified form such as `pi: someNS.ExtensionAPI` as a carrier, but the textual-annotation clause — the operative reading, since the rule exists to let a static-AST walker decide carrier status without a TypeScript-program load — matches only the bare literal token and excludes that form. The two clauses cannot both hold, and a namespace-qualified form would simultaneously be admitted here and forbidden by the off-canonical-annotation prohibition in `audit-recognised-shapes.md`. Because carrier detection is the root predicate of every downstream category-(1)/(3) decision, two implementers partition the same source tree differently, breaking the surface-set-closure guarantee the audit exists to provide.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** `e5cd514` (2026-05-09) `pi-loom spec: resolve "T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit"`
**History:** `git log -G "qualified or unqualified" -- docs/**.md` lists `e5cd514` as the earliest commit introducing the parenthetical, authored into `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` (the pre-split monolith). The text was carried unchanged through `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04, the 100KB-cap split into `pi-integration-contract/` subdirectory pages, which moved the clause into `audit-target-categories.md` verbatim) and was untouched by the subsequent realignment commits `23d28a1`, `81b0e67`, `d8d5ad0`, and `7e3e94e`. The textual-annotation clause and the "qualified or unqualified" parenthetical were authored together in the same paragraph in `e5cd514` — the contradiction has been present in every form of the carrier rule since the inventory-closure-audit obligation was first pinned. No later commit narrowed, widened, or re-justified the parenthetical; the downstream bare-literal prohibitions in `audit-recognised-shapes.md` were authored in the same commit and have been mutually inconsistent with it from the outset.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the category-(1) and category-(3) carrier-annotation parentheticals in `audit-target-categories.md` §`audit-target-surface-categories` to drop "qualified or unqualified, including `import type` re-imports" and state that the carrier annotation matches only the bare literal token (`ExtensionAPI` for category (1); `ExtensionContext` / `ExtensionCommandContext` for category (3)), resolved against the textual annotation. Preserve the unrenamed `import type { ExtensionAPI } from "@earendil-works/pi-coding-agent"` case as a carrier, since its parameter-position annotation remains the bare literal. Align the carrier rule to the existing bare-literal off-canonical-annotation prohibition in `audit-recognised-shapes.md` rather than restating it.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: `audit-recognised-shapes.md` — its bare-literal off-canonical-annotation prohibition is already correct and is the consistency anchor this edit aligns to.
