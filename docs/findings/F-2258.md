## Problem

In version-bump checklist item (e) (`id="bump-checklist-slash-dispatch-serialisation"`), the recovery-serialisation clause reads "the bump **MAY merge only when** that second-pass outcome is `pass` under the pass-evidence rule above." This combines an RFC-2119 permission modal (`MAY`) with a restrictive qualifier (`only when`), which admits two readings: merging is permitted-but-not-required once the outcome is `pass`, or merging is prohibited until it is. The trailing sentence ("does not clear the merge gate") signals the prohibitive reading, but the load-bearing clause itself does not say so, so a contributor extracting the rule in isolation can land on the permissive reading. Because this gate is self-enforced under editorial review rather than by `npm test`, the wording is the only thing preventing a contributor from merging a bump whose recovery serialisation never re-established the precondition (b) non-overlap property.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commit:** `c0259ca` — "pi-loom spec: resolve 'Version-bump procedure: four obligations have no verifiable acceptance criterion'" (2026-06-07)

That commit created item (e) together with its second-pass acceptance criterion and the "the bump MAY merge only when … does not clear the merge gate" phrasing; the clause was ambiguous from its first appearance. Subsequent edits to item (e) (e.g. the recovery-mutex → recovery-serialisation rewording) carried the modal phrasing through verbatim without revisiting it.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the merge-gate clause in item (e) (`id="bump-checklist-slash-dispatch-serialisation"`) to state the prohibition directly: replace the permissive "the bump **MAY merge only when** that second-pass outcome is `pass`" with a prohibitive "the bump **MUST NOT** be merged until that second-pass outcome is `pass`", retaining the existing reference to the pass-evidence rule.

## Solution constraints

- Clarity-only: the merge gate's normative semantics MUST NOT change — the bump remains prohibited from merging until the second-pass outcome is `pass`.
