## Problem

The *Category-(1)/(3) inventory join key (per-category specialisation)* rule at `#audit-category-1-3-inventory-join-key` in `audit-resolution.md` resolves a member access by exact match between the accessed `<member>` and the rightmost dot-separated segment of *any* `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` entry's `path` field, never constraining the matched entry's carrier (category-(1) `pi` vs category-(3) `ctx`/`ExtensionContext`/`ExtensionCommandContext`) to agree with the access's carrier. A `pi.modelRegistry` access therefore resolves against a category-(3) entry `path: "ctx.modelRegistry"` (and symmetrically), and nothing bounds the matched entry to category-(1)/(3) kinds, so a colliding category-(2) entry can also absorb the access. This is a soundness hole: an off-inventory `pi.<member>` surface with no category-(1) entry is silently authorised by an unrelated entry sharing a member name, defeating the surface-set-closure guarantee. The companion `literal-read/duplicate-rightmost-segment` uniqueness rule (at `#audit-category-2-inventory-join-key`) compounds this by never stating whether its uniqueness is scoped per-carrier or jointly across categories (1) and (3).

Subsequent edits to the paragraph (`f5e89f4` 100KB-cap shard split, `81bf52a` `spec/` → `spec_topics/` collapse, `23d28a1` F-0958 basename realignment, `5e73072` npm scope rename, `9e53613` H1/plan-leaf-ID corpus sweep, `6829a79` Pi-SDK assumption cluster) preserved the unscoped wording across every relocation. The defect is structural to the paragraph as first written: the per-category framing is stated in the heading and in the reasoning about entry shapes, but the `kind` filter is never bolted onto either the match rule or the duplicate-segment uniqueness rule.

## Solution approach

Clarify the category-(1)/(3) rightmost-segment match at `#audit-category-1-3-inventory-join-key` so it resolves an access only against an entry whose carrier agrees with the access's carrier, and state explicitly that category-(2) entries never participate in rightmost-segment matching. Clarify the `literal-read/duplicate-rightmost-segment` uniqueness rule at `#audit-category-2-inventory-join-key` as per-carrier, so two entries collide only when they share both carrier and rightmost segment.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the category-(2) leftmost-segment named-import resolution semantics at `#audit-category-2-inventory-join-key`; only the rightmost-segment uniqueness scoping for categories (1)/(3) may change there.
