## Problem

`V9b` bundles three independently-scoped deliverables in one leaf: registration steps 1–5, the `ReloadFailureInjector` test-seam declaration plus two of its reload-injection arms, and the model-reference-matcher production wiring that injects loom's exact-match resolver into the parser seam `V6a` defines. The registration and injector deliverables are genuinely coupled, but the matcher-production wiring shares nothing with them beyond running at the load pass — it carries its own test bullet and is the sole reason `V6a` appears in `V9b.Deps`. A reviewer must evaluate three unrelated mechanisms across three spec pages in one pass, and the matcher obligation is the most likely to be missed because the leaf's title and bulk are about registration.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `d64dce5` (2026-06-11, "resolve \"Watcher-time reload failure-injection seam under-specified and ungated\""); `5953b18` (2026-06-11, "resolve \"V6a model-reference-matcher seam has no declared producer\"")
**History:** Registration steps 1–5 were present in `V9b` from the leaf's inception (`c6a664e`, the initial plan build). The leaf grew to three deliverables through two later, independent fixes that each parked a new obligation on it: `d64dce5` added the `ReloadFailureInjector` declaration site and reload-injection arms (it is the first revision of `V9b` containing `ReloadFailureInjector`, per `git log -S 'ReloadFailureInjector' -- 'docs/plan_topics/V9b*.md'`), and `5953b18` added the model-reference-matcher production-wiring point (first revision containing `model-reference-matcher production wiring point`, per `git log -S`). Neither commit intended over-bundling; each chose `V9b` as the natural owner (the watcher/registration leaf for the injector, the load-pass leaf for the matcher producer). The over-bundling is the joint product of the two. A related coverage-row variant of this concern was raised earlier as `F-0318` and resolved by `c1fa860` with per-facet sub-IDs on the `cka-23` row rather than a leaf split, so the three-deliverable size persisted.

## Solution approach

Split `V9b` by extracting the model-reference-matcher production-wiring deliverable into a new paired leaf (`<new>`/`<new>-T`) under `docs/plan_topics/`, leaving the coupled registration + injector deliverables in `V9b`. Strike the matcher prose, the matcher **Tests** bullet, and the matcher **Ships when** clause from `V9b-registration-reload-wiring.md` and `V9b-T-registration-reload-wiring.md`, and remove the now-unneeded `V6a` edge from `V9b.Deps`. Retarget the cross-resolution **Deps** edge and bullet in `V11a-binder-model-resolution.md`/`V11a-T-binder-model-resolution.md` from `V9b` to `<new>`, update the producer pointer in `V6a-frontmatter-contract.md` to name `<new>`, and link `<new>` into the V9 slice listing in `docs/plan.md`.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the `cka-23` (registration) and `cka-37` (`model:` resolution) coverage-matrix rows stay mapped to `V9b` and `V6a` respectively — the matcher wiring is a production point witnessed through those rows, not a coverage row of its own, so `coverage-matrix.md` needs no closing-leaf change.
- The spec is read-only for this fix.
- Allocate the new leaf's ID per the plan's `V<N><letter>` scheme (next free letter in the target phase); do not mint a final ID arbitrarily or reuse a retired ID.
