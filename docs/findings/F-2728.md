## Problem

The *Sequential by default* rule (`conventions.md` §Cross-cutting rules) permits a Promise combinator in `src/**` only when the `// allow:` token resolves to a matrix REQ-ID / `cka-<n>` row (requirement a) **and** the cited obligation's normative text actually mandates concurrency at the operation the combinator implements (requirement b). Its two mechanical surfaces — the `H2a` `no-restricted-syntax` allow-list and the `H5a` closing-gate token-resolution arm — verify only requirement (a); neither inspects the cited obligation's prose, so a leaf can cite any resolvable token, including one unrelated to concurrency, and pass CI. The rule's **Enforcement posture** concedes only the blocking-runtime residue (residue-inspection item 3) and is silent on the combinator requirement-(b) gap, while the *Doc updates* grouping lists *Sequential by default* among rules "backed by a mechanical surface" — overstating what the surface verifies. The sibling *Specific exception types only* (broad-catch) rule discloses the identical requirement-(a)-only split and routes its requirement-(b) residue to self-review and residue-inspection item 6; the combinator arm has no such owner.

**History:** The requirement-(b) wording and the token-only gate have been present since the rule's inception. The defect is not a single regression but an emergent inconsistency: the sibling broad-catch (`6ad39e0`) and blocking-runtime (`c33d309`) rules each grew an explicit requirement-(b) / residue concession, while the Promise-combinator arm of *Sequential by default* never did — even as the *Doc updates* grouping (`c06749d`) continued to assert mechanical backing and the carve-out was further refined (`d31eeef`) without closing the gap.

## Solution approach

Mirror the broad-catch enforcement-posture treatment. In the *Sequential by default* **Enforcement posture** (`conventions.md` §Cross-cutting rules), add a concession that the `H2a` `no-restricted-syntax` allow-list and the `H5a` closing-gate token-resolution arm verify only requirement (a) and do not verify that the cited obligation mandates concurrency at the operation the combinator implements, and route that requirement-(b) residue to the *Per-phase TDD ritual* self-review step and to a new release-time residue-inspection item. Add that item to `conventions.md` §Loom 1.0 release-time residue inspection (parallel to the broad-catch item 6), charging a reviewer to confirm each combinator `// allow:` site's cited obligation mandates concurrency at the operation, with a corresponding **Clean when** condition. Update the "nine-item manual sweep" count in `docs/plan_topics/H6a-live-corpus-activation.md` Adds. to match the new item total.

## Solution constraints

- The spec is read-only for this fix: requirement (b) is evaluated against existing spec obligation text, and no spec edit is needed.
- The *Doc updates* grouping may keep listing *Sequential by default* among the mechanically-backed rules — out of scope to delist it, since disclosing and routing the requirement-(b) residue removes the overclaim.
