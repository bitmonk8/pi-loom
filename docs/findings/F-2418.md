## Problem

The inventory-closure audit's four-field violation record (`path`, `line`, `symbol`, `proposed-resolution`), pinned in the *Per-family record-shape table* and the *Wire serialisation* clause, claims byte-identical output across hosts that a CI parser may rely on, but leaves four field-level encoding questions unanswered. (1) The *Multi-line surface placement* rule defines an *originating line* for marker placement, but no clause states normatively that the emitted `line` field equals it, so an implementation could emit line 1 of any multi-line span and still satisfy every record obligation. (2) No clause states whether `line` is 0-based or 1-based — conspicuous because the *Source-file encoding* decode-error offset is explicitly 0-based. (3) The emitted `path` field's normal form (relative/absolute, separator, canonicalisation) is unpinned, and the rightmost-`:` split rule anticipates host-dependent Windows drive-prefix paths, directly undercutting the byte-identical determinism claim. (4) The mandated zero-based decode byte-offset has no home among the four parse-failure record fields.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `e5cd514` (2026-05-09, `pi-loom spec: resolve "T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit"`); `6829a79` (2026-06-06, `pi-loom spec: resolve "Pi-SDK hidden-assumption cluster (strictCapable absence, decode steps, pi.on origin)"`)
**History:** The audit apparatus — the four-field record, the *Per-family record-shape table*, the *Wire serialisation* `path:line` packing, and the *Multi-line surface placement* originating-line rule — was introduced whole in `e5cd514`, authored into `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md`. From that inception the `line` field's multi-line value, its numbering base, and the emitted `path` normal form were left unspecified; those three facets are present-since the apparatus's inception. The fourth facet (and the "conspicuous 0-based gap" framing of the second) arose from interaction with `6829a79`, which later added the *Source-file encoding* decode rule mandating a `zero-based byte offset` for the first invalid byte sequence: that commit pinned a 0-based positional value the four-field record has no field to carry and against which the record's own silent `line` base became conspicuous. A subsequent reorganisation (`f5e89f4`, 2026-06-04, the 100 KB / 30 KB shard split) relocated the apparatus verbatim from `pi-integration-contract.md` into the `audit-*.md` topic pages without touching any of the four gaps; it is not an introducing commit. `git log --follow`/`-S` over `four fixed fields`, `path:line`, `Multi-line surface placement`, and `zero-based byte offset` confirm this two-commit attribution; the corpus is git-tracked throughout.

## Solution approach

Add normative pins at `id="audit-failure-surface-contract"` (or the *Per-family record-shape table* preamble) fixing the emitted `line` field to the *originating line* defined by *Multi-line surface placement*, and state the `line` numbering base explicitly so it is distinct from the 0-based byte-offset rule. Pin the emitted `path` field to a host-independent canonical form so the byte-identical-output determinism claim holds across hosts. Give the mandated zero-based decode byte-offset (the *Source-file encoding* rule) a defined carrier field in the `id="audit-infrastructure-failure-handling"` parse-failure record, stating which field and its encoding.

## Solution constraints

- Any carrier token added for the decode byte-offset MUST stay within the no-tab / printable-ASCII constraints the *Wire serialisation* clause already imposes on record fields.
