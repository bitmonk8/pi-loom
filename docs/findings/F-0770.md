## Problem

The closing sentence of **System notes** in `runtime-event-channel.md` splices a present-tense obligation ("Implementers must guard against re-entry") onto a conditional whose antecedent is a hypothetical future edit ("if a future `loom/runtime/*` handler ever routes diagnostics back through `loom-system-note`…"). Two readings result: (a) the re-entry guard is required now regardless of present handlers; (b) the guard is deferred until some future handler starts routing diagnostics back, so loom 1.0 implementations may omit it. Reading (b) is not vacuous: step 2 of the fallback emits a `loom/runtime/system-note-delivery-failed` diagnostic through the standard diagnostics channel, which is itself the `loom-system-note` envelope, so a live re-entry path into `pi.sendMessage` already exists today. An implementer who reads (b) can ship a step-2 emission that re-enters `pi.sendMessage` and recurse-loops on the first synchronous `pi.sendMessage` throw — the exact loop the rule exists to prevent — with no observable conformance signal.

The temporal-scope ambiguity therefore originated with the first author of the fallback chain and has survived every later edit untouched.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the closing re-entry-guard sentence of **System notes** so the obligation reads as an unconditional present-tense MUST that binds loom 1.0 implementations now, and demote the hypothetical-future-handler framing to non-normative motivation rather than the antecedent that gates the guard. Ground the present-day re-entry path (step 2's standard-diagnostics-channel emission ships in a `loom-system-note` envelope) so the guard's current relevance is unambiguous.
