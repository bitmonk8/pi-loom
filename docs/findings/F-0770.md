## Finding

The closing paragraph of **System notes** (runtime-event-channel.md) ends with:

> Implementers must guard against re-entry: if a future `loom/runtime/*` handler ever routes diagnostics back through `loom-system-note`, the diagnostic step in this fallback MUST NOT re-invoke `pi.sendMessage`.

The wording splices a present-tense obligation ("Implementers must guard against re-entry") onto a conditional whose antecedent is a hypothetical *future* edit ("if a future handler ever routes…"). Two natural readings result: (a) a current MUST — implementations must wire the re-entry guard now, regardless of whether any present handler exhibits the routing pattern; or (b) a deferred MUST — the guard is required only once some future `loom/runtime/*` handler starts routing diagnostics through `loom-system-note`, and loom 1.0 implementations may omit it.

Reading (b) is not safely vacuous. Step 2 of the fallback chain emits a `loom/runtime/system-note-delivery-failed` diagnostic "through the standard diagnostics channel (see [Diagnostics](../diagnostics.md))," and that standard channel is itself the `loom-system-note` envelope (per `diagnostics/code-registry-runtime.md`: `system-note-delivery-failed` is "emitted by the system-note fallback path", and `diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md`: the renderer "formats these into transcript-persistent, dim-styled notes"). The diagnostic step therefore *already* has a viable path back into `pi.sendMessage` today — not merely a hypothetical future one — and an implementer who reads (b) and concludes "no live handler routes diagnostics back, so no guard yet" can ship a fallback whose step 2 re-enters `pi.sendMessage`, exactly the loop the rule was meant to prevent. Conformance for the `loom/runtime/system-note-delivery-failed` test in plan leaf `V7d` (which asserts the fallback "continues without aborting the slash-command handler or spawned subagent session") is undecidable under reading (b): two implementers can both pass the test today while disagreeing on whether the guard is normatively required.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace the closing sentence of **System notes** so the obligation is unambiguously present-tense, and demote the hypothetical-future framing to a non-normative note that motivates the rule rather than gating it.

Concretely, in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/runtime-event-channel.md`, change

> Implementers must guard against re-entry: if a future `loom/runtime/*` handler ever routes diagnostics back through `loom-system-note`, the diagnostic step in this fallback MUST NOT re-invoke `pi.sendMessage`.

to

> The diagnostic step in this fallback MUST NOT re-invoke `pi.sendMessage`; implementations MUST guard against re-entry at this site. *(Non-normative note: the present-day path to re-entry is step 2's standard-diagnostics-channel emission — `loom/runtime/system-note-delivery-failed` ships in a `loom-system-note` envelope per [Diagnostics](../diagnostics.md), so a step-2 implementation that delegates to the same `pi.sendMessage` call as the originating note would loop; the guard also forecloses any future `loom/runtime/*` handler that adds another routing back through this channel.)*

Implementer-relevant edge cases:

- The guard applies to the *step-2 diagnostic emission* only; step 1 (`ctx.ui.notify`) is already a different SDK surface and is governed by its own wrap-and-fall-through rule.
- The guard does not prohibit the diagnostic from being delivered via the `loom-system-note` *envelope shape* (the diagnostic-shape rendering is unchanged); it prohibits routing that emission through the same `pi.sendMessage` call object whose synchronous throw triggered the fallback. A separate write to the underlying transcript-persistence surface that bypasses `pi.sendMessage` (or terminates at `console.error` when no such bypass exists) is what the rule contemplates.
- Plan leaves `V7d-T` and `V7d` should grow a fixture asserting that under a synchronous `pi.sendMessage` throw, step 2's diagnostic emission does not produce a second `pi.sendMessage` invocation — this is the observable witness for the MUST.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Reading (b) is reachable today: step 2 of the fallback emits through the standard diagnostics channel, which is `loom-system-note`. An implementer who treats the guard as deferred can wire step 2 to `pi.sendMessage`, producing an unbounded recurse-throw loop the first time `pi.sendMessage` synchronously throws (the precise scenario the fallback exists to handle). Two conforming implementers can therefore diverge on whether the system-note delivery path is loop-safe, with no observable conformance signal until production hits a sendMessage throw.
