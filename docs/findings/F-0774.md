## Finding

The runtime-event channel pins a consumer-side deduplication tuple `(kind, query_site, message, occurred_at)` and asserts that "Two emissions from the same `query_site` with the same `kind` and `message` but distinct `occurred_at` values represent two distinct occurrences." That sentence is one-directional: distinct timestamps ⇒ distinct occurrences. The converse — equal timestamps ⇒ same occurrence — is what consumers actually rely on when they apply the tuple, and the spec never states the clock-resolution premise that would make the converse hold.

`occurred_at` is stamped via `Clock.wallNow()`, whose normative contract (declared at [`host-interfaces-services.md` *`Clock` / `FakeClock` interface*](./host-interfaces-services.md#clock--fakeclock-interface) and wired through `Date.now()` in the `WallClock` adapter) is Unix epoch *milliseconds*. Two genuinely-distinct same-site occurrences that fall inside one `wallNow()` tick (the same millisecond at best; potentially coarser if a future host backs `wallNow()` with a lower-resolution source) therefore receive byte-identical dedup tuples and collapse under any consumer that honours the suggested dedup. Because the cascade-twin re-emission rule deliberately re-uses the originating instance verbatim, the collapse case is observationally indistinguishable from the cascade-twin case the dedup tuple was designed to fold — there is no way for a consumer to tell them apart.

The spec already labels the consumer-side dedup as "a non-normative consumer concern and not part of loom 1.0 conformance," so the right fix is to acknowledge the collision case on the same footing rather than tighten the clock contract: state that the dedup tuple may collapse distinct same-site occurrences that fall inside one `wallNow()` tick, and that this collapse is acceptable under the non-normative framing.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `bc50e69` (H2 Clock seam — watcher debounce, scanPackagesTimeoutMs, RuntimeEvent occurred_at, 2026-05-05) and `9183961` (RuntimeEvent.occurred_at source clock contradicts the Clock.now() monotonic pin, 2026-06-04)
**History:** `git log -G "kind, query_site, message, occurred_at" -- docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/` shows the dedup tuple first appearing in `bc50e69`, which simultaneously (a) introduced the `occurred_at` field on `RuntimeEvent` stamped via `Clock.now()`, (b) added the dedup tuple `(kind, query_site, message, occurred_at)` to the *Deduplication and lifetime rules* paragraph, and (c) added the asymmetric "distinct `occurred_at` values represent two distinct occurrences" sentence — but stated no resolution premise for the converse direction. The asymmetry was latent at this point because `Clock.now()` was monotonic and the resolution was unpinned. `9183961` later split the seam into `now()` (monotonic, deadline math) and `wallNow()` (Unix epoch ms, `occurred_at`-only) and re-routed the `occurred_at` stamp to `wallNow()`; that re-routing concretised the resolution to "epoch ms" and made the collision case empirically reachable. Neither commit added the converse-direction acknowledgement. The defect is therefore the *interaction* between the dedup tuple authored in `bc50e69` and the wall-clock-stamping rule finalised in `9183961`, not a fault in either commit alone.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/runtime-event-channel.md`, extend the sentence that introduces the consumer dedup tuple under *Deduplication and lifetime rules* with a clock-resolution clause. Concretely, change:

> Consumers that aggregate the event stream may deduplicate on `(kind, query_site, message, occurred_at)` to collapse the cascade twin; this is a non-normative consumer concern and not part of loom 1.0 conformance.

to add a following sentence:

> Because `occurred_at` is stamped via `Clock.wallNow()` (Unix epoch milliseconds), two genuinely-distinct same-site occurrences that fall inside one `wallNow()` tick will carry identical dedup tuples and collapse under this aggregation. This collapse is an accepted consequence of the non-normative dedup rule above; consumers that need to distinguish such bursts must use a side channel (e.g. an event-stream sequence number they assign on receipt) rather than `occurred_at` alone.

This phrasing keeps the dedup rule's non-normative framing intact, avoids tightening `Clock.wallNow()`'s contract (and so does not touch `host-interfaces-services.md` or `V8d`), and explicitly disposes of the converse direction the existing "distinct `occurred_at` values represent two distinct occurrences" sentence left unstated. Implementer edge case to watch: the sentence must sit inside the bullet that already labels the dedup as a non-normative consumer concern, not in the surrounding paragraph that carries the normative cascade-twin verbatim-copy obligation — placement outside that bullet risks promoting the collision-acknowledgement to a normative claim about runtime emission, which would conflict with the *exactly once per occurrence* origin-emission rule.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A conforming runtime and a conforming consumer can disagree about whether two rapid same-site emissions are "the same occurrence" without either being wrong, because the spec leaves the converse direction of the dedup tuple unstated. No conformance failure results — the consumer dedup is explicitly non-normative — but operator-facing aggregators that count occurrences may under-count rapid bursts with no in-spec acknowledgement that the under-count is expected behaviour.
