## Problem

In the **Unknown-reason rule** paragraph (`#unknown-reason-rule`), the sentence pinning the `details.observed` coercion — `Pin the coercion to String(event.reason)...` — opens with a bare editorial imperative and carries no RFC-2119 modal, while the sibling coercion obligations in the same paragraph all use MUST. A reader applying the spec's modal vocabulary cannot tell whether using a template-literal coercion (`` `${event.reason}` ``) instead of `String(event.reason)` is non-conformant or merely discouraged. The sentence names a real defect the choice prevents — a template-literal coercion re-introduces the `Symbol`-coercion `TypeError` that `String()` is chosen to avoid — so the obligation is meant to bind, but the wording fails to say so.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 6cd6c56 — pi-loom spec: resolve "details.event.reason coercion is unspecified for non-string Pi values" (2026-05-07, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The "Pin the coercion to `String(event.reason)` explicitly …" sentence was added to `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` in commit 6cd6c56 as part of resolving a prior coercion-shape review item; the same edit introduced sibling clauses that already used MUST, but the new sentence was written as a bare editorial imperative. The sentence has been carried through subsequent edits (the `f5e89f4` corpus split that moved it into `unknown-reason-rule.md`, and the later `aa2f9d7` / `c326bfa` / `a7d2a80` edits to surrounding clauses) without its modal being normalised.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the `Pin the coercion to String(event.reason)...` sentence so the `details.observed` coercion obligation is stated with a MUST modal, aligning it with the sibling coercion MUSTs already in the paragraph. Preserve the rationale clause that names the template-literal `Symbol`-coercion `TypeError` the obligation prevents.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the symmetric template-literal gap on the sibling `String(error)` coercion in the `"throw:<String(error)>"` discriminator further down the paragraph — normalise only the `details.observed` site.
