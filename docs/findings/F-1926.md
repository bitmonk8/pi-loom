## Finding

Both interleave notes in `docs/plan.md` â€” the one heading the V9 slice and the one heading the V11 slice â€” assert that `V11a` (Binder-model resolution) "is a prerequisite of `V9c`/`V9i`/`V9j`" and draw the build seam `V9b â†’ V11a â†’ V9c`/`V9i`/`V9j`. The authoritative `Deps` fields of the cited leaves do not support that claim for two of the three named leaves:

- `V9c` (`V9c-T`, `V9a`, `V8a`, `V17a`) omits `V11a`, and its body (prompt-mode conversation drive, active-set gating, cancel-forwarding) consumes no binder-model resolution.
- `V9i` (`V9i-T`, `V9a`, `V17a`, `V6a`, `V8a`, `V3d`, `V4c`, `H4c`) omits `V11a`. Its pre-spawn model guard inspects the subagent loom's resolved `model:` value (frontmatter `model:` or inherited `ctx.model`, owned by `V6a`), not the `bind_model:` resolution `V11a` owns.
- Only `V9j` (`V9j-T`, `V4d`, `V11a`) actually lists `V11a`, consistent with the note.

The `Deps` are correct and the narrative is overbroad: the notes name `V9c`/`V9i` as gated behind `V11a` when neither consumes it. The DAG is misstated at a core seam.
