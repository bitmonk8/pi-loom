## Problem

The opening orientation paragraph of `governance.md` inlines a parenthetical
REQ-ID enumeration that contradicts the page's own authoritative active/retired
sub-tables in three ways: the range "GOV-3 through GOV-9" lists retired GOV-4 as
live; the inline retired sub-list omits GOV-4 and GOV-21; and live rules GOV-19,
GOV-20, GOV-22, GOV-23, GOV-24, and GOV-25 through GOV-29 appear nowhere. The net
effect is an unreliable at-a-glance inventory on the very page the glossary and
other pages name as the authoritative REQ-ID registry. A maintainer or tool
trusting the orientation line concludes that GOV-4 is live and that several live
GOV IDs do not exist.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** 64cdc60 — pi-loom spec: resolve "governance.md plan-corpus dependency + corpus-direction articulation" (T27 + T28 cluster) (2026-05-26, Thomas Andersen); 1d773d6 — pi-loom spec: resolve "GOV-3/GOV-6 subdirectory scan scope and prefix-table hub-stub bindings" (2026-06-04, Thomas Andersen); 8e3ccb4 — pi-loom spec: resolve "GOV-21 bundles five independently testable sub-clauses" (2026-06-05, Thomas Andersen); fe8aa5c — pi-loom spec: resolve "GOV-12 bundles ≥3 independently-testable sub-obligations" (2026-06-05, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** Commit 64cdc60 rewrote the orientation paragraph into the "GOV-3 through GOV-9" range form, at which point GOV-4 was still live and the range was accurate. The summary then drifted as the registry it describes was edited around it: 1d773d6 retired GOV-4 without narrowing the range, 8e3ccb4 retired GOV-21 without adding it to the inline retired sub-list, and fe8aa5c (the most recent edit of the line) added GOV-30/GOV-31 from the GOV-12 split but left the stale range, the missing retired IDs, and the omitted live rules (GOV-19/20/22/23/24/25–29, added incrementally across intervening commits) unreconciled. The defect is the cumulative product of partial updates to a free-prose summary that no lock-step gate covers, not any single commit's error.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the opening orientation paragraph of `governance.md` to delete the
parenthetical REQ-ID enumeration and its inline retired sub-list, replacing the
inline inventory with a description of the classes of rules the page governs.
Add a forward-link to the authoritative active prefix tables
(`governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-a.md`,
`governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`) and the *Retired REQ-IDs* table on
`governance/anchor-scheme-and-retired.md`.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the GOV-16 inline-label paragraph and the GOV-17/GOV-18
  corpus-direction clause in the same sentence — leave those references intact.
