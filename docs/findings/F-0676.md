## Finding

The `details` shape conventions paragraph in `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md` (the `<a id="session-shutdown-details-conventions">` block) opens with: *"Diagnostics that surface the Pi `session_shutdown` event payload — currently the per-invocation `loom/runtime/cancelled-by-session-shutdown` note and the teardown-handler `loom/host/session-shutdown-runtime-degraded` row, both registered below — nest the payload under `details.event.{...}` …"*. The word **currently** carries an open-list reading: a reader scanning this sentence expects further entries to be admissible at the spec's discretion. The very next sentence then closes the set: *"**Closed at `{reason}` in loom 1.0.0.**"* and the same paragraph later restates that any future addition (SDK-derived or runtime-constructed sibling) *"requires a spec edit to this paragraph and to the affected row(s) below before it is permitted on the wire."*

The opening word therefore contradicts the paragraph's own closure pin. The two codes named are not a snapshot of an open list — they are the entire set the convention covers in loom 1.0.0, and the only way to add a third is to amend this paragraph. A drive-by reader who stops at the opening sentence may treat the list as illustrative and assume the runtime is free to surface other `session_shutdown` consumers under `details.event.*` without a spec edit, which is exactly the dynamic-field-set behaviour the *Runtime construction obligation* clause forbids further down.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** `52b9101` (`pi-loom spec: resolve "T24 — Fork-reason watcher closure leaves the extension in an unspecified, silently degraded state"`, 2026-05-11)
**History:** The `session-shutdown-details-conventions` paragraph was added in a single commit (`52b9101`) to the then-monolithic `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics.md`, and both the *"currently the per-invocation … and the teardown-handler … row"* opener and the *"Closed at `{reason}` in V1"* closure sentence were present in that initial introduction — the inconsistency has existed since the paragraph first landed. `f5e89f4` later split `diagnostics.md` into the subdirectory+index layout, moving the paragraph verbatim into `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md`. `f2a82ad` made an unrelated edit to the same paragraph (`basename-hyphen-normalised` clause removal) without touching the opener. The "V1" → "loom 1.0.0" rename in the closure sentence happened in a later editorial pass that, again, left the contradictory opener untouched. No commit ever revisited the qualifier word.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace the word **currently** in the opening sentence of the `session-shutdown-details-conventions` paragraph with **exactly**, matching the spec's existing house style for closed sets (cf. the location-less-diagnostics paragraph two paragraphs above, which uses *"A closed set of diagnostics …"* phrasing). The full clause becomes: *"Diagnostics that surface the Pi `session_shutdown` event payload — **exactly** the per-invocation `loom/runtime/cancelled-by-session-shutdown` note and the teardown-handler `loom/host/session-shutdown-runtime-degraded` row, both registered below — nest the payload under `details.event.{...}` …"*. No other wording in the paragraph needs to change; the existing *"Closed at `{reason}` in loom 1.0.0"* sentence and the *"requires a spec edit to this paragraph"* obligations downstream already carry the normative pin, and "exactly" simply aligns the opener with them.

Edge cases for the implementer: the same paragraph contains the standalone word "currently" zero other times (verified by grep); the edit is a one-word substitution. Do not generalise to other paragraphs that use "currently" — this finding is scoped to this sentence only, where the immediately-following closure sentence makes the qualifier self-contradictory.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A careful reader who continues past the opening clause to the bold *"Closed at `{reason}` in loom 1.0.0"* sentence resolves the ambiguity correctly; nobody implementing strictly from this paragraph will get the wire shape wrong. The only observable cost is the half-second of re-reading the contradicted opener forces on every reviewer.
