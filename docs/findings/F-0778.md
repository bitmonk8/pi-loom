## Finding

The bold paragraph heading *"Success-side null-policy."* and its matching anchor `success-side-null-policy` in `runtime-event-channel.md` are mis-named for their subject. Read literally, "null-policy" denotes a rule about how the runtime treats `null` values — their permissibility, encoding, or wire shape — none of which the paragraph addresses. The paragraph's actual content is that a loom terminating with `Ok(v)` causes **no `loom-system-note` emission** keyed on the `Ok(v)` outcome (with consequent clarifications about pre-evaluation surfaces and the mode-dependent operator-observable surfaces). The rule is a no-emission rule on the success side of the always-log partition, not a null-handling rule.

The naming defect is compounded by reach: the anchor is the link target of inbound cross-references from `overview-and-orientation.md`, `slash-invocation.md` (twice), `pi-integration-contract/conversation-drive.md`, and `implementation-notes.md`, each of which carries the misleading token into prose like *"prompt-mode user-visibility … follows the [success-side null-policy](…)."* A reader hitting any of those links arrives at a paragraph that does not match the term they were primed for, and the plan leaves `V9d` and `V9d-T` (`runtime-event-channel.md` slice) restate the term verbatim in their **Adds** / **Tests** bullets, propagating the mismatch into the implementation contract.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Rename the anchor and the bold paragraph heading to a name that describes the no-emission semantics. Use `success-side-no-emission-rule` for the anchor and *"Success-side no-emission rule."* for the bold heading. In the same edit:

- Update the `<a id="…"></a>` and the bold heading in `runtime-event-channel.md`.
- Update the five inbound link targets (`#success-side-null-policy` → `#success-side-no-emission-rule`) in `overview-and-orientation.md`, `slash-invocation.md` (two occurrences), `pi-integration-contract/conversation-drive.md`, and `implementation-notes.md`, and replace the visible link-text token *"success-side null-policy"* with *"success-side no-emission rule"* at each site so the visible text matches the slug.
- Update the plan leaves `V9d-runtime-event-channel.md` and `V9d-T-runtime-event-channel.md`, where *"success-side null-policy"* / *"null-policy"* appears in the **Adds** and **Tests** bullets, to use the new term.

Edge cases for the implementer:

- Search for the literal token `null-policy` across `docs/`, not just `success-side-null-policy`; the `V9d` leaf shortens the term to *"the null-policy"* on its second mention, which must also be retitled.
- The anchor lives at the *paragraph* level, not a heading level; there is no `##` / `###` to retitle separately — the only structural surface is the inline `<a id>` and the bold-prefixed first sentence.
- Leave the actual paragraph body (operator-observable surfaces, pre-evaluation surfaces, mode-dependent text) unchanged; the rename is name-only.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Readers and implementers following any of the five inbound cross-references arrive at a paragraph whose subject does not match the link text's connotation, costing a re-read. The actual behavioural contract is not in doubt, and no two reasonable implementers diverge on what to build — the prose under the heading is unambiguous. The cost is purely orientation overhead, paid at every cross-reference traversal and every reading of the V9d leaf bullets that quote the term.
