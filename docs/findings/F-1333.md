## Problem

The `loom-system-note` operator-event paragraph at `diagnostic-shape.md:20` ends "Renderers MUST switch on which key is present and MUST NOT assume both", bundling two independently-failable renderer obligations — a positive dispatch obligation and a negative invariant — under no REQ-ID anchor on a non-narrative `DIAG`-prefix page, leaving GOV-9's `#prefix-n` cross-link contract unsatisfiable and GOV-22 coinage owed. The same sentence states that "two" `details` payload shapes are disjoint by key, but the authoritative inventory at `runtime-event-channel.md#system-note-details-shapes` enumerates four disjoint shapes and states the renderer invariant in a single MUST. The diagnostic-shape.md sentence is therefore both a compound un-anchored MUST and a stale, narrower restatement of a contract another page already owns.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (at this site)
**Introducing commits:** `b3bc4ce` (2026-05-04) introduced the sentence with its compound `MUST … MUST NOT` form in the pre-shard `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics.md`; `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04) carried it verbatim into the sharded `diagnostic-shape.md`; `038dab3` (2026-06-05) rewrote the leading clause without touching the trailing compound MUST.
**History:** `git log -S "Renderers MUST switch on which key is present"` reports exactly that chain; `git log -L 18,22:docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md` confirms the compound form has been continuous. GOV-22 was introduced later (`4f588e0`, 2026-06-03), so the un-anchored compound MUST predates the rule that now obliges coinage; it is standing un-anchored residue under `coverage-matrix.md` §Code-keyed obligation areas rather than a regression.

## Solution approach

Delete the trailing compound MUST clause from the `loom-system-note` operator-event paragraph at `diagnostic-shape.md:20` and rewrite the surviving framing as a non-normative cross-reference to `runtime-event-channel.md#system-note-details-shapes`, which owns the key-dispatch and no-multiple-keys obligations against the complete inventory. Correct the surviving cardinality from "two" to "four".

## Solution constraints

- Do not coin DIAG-5/DIAG-6 or any new DIAG ID for these renderer obligations: `runtime-event-channel.md#system-note-details-shapes` already owns them against the complete inventory, so co-locating DIAG IDs would freeze a stale subset into the DIAG namespace and create dual-ownership drift.
