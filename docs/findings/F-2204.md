## Finding

QRY-14 (`query/query-tool-loop.md`, *Typed queries are tool-loop-shaped*) introduces the typed-query mechanism as a "**two-phase** loop" and then lists its two elements with categorically asymmetric labels: step 1 is "*Free phase.*" (phase vocabulary) while step 2 is "*Forced respond turn.*" (turn vocabulary). An umbrella named "two-phase loop" implies two peers of the same kind, but the labels present one phase and one turn.

The asymmetry is not arbitrary — it tracks a real structural difference. The free phase spans multiple tool-call rounds (a genuine multi-turn phase), whereas the terminator is exactly one turn (at `max_rounds: 0` it is the only turn issued). "Forced respond turn" is the more accurate word for that single terminating turn, and it is the entrenched cross-page term: it appears in QRY-15, QRY-16, the glossary, `conversation-drive.md`, `implementation-notes.md`, `ceilings-3-and-4.md`, the `queryerror-variants.md` failure surface, and roughly a dozen other spec and plan files. The defect is therefore in the umbrella label "two-phase loop" asserting two phases when one element is a single turn — not in the element labels themselves.

The meaning is recoverable from context; nothing diverges behaviourally. This is a terminology-symmetry defect in a normatively load-bearing section.

The defect has existed since the construct was first coined; no later commit introduced or worsened it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Relabel the second element to a phase

**Approach.** Rename step 2's label from "*Forced respond turn.*" to "*Forced respond phase.*" so both numbered elements read as phases, matching the "two-phase loop" umbrella.

**Spec edits.** Change the step-2 label in QRY-14. To stay consistent, the entrenched bare term "forced respond turn" would also have to migrate to "forced respond phase" wherever it names the construct (QRY-15, QRY-16, glossary, `conversation-drive.md`, `implementation-notes.md`, `ceilings-3-and-4.md`, `queryerror-variants.md`, and the plan leaves).

**Pros.**
- Preserves the "two-phase loop" umbrella verbatim, so the `conversation-drive.md` / `implementation-notes.md` "two-phase" usages stay valid unchanged.

**Cons.**
- "Phase" overstates the element: it is exactly one turn (the only turn at `max_rounds: 0`), so "turn" is the more accurate word.
- Requires a wide rename of the entrenched "forced respond turn" term across ~12+ files; a partial rename leaves the corpus worse off than today.

**Risks.** Stale "forced respond turn" survivors after an incomplete sweep would reintroduce the very asymmetry this finding flags, on a larger surface.

### Option B — Rename the umbrella so it does not assert two phases

**Approach.** Keep both element labels ("Free phase", "Forced respond turn") and rename the construct from "two-phase loop" to a label that does not imply two phases — e.g. "two-part loop" or "free-phase / forced-respond-turn loop".

**Spec edits.** Change "runs a **two-phase** loop" in QRY-14 to "runs a **two-part** loop" (or the spelled-out form). Repoint the other umbrella usages for consistency: `conversation-drive.md` ("a **two-phase tool-loop conversation**"), `implementation-notes.md` ("the two-phase loop"), and the glossary entry if it names the construct. Leave every "free phase" and "forced respond turn" occurrence untouched.

**Pros.**
- Keeps both accurate, entrenched element terms ("forced respond turn" is correctly a single turn).
- Edits the small set of umbrella callsites rather than the large set of "forced respond turn" callsites.

**Cons.**
- Touches a handful of pages that currently spell "two-phase".

**Risks.** Minor: a missed umbrella callsite leaves mixed "two-phase" / "two-part" spellings; bounded by the small callsite count.

### Recommendation

Adopt **Option B**. The second element genuinely is a single terminating turn, so "forced respond turn" is the correct word and should stay; the defect lives in the umbrella claiming two phases. Renaming the umbrella to "two-part loop" both removes the false implication and edits the smaller, contained set of callsites. When making the edit, sweep the three other umbrella spellings (`conversation-drive.md`, `implementation-notes.md`, glossary) in the same change so no mixed "two-phase" / "two-part" spelling remains.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

If shipped unfixed, an "two-phase loop" umbrella names two peers while labelling one a phase and the other a turn; a reader may briefly expect a second multi-turn phase that does not exist. No implementation behaviour diverges and the intended structure is recoverable from the surrounding prose.
