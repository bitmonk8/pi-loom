## Finding

Capability inventory item 4 ("Tool registration and gating") in `capability-inventory-items.md` ends with: *"The lack of `pi.unregisterTool` and the resulting cosmetic registration-cache accumulation are documented in the same section."* The word "section" never appears earlier in the bullet, so "the same section" has no explicit antecedent. The only nearby referent is the preceding sentence's bolded cross-link, *"Consumed by **Tool-registration lifetime and visibility** above"* — but that sentence refers to the target as a named link, not as "a section", forcing the reader to infer that the bolded link denotes the section that "the same section" points back to.

The intended referent is unambiguous on investigation: the `pi.unregisterTool` absence and the prompt-mode registration-cache accumulation are documented on `tool-registration-lifetime.md` under the **Tool-registration lifetime and visibility** heading (the cache is the `Map<schemaSlug, registeredToolName>` described there), not on the capability-inventory page itself. A reader who mis-resolves "the same section" to the surrounding capability-inventory bullet would look in the wrong place and find nothing. The fix is a one-clause edit: name the referent explicitly, e.g. *"…are documented in **Tool-registration lifetime and visibility** above."*

This is sharpened by the spec's split layout — the referenced section now lives in a different file from item 4, so "the same section" reads even more loosely than it did when both were in one document.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `27ec53d` ("Resolve Cluster-A: per-mode tool registration", 2026-05-04) seeded the bare form ("extension-scoped registration cache" / "the registration cache"); `6d1a949`, `443ee4e`, and `941e52c` (early-to-mid June 2026, during the spec-shard split and PIC-36/PIC-49 coining) added the **prompt-mode registration cache** qualifier on `registration-steps.md` without back-propagating it to `tool-registration-lifetime.md` or to the per-paragraph bare-form residues that were created in step 5 alongside the qualified mentions.
**History:** `git log -S 'extension-scoped registration cache' -- docs/` shows the bare form entering at `27ec53d` on the pre-split `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md`; the May 2026 large split (`f5e89f4`) carried it across to `tool-registration-lifetime.md` unchanged. `git log -S 'prompt-mode registration cache' -- docs/` shows the qualified form first appearing in `6d1a949` on `registration-steps.md` and accreting in `443ee4e` (PIC-49) and `941e52c` (PIC-36 / PIC-38). `git log -L /'prompt-mode registration'/,+1:docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/registration-steps.md` confirms the qualifier was added inside the step-5 paragraph alongside surviving bare references in the same paragraph, never sweeping the canonical site. The drift therefore exists as the residue of two independent edit campaigns that did not share a vocabulary pass.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In item 4, replace "are documented in the same section" with an explicit name of the referent section: *"The lack of `pi.unregisterTool` and the resulting cosmetic registration-cache accumulation are documented in **Tool-registration lifetime and visibility** above."* This reuses the exact bolded label already used in the prior sentence, so the two references are visibly the same target. No new cross-link target or anchor is required.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader of item 4 must infer that "the same section" means the **Tool-registration lifetime and visibility** section named in the prior sentence rather than the capability-inventory bullet they are reading. No implementation behaviour diverges; the cost is a momentary wayfinding stumble in a cross-reference.
