## Finding

The **Cancellation** paragraph in `docs/spec_topics/binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md` says: "The runtime checks `ctx.signal` immediately before issuing the binder call and forwards the signal into the binder inference call as its `options.signal`." The same page's failure-modes table reuses the same wording in its sixth row: "`ctx.signal` aborted before or during the binder call".

`docs/spec_topics/cancellation.md` is the single source of truth for the cancellation surface. Its **Signal source** paragraph pins `loomAbort.signal` as "the single source of truth that every downstream component sees" and writes "never `ctx.signal` directly". It further requires the runtime to "MUST tolerate `ctx.signal` being `undefined` at slash-command entry" because Pi documents `ctx.signal` as `undefined` in idle, non-turn contexts — exactly when the slash-command handler (and therefore the binder call) fires. `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/binder-inference.md` already wires the binder consistently with `cancellation.md`: "`options.signal` is `loomAbort.signal` (always defined; see **Cancellation source** below)."

So the binder page's wording is the lone divergent reading. A literal implementer following only that page would read `ctx.signal` at slash entry (receive `undefined`), forward `undefined` as `options.signal`, and lose every binder-call cancellation — including the pre-call checkpoint, the in-flight provider abort, and the surfacing of the `argument binding cancelled` system note. The fix is a localised wording correction on this page (the **Cancellation** paragraph and the table row).

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Make two byte-level edits on `docs/spec_topics/binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md` so the page is consistent with `cancellation.md` and `binder-inference.md`:

1. In the **Cancellation** paragraph, replace "The runtime checks `ctx.signal` immediately before issuing the binder call" with "The runtime checks `loomAbort.signal` immediately before issuing the binder call". The remainder of the sentence ("and forwards the signal into the [binder inference call] as its `options.signal`") is already accurate and unchanged.
2. In the *Failure-mode templates* sixth row's **Cause** cell, replace "`ctx.signal` aborted before or during the binder call" with "`loomAbort.signal` aborted before or during the binder call". The **System note** cell (`loom /<name>: argument binding cancelled`) is unchanged.

Edge cases for the implementer to confirm during the edit:

- Do not add a parenthetical "(see [Cancellation — Signal source])" cross-reference inside the cell — the table is byte-stable and adding visible content there changes the rendering surface.
- The **Cancellation** paragraph's reference to "the bypass path … cancellable at the next regular checkpoint inside the loom body" is unaffected; it does not name a specific signal and already relies on the general checkpoint contract.
- After the spec edit, the **Adds** field of `plan_topics/V11j-binder-call-cancellation.md` ("`ctx.signal` forwarded into the binder's provider invocation as `options.signal`") must be updated in lockstep to say `loomAbort.signal`; otherwise the plan/spec consistency gate trips.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

An implementer who reads only the binder page will read `ctx.signal` at slash entry, observe `undefined` (Pi's documented idle-context value), and forward `undefined` into `options.signal`. The binder LLM call then becomes uncancellable: Esc during the in-flight binder call cannot abort it, the budgeted retry cannot be suppressed, and the `argument binding cancelled` system note never surfaces. Two implementers reading the binder page in isolation versus alongside `cancellation.md` will produce behaviourally divergent runtimes.
