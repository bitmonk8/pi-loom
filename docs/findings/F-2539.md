## Problem

Under the `gov-8` anchor in `req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`, the *Scope note* and the ex-GOV-14 *Editorial note* both state that GOV-8 is a bookkeeping discipline that does not substitute for the GOV-15 equivalence goal; `gov-15` itself restates this a third time. The *Editorial note*'s closing reviewer-posture clause keeps GOV-18-excluded reviewer-conduct content alive in the corpus.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-remediation (multiple commits)
**Introducing commits:** `5a772e3`, `7995d3c`, `2ff03c2`
**History:** Each fragment entered as a by-product of resolving an earlier spec-review finding, not at corpus inception:

- The GOV-8 editorial note was added by `5a772e3` (resolve "GOV-14 two SHOULD-NOTs admit no mechanical witness", 2026-06-05), which retired GOV-14 and demoted its reviewer-posture text to a non-normative note retained at the former site.
- The retired-table migration HTML comment was added by `7995d3c` (resolve "GOV-15 buried in retired-prefix-table graveyard file", 2026-06-05), which relocated GOV-15 to `source-language-stability.md`.
- The "rejected-candidate record (closed)" paragraph was added by `2ff03c2` (resolve "Source-language stability bullet leaves equivalence undefined…", 2026-05-10) and carried forward unchanged through the file-split commit `f5e89f4`.

## Solution approach

Restructure the two consecutive notes under the `gov-8` anchor into a single non-normative note that states the bookkeeping-vs-equivalence point once and forward-links `gov-15`. Delete the closing reviewer-posture clause.
