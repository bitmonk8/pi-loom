## Finding

The §Release-gate paragraph in `docs/plan.md` states that `H6a` "sequences immediately after the canary (its **Deps** name `H5b`)", naming only `H5b`. `H6a`'s authoritative `Deps` field is `H5b, H7a`: the leaf also depends on the terminal integration-acceptance leaf `H7a`, which produces the committed multi-feature fixture `.loom` that `H6a`'s manual real-host smoke release-gate criterion drives against the live host. The `H7a` edge is pinned directly on `H6a` (not threaded through `H5b`) because `H7a` closes no new spec REQ-ID and is therefore not a member of `H5b`'s coverage-producing set.

A reader sequencing from the §Release-gate section alone would conclude `H6a` is pickable once `H5b` completes, missing the `H7a` prerequisite. Execution is not actually broken — the authoritative pickability predicate (How-to-use step 3) routes the implementer to the leaf's own `Deps` field, which correctly carries `H5b, H7a` — so this is a narrative under-declaration in the master-index prose, not a defect in the leaf's dependency edges.
