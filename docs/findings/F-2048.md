## Finding

The glossary's `in-loop` and `query-terminating` entries each close with the same contrast sentence: *"Contrast with \*[the other term]\* and the slash-load `params` cross-routing arm — the three together partition ceiling #4's enforcement-point arms per the per-boundary table."* This asserts an exhaustive tripartite partition of ceiling #4's enforcement-point arms whose three members are presented as co-equal peers.

Two of the three members — `in-loop` and `query-terminating` — are spec-coined terms with their own glossary entries and `<a id="...">` anchors, discoverable directly and citable by name. The third member is carried only by the descriptive phrase "the slash-load `params` cross-routing arm." It has no coined short name, no glossary entry, and no anchor, even though it is named as a partition peer of the other two. The underlying arm is real and fully specified elsewhere (the `params` validation row of the per-boundary destination/surface table at `ceilings-3-and-4.md#ceiling-4-table`, and CIO-1 at `#cio-1`, which describe how the slash-load `params` arm cross-routes to ceiling #3's load-time / no-retry classification), but the glossary registers two of three peers and leaves the third without a term a reader can look up or body prose can reference.

The asymmetry is purely a naming/registration gap: a reader who encounters the partition has a direct glossary target for two arms and a long phrase for the third.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** present-since-inception (of the affected entries)
**Introducing commits:** `8168ea8` — "pi-loom spec: resolve \"T30 ceiling #4 model-driven row trichotomy carve-out\"" (2026-05-10)
**History:** The `in-loop` and `query-terminating` glossary entries, their `<a id="in-loop">` / `<a id="query-terminating">` anchors, and the tripartite "Contrast with … the slash-load `params` cross-routing arm — the three together partition" sentence in both entries were all introduced together in a single commit (`git log -S` on the anchors and on the partition phrase against `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` returns only `8168ea8`). The naming asymmetry — two arms coined and registered as terms, the third left as a bare descriptive phrase — was therefore present at the moment the entries were created; no later commit introduced or widened it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Coin and register a short term for the third arm

**Approach:** Coin a short name for the slash-load `params` cross-routing arm (e.g. `slash-load cross-route` or `params cross-route`) and add a slim glossary entry with its own `<a id="...">` anchor, defining it as the ceiling-#4 enforcement-point arm whose breach cross-routes to ceiling #3's load-time / no-retry classification. Update the two contrast sentences in `in-loop` and `query-terminating` to reference the new coined term, putting all three partition members on equal footing.

**Spec edits:**
- Add a `<a id="...">`-anchored entry for the coined term in `glossary.md`, pointing its `See:` at `ceilings-3-and-4.md#ceiling-4-table` and `#cio-1`.
- Replace the long descriptive phrase in both the `in-loop` and `query-terminating` contrast sentences with the coined term.

**Pros:**
- Restores symmetry: all three partition peers are coined, anchored, and individually discoverable/citable.
- Gives body prose a term to reference the arm directly.

**Cons:**
- Adds a third coined term to maintain and keep aligned with the per-boundary table.
- The arm is already canonically owned by the per-boundary table; a glossary term risks a second near-authoritative description to keep in lock-step.

**Risks:** A new term must be checked against GOV-16 inline-label / naming conventions and the existing naming-drift synonym lists; coining it carelessly could itself become spec-prose drift.

### Option B — Demote the partition naming to a non-normative aside

**Approach:** Reduce the contrast statements in both entries so they no longer name three peer arms. Instead, point at the per-boundary table as the authoritative partition and describe the third arm only by reference (e.g. "the remaining `params`-validation arm, which cross-routes per the per-boundary table"), without asserting a coined tripartite peer set.

**Spec edits:**
- Rewrite the closing contrast sentence in both `in-loop` and `query-terminating` to a non-normative aside that defers the full partition to `ceilings-3-and-4.md#ceiling-4-table` rather than enumerating three named peers.

**Pros:**
- No new coined term to register or keep aligned; the per-boundary table remains the single owner of the arm classification.
- Smallest surface; removes the asymmetry by not claiming a peer the glossary does not register.

**Cons:**
- Loses the crisp "three together partition" framing in the glossary entries.

**Risks:** Minimal; care needed so the aside still makes clear the three arms are exhaustive.

### Recommendation

Option B. The per-boundary destination/surface table already canonically owns ceiling #4's enforcement-point partition; demoting the glossary contrast to a non-normative aside removes the naming asymmetry without coining a third near-duplicate term to keep in lock-step. Watch that the rewritten aside still conveys exhaustiveness (three arms cover all of ceiling #4) so the partition guarantee is not lost.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader or author who needs to reference the third partition arm has no glossary term to cite, unlike the other two peers; the arm is recoverable only by reading the per-boundary table. No runtime behaviour diverges — the arm itself is fully specified on its canonical page — so this is a discoverability/guidance gap, not a correctness defect.
