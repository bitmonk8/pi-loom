## Problem

The `Pi tool` vs. `` `.loom` callable `` glossary entry carries a normative MUST-level terminology-usage mandate (requiring the canonical term `.loom callable` and naming synonyms to avoid), but the `glossary.md` preamble disclaims normative authority and `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` classifies `glossary.md` as narrative ("no IDs"). The canonical pages the entry points at ‚Äî `tool-calls.md`, whose opening contrasts a Pi tool against a `.loom` callable, and `frontmatter.md`, which defines the `tools:` field ‚Äî introduce the distinction normatively but carry no terminology-usage requirement or synonym-avoidance list. A reviewer consulting the canonical pages for requirements never encounters the MUST, and the glossary preamble formally outranks the rule with the very canonical pages that omit it.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** specific-commit
**Introducing commits:** `fec0870` ‚Äî "pi-loom spec: resolve \"callable-set prose synonyms erode no-inheritance invariant\"" (2026-05-06)
**History:** The `Pi tool` vs. `` `.loom` callable `` glossary entry predates `fec0870`; before that commit it carried only the descriptive contrast between the two entry kinds, with no usage obligation. `fec0870` added the normative *"Authors and implementers MUST use the canonical term `.loom callable` ‚Ä¶"* sentence directly into the glossary entry, placing a MUST-level mandate on a page the glossary preamble and `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` (`glossary.md` ‚Üí "no IDs ‚Äî narrative") both designate descriptive/non-normative. The defect entered with that single edit; the entry has since been carried unchanged through the `docs/` relocation (`31ff060`) and the `spec_topics/` collapse (`81bf52a`).

## Solution approach

Move the terminology-usage mandate off the `glossary.md` entry onto a single canonical page ‚Äî either the `tool-calls.md` opening Pi-tool / `.loom`-callable contrast or `frontmatter.md`'s `tools:` definition ‚Äî and demote the surviving glossary copy to a descriptive pointer at that canonical site.

- The relocated mandate is a defining obligation site on a non-narrative page; it MUST carry a co-located REQ-ID anchor per GOV-22 (allocate the next free numeric tail under the destination page's registered prefix).
